<question>1</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Elara’s motive—returning to fuse with the forbidden hum—is clear and consistently embodied through tactile choices (approach, restraint, surrender). The internal conflict between literal paraphrase and dissolution is credible and enacted, not summarized. The change at the end (becoming a conduit) is earned, though the felt cost remains subtle (a renunciation of old methods rather than a painful sacrifice). Absence of an external counterforce limits pressure on her micro-choices.</comment>
<question>2</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Accumulative/Textural lens: the piece builds pressure via density and cadence toward a Conceptual Repricing—art as fusion rather than description—enacted by the luminous poem. Orientation is clean early (“after the ban lifts,” cobalt atrium, frozen lake). Closure lands on the page with the poem’s creation and dissolution, though cost is conceptual more than consequential. The structure is cohesive and single-scene with one meaningful deepening.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Setting is vivid and integral: the cobalt atrium, frozen lake, and humming quartz colonnade shape perception and action, and the final revaluation ties the hum to “the lake’s frozen currents” and the atrium’s memory. Sensory specificity is strong across sound and touch (pressure in bones, hoarfrost underfoot). World rules feel coherent and learned through interaction. The environment charges the final image rather than serving as a postcard.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she can meet the Colonnade’s song without reducing it—tightens when she resists literal translation and chooses dissolution. Escalation reads as a deepening of resonance and surrender rather than a series of turns, apt for the chosen lens. The price (abandoning former poetics) is felt but not sharp; no external consequences test her stance. Still, the cadence shift around the “tremor of certainty” provides a clear peak.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Theme coheres around boundary dissolution and art as fusion, supported by recurring images that change meaning. However, explicit thesis statements (“true art is: not description, but fusion”; “the vast, beautiful truth of connection”) reduce subtext and invite a mild sermonizing feel late. The best moments embed theme in image (the poem made of light and sound, then dissolving). The resonance lingers but could trust implication more.</comment>

The prose modulates well between hush and surge.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Line-level craft is strong with rich images and disciplined syntax; the read-aloud cadence mostly breathes. The final lines land cleanly without scaffolding.</comment>
