<question>5</question><grade>9.6</grade><comment>The theme of memory versus institutional erasure emerges organically from a powerful set of interconnected images: the death certificate, the invalid barcode, the poison label, and the micro-recorder. <question>6</question><grade>9.5</grade><comment>The third-person limited POV is executed with exceptional control, maintaining a consistent, lyrical voice that mirrors the story's dreamlike and oppressive atmosphere. Mara's perceptual signature, focused on sound, texture, and rhythm, is distinct and unwavering. <question>7</question><grade>9.1</grade><comment>The prose is dense, evocative, and largely flawless, with a strong internal rhythm. <question>8</question><grade>9.0</grade><comment>While the "person erased by bureaucracy" is a familiar trope, the story transforms it through its central, chilling metaphor of a chemical agent that dissolves histories and expands with the tide.
