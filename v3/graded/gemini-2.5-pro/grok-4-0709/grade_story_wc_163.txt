<question>4</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Elara's isolation versus her desire for belonging—is established early. However, the pressure remains static for much of the story. The cost of her final attunement is not deeply felt, making the resolution feel more like a gentle arrival than an earned victory over a significant obstacle.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The theme of finding belonging in a place rather than with people emerges organically from the setting and Elara's unique profession. This thematic echo slightly flattens the complexity, stating what the imagery is already effectively showing.</comment>

The prose has a specific texture, though it sometimes leans into a repetitive cadence of complex sentences. There are no major flaws, but the voice lacks the modulation that would heighten the tension during the story's climax.</comment>
