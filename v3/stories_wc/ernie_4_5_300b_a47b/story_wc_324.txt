Lila adjusted her headset, the mist curling around her ankles like spectral cats as she stepped into the atrium. [25] The glass dome above filtered twilight into a bruised violet, the air thick with the scent of damp moss and forgotten promises. [80] The photograph wasn’t just a memento; it was a clue. The dead called here, their voices faint as static, and Lila’s task was to assure their loved ones that their final words hadn’t vanished into the ether. [108]

[132] “What didn’t you finish?” she murmured, pulling a hearing aid from her bag—a relic from a previous case, its battery long dead but still capable of capturing vibrations. But the core concept of her work—incidental heroism—demanded she try. [191] She closed her eyes, letting the mist coat her lashes, and followed the sound. [199]
