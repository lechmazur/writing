Lirah, with her hands calloused from weaving supple reeds harvested from distant, fog-shrouded marshes, felt the luminescent ache of isolation deep in her bones. [73]
She had always been boldly sympathetic, her heart reaching out to mend the frayed edges of others' sorrows, yet she remained an outsider, ungrafted to the webs of kinship that bound the orchard's inhabitants. [105]

Lirah's motivation burned brightly: to graft herself onto this community, to become a vital strand in their intricate web, no longer adrift in the crater's glowing void. [159]

Whispers in the form of shared glances and reed-woven symbols spread among the orchard dwellers: this tag was a key to unraveling lost kinships, a map to forgotten bonds that could strengthen their communal web. [232]
But the full tag was scattered, hidden across the orchard's perilous edges, where the crater's fumes whispered temptations of oblivion. [250]
