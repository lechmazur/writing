[16] Her ambition was a quiet, deep-rooted thing: to understand the silent language of plants, a skill that demanded more than just knowledge. [67] This year, the shift felt different, more significant, for cradled in damp moss within her treetop conservatory was a single corpse flower seed. [89]

It was her ultimate test, a dense, stony promise of a magnificent, malodorous bloom that few herbalists ever managed to coax into existence. [113] To succeed would be to achieve a perfect state of balance, to cultivate serenity within the constant, gentle motion of her home.
