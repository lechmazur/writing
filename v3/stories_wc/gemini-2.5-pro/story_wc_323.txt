[20] The iron was cold and impossibly heavy, a relic from a surface world most of his students had never seen. [38] It was a grounding tool in his lessons, a symbol of the old physics, the old world. [54] But since the Visitors arrived in the silent space above their ocean, the link felt different, less like an anchor and more like a shackle. [77]

[99] Life was a careful routine of pressure checks, sonar sweeps, and recycled air, a fragile existence that had always felt complete. [120] The discovery of aliens had shattered that completeness, leaving behind a quiet, somber marvel in its wake. [136] It was this feeling that drove Kaelen’s new obsession, a professional need that bordered on the spiritual. [153] He had to learn to navigate by starlight. [161]

[244] His was a balancedly curious nature, not prone to wild fantasy, but this new reality demanded a new cartography. [482] It was a map, written in the language of physics, a current flowing between the stars.
