[32] She was a firefighter, the daughter of an arsonist, and this place was her only church. [76] Her posture was gracefully alert, a habit learned in burning buildings that now served her in stillness, attuned to the whisper of wind across the dunes. [103]

[132] Elara’s breath caught; the conservatory was a monument of glass and ancient wood, a place her father had once threatened but never touched, a line even he wouldn’t cross. [164] Her calm was not shattered but sharpened, her movements fluid as she rose. [177] This was not just a job; it was an inheritance she was determined to rewrite. [192]

[248] She saw not just its destruction, but the deep, weeping gashes in its trunk where the fire had burned hottest, like mortal wounds. [479] She used the strong red thread to lash her graft to the tree, the vibrant color a stark promise against the charcoal bark.
