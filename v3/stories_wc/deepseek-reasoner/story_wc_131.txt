<story>Silas lived in the whisper-thin spaces between things, a crawl space dweller moving through the walls of the great library. [21] His world was one of dust motes and forgotten histories, and he was kindly methodical in his care of them. [37] While the city’s lights flicker after an unexpected outage, a sapphire hush fell through the arched windows, the pre-dawn gloom dyed a deep, tranquil blue. [64] This was his time, his setting: the shadowed library at dawn. [76] His motivation, a quiet yearning he seldom voiced, was to become visible to those who need you, not as a man, but as a presence, a solution found in the stacks. [105]

[138] Silas slipped into a service corridor, his hands finding familiar holds in the dark, his method always the same: uncovering in pipe chases and air ducts the things the building itself had swallowed. [174] Behind a loose panel near the floor, his fingers brushed against cold, curled metal. [190] It was a ferromagnetic tape reel fragment, brittle and sharp-edged, a ghost of obsolete technology. [209] He almost left it, but a compulsion made him cache it in his leather apron pocket. The lights outside stuttered again, but Silas was already moving, a new purpose in his methodical work. [520] He gathered books—not the dense academic tomes, but the ones with worn spines and stories of comfort and adventure. [647] Her fingers brushed the cover, and she looked around, confused, before sinking down to read. [667] She was no longer alone. [673] Silas felt a profound visibility, not of his person, but of his intent. [688] He was the hand that offered the book. [698] The city’s power finally surged back on, but the library retained its sapphire hush, a sacred space between night and day.
