[45] His home was a dry chamber in the masonry, a pocket of air in the sunken city ruins, accessible only by diving through sedimentary layers of silt and forgotten history. [75] The world above was quiet now, populated by simple survivors who saw the past as a curse. [93] Silas saw it as a library, and he was its last, rationally rebellious librarian. [108]

[140] He would agonize for hours over a single line of text from a salvaged book, cross-referencing it with star charts etched onto his chamber wall. [167] The competing narratives of history tormented him; was the fall a result of biological inevitability or a failure of philosophy? [191] He needed to know which story was true. [199]

[749] He began to explain the chronometer, how its gears mirrored the heavens, transforming a narrative of conflict into one of observation and method.
