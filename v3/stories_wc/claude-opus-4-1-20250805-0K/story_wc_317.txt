[20] As proprietor of "The Paradox Box," the city's most challenging escape room, she was notably receptive to puzzles that arrived uninvited. [40] But this one made her hands tremble. [47]

[94] This pocket of wilderness existed impossibly between office buildings, as if the city had forgotten to pave it over. [113] The air held that storm-born silence that follows lightning but precedes thunder—electric, waiting, dense with unspoken change. [131]

Mira understood spaces that shouldn't exist. [137] Her escape rooms bent reality through orthogonal approaches, each puzzle intersecting the next at unexpected angles, forcing players to think sideways, upward, inward. [158] She'd built her reputation on creating environments where logic had to flow like water, finding new channels when old ones proved false. [180]

[204] These seeds were nearly impossible to obtain legally, blooming once a decade with a stench that could clear buildings. [223] Yet someone had chosen her to receive it. [231]

[308] Innovation districts, they called them, though Mira knew better. [318] True innovation required negative space, rooms to breathe, places where strange seeds could take root. [334]

Mira smiled, understanding finally. [582] She wasn't just safeguarding space for innovation; she was innovating space itself. [594] The corpse flower would bloom soon, not with death's perfume but with the fragrance of hidden possibilities.
