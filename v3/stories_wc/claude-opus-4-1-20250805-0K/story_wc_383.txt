[24] The velvet garden stretched before her in twilight's embrace, its purple blooms so dark they seemed to absorb the dying light rather than reflect it. [48] She'd been coming here every evening since the Institute released her, drawn by something she couldn't name, carrying half a shell she couldn't explain. [72]

[91] The Cognitive Quarantine Program had promised to archive her trauma, lock it away where it couldn't hurt her anymore. [132] She'd emerged skeptical of her own emptiness, charmed by the peace but haunted by its price. [148]
