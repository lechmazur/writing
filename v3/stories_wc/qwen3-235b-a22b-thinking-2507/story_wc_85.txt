[235] Now, haunted by the weight of unspoken words he’d helped bury, he sought the one truth he’d deliberately obscured: what happened to Elara Vanya. [410] Silas had quelled the inconvenient truth by crafting flawless false memories, making her vanish from her own lineage as cleanly as dust motes in the sieved hush. [499] He’d told himself it was just business, another transaction in the shadow trade of identity. [555] But Elara’s face, caught in a smuggled photograph showing fierce eyes and a defiant set to her jaw, had haunted his sleep, a ghost of the vibrant woman his false memories had extinguished. [665] He needed to discover the weight of that withheld truth, to know if she’d survived the purge her altered history likely triggered. [734] The cobalt tiles beneath him, once vibrant under the midday sun, now felt cold and accusing, mirroring the chill in his own chest. [816] His method was desperate, born of years scouring coastal ruins: sifting through driftwood carvings left by nameless refugees, their messages to the sea etched in wood, hoping for a fragment connected to her flight. [998] He pried the waterlogged piece free, the wood splintering under his urgent grip. [1135] Recognition struck him like a physical blow; he’d seen this exact broken hand before, embedded in the lid of Elara’s father’s antique chronometer during their first meeting. [1582] The sieved hush deepened, the distant crash of waves suddenly muffled as the enormity settled upon him. [1645] He hadn’t just altered a record; he’d silenced a life, his false memories becoming the weapon. [1708] Tears, hot and long-denied, tracked through the grime on his face, not for absolution he didn’t deserve, but for the vibrant woman extinguished by his transaction. [1798] He closed his fist around the watch hand, the sharp edge biting into his skin, a tangible anchor to the truth he could no longer deny. [1885] The cobalt terrace, vast and empty under the bruised twilight sky, offered no judgment, only the stark clarity of consequence. [1964] He would not manufacture another falsehood. [1992] Rising stiffly, the driftwood scattered forgotten at his feet, Silas turned his back on the sea, carrying the weight of the watch hand and the sieved hush within him, the tempered regret of a man finally facing the ruins he’d built.
