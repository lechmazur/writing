[25] Her boots crunched over irradiated gravel, the only sound besides the wind's hungry howl, in this place the old maps called the Dust Bowl Orchards. [55] Once, these rows held peaches and cherries; now, gnarled apple trees, impossibly green and heavy with fruit, defied the poisoned sky, thriving where nothing else could. [364] She knelt, brushing radioactive grit from the stone, feeling its cool, dense weight. [395] It wasn't just a gem; it was a key. [1002] She hadn't just debunked a rumor; she'd unearthed the deliberate architecture of their suffering. [1037] The comfortable lies were a dam holding back a flood of guilt and greed. [1067] Intuiting the full scope—the scale of the betrayal, the calculated sacrifice—she understood her real work began now. [1105] She carefully placed the flawed diamond back into the moss at the stillpoint's core; it belonged here, a silent witness. [1145] Gathering the reassembled letters, Lena shouldered her pack, the weight of truth heavier than any stone. [1179] She turned her back on the thriving orchard, walking towards the storm, carrying the excavation of truth out of the wasteland and into the light. [1226]
