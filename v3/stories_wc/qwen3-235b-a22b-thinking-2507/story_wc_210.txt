Its surface, smooth as fossilized tears, held no seam, no stopper, no hint of how it was made or what secrets it kept sealed within. [49]
Around him, the ghost town lay silent, its wooden buildings leaning like weary bones beneath a sky bleached pale by regret. [75]
After the last wish was granted—the final plea for freedom from sorrow that scattered the townsfolk like ash on the wind—the Cove had become a hollow shell. [110]
He remembered the lanterns: once, they’d glowed like captured stars above the cobbled streets, guiding lovers and laborers home through the salty dark. [146]
Now, only frayed strings dangled from empty hooks, and the cove’s paper lanterns were ash and memory. [171]
The alchemist turned the bottle slowly, its golden depths catching the weak afternoon light, but he refused the illusion it offered—a flicker of warmth that promised comfort. [210]
He knew better than to seek solace in phantoms; the bottle’s glow was mere refraction, a trick of the resin, not a doorway to what was lost. [247]
False comfort had always been the Cove’s poison: the townsfolk had wished their sorrows away rather than face them, trading truth for fleeting ease until only he remained. [288]
He rejected it utterly, for to embrace such illusions was to doom any future to the same fragile, broken pattern. [319]
His fingers tightened around the bottle, its unyielding form a reminder of what could not be undone—the past was fixed, sealed as tightly as the vessel he held. [358]
Yet, in that fixedness, he found clarity: if the old civilization had crumbled under the weight of its own illusions, he would conceive a new one from the bedrock of reality. [402]
His motivation burned fierce and pure—to reimagine an entire civilization, not as a replica of the past, but as a living testament to resilience. [440]
He would build it not on wishes, but on the unshakeable foundation of what could be, brick by brick, idea by idea. [474]
But how to hold such vastness in a single mind? The answer came not from scrolls or stones, but from the quiet engine of his own working memory. [512]
He closed his eyes, shutting out the desolation, and began to conceive. [533]
First, the streets: not the cobbled lanes of before, but paths of woven seagrass, resilient and salt-tolerant, curving gently to follow the land’s natural contours. [573]
