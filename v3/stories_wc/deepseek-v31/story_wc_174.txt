[164] Tradition held that whoever held it must speak a truth, spinning a tale from the previous speaker’s ending. [214] Elara’s motivation, to explore ethical storytelling, felt suddenly flimsy, a theory shattered by practice. [297] He would wield grotesque marionettes to tell satirical fables that skewered the powerful, his performances a beautiful chaos that left audiences unsettled and questioning. [363] Her perfect calligraphy could be ignored; his ragged puppets could not. [381]

[642] She gave it a face cut from the word "Liar," and another from the word "Truth." [732] The puppet, animated by the hand that once only crafted perfect lines, jerked to life.
