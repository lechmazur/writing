[52] The council said to rebuild as before, but Thea knew those old ways were the very precedents now broken. [68] She was hauntingly optimistic, a trait that unsettled her neighbors, for her smile held the ghost of all they had lost. [88] Her motivation was not to reconstruct, but to carve a path from ruin to reverence, to find a new way to honor the sea. [109]

[158] That night, under a bone-white moon, Thea began to sew. [194] She worked until exhaustion took her, and she slept on the cool sand. [207]

[237] Thea was not afraid, for she remembered the path she had sewn in her waking hours. [254] This was her method: via overlapping sleepwalking paths, her conscious practice guiding her unconscious journey.
