<story>The world after the Silencing was one of quiet purpose, and Elara was seasonally mute. [65] The village below depended on her; the bells did not tell time but tuned the very atmosphere, balancing the hum of the world. [88] Her motivation was pure and constant: to preserve the color of wonder in a graying age. [105]

Elara’s muteness was not a lack, but a different kind of listening. [118] She felt the discord through the soles of her boots, a wrongness vibrating up from the earth. [136] The great bells, which usually sang with a warm, bronze vitality, now emitted a thin, sour note. [156] Something fundamental was out of tune. [163] She climbed the spiral stairs to the bluff’s summit, the stone biting cold even through her thick gloves. [183] The air was thin, the sky a pale bowl holding the sun. [196] The village was a smudge of color far below the cloud line. [209]

[286] It was meant to glisten with a captured, eternal light, but now it was dull, frosted over with a strange, grey film. [363] This dying ice was their source, its unique crystalline structure filtering pure harmony from the chaos of sound. [384] To lose it was to lose wonder itself. [426] This was her method: understanding through kinetic sculpture, reading the push and pull of unseen forces. [448]

[673] A single, clear note hung in the air, so pure it felt like the first idea of sound. [693] The note spread, traveling down through the clouds, into the soil, through the roots of trees and the bones of the sleeping villagers. [718] The sourness was gone, replaced by a deep, fundamental chord that was the opposite of noise—it was the symphony of things as they were meant to be. [751] It was a truth of balance, of cold and warmth, of silence and song. [767]
