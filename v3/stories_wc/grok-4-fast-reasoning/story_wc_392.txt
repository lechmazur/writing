The death in question was that of Marcus Hale, a veteran maintenance worker who had slipped into a flooded shaft during the first deluge, his body recovered only after the water levels stabilized. [121]
Officially ruled an accident, but Elias's instincts—sharpened through empirical observation of patterns in fractures, tool placements, and witness hesitations—whispered of something preventable, perhaps a overlooked warning or a rushed repair. [165]
