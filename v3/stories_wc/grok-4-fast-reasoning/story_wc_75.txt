[18] The city, carved into living rock behind the cascade's veil, sheltered displaced persons—refugees from forgotten wars who had woven their histories into this damp sanctuary. [43] Elara, historically oriented to her core, curated exhibits that preserved their fading legacies, driven by a fierce motivation to rescue dying arts before they dissolved like mist. [81] Rosewater dread clung to her days, a subtle perfume of unease born from the knowledge that time eroded even the sturdiest traditions.
