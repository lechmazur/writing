The bloodhound called Scraps limped between towers of rusted drones, her nose still able to sift copper from iron even though the last resource war had ended five seasons ago. [35]
She had once trailed the scent of corpses across this cracked wasteland for the generals, but now she searched only for the living, driven by the wild hush that follows artillery and the deeper need to translate distance into closeness. [54]
Tonight the scrapyard settlement of Half-mast glimmered with cook-fire embers, and Scraps carried in her jaw an autumn leaf preserved in wax, a relic she had found wedged in the cockpit of a downed recon plane. [77]
The leaf’s crimson veins appeared black under the bruised sky, yet when moonlight struck the wax it offered tinted revelations, brief flashes of orchards that had existed before the soil turned to glass. [101]
A girl named Kite watched from a perch of twisted rebar, her eyes the same burnt orange as the leaf. [118]
Kite’s brother had vanished while scavenging the eastern flats, leaving only a phonograph cylinder wrapped in oilcloth, its grooves scored by a stylus that had cut words instead of music. [141]
Scraps smelled the cylinder before she saw it: the metallic breath of cobalt, the ghost of a voice. [158]
Kite slid down, landing barefoot beside the hound, and whispered, “He said the answer to where he went is inside, but it speaks only in riddle.” [181]
Scraps, retired from finding bodies, felt the old tug anyway, the intuitive descriptive tug that once let her map death across kilometers of scorched sand. [205]
