[136] It was a lost language stone, its surface etched with symbols that both intrigued and frightened her with their familiarity, as if she had known them in a life before this one. [164] The stone hummed with energy when she touched it, as if awakening from millennia of slumber, sending vibrations through her hand that matched the rhythm of her own heartbeat. [191]

The solution came through an unexpected method: via the texture of a feather. Maven decided to seed her newfound power, to plant determination in the soil of doubt that had grown within her during years of searching.
