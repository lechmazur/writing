[37] In the glass jar beside them rested a delicate moth wing, iridescent even in the soft cloudlight, a reminder of all the intricate patterns being erased by time and indifference. [39] They remembered stories their grandmother had told about the great migrations, how the trees would bend under the weight of so many resting butterflies, how the air would hum with their collective presence like a divine chorus.
