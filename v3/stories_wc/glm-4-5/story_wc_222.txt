[47] Chivalrous by nature, Kael had stolen these pieces from his master's collection not out of malice, but because he knew their secrets would be needed to amend whatever was happening to the sky. [73] Each piece contained a fragment of knowledge about the spire, and swapping them in the correct sequence at the correct locations would reveal the path forward. [95]

[119] The ice that encased the ancient structure glowed with an otherworldly blue light, reflecting the wrongness of the sky above—too green, too vast, too full of impossible constellations. [146] Inside the first chamber, Kael placed a knight piece on a pedestal, watching as the stone floor shifted, revealing a narrow staircase spiraling upward. [171] The air grew thinner, colder, as he ascended, each step bringing him closer to the pocket sundial lid he had journeyed to find. [195]

[251] The altar slid aside, uncovering a hidden passage that led to a library of books written in languages that had never been spoken. [278] In the center of the library lay the bishop's spot, where Kael placed the corresponding chess piece. [389]

[767] "But remember, apprentice of wonder, there will always be new mysteries to embrace, new unknowns to greet with open arms."
