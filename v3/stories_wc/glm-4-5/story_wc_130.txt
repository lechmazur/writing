Her motivation was singular: to understand the mathematics of emotion, to quantify the unquantifiable aspects of human feeling that had eluded scholars for centuries. [139]

Elara believed that by studying these patterns, she could dovetail the physical world with the emotional realm, creating a bridge between them. [251]
