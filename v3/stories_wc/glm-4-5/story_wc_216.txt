[15] After numbers changed meaning during the Great Reckoning, only those like her could still interpret the ancient symbols that once governed clocks and calendars. [32] Her motivation was simple yet profound: to understand the texture of time passing, not just measure its relentless march. [48] Each day, she would descend the spiral staircase of the silo, her fingers tracing the emerald-veined walls that seemed to pulse with temporal energy. [70]

[101] Elara had spent years studying the capsule, learning to navigate its fractured chronology. [117] Within its depths, she had found a single object: a cremation urn fragment, cool to the touch and humming with residual energy. [137] The fragment bore markings that predated the Reckoning, symbols she had been slowly decoding. [153]

[367] The more she learned, the more she felt the weight of her responsibility as a timekeeper. [386]
