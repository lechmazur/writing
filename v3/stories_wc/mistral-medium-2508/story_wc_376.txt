Elias, the city’s last gilded-leaf calligrapher, sat hunched over his desk, fingers trembling as he traced the veins of a half-finished letter—the ink bleeding where his breath had dampened the paper. [49]

He couldn’t lie. [52]

That was why the postal plate fragment had been slipped under his door. [91]

A jagged sliver of zinc, etched with coordinates half-scratched away, its edges sharp enough to draw blood when he turned it in the lamplight. [106]

He shouldn’t have pieced it together. [134]

Elias had spent his life crafting words that couldn’t wound, gilding truths until they gleamed with harmless beauty. [190]

Then, the Archive. [286]

A cavern vast as a cathedral, its ceiling strung with thousands of letters, their edges curling like dying petals. [298]
