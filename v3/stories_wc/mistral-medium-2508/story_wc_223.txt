The whispering gallery was a hollowed-out cathedral of forgotten kings, its arches humming with the ghosts of vows never kept. [16]

Liora traced the perimeter in slow, deliberate steps, her boots pressing into the dust in a pattern only she understood—*remember*, then *unbind*, then *tell*. [37]

Liora knelt, running her fingers over the empty squares. "They didn’t surrender," she murmured. "They *ascended*." [131]

The penumbra had no form, but it had hunger—a benevolently ruthless thing, devouring memory but preserving the shape of longing. [149]

She had learned to walk against it, her steps spelling words in the dust like incantations. *Stay.* *Witness.* [161]

The interregnum was the quiet between devourings, when the penumbra slept and the world held its breath. [175]
