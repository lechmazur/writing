A star, trapped in water. [253]

The boy knelt, his calloused fingers brushing the water’s skin. "He used to tell me their names," he whispered. [378]

The guardian tilted the mirror upward, letting the stars spill across the dock, across his hunched back, until he was wrapped in their borrowed light. [400]

For the first time, the tension in his shoulders eased. He didn’t need absolution. He needed to remember that darkness wasn’t the absence of stars—it was the space that let them shine. [428]

She lowered the mirror, her work done. The shutters stilled. [436]

The guardian stayed, watching the rain erase the patterns from the wood, the stars blur into the canal’s dark embrace. [476]

Somewhere, a shutter creaked. Somewhere, a secret unspooled. [484]

She waited, spiralingly calm, for the next fractured soul to find its way to the water’s edge. [498]

The dock held its breath. The stars held their light. [505]

And the canal, as always, held them both. [512]
