He didn’t consult it for hours; he consulted it for angles—for the tilt of memory, the slant of regret. [79]  
By the silence of morning radio—when static hums louder than voices, when the world holds its breath between night and news—he knew it was time. [104]

This was when truth surfaced—not shouted, not sold, but salvaged, piece by quiet piece, from the murk of misremembered things. [153]  
He had trained his instincts for decades: not to hunt, but to listen; not to chase, but to wait until the past revealed its shape like a wreck breaching low tide. [184]  
His mentor, long gone, had called it “serene upheaval”—the calm that precedes the rearrangement of everything you thought you knew. [208]  
The seeker knelt, letting his fingers brush the canal’s surface—not to retrieve, but to feel the temperature of forgotten time. [230]  
Beneath him, something shifted: not fish, not current, but intention—a glint, half-buried in silt, catching the last violet light. [253]

Instead, he opened the sundial, angling it so the fading sky cast one last, thin shadow across its face—a line pointing not to noon, but to truth. [309]  
The glint below responded—not magic, not fate, but physics and patience aligning as the water stilled just enough to mirror the dial’s arc. [337]  
He smiled, thoughtfully clear, as realization settled: the lies weren’t built to deceive others, but to shelter the liars—from shame, from consequence, from the unbearable weight of being known. [375]

Behind him, the canal returned to its murmur, cradling secrets it no longer needed to keep. [702]
