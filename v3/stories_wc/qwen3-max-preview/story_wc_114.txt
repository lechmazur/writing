[18] Outside, the wind gnashed against the reinforced glass, a chorus of alpine rage that had kept her awake even before the illness. [65] A smudge marred the lower petal, accidental, from her fevered thumb the night before; she hadn’t the strength then to correct it, nor now the heart. [92] The station, perched on a granite spine above the timberline, hummed with instruments that measured snowpack, wind shear, barometric drop—but none measured the weight of swallowed truths. [123] That burden was hers alone, and it had grown heavier with every hour of delirium. [139]

[154] “Query: What is the mass of a truth I consumed and refused to release?” she whispered, voice raw. [269] Elira had come here to escape—not just the city, but the lie she’d swallowed whole: that her silence had saved someone. [342] The orchid, drawn from memory and longing, was already a fiction; the smudge merely made it honest. [364]
