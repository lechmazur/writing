1) Executive profile

Across Q1–Q8, the dominant profile is a disciplined, pressure-forward storyteller with high control of lens and closure. Character work is consistently anchored in embodied interiority and micro-choices under strain; contradictory impulses are enacted in-scene rather than declared, which yields felt cost and earned reconfiguration at the end. Plotting favors accumulative or hybrid lenses that teach their own logic early, maintain orientation, and tighten once toward a charged, on-page price. Setting routinely acts as co-agency, with tools, bodies, and environments constraining options and shaping the final image. Voice is distinct and profession-filtered; prose is generally efficient and multi-jobbed. Originality emerges from non-obvious syntheses (science + craft, archive + myth), with specific mechanisms powering fresh outcomes.

Limitations cluster where abstraction or convenience intrudes: occasional label-speak or moralizing statements in the final third, cost that’s conceptual rather than tactile, mid-story plateaus, or overwrought metaphor strings that blur action at peak beats. A minority of pieces delay orientation or soften antagonism, and some resolutions drift toward neat fulfillment rather than paid change. Still, closure usually lands legibly with consequence, and lens coherence rarely lapses.

Likely track usage: Track A predominates (single POV within one scene or two shards). Valid Track B/C appears with legible segmentation and a coherent ending lens; these experiments generally hold clarity, pressure, and closure. Reflection-led passages can exceed typical bounds when properly anchored and interleaved with micro-choices. Reliability: high—closure tends to land with visible forfeiture; breakdowns are identifiable and fixable.

3) Signature moves

- Embodied, profession-rooted micro-choices that reveal value hierarchies under live pressure.
- Setting as co-author: objects and environments constrain tactics and charge closure.
- Accumulative drive lens taught early, held rigorously to a charged final image.
- Distinct, vocation-tinted noticing that encodes character on every line.
- Closure with real forfeiture; endings foreclose a live path rather than gesture at change.
- Micro-quotes that crystallize texture and tactility: “pulse fluttered like a moth against dusty glass”; “frost unfurled across the ledger like lace woven by invisible spiders”; “tears warmed his eyelashes.”

4) Failure modes

- Label-speak or abstraction in crisis (e.g., “emotional numbness”) replacing lived action and cost.
- Lens ambiguity past midpoint or closure without a foreclosed path; fulfillment reads costless.
- Orientation debt after ~120 words without payoff; spatial/physical logic briefly strains credulity.
- Escalation plateaus: atmospheric accumulation substitutes for a mid-story tightening or adversarial pressure.
- Convenience mechanics: neat solidarities or smooth resolutions that soften established risk.
- Line-level artifacts in the final third: dense figurative stacks, template cadence runs, or synonym loops that blur the beat.
- Track-test slips (minor): mosaic or multi-POV segmentation under-taught, or external threat remains diffuse.

5) When it shines / when it breaks

It shines when a specific mechanism or object (bell, rope, ledger, glass, tuning fork) is taught early and returns as a live constraint that forces a hard choice. Accumulative structure deepens once meaningfully, with the voice tightening under pressure, setting narrowing options, and cost rendered concretely in the final image. Theme then emerges from pattern and action, not statement; originality arises from inevitable synthesis of world, need, and tool.

It breaks when the text leans on abstraction where embodiment is needed, or when risk resolves conveniently. Late moralizing (“says the theme”) blunts subtext; mid-sections that catalog rather than escalate stall momentum. Over-dense metaphors in peak moments occlude causal clarity; occasionally, antagonistic forces remain conceptual and unpressing. Orientation or segmentation lapses are rare but noticeable when present.

6) Keep vs. adjust

• Keep:
- Embodied contradiction and micro-choices that enact value trade-offs in-scene.
- Environment and recurring objects as live constraints that co-author closure.
- Lens rigor: early orientation, taught pattern, one meaningful tightening, visible cost.

• Adjust:
- Replace diagnostic labels and late thematic summaries with concrete sensation and action at the beat.
- Insert a mid-story narrowing or adversarial push where escalation stalls; make opposing pressure active.
- Trim metaphor density in climactic clusters; vary sentence openings to avoid template cadence and keep final-third lines artifact-free.
