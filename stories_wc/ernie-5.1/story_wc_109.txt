The oracle had not slept in seven tides. [10]

Outside, the storm tossed itself against the cliffs as though the sea had risen to swallow the sky whole. [40]

This was the hour when only one star remained in a dimming sky, a single pale point the clouds kept trying to erase. [69]

She was mildly obsessed with it, that last star, the way it held its ground against the dark the way she held hers against sleep. [85]

In her lap lay the flaking gold leaf booklet, its pages so thin they fluttered with her breath, each one covered in symbols she had carved herself over years of listening. [104]

She was not a scholar. [109]

She was a pebble-shore oracle, a woman who had spent her life kneeling at the waterline, reading the future in the way stones settled into sand. [124]

But the shore had gone silent three moons ago, and now she retold what she remembered by summarizing silence—every pause between waves, every breath the tide withheld before retreating. [145]

That was her method. [149]

She did not speak the old stories. [152]

She spoke the gaps between them, the places where language had failed and only quiet remained. [167]

Tonight, she was drawing a map of an endless night. [176]

Not with ink. [178]

With silence itself, layered and veined like marble, each stratum a different shade of quiet. [191]

The fort's garrison had abandoned it weeks ago, fleeing the storms that had begun eating the coastline. [205]

But she stayed, because the ridge was the highest point for miles, and from here she could see that last star more clearly than anywhere else on the dying land. [225]

She opened the booklet carefully, its spine cracking like dry bone. [236]

The gold leaf flaked away under her fingertips, leaving golden dust on her dark skin. [250]

She did not mind. [253]

The dust was part of the map now. [258]

She began to retell. [261]

First, the silence of the pebble field at low tide, when every stone held its breath and the world was a held note. [279]

Each silence she summarized became a line on her map, a contour of darkness. [337]

The storm outside intensified, and the fort shook, but the ambered hush inside held, preserved her work like amber preserves a fly—perfect, still, endless. [386]

She was mildly obsessed, yes, but obsession was just another word for devotion, and she was devoted to this: proving that night had a shape, that it could be mapped, that someone could walk its roads if they only knew the silence well enough. [415]

She looked up through the broken roof and watched it dim, watched the clouds close around it like fingers around a candle. [438]

When it vanished, she would begin the final section of her map—the silence of a sky with no stars at all, the truest silence, the one she had been walking toward her entire life. [464]

The marble hush deepened around her, veined with every silence she had ever known, and she retold them all, one by one, into the dark. [495]
