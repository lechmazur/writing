The garden had no edges, only the slow approximation of infinity where light bent around clusters of dying stars and new ones whispered into being, each bloom a nebula unfurling in colors that had no names in any language still spoken. [41]
Maren drifted through it all, her translucent hands cupping a single brass gear from a calculating machine that had belonged to her grandmother, and her grandmother's grandmother, and every woman in the line before them, each one adding her own wear to the teeth until the metal sang with accumulated history. [92]
The gear was warm. [96]
It was always warm, though nothing here burned, because burning was precisely what they had left behind. [113]
That was the point—to share light without using fire, to carry illumination through something gentler, something that wouldn't consume what it touched, something that could be passed hand to hand across the quiet sigh of falling petals without scorching the fingers that received it. [158]
The petals themselves were ancient, each one a compressed fragment of stellar matter that would take centuries to dissolve, and as they drifted downward in their endless, patient descent, Maren listened. [189]
She sonar pinged the dark between the nebulas, sending out pulses of sound that returned shaped like memories: her mother's voice humming a frequency only daughters could hear, her great-aunt's laugh echoing across light-years, the particular hum of the old calculating machine as it worked through orbits no one had asked for, generating numbers that meant nothing except that someone, somewhere, had once cared enough to calculate them. [257]
The generational patterns were not chosen. [263]
They were inherited, like the gear, like the garden itself, like the need to move through darkness without leaving a scar, like the compulsion to transit from one burning star to the next, always carrying the light forward, never letting it ignite. [305]
Her ancestors had all made the same journey—from the incandescent cores of their original stars to this quiet place where they could tend light instead of wielding it, where illumination meant something softer than destruction. [341]
Each one had carried the gear forward, each one had learned to sonar ping the void and read the echoes as a map of who they were becoming, and who they were leaving behind. [375]
Maren understood now why the serenity here felt troubling. [384]
It was not peace. [388]
It was the peace of things that had stopped trying to change, of patterns so deep they had become geological, of love so repeated it had calcified into something beautiful and terrible. [420]
The petals fell and fell and fell, and the garden remained exactly as it was, approximating infinity by simply never ending, never beginning, just continuing—a loop with no exit, a song with no final note. [456]
