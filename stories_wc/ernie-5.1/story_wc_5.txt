The orchard had forgotten how to grow, but it remembered how to hold light, and that was enough. [17]
Deep in the subarctic crater, where the wind moved like something grieving, Maren knelt among the withered trees and waited. [37]
She was a flash-paper alchemist, which meant everything she touched wanted to burn, and everything she made had to be caught before it vanished. [61]
This was the work of patience, not the quiet kind, but the kind that lived in the body, in the muscles, in the slow exhale that kept her hands from trembling. [92]
The world held its breath. [97]

It had once held an image of a garden that never existed, a place where fruit hung heavy and the sky was always warm. [162]
Now it would hold something truer. [168]
From her satchel she drew the starlight she had safekept for eleven years. [181]

Now she needed to seal it into the plate so it would last long enough to become a map. [251]

The word stay at the center, because that was what every heartbeat was really saying—stay, stay, stay. [411]
