<story>Maren had not smelled rain in eleven months. [53] She needed to feel something other than the flat, sterile nothing where scent used to live. [124] "That belonged to my mother," he said, and his voice carried the same trembling calm that filled the fog outside. [592] She was an aromatherapist who could no longer smell, and she had just found the most fragrant thing in the room: an ordinary miracle, waiting to be humanized by hands that knew how to listen.
