[38] Maren stood at its edge, infinitely patient, as she always was when the world agreed to be still. [233] Each knot was a syllable, each cluster a sentence, and the weavers had hidden their final instructions in this very rope, passed hand to hand through generations of men who never knew what they carried. [307] To preserve the method, not the object, because objects broke and ropes rotted, but the way of decoding endured only in the hands of someone willing to be infinitely patient. [539] She had learned long ago that the weavers never revealed themselves all at once; they gave their knowledge in pieces, scattered across time and material, trusting that the patient would find what the impatient could not.
