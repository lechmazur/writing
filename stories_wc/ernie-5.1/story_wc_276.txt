[72] Her quarry swam in frequencies too high for any net, too old for any hook, too sacred for any hand that trembled. [92] She wanted the echo of a goddess—the last vibration of a name the world had forgotten how to speak, a sound so pure it could unravel the silence that had swallowed the sky. [138] Orion dragged his belt through the cracks between stars, constellations stumbling like drunk children, their ancient light bending where it should have been straight, painting the darkness with crooked, wandering paths. [244] She had learned long ago, in the first years of her fishing, that the cool head was the only head that survived in a place like this. [270] She drew the rail spike from her belt. [278] Its iron edge was dull and ancient, carved with marks that meant nothing in any living language but everything in the language of absence. [302] This was her tool, not for killing but for listening, not for piercing but for opening. [320] She pressed its tip against the nearest stalk and felt the garden shudder, felt the sound waves recoil like startled animals. [564] The echo of the goddess. [640] The constellation above her shuddered, and for one breath, just one, the stars held still in their sleepwalking, as if even they remembered what it meant to be heard.
