[128] Tonight, a woman approached with a ticket bearing a doodle Maren couldn't immediately parse—a circle with a crack running through it, and something that might have been a hand reaching in or pulling away. [162] Maren stamped it without comment, but her eyes lingered. [169] This was how she eavesdropped: not by listening but by reading the small, coded confessions people left on paper they thought no one was paying attention to. [197] The forecourt's mistrust had settled like sediment over months—people came, exchanged their doodled tickets, and left without looking at each other, each one guarding something behind their silence. [231] Maren understood the architecture of that silence because she lived inside it.
