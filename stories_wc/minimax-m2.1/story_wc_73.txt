[168] What kept her moving was not hope of escape but something more fundamental: the need to understand. [306] The dice had become her instruments of measurement, her way of testing whether the laws of probability held in this impossible space. [366]

[434] The numbers that emerged seemed to form a pattern, a code hidden in the randomness that she had been slowly decoding with each throw. [497] The chipped die landed on calculations she had been pursuing for months, as if the damage to its surface had aligned it with some deeper truth about the structure she inhabited. [715] She was trying to capture the texture of a missed opportunity, the feeling of almost understanding something crucial before it slipped away. She had identified the core concept of perpetual motion early in her imprisonment. [897] The staircase moved without moving, existed without existing in any fixed way, occupied every point and no point simultaneously. [960] Her latest calculations suggested she had been approaching the problem from the wrong direction entirely. [1014] The rupture she had been seeking was not a physical break in the structure but a conceptual one, a flaw in the mathematical proof that held the impossibility together. [1151] When she examined it again in this new light, she saw what she had missed: the formula wasn't wrong, she had simply been applying it to the wrong variable. [1373] The mathematical proofs had led her to a conclusion both simpler and more complex than she had imagined. [1435] The staircase was not a trap constructed to contain her but a structure that responded to perception, existing in a state of quantum-like potential until observed with sufficient clarity. [1513] She had been trying to rupture the walls of her prison when she should have been trying to rupture her understanding of what the prison actually was.
