[218] Sera had noticed the pattern first in her sketches—how the sounds around her would stop mid-note, mid-breath, leaving only a suspended emptiness that seemed to hold its own weight. [280] The interrupted cadence troubled her deeply, not merely for its strangeness but for what it suggested: something was stealing the city's rhythm, cage by cage, and no one else seemed to hear it. [1097] She took a moment from the giant's nap, then another, each stolen fragment allowing her to reach further into the trapped cadence and gently, persistently uncage its components.
