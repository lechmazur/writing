[51] When reports arrived of strange humming sounds emanating from an abandoned observatory on the northern cliffs, she packed her equipment with the same methodical precision she applied to every investigation, expecting another straightforward explanation. The observatory stood against a bruised twilight sky, its copper dome pitted with rust and its windows dark as dead eyes. [166] Elena had visited dozens of such places during her career, structures where wonder went to decay, and she felt the familiar mixture of melancholy and purpose that came with demystification. [229] There was something rather beautiful, she often thought, about explaining away magic—the way it revealed the equally marvelous machinery of the natural world beneath the theatrical trappings of the supernatural. [293] This gloomy cheerfulness had sustained her through years of disappointments, each busted myth leaving her certain that reality, properly understood, was more extraordinary than any fiction. [468] Its body had been carved with evident skill, but the neck remained unattached, strings hanging loose like abandoned thoughts. Elena approached the instrument with professional curiosity, expecting nothing more than an abandoned project left behind by some eccentric craftsman. [813] The words were muffled, but the emotional weight was unmistakable: regret, longing, and an odd sense of unfinished business. [872] When the hum faded, Elena was left trembling, the painted seashell warm in her hand where she had not consciously reached for it. [928]
