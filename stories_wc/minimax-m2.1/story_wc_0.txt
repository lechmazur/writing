The scientific mind she had trained to preprocess reality into neat equations and predictable trajectories struggled to categorize the small, damp creature trembling against her finger. [141]
She had always preferred the dramatic clarity of supernovae, the elegant violence of neutron stars consuming companions until the inevitable explosion, where endings came swift and definitive. [170]
The butterfly's dust - those infinitesimal scales that gave color to flight - began to transfer to her skin as its wings failed one last time, releasing a golden fingerprint that would linger for hours. [199]
Mira understood, in that moment, that she was witnessing a different kind of ending entirely, one that happened through accumulated dust patterns rather than explosive disintegration. [229]
The decay was so gradual she could not pinpoint its origin, could not draw a line between living and dying, could not reduce it to a single calculation that explained everything. [263]
She thought of all the needless drama she had extracted from her data over the years, the way she had framed stellar deaths as cosmic battles, as warfare between matter and gravity, as if the universe required human narrative to be meaningful. [300]
Perhaps her aunt had understood something she never had, spending fifty years in this greenhouse, watching plants grow and die in their small cycles, never demanding that nature perform for her intellectual satisfaction. [336]
The painted lady released its final dust as its legs curled inward, and Mira noticed how the golden particles had settled into the grooves of her thumb in a pattern that reminded her of the light curves she had spent her career analyzing. [372]
But this pattern would not predict an explosion or a collapse; it would simply disperse with the next rain, leave no record, matter to no one but herself in this particular moment. [405]
She found this strangely liberating instead of nihilistic, as if the smallness of the butterfly's ending released her from the burden of making every observation significant. [435]
When she returned to the greenhouse three days later to help clear the debris, she discovered that the dust pattern had spread, carried by condensation and air currents, into a shape that seemed almost intentional on the surviving leaves. [474]

Mira sat down on an overturned pot, her scientific training telling her one thing while her heart - that unreliable organ she had tried to train out of her research - whispered something gentler about endings and scales and what it meant to witness properly. [553]
She had come to strip needless drama from her life, to reduce everything to its essential processes, and instead the universe had delivered her a greenhouse full of small, undramatic deaths that somehow contained multitudes. [598]
In the end, she understood, both the supernova and the butterfly were simply ways that matter rearranged itself over time, neither more meaningful than the other, each beautiful in its own timescale and its own humility. [658]
