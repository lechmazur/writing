[31] The structures stretched upward in impossible formations, their corrugated walls forming corridors that shifted with the tides, and within those corridors, the nameless one collected fragments of overheard stories like shells gathered from a shore that never remained still. [56] This was how memory could be approached—not through documents or declarations, but through the quiet detritus of human speech that accumulated in corners and near doorways where workers paused to speak. [139] The officials had confused names, or dates, or perhaps they had simply needed someone to mark gone, and the nameless one had been convenient in their anonymity.
