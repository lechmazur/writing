[25] The amber brooch pressed cold against her chest, its warm golden surface the only warmth left in a world that had forgotten how to hold heat. [51] She followed the rain trails down the eastern ridge, where water had carved temporary rivers through the cracked earth, each trickle a miracle, each pool a congregation. [91]

[225] At low tide, a causeway of black stone connected it to the mainland, and Mira's bare feet found the worn path by memory alone, her eyes too busy drinking in the transformations that water had wrought. [270] The waters had risen in places, swallowed whole communities, but here they had retreated, revealing what the sea had kept hidden for decades: stone pillars rising from the tidal flats, ancient and patient, waiting to be found again.
