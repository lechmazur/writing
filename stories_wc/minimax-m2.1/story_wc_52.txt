[418] He had been here and gone, as all things are, but she had refused to let him become merely absent. [615] She had never asked him to stop performing, never demanded he choose between the roar and the domestic. [1034] Outside, the rain continued its patient conversation with the glass, and inside, the shavings continued their patient conversation with the floor. [1090] She was harvesting lessons from loss, one curl of ash wood at a time, and the lessons were these: that love does not disappear but transforms, that wild things can be contained without being tamed, that here and gone are not opposites but a single breath, held and released. [1275] It would contain him now—not imprisoned, not gone, but held.
