[121] That was seven harvests ago, and I had heard nothing since—not a signal, not a message, not even a rumor of his survival. [136] The silence after war could be louder than the bombs themselves, a wild hush that settled over the cracked earth and refused to break. [149]

[338] The riddle of the cylinder resolved itself not as a puzzle to solve but as a door that opened when I stopped trying to force it. [387] He had left to translate the distance between us into something we could cross, had searched for years for a way to send word across the wasteland that claimed so many. [403] The cylinder was his answer, buried where I would find it when I was ready to hear it, preserved in wax like the autumn leaf as proof that beauty could survive the fire.
