[165] Yara knelt on the frost-laced floor before the ceremonial rope—a twisted thing of hemp and silver thread that had bound the sacred scrolls for three hundred years, now fallen into disuse when the last scroll was transcribed and the last voice fell silent. [561] She had found it among the ruins of the lower chambers, water-damaged but still legible—the ghost of a song that had been sung in this very space, a lullaby that mothers once hummed while their children slept, a remembrance of names spoken before the children could speak them. [944] She was not destroying the rope's history but extending it, adding her own thread to a story that had been waiting for someone to continue it.
