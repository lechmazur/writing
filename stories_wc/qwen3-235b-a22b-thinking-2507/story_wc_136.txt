[155] There, in the fractured stone, the cracks formed deliberate arrows pointing east toward the derelict railway district. [245] Unlike ordinary punches, this one left no physical hole—only a shimmering, temporary mark visible only to those who knew how to look. [282] Elias pressed it into the first arrow-shaped crack, and the stone glowed faintly blue before fading. [340] Each arrow led deeper into districts erased from official maps, where laundry lines sagged like spiderwebs and shadows clung thick as tar. [992] Elias emerged not with a map, but with a new route forward—one paved with honesty. [1021] He walked home as the city stirred, the unspoken weight lifted, the fortress now part of him.
