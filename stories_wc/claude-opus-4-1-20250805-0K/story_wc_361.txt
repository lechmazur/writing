[21] She had been a relic courier for seven years now, but her hands still shook whenever she carried something this old, this significant. [94] This was where couriers came to test their relics, where the boundary between the physical and the ephemeral grew thin. [161] The delicate wings, preserved in crystal resin for countless centuries, trembled as if remembering flight. [177] This was what Corvin had taught her before his absence: that relics weren't dead things but seeds waiting for the right conditions to bloom. [202]

"Every artifact extends brief encounters," he'd told her once, his weathered hands steady where hers jittered. [218] "A potter's thumbprint in clay lets you shake hands across millennia. [229] A soldier's worn medallion carries the weight of their fears. [240] Our job isn't just to deliver these objects—it's to coax wonder from the routine act of preservation." [258]
