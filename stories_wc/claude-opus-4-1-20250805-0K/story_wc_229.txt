[20] Assistant keeper Mara pressed her palm against the cool stone, feeling the structure's ancient pulse beneath her fingertips. [37] The heat wave had finally broken in the night, leaving the air crisp with salt and possibility. [54]

She uncorked the vial with trembling fingers, releasing the scent of night-blooming jasmine that shouldn't exist here, miles from any garden. [76] But it did exist, carried on vibrations of intent so pure they could manifest memory into molecules. [93] The cork rolled across her palm, warm as a living thing. [104]

"Please," she whispered to the empty keeper's quarters above. [113] "Come back to me." [117]

For three years, she'd tended this lighthouse alone, ever since Thomas had dissolved into the morning mist, leaving only his intentions behind. [140] The townspeople said grief had driven her mad, that she spoke to shadows and kept vigil for a ghost. [159] They didn't understand that love could purify intentions until they became tangible, that longing could beckon across dimensions. [178]

[220] Reality had grown thin, and she'd seen him — Thomas, caught between worlds, trapped by his own guilt over the accident that had taken him. [246] His intention to protect her had become a prison, his love a chain that kept him suspended in the in-between. [266]

[287] She climbed the spiral stairs, each step a prayer, the jasmine scent growing stronger as she ascended. [304] At the lamp room, she found him as she always did at dawn — a shimmer in the air, a distortion of light that hurt to look at directly. [334]

"Thomas," she said, her voice breaking with screaming tenderness, that particular ache of loving someone you cannot touch. [352] "You don't have to protect me anymore." [359]

The shimmer wavered, and she felt his confusion like static electricity against her skin. [373] She held up the cork, such a simple thing, but it had sealed away her garden's scent for months while she'd learned to purify her intentions, to strip away anger and desperation until only love remained.
