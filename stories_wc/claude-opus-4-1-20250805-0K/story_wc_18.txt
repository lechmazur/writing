[24] Elara worked by touch more than sight, her fingers reading the curve of glass the way others might read braille. [43] She had learned this trade from her grandmother, who claimed mirrors weren't just reflections but windows between what is and what could be. [66]

[256] She walked to her collection of salvaged mirrors from the district's abandoned houses, each one bearing the patina of neglect and time. [279]
