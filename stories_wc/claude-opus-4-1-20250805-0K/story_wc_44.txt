[48] The world still reeled from sealed awakenings, those moments when ordinary objects briefly remembered they were something more. [65]

[106] Now, atop this floating isle tethered to nothing but starlight, she hoped the observatory's ancient telescopes might reveal where those shadows went when the sun reached its zenith. [135]

Lyra inserted the key with the same quietly deliberate precision she applied to every rune she traced. [257] The automaton stirred, but something was wrong—its movements were reversed, writing backwards across parchment, unsealing rather than recording. [276] She watched it relearn its purpose in reverse, each line erasing knowledge rather than preserving it. [293]
