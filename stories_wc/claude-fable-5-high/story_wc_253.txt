After the happiness mandate, grief became a licensing issue, and the Bureau sent performers to manage it. [17] Casia was one of them, a blank-faced performer trained to fill silence before silence could mean anything. [34] Her face had stopped producing expressions years ago, which the Bureau counted as professional neutrality. [49] The assignment was a farmhouse overshadowed by giant wind turbines, their blades turning with a patience nothing human had. [68] A widow named Ottoline lived there, flagged for insufficient joy after her husband's death. [82] Casia's job was remediation: thirty days of mandated lightness, then a report that decided everything. [97] The turbines made a low hum that soaked the fields, so constant it functioned as a kind of quiet. [116] On the kitchen table sat a dried flower bouquet, brittle and brown, the only unregulated object in the house. [135] "Mourning artifact," Casia said, reading from her checklist. [143] "Wedding artifact," Ottoline said, and did not move it. [152] Casia wrote nothing down, which was the first thing she did wrong. [164] The technique she had been issued was dramatic narration of mundane tasks. [176] "Behold," she announced over the breakfast dishes, "the heroic plate, scoured of its terrible egg." [191] Her voice carried theatrical weight while her face carried nothing at all. [203] The Bureau believed grand language could crowd grief out of a room. [215] Ottoline listened the way fields listen to weather, without objection or interest. [227] In the evenings the widow sat on the porch and whittled, pulling a small knife through pale wood. [245] She was making her husband's hands, she explained, one knuckle at a time, because she was forgetting them. [263] This was the kind of statement Casia was required to report. [274] Instead she narrated it: "Observe the sculptor, liberating a hand from a block of stubborn pine." [290] Ottoline almost smiled, which Casia recorded as compliance, which was not exactly a lie. [304] Casia understood that she occupied morally ambiguous ground, and she made no effort to leave it. [320] She took the Bureau's money to erase grief; she stayed in the farmhouse to do something quieter. [337] Her own daughter had died the spring the mandate passed, and Casia had been forbidden to feel it. [355] The feeling had gone somewhere; her face had gone with it. [366] What remained was a theory: that grief could not be deleted, only carried lightly, the way wind carries seed. [385] She wanted to cultivate lightness in grief, hers and Ottoline's both, under the Bureau's nose, using the Bureau's tools. [404] So the narration changed, by degrees nobody could prosecute. [413] "Behold the bouquet," she said one morning, dusting around it, "which refuses to die further, having finished the job already." [433] Ottoline laughed once, a dry sound, and then looked frightened of herself. [445] "That counts as happiness," Casia said flatly, "I checked the statute." [456] On the nineteenth day the turbines stopped for maintenance, all of them at once. [470] The hum that had stood in for silence collapsed, and underneath it was the real thing. [486] A remade hush settled over the farmhouse, enormous and original, the silence the mandate had been built to abolish. [505] Ottoline stood in the yard and began to shake. [514] Casia's training said: narrate, loudly, immediately, fill the gap before grief does. [526] Casia said nothing. [529] She brought the whittled hands out to the porch and set them in Ottoline's lap, and the two women sat inside the hush as if it were a room they had rented. [561] Ottoline wept without apology; Casia did not weep, having nothing where weeping starts, but she stayed. [577] When the blades resumed at dusk, the hum returned, but it sounded different now, like something covering for them. [596] Casia filed her report on the thirtieth day. [604] "Subject demonstrates regulation-compliant affect," she wrote, "including spontaneous laughter," and she did not mention the bouquet, the hands, or the hour of silence. [627] It was a true report and a false one, and she signed it without expression, which was the only way she signed anything. [650] At the gate, Ottoline pressed something into her hand: a small whittled bird, rough but light. [666] "For your daughter," Ottoline said, though Casia had never spoken of her. [678] Grief recognizes grief, apparently, even through a face that gives nothing back. [690] Casia walked out under the turbines, narrating to no one: "Behold the operative, departing, carrying contraband lightness." [707] Her face did not change. [712] But the bird in her pocket weighed almost nothing, and she noticed the almost, and the noticing was the closest thing to feeling the mandate had left her. [740] Behind her, the hush waited under the hum, remade, patient, hers whenever she chose to stop and hear it. [759]
