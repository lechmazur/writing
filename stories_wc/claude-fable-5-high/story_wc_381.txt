<story>
Mirei had been a summoner of silences long before anyone thought silence could be summoned. [15] She lived alone in a nocturnal haven beside a quiet brook, a hollow of mossed stone where the water spoke only in undertones. [38] Other summoners called fire or storm; she called the spaces between sounds, and the spaces obeyed. [54] It was lonely work, which suited her, because what she guarded could not be shared. [69]

Seven years ago a dying oracle had spoken a prophecy in code, a sequence of clicks and held breaths that, once decoded by any living mind, would begin to come true. [100] The prophecy promised a burning of the valley, framed as deliverance. [111] Mirei had heard it whole and refused to understand it, holding the sounds in her memory like sealed letters. [130] But the code was leaking. [135] Lately, amid whispered secrets traded in the market towns downstream, fragments of the oracle's clicks had begun surfacing, passed mouth to ear by people who did not know what they carried. [166] Each whisper carried the code a little closer to assembly. [176] Tonight she would hush it for good. [183]

Her method was old and exacting: she would hush the prophecy by mapping silence itself. [198] Every quiet in the world had a shape, she had learned, the way every sound has a wave. [216] She knelt by the brook with a lantern turned low and unfolded her chart, a sheet of rice paper marked with the silences she had collected over seven years. [245] The pause between an owl's two calls. [252] The held breath of a child hiding. [259] The stillness inside a bell after its ring has died. [269] Plotted together, the silences formed a pattern that was terrestrially cosmic: rooted in mud and reedbeds, yet matching, point for point, the dark intervals between stars. [295] The earth's hush and the sky's hush were one fabric, and she could fold it. [310]

Folding was the summoning. [314] From her sleeve she drew a crane's origami wing, the last surviving piece of a paper bird her teacher had folded from the map's first draft. [340] The rest of the crane had burned the night the oracle spoke; only the wing had flown clear of the fire. [361] Its creases held the original folding sequence, the grammar of how silence bends. [374] Without it, her map was only ink. [381]

She laid the wing on the chart and began to fold, matching crease to constellation, valley fold to valley, mountain fold to the ridgeline above the brook. [408] The lantern's small flame steadied into a tranquil blaze, burning without flicker, as if even fire had agreed to listen. [428] Quiet began to flow. [432] It moved like the brook beside her, but in reverse, running upstream from the paper into the world. [450] She felt it pass through the reeds and out across the valley, finding every mouth that held a fragment of the code. [472] It did not steal their voices. [478] It only smoothed the fragments, softening clicks into ordinary pauses, turning held breaths into simple rest, until the code lost its edges and could never again be assembled into meaning. [508]

But the prophecy fought. [512] The fragments she still carried in her own memory woke and pressed against her teeth, demanding to be spoken, decoded, fulfilled. [533] Her lips parted against her will, and the first click formed at the back of her throat. [550] She understood then why the work demanded a solitary summoner: anyone beside her would have heard, and hearing would have been enough. [572] There was one fold left, and only one surface left to fold. [584] She lifted the crane's wing to her lips and creased it shut around her own breath. [600]

The click dissolved before it could sound. [607] The smoothed hush settled over the hollow like a hand laid gently on a drum. [622] In her memory, the prophecy's code unraveled into plain quiet, the way a knot becomes rope again. [639] When it was done, the wing lay open on the chart, its creases blank, its work spent. [656] Mirei breathed out slowly and heard nothing dangerous in the exhale, only breath. [669]

Downstream, she knew, gossips would go on trading whispered secrets, never noticing that one secret had quietly left them. [688] The valley would not burn. [693] The lantern still held its tranquil blaze, and the brook resumed its undertone, and the stars kept their dark intervals exactly where her map had promised they would be. [722] She folded the blank wing one last time, not for power, just for the shape of a bird, and set it on the water. [746] It flowed downstream, white against the dark, carrying nothing at all. [757] For the first time in seven years, the solitary summoner sat in silence she had not made, and found it enough. [778]
