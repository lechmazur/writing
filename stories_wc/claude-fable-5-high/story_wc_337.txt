Mara restored broken masks for the drowned, and the village paid her in silence. [14] Each funeral mask that shattered loosed a spirit onto the tideline, and only a whole face could call it home. [34] She worked at night in the prism sanctuary, a chapel of salvaged glass on the headland, where starlight came apart into slow colors across the floor. [60] The glass had been ground from bottles the sea returned, and the dead were said to trust light that had been broken and reassembled, like them. [86] Tonight the spirits would not settle. [92] They pressed against the panes like weather, restless, lucid, refusing every mask she offered. [106] Mara understood why when old Teodor climbed the headland carrying his grandfather's net. [119] "It accumulates silence," he said, laying the centuries-old mesh across her workbench. [131] "Every knot my family tied over a drowned man's name holds the words we never spoke about him." [149] She touched the net and felt it: an interlaced hush, generations of withheld grief knotted into hemp, each crossing a sentence swallowed instead of said. [174] No wonder the masks failed. [179] A mask gives a spirit a face, but a face means nothing to the unnamed. [194] "They cannot rest because the truth of their deaths was buried in this weave," she said. [210] "Then we must exhume it," Teodor answered, "knot by knot, and I will tell you what each one hides, if you will hold what I tell you." [237] Mara took up her tapestry needle, the blunt steel she used to stitch leather straps into mask backs, and slid its eye into the first knot. [263] A finer point would have cut the fibers and killed whatever they held; only the needle's patient bluntness could loosen without wounding. [285] The knot gave. [288] "Anders," Teodor whispered. [291] "He did not fall; my grandfather cut him loose to save the boat in the squall of 1761." [309] The hush around them thinned by one voice. [317] Outside the prisms, a shape that had paced the glass for years went still, listening. [332] They worked through the night that way, secret by secret, the unraveling and the telling braided into one act. [351] Each confession Teodor gave her loosened his hands as the needle loosened the hemp, until Mara could not tell which unknotting drove the other. [375] Stolen catch shares. [378] A brother let go too soon. [384] A widow lied to, kindly, for forty years. [392] The starlight, split by the prisms, laid bands of violet and amber across the slackening net, as if grading each freed strand by color. [416] But unrest sharpened as they neared the net's heart. [425] One knot remained, doubled and tarred, older than the rest, and Teodor's mouth closed around it like the knot itself. [445] "Whose?" Mara asked. [448] "Yours," he said. [451] "Your father did not drown trading up the coast. [460] He drowned here, hauling my son out of the breakers, and we tied his name into the net because we could not face you with it." [486] The sanctuary went very quiet, but it was a different quiet now, not the hoarded kind. [502] Mara understood, at last, why every mask she had made for her father's spirit had cracked in her hands before dawn. [523] She had been restoring faces over a lie, and a lie cannot bear a face. [538] Her hands shook, but the needle did not. [546] She worked the eye into the tarred coils the way she worked filler into a fractured cheekbone: slowly, without forgiveness or blame, only attention. [570] The knot resisted, then surrendered, and the last of the interlaced hush came undone across her knees. [587] Through the prisms she saw them: the drowned, gathered on the starlit headland, no longer pacing. [603] Among them stood a shape she knew by its shoulders alone. [614] She took up the mask she had failed to finish for nine years, and now the plaster held. [632] She carried it out under the wheeling stars and set it on the grass, and the shape knelt to it, and was grounded, and was gone. [658] One by one the others followed, each spirit settling into the soil it had been denied, weighted home by truth instead of rope. [681] By dawn the net lay unraveled in the sanctuary, just honest hemp, holding nothing. [695] Teodor wept without sound, but his silence was spent now, not saved. [707] Mara threaded the tapestry needle with a strand of the freed cord and stitched it into the strap of her next mask, so that whatever face she restored from now on would carry, in its binding, one fiber of the truth.
