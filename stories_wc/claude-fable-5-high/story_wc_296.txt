The mirror trick failed at dusk, as Maren had half expected it would. [13] She had angled the silvered glass over the cursed lake, whispered the old finding-words, and watched her own reflection refuse to appear. [35] Mirrors showed surfaces, and what she needed was buried under one. [46] Maren was a druid who found lost things: rings, lambs, the names of forgotten wells. [61] What she had never found was the confession her brother Tomas made the night he drowned, the words the lake froze mid-air and swallowed whole. [86] Twenty years she had guarded herself against that absence, growing a hedge of silence around her heart, thorned and tidy. [106] Now the mirror lay dark in her hands, and only one path remained. [119] She would go to the vortex. [125]

At the heart of the lake, where the curse pulled hardest, an entire orchard turned in slow rotation: apple trees ripped from the drowned shore generations ago, roots trailing like hair, blossoms and rot circling together in green water. [164] The lake kept what it took, and it spun its keepings forever. [176] Somewhere in that spiral hung Tomas's frozen confession, a shard of breath-ice the curse had never let melt. [194]

From her pouch Maren took the seed she had carried for a decade and never dared plant. [211] It was a flesh-eating plant's seed, black and toothed, grown by the old wardens to devour curses, which are only flesh that has forgotten its body. [237] The seed required payment in blood and truth, which was why she had waited. [251]

She walked onto the water with a soldier's calm, the warlike serenity of someone who has rehearsed her own undoing and approved the plan. [275] Her finding-gift could not aim straight at grief; it worked through expanding circles, the way ripples find a shore. [294] So she circled the vortex once at its rim, naming small lost things aloud: a fishhook, a wedding band, a boy's wooden flute. [317] Each name rose glinting from the water and the curse loosened a fraction, paying out slack like a fisherman testing a line. [339] Her second circle was wider in power though tighter in stride, and she named larger losses: her mother's laugh, the orchard itself, the summer before the drowning. [366] The trees groaned in their gyre. [372] Branches that had bent for twenty years began, impossibly, to unbend, straightening toward a sky they no longer remembered. [391]

On the third circle the water rose against her, cold hands of current grabbing her ankles, and she understood the curse knew what she was hunting. [417] She did not fight it loudly. [423] She let her hedge of silence open one gate, and spoke her true purpose into the spray: she had not come for proof of Tomas's guilt or innocence, but to learn what mirrors do not show, which is the inside of a turned-away face. [467] Her own face, that night on the dock, when he tried to tell her something and she walked away. [486]

The vortex shuddered. [489] At its center, among the wheeling apple trees, something small caught the light: a sliver of ice shaped like a held breath. [511] Maren bit her thumb, pressed blood to the black seed, and gave it the truth it demanded: I left him because his confession frightened me before I ever heard it. [541] She threw the seed into the eye of the vortex. [551] It sprouted in the air, ravenous and green, and it did not touch the trees or the water or the small white shard. [574] It ate the curse itself, jaw by leafy jaw, the way grief is eaten: not all at once, but in widening rings. [596]

The orchard slowed, settled, and stood. [602] Root found mud; branch found sky; the lake lay flat as the mirror that had failed her. [619] The ice shard drifted to her hand like a tame bird. [630] She held it to her ear and let it melt. [640] Tomas's voice came out of the thaw, twenty years young: I'm the one who poisoned the orchard, Mare, I was angry, I didn't know the curse would answer, and I'm going under to ask it to stop. [677] Not a crime confessed but a rescue begun. [685] He had drowned trying to undo what he had done, and she had spent twenty years guarding herself against a brother who died generous. [709]

Maren stood a long while in the unspinning orchard, feeling her own braced shoulders unbend at last. [726] Then she did what finders do with found things. [735] She carried the melted words home in her chest, where no mirror could reach them, and where, at last, they could be seen. [758]
