Mirren had failed the Athanor Guild's trials nine times, and the masters called her the hopeless alchemist with the gentle cruelty of people who believe they are being kind. [29] Failure, however, was not what frightened her. [36] What frightened her was the letter sealed in red wax on her workbench, naming her successor to Bishop Aldous of the Ember See, a destiny arranged before her birth by a family hungry for holiness. [71] The bishop had died in autumn, and his crozier had come to her broken, snapped at the crook where he had leaned on it for forty years. [98] Accept the staff, mend it, and her life would close around her like a reliquary lid. [114] So instead she carried the two silver pieces to the ember nexus at twilight, the caldera beneath the old foundry where every banked fire in the city drained its dying heat into one slow red pool. [150] Frost rimed the iron stairs even here; the nexus burned without warming, a hearth that hoarded its glow like a flower frozen at the instant of opening. [177] Mirren was stoically sensitive in the way of people raised to be vessels: she felt everything and showed nothing, and the nexus, she suspected, was the same. [204] Her plan was heresy by any measure: she would not mend the crozier; she would vivify it. [221] If the staff lived, it could choose, and a thing that chooses cannot be inherited. [236] No one would hand a breathing relic to a failed alchemist, and her stifling destiny would crack open like a seed husk. [258] She had no grand formula, only her one true method, the one the masters mocked: successive approximation, the patient art of being wrong by smaller and smaller degrees. [286] First attempt: she bound the broken halves with quicksilver and prayed warmth into them. [300] The silver wept and went still. [306] Second: she added breath, her own, exhaled across the fracture until her lips blued in the cold light. [324] The metal shivered, then slept. [330] Third, fourth, fifth: blood, salt, the ash of her guild rejection letters. [342] Each failure was closer than the last; she could feel the staff leaning toward life the way a sunflower leans through fog, and she logged each error in her notebook with numb fingers. [375] By full dark the nexus pulsed beneath her like a patient heart, and still the crozier would not wake. [394] She sat back on her heels, fished in her pocket for a coin to scrape the residue, and found only a copper penny, thin from handling. [420] It slipped from her stiff fingers and rang down the iron stairs, and somewhere in that small bright falling sound the understanding finally arrived. [444] When the penny dropped, so did the truth she had been approximating all night: she kept trying to join the halves, to restore the staff's old shape, and the staff did not want its old shape. [480] Nothing is born without something being unmade; the seed splits, the chrysalis ruptures, the old fire must collapse before the new one draws breath. [504] Creation was not the opposite of destruction; it was destruction continued past the point of despair. [520] Her hands shook, but her face, trained by years of hiding, stayed calm as carved frost. [536] She lifted the crook, the beautiful, broken, holy crook, and brought it down against the caldera's rim until it shattered into bright splinters. [559] Then, splinter by splinter, she fed it to the nexus, adjusting after each piece: more breath here, less salt there, error shrinking toward zero. [583] The last fragment went in with the copper penny, retrieved from the stairs, her own small worn token of self laid on the fire. [607] The nexus exhaled. [610] Out of the red pool rose something that was not a crozier and not a creature but the gesture both had been reaching for: a curved stem of living silver, blooming at its crook into a flower of cold flame, petals open and utterly still, like spring caught and kept at the moment of arrival. [665] It turned toward her the way a face turns, considering. [675] Then it leaned away, choosing the dark tunnels, choosing itself, and Mirren laughed for the first time in a year. [695] Let the See appoint someone else; their relic had walked away, and no destiny survives the escape of its symbol. [715] She climbed the frosted stairs into the last violet edge of twilight, a hopeless alchemist no longer, only an unfinished one, wrong by smaller and smaller degrees, and finally, blessedly, free to keep failing toward whatever she was becoming. [754]
