Vesna ground her own glass from orchard sand, because the fog ate anything brought in from outside. [17] She was the last mirror maker in the ruined orchard district, where dead apple trees stood like glass mountains in miniature, every branch sheathed in centuries of frozen breath. [46] The fog had come three hundred years ago, when the city's shared dream slipped its sleeper and ran. [64] A dream loose in the waking world erases boundaries; this was the first thing her grandmother taught her. [82] Sky bled into ground, road into river, memory into rumor, until the fog was simply the color of nothing being separate from anything else. [106] Mirrors were the only things the fog respected, because a mirror is a boundary that argues back. [123] Vesna's grandmother had spent her life silvering small panes to keep doorways from dissolving, and had died with the work unfinished and the dream still loose. [149]

On the morning Vesna decided to capture it, she dug a strongbox from beneath the oldest tree and found the astronomer's broken sextant. [172] The astronomer had been the city's last sleeper, the one whose dream had escaped, and the instrument was the last thing he had touched. [196] Its index mirror was shattered, its horizon glass clouded blind, its arc snapped at forty degrees. [212] But Vesna understood instruments the way her grandmother had understood doors. [223] A sextant works by erasing a boundary on purpose: its mirrors lay the sun down upon the horizon, joining sky and earth in one reflected image so their distance can be known. [255] The astronomer had erased that boundary once too often, in sleep, without the discipline of measurement, and his dream had walked out through the gap. [280] What is broken open can be closed by the same hinge. [291] Vesna would recreate the sextant, mirror by mirror, and use it to take the dream's measure. [307]

She worked through the winter. [312] She melted orchard sand in her grandmother's kiln, ground the new index mirror with ash and patience, and silvered it with the last of the old stock. [339] The horizon glass she made half-silvered, half-clear, as the instrument demanded: a boundary you could see through and into at once. [360] The fog pressed at her windows the whole time, curious, the way a cat watches a cage being built. [379] She knew when to act because her grandmother's notes said the dream surfaced only upon the shimmer of twilight frost, the one hour when the fog itself half-froze and grew thin enough to show its shape. [415]

On the chosen evening she walked into the orchard, boots laced tight, pockets weighted with kiln stones, because a dream will try to lift you, and the only defense is to stay firmly grounded. [449] Frost climbed the dead branches and lit them like glass mountains catching a sun that no one had seen in three centuries. [471] And there, between the rows, the dream walked: a tall blur of orchard light, apple blossom and star charts braided together, the shape of everything the city had wanted before it forgot wanting. [504]

Vesna raised the recreated sextant. [509] Her hands shook, and the image shook with them, and she understood the final note in her grandmother's book: an instrument is steadied not by the grip but by the breath. [540] The fog was the dream's breathing; she could hear it now, a slow tide through the dead trees. [558] So she matched it. [562] Inhale as the fog drew back, exhale as it rolled forward, until her lungs and the lost dream moved as one bellows. [584] Through that synchronized breathing the sextant steadied, and in the half-silvered horizon glass two images slid together: the dream's bright figure, and the dream's reflection, laid one upon the other like sun upon sea. [618]

A dream cannot bear to meet itself. [625] Seeing its own edges for the first time in three hundred years, it remembered that it had edges, and the remembering pulled it inward like water finding a drain. [654] The fog rushed past Vesna from every direction, tugging at her coat, lifting her heels; the kiln stones held her down while three centuries of unbounded sleep funneled into the small index mirror. [687] She kept breathing in time until the last breath was hers alone. [699]

Then there was sky. [703] Real sky, violet with evening, and a horizon lying clean across the world like a drawn line. [720] In the orchard, frost was already melting from branches that, for the first time in living memory, showed buds. [739] Vesna looked into the sextant's mirror and saw the dream curled there, small and folded, like a city asleep. [758] She carried it home and set it on the workbench, between her grandmother's unfinished panes, where boundaries are made, and kept, and sometimes, carefully, opened. [783]
