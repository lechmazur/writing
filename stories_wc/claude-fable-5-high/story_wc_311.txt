The reaper climbed the observatory stairs at dusk, the hour when his kind could almost pass for human. [18] He had not harvested a soul in forty days, not since he found the recordings. [33] They had been waiting in the observatory's basement, wax cylinders sleeved in cotton, labeled in Mara's careful hand. [51] Mara, who had been an astronomer, and his, briefly, in the way a shoreline belongs to the tide. [69] Reapers are not supposed to grieve. [75] But reapers are echoes by nature; he carried the last phrases of everyone he had ever taken, words their speakers had forgotten even as they spoke them. [102] Wait for me, he murmured on the seventh step, in a dead farmer's voice. [116] Is it deep, he said on the twelfth, in a drowned girl's. [128] The phrases rose unbidden whenever he climbed toward Mara; even his haunting was borrowed. [142]

In his coat he carried a single page torn from her atlas, the one she had inked with sea monsters. [162] She had drawn serpents over the unmapped waters, not as decoration but as doctrine. [176] A fear with a name is half-tamed, she used to say. [187] Sailors who could point at the leviathan on the chart slept better than sailors who only felt the dark water under them. [209] It was why she had loved him, he suspected, or loved at him, across the distance their natures demanded. [228] He was the nameless fear, and she had spent three years trying to chart him. [243]

The recordings were her method. [248] On each cylinder she had spoken a name she invented for a terror that had none: the dread of dying mid-sentence, the fear that love outlives memory, the horror of being forgotten by the one who carries you. [286] Thirty-nine names, and the fortieth cylinder blank. [293] She had died before recording it, on the night she climbed to the great lens during the meteor storm and refused to come down. [317] He had come for her that night and she had bargained: take the names instead of me. [334] He had refused the trade; the law refused it for him. [345] So she had made the sacrifice anyway, in the only direction left: she gave her remaining hours to finishing the cylinders, and let the cold of the open dome take her while she worked. [379] Her last phrase lived in him now, the way all last phrases did. [392] He had never let himself speak it. [399]

Tonight he reached the dome as the sun guttered, and dusk poured through the slit in copper and violet. [418] He set the atlas page on the brass table beneath the great refractor. [431] He had learned, from her notebooks, what the observatory was actually for. [443] Not for seeing stars, or not only: the old astronomers had built it to fix things in place, the way light through a prism fixes a spectrum, holding what would otherwise dissolve. [475] A name spoken into scattered light could crystallize. [483] It could become a thing the living might find and hold. [494]

He played the cylinders one by one into the dying light. [505] Her voice rose, thin and bright, naming each terror, and where the dusk broke against the prisms, the names hardened into small glass shapes that fell like hail onto the sea monsters of her map. [540] Thirty-nine crystals, each one a fear that no sailor or widow or child would have to feel namelessly again. [559] He arranged them along the inked serpents, beasts crowned at last. [570]

Then he stood before the fortieth, the blank one, and understood what she had left undone on purpose. [588] The last nameless fear was his. [594] The fear of the one who ends things: that he ends, too; that grief in a deathless creature has nowhere to drain. [616] She had not named it because it was not hers to name. [628] It was his last phrase to give. [635]

He bent to the recording horn, and for the first time he let her final words leave him, the ones she had forgotten even as she spoke them into the freezing dark. [667] Stay until the light scatters, he said, in her voice. [677] And after her words, his own, the first phrase that had ever belonged to him: I name you Mara's Hour, the fear of loving what you must carry away. [706]

The dusk shattered through the prisms and the fortieth crystal formed, warmer than the rest, faintly luminous, like a star drawn by hand. [729] He placed it at the center of the atlas page, in the open water where she had written, long ago, here there is nothing to fear that cannot be named. [759] Then he descended the stairs, lighter by one phrase, leaving the map for whoever climbed next. [775] Behind him, in the emptied dome, the dusk kept her hour. [786]
