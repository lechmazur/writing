Mara kept the shadows the way other people kept bees. [10] She was the tender of them, the shadow keeper of the burned chapel, and she handled each one as if it might bruise. [33] The sanctuary had been a chapel once, before the fire ate it down to ashen charcoal ribs, and now it stood open to starlight, black beams framing the constellations like a map of everything she had left. [70] Six months had passed since she cut off all contact with her sister. [83] She had done it the way she did everything: carefully. [93] When she left, she burned the rope bridge across the gorge plank by plank, wetting the grass first, watching each ember die before lighting the next. [119] Mara burned bridges carefully; it was the only tenderness she knew how to give herself. [134] All she carried from the old house was the doorknocker, a heavy brass ring pried from the front door, worn now on a cord against her sternum. [161] Lena had called it stealing. [166] Mara had called it keeping the only part of home that ever let anyone in. [181] The shadows came to her from the grieving, the ones who could not carry their own darkness anymore. [199] They drifted up the gorge and settled in the chapel like smoke remembering the fire. [214] Her work was simple and impossible: she taught the suffocating to breathe. [226] A shadow arrives airless, clenched, all the grief its person refused to exhale, and Mara would sit with it until it learned the rhythm again. [251] She did this by linking through scars. [258] Every shadow kept the marks of its body, and every mark on Mara's skin could answer one. [275] She would find the matching wound, press her scar to theirs, and let breath pass between them like water finding level. [296] But the threshold was fading. [301] The chapel doorway, the charcoal frame that kept the shadows in and the world out, had begun to thin, going gray at the edges like dawn arriving where no dawn should. [332] Thresholds are held up by what crosses them, and nothing had crossed to Mara in six months. [349] A door no one knocks on stops believing it is a door. [361] The shadows felt it first. [366] They began to encroach, sliding past the frame at night, pooling in the gorge, reaching toward the village lights like the fingers of the drowned. [391] Mara herded them back with lantern and patience, but each night they pushed farther, and each night the doorway was paler. [412] Then, on a night of hard starlight, a new shadow climbed the gorge alone. [426] It came up the path where the bridge used to be, as if it remembered a crossing that no longer existed. [447] It was suffocating worse than any she had tended, folded small, not breathing at all. [462] Mara knelt and searched its surface for a scar she could answer. [474] She found it on the left palm, a thin crescent, and her own left palm went cold. [491] She knew that crescent. [495] Two girls, one fence wire, one shared dare, two matching cuts they had pressed together afterward, swearing the blood made them one person split in half. [521] Lena. [522] Her sister was alive somewhere, but she had stopped carrying her own grief, and it had walked here, to the one door that might still know her. [549] Mara understood then what her careful burning had done. [558] She had not spared Lena the fire; she had only left her on the far side of it, holding all that smoke alone. [581] She pressed her crescent scar to the shadow's. [589] Breath moved. [591] It was like inhaling their mother's funeral, the silence after the last unanswered letter, every loaded emptiness Mara had bricked herself away from. [614] She did not pull back. [619] In, she breathed, and the shadow shuddered. [626] Out, and it loosened. [630] They breathed together until the shadow stood upright and light passed through it, and the chapel's other shadows grew still, watching something they had never seen: the keeper being kept. [660] But the doorway behind them had gone nearly transparent. [669] One more night and there would be no threshold at all, only shadows and the open world. [686] Mara lifted the doorknocker ring from her sternum. [694] A door is only a door if someone knocks. [703] She pressed the brass ring against the fading frame, charcoal flaking under her fingers, and knocked three times, the way Lena used to knock on her bedroom door after every fight. [734] The sound carried down the gorge like a struck bell. [744] The threshold darkened, deepened, held. [749] At dawn, Mara walked Lena's breathing shadow down to where the bridge had been and began, plank by plank, to build it back, as carefully as she had burned it. [779] Some doors, she thought, you do not keep; you answer them. [790]
