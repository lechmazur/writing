Ser Calloway had not spoken aloud in three winters, and the orchard did not require him to. [17] He kept his watch inside the alchemical greenhouse the way a tongue keeps returning to a broken tooth. [35] Night had not lifted from the glass in years, an endless dark that the alchemists had distilled and then drowned in. [56] The trees still bore fruit anyway, pears of pale silver light, each one factually mystical, measured and catalogued by men who no longer existed to read their own ledgers. [85] Calloway had come here after the war to guard something, though no one had told him what. [102] He suspected, in his withdrawn way, that he was guarding himself from the world. [116]

On the night everything changed, he found the program beneath the seventh tree. [129] It was a playbill from a marionette theater, abandoned and water-stained, its ink the brown of old blood. [147] THE ORCHARD OF WHAT MIGHT BE, the title read, performed once, for an audience of alchemists, on the last night anyone had seen the sun. [172] Calloway turned it over with gauntleted fingers and felt his pulse stutter. [184] The acts were listed like instructions. [190] Act One: The Knight Removes His Armor. [197] Act Two: The Knight Walks the Old Rows. [205] Act Three: The Knight Speaks. [210]

He should have burned it. [215] Instead he stood very still while something in his chest, rusted shut since the war, began to ache open. [234] His hands moved before his doubt could. [241] He worked to unsnap the clasps of his breastplate one by one, each release a small confession, until the steel fell away and the orchard's cold light touched his shirt for the first time in years. [277] He shivered, and the shiver was not entirely fear. [286]

Act Two required the old rows, and here the program offered its strangest gift. [300] Its fold lines, creased and re-creased, formed a map of the orchard's original planting, paths the alchemists had walked while singing growth into the seeds. [325] The orchard could only be woken through forgotten patterns, and the program remembered what every living person had forgotten. [344] So Calloway walked. [347] He traced the creases tree by tree, a figure-eight here, a long spiral there, his boots printing the dark soil with the choreography of dead men. [373] By the third circuit, the pears began to brighten. [382] By the fifth, the glass overhead rippled like the surface of a pond, and Calloway understood that reality itself was shifting around him, the greenhouse becoming less a place than a stage. [414] The trees lifted their branches like arms on strings. [423] Above them, in the dark beyond the glass, he could almost see the crossbars of some immense puppeteer, and the sight did not frighten him. [448] It felt like being held. [453]

Act Three: The Knight Speaks. [458] He had withheld his voice for three winters because the last words he had spoken were an oath to a king who spent ten thousand lives on a border no one remembered. [490] Oaths were dangerous instruments. [494] But the orchard was waiting, and the fruit hung swollen with futures, and Calloway wanted, more than he had wanted anything, to capture the shape of possibility before it withered on the branch. [527] Not for a king this time. [533] For whatever might still be. [538] So he gave his murmured allegiance, barely louder than breath, an oath sworn not to a crown but to the unfinished, the unwritten, the could-yet-be. [563] I serve what might happen, he whispered. [570]

The orchard heard him. [574] The nearest pear dropped into his open palm, warm as a sleeping bird, and when his fingers closed around it, light spilled between them in every direction at once. [603] He saw the shapes inside it, his own life branching, a hundred Calloways who had never gone to war, who had married, who had planted gardens, who had laughed. [632] The seeing was almost unbearable, a bliss that shook him like a fever, and he wept standing up without making a sound. [654]

When the light dimmed, the endless night outside the glass had thinned at one edge, gray bleeding into black. [673] Not dawn, not yet, but the possibility of dawn, which the orchard had taught him was nearly the same thing. [693] Calloway folded the program along its old creases and tucked it inside his shirt, over his heart, where the breastplate used to be. [716] He left the armor among the roots to rust into the soil. [728] There would be other rows to walk, other patterns the dead had left for the living to remember. [746] He walked out of the greenhouse still trembling, still blissful, a knight again, sworn at last to something worth the kneeling. [767]
