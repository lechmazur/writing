She had built her reputation on being skillfully observant, and her favorite diagnostic tool cost nothing: dust. [124] Dust accumulates honestly. [127] It thickens where nothing moves and thins where something does, and by analyzing dust accumulation patterns she could read a machine's secret habits the way a tracker reads snow. [156] The ticket office told her a strange story. [164] The counter wore decades of gray felt, undisturbed. [198] Mara had a private rule, half obsession and half vocation: she needed to discover what ceilings hide. [215] Ceilings were where buildings lied. [220] Floors confessed everything; ceilings kept counsel. [226]
