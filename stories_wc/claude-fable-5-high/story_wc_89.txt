Mara had spoken to beasts all her life, but she had never learned how to speak to the dead. [19] The orchard helped her pretend otherwise. [25] It bloomed under permanent moonlight, pear blossoms silvered open at all hours, and the apples never ripened past their cold perfection. [46] Time did not pass here so much as hold its breath. [57] It was the only place she could bear to live after Ashan died. [70]

Ashan had been her phoenix, raised from a coal-warm egg, taught to land on her wrist like a thought arriving. [90] When he burned, she had told herself what every beast whisperer knows: phoenixes return. [104] And he had returned. [108] Three nights ago the ashes had stirred, and a new bird rose, gold-eyed and flawless and utterly unacquainted with her. [128] He perched in the highest pear tree now and regarded her the way stars regard sailors: brightly, and from very far away. [150] The bond had not survived the fire. [157] Resurrection, it turned out, was not the same as return. [167]

She still carried the fortune from the cookie he had stolen off her plate the night before he burned. [186] He had shredded the cookie and left the slip untouched, almost ceremonially, beside her cup. [201] It read: WHAT YOU LOVED IS NOT BEHIND YOU. IT IS AHEAD, WEARING A DIFFERENT SHAPE. [217] She had laughed then. [221] She was not laughing now. [226]

Beast whisperers bond through scent before sound; this was the first law of her craft. [241] So she knelt beneath the pear tree with her mortar and her last hoarded treasures: saffron threads, black cardamom, a sliver of true cinnamon from a tree that no longer grew anywhere. [273] These were the spices she had ground into Ashan's first feed, the smell of his hatching, the smell of them. [293] If she could conjure that scent again, she might conjure the bond with it, summon the old recognition up through the new body like water through roots. [320] Her hands were paradoxically steady, the way hands get when everything that could shake has already broken. [337]

She ground the spices and the orchard leaned in. [346] The scent rose, warm and ancient, and the moonlight seemed to thicken around it like honey around a spoon. [365] Ashan's head turned. [368] But something else turned too -- something inside her own chest, vast and slow, like a door she had been leaning against her whole life. [392] She understood, with cold clarity, what the magic actually asked. [402] The scent could not drag him backward into the creature he had been; fire had closed that door forever. [421] But it could carry her forward. [427] To meet a remade thing, she would have to be remade. [438]

Heat gathered under her skin like an opinion she had not agreed to. [451] She felt feathers waiting in her arms the way leaves wait in winter wood. [465] Every instinct screamed refusal. [469] She had spent her life translating beasts precisely so she would never have to become one; the border between herself and the wild had been her whole profession, her whole self. [500] She gripped the cold grass and held her own shape with clenched teeth. [513]

Then Ashan descended, branch by branch, drawn down through the spiced air, and landed an arm's length away. [531] Close enough that she could see he was trembling too. [541] He did not remember her, but he was waiting, the way the universe waits at the edge of every birth: patient, enormous, undecided. [564] What you loved is not behind you. [571] It is ahead, wearing a different shape. [578] Perhaps the fortune had never been about him. [586]

She exhaled, and stopped holding. [591] The change came gently after all that dread, like falling asleep in a warm room. [606] Her grief loosened into feathers; her silence opened into a long copper throat. [619] She did not stop being Mara; she became, instead, a Mara that fire could speak to. [635]

Ashan made a low sound, the first sound of his new life, and it landed in her like a name. [655] Recognition moved between them -- not memory, but something older than memory, the gravity two stars feel before they have ever been introduced. [677] The bond did not return. [682] It was reforged, hammered new on the other side of death, where she had finally agreed to go and meet it. [703] They rose together over the orchard, two embers in the permanent moonlight, and the pear trees released their blossoms upward, as if even the ground had decided, just this once, to let something go.
