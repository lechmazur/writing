Mara stepped off the edge of the broken stair and did not fall. [13] She had stopped falling years ago, on the night the timelines fractured and gravity forgot her name. [30] Below her, the viaduct glowed after the rain, its wet stones holding light the way skin holds a bruise. [49] She drifted down to it, boots kissing the luminous stone. [59] In her satchel rode the ancient clay tablet, its runes worn smooth by a hundred of her own thumbs. [78] The tablet was older than the fracture, and it was the only object that remembered every version of her. [97] Each time the world reset, the viaduct rebuilt itself through procedural generation, arches assembling from rules instead of memory. [116] Same seed, different city: a labyrinth grown fresh each cycle from the grammar of her own regrets. [133] The runes told her how to read it. [141] Retrace, they said, in a language only the broken could pronounce. [152] Retrace your steps through every life you abandoned, and the maze will end where you began. [168] Tonight the air smelled of ozone and wet clay, bright and somehow dimming, like a lamp deciding to die. [187] A single raindrop hung above a puddle ahead of her, suspended mid-fall. [199] This was the door. [203] The fracture had left seams in time, and the widest one opened between the drop and the ripple, an interval that should have lasted nothing and instead lasted as long as she could bear it. [238] Inside that interval, she was periodically eternal: deathless for the duration of the pause, mortal again the instant the water broke. [259] She stepped into the stillness. [264] The viaduct unfolded. [267] Arches multiplied down its length, each one generated from the last by rules she could almost feel: turn left at the choice you refused, descend at the apology you never gave. [298] The labyrinth was not made of stone. [305] It was made of her. [310] She had entered it forty-one times. [316] Forty-one times she had fled before the center, because the center held the version of her who had let herself fall. [337] The tablet warmed against her hip as she walked. [346] Its runes brightened, then darkened, then brightened, a heartbeat of clay. [357] She retraced. [359] Here was the arch where, in one timeline, she had jumped from the viaduct and learned that gravity refused her. [379] Here was the stair where, in another, she had carried her dying mother up into the rain because the hospitals had fractured too. [402] Each generated arch was a memory rendered honest, and honesty had a darkening brightness to it, beautiful and getting harder to look at. [425] The glow of the stones deepened from gold toward amber, toward rust. [437] The drop above the puddle had descended a hair's width. [447] Her eternity was running out. [452] At the labyrinth's center stood a woman with Mara's face, standing on the stones the ordinary way, heavy, obedient to the earth. [474] "You float because you refused to land," the other Mara said. [485] "You fractured the timelines yourself, the night you could not choose, so you chose everything at once." [502] Mara's throat closed. [505] She had told herself the fracture was the world's fault, some cosmic accident, but the tablet had always known better. [525] She drew it out. [529] The runes spelled her own name, over and over, in forty-one variations. [541] To conquer the labyrinth of self, the final rune read, surrender the self that conquers. [556] She understood, finally, that escape had never been the exit. [566] Choosing was. [568] "I stay," Mara said, to her double, to the maze, to the trembling drop. [582] "I choose this one life, with its weight." [590] She pressed the tablet to her chest and let gravity remember her. [602] Her boots settled onto the stone with a heaviness that felt like mercy. [615] The double smiled and dissolved into rain. [622] Around her, the procedural arches stopped multiplying; the rules had nothing left to generate, because she had stopped being forty-one women and become one. [646] The drop fell. [649] The ripple spread, and time resumed, and her eternity ended as all her eternities did, on schedule, in water. [668] But this time no new viaduct assembled itself from the grammar of regret. [681] There was only one viaduct now, glowing softly after the rain, its light fading the way evening fades, not into nothing, but into night, which is its own kind of beginning. [712] Mara walked home across it, heavy as anyone, carrying a tablet whose runes had gone blank and quiet at last. [732] Behind her, the puddle stilled. [737] Somewhere between the drop and the ripple, she had finally landed. [748]
