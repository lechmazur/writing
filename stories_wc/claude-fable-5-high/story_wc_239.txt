The temple library had been lost so long that time itself had forgotten where it left it. [17] Mavet arrived there the way all witnesses arrive at endings: by being needed. [30] She was the end of history's witness, the one appointed to watch the last fact close its eyes and to sign for it. [53] The universe was hiccuping. [57] Between its last exhale and whatever came next, a stutter had opened, a held breath wide enough to hold one library and one decision. [81] Inside, the hush was not silence; it resounded, the way a struck bell keeps ringing after sound gives up. [100] Shelves rose in a spiral from a sunken center, coil upon coil of stone galleries climbing toward a ceiling no lamp had ever found. [124] At the center stood the disputants. [130] One was the universe as it believed itself to be: vast, lawful, radiant with purpose. [145] The other was the universe as it suspected itself to be: an accident wearing borrowed mathematics. [161] Neither could begin again until they agreed on what they had been. [173] "You are the arbiter," they said together, two voices making one echo. [185] Mavet had arbitrated endings before, but never by the spiral rite. [196] The library's law was old and simple: the arbiter walks the coiled galleries outward, shelf by shelf, and with every step away from the center she must travel further into herself, until the path out and the path in arrive at the same door. [240] Whatever truth survived that double journey would bind them both. [250] She was given no scepter, no scale, no book of law. [261] From the lowest shelf, between gilded cosmologies and chained grimoires, the library offered her a softcover dictionary, dog-eared, its spine creased white, the kind of thing a student abandons on a train. [293] She understood at once. [297] Illusions are not built from lies; they are built from definitions left unexamined. [310] To unravel a cosmic illusion, you do not need thunder; you need to look up the words it hides behind. [330] She began to walk. [334] First coil. [336] The radiant universe followed at her left shoulder, the doubting one at her right, their light and shadow braided on the stone. [358] "Define purpose," she said, and opened the dictionary. [366] The entry had rewritten itself for her, as everything in the library did: purpose, noun, a story told by survivors about the route they happened to take. [393] The radiant universe flinched. [397] Second coil, and a step inward: Mavet remembered her first ending, a small green world that had called its extinction destiny because the word hurt less than accident. [425] "Define accident," she said on the third coil. [433] Accident, noun: an event awaiting a witness patient enough to find its cause. [446] The doubting universe went quiet. [451] So it went, coil after coil, definition after definition, her steps wanderingly exact: they meandered like a riverbed and yet each landed precisely where the rite required, the wandering itself a kind of measurement. [485] Outward she walked through galleries of dead languages; inward she walked through every ending she had ever signed for. [504] On the seventh coil she found her own entry. [513] Witness, noun: one who believes that watching is not participating. [523] She stopped. [525] The hush deepened until it rang in her teeth. [534] That was the cosmic illusion, the deepest one, the one the disputants had built their quarrel upon: that meaning and accident were two things, that the universe could be either lawful or random, that a watcher stood outside what she watched. [575] She had spent her whole service believing history ended somewhere she was not. [588] On the last coil, at the library's outer door, she arrived also at her own center, and the two arrivals were one arrival, as the rite had promised. [616] She turned to the disputants, who had walked the whole spiral with her and were no longer entirely two. [635] "Here is my arbitration," she said. [641] "You are not a purpose wearing matter, and you are not an accident wearing mathematics. [656] You are one event still defining itself, and your quarrel was only a word split in half." [673] She opened the softcover dictionary to its final page, which had always been blank, and wrote a single entry to unify them: universe, noun, a witness slowly learning what it means. [704] The two disputants leaned together like flames sharing a wick and became one continuing thing. [719] The hiccup released. [722] All of it, the rite, the walk, the arbitration, had lasted no time at all; across a universe's hiccup is exactly how long a held breath lasts. [749] Time remembered where it had left the library and came back for it, sheepish as a librarian returning an overdue book. [770] Mavet stepped through the door into the new beginning, no longer outside it. [783] Behind her, in the resounding hush, the dictionary kept its newest word warm. [796]
