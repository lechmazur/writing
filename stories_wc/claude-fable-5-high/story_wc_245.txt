Orin had been blind since the fever, but the forest had never asked her to see. [16] It spoke in ultrasound, in frequencies pitched above any ear, and she read it the way she read everything: through her skin. [38] Languages soaked into her like water into chalk; press her palm to birch bark and the birch's grammar settled into her blood by morning. [62] It was how she made her maps: not of distances, but of what places meant. [77] She had charted the whole singing forest except one region, the blank at its center where her hands always faltered, because that was where Senna had died. [104] Two years of careful avoidance had made her maps precise and her heart a smudge. [119]

She wore Senna's compass ring on her left thumb, a band of platinum with a needle set flush into it, a needle that had never pointed north. [146] Senna, who had been a dowser, used to laugh and say it pointed at whatever you could not stop wanting. [166] For two years the needle had lain dead. [174] Then, on the night the constellations paused, it woke. [183]

Every nineteen years the stars in that country hesitate, holding their old shapes for seven nights before sliding into new ones, and the forest changes its song to match the sky. [214] Orin felt the pause begin as a stillness on her cheekbones, and felt the ring tremble against her thumb like a small held breath. [238] It pointed toward the blank at the center of her map. [249] She could have taken the ring off. [256] Instead she packed her stylus and wax tablets and walked into the trees. [269]

The ultrasonic forest pressed its voices against her: alarm calls of bats stitched through the canopy, the high creak of growing wood, pollen counts announced in pitches that prickled her forearms. [300] She let her bare hand trail along trunks as she went, and the forest's language seeped in, syllable by resinous syllable. [321] On the second night she understood what the trees were saying. [332] They were not speaking; they were singing single notes, one tree at a time, each note a half step from the last. [354] A trail. [356] Someone, or something, had taught the forest a melody and scattered it tree by tree toward the center, the way you might drop crumbs of bread. [382] Orin knew the intervals before her mind named them. [391] It was the tune Senna used to hum while dowsing, the one she had never hummed all the way through, claiming the ending wasn't ready yet. [417]

Orin's grief, which she had kept folded like an old map, opened along every crease. [432] She followed. [434] The ring's needle and the trail of notes agreed at every fork, and she began to understand that she was dowsing now, not for water but for whatever lay at the bottom of her own avoided dark. [471]

On the fifth night of the pause, the voices began to thin. [483] By the sixth, the air against her skin went quiet in a way air never goes. [499] She stepped into a clearing where no frequency moved at all: the stillpoint, the one place the forest's vast song bent around and could not enter. [525] Senna had been searching for it when she died. [534]

Orin knelt in the silence and laid both palms flat on the earth. [547] Languages rose into her skin: not the forest's, this time, but a slower tongue, the deep grammar of roots and patience, and braided into it, unmistakable, the last bars of Senna's unfinished melody. [580] Senna had reached the stillpoint after all. [587] She had reached it, and finished her song, and left it here in the ground where only Orin's hands could ever read it. [610] The ending was not sad. [615] It resolved, gently, on the same note it began with, the way a journey resolves when you finally stop running from its center. [638]

Orin stayed through the seventh night, learning the melody by heart and by skin. [652] She wept without noise, because the stillpoint held even her sobs, and there was a strange mercy in that: grief with the volume taken away, so she could finally feel its shape. [684] When the constellations shuddered and resumed their slow migration, the forest's new song poured back into the world, and the trail of notes dissolved into ordinary singing. [711] The ring's needle went still on her thumb, pointing nowhere, wanting nothing. [723]

Orin walked home and unrolled her great map of the forest. [734] In the blank center, she did not draw trees or paths. [745] She pressed musical notation into the wax, the whole melody, beginning to end, so that anyone who found the map could follow it inward. [769] It was the truest chart she ever made: not of where the stillpoint was, but of what it meant to arrive. [790]
