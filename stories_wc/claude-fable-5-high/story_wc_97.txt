Elias Vane played the last piano in Terminus Nine, the high station where airships moored against the wind. [18] Travelers no longer trusted their compasses, which had flickered uselessly since the magnetic storms began, so they navigated by his music instead. [40] Certain chords, the harbormasters swore, rang true toward north when every needle lied. [53] Elias did not feel like an instrument of navigation. [62] He felt like a wistful man keeping a broken anniversary clock on the piano lid, watching its frozen pendulum and remembering Mara. [84] The clock had stopped at 7:14 on their tenth anniversary, the night her survey ship vanished into the cloud deck and never descended. [107] Official word called the loss weather. [113] Elias called it unfinished. [117] Then, in the spring of the third year, a courier left a film canister on his bench with no note. [137] The reel was black-and-white footage of Mara's last expedition, the kind of record the survey office had claimed was destroyed. [157] He threaded it through the station's old projector after midnight, when the waiting hall was empty. [173] Mara appeared in silver and shadow, laughing at the rail of her ship, pointing upward at something the camera refused to hold. [195] Then the film stuttered, and between two frames he saw it: hidden sections spliced into the reel, single frames of charts, bearings, and a sketch of an arbor rooted in nothing but sky. [228] Someone had smuggled a map through the censors the only way left: one frame at a time, invisible at speed, legible only to a patient hand. [254] Mara had been strategically creative all her life; he recognized her handwriting in the margins. [269] The hidden frames described a tidal arbor above the clouds, a grove of sky-rooted trees that rose and sank with the vapor tides, sheltering anyone the storms had stranded. [298] A refuge, her notes said, that surfaced only between dawn and dusk and could be entered only until the horizon invites night. [320] After dark, the tide pulled it under the cloudline, beyond any reaching. [332] The last frame was a single sentence: the trees answer the same chords the compasses fail. [348] Elias understood what fate had written for him: a widower at a piano, playing other people home until he died at his bench. [371] He decided to surprise fate with an unplanned encore. [380] He told no one and chartered a battered mail skiff whose pilot owed him for years of free requests. [399] He took the reel, a portable harmonium, and the broken clock, because a man should bring his unfinished things to their finishing place. [422] They flew at first light, the skiff's compass flickering like a candle in argument with the dark. [439] Elias played Mara's chords against the engine noise, and where the needle spun, the air itself seemed to lean a direction. [460] All day they climbed through cloud canyons, following the spliced bearings frame by frame, the pilot cursing softly, Elias counting daylight like coins. [483] His longing had gone steely over three years; it no longer wept, it calculated. [497] At the hour the sun began to redden, the clouds parted like a drawn curtain. [512] The arbor stood above the cloud sea, immense and slow, its roots trailing in vapor, its canopy combing light out of the west. [535] Already the tide was turning; the grove was settling, inch by inch, toward the cloud deck. [551] Figures moved among the branches, stranded survivors in salvaged coats, and one of them stopped walking when she saw the skiff. [572] The trees would not let the skiff land; the air around them shuddered the engine to a stall. [590] So Elias did the only navigation he had ever been good at. [602] He unstrapped the harmonium on the open deck and played the chord the compasses failed, then the next, then the whole hidden song her frames had spelled. [629] The branches turned toward the sound the way sunflowers turn, and a limb broad as a pier reached out and held still beneath the hull. [654] He crossed it with the clock under his arm while the horizon went amber, then bruise-colored, inviting night with both hands. [675] Mara was thinner, gray at the temples, and entirely alive. [685] She had sent the reel through three smugglers, never certain any was honest. [698] He held out the anniversary clock, its pendulum still locked at 7:14. [710] She opened the glass dome, touched the pendulum, and set it swinging by hand, the way you restart anything that grief has stopped. [733] Below them, the tide drew the arbor down into the clouds, and the skiff's pilot waved once and turned for home. [754] Elias did not watch him go. [760] Fate had planned a quiet ending at a station piano, and he had played past the final bar instead. [779] The encore, he decided, would last until something better than night invited him away.
