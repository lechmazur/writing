The grotto sat above the clouds, a hollow of umbral stone where daylight arrived only as a thin green seam along the ceiling. [23] Below, terraced vines clung to the mountain's shoulder, and the hush that rose from them at dusk was the only sound the grotto ever kept. [48] Maren was its caretaker, and she had never once asked to be relieved. [61] Souls came to the grotto to be reborn. [69] They drifted up through the cloud floor as pale moths, settled on the wet stone, and waited for her hands. [89] For each one she pressed a small disc of beeswax, and with the point of an antique hairpin she scalloped its edge, a ring of careful half-moons that told the world below what shape of life the soul had earned. [129] Smooth edges returned as grass or rain; scalloped ones returned as people. [141] The hairpin had belonged to her sister Ila. [149] Eleven years ago, in the village under the vines, Ila had sold the location of the old grotto path to men who wanted to bottle the souls and sell rebirth to the rich. [182] The men had come at night and torn the previous caretaker, their mother, from the stone she guarded. [200] Their mother did not survive the fall through the clouds. [210] Maren had spent a year wanting to be made of something harder than grief. [224] Then, on a morning she still could not explain, she had walked to Ila's door, set the hairpin in her own hair, and said, I forgive you, because I cannot carry you and the mountain both. [260] After forgiving the unforgivable, she took her mother's post and climbed above the clouds, hoping the work would show her where contentment actually came from. [285] It had not, yet. [289] The work was good; the work was not the answer. [299] Then, on the first cold evening of autumn, a moth came up through the clouds wearing Ila's unmistakable gray. [318] Maren knew her sister had been ill; she had not known the illness had won. [333] The moth settled on the wet stone, and the choice settled on Maren. [346] Smooth edge or scalloped. [350] Rain, or another human life with all its capacity to betray. [361] When Maren could not think, she climbed. [368] A single fig tree grew at the grotto's mouth, roots wedged into a fault in the umbral rock, branches reaching out over the white sea of cloud. [395] Her mother had taught her this: thinking done on the ground stays on the ground, but a mind carried up into branches can see around its own corners. [423] So Maren climbed, the hairpin holding her braid, the wax disc warm in her pocket, until she sat where the leaves thinned and the sunset poured across the cloud floor like wine across a table. [458] Far below, the vineyards were going dark, and their hush rose to meet her, that particular silence of things growing without apology. [480] She thought about what forgiveness had actually been. [488] Not a feeling; she had not felt warm toward Ila for years afterward. [501] It had been a decision, made kindly, the way a vintner prunes: not to punish the vine but to let next year exist. [524] And there, with her legs hooked over a branch above the clouds, Maren finally saw around the corner of her own mind. [546] Contentment had no source she could visit, no spring hidden in the rock. [559] It was made fresh each time someone chose to keep the cycle turning instead of stopping it where it had hurt them. [581] Rebirth was not a wheel that ran on its own; it ran on ten thousand small forgivenesses, hers among them. [601] To smooth Ila's disc would be to take one turn of the wheel hostage for a wound already forgiven. [620] She climbed down in the last light. [627] In the grotto, the moth waited, gray wings folded like hands. [638] Maren drew the hairpin from her braid, its silver worn soft by three generations of women, and pressed its point into the warm wax. [662] She scalloped the edge slowly, each half-moon deliberate, each one a yes. [674] Come back as a person, she told the moth. [683] Come back able to betray, and able, this time, to choose otherwise. [695] The moth pressed itself to the disc and dissolved into it, and the wax sank down through the cloud floor toward the sleeping vineyards, toward some cradle waiting in the village below. [727] Maren slid the hairpin back into her hair and felt, at last, the thing she had climbed the mountain to find. [748] It was quiet, like the vines at dusk. [756] It would need to be made again tomorrow. [764] She found she did not mind. [770]
