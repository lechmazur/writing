Mirei earned her living drawing what other people refused to look at closely. [13] She was a natural history illustrator in Veshtower, a city raised on the shoulders of a sleeping giant, where canals ran not through streets but across rooftops, weaving silver water between the towers. [46] Everything in Veshtower happened across a giant's nap; when he woke, every few generations, the towers were rebuilt on his settling bones, and life began again. [72] The giant's heartbeat kept the city's rhythm, a slow drum under everything. [84] That spring, the drum began to falter. [91] Beat, beat, silence; an interrupted cadence, like a song stopping just before resolution. [104] The wardens called it nothing. [109] Mirei, who had spent twenty years drawing the city's small lives, knew better. [122] The echo-wrens had stopped singing. [127] She had illustrated them for the Guild's bestiary: small grey birds that did not make sound but carried it, swallowing voices in one district and releasing them in another. [156] The Guild had caged hundreds of them in the aviary atop the Cartographers' Tower, harvesting echoes to sell as recorded messages. [177] Mirei believed the wrens fed the giant his dreams, returning the city's sounds to the deep ear below. [195] Cage the birds, starve the dream; starve the dream, and the sleeper begins to wake. [210] Her grandmother had left her one thing: a pendant shaped like an infinity symbol, two loops of dull pewter crossing at a worn center. [234] It sculpts time itself, her grandmother had said, but only by borrowing. [246] Press the crossing point, and an hour flows to you from your own future; you must repay it, later, in weakness and lost days. [270] Mirei had never dared use it. [276] But she had a question that had pulled at her since childhood, when she first heard her own voice return to her, days late, from a wren's open throat: where are echoes born? [309] If the birds died caged, the question died with them, and so might the city. [324] On the night the heartbeat skipped twice in succession, Mirei took her flat-bottomed skiff onto the rooftop canals. [342] She poled between towers, under rope bridges, past gardens floating on barge-roofs, toward the Cartographers' Tower. [358] Wardens patrolled the aviary at every bell; she would never pass them in ordinary time. [373] So she pressed the pendant's crossing point and felt tomorrow drain into tonight. [386] The water stilled mid-ripple. [390] A warden's lantern hung frozen, its flame a sculpture of yellow glass. [402] She walked across the motionless canal as if it were marble, climbed the aviary stair, and stood among five hundred silent cages. [424] The wrens were not frozen. [429] They watched her with bright, patient eyes, creatures who had never lived inside time the way people did. [447] Of course, she thought, her illustrator's mind assembling years of sketches into a single understanding. [462] An echo is a sound that borrows time, leaving one moment to be repaid in another. [478] The wrens and the pendant worked on the same principle. [488] Echoes were born wherever time was lent. [495] She began to uncage them, latch after latch, her borrowed hour spending itself with every door. [511] The wrens did not scatter. [516] They gathered above her in a grey wheel, waiting, as if they knew she had paid for this moment with pieces of her own life. [541] When the last cage opened, the borrowed hour ran out. [551] Time crashed back; the lantern swung, the canal rippled, and the wardens shouted from the stair. [567] The wrens dove past them in a single ribbon, down the canal channels, down the tower walls, down toward the giant's buried ear. [590] Mirei gripped the rail and listened. [596] Beat, beat, silence; then, where the silence had been, every stolen sound returned at once: years of harvested voices, lullabies, arguments, bells, pouring into the gap like water into a dry channel. [628] The interrupted cadence resolved. [632] The heartbeat steadied beneath the city, slow and whole. [641] The wardens found only an exhausted woman holding a pewter pendant, too weak to arrest convincingly, repaying her debt of hours in trembling and sleep. [666] They let her go; a city that owed its continued nap to someone tends to misplace the paperwork. [684] Weeks later, recovered, Mirei sat on her own rooftop with her sketchbook. [696] A single wren landed on her inkwell and opened its throat. [707] Out came her grandmother's voice, decades borrowed, finally repaid: time is a loop, little one, like the pendant; what you lend always finds its way home. [733] Mirei drew the bird with its beak open, and beneath it she wrote the answer she had gone looking for. [753] Echoes are born wherever a moment is given away and trusted to return. [766] Above her, the canals carried their silver weight between the towers, and below, the giant slept on, dreaming the city's voices, his long nap unbroken. [791]
