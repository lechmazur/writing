Wren's gift had always worked backward. [6] She dreamed in vivid prophecy, but the dreams refused interpretation until after the events they foretold had already passed. [25] Foresight that arrived only as hindsight, her grandmother had called it, a lantern lit at the bottom of the stairs you already fell down. [49]

So when the path opened for her, she did not trust it. [61] The rewilded forest had swallowed the old highways decades ago, and its trails rearranged themselves like sentences being revised. [80] Yet this one held steady, a green throat leading down to a bunker draped in vines so thick the door looked grown rather than built. [105]

Thunder muttered in the west. [110] The storm would arrive by nightfall, and the path, she suspected, would not survive it. [125]

Inside, the bunker breathed cold and mineral. [132] A single document waited on a steel table, folded and fixed with a wafer seal, the brittle red disc stamped with a sigil she half recognized from her dreams. [161] Around it, the walls were dense with older copies of the same directive, each more decayed than the last, ink ghosting toward blankness. [184] Whoever had built this place had issued one order, and time had been eating it ever since. [201]

The oldest legible copy read: WALKER, OPEN ONLY WHEN YOU UNDERSTAND WHY YOU WERE CHOSEN. [216] Newer copies had lost words to damp and dark until the latest said only: OPEN... WHY... CHOSEN. [233] A directive decaying into a question. [239] The same question that had driven her into the forest at all: why do some paths choose their walkers? [258]

She had asked it since childhood, since the night her sister followed a trail that closed behind her and never reopened. [279] Wren had dreamed that loss in perfect detail, and understood the dream only the morning after, when understanding was worthless. [299]

She slept on the bunker floor because the directive left her no other instrument. [313] If understanding was the price of opening the seal, dreams were her only currency, however badly they spent. [331]

The dream came precise and playful, the way her dreams always were, exact as an engineer and teasing as a child. [352] It showed her a door, then hid the door behind her own hands. [365] It showed her sister walking backward out of the forest, laughing, stepping into footprints before they were made. [383] It showed the wafer seal unbroken, then broken, then unbroken, flickering like a coin deciding which face to wear. [402]

She woke to thunder directly overhead, vines slapping the door, the storm no longer approaching but arrived at the threshold. [422] And as always, the dream sat in her mind, sealed against meaning. [434]

Except. [435] She had spent her life waiting for hindsight to unlock her visions, treating interpretation as something events performed upon her. [455] But the dream had shown her sister stepping into footprints before they were made. [469] What if interpretation worked the same way? [476] What if she could act first and let the action become the meaning, make the hindsight by hand instead of waiting for the world to manufacture it? [503]

Her hands shook with grit, not grace, as she lifted the document. [515] She cracked the wafer seal. [520] Red flakes scattered like dried blood across the steel. [529]

The paper inside was blank. [534] And in the instant of seeing that blankness, hindsight finally arrived, flooding backward through the dream and lighting every image at once. [556] The door hidden behind her own hands: the answer was never written, only enacted. [570] The coin flickering: the directive decayed because it was meant to, shedding words until only the question remained, because the question was the directive. [594] Paths choose their walkers the way wounds choose their questions: they open for whoever cannot stop asking. [611] Her sister had been chosen by a different asking; this path had been hers all along, shaped to the exact silhouette of her grief. [635]

Something in her transcended then, quietly, without trumpets. [643] Not above the curse but through it: she would never see forward, but she could now walk forward and trust the meaning to catch up, the way thunder catches lightning. [673]

She wrote a new directive on the blank page in her own hand: WALKER, OPEN ONLY WHEN YOU UNDERSTAND WHY YOU WERE CHOSEN. [696] She folded it, melted a fresh wafer over candleflame, and pressed the seal home. [710] Let it decay. [713] Decay was how the question stayed sharp. [720]

Then she opened the bunker door into the full violence of the storm. [733] Rain hammered the vines silver; the forest roared like something rewilding itself all over again. [748] The path home was gone, as she had known it would be. [760] She stepped into the dark anyway, making footprints for some earlier self to dream of, and somewhere behind her, the meaning followed. [782]
