Marisol Veche had spent eleven years tracing where things came from, but the violin refused every archive she opened. [19] It lay in the velvet observatory at midsummer, transparent and cold, a violin carved entirely from ice crystal that did not melt. [41] The Empire of Thaal had paid her passage north and promised her a wing of the imperial library if she could authenticate it. [64] Their promise still glowed, the way fresh promises do, and Marisol knew from experience how quickly that light went out. [84] She had until it faded to decide what the violin was, and therefore whose. [98]

The observatory's dome was lined in worn purple velvet, and at midsummer the sun barely set, so the telescopes stood useless and the room belonged to plants. [125] Mulberry cuttings crowded every sill, left by some astronomer who had loved fruit more than stars. [141] Marisol heard them, which was the problem. [148] Three years ago a fever had rewired something in her, and ever since, the green world spoke through her skull in slow vegetable sentences. [172] She had told no one; provenance work demanded credibility, and credibility did not survive conversations with shrubs. [189]

The mulberries remembered the violin's maker. [195] They gave her this in images: a luthier with frostbitten hands, a woman who had carved the instrument from a glacier's heart for her daughter, who died before she could play it. [227] The luthier had buried her grief in the soil beneath a mulberry tree, and the roots had carried the story for sixty years, propagating it cutting by cutting, sill by sill, until it reached this dome. [263] No document could prove any of it. [270]

So on the seventh night, sleepless under the unsetting sun, Marisol turned to her other disreputable habit: she read futures in coffee stains. [293] Her grandmother had taught her: drink to the dregs, overturn the cup on the saucer, wait, and read what dries. [313] The stain that night was a ring full of holes, like a sieve, like lace. [328] Perforated destiny, her grandmother would have called it: a future not sealed but punctured, each hole a place where a person might still slip through and change things. [356] In one hole she saw the violin in a Thaalish vault, certified, catalogued, silent forever. [371] In another she saw it returned north, played at last over a grave, and herself stripped of her post, her name struck from every register she had spent a decade earning her way into. [405]

That was the weighing, then: a dream against an empire. [415] Not even her own dream. [420] A dead girl's, and a grieving mother's, held in trust by trees. [432] The ache of it settled in her chest, dark and sweet and staining, the way crushed mulberries stain, a hurt that would not wash out. [457]

The imperial courier was due at dawn, though dawn here was only a deepening of gold. [473] Marisol lifted the violin from its velvet cradle. [481] It was lighter than glass and colder than honesty. [490] Through the transparent body she could see the dome's mulberry leaves, warped and green, as if the instrument were already full of growing things. [514] Hurry, the cuttings murmured, in their patient way that was never hurried at all. [528] Before the promise loses its glow. [534]

They were right. [537] Once she signed the certificate of provenance, the empire's promise would be kept and therefore dead, and the holes in the stain would close. [561] A perforated destiny only stayed open while you still owed someone an answer. [574]

She wrote her report in the gold half-light. [582] Provenance: established. [584] Maker: a northern luthier, name recoverable through parish records she now knew where to find. [599] Rightful ownership: the maker's surviving kin, to whom the instrument must legally be returned before any sale. [616] It was the truest document she had ever produced, and it would end her career by noon. [633]

Before she sealed it, she did one more thing. [642] She took six mulberry cuttings from the sills, wrapped their stems in damp cloth, and packed them beside the violin in its traveling case. [666] Wherever the instrument went home to, the witnesses would go with it, to root and propagate and keep telling the story after every paper record was burned or lost. [695]

The courier arrived as the gold deepened. [702] Marisol handed over the report, watched his face change, and felt the empire's promise gutter and go dark inside her like a lamp. [725] The ache stayed, mulberry-dark, and she did not mind it. [735] Some stains were the proof that something real had been held. [746] Behind her, under the velvet dome, the remaining cuttings turned their leaves toward the case as it was carried out, saying goodbye in the only direction they knew: toward the light. [777]
