Mara kept the watch in the arbor, a living platform of grafted cedar limbs anchored to the mountain's last spire, where the clouds pooled silver below her and the stars wheeled close enough to read. [35] Every midnight she sought the patterns: how the cloud sea creased before a downdraft, how the high winds braided themselves into lanes a falling body could survive. [62] The couriers jumped from the night freighters on her signal alone, trusting a lamp they had never met. [80] That was the watcher's burden: to be believed completely, and to deserve it every single night. [96]

In her coat pocket she carried a parachute buckle, scorched along one edge, recovered from the slope eight years ago. [116] It had belonged to her brother Teo, who jumped on her signal the night a rogue shear rewrote the lanes faster than she could warn him. [142] The buckle had held; the canopy lines above it had not. [153] She kept it not as grief but as instruction. [162]

Tonight the radio had been all static since dusk, a gray hiss that filled the arbor like snow. [180] Then, just past midnight, the static cleared, and a voice fell through it. [193] "Watcher station, this is courier Ilan, already in the drop chute, repeat, already falling, freighter could not hold position." [212] Mara's stomach dropped with him. [217] He had jumped blind, on the old vector, the one she had charted at dusk before the storm cell drifted east and folded the second lane shut. [244] If he flew the course she had given him, he would meet the rock face below the eastern shoulder. [263] She had perhaps ninety seconds to revise a path she had sworn to him was safe. [279]

Here was the cruelty of the work: a correction, given wrongly, sounds exactly like panic, and a courier who hears panic stops trusting the light. [304] A courier who stops trusting the light improvises, and improvisation above this mountain was how families ended up with buckles instead of brothers. [327] So her voice stayed level while her hands moved fast. [337] "Ilan, I have you," she said. [343] "Hold your heading." [346] "I am refining your line." [351] Refining, not changing; the word mattered. [357]

Her eyes ran the cloud sea while her fingers found the signal lamp's shutter without looking. [373] Eight years of nights had stored the codes in her wrists, not her mind; she could not have recited the long-flash sequence for a westward drift, but her hand could send it asleep. [406] The trouble was that no code existed for what the storm had done. [419] The remaining lane bent twice, a shape the signal book had never imagined. [432] So she invented, attentively, the way Teo had taught her to splice rope: never cut what you can rejoin. [451] She took the westward-drift code Ilan already knew and doubled its interval, turning one familiar instruction into two staged turns. [471] A new signal built from an old one would read as guidance, not as a watcher losing her nerve. [490]

"When my light doubles, fly the drift twice," she said into the radio. [503] "Same turn you have always made." [509] "Just make it again." [513] A pause stretched, three heartbeats long. [519] "Copy, watcher," Ilan said, and his voice held steady, which meant hers had. [532]

She worked the shutter, and her wrist made the rhythm her memory could not name. [547] Long, long, dark; long, long, dark. [553] Below, a gray seam opened in the cloud sea, and a dark canopy slid through it, banked once, banked again, exactly where the bend demanded. [578] Then the clouds closed and took him, and the night went absolutely still. [591] There is a silence after a signal that behaves like sound, repeating itself inside the chest long after the lamp goes dark. [613] Mara stood inside that soundless echo and waited, because waiting was the half of the burden no one wrote into the signal book. [636]

The radio crackled once, then cleared again. [643] "Down safe in the lower meadow," Ilan said. [651] "Watcher, that second turn was not in my training." [660] "It will be," she said. [665]

When the channel closed, she opened the signal book and revised it by lamplight, adding the doubled-interval code in careful ink, so the next watcher's hands could learn what hers had improvised. [697] She practiced the new sequence twenty times on the cold shutter, until thought fell away and only the wrist remembered, because muscle is the one archive that does not panic. [727] Then she took out Teo's buckle and set it on the open page like a paperweight. [743] It had held, that night; the failure had been above it, in lines she had not thought to question. [762] Trust was the same kind of hardware, she thought: it does not break at the buckle, but in the connections nobody bothers to revise. [786] Above the clouds, the arbor creaked, and the watcher kept watching. [797]
