The anesthesia wore off the way floodwater recedes, leaving Mara stranded in her own body. [15] The surgeons had threaded sensors along her spine, and now she could feel the exact angle of every joint, the precise weight of her own tongue. [41] Proprioceptively enhanced, the consent forms had said, as if her body needed more ways to report on itself. [59] The recovery ward opened onto the forest, and the forest glowed. [70] After the war crimes tribunal, the new government had seeded these hills with engineered pines that drank ultraviolet light and bled it back at dusk, violet halos around black needles. [100] They called the place Penance: former interrogators served their sentences here, tending trees planted over the demolished detention site. [119] Mara had volunteered for the implants because she was a mathematician, and because the tribunal had asked her an impossible question. [140] How much mercy is owed, the chief justice had said, and to whom, and at what rate of decay? [159] Nobody had an equation for that. [165] Mara intended to find one. [170]

Her own data set lived in her nervous system. [179] Eleven months in the white room had taught her body a vocabulary her mind refused to speak: the flinch at fluorescent hum, the locked breath when doors latched, the way her shoulders climbed toward her ears at the smell of wet canvas. [221] She could not remember the interrogations in words, but the implants could now measure what her body remembered for her, in milliseconds and millimeters. [245] Trauma as instrument; flinch as datum. [251] If she could quantify what was taken, she reasoned, she could quantify what was owed back. [267]

On the third evening she walked into the violet trees, the strap in her coat pocket. [283] It was pewter-buckled, cut from the sensory deprivation mask they had cinched over her face, the only artifact she had claimed from the evidence locker. [308] She carried it the way other people carried rosaries, thumbing the cold metal to remind herself which century she was standing in. [330] The forest disoriented her beautifully. [335] Light pooled in the needle canopy and dripped down trunks in slow indigo veins, and her new senses mapped every root beneath her boots before she stepped. [362] She felt displaced and rooted at once, a stranger whose feet nonetheless knew this ground, because this ground had once been the floor of the white room. [389]

Then a man stepped out from between the pines, carrying a pruning saw, wearing the gray coverall of the sentenced. [409] Her body recognized him before she did. [416] Her knees braced, her weight dropped low and back, hands rising, the ancient mathematics of an animal preparing for the worst. [437] The implants recorded it all: a 340-millisecond response, cortisol spike, pupils flooding. [449] Eleven months condensed into one number. [455] He had been the one who buckled the mask. [464] She knew it not from his face, which she had never been allowed to see, but from the sound of his breathing, which her body had filed away like a fingerprint. [495]

He saw her stance and understood. [501] He set the saw down in the violet needles and knelt, slowly, hands open on his thighs. [518] "I water the trees over the cell blocks," he said. [528] "Yours was under the tallest one." [534]

Mara's pulse hammered, and the implants narrated it, and somewhere beneath the terror a calmer voice began taking notes. [553] Here was the missing variable. [558] Mercy could not be computed from harm alone; it required a second body in the equation, one willing to kneel. [578]

She took the pewter strap from her pocket. [586] Her hand shook; the sensors gave her the tremor's exact frequency, and the number steadied her, because a measured thing is a survivable thing. [610] "Do you know what this is?" she asked. [618] He looked at the strap and his own body answered: a flinch, shoulders curling inward, the same grammar of remembering she carried. [640] "I buckled it forty-one times," he said. [647] "I counted. [649] Counting was how I pretended it was procedure and not cruelty." [660]

Forty-one, she thought. [663] She had not known the number, but her body had; her flinches had always come in clusters, in waves she could never explain. [686] Now the data had a denominator. [692]

She did not forgive him; the equation was not finished, and false mercy was just another kind of lie. [711] But she hung the strap on a low branch of the tallest pine, where the ultraviolet sap would tarnish the pewter season by season, a variable left in the open to decay at its own measurable rate. [748] "Keep watering," she said, and her knees unlocked. [756]

Walking back, she felt the forest hold her weight, root by counted root. [769] Mercy, she wrote that night, is the integral of harm acknowledged over time remaining, and the proof would take the rest of her life, and that was acceptable. [797]
