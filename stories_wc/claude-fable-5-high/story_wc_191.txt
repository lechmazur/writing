Vessa was the last lore keeper of Halcyon Spire, a city that remembered the future better than it lived it. [20] Holograms still flickered in the plazas, looping coronations that had never happened. [32] For two hundred years the throne above the city had stood unclaimed, because no one could find the way up to it. [54] The Stairway of Ascension had been hidden by the last monarch, who trusted no heir of blood, only an heir of memory. [76] Vessa kept that memory in a sliver of crystal: a ruby flake, thin as a fingernail, etched with the monarch's final archive. [98] Tonight the flake had finally yielded its cipher to her old reader, and the message was brief. [115] Follow the water that wears every color; the stair sleeps under paint; you have until sunrise, when the archive burns itself clean. [137] A wiser keeper might have filed the warning and waited. [147] But Vessa had always been wisely adventurous, the kind of scholar who tested doors instead of cataloguing them, who packed rope alongside her annotations. [171] She chose to sally out at midnight, slipping past the curfew drones with the flake sealed in a locket at her throat. [193] The water that wore every color could only be the falls beyond the old transit fields, where coolant runoff and mineral light made the cascade shimmer like a kaleidoscope. [222] She reached it before the second moon set, mist beading on her coat like static. [237] Behind the falling spectrum she found a seam of dry air, and through it, impossibly, an orchard. [254] Trees grown from preservation-era rootstock stood in patient rows, their fruit glowing faintly with bioluminescent skins, tended by no one for two centuries and thriving anyway. [280] It smelled like the past she had only read about: pollen, rain, real soil. [294] At the orchard's heart stood a maintenance shed, its walls slathered in generations of municipal gray. [310] The stair sleeps under paint. [315] Vessa drew her conservation kit, the same solvents she used to rescue water-damaged manuscripts, and began to work. [333] Through the peel of paint layers, hidden murals surfaced like memories returning. [345] The first layer revealed the monarch planting the orchard. [354] The second showed citizens climbing a spiral of light. [363] The third showed the throne itself, empty, with an inscription beneath: not for the strongest, but for the one who remembers why we built anything at all. [390] Each mural held a glyph; together they formed a sequence. [400] Her hands shook, not from fear but from the vertigo of touching a message meant for someone exactly like her. [420] The eastern sky was already softening toward gray. [428] She had until sunrise, and the solvent worked slowly. [437] The final layer resisted, lacquered hard, and she scraped it free with the edge of the locket itself, the ruby flake's housing biting into industrial enamel. [463] Beneath lay a painted doorframe and a shallow socket, faceted, exactly the size of the flake. [479] Of course. [481] The cipher was never the message; the flake itself was the key. [493] Vessa pried it from the locket and pressed it into the wall. [505] The murals lit from within, every glyph kindling in sequence, and the shed's back wall dissolved into a column of pale steps spiraling upward through the rock behind the falls. [535] The Stairway of Ascension hummed with old power, smelling faintly of ozone and apple blossom. [550] She climbed as the orchard brightened below her, the kaleidoscopic falls scattering dawn into a thousand small rainbows. [568] At the top, the throne waited on an open terrace above the city: a plain chair of woven alloy, humbler than any hologram had promised. [593] Vessa stood before it as the sun broke the horizon. [603] Below, the looping coronations in the plazas flickered, stuttered, and went dark, the false futures finally erased. [620] She did not sit immediately. [625] A keeper's first instinct was to record, so she noted the date, the murals, the orchard, the way the light fell. [646] Then she thought of the inscription: the one who remembers why we built anything at all. [662] She remembered. [664] Not the coronations, but the orchard; not the spire, but the hands that had planted rootstock behind a waterfall so something living would outlast the machines. [690] Vessa sat, and the city below woke to a throne no longer unclaimed, held by its keeper of lore. [709] Her first decree, drafted before her tea cooled, opened the orchard to everyone. [722] The stairway, she ordered, would never be painted over again. [732]
