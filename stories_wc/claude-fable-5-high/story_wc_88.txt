After the procedure, Marek expected silence in his head, and he got it. [13] What he had not expected was the silence everywhere else: in faces, in music, in the eyes of the dog next door. [35] The clinic had promised a clean slate, and a clean slate was what they scraped him down to. [53] Whatever he had done before, whatever debts of cruelty he had run up, were gone, along with the part of him that could care that they were gone. [81] They let him keep one object: a baseball, scuffed and yellowed, signed in looping ink by someone named Ondrej Vance, dated thirty-one years from now. [106] The intake nurse had laughed at the date and called it a printing error. [120] Marek turned the ball in his hands every night, delicately robust, its stitches tight as sutures, and felt nothing, which was the problem. [143]

The newspapers found him three weeks later. [150] Someone left the Herald folded on his stoop each morning, and at first he read it like anyone reads anything, flatly, dutifully. [172] Then he noticed the spacing. [177] Between certain lines of the obituaries, the leading widened by a hair, and if you read only those gaps, ran your eye along the white instead of the black, words assembled themselves. [209] BRING THE BALL, they said; TAKE THE AQUEDUCT STAIRS; WHEN THE TUNNELS BRANCH, CHOOSE WHAT YOU CANNOT JUSTIFY. [227] A man with nothing inside him has no reason to refuse a riddle, so Marek went. [243]

The aqueduct stairs descended past the city's plumbing into older stone, and the journey became a labyrinth almost immediately. [262] Every junction offered two mouths of dark, and each was labeled in chalk. [275] Not with directions, but with choices: THE LIE THAT SAVED YOU and THE TRUTH THAT DIDN'T; THE MONEY and THE GIRL; WHAT YOU DID and WHAT YOU TOLD YOURSELF. [304] He could not remember any of it, but his body could; his pulse climbed at certain words like a dog hearing its old name. [328] He chose against his pulse every time, took the tunnel he could not justify, and the labyrinth let him through. [348]

The last passage opened onto a lake. [355] He stood at its rim and felt the closest thing to feeling he had managed in weeks: jaded wonder, the awe of a man who knows he is being played and is glad anyway. [389] The water glowed from beneath, blue-green and breathing, lit by colonies of something living that pulsed brighter when he moved. [409] A woman sat on a flat stone at the shore, folding a newspaper into smaller and smaller squares. [427]

"You read between the lines," she said. [434] "Most of you don't." [438]

"Most of me?" [441]

"I've sent that message to every version of you the procedure produced," she said. [455] "In every branch where you chose the wipe. [463] You're the ninth to make it down." [470]

He held out the baseball. [475]

"Ondrej Vance," she said, taking it, "is who you become in thirty-one years if this works." [491] "He signed it the day he stopped being you, and he sent me back to deliver it to a man who hadn't ruined anyone yet." [516] "You kept it through everything you did. [523] Through the procedure, when you let them take the rest." [533]

"Why a lake?" he asked, because it was easier than asking why him. [546]

"I kindle empathy where none remains," she said, nodding at the glow. [558] "The organisms answer movement. [562] They light up for each other. [568] The smallest things down here respond to a neighbor's stirring, and you, a whole grown man, can't." [585]

"The wipe doesn't remove empathy," she said. [592] "You burned that out yourself, years before, one justification at a time. [604] The clinic just took the evidence." [610]

She pressed the ball back into his hands. [618] "The labyrinth was the treatment. [623] Eight of you chose the comfortable tunnels and walked in circles until they surfaced, unchanged. [638] You chose against yourself forty times. [644] That's not memory. [647] That's practice." [649]

Marek looked at the ball, its delicately robust seams holding two halves of hide together over an empty core, and something in his chest lit, faint as the water. [678] Not a memory. [681] An answer to movement. [685]

"What does Vance want from me?" he asked. [693]

"Nothing from you. [696] Everything for you. [699] He wants the man who walks out of here to be someone he doesn't have to apologize for." [717]

She stood, dropped her folded newspaper into the lake, and the water flared around it like applause. [734]

Marek climbed back through the labyrinth, and at every junction he stopped and read both chalked choices aloud, letting his pulse rise, letting it hurt. [759] On the surface, the morning Herald lay on his stoop, ordinary, its lines evenly spaced. [774] He read it anyway, all of it, the obituaries especially, and for the first time the names belonged to people. [794]
