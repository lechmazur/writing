They burn spies the way frost burns leaves: quietly, completely, and without apology. [13] Mara Voss had been burned in November, her name leaked, her networks folded, her usefulness declared extinct by men who never learned to spell it. [38] So she wintered on Zephyr Bluff, a shoulder of rock above the gray strait, where mist came up the cliff face like breath from something sleeping. [64] The world there kept a silver hush, all sound wrapped in wool, and she told herself she liked it. [83] She did not like it. [88] What she liked was the engraved pillbox on her windowsill, silver gone soft with handling, its lid cut with a compass rose and four words: FIX WHAT YOU BREAK. [117] It had belonged to Anton, her asset in Tallinn, the man her leaked name had left exposed; he had pressed it into her hand on the night she failed to get him out. [150] Inside it she kept nothing, because nothing was what she had managed to save. [164]

Then, at the tilt of the earth toward spring, the snow began to speak. [178] She found the first message on the eastern path: a line of bootprints stamped into accumulated snow, their spacing irregular in a way no honest walker's would be. [206] Long step, short step, long, long, short: the old field cipher she and Anton had invented for streets too watched for paper. [228] She read it three times before she let herself believe it. [239] It said: ALIVE, NORTH STAIR, THURSDAY. [245] Anton was supposed to be in a prison hospital or a grave; either way, the snow was claiming otherwise. [264]

It could be a trap, of course; burned spies make tidy bait, and the people who burned her would enjoy the symmetry. [286] Mara had always been prudently daring, the kind of officer who jumped, but only after measuring the drop. [304] So she measured. [307] For two days she walked the bluff like a tourist, mapping sightlines, counting the fishing boats, learning which lights in the village below went out and which only pretended to. [337] On the second night she answered in kind, walking her own coded steps along the cliff path where fresh snowfall would hold them, spelling the one question only Anton could answer. [368] By morning the mist had pearled every print, and by afternoon a reply lay stitched across the drift below the lighthouse: GREEN, AND YOU PAINTED IT. [394] No interrogator could have pulled that from him; it was theirs alone, a joke from a safe house in Narva. [414]

She stood in the silver hush with her heart going like a wire in wind. [429] She had spent the winter wondering not whether she was brave but where the bravery had gone, the unspent kind, the courage she had been saving for a moment that never quite arrived. [462] She had come to the bluff, she understood now, to find where unused courage waits; apparently it waits in snow, in step-codes, in the patience of a man she owed a life. [494]

Thursday came wrapped in fog so thick the lighthouse beam looked like a scarf being pulled through milk. [512] She took the north stair down the cliff, one hand on wet rock, the pillbox riding in her coat pocket like a small cold heart. [537] He was thinner, grayer, listing on a cane, but his eyes still did their old arithmetic when they found her. [557] "You kept it," he said, nodding at her pocket, somehow knowing. [568] "I kept it empty," she said. [574] Then he vouchsafed, in a voice rubbed thin by prison, the thing he had crossed a border to deliver: the name of the officer who had sold them both, and the proof, sewn into the lining of his coat. [613] "I am not here for revenge," he said. [621] "I am here because broken things bother me, and we are broken, and so is the service that broke us." [641]

Fixing what is broken, Mara thought, begins with admitting which pieces are yours. [654] She took out the pillbox and opened it, and Anton rolled the microfilm tight and set it inside, where her failure had lived all winter. [679] The lid clicked shut over its engraved command, no longer an accusation but a route. [694] They climbed the stair together as the mist began to lift off Zephyr Bluff, the strait turning the pale color of a blade being drawn slowly. [720] Behind them, the tide reached the lowest drifts and started erasing their conversation, word by careful word. [737] Spring would take the rest of the snow, and with it the last evidence that two ruined people had stood on a cliff and decided to be useful again. [766] Mara felt the pillbox knock against her ribs as she walked, full at last, and her unused courage rose to meet it like water finding a level. [793]
