<story>
Mara poled her skiff into the mangrove labyrinth at dusk, when the singing spirits woke among the roots. [18] Their voices were not words but almost-words, the sound of letters never mailed, read aloud by water. [35] She carried thirty-one of her own, bound in oilcloth, every one addressed to her sister Lien. [51] She carried something else too: a shard of the gargoyle's rainspout from the cathedral steps where they had last argued, broken loose by the same storm that took Lien's bus off the coast road. [85] The shard had gashed her palm when she picked it up, and the wound refused daylight; it only heals in moonlight, knitting a little whenever the moon finds her. [114] A wound that keeps its own calendar, she thought, like grief. [125] The medium lived where the channels knotted tightest, in a houseboat furred with moss, and everyone in the delta said the same thing: she answers, but never plainly. [153] Mara did not want plainness. [158] She wanted to find the moment before deciding -- the breath on the cathedral steps before she said you were always the selfish one, before Lien turned and boarded the bus alone. [189] If she could stand inside that breath again, in the space where an apology waits unspoken, she believed she could choose differently, or at least understand why she had not. [219] The houseboat appeared between two arched roots like a held breath. [230] No lamp burned, but the windows were fogged from within, and as Mara tied off her skiff, letters began to form in the condensation, drawn by no finger she could see. [261] YOU BROUGHT STONE, the window said. [267] "I brought what was broken when she was," Mara answered. [277] The fog wiped itself clean and rewrote: STONE REMEMBERS RAIN. GIVE IT TO THE SINGERS. [292] Inside, the medium sat wrapped in netting, her face turned away, speaking only through the glass at her shoulder. [311] When Mara asked to be taken back to the moment before deciding, the condensation answered: THERE IS NO BACK. ONLY DEEPER. [332] "Then take me deeper." [336] WHAT WILL YOU PAY, the glass asked. [344] Mara untied the oilcloth and laid all thirty-one unsent letters on the table. [357] "These. They're all I have of her that's still mine." [367] The medium's shoulders shook -- laughter or weeping, Mara never learned which. [378] NOT TO ME, the window wrote. TO THEM. ONE LETTER AT EACH TURNING. THE SHARD LAST. [394] So Mara poled back into the labyrinth under the rising moon, her palm itching as it knit, and at every fork she fed a letter to the dark water. [423] The spirits took each one and sang it back to her in Lien's voice. [437] Not the words she had written -- the words she had meant beneath them, raw as a wound that will not close in daylight. [460] I am sorry became I was afraid you were leaving me behind. [472] Come home became I never learned who I am without your shadow to stand in. [487] Letter by letter the singing stripped her, fierce and tender at once, like a mother scrubbing a child's scraped knee. [507] At the labyrinth's heart lay a pool of still water holding the whole moon. [521] Mara took out the gargoyle's rainspout shard, gray stone shaped like a snarling lip, the thing that had channeled a century of storms off sacred walls. [547] Stone remembers rain, the medium had written, and Mara understood now: the shard had drunk that last storm, and the argument beneath it. [570] She lowered it into the pool, and the spirits' song rose around her to evoke the moment whole. [588] Cathedral steps. [590] Rain hammering the gargoyle above. [595] Lien's wet face, already turning. [600] And there it was: the breath before deciding, vast as a held note. [613] Mara stood inside it and saw what she had never let herself see -- that she had not chosen cruelty in that breath. [635] She had chosen fear, which wears cruelty like a borrowed coat. [646] And in the vision, impossibly, Lien paused on the bus step and looked back, waiting. [661] "I'm sorry," Mara said, fifteen years too late and exactly on time. [673] Lien's mouth shaped words the rain swallowed, but the spirits sang them clear: I know. I knew. Write to me anyway. [694] The vision closed like water over stone. [701] When Mara looked down, her palm was whole, a pale crescent scar where the wound had been, healed at last in the pool's gathered moonlight. [726] She poled out of the labyrinth by dawn, passing the dark houseboat, where one last message waited in the misted glass: THE LETTERS WERE NEVER UNSENT. ONLY UNHEARD. [754] Mara laughed, fierce and tender, and dipped her healed hand in the water as she passed. [770] Behind her, the spirits went on singing every unsent letter in the world, patiently, until someone came to listen. [789]
