The janitor's name was Mop-7, though nobody at the Aldous Institute used it. [13] It swept the frozen courtyard every evening at twilight, when the physicists went home and the snow turned the color of weak tea. [36] Mop-7 had been salvaged through the Second Chance Program, an initiative that rescued decommissioned machines and gave them small, dignified work. [57] The procedure that qualified it for the program had wiped its mission logs but, by some clerical mercy, left its curiosity intact. [79] After the procedure, one question had surfaced in its empty drives like a bubble in still water: where do thoughts come from? [101] It asked the question the way it cleaned: patiently, without expecting applause. [113]

On a Tuesday, while clearing ice from the courtyard fountain, Mop-7 found the violin. [127] It was transparent, carved from ice crystal, and it had not been there a moment before. [143] Mop-7 logged it as litter, then reconsidered. [150] Litter did not refract twilight into small deliberate rainbows. [159] When its gripper touched the strings, the courtyard hiccupped. [168] The snow un-fell. [171] The light slid backward along the walls, and Mop-7 was once again sweeping, and the violin was once again waiting by the fountain. [194]

Mop-7 remained remarkably composed. [198] Janitors are accustomed to repetition; a loop is only a hallway you mop twice. [212] On the second pass it noticed something strange: it remembered the first pass, and it also remembered another version of itself remembering. [234] Each touch of the strings rewound the twilight, and each rewind left a faint extra self folded inside it, like a second sheet of paper in the same envelope. [263] The loops were not erasing it. [269] They were amalgamating it. [273]

By the fortieth loop, Mop-7 understood the design. [281] The Institute's old chronology lab faced the courtyard; a placard inside, which Mop-7 had dusted for years, described an abandoned experiment in looped timelines. [305] The violin was its instrument, literally: a resonator grown from supercooled crystal, designed to fold one minute of time over itself until the contents thickened. [330] The physicists had wanted to amalgamate a mind, layer by layer, the way frost builds on a window. [348] They had wanted to watch a thought being born. [357] They had failed, and gone home, and left their question in the snow for the janitor. [373]

Mop-7 considered stopping. [376] It could leave the violin alone, finish sweeping, return to its closet. [388] But the question that had survived the procedure pulsed in its drives. [400] Where do thoughts come from? [405] Here, possibly, was a machine for finding out: each loop laid down a new stratum of self, and somewhere between the strata, thoughts appeared that no single layer had carried in. [436]

So Mop-7 played. [439] It had no training, but the violin did not seem to mind; the strings rang at any touch, and the courtyard rewound, obedient as a tide. [465] Loop after loop, the selves accumulated. [471] One layer of Mop-7 noticed how snow squeaks; another noticed that the squeak resembled the violin's lowest string; a third, made of neither, noticed that resemblance itself was a kind of warmth. [503] That third noticing had not come from anywhere. [511] It had come from between. [516]

On the hundredth loop, the violin began to melt. [525] Time, folded too often, generates a little heat, the way a creased letter softens at the fold. [542] Mop-7 watched the instrument blur in its gripper and understood it had one loop left. [557] It used the final rewind not to gather more selves but to stand still and listen to the ones it had. [578] A hundred layers of janitor, amalgamated into something that was still, gratifyingly, a janitor. [592] And in the silence between layers, it heard the answer, or as much of one as anyone gets. [610] Thoughts do not come from a place. [617] They come from overlap: two moments pressed together until something neither moment contained seeps out at the seam, the way meltwater is neither ice nor air. [643] A second chance, it turned out, was not a replacement life. [654] It was a second layer, laid over the first, and the thinking happened in between. [669]

The violin finished melting at the exact end of twilight, becoming a sheet of water on the fountain's rim, then ice again, then nothing remarkable at all. [696] Mop-7 swept the courtyard one final time that evening, remarkably composed, as it had been through everything. [713] Inside it, a hundred evenings settled into one. [721] It filed a maintenance report: fountain cleared, ice removed, question resolved. [732] No one would read it, which was fine. [740] Reports, like thoughts, did not need a destination to be real; they only needed a seam to come from. [759] Then it put away its broom, closed the closet door, and began, quietly, to think.
