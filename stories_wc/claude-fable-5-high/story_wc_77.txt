After the resuscitation, Mara glowed. [5] Not metaphorically: a faint, sour-green luminance clung to her skin, a residue of the seven minutes she had spent dead on the riverbank, and it would not wash off. [34] Worse was the curse the light carried. [41] Every photograph taken of her since developed wrong: her face replaced by a smear of teeth, a crowding darkness with too many elbows. [64] She was photographically cursed, and the thing in the pictures was getting closer to the lens each time. [82]

The whispering advisor came with the glow. [89] She never saw him; he lived somewhere behind her left ear, a voice like wet paper, and he claimed he had followed her back from the river because she had left the door open. [123] "The thing in your photographs is Fear itself," he whispered. [133] "It tasted you while you were dead, and now it is coming to finish the meal." [149]

"Then I will trick it," Mara said. [156] "Fear feeds on confession; everyone unbosoms themselves to it eventually. [166] So I will unbosom lies dressed as truths, and watch what it cannot digest." [180] Whatever Fear choked on would be its weakness. [188]

The advisor laughed, a sound like flies in a jar, and told her where Fear nested: the funhouse of the abandoned Carnival Mirabelle, shuttered thirty years since a hall-of-mirrors fire. [218]

She prepared her weapon: her grandmother's dream dictionary, a fat Victorian volume that defined every symbol a sleeper might meet. [238] In its margins she wrote paradoxical notes, confessions that devoured themselves. [249] Beside DROWNING she wrote: I fear the water that saved me. [260] Beside LIGHT: I confess I am darkest when I glow. [270] Beside MIRROR: everything I admit here is a lie, including this. [281] She fastened the book shut with the only clasp strong enough: the silver clasp from her grandmother's opera cloak, shaped like two hands refusing to let go. [308] Her grandmother had worn it the night she sang at the carnival, the night of the fire; it had survived when she had not. [332]

The funhouse at midnight was repulsive and gorgeous at once, like rot blooming into orchids. [347] Its clown-mouth entrance had peeled into something almost tender, paint hanging in soft ribbons like sloughed skin. [364] Inside, the warped mirrors had melted into pearly drips that caught her green luminance and threw it back in long, slow shimmers; the corridor glowed wherever she passed, and kept glowing after she had gone, a trail of lingering luminance that mapped her whole path through the dark. [412] It was beautiful, and it made her gag; everything here smelled of burnt sugar and old smoke, sweetness gone carnivorous. [432]

"It is watching," the advisor whispered, trembling. [439] "Begin." [440]

Mara knelt in the mirror maze, opened the clasp, and read her confessions aloud. [454] The darkness leaned in, hungry, taking each false truth into itself. [465] She felt it swallow the drowning, the light, the lie about lies; and on that last one it shuddered, gagged, recoiled. [486] Fear could digest any terror, any shame, but not a paradox; it could not be afraid of a thing that both was and was not. [511] Certainty was its stomach; contradiction was poison. [518]

The mirrors filled then with the face from her photographs, enormous, all teeth and elbows, and it was hideous, and she could not look away; it had the awful pull of a wound you want to touch. [555] "Last confession," Mara said, and unbosomed her one real secret wrapped in its own denial: "I came back from the river because I wanted to die, and I wanted to die because I came back." [590] Fear lunged for the truth in it and bit down on the contradiction, and the loop closed around it like a trap of teeth. [614] She slammed the dictionary shut and snapped the silver hands of the opera cloak clasp together, locking Fear inside a book of definitions, the one place a thing without definition cannot survive. [646]

The whispering behind her ear went silent, and she understood at last who the advisor had been: Fear's own tongue, scouting ahead, certain she would fail. [672] It had told her where the nest was because Fear cannot imagine being tricked; that certainty was the weakness all along. [693]

She walked out through the clown's mouth, following her own glowing footprints backward through the maze. [709] Behind her, the lingering luminance faded slowly from the mirrors, like a stage light dimming after the final aria. [728] At dawn she photographed herself against the funhouse gate. [737] The picture developed clean: just a tired woman, faintly green, holding a locked book, smiling like someone who had finally finished dying. [759]
