In the village of Hollowmere, no one had made anything beautiful for nine years. [14] The potter's wheels stood still, the weaver's loom held a song half-finished, and the children drew only straight gray lines. [34] Only Vesna, the moonstruck dreamer who slept by day and wandered by night, still believed the silence could be broken. [54] She had one strange gift: she heard the unspoken words people carried, the sentences caught behind their teeth. [72] Walking past the potter, she heard him think, I have forgotten what my hands were for. [88] Past the weaver: The colors left me, and I never said goodbye. [100] Their longing pressed against her ribs like a second heartbeat, and she could not rest until she answered it. [119]

Her grandmother had left her a single inheritance: an astrolabe of black brass, its rings etched with constellations no chart had ever named. [142] Folded inside its case was a note in fading ink: We are remembered stardust, child. [157] What the sky forgets, we lose; what it remembers, we become. [168] Vesna decided the sky had forgotten Hollowmere, and that someone must remind it. [181]

At the edge of the village stood an ancient stone archway, the last bone of a temple whose name had eroded with its walls. [205] On winter nights the aurora poured through it like dye through water, green and violet, so the arch seemed a doorway lit from the other side. [231] There Vesna began her work. [236] She raised the astrolabe and waited for falling stars, and when one streaked overhead she turned the rings to trace its path, letting the needle write the arc into the brass. [267] Each traced fall was a syllable, she felt sure, in some sentence the sky was trying to finish. [285]

She did not count the nights. [291] She kept no calendar, marked no moons; the work unfolded across an unwritten calendar of its own, measured only in meteors caught and arcs recorded. [316] Time in Hollowmere had stopped meaning anything anyway; a village without making is a village without days. [333]

Something changed in her as the tracings accumulated. [341] A dark joy took root, a shadowed rapture: she was exhausted, hollow-eyed, half-frozen, yet every falling star felt like a word whispered directly to her, and she could not stop listening. [372] The unspoken words of the village grew louder in her too, until the potter's grief and the weaver's mourning braided themselves into her own pulse. [397] She understood then what she was doing: their creativity was not dead but trapped, sealed inside them like light inside stone, and the sky's forgetting was the seal. [425] Every arc she traced was a key turning. [433]

On the night the last ring of the astrolabe filled, a storm of meteors fell, more than she could follow. [453] She chased them with the needle until her fingers bled and the brass burned cold, and still the sky poured down its bright handwriting. [477] Then, beneath the archway, she heard a new unspoken voice, vaster than any villager's, and it said only: Rest. [496] She almost refused. [499] Rest felt like surrender, like one more silence in a village drowning in silences. [513] But the voice was patient, and she finally understood: the tracing was finished, and a key, once turned, must be released. [534] Her grasping was the last lock. [540]

Vesna set the astrolabe down on the temple stone, lay beneath the aurora-lit arch, and let herself rest for the first time in uncounted nights. [565] As she slept, the arcs etched in the brass rose off the instrument like smoke, threading through the archway into the green-violet light. [588] The sky read them, and the sky remembered. [596] It remembered that the potter's hands were made of the same dust as nebulae, that the weaver's colors had been borrowed from dying suns, that every soul in Hollowmere was stardust that had simply forgotten its first fire. [634]

Vesna woke at dawn to sounds she had never heard: the creak of a wheel turning, the rhythm of a loom, a child singing nonsense to a drawing of impossible birds. [665] Walking home, she listened for the unspoken words and found them changed. [677] The potter's silent voice now said, My hands remember. [686] The weaver's: The colors came back, and they brought friends. [696]

The astrolabe lay quiet under the arch, its rings blank again, waiting for some future forgetting. [712] Vesna kept it oiled and kept her vigil lightly now, sleeping when sleep came, dreaming when dreams did. [730] She had learned the village's secret and her own: that making is remembering, that rest is part of the key, and that even the dust of dead stars can be coaxed to shine, if someone moonstruck enough is willing to trace the way home. [774]
