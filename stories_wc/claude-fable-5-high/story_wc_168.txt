The moonlit ridge path unspooled ahead of Iris like a pale rope thrown across the dark. [16] She climbed it the way she did everything now, slowly, on the bad ankle, with the cracked porcelain box pressed against her ribs. [39] Behind her, the rookie kept his distance, boots loud, breath louder. [50] His name was Dane, and he had frozen on the Miller Creek line in August, and nobody on the crew would say it to his face. [76] Iris had said it, gently, and then told him to come walk with her on the night of the eclipse. [96] Above them the slow dance of celestial bodies had already begun, Earth's shadow taking its first careful bite of the moon. [117]

"Ten years ago tonight, this whole ridge was burning," she said. [128] "Same eclipse. [130] The crews joked that even the moon wanted to look away." [141] Dane said nothing, which she counted as a kind of wisdom. [152] The scar on her neck pulled tight in the cold, the old map of Hollow Creek written in her skin. [172]

At the switchback she stopped where two firs had survived, black-barked and stubborn, and sat on the stone between them. [192] "You're going to tell me a story," Dane said, wary. [202] "No," Iris said. [205] "I'm going to think out loud, and you're going to decide what it means." [219]

She set the porcelain box on her knee. [227] The crack ran through a painted wren, splitting the bird's wing from its body. [241] Her hands, ruined and rebuilt by three surgeries, were still gently skilled; she opened the lid without a sound. [260] Inside lay a child's barrette, half-melted, shaped like a star. [270]

"Her name was Sophie," Iris said. [276] "Eight years old. [279] Her father's cabin sat below this ridge." [286] She told it plainly, the way fire reports are written, because the plainness was the only container strong enough. [305] The wind had shifted; the crown fire jumped the line; she'd had time to carry one person out of the smoke, and the father, pinned under a fallen beam, had looked at her and said, take her. [342] So she had. [345] Sophie had clutched her mother's porcelain box the whole way down, and it cracked when Iris fell on the scree, shielding the girl with her own burning back. [373]

"In the ambulance she asked me if her dad got out," Iris said. [386] "And I said yes." [390] Dane went very still. [394] "I bought her one more night of believing the world was kind," Iris said. [408] "The price was that I stopped believing it myself. [417] Bargained innocence. [419] Hers for mine. [422] I have never once regretted the trade, and it has never once stopped hurting. [436] Both of those things are true. [442] That is the whole lesson." [447]

The moon had gone copper now, a coal banked in the sky's hearth. [460] "Why tell me?" Dane asked. [465] "I'm the guy who froze." [470] "Because you've been treating your fear like it's a private disease," she said. [483] "Pain you keep locked up stays foreign, even to you. [493] Pain you examine, turn over, speak aloud, that becomes a language everybody alive already knows. [508] Translated pain is a universal language. [514] I came up here every eclipse for ten years to learn the grammar." [527]

She did not try to enlighten him with speeches. [536] She only sat, reflecting deeply, letting the silence do the heavier teaching, and somewhere in that long copper darkness Dane started to talk. [559] He talked about the wall of heat at Miller Creek, about his legs refusing him, about the shame he'd been carrying like a second pack. [584] Iris listened the way she bandaged: firm pressure, no flinching. [594] Hard softness, the old captain had called it, the only temperament that survives this work. [609]

When he finished, the moon was crawling back out of the shadow, edge by silver edge. [625] "Sophie's twenty-three now," Iris said. [630] "She writes to me. [634] She knows the truth; I told her when she was twelve, and she forgave me before I finished the sentence." [654] She closed the box and held it out. [662] "She asked me to leave this up here someday, with somebody who needed it. [676] Cracked things still hold what matters." [682]

Dane took it with both hands, careful as a man receiving a sacrament. [695] Then he set it on the stone between the firs, where the moonlight could find it. [711] "I froze," he said again, but differently now, a fact instead of a verdict. [725] "You did," she agreed. [729] "And next season you won't, because tonight you learned the words for it." [742]

They started down the ridge path together, the restored moon laying their two shadows side by side, one limping, one steadier than it had climbed. [767] Behind them, between the two black firs, the wren on the box waited out the dark, its broken wing still painted mid-flight. [789]
