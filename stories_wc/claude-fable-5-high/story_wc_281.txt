The recall notice arrived the same night as the static shock, and Theo felt the whole network of borrowed minds go dark at once. [24] Juna found him on the floor of his light painting studio, surrounded by tripods and unlit LED wands, holding his head as if it were an emptied room. [52] She had never taken Veris, the telepathy pill everyone swore would end loneliness forever. [66] She was a skeptic the way some people are gardeners, gently, daily, with dirt under her nails, and she loved Theo too much to pretend otherwise now. [93] "I told you a pill could not carry a person," she said, kneeling beside him, "but I never wanted to be right like this." [117] The manufacturer's message had been clinical: neural scarring, immediate discontinuation, refunds pending. [129] What it could not refund was the year Theo had spent feeling his collaborators' moods bloom inside him like weather, painting with light while strangers' joy steadied his hands. [158] Now the silence in his skull had texture, he said, like static that had stopped but left its shape behind. [178]

Juna stayed the night, and in the morning she emptied her coat pocket onto his workbench: a muscovite geode sliver, thin as a fingernail, that she had carried since graduate school. [209] Held to the studio's one bare bulb, the mica split the light into layered sheets, each sheet containing smaller sheets, fractally precise all the way down. [235] "Look," she said. [238] "The light is not gone when it splits. [246] It just travels paths you have to learn to see." [256] Theo laughed for the first time in days, a small wet sound. [268]

Juna was not done. [272] She had spent her career studying somatic markers, the body's quiet telegraph: the flutter in the gut before a decision, the warmth in the chest before a word. [300] Veris had bypassed all of it, mainlining thought to thought, and that, she suspected, was why losing it felt like amputation. [321] The pathways were not destroyed; they were hidden, unused, dark. [331] She wanted to illuminate them. [336]

So together they devised an exercise in the only language they both trusted: light. [350] Theo would stand in the dark studio with a wand in each hand while Juna's camera held its shutter open. [370] Whenever his body spoke, a tightening throat, a prickle along the forearms, a heartbeat skipping toward grief, he was to trace it in the air, giving each marker its own color and gesture. [403] The first photographs were chaos, scribbles of panic in red. [413] But Juna taped each print to the wall anyway, and slowly a vocabulary emerged. [427] Blue spirals meant the hollow ache of missing other minds. [437] Amber zigzags meant the old static-shape stirring. [444] And one night, weeks in, a slow green arc appeared that neither of them had named yet. [461] "What was that one?" Juna asked. [467] Theo looked at his own hands as if they had answered before he could. [481] "You leaned against the doorframe," he said. [488] "My chest went warm. [492] I think the green is you." [498]

Juna, who did not believe in telepathy, believed entirely in that. [509] The body had always been transmitting; the pill had only shouted over it. [522] Word spread, as word does, through the community of the recalled. [533] They came to the studio in twos and threes, people who had heard each other's thoughts for a year and now could not meet each other's eyes. [560] Juna taught them to listen downward, into wrists and stomachs and breath, and Theo taught them to paint what they found there. [582] Before each session, she passed the muscovite sliver around the circle, letting everyone watch ordinary lamplight fracture into its hidden, orderly infinities. [604] "Nothing was taken from you," she would say. [612] "You were taken from yourselves. [617] We are walking back." [621]

The photographs from those sessions hung along the studio's long wall, hundreds of them, and visitors said the room felt like standing inside a willow made of glass, something that bent with every grief and broke under none of them, every translucent branch catching light. [666] Theo never stopped missing the chorus of other minds. [675] But one evening, packing up the wands, he caught Juna watching him and felt the green arc rise in his chest before either of them spoke. [701] "Say what I'm feeling," he challenged her, grinning. [709] She studied his shoulders, the angle of his breath, the way his thumb circled the sliver of stone she had finally given him to keep. [734] "Hope," she said. [737] "The stubborn kind." [740] She was right, and she had read it nowhere but his body, along pathways that had been lit there, patiently, all along. [762]
