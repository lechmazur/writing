The observatory hung forty kilometers above the desert, tethered to nothing, riding the stratosphere like a held breath. [18] Amal had lived aboard it for six years, ever since the wedding. [30] She did not say survived; survived implied an ending, and nothing had ended. [43] The drone had come at the moment of the toast, and the courtyard had become arithmetic: forty-one guests minus thirty-three. [63] Tariq had been holding her hand. [69] What remained of him fit inside the question she had been asking ever since: where does a life's unused potential go? [90] Not the body, not the memory—the rest of it. [100] The children unconceived, the words unspoken, the decades of mornings that had been scheduled and then canceled. [117] Energy is conserved, she reasoned, with the cold stubbornness of the bereaved; potential must pool somewhere. [133] So she hunted it. [137]

Her method was perturbation theory, borrowed from her old quantum coursework and bent toward heresy. [152] Treat the observable universe as the solved system, the zeroth order, the boring Hamiltonian everyone agrees on. [169] Then look for the corrections—tiny, higher-order deviations in the gravitational field, places where reality sagged under a weight that was not officially there. [193] If unrealized futures had mass, however slight, the perturbations would betray them. [205] The observatory's interferometers were sensitive enough, drifting up here above the atmosphere's muddy noise. [219]

On her console sat the geode crystal, halved, its amethyst throat catching the instrument light. [234] Tariq's wedding gift, found in the rubble, split open by the blast. [246] He had meant for them to crack it together, a custom from his grandmother: a plain stone, hiding a cathedral. [266] She kept it because it was the only proof she trusted: dull surfaces lie, and emptiness can be ornate. [285]

Tonight the hunt had nearly killed the station. [293] She had pushed the detector array past rated load, chasing a third-order anomaly over the ruins of her home district, and the surge had tripped every breaker on the ring. [323] Protocol demanded she wait while the circuit breakers cooled—forty minutes of enforced stillness before she could power the filters back up. [345] But the raw feed still trickled through on backup batteries, unfiltered, unwashed, every algorithm that normally scrubbed the data now dark. [366] She understood, suddenly, what the filters had been doing for six years. [378] They had been protecting her. [383]

The raw signal poured across her screens, and it was not noise. [395] It had structure—dense, branching, recursive—pooled exactly where her models predicted, in the courtyards and hospitals and border roads, everywhere lives had been interrupted rather than completed. [423] Potential did pool. [426] It gathered like groundwater in the hollows violence carved. [435] And it was thickest over her own wedding courtyard. [444]

She felt it before she could measure it—a pressure behind the sternum, intangibly tangible, the way a stare touches the back of the neck. [469] The instruments could only show its shadow; her body registered the thing itself. [482] Without filters, without the comfortable abstraction of cleaned data, the awakening came all at once and would not be polite about it. [504] She saw Tariq's unlived decades not as poetry but as a reservoir, real as aquifer, real as ore. [522] She saw thirty-three guests' worth of canceled mornings shimmering in the gravitational residue like heat off asphalt. [539] She laughed, once, an ugly sound in the humming dark. [549] It was horrible, and it was the most alive she had felt since the toast. [564] Grief, it turned out, had been astronomy all along: the study of light from things already gone. [581]

She picked up the geode and held its broken face against the screen's glow. [595] The same architecture. [598] The crystal had grown inward, into the cavity, because the void was the only place left to grow. [616] That was what the signal was doing over the courtyard—crystallizing, slowly, in the hollow where forty-one futures had been excavated by a machine that never landed. [643]

The breakers ticked as they cooled, counting down to the moment the filters would return and sand all of this smooth again. [665] She had perhaps twenty minutes of unmediated truth left. [674] So she recorded everything—raw, untreated, inadmissible to any journal—and she wrote the first honest sentence of her career: the dead do not take their potential with them; it pools, it waits, it grows facets in the dark. [713]

Whether anyone could ever draw from that reservoir, she did not know. [725] But the hunt was no longer for evidence. [733] It was for a way in. [739] When the breakers reset and the filters rose like merciful cataracts over the feed, Amal was already plotting the next perturbation, the deliberate disturbance that might crack the pooled potential open the way the blast had cracked the geode. [778] Some stones only show their cathedrals when broken. [786] She intended to break this one gently. [793]
