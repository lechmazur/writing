On Pomarine, the orchards grew upside down as often as up, their roots threaded through floating arches of rust-colored stone. [20] The arches drifted slowly above the valley, and apples fell upward into nets the settlers had learned to string between them. [41] Sef the blacksmith rarely spoke above the hiss of her forge. [52] When the magician Orrun had lived among them, she had only nodded at his promises, too meek to ask what he meant by selling awakenings. [77] Orrun's trade was simple and terrible: he would wake a dead thing, an orchard row, a well, a heart, and in exchange he took a memory of your choosing. [106] The settlers bartered freely, and the valley bloomed, and slowly nobody remembered how anything had been done before him. [125] Then Orrun left, the way magicians do, taking the memories and leaving only a bolt of his silk draped across Sef's anvil. [147]

The arches began to fall a season later. [155] The first one came down on the eastern grove, crushing forty trees into splinters and rubble. [171] The settlers stood at the crater's edge in that strange season between understanding and confusion, knowing the arches fell because Orrun's wakings were unraveling, yet unable to recall what had held them up before. [205] They had bartered that knowledge away. [211]

Sef had bartered nothing. [215] She had been too shy to approach the magician, and now her timidity looked like wisdom: she alone remembered the old miners' talk. [238] Before the orchards, Pomarine had been a mining world. [247] The arches floated because veins of lifting ore ran through them, and the abandoned shafts beneath the valley still climbed inside the stone like marrow through bone. [274] The ore had been failing for years; Orrun had merely charmed it awake, and his charm was dying with his absence. [295]

At the village meeting, voices rose toward panic. [303] Sef stood, and the room quieted, mostly from surprise. [312] "The shafts still reach the arch roots," she said, barely above a whisper. [325] "I can climb them and brace the failing veins with iron; we do not need him." [341] Nobody believed her, but nobody had a better plan, so they let the meek blacksmith try. [357]

She worked with quiet determination, never rushing, never explaining twice. [367] She forged collars and ribs of iron to splint the dying veins, then cut Orrun's silk into long strips. [386] The silk held residue of his wakings; wherever it touched failing ore, the stone stirred faintly, like a sleeper turning over. [407] It would not wake the veins alone, but wrapped around her iron splints, it bought the metal time to take the load. [429]

For a month she descended into the abandoned shafts each dawn, lantern hooked to her belt, climbing the old ladders up into the hollow hearts of the arches. [457] Every motion was deliberate; one careless hammer blow could shatter a vein and drop ten thousand tons of orchard-bearing stone. [477] She measured, marked, set her splints, bound them in silk, and moved on. [490] In the crushed eastern grove she did something else. [499] Evenings, after the shafts, she sorted through the rubble for living roots, grafting broken branches with iron sleeves and silk bindings, coaxing resilience from the wreckage one tree at a time. [530] The settlers watched, and slowly began to help. [538]

When the second arch shuddered, the whole valley heard it groan. [549] Sef was inside it. [553] She felt the vein crack beside her ear and did not run. [565] She set her last splint, wound the final strip of magician's silk around it, and struck the locking pin home with three measured blows. [589] The arch steadied. [592] The groaning faded into the ordinary hum of floating stone. [602]

When she emerged from the shaft mouth, dusty and bleeding from her knuckles, the settlers were waiting under a sky the color of hammered azure. [627] Someone asked what they owed her. [633] Sef thought of Orrun, of awakenings bought with pieces of yourselves, and shook her head. [648] "Learn the shafts," she said. [653] "Learn the splints, so you never have to trade your memories for rescue again." [667] It was the longest speech anyone had ever heard from her. [678]

That winter, apprentices crowded her forge, and maps of the old mine shafts hung where Orrun's contracts had once been posted. [699] The eastern grove put out new blossoms through its iron sleeves, white flowers against blue sky, a quiet defiance of every falling thing. [722] And if the arches still drifted on borrowed strength, they drifted on strength the valley had built itself, splint by splint, graft by graft, with nothing bartered away but sweat. [752] Sef went back to her anvil and did not need to say anything more. [766]
