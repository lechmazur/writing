The city of Ten Shells drifted on the backs of gargantuan turtles, and Mirel had spent eleven years mapping the spirals of their carapaces. [24] She was a spiral-shell cartographer, the last one the Guild still paid, because nobody else believed the patterns were going anywhere. [45] Mirel believed. [47] The shells grew in coils, and the coils grew in grammar, and the grammar, she was certain, was a language the turtles wrote with their own bodies over centuries. [76] She was quietly driven the way tidewater is driven: no shouting, no speeches, only a constant pressure in one direction. [96] The direction was the horizon, where the watchers stood. [105] Everyone in Ten Shells knew them: tall, motionless silhouettes standing in the shallows of the world's edge, visible only at noon, when the light lay flat and merciless on the sea. [136] Nobody discussed them in daylight. [141] The dread of Ten Shells was a sunwashed dread, bright and warm and bleached of all excuses, and it made people polite and forgetful. [165]

Twenty years ago an expedition had rowed out to question the watchers. [177] Mirel's mother had gone with them, carrying a reed whistle whose seven notes matched the seven intervals of the oldest shell spiral, the one the founders called the First Word. [207] The expedition never returned. [211] Afterward the city sealed its harbor gate, and after the gate rusted shut, sealing it became a virtue instead of a panic. [233] No one left Ten Shells now. [239] No one wanted to. [243] Mirel wanted to. [246]

On the morning the third turtle cracked, she was the only one who understood what the crack was saying. [265] It opened across the carapace beneath the Salt District, a black seam two hundred paces long, and the magistrates declared it ordinary settling, the way old houses settle. [293] Mirel crawled the seam for three days with chalk and calipers. [304] The crack was not random. [309] It branched in sevens. [313] It followed the intervals of the First Word, but inverted, the way a question inverts a statement. [330] She worked through pattern languages all that white-hot week, laying her mother's old transcriptions beside her rubbings of the fracture, until the translation sat in front of her, plain as noon. [361] The turtles were not settling. [366] They were answering. [369] Someone, twenty years ago, had finally asked the watchers something, and the reply had been traveling back through water and shell and bone ever since, and the turtles were breaking themselves open to write it down. [405] The reply was incomplete. [409] The crack ended mid-phrase, at the seventh branch, waiting. [418] It was waiting for the seventh note. [425]

Mirel went down to the rusted gate at low tide. [435] Wedged in the orange scale of its hinges, where the harbor current pressed everything lost back against the city, she found driftwood, net floats, and a reed whistle bleached the color of bone. [468] Her mother's mark was still scratched into its mouthpiece. [477] The sea had carried it home and the gate, rusted shut, had refused to let it in. [494] So the expedition had reached the watchers. [501] They had asked. [504] And the question, Mirel understood with a calm that frightened her more than terror would have, had cost them their voices, their boats, everything except the instrument itself, sent back like a receipt. [537]

She could have buried it. [542] Instead she climbed the Salt District at noon, when the watchers stood out on the bright water like nails driven into the sky's hem, and knelt at the seventh branch of the crack. [575] The sun was gorgeous and indifferent. [581] Laundry snapped on lines. [585] Children argued over a melon rind. [591] Nobody looked at the horizon, because nobody ever did. [600] Her mother had gone out to question the watchers with sails and oars and courage. [615] Mirel would question them the way a cartographer questions anything: by completing the pattern and seeing what the answer maps. [635] She raised the whistle and played the seventh note into the crack. [647]

The sound was small, a reed's thin breath, almost nothing. [657] The turtle heard it. [661] The seam ran forward under her knees, branching, curling, finishing its sentence in a long grinding whisper that shivered every rooftop in the Salt District. [686] She copied it as it formed, hands steady, chalk flying. [696] The completed reply, read through the pattern language her whole life had built, said only this: WE WATCH SO IT SLEEPS. [717] DO NOT WAKE THE QUESTION AGAIN. [723]

On the horizon, for the first time in anyone's memory, one of the watchers slowly turned its head toward Ten Shells. [744] The afternoon stayed beautiful. [748] The light stayed gold. [752] Mirel sat in the warmth with her mother's whistle in her lap, the answer in her notebook, and the long, patient, sunlit dread of being, at last, the one who was seen. [784]
