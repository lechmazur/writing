Mara Voss made maps no one could read with their eyes. [11] She charted places by their textures, their echoes, the taste of their dust, because some places refused to hold still for sight. [33] The abandoned repair bay at Telfer Spaceport was the worst offender she had ever surveyed. [48] Its geometry drifted on a cycle, never quite the same bay twice, the drift arriving on schedule but never in the same shape. [71] Cyclically spontaneous, her old instructor had called it, as if the contradiction explained anything. [85] Mara knew better now: the bay was a loom, and every cycle it wove itself through parallel universes, threading one version of the room into the next. [112]

Eleven years ago, her brother Ilan had been a repair tech here when the spaceport was sealed overnight. [130] The authority's report said decompression accident; it said nothing else, and the silence had a mass she could feel pressing on her chest at night. [155] She had come to discover the weight of that withheld truth, to measure it the only way she knew how: by hand, by ear, by tongue. [181]

The crossings opened only in the murmur of half-remembered dreams, that thin hour when the mind loosens its grip and the bay loosens its seams. [206] So Mara slept on a cot between the gantry rails, half-waking on purpose, training herself to map while dreaming. [225] Her instrument was a green glass shard, recovered from the bay floor on her first survey. [241] It had once been part of a coolant gauge from Ilan's station; she recognized the etched serial under her thumb the way she recognized his handwriting. [267] The shard did not belong fully to any one world, so it hummed against her skin whenever the weave drew near, colder on the side facing the next universe over. [297] It was a compass for a cartographer of the senses, and it was all she had left of him. [316]

On the ninth night, the cycle came early. [324] She surfaced into the gray murmur of a dream about rain and felt the shard go ice-cold against her palm. [344] The bay smelled suddenly of ozone and machine oil, fresh oil, oil that had not existed here for eleven years. [364] She followed her own map: fourteen steps along the rail where the floor went from gritty to smooth, left at the echo that doubled back wrong, down into the pit where the air tasted of copper. [400] There the weave opened: a seam of overlapping rooms, a dozen repair bays braided through one another like wet rope. [420] In one strand, the bay still ran; she could hear voices, a torch hissing, someone laughing at a joke she would never hear the start of. [446] And in that strand, Ilan was alive. [453] The shard told her so, warming toward him like a hand turning toward a fire. [468]

But the weave was not still. [474] She felt the threads pulling, the cycle preparing to bind that living bay to a strand where the coolant lines had already ruptured, where the accident was about to happen again. [505] Then she understood the withheld truth, and it was heavy enough to buckle her knees. [520] There had been no single accident. [526] The authority had known the bay wove between worlds, had known the cycle dragged catastrophe from one universe into the next, and had sealed the port rather than admit it could not stop the loom. [561] Her brother had not died once; he had been dying over and over, in strand after strand, every cycle, for eleven years. [583] The truth weighed exactly that much: eleven years of repetitions, and one more beginning now. [598]

Mara could not stop the weave, but a map maker knows that a path can be redirected if you change what the traveler senses. [622] The loom chose its threads by resonance, by likeness, the way her shard chose its temperature. [638] So she pressed the green glass into the seam where the strands touched and gave the weave something else to follow: the shard's own history, its serial number, its long quiet years in a world where the bay stood empty and nothing exploded at all. [683] The threads hesitated, tasted the shard, and turned. [691] The living bay slid past the rupturing one and braided instead into her own silent, harmless ruin. [708] For one breath the two rooms occupied the same place, and a man in a tech's jacket looked up from his station, straight at her, puzzled, alive. [735] Then the cycle closed, taking the shard with it, leaving her hand empty and warm. [750]

Mara sat in the dark and added a new line to her map: a smooth place on the floor, a doubled echo, a seam that now led somewhere safe. [779] Some truths, once weighed, can be set down. [787]
