The grotto hung inside a hollowed cloud-stone, tethered to nothing, drifting where the aurora pooled thickest. [16] Sera worked there alone, beautician to the dead, mortician to the beautiful, the last hands a body knew before the sky took it back. [40] Tonight the lift-basket brought her Maren, the woman who had trained her, laid out beneath green light like a queen of some drowned country. [64] Sera washed the cold face first, as she had been taught, because grief works better through the hands than through the eyes. [86] Then she began the true work, the work only the grotto allowed. [98]

Every mind, dying, leaves a residue: a faint static that clings to the body for a single night before oblivion claims it whole. [121] Maren had taught her to listen to that static, to find the one pattern in it that was still a person, and to capture that last spark inside an emerald chip before it dissolved forever. [156] The chips went home with the families, warm green stones that hummed a remembered laugh, a lullaby, a name. [175] Beauty was not vanity here; it was the lure. [184] A spark would not surface for a stranger's rough handling, but it rose toward tenderness the way fish rise toward light. [205] So Sera painted Maren's lips their old defiant red and combed silver hair into the braid Maren had worn for sixty years. [227] She infused the skin with balm pressed from aurora-fed lichen, working it in slow circles until the body seemed to hold a little of the green fire above. [255]

Then she set the listening crown on Maren's brow, slotted a blank emerald chip into its socket, and put the receiver to her own ear. [280] Static. [281] Only static, gray and featureless as cloudbank. [288] Sera's stomach dropped. [291] In nineteen years she had never lost a spark, and she could not lose this one, not the woman who had given her every skill she owned. [318] She made herself lucidly watchful, the state Maren had drilled into her: awake past tiredness, calm past grief, attention spread thin and bright as frost. [343]

Hours passed. [345] The aurora rippled through the grotto's open mouth, washing the walls in slow green tides. [360] Still nothing rose from the noise, and somewhere below the noise the night was running out. [376] Near dawn she reached the threshold of stillness, the place her teacher had only ever described and never shown her. [396] It was not silence; it was the doorway just before silence, where even her own heartbeat stepped aside and stopped interrupting. [417] And there, at last, she heard it. [424]

The static was not empty. [429] It pulsed, faintly, in time with the aurora's flicker on the grotto walls. [442] Maren had not faded; she had hidden, weaving her last pattern into the one rhythm she trusted her student to be watching. [464] A final test, or a final gift; with Maren the two had never been different things. [480] Sera laughed once, soundlessly, and began to transcribe. [488] Each pulse of light, each answering pulse of noise: she matched them, pair by pair, until a shape stood up out of the static like a figure walking out of snow. [519] It was a voice. [523] It was Maren's voice, saying the first thing she had ever said to a frightened apprentice with trembling hands: the dead are not gone until no one is listening. [552]

The spark surged toward the crown, drawn by the painted lips, the braided hair, the lichen-warm skin, everything tender Sera's hands had prepared for it. [577] Sera held the threshold open, lucid, unblinking, while the emerald chip drank the pattern down. [592] When it was done, the static went truly gray, and oblivion took the rest without resistance, the way tide takes a finished sandcastle. [615]

Dawn never quite came to the grotto; the aurora simply softened, like a room where someone has lowered their voice out of respect. [638] Sera lifted the chip from its socket. [645] It glowed faintly green, warm as a held hand, and when she raised it to her ear it hummed the lesson back to her in Maren's unmistakable, amused voice. [674] Maren had no family; the chip would go home with no one. [686] So Sera did what her teacher must have intended all along: she set it into the listening crown's empty second socket, where an instructor's voice could guide whoever wore it next. [717] Someday another apprentice would climb the lift-basket into this hush of green light, and the dead would teach the living, as they always had here. [742] Sera washed her hands, covered the empty body gently, and sat for a while at the grotto's mouth, watching the aurora find its patterns in the dark. [769] She was still listening. [773]
