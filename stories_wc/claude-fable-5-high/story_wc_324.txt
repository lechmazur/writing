The dead call between the last bell and full dark, and Mira Quell had learned to be listening. [18] She was a contact tracer for hearts, the only one the city's grief registry still employed. [34] When someone died alone, their unfinished love did not simply stop; it traveled, person to person, like a fever nobody named. [55] Her job was to find everyone a dead heart had touched and assure them, in plain words, that they were not next. [77] Tonight's case was Elias Marsh, eighty-one, found in his chair with the radio still on. [92] The registry gave her two things: his last photograph and his decades-old hearing aid. [106] The photograph showed Elias in the conservatory atrium, smiling at someone just outside the frame. [121] That cropped absence bothered her more than it should have. [131] Mira had always been persistently curious, the kind of curious that lost her friends and found her strangers. [149] She wanted to know who Elias had been smiling at, because that person was a contact, and contacts left unwarned could break. [171]

The hearing aid was her instrument. [177] A heart that has been loved leaves a faint pulse in whatever its owner listened through, and Elias had listened through this small beige shell for forty years. [205] Hold it near a place the heart had loved, and the pulse quickened. [218] So she went to the conservatory at twilight, when the dead call loudest. [231]

The atrium stood mist-clad and silver, fog pooling under the glass dome like breath that had nowhere to go. [250] Every footstep she took came back to her hushed, as if the building were swallowing sound to save it for later. [271] She lifted the hearing aid and let it taste the air. [282] A pulse, faint as a moth against a window. [291] It strengthened near the ferns, weakened by the fountain, surged at a bench beneath the dripping palms. [308] Someone was sitting there. [312] A woman, old as the photograph was new, with a coat buttoned wrong and a paper bag of pills in her lap. [334]

"Ruth?" Mira guessed, because the registry file had whispered the name once and then redacted it. [350] The woman did not startle. [355] "He used to say my name like that," she said. [365] "Soft, like he wasn't sure I'd answer." [372] Mira sat down without being asked, because curiosity had never taught her manners. [385] She showed Ruth the last photograph. [391] "You're outside the frame," Mira said. [397] "He was smiling at you." [402] Ruth's hands tightened on the paper bag. [409] "We quarreled in March," she said. [415] "Fifty-three years, and we ended on a quarrel about a kettle. [426] He died thinking I was done with him. [434] I came here to be done with everything." [442]

The hush in the atrium deepened, reeling, as if the mist itself had heard and pulled back to listen. [461] Mira had come to deliver a procedure, a warning, a form to sign. [474] She had not come to save anyone; saving was never in the job description. [488] But she believed, with the whole stubborn machinery of her own heart, that the most vulnerable voices were the ones convinced nobody was listening. [512] Protecting them was the only reason she had kept this strange, sad work. [525] So she pressed the hearing aid gently into Ruth's palm. [535] "Listen," she said. [538]

The pulse inside it was racing now, quick and bright, no moth but a wing-beat, a heart hurrying toward a door. [559] Ruth held it to her ear, and her face came apart slowly, the way ice does in April. [577] "It's him," she whispered. [581] "It beats faster when it's near you," Mira said. [590] "It has, every step I took toward this bench. [599] He wasn't done with you. [604] He was waiting for twilight, when the dead are allowed to call, so he could say so." [621]

This was the assurance the registry trained her to give, the official sentence: you are not responsible, you are not next, you are not alone. [646] She had said it a hundred times to a hundred bowed heads. [658] She had never before watched it land like a rope thrown across dark water. [672] Ruth set the paper bag on the bench between them and did not pick it up again. [689] They sat in the reeling hush until the dome went dark and the mist thinned to threads. [706] When the pulse finally slowed and stilled, it did so the way a hand relaxes after being held. [724]

In her report, Mira wrote only: one contact located, assurance delivered, no further spread expected. [739] Nowhere did she write that she had taken the pills to the night pharmacist herself, or that Ruth had asked to keep the photograph, or that heroes are mostly just curious people who refuse to stop at the edge of a frame.
