I came to Wren Hollow as an apprentice herbalist, though my real credentials were folded into a duffel bag: night-vision lenses, plaster for casting tracks, and a notebook labeled ANOMALOUS FAUNA. [31] Cryptid research does not pay, but bitterness does, and I had been paying myself in it since my brother Eli walked out of the pines eleven months after vanishing, smiling like a stranger. [64] The town called them the Returned. [70] Eleven people, gone and given back, each one wrong in a way no doctor could chart. [86] They moved as if gravity were negotiable, hopping curbs in long floating arcs, balancing spoons on the air for a heartbeat too long, laughing at the ground itself. [114] Gravitationally playful, I wrote, and underlined it twice, hating how much it sounded like joy. [129]

Marisol Vane, the herbalist, hired me without checking a single reference. [140] Her garden grew underground, in a root cellar widened over generations, lit by mirrored shafts that funneled winter sun down to beds of valerian, moonwort, and pale fiddleheads that turned to face you when you spoke. [176] The Returned came to her after midnight, and I came to spy on them. [190] What they did there was not medicine. [197] Marisol kept an old cordless phone on a shelf between seed jars, its antenna bent, its base plugged into nothing. [217] One by one the Returned would press it to their ears and confess into the static, voices low, frost on their breath even in that warm earth. [244] I hid a recorder in the valerian and learned that the static answered back. [258]

Eli came on the third night. [264] He did not see me crouched behind the seed racks; he only took the phone, exhaled, and said, I'm sorry I can't stay heavy for them. [290] The static crackled like ice breaking on a far lake, and inside it I heard chords, not words, a music with no key signature and no intention of acquiring one. [320]

Marisol found me at dawn, my recorder in her palm like a confiscated toy. [334] You're the cryptid hunter, she said, and I did not bother denying it. [347] Instead of evicting me, she dug into a bed of black soil and handed me a flint arrowhead, knapped edges still sharp after what she claimed was three thousand years. [377] It weighed nothing. [380] It hovered a hair above my palm, drifting in lazy orbits around my thumb. [394] Someone shot at the song once too, Marisol said. [403] The hunter missed, then vanished, then returned changed, and his arrowhead has been laughing at gravity ever since. [421]

She explained what the garden was for. [428] The Returned were not victims and not monsters; they were metamorphosing, slowly, into notes of something she called a harmony untamed, a wild music that took people the way spring takes ice. [460] Her herbs slowed the change enough for goodbyes; her phone let them confess their last human weights into the static, so they could rise clean. [485] Everything you've been hunting, she said, is just the song's footprints. [496]

I wanted to publish, expose, burn the whole tender conspiracy down. [507] Bitterness is a fuel that mistakes itself for a compass. [517] But that night Eli waited for me by the seed racks, both feet a polite inch off the floor. [536] He held out the cordless phone. [542] Your turn, he said. [546] I pressed it to my ear and confessed everything: the undercover lie, the recorder, the eleven months I spent furious at him for coming back wrong instead of grateful he came back at all. [580] The static took each word and gave back chords, and somewhere in those chords I heard my own grief transposed into a melody so absurdly vast that I laughed out loud. [611] That, the static seemed to say, is the whole trick: the universe is not cruel, only joking on a scale we mistake for cruelty. [635]

Eli left in February, on a night so cold the stars cracked. [647] He rose from the garden's mirrored shaft like a balloon someone loved too much to hold, and the fiddleheads all turned to watch him go. [672] I stayed. [674] I burned the notebook and apprenticed for real, learning valerian from moonwort, learning which teas keep a changing body anchored long enough to say what needs saying. [701] The arrowhead orbits a jar on my shelf now, a small flint moon, proof that even weapons can forget their grudges. [722] Some mornings my heels lift off the floor before my coffee is done. [735] I am not afraid. [739] When the song comes for me, I will pick up the dead phone, confess my last little heaviness into the static, and go up laughing, one more punchline in the long, cold, hopeful joke of the sky.
