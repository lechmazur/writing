Marlow the sea-foam recollectionist worked the shore each dawn, scooping bubbles into jars before they popped. [16] Every bubble held a memory the sea had stolen from someone, and her job was returning them. [33] She did it cheerfully, the way gravediggers whistle, because somebody must. [44] Lately, though, the foam came back wrong, riddled with holes like lace chewed by moths. [59] Whole paths were missing from the memories: the cliff stair to the lighthouse, the orchard lane, the road her mother once walked home along. [83] Worse, the paths were vanishing from the land itself, grass closing over them like water over a stone. [101] Marlow could not ask the villagers; whatever the foam forgot, they forgot too. [114] She could only conjecture, and her best conjecture was this: something inland was eating the routes between rememberings. [132] She had until her own memory of her mother's road faded, which the holes in her morning jars suggested would be soon. [154] So she packed bread, three jars, and the crater glass. [164] The crater glass was a green lens fused when a falling star struck the dunes; held to the eye, it transliterates emotions into a wobbly script anyone patient can read. [194] Grief writes in long loops; joy writes in stubby capitals; foam, being mostly grief, writes in loops. [211] But which way inland? [215] The answer arrived on wings. [220] A migrating bird crossed the headland, gorged on berries, dropping seeds as it flew. [234] Through the crater glass, the seeds shone with the bird's homesickness, written in small fierce capitals: home, home, home. [253] The bird flew the same route every year, and its route never vanished. [266] Marlow grinned at the gloom of it and followed the scattered seeds inland, jar to jar, drop to drop. [285] The seed trail ended at a hill split open like a dropped egg. [298] Inside was a cave within a giant crystal geode, every facet iridescent, throwing her reflection back in a hundred slightly wrong colors. [320] It was the most beautiful place she had ever dreaded. [330] The facets were not empty. [335] Each crystal pane held a path: the cliff stair, the orchard lane, her mother's road, all hoarded here like pinned butterflies. [356] Marlow lifted the crater glass and read the cave itself. [366] The walls were covered in loops, long and trembling: loneliness, loneliness, loneliness. [378] The geode was not a thief by nature; it was a collector of abandoned things. [393] Here, then, was her answer: paths disappear when nobody walks them with feeling anymore. [407] Unloved roads pull free of the world like missing pieces from a puzzle, and the geode gathers them so they will not simply die. [431] The bird's route survived because the bird wanted, fiercely, every single year. [443] Wanting was the glue. [447] Marlow sat down among the glittering hoard and laughed, a small wet laugh, because the diagnosis was so simple and so sad. [469] Her village had stopped wanting its own roads. [477] She could not blame the geode any more than she could blame the sea for keeping what people let go. [497] But she could bargain. [501] She opened her jars and poured the morning's foam across the crystal floor. [514] Through the crater glass, the foam's grief-loops tangled with the cave's loneliness-loops until the two scripts read each other. [533] Keep the memories of the paths, she offered aloud, but give back the paths themselves. [548] I will bring you foam every season, so you are never abandoned either. [561] The iridescence shivered, considering. [565] Then one facet went dark, and far away, Marlow felt rather than saw a road unrolling itself back across the headland. [586] She walked home along her mother's road while she still remembered to love it, naming every rut out loud. [605] At the village she gathered the children and gave each one a path to want: this stair, that lane, this bend by the willow. [629] Walk it angry, walk it happy, she told them, but never walk it numb. [643] Every autumn after, when the migrating bird crossed over dropping its seeds, Marlow followed the green trail back to the geode with full jars. [667] She read its lonely walls, poured out company, and collected one returned road in trade. [682] The villagers never learned where their lost lanes went or how they came back, and Marlow let them conjecture. [701] Mystery, she figured, was its own kind of wanting. [710] On the last morning anyone saw her, she was old and cheerful and gloomy as ever, scooping foam at dawn. [730] The bubbles came up whole now, no holes, no missing pieces. [741] Through the crater glass, the last jar she ever filled read, in stubby joyful capitals, a single repeated word: kept. [761]
