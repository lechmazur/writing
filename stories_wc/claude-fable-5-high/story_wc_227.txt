The workshop hung on a raft of brass gears above the hissing acid sea, and I, Verel, was the only alchemist left to doubt it. [25] Every cog beneath my feet turned because the old masters had believed in turning, and belief was the one reagent I had never managed to distill. [51] The sea was eating us anyway. [57] Each tide, the acid climbed another tooth of the great wheel, and the workshop sang a lower, sicker note. [76] At the center of the floor stood the statue, a robed figure carved centuries ago, its stone face pitted with pockmarks like a map of grief. [102] The masters' last instruction, scratched into a copper plate, said only this: mend her in gold, and the world will remember why it floats. [126] Kintsugi, they had called it elsewhere, in drowned countries: the repair that does not hide the breaking but honors it. [146] But gold alone was not enough. [152] Each pockmark wanted a memory pressed into it, a true one, or the seam would not hold. [169] That was where the charm came in. [176] On my key ring, between the workshop keys, hung a small tin charm shaped like a thimble, my grandmother's last gift. [197] She had been a quilter, and the charm did what she had done: it quilts memories together, stitching scraps of different lives into one warm, coherent thing. [224] Whenever I held it, I felt not only my own past but hers, and her mother's, a patchwork going back farther than the sea. [248] The trouble was the statue's lower half. [255] It stood in the flooded ballast chamber, submerged to the waist in cold brine the gears pumped in to balance us against the acid outside. [280] Half her wounds could only be read underwater. [288] So my work was measured between breaths held underwater: dive, find a pockmark by touch, surface, and pour the gold before my fingers forgot its shape. [314] And as I poured, I had to narrate. [322] That was the alchemy's cruelest demand: a memory only set in the gold if it was spoken aloud, given away, made into story. [345] The first night I matched a crater on her shoulder, ragged like a star, with the charm's memory of my grandmother burning her quilting frame for heat in the last winter ashore. [377] I spoke it into the gold, and the seam took, glowing faintly, and somewhere below, a gear that had ground for years ran suddenly smooth. [402] The matching was everything. [406] A round, shallow pit wanted a soft loss; a deep gouge wanted a betrayal. [420] When I guessed wrong, the gold turned gray and crumbled, and the acid outside rose a finger's width, as if the sea fed on failed meaning. [446] I began to understand what the masters had built: not a machine that floated on water, but a machine that floated on significance, and the statue was its heart. [475] Oblivion was not the acid; the acid was only what oblivion looked like from a deck. [491] My task, dive by dive, was to conjure meaning from the edge of it, one gilded scar at a time. [511] By the tenth night, only one pockmark remained, the deepest, set over her stone heart, below the waterline. [529] The charm had nothing left that fit. [536] I pressed my thumb to every stitch of its patchwork and found only borrowed griefs, all too shallow. [554] Then I understood, with the terrible clarity of a spell turning on its caster, what shape the wound was. [573] It was doubt. [576] It was mine: every formula I had never trusted, every masterwork I had abandoned half-mixed, the whole hollow of my uncertainty, carved centuries early into her chest as if she had been waiting for me to grow it. [614] I unhooked the charm from the key ring and closed it in my fist, taking one breath of the oily dark, and went down. [638] Underwater, fingers reading the wound, I found it shaped exactly like a thimble. [651] I pressed the charm into the stone heart, and against it I pressed my own memory: the night I nearly let my life dissolve because I believed it added up to nothing. [683] The charm stitched the doubt to my grandmother's stubbornness, to a stranger's drowned lullaby, quilting the hole into a patch. [703] I surfaced and narrated all of it aloud, gasping, voice raw, sealing the seam with words instead of gold. [722] Below me, the tin charm flushed gold through the water, the cheapest metal in the workshop transmuted at last. [741] Every gear in the floor turned over at once, like a great animal rolling in its sleep, and the acid sank back a full tooth of the wheel. [769] I am still uncertain; the gold does not erase that. [779] But the statue wears my doubt as a bright seam now, and we float on it. [795]
