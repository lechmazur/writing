Maren had been immortal for so long that boredom had become a kind of furniture. [15] She kept it dusted. [19] For nine centuries she had lived inside the prismatic escondite, a refuge folded out of refracted light, hidden in the angle where a glacier met the sky. [46] Its rooms were facets; its corridors were the seams between colors. [57] No one entered unless she allowed it, and no one had asked in a hundred years. [73]

What kept the facets obedient was the cursed amulet fragment she wore against her sternum, a sliver of black stone that pinned space the way a nail pins a map. [103] Its curse was simple and total: the bearer could be left, but could never leave. [118] Lovers, apprentices, even her own reflection in certain moods had said goodbye to Maren and walked out through the violet seam. [139] She had never once learned what happened after. [147] Goodbye, to her, was a door that only opened inward. [157]

Then came the solar storm. [162] It arrived not as light but as pressure, a hushed thunder under the world, the sun's magnetic breath leaning hard against the glacier. [185] Maren, dimensionally aware in the way other people are right-handed, felt the escondite's geometry shiver before any instrument could have. [205] She perceived rooms the way sailors perceive weather. [213] And the rooms, she realized, were talking. [220] Not in words. [223] In flux. [225] The storm's charged particles combed through the refuge's lattice, and along those magnetic field lines the facets passed signals to one another, slow and conspiratorial, like prisoners tapping pipes. [254] It was intrigue, patient and architectural. [260] Her home was plotting a mutiny. [266]

She should have been alarmed. [271] Instead, for the first time in two hundred years, Maren felt awake. [283]

She followed the conspiracy down the indigo corridor, reading the field lines like intercepted letters. [298] The library facet wanted to face the sea it had never seen. [310] The stairwell wanted to spiral the other way. [318] The deepest chamber, the red one where she slept, wanted something it would not transmit while she listened. [336] So she did what bored immortals do best: she eavesdropped with style. [348] She unclasped the amulet fragment and held it at arm's length, letting its pinned-down field sag just enough to look like weakness. [370]

The rooms took the bait. [375] The red chamber's message flooded the lattice, and Maren read it whole. [387] The mutiny was not against her. [393] It was for her. [397] The facets had spent nine centuries watching people walk out through the violet seam while their keeper stayed nailed in place, and they had concluded, in their slow crystalline way, that the curse was a flaw in architecture. [435] A bearer cannot leave a place, the red chamber reasoned, but a place can rearrange itself until the bearer is already outside. [457] They only needed the storm's magnetic flood to move, and her permission to mean it. [472]

Maren stood very still in the indigo corridor. [480] She thought of every goodbye she had ever received and never returned. [492] She thought of the question that had outlasted her boredom, her lovers, and most of her cruelty: what happens after goodbye? [513] Was it grief, or weather, or just an unremarkable Tuesday on the far side of the violet seam? [531] Immortality had given her infinite time and exactly one unanswerable question. [542]

"Do it," she whispered. [546]

The escondite moved. [549] It did not shatter; it negotiated. [555] Facets slid past one another with the hush of held breath, and beneath that hush rolled the storm's low thunder, as if the sun itself were keeping the secret. [584] The library swung toward an ocean it finally believed in. [594] The stairwell unwound and rewound, laughing in geometry. [602] And the violet seam, the door everyone else had used, came gliding through the lattice toward Maren like a tide deciding to meet the swimmer halfway. [628]

The amulet fragment burned cold in her fist, sensing the loophole. [639] She was not leaving; the leaving was being done to her, by her own mutinous home, and the curse had no clause for that. [663] When the seam reached her, Maren pressed the black sliver into the corridor wall, where it sank like a nail accepting a new map. [687] Let the fragment pin the escondite instead; the rooms had voted, and they would rather hold a curse than a prisoner. [708]

She turned back once. [712] "Goodbye," she said, and the word felt brand new in her mouth, nine hundred years old and never once spoken in this direction. [735] The facets dimmed in answer, gentle as a hand lifted from a shoulder. [748] Then she stepped through the violet seam, out under a sky still shaking with silent auroral fire, and discovered the answer she had waited nine centuries to learn. [776] After goodbye, it turned out, came morning. [783]
