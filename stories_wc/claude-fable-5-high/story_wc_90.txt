The Penance was a ship stitched from older ships, her hull a quilt of salvaged plating, and she had been falling toward nothing for sixty years. [26] Aboard her lived Mireille, the last human ever born, and the woman who had refused every weapon offered to defend her, Sister Anh. [49] Anh had been a pacifist before the wars and remained one after them, when refusal cost more. [66] She did not fight the dark; she sat with it, which is harder. [79] The ship's gravity had a flaw the engineers never fixed: a body grew gravitationally lighter when happy. [96] Grief pinned you to the deck; joy lifted your heels until you drifted. [109] For sixty years, nobody aboard the Penance had floated. [118]

In the chapel bay, behind a pane of scavenged glass, rested Kwan Yin's Tear: a teardrop of fused crystal recovered from a drowned temple on Earth. [144] Legend said the goddess of mercy wept once for every species that ended itself, and this was the only tear that did not evaporate. [168] The Tear hung beside a door that was not a door. [179] Mid-ship, where two salvaged hulls had been welded together, the seam had folded into a locked dimension: a hairline of impossible space the instruments called the Aperture. [206] Probes vanished into it and returned recordings of green hills and weather. [218] A world waited inside the seam, but the seam would not open for machines, for cutting torches, or for prayer alone. [239] Anh wanted to push it open before she died, because Mireille deserved a sky. [253]

The discovery came by accident, in the archive room. [262] Anh was sorting the dead crew's belongings when a faded photograph caught the chapel light, and the long-dried tears upon it shimmered faintly, like frost finding a sunrise. [290] She held the photograph near the Aperture, and the seam breathed. [301] Mourned-over images were keys; the salt of old weeping carried something the locked dimension recognized. [316] Sorrow witnessed, sorrow held, sorrow turned over in the hands until it stopped being an enemy. [332] The monks of her old order had a name for that turning: shadow work. [346]

So Anh began to stitch. [351] Night after ship-night, she sewed the wept-on photographs into a single great banner, edge to edge, face to face: weddings, funerals, a dog on a porch that no longer existed, a city before its burning. [386] Each stitch required her to look at what the photograph held and let it hurt. [401] She grew heavy at the work; some nights her knees would not unbend from the deck. [417] Mireille, seventeen and curious, found her there. [424] "You never fought anyone," the girl said, "but you fight this every night." [437] "I don't fight it," Anh said, threading the needle again. [447] "I keep it company until it trusts me." [455]

When the banner was finished, it weighed almost nothing and almost everything. [467] Anh carried it to the seam with Kwan Yin's Tear sewn into its center like a clasp, the crystal cool against her palms. [490] The shimmer of every dried tear woke at once, a constellation of small mournings, and the Aperture widened from hairline to handsbreadth. [512] But it stalled there, trembling, waiting for one more thing. [522] Anh understood, and her ghostly resolve settled over her like a second skin. [535] The door had been fed grief; now it needed someone light enough to push. [549] She had not been light in sixty years. [557]

So she did the last of her shadow work standing up. [568] She named, aloud, the brother who died because she would not raise a rifle. [582] She named the years of guilt she had mistaken for principle. [593] She named them, and then she forgave the woman who carried them, which was the one mercy she had never permitted herself. [615] The joy that came was not loud. [622] It was the quiet of a debt dissolving, and her heels left the deck. [636] Weightless, she set both palms against the seam and pushed. [646]

It opened like a wound deciding to be a doorway. [656] Green light spilled into the Penance, smelling of rain. [665] Mireille stepped to the threshold and looked back. [673] "Come with me," she said. [678] Anh hovered, hands still on the banner, and felt how little of her remained, how much of her had already been spent holding the door. [703] "Someone has to keep it company," she said. [711] "Doors remember who is kind to them." [718]

Mireille walked into the green and did not see the seam close, because it never did. [734] On the new world's first nights, she sometimes saw a faint shimmer along the horizon, like dried tears catching light. [754] She understood it as Anh, drifting weightless in the old recycled ship, happy at last, too light now ever to land, holding the way open for whatever came after the last human born. [787]
