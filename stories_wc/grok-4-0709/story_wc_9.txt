She had left the newborn with her sister below, in the mist-shrouded village, knowing this journey was her cloudbound repentance for years squandered in solitary divinations, ignoring the warmth of family bonds. [114]
Elara was willingly perplexed by the universe's riddles, embracing confusion as a path to deeper truths, her mind a deliberate labyrinth where clarity emerged only through wandering. [139]

As an onyx-ink cartomancer, Elara divined futures by spilling dark ink onto cards, interpreting the chaotic patterns as maps of destiny. [320]
But tonight, her quest demanded more than ink; it required invoking a family custom of tracing hands on birthday tablecloths, a ritual her ancestors had practiced to mark growth, to weave the present into the fabric of memory. [356]

That loss had set her on the path of cartomancy, seeking answers in ink rather than in the living traces of love. [453]
Now, to find where lost time accumulates, she would blend her art with the old custom. [467]
