[154] To Cassian, however, the shard held the shimmering refractions of a peaceful, artistic era, and he was driven by a desperate motivation to reimagine an entire civilization through its light. [184] He refused to let the victors of the war rewrite the beauty of what had been lost. [490] He knew the Ministry would soon arrive to confiscate the shard and declare his findings a heretical delusion. [565] This chronicle would survive even if he did not, serving as a beacon of hope for future generations who dared to dream of liberty.
