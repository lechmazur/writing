"I believe this belongs to you," Elias said, approaching the lone figure at the archives desk in the library's upper pavilion, where roof panels had been retracted to reveal a canopy of stars. [181]

"Oh! Thank you," she murmured, taking the envelope with fingers stained from decades of handling rare documents. [215]
"Elias Kane, cargo broker by day, reluctant patron of the arts by night," he introduced himself with a tentative smile, careful to match his exuberance to the quiet dignity of the setting. [243]
"Eleanor Birch," she replied softly, "I manage the regional shipping manifests from the early 20th century—not many people need the archives these days." [269]
Elias felt a familiar stirring, the one that always came when he encountered those who worked in obscurity, silent contributors whose labor underpinned others' success. [293]

"I'd like to learn about your work proper sometime," he offered, "perhaps over coffee before the library opens?" [411]
