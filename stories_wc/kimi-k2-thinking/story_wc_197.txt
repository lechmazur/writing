Her colleagues called it "maintenance"; Mara called it the tyranny of indifference, and she had sworn to hold the line. [73]

But plans unravel at the worst possible moments. [97]

The relay station's location had been purged from every database, erased from revised histories even in her own memory. [135]
All she had left was a phone booth key from a defunct phone company, tucked in her boot since her grandfather's funeral. [156]
He'd been a lineman before the System, and his stories about copper wires carrying voices now seemed like fairy tales. [174]
Mara was carefully resourceful, though, and resourcefulness doesn't need perfect conditions—only possibilities. [187]
