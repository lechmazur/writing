[47] That was precisely why Maren had come. [54]

[85] Most interpreters worked in comfortable observatories with heated floors and funded grants. [96] Maren worked in ruins, because ruins were honest, and honesty was the only instrument she trusted anymore. [113]

[167] She had learned to tune her portable receiver not by calibrating to known wavelengths but by discarding assumptions about what a signal should sound like. [192] Most interpreters failed because they listened for language. [200] Maren listened for the spaces between language, the architecture of silence. [211]

[340] A shard of hush, she called it, a fragment of silence so pure it seemed to predate sound itself. [359] Finding it again had become the organizing principle of her life. [370]
