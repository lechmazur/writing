<story>

Maren had spent thirty years seeing what others could not. [10] She had predicted earthquakes by the taste of Tuesday mornings, foretold marriages by the way strangers' shadows overlapped on sidewalks, and once warned an entire coastal village of a tsunami three days before the ocean even thought of retreating. [44] So when the pilgrims began arriving at the Crystal Caves of Elori, drawn by rumors of a place where the earth itself sang, Maren assumed she would see whatever waited inside long before it mattered. [73]

She didn't. [75]

The cave mouth opened like a throat mid-song, and the first thing Maren noticed was that her gift went silent. [94] Not quiet -- silent, the way a room goes silent when someone says the unsayable. [108] She pressed her palm against the crystalline wall and felt nothing: no futures branching, no whispered probabilities, no trembling filaments of what-comes-next. [127] For the first time in three decades, she was as blind as everyone else. [140]

The pilgrim who had arrived just before her was a young woman named Sable, slight and sun-darkened, carrying nothing but a leather pouch at her hip. [165] Sable moved through the entrance corridor with a kind of reverent fearlessness, trailing her fingers along formations that chimed at her touch -- deep resonant tones that bounced between the walls and layered into accidental harmonies. [196] The crystals that studded every surface were translucent, catching the bioluminescent glow of organisms that lived in the cave's moisture, throwing light and shadow in steadily unpredictable patterns. [222] One moment, the chamber ahead blazed with pale blue radiance; the next, it folded into a darkness so complete it felt bright, as though the absence of light had its own luminosity. [252]

Maren followed Sable deeper, and the cave sang louder. [261] Each footstep produced a different pitch depending on where it landed, and the overtones cascaded upward through hollow crystal columns like cathedral organ pipes. [282] The overlapping shadows cast by multiple light sources -- the glowing organisms, the reflective crystals, the faint daylight dying behind them -- created shifting geometries on the walls. [305] Maren watched these geometries and realized they were not random. [314] Something moved through them, inhabiting the spaces where shadow met shadow, using the intersections as doorways. [327]

"You see them too," Sable said without turning around. [335]

"I don't see anything," Maren admitted, and the honesty hurt like swallowing glass. [346] "I feel them. Something in the overlapping shadows." [353]

"Unseen forces," Sable said simply. [358] She opened her leather pouch and held up a small, luminous sphere -- a cave pearl, perfectly round, formed over centuries by mineral-rich water patiently coating a grain of sand. [385] It caught the bioluminescence and magnified it, throwing prismatic light across the chamber. [396] The shadows shifted violently, rearranging themselves, and the cave's song changed key. [407]

"My grandmother found this pearl forty years ago in these caves," Sable said. [419] "She told me it was a seed. That whatever lives here gave it to her so she would bring it back." [438]

"Why?" Maren asked. [441]

"To magnify everyday magic." [445] Sable placed the pearl on a flat crystal formation at the chamber's center. [457] The tone that rose from the contact was so pure it made Maren's sternum ache. [470] The overlapping shadows converged on the pearl, folding over it like hands cupping a flame, and the cave's bioluminescence intensified tenfold. [490]

Then Maren saw. [493]

Not the future -- not the branching, probabilistic sight she had relied on for decades -- but the present, rendered in staggering fullness. [512] She saw the water droplets that had spent centuries shaping the pearl. [523] She saw the organisms pulsing in the moisture, each one a tiny lantern burning with purposeless, exquisite life. [539] She saw the shadows not as absences but as presences: forces that had inhabited this cave since before humans named darkness, forces that were steadily unpredictable because they were alive in ways no prediction could contain. [571]

She saw Sable's grandmother as a young woman, kneeling in this exact spot, weeping for reasons the cave understood and she did not. [594]

The clairvoyant who had never failed to see the future stood in the bright darkness of the Crystal Caves of Elori and, for the first time, truly saw the present. [625] The pearl pulsed gently. [629] The shadows overlapped and breathed. [634] The cave sang a chord that contained every note Maren had ever missed while looking ahead instead of around. [651]

"I didn't see this coming," she whispered, and she was laughing, and the laughter became part of the song. [669]

Sable smiled. [671] "Nobody does. That's the point." [676]

They stayed until the bioluminescence dimmed to its resting glow, until the shadows settled into gentler configurations, until the cave's music softened to a hum that Maren would carry in her bones for the rest of her ordinary, unpredictable, luminous life. [713]
