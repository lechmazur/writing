She turned the empty chrysalis in her gloved fingers, its paper-thin shell catching the light like a translucent fingernail. [34]
Yesterday it had held her sister’s final letter, the one that arrived after the funeral, as if grief itself had pupated and hatched too late. [49]
Mara had come here to scatter the ashes, but the wind had stolen them, whirling the gray cloud into the eddies like river foam, leaving her with only this brittle husk. [68]
She had always solved problems by watching polymers branch and link; now she needed a divergent path that could braid separation back into connection. [84]
The ravine sang around her—water slapping rock, cicadas rising and falling—symphonically minded, the way her sister used to hum while painting wings on synthetic butterflies. [103]
Mara pressed the chrysalis to her ear, half expecting a heartbeat, and instead heard the hollow echo of her own held breath. [120]

An insight surfaced—if she could mimic this architecture in a resin, she could weave a lattice that held absence without breaking. [174]

To atone for refusing the final call because she was in the cleanroom perfecting a batch that would never matter as much, Mara would translate silence into palpable resonance. [248]
She climbed the ravine wall, knees trembling, loosened grief slipping down her cheeks like the chalky runoff after rain. [267]
