The rainwashed library at midsummer smelled of cedar and old paper, the kind of scent that clings to memory like a half-remembered lullaby. [24]  
After the heart attack, Mara returned to the place her father had once called “the only honest casino,” not to gamble, but to widen perspectives responsibly. [38]

Each scrap contradicted the last: “The only way out is further in,” read one, beside a doodle of a clover with four wilted leaves. [104]  
Another: “Probability is just nostalgia for the future.” [112]
