[45] Her gift had always been a curse: to taste memories through touch, to subsume the emotional residue of every surface her skin met. [130] She'd learned to read them like recipes—too much regret made the sand bitter, too much hope left it sticky against the tongue. [235] Her fingers traced the coral walls as she descended, each polyp a tiny mouth whispering stories of the sea this desert had once been. [260] The memories here were different: not human but oceanic, vast and patient and utterly indifferent to the brief sparks of human sorrow above. [285]
