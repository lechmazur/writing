The underestimated rebel, Mira, crouched beside the auric lagoon at dusk, watching the starlight ripple across the water like a secret language. [24]
She had been labeled a nuisance, a street artist whose stencils of winged phones were scrubbed away before sunrise, but tonight she was carefully resourceful, turning the city's indifference into her ally. [45]

Those reels, she had learned, held the revised histories—edited conversations where dissidents vanished mid-sentence, where lovers became strangers, where the tyranny of indifference was manufactured one clipped syllable at a time. [95]
Mira’s plan had unraveled at sunset when the security drones reappeared over the lagoon, their lenses hunting for the heat signature of anyone who still believed words mattered. [117]
