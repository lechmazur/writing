She had crossed the hush of cosmic possibility for three seasons, following rumors of a lock that opened only when the first star seen at twilight whispered its hint. [47]

When the ink settled, she found herself back at the nexus, the gates now gone, only Vega overhead, cooler, satisfied. [335]
The velvet pouch lay empty; the ribbon had become a thin scar around her throat, a quiet collar of improbable longing that hummed when she breathed. [350]
She spoke her first sentence of the new life: “I am no longer the locksmith of what frightens me.” [365]
The words rose as visible constellations, rearranging Vega into a new pattern that future travelers would call the Keybearer, though they would never know her name. [380]
