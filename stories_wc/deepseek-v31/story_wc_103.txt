[48] Her motivation, however, was not merely botanical; it was to discover the texture of time itself, to feel the grain of years as one might feel the grain of wood. [75] The garden was her laboratory, its overgrown state a perfect record of decay. [87]

[128] Her only anchors to that day were a series of fading photographs, their edges blurring and their colors leaching away like sand through an hourglass. [152] They showed her mother, smiling, holding a small, curved shard of polished stone that caught the light like glass. [172]
