[48] This changed when she inherited her grandfather’s rusted greenhouse on the outskirts of the city. [64] The glass panels were clouded with grime and the iron skeleton wept orange tears onto the cracked stone floor. [81] It was a ruin, but standing amidst the skeletal remains of his beloved plants, she felt a motivation ignite within her: to create something beautiful from the ruins. [107]

Tucked inside a dusty, leather-bound journal was a single, iridescent seed, labeled simply ‘Knowledge Fruit’. [124] Her grandfather’s notes, scribbled in the margins, were full of an informally wise charm, speaking of flavours that could rewire a mind, not just recall a moment. [152] "The palate remembers, my dear," he had written, "but the soul learns through synaptic plasticity, forging new paths with every new taste." [175] She decided to plant it. [179]

[210] Each cut was a small act of defiance against the decay, a gentle rebellion against the entropy that had claimed her grandfather’s legacy and the emotional apathy that gripped the city. [244] As she worked, she sampled a withered leaf from a mundane mint plant, and was jolted by the memory of her grandfather patiently teaching a young, frustrated Elara how to properly pinch back the buds for fuller growth.
