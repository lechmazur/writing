[113] It was here she tended to her most crucial project, a sprawling gourd vine. [156] Each gourd, once matured and harvested, became a vessel, its specific seed patterns encoding phrases of rebellion. [177]

[182] They were a secret language club of botanists and dissidents, their conversations held not in words but in the arrangement of petals and the spacing of vegetable rows. [237] A row of marigolds meant a ministry scout was near; a spiraling patch of thyme indicated a safe house was compromised. [262]

[287] But the pressure was mounting, the cognitive meters becoming more sensitive, their constant psychic taxation leaching the color from people’s inner lives. [744] The seeds within whispered now, not of defiance, but of the beautiful, converging chaos of a garden growing wild and free.
