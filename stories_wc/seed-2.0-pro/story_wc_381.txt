The solitary summoner knelt in his nocturnal haven beside a quiet brook, pine boughs woven into a low shelter that blocked the cold autumn wind and caught the faint, fragmented half-truths that defined his work amid whispered secrets. [38]
He had come to this remote spot for one purpose only: to hush a prophecy spoken in code, a string of seemingly nonsensical syllables that had been spreading through village markets for weeks, stoking fear of an otherworldly invasion that did not exist. [81]
The only tool he needed rested on the flat mossy stone before him: a crane's origami wing, folded by a priestess who had died a hundred years prior, its edges worn soft from generations of summoners who had used it to muffle untruths. [124]
His ritual worked by mapping silence, marking each gap between the brook’s gurgle, each pause between the rustle of oak leaves, each lull in the distant hoot of an owl, to weave a barrier that would snuff out the coded prophecy before it could reach more ears. [171]
He closed his eyes, breathing in the damp pine and cold stone scent, and began to pull the first threads of the smoothed hush around him, the stillness growing thick enough to press against his cheeks, even as the brook continued to flow a few feet away. [218]
The power he pulled on was terrestrially cosmic, drawn both from the rich loam beneath his knees and the pinprick stars peeking through gaps in the pine shelter, warm and thrumming without a single spark of the violent energy most associated with summoning work. [262]
This was the tone of his craft, a tranquil blaze that burned bright enough to rewrite scattered untruths without scorching the fragile memories and loyalties of the people who had heard the prophecy’s coded fragments. [297]
He reached out with one calloused finger, brushing the edge of the crane's origami wing, and watched as it glowed with the same soft golden light that wrapped around his shoulders, the smoothed hush expanding outwards to cover first the shelter, then the brook, then the surrounding oak grove. [346]
He tracked each pocket of silence with precise, practiced focus, the method of mapping silence letting him trace the exact paths the coded prophecy had traveled over the past month, each whisper leaving a faint, rippling mark in the gaps between sounds. [388]
The terrestrially cosmic power continued to flow through him, fed by the steady gurgle of the brook and the slow spin of the constellations above, the tranquil blaze of his ritual never wavering, never growing too hot to handle. [427]
When he had mapped every last ripple of the prophecy’s spread, he curled his fingers gently around the crane's origami wing, and pulled the entire web of untruths into the smoothed hush, erasing every last trace of the coded syllables from every mind that had heard them. [474]
He held the hush for three full breaths, making sure no fragment of the prophecy lingered in the air, before releasing his grip on the origami wing, letting the golden light fade back into the loam and the stars, the flow of the brook returning to its normal volume as the ritual ended. [527]
He stayed kneeling for another hour, sipping mint tea he had brewed over a small bed of coals before starting the ritual, watching as fireflies flickered above the brook, their glows matching the faint remaining embers of the tranquil blaze that still hummed low in his bones. [574]
The solitary summoner had long grown used to working alone, to carrying the weight of hushed prophecies and quiet protections that no one else even knew existed, and this small victory felt as bright and steady as the stars above his shelter. [616]
He tucked the crane's origami wing into the leather pouch at his belt, brushing off the stray pine needles that had fallen on it during the ritual, already planning his next stop where another faint, coded whisper had been reported two days prior. [659]
As he packed up his small collection of ritual supplies, he paused for a moment to listen to the brook continue to flow over smooth river stones, the air clear of any lingering untruths, the smoothed hush having settled into the very fabric of the nocturnal haven for anyone who might need it in the future.
