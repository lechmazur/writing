Three weeks after the tower falls, Lila tends to her desert rose farm inside the dented glass dome her father built when she was twelve. [25]
She is the bounty hunter’s pacifist daughter, who refused to carry a sidearm even when raiders scoured the outlands in the tower’s smoldering aftermath. [49]
She is senselessly perceptive, able to spot a single aphid on a rose leaf thirty feet away, or tell when her father is hiding a fresh bullet wound under his leather duster before he even steps through the dome’s airlock. [89]
Tucked in the breast pocket of her linen work shirt is a frayed scrap of canvas from an airship ballast sandbag, a souvenir she fished out of the crater left when the tower collapsed into the old trade port. [128]
This scrap holds what she calls engraved quiet, the faint, steady hum of all the journeys the sandbag took before the tower’s explosion ripped it from its moorings, quiet that outlasted the blast and the weeks of chaos that followed. [168]
Her deepest motivation is to weave continuity through chaos, to turn the fragments of the old world into something that can nurture life rather than tear it apart. [196]
Her chosen method for this work is inculcating plants with values, pressing small pieces of meaningful old-world fragments into the soil around each desert rose seedling to pass their accumulated calm onto the growing plants. [231]
The light through the smudge-streaked dome glass glows with a tranquil blaze at golden hour, painting the pink petals of the desert roses and the tan dust of her work boots in warm, honeyed light that feels equal parts soft and unshakable. [273]
Her father steps through the airlock then, his face smudged with dust, and she watches him pause to sheathe his hunting knife at his hip before he meets her eyes, his usual sharp edge softened by the warmth of the dome. [314]
He holds out a small burlap sack filled with more fragments he collected on his latest run: bits of old passenger manifests, a shard of a pilot’s compass, a scrap of wool from a trader’s coat. [350]
She takes the sack with a small smile, grateful that he has stopped bringing her rations and ammunition as gifts, choosing instead to honor her work even if he does not fully understand it. [384]
Later, as she kneels beside the row of youngest seedlings, she pulls the airship sandbag scrap from her pocket, running her thumb over its frayed edges to feel that familiar engraved quiet hum against her skin. [420]
She digs a small hole in the dry, nutrient-rich soil at the base of the strongest seedling, tucks the scrap inside, and covers it gently, whispering a quiet reminder of the journeys it carried so that the plant will absorb its steady, unhurried energy. [464]
Her father leans against the airlock frame watching her, his hand resting on the sheath of his knife, and for the first time since the tower fell, he does not flinch at the distant sound of a raider siren wailing over the dunes. [507]
The tranquil blaze of the setting sun seeps through the dome, turning the edges of the desert rose petals translucent, and Lila can feel the quiet of the sandbag scrap spreading through the soil, steady and unbreakable. [544]
Her senselessly perceptive gaze catches a tiny new bud forming on the seedling she just tended, so small no one else would see it, and she smiles, knowing her work is already taking root. [578]
By the time the first stars prickle the darkening sky outside the dome, she has tucked half the fragments from her father’s sack into the soil, each one carrying a piece of the old world’s quiet to feed the new life growing in the farm. [623]
She knows that when the seedlings are large enough to transplant, she will give them to families across the outlands, carrying that engraved quiet and continuity to every corner of the region torn apart by the tower’s fall. [661]
Her father brings her a mug of warm mint tea before he leaves for his next run, and for a second, the tranquil blaze of the dome’s interior lights catches the faint, proud smile on his face, the same smile she sees on the faces of everyone who receives one of her desert roses. [715]
Chaos still rages beyond the glass walls, but inside the dome, Lila has built something unshakable, one small scrap and one tiny seedling at a time, weaving quiet continuity into every inch of the soil beneath her feet. [753]
