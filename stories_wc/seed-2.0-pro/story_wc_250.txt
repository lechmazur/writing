He existed within the edge of that half-spoken secret, his days and nights structured around the quiet promise he had made to her as he searched the canal banks for three straight weeks, turning up nothing but her sodden dentist’s bag half-buried in reed mud. [153]

He would weave these patterns across the water and the dock, sending thin streams of light into the canal’s darkest nooks, into the gaps between dock planks, into the shadowed reed beds that lined the bank where his sister had last stood. [312]

He never spoke a word through any of this, his silence not coldness but a form of silent empathy, a way of meeting the dark’s own quiet on equal ground, of honoring the sister who had been too shy to tell anyone she feared walking home alone after dark. [414]
