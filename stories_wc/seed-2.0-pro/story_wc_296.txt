The guarded druid stood at the edge of the cursed lake, the swirling orchard vortex at its core throwing flecks of frosted apple blossom and frigid water against his leather pauldrons, his face set in the warlike serenity he’d cultivated through ten years of fighting corrupted land spirits. [48]
He was here only after the magic trick failed, the illusionist he’d paid three pouches of star moss to scry his missing sister having vanished mid-incantation, leaving nothing but a crumble of ash and a single flesh-eating plant seed tucked in the seam of the scrying mirror’s frame. [96]
The druid’s gift, the quiet magic that let him find lost things, hummed under his skin, pulling him toward the vortex not with urgency, but the steady thrum of a war drum played slow, matching the rhythm of his breath. [136]
He’d spent two years chasing every lead, every half-truth from travelers and spirits alike, driven by the quiet, unshakable motivation to discover what mirrors don’t show: the fate of the sister who’d vanished without a trace the night their village was raided, when he’d been too young and too scared to fight beside her. [190]
He knelt, pressing the flesh-eating plant seed into the cold mud at the lake’s edge, and began to unbend the rigid wards he’d wrapped around his own heart for a decade, letting his grief and his hope bleed out into the soil to feed the magic he was about to call. [241]
The druid had learned long ago that the only way to pierce cursed barriers was through expanding circles, so he traced a ring in the mud around the seed first, then another three feet out, then another, each line glowing with faint golden light that cut through the fog rolling off the lake. [294]
The seed sprouted almost instantly, its thorned vines snaking out along the glowing lines, devouring the curse-tainted mud as it grew, until it reached the edge of the lake and pushed into the frigid water, cutting a path straight toward the swirling orchard vortex. [338]
He followed the vines, his boots planted firm on the tangled thorns that didn’t prick his skin, recognizing the same magic his sister had always woven, the magic that let poisonous plants bend to her will and lost trinkets find their way back to their owners. [384]
When he reached the center of the vortex, he found her frozen in a block of glimmering ice, her hand outstretched, her lips parted mid-speech, the frozen confession he’d been half-terrified to find hanging in the air around her: she’d stayed behind to hold the raiders off, she’d never blamed him for running, she’d been waiting for him to find her this whole time. [448]
The warlike serenity never left his face as he rested a hand on the ice, letting the flesh-eating plant’s vines curl around the block, their acidic sap eating through the curse-frost without harming his sister, his magic guiding their path to leave her skin unmarked. [493]
He’d come here to learn what mirrors couldn’t show, and now he saw it: not a ghost, not a memory of a girl he’d failed, but a sister who’d loved him enough to trap herself in the curse rather than let the raiders follow him to the safe village in the hills. [545]
When the ice cracked and fell away, she stumbled into his arms, her first words a laugh that matched the hum of his find-lost magic, her pockets still full of the shiny stones she’d always collected for him when they were children. [587]
They walked back along the vine path together, the druid still holding the now-dormant flesh-eating plant seed in his palm, the expanding circles of his magic spreading out past the lake to lift the curse from the surrounding land, turning the black, thorny underbrush back into wild blueberry bushes and apple saplings. [639]
The warlike serenity settled deeper in his bones now, no longer a shield against grief, but a quiet promise that he’d never again let the people he loved be lost to the shadows mirrors couldn’t reach. [675]
