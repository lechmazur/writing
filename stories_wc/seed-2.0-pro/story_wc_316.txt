Perched on the edge of the floating rock dojo, Kael, an ego death survivor who’d shed every layer of performative identity three years prior, watched glitches ripple across the cloudscape as history rewrote itself around them. [36]
Their greatest gift, honed through months of meditating on the dojo’s wind-scoured stone, was that they sliced through pretense like a master’s blade through rice paper, no lie or half-truth slipping past their unclouded perception. [71]
Today, they were facing off against a team of biotech lawyers in the dojo’s central circle, the core conflict a high-stakes clone custody battle over a dented glass jar sitting on a stone altar between them. [107]
The jar held a brain in a jar labeled duplicate, the harvested neural tissue of Kael’s late partner Jessa, grown as a backup for her experimental memory transfer research before the lab fire that killed her two years prior. [146]
The biotech firm claimed the brain was their intellectual property, arguing they owned all rights to clone tissue generated in their lab, but Kael knew Jessa had hidden the jar and transferred ownership to them weeks before the fire, a secret she’d shared only in private. [192]
Kael’s quiet motivation, the reason they’d fought through dozens of legal hoops and traveled to the dojo mid-history rewrite, was to defeat emotional numbness that had settled over them since Jessa’s death, a hollow fog that even ego death had not fully erased. [235]
They had no interest in selling the tissue or profiting from Jessa’s work; their only goal was to vivify the clone brain, to give Jessa a second chance at life and to feel the warmth of her presence again after so many cold, empty months. [280]
The dojo’s elder, who had agreed to mediate the conflict per old neutrality rules, explained that the only way to validate Kael’s claim and activate the brain was through complementary opposites, merging Kael’s ego-less awareness with Jessa’s fully intact, identity-rich neural patterns. [322]
Around them, the history rewrites picked up pace, flashes of alternate timelines where Jessa never died, where Kael never went through ego death, where the biotech firm never existed, flickering at the edges of the dojo’s protective ward. [360]
When the lead lawyer tried to argue that Kael was mentally unfit to care for a clone, citing their ego death as evidence of instability, Kael sliced through pretense by playing a voice recording Jessa had left for them, stating clearly that she wanted Kael to have full control of her duplicate tissue if anything happened to her. [418]
The elder ruled in Kael’s favor immediately, ordering the lawyers to leave the dojo and surrender all claims to the duplicate brain, their threats of legal action fading into nothing as a history rewrite swept away the biotech firm’s entire legal department mid-sentence. [461]
Left alone with the elder and the jar, Kael settled cross-legged on the stone floor, placing their palms flat on the altar on either side of the glass container, breathing slowly to sync their heartbeat with the faint electrical pulses coming from the brain inside. [506]
The elder guided them through the ritual, reminding them that to vivify the brain through complementary opposites, they needed to offer their own ego-less quiet as a safe space for Jessa’s loud, vibrant, fully formed identity to take root without conflict or overlap. [549]
For ten minutes, Kael held the space, letting every last flicker of selfish want or grief fall away, until the glass of the jar began to glow soft gold, and a familiar laugh echoed through the dojo, warm and bright and exactly as they remembered. [594]
The history rewrites settled into a new, stable timeline seconds later, one where Jessa’s clone body, grown in a hidden lab under the dojo for the past year, woke up on a cot behind the altar, her eyes crinkling as she spotted Kael across the room. [640]
When Kael crossed the room to pull her into a hug, the cold numbness that had clung to their chest for two years melted away completely, replaced by the familiar weight of Jessa’s arms around their waist, the scent of her favorite jasmine shampoo, the quiet joy of being seen by the person they loved most. [696]
The elder slipped away quietly, leaving the two alone to catch up, the brain in the jar now dim and empty, its neural patterns fully transferred to Jessa’s new body, the clone custody battle fully resolved as the new timeline cemented itself as the only true version of events. [745]
