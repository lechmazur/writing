Marr, the hopeless alchemist, huddled on the cracked basalt lip of the ember nexus at twilight, gloved fingers brushing the splintered oak of a broken bishop’s crozier he’d pulled from a peat bog three moons prior. [36]
Stoically sensitive to the hum of latent magic in even the most ruined artifacts, he’d spent two years chasing every failed experiment, every dead-end lead, all to interrupt a stifling destiny that bound him to spend his life forging low-grade healing salves for the village healer. [82]
The air around the nexus held the sharp, delicate hush of a frozen bloom, frost feathering the edges of his wool cloak even as the glowing embers 20 feet below pulsed with low, thrumming heat. [117]
This was the first night he’d dared bring the crozier to the nexus, and he felt the shift the second he laid the cracked wood on the flat stone altar beside him, the moment when the penny drops that every past failure had not been a waste, but a step. [167]
His goal was to vivify the broken crozier, not by waving a wand and shouting an incantation, but through successive approximation, tiny, intentional tweaks to his ritual each time he’d tested a shard of the artifact’s wood over the past month. [208]
He’d spent weeks wrapping his head around the core concept of creative destruction: the old, rotted magic trapped in the broken crozier would never serve its new purpose if he didn’t first melt down the fragments of its old identity as a symbol of rigid religious doctrine. [255]
He brushed frost from the crozier’s chipped silver finial, the face of a saint worn smooth by centuries of rain and prayer, and drew the first of three runes he’d adjusted 17 times across the wood’s splintered surface. [293]
The embers below flared, sending a shower of golden sparks up to catch on the edge of his cloak, and he did not flinch, his stoic nature keeping his hands steady even as his sensitive core ached for the old magic he was about to unmake. [339]
When the final rune was complete, he lifted the crozier with both hands and tipped it over the edge of the basalt lip, watching as it hit the glowing embers and began to smolder, its oak frame crackling as the old magic seeped out in thin, silver wisps. [387]
Each small adjustment he’d made to the runes, each test burn of a crozier splinter, each failed attempt to channel nexus energy guided the process now, the magic weaving itself into something new rather than dissipating as it had in all his prior trials. [431]
Slowly, the smoldering remains of the crozier began to lift, twisting and knitting themselves into a new shape, a curved staff wrapped in glowing ivy, the old saint’s face replaced with a swirling orb of ember light that pulsed with the energy to uproot the rigid village rules that had trapped him. [483]
He reached out and closed his hand around the staff’s cool, smooth oak handle, and the weight of the stifling destiny he’d carried since childhood lifted, the magic humming in his bones as he realized he’d succeeded, that creative destruction had not just remade the crozier, but remade his path. [533]
The frost that had clung to his cloak all evening began to melt, the frozen bloom hush of the nexus softening into something warm and alive, the twilight deepening into indigo as the staff’s light cast golden streaks across the basalt rock. [575]
He thought of all the times the village elders had called him a hopeless alchemist, wasting his time on trivial experiments instead of leaning into the safe, small role they’d carved out for him, and he smiled, a small, quiet thing that barely moved his lips. [621]
He didn’t know exactly what he would do next, where the staff would lead him, but he knew he would never go back to the small cottage at the edge of the village, stirring salves and pretending he was content with the life others had chosen for him. [669]
As he turned to walk away from the ember nexus, the staff glowing steady at his side, he knew that every failed attempt, every small tweak, every act of breaking what was old to make space for what was new, had been worth every second. [714]
