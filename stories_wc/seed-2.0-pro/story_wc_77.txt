Three days after the resuscitation, I stumbled through the splintered turnstile of the abandoned carnival funhouse, the air thick with the sickly-sweet rot of melted cotton candy and rusted metal that clung to my throat like a half-swallowed secret. [39]
The space hummed with a repulsive allure, cracked distorting mirrors glinting to show versions of me with too many teeth or too long limbs, each warped reflection tugging at my curiosity even as my skin crawled with the urge to run. [80]
Huddled in the far corner, half-obscured by a tattered velvet curtain, was the whispering advisor, his face half-shadowed, a dented brass opera cloak clasp glinting at the throat of his threadbare frock coat. [113]
The clasp was photographically cursed, he’d told me when we first met, any image taken of it trapping a sliver of the viewer’s deepest fear to feed the lingering luminance that seeped from its etched floral patterns after dark. [152]
We’d come here for one reason only: to trick fear into revealing its weakness, so I could finally shake the cold, skeletal shadow that had clung to my bones ever since my heart stopped on that operating table. [190]
Our method was unorthodox, mapped out through paradoxical notes in a dream dictionary he’d stolen from a 1920s medium’s archive, each entry contradicting the last to slip past fear’s usual defenses. [221]
He gestured for me to sit on a splintered wooden crate, then told me to unbosom every shred of terror I’d felt in the liminal space between death and resuscitation, to speak each ugly, tangled thought aloud so the cursed clasp could feed on it. [266]
I hesitated for a beat, then let the words pour out: the weight of nothingness, the certainty I’d never see my sister’s face again, the sharp, burning cold that had seeped into my marrow even as the doctors pounded on my chest. [308]
As I spoke, the lingering luminance of the clasp brightened, casting pale, wavering gold across the warped mirrors, each reflection now showing not my distorted form, but the tall, shadowy figure of my fear, its form shifting between hooded reaper and disapproving stranger and the snarling dog that had chased me as a child. [362]
The whispering advisor leaned in, his voice so soft it was almost lost to the creak of the funhouse’s rotting floorboards, telling me to watch closely, that fear always revealed its softest spot when it was too busy preening for an audience. [404]
Sure enough, as the figure shifted again, I spotted it: a tiny, glowing crack at the center of its chest, exactly the shape of the opera cloak clasp, the same crack I’d seen on the entries of the dream dictionary where the notes overlapped and contradicted each other. [452]
The repulsive allure of the shadow figure grew stronger as it noticed me staring, its form shifting to the face of my dead mother, to the doctor who’d told me I might not make it, to the empty classroom where I’d been bullied for months as a teen, each face pulling at my grief and terror even as I forced myself to hold its gaze. [517]
The advisor whispered that the crack was the space where fear couldn’t lie, the part of it that relied entirely on my belief in its power to exist, and that all I had to do was reach out and press the clasp to that crack to break its hold on me forever. [569]
I stood on shaking legs, the lingering luminance of the clasp warming my palm as I plucked it from the advisor’s coat, stepping closer to the warped mirror where the figure loomed, every instinct screaming to run even as I knew this was the only way to free myself. [618]
As my fingers brushed the cold glass of the mirror, the figure screeched, shifting through a dozen more terrifying forms in the space of a second, but I didn’t look away, pressing the clasp hard against the glowing crack right where its heart would be. [663]
For a second, the entire funhouse was flooded with that soft golden luminance, and I felt every weight I’d carried since the resuscitation lift off my shoulders, the cold in my bones melting away like frost under the morning sun. [703]
When the light faded, the shadow figure was gone, the mirrors only showing my own tired, grinning face, and the whispering advisor was gone too, leaving only the tattered dream dictionary on the crate, its paradoxical notes now faded to blank ink where they’d once overlapped. [749]
I slipped the opera cloak clasp into my jacket pocket, its lingering luminance still warm against my chest, and walked out of the funhouse into the crisp autumn air, knowing I’d never let fear dictate my choices again, that I’d outsmarted it the day I chose to come back to life. [800]
