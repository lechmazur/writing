The gravity defier perched on the rain-slicked stone edge of the glowing viaduct, boots dangling over the still, dark pool 40 feet below. [23]
She hovered in the narrow, stretched timeframe between the drop of a stray raindrop from the viaduct’s arch and the first ripple it would leave on the pool’s surface. [52]
Clutched in her left hand was the ancient clay tablet with runes, its edges worn smooth by thousands of years of passing between versions of herself across fractured timelines. [81]
She was periodically eternal, dying and resetting every time she failed to untangle the mess of contradictory selves that tangled like weeds across every branch of her timeline. [109]
Her only motivation, the one that pulled her through every reset, was to conquer the labyrinth of self, to stitch the broken pieces of who she was into a single, coherent whole that could outlast the reset cycle. [147]
Tonight, she planned to retrace the path of every past version of herself through procedural generation, using the runes on the tablet to map every possible choice she had ever made, every turn she had taken that split her timeline further. [188]
The viaduct’s soft, golden bioluminescent moss cast a darkening brightness over the scene, its glow dimming at the edges as the weight of all her fractured timelines pressed down on the small pocket of frozen time she occupied. [225]
She pressed her thumb to the largest rune on the tablet’s face, and a flood of overlapping images burst into the air between her and the pool, each a snapshot of a different version of her standing on this same viaduct edge at different reset points. [272]
The procedural generation algorithm built into the runes wove the snapshots together into a continuous, branching path, each fork representing a choice that had split her timeline and created another version of her trapped in a separate loop. [310]
She pushed off the viaduct edge, gravity bending around her as she floated over the pool, ready to retrace every branch of the path until she found the root of the fracture that had first split her apart. [348]
As a periodically eternal being, she had tried this hundreds of times before, but each reset had wiped her memory of all but the core drive to fix herself, leaving her with only the tablet as a guide. [386]
This time was different, though, because she had caught the raindrop mid-fall, stretched the tiny sliver of time between the drop and the ripple long enough to hold all the branches of her timeline at once, no reset possible until she reached the end of the path. [433]
The darkening brightness of the viaduct shifted, golden light turning brassy at the edges as shadowy figures of her other selves began to materialize along the branching path, their faces twisted with the grief and regret that had split their timelines in the first place. [478]
Each turn of the path was a new corridor in the labyrinth of self, each shadow self a test: would she reject the part of her that had run from grief, or would she embrace it, integrate it, heal the split that had trapped them all in loops? [526]
She floated past the first shadow self, the version of her that had abandoned her dying sister to run from the pain, and reached out to take its hand instead of turning away, as she had in every previous attempt. [566]
The shadow dissolved into warm light that seeped into her chest, and the runes on the clay tablet glowed brighter, filling in a blank spot on the procedural map that had been empty in all her prior tries. [604]
She moved to the next shadow, the version of her that had lied to her best friend to avoid accountability, and again reached out, pulling that version into herself, and the map filled further, the number of branching paths shrinking as she integrated each lost piece. [650]
The raindrop hung inches from the pool’s surface, the stretched time beginning to fray at the edges as she neared the end of the path, the last shadow self waiting at the center of the now-collapsed labyrinth. [687]
That final shadow was the version of her that had first cursed herself to periodically eternal resets, believing she was too broken to ever be whole, and when she pulled that version close, the entire map merged into a single, straight line. [729]
The raindrop hit the pool, sending a single, unbroken ripple across its surface, the darkening brightness of the viaduct settling into a steady, warm gold as the fractured timelines collapsed into one, and the gravity defier, whole at last, touched down on the water’s surface without sinking, the labyrinth of self finally conquered.
