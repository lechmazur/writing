The first thing that registers, as you drift through the veil of sleep, is the sharp, sweet tang of frost on wild clover wrapping around your ankles. [27]
You are standing in a frozen garden in mist, thin pale light seeping through the cloud cover to paint faint gold streaks across ice-crusted peony bushes. [53]
A low, rustling sound draws your gaze to a hunched figure perched on a cracked stone bench, fur matted with frost and pages of half-charred books sticking out of the gaps in its clawed leather armor: the feral library guardian, displaced from its home realm three cycles prior when a rogue cultist tore a rift between dimensions. [110]
It twists a dented, obsidian cultist's pendant between its clawed thumb and forefinger, the etched eye at its center glowing faintly whenever a ray of light hits its faceted surface. [140]
The guardian is dimensionally homesick, you realize without it speaking, its golden slitted eyes fixed on the hazy line where light bleeds into shadow, the thin, fragile boundary between this realm and the infinite library it once called home. [179]
That line is the boundaries of light, the core concept that has anchored its work for centuries, marking the edges where worlds fray and stories can slip from one reality to the next. [212]
Its chosen action these past months has been to wait, not hunting for the escaped cultist or forcing the rift open to claw its way home, but sitting quiet in this garden, tending to the frost-flecked plants and watching the light shift. [254]
Its method for stabilizing the fraying rift, it explains in a voice like crumpling parchment, is interiorizing exteriors: it stores every detail of this garden, the crunch of frost underfoot, the soft curl of mist around stone, the way light stretches at dusk, in the infinite internal shelves of its memory, weaving this realm into the fabric of its home library to stitch the rift closed from both sides. [323]
When you ask why it does not push harder to return to the home it misses so deeply, it tilts its head, the pendant catching a stray sunbeam to send a prismatic spray of color across the ice at your feet. [364]
Its motivation, it says, is to make friends with uncertainty, to stop fighting the possibility that it might never walk the stacks of its home library again, that this frozen, misty garden might be the new place it belongs. [403]
The air hums with the soft, disorienting clarity of foggy lucidity, the feeling that you are both deeply asleep and wide awake, that the line between your own thoughts and the guardian's memory is as thin as the boundary of light it tends. [446]
It offers you a small, ice-flecked peony bloom, its petals stiff but still holding a faint pink hue, and you tuck it behind your ear, feeling the cold seep gently into your skin. [479]
For a moment, you can see the shelves of its library stretching out behind the mist, leather-bound books glowing with faint golden light, the frozen garden beds overlapping with rows of shelves holding stories from a thousand different worlds. [518]
The guardian says that every time it adds a new memory of this garden to its internal shelves, the rift grows a little more stable, and it no longer cares if stability means going home or staying here forever. [557]
You sit beside it on the stone bench, watching the light shift across the mist, and for a while neither of you speaks, content to wait alongside it, to let uncertainty wrap around you as softly as the mist wraps around the frozen rose arbors. [602]
When you begin to drift back toward wakefulness, the cold of the stone bench fading and the mist starting to clear, you reach up to touch the peony behind your ear, and find it still there, soft and warm, a real souvenir of the dream you were sure you had imagined. [653]
You glance out your kitchen window at the gray, misty morning, and you swear you can see a flash of golden fur and glinting claw through the fog, the guardian still waiting, still tending to the boundaries of light, still learning to love the not-knowing that binds all worlds together. [703]
