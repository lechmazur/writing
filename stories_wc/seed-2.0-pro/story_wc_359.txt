Her mission, passed down across generations, is to certify wonder as essential to all mortal and immortal realms, a task that can only be completed at this exact, threshold-crossing moment of first creation. [136]
The air in the cavern holds a sculpted hush, so dense and intentional she can almost run her fingers over its smooth, cool edges, no wind, no distant bird call, no crack of shifting ice to break the perfect tension of the moment. [179]
The atmosphere hums with oscillating repose, swinging soft between quiet stillness and thrumming potential, like a string plucked and held mid-vibration, ready to sing at the lightest touch. [207]

Green and violet ribbons spill across the sky slit first, seeping into the amber sheet until the etched notes glow with the same iridescent light, and Elara lifts her brass mallet to the first of the 12 ice bells hung in a semicircle around the cavern’s center. [301]
Each strike sends a prism of light arcing across the ice walls, the bells’ song matching the notes on the amber sheet, shifting from warm honey to cool sapphire in Elara’s synesthetic perception as she works her way through the short, sacred composition. [344]
She can feel the sculpted hush bend around the sound, not breaking, but reshaping itself to hold the melody, the oscillating repose tipping gently toward vibration before settling back into soft stillness between each note. [379]
Half a world away, the sculptor pauses for half a breath, his fingers tingling with an unnameable warmth, the marble beneath his chisel seeming to glow for a split second before returning to its cool, unmarked white. [416]
