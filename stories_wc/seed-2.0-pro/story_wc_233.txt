The disenchanted queen had ignored her high council’s final warning three hours prior, slipping out of the silk-draped palace before dawn with only a frayed wool cloak and a tattered page of dream journal tucked in her belt. [38]
For six months, she’d woken each night gasping, visions of glowing blue stone and dripping light seared behind her eyes, and her only driving goal was to unravel the mystery of the recurring dreams that had drained all joy from her royal duties over the past year. [78]
The guards posted at the ridge’s base had been paid off weeks earlier, so she faced no resistance when she began to climb the wind-scoured stone path, her leather boots slipping on loose shale as thin cloud tendrils curled around her ankles, the air sharp with pine and the faint, metallic tang of something not quite of the mortal world. [127]
When she finally pulled herself over the last jagged ledge, she froze: the ember grotto on the ridge sat half-hidden behind a curtain of wispy silver moss, its entrance glowing with the soft blue hue she’d seen a hundred times in her sleep, perched high enough that the entire world below was swallowed by a sea of fluffy white cloud. [179]
This was the cobalt grotto above clouds she’d dreamed of every night, the site the council had begged her not to seek out, framing it as a cursed site that stole the souls of any royal who dared cross its threshold. [218]
She lifted her chin, her voice softly commanding as she ordered the invisible presence she felt lingering at the cave mouth to show itself, no trace of fear in her tone even as the hair on the back of her neck prickled with the unmistakeable hum of ancient, unregulated magic. [264]
When the moss curtain parted on its own, she stepped inside to find the grotto’s walls streaked with bleeding starlight, thin streams of glowing silver liquid dripping down cracks in the cobalt stone to pool in small, iridescent puddles at her feet, each one swirling with fragmented clips of the dreams she’d been having for months. [320]
A tall, hooded figure stepped out from the shadow of a twisted stone stalagmite, and for a long, taut moment they stood staring at each other, neither moving, until a shared exhale spotting the first fireflies of summer broke the tense silence, the tiny glowing bugs drifting between them like scattered, warm embers. [374]
The lacy menace of the space settled over her then, soft and glittering but sharp at the edges, as she noticed that each firefly that drifted too close to the grotto’s walls dissolved into a puff of silver starlight, leaving no trace that it had ever existed. [419]
She reached for the small dagger she’d hidden in her cloak’s inner pocket, but the figure lifted a hand to stop her, its sleeve stitched from the same glowing, bleeding starlight that streaked the walls, the edges sharp enough that she could see them slice through a stray firefly that drifted too close, leaving a faint trail of glowing dust in its wake. [479]
The figure pulled back its hood to reveal a face identical to her own, only older, streaked with the same silver starlight that ran down the grotto walls, and explained that she was the version of the queen that had ignored the same council warning a hundred years prior, trapped in the grotto for daring to uncover the truth that the royal line was built on stolen star magic siphoned from the grotto’s core. [544]
Her recurring dreams, the figure explained, were echoes of the trapped queen’s own memories, designed to draw each new heir to the grotto to try to break the curse that bound all of them to the council’s lies, fed by the bleeding starlight that seeped out of the core with every year the curse remained unbroken. [593]
The queen’s fingers curled around the tattered dream journal page in her belt, and she nodded, knowing that even if she faced the same fate as the woman standing in front of her, she would not leave until she had unraveled every last thread of the secret that had poisoned her family’s reign for centuries. [643]
The trapped queen smiled, and gestured to the deepest part of the grotto, where a glowing core of pure starlight pulsed behind a wall of lacy, sharp-edged silver mist, waiting for someone brave enough to reach past the menace and set it free. [691]
