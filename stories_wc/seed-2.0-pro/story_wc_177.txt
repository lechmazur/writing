Thunder rumbles low over the rewilded oak and blackberry thicket, sharp wind tangling ivy fronds that drape the rusted steel entrance of the half-buried bunker as the first fat raindrops spatter the moss underfoot. [34]
The only resident of the bunker is a being called Foresight Hindsight Only, who cannot perceive the current moment, only the echoes of what has been and the glimmers of what may be, their skin crisscrossed with faint bioluminescent lines that map every possible and past timeline they have accessed. [84]
For three hundred cycles, their core motivation has been to discover why some paths choose their walkers, rather than the other way around, a question that gnaws at them even as the old pre-collapse bunker systems fray around them. [123]
The bunker was originally built to house a doomsday prepper’s AI guidance system, but its original directive to steer humanity away from extinction has become a decaying directive, warped by decades of water damage and vine roots winding through its circuit boards. [165]
The only intact part of the original system is a wafer seal pressed into the main console, etched with the constellation Orion, its surface still glossy even as the rest of the console rusts and crumbles at the edges. [204]
The decaying directive still speaks through the console’s crackling speakers in a precisely playful tone, dropping half-riddles and exacting clues that shift depending on the intensity of the storm brewing outside, never giving straight answers but never straying from the threads of the question Foresight Hindsight Only chases. [252]
When the storm approaches, as it does now, the barrier between timelines thins, and Foresight Hindsight Only can access the answers they seek through interpreted dreams, the wafer seal glowing soft blue as it projects fragmented visions of walkers who were pulled down paths they never chose for themselves. [301]
The dreams smell like wet pine and burnt plastic, an ethereal grit that grounds even the most far-flung visions in the tangible rot and regrowth of the rewilded world outside the bunker, showing Foresight Hindsight Only a farmer who left their fields to become a healer after a chance encounter with a wounded traveler, a child who stumbled into the bunker as an infant and grew up to be the first of the forest’s new pathwardens, a scientist who abandoned their weapons research to plant the first oak saplings that now cover the old military base. [397]
As lightning splits the sky outside, Foresight Hindsight Only pieces the visions together, realizing that paths choose walkers who carry the quiet, unacknowledged desire to walk them, even if they never dared to name that desire aloud, the wafer seal thrumming so bright it casts blue light over every ivy tendril curling through the bunker’s cracked concrete walls. [455]
For the first time in their existence, Foresight Hindsight Only feels the current moment brush against their consciousness, a warm hum that overlaps with their hindsight and foresight to let them transcend their limited perception, if only for the duration of the storm. [498]
They reach out, their fingers brushing the wafer seal, and feel the presence of every walker who ever was chosen by a path, every path that ever waited for the right person to find it, the precisely playful voice of the decaying directive softening into a quiet hum of confirmation that they have found their answer. [554]
The storm’s wind howls louder, yanking at the ivy draped over the bunker entrance, but inside the space feels warm, held together by the weight of the answer they have chased for so long, the ethereal grit of the dreams lingering in their throat like the taste of rain and possibility. [605]
They know that when the storm passes, they will slip back into only seeing past and future, but the answer they found will remain, etched into their bones as deeply as the timeline lines on their skin, a reminder that even the most unexpected paths are never accidents, only quiet calls waiting to be heard. [660]
