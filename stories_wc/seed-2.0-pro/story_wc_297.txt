The cobalt glade at dusk hummed with bioluminescent moss, its edges softened by the purple haze of a world where the future had arrived three generations early, uninvited and unmoored from the old pacts. [34]
Perched on a half-rotted oak stump at the glade’s center was Lira, the root-bound visionary, her calves fused into the soil below with gnarled, vine-like roots that tied her to the glade’s collective memory. [68]
Purposefully curious, she had spent every waking moment since the future’s early arrival tracing the cracks in the eclipsed covenant, the ancient agreement that once let glade guardians walk between waking life and the shared dreamscape of every living thing within a hundred miles. [112]
Her only motivation, the thing that kept her from sinking fully into the soil and giving up when the covenant first shattered, was to find the intersection of dreams, the hidden crossroads where she could renegotiate the pact and stitch the waking and dreaming worlds back together. [159]
Three days prior, a wandering herbalist had left her a small, gnarled maze topiary clipping, its tiny, twisted branches carved to mirror the layout of the old dream roads that had vanished when the covenant broke. [195]
A jolt of anxious elation had shot through her when she first held it, because she knew the clipping was not just a trinket, but a key, if she could only figure out how to use it. [232]
Last night, a voice in her half-dream state had told her to heed the faint thrumming in the scars that laced her forearms, each one earned during a failed attempt to cross into the dreamscape after the future arrived. [271]
She had spent the entire day testing that advice, pressing the clipping to each scar, until she realized the method was linking through scars, pressing the cut end of the topiary clipping to the oldest, deepest scar on her left wrist, the one she had gotten when she first swore to uphold the covenant as a teen. [328]
The second the rough, woody end of the clipping touched the raised, silvery line of her scar, the glade’s moss flared bright blue, and the air filled with the soft, chiming sound of dream roads waking from their slumber. [367]
Anxious elation bubbled higher in her chest, making her hands shake, as she watched the tiny branches of the topiary clipping unfurl, growing into a full-size maze of glowing hedges that stretched from her feet out to the edges of the glade, its paths twisting exactly like the dream roads she remembered from before the future arrived early. [425]
She did not have to walk, because her roots stretched deep enough to follow every path of the maze at once, tracing the turns until she felt the warm, familiar pull of the intersection of dreams, a soft golden light glowing at the maze’s exact center, just a few feet from where she sat. [479]
The golden light wrapped around her like a blanket, and she heard the voices of the old covenant guardians, the ones she had thought were lost forever when the pact was eclipsed, greeting her by name and thanking her for not giving up even when all hope felt lost. [528]
She took a slow, shaky breath, her anxious elation softening into quiet, steady hope, as she laid out her case for renegotiating the covenant, explaining that the early arrival of the future had not erased the need for connection between waking and dreaming, for people to hold space for each other’s hopes and fears even when the world felt unmoored. [588]
The guardians listened closely, and after a long, quiet moment, they agreed to restore the covenant, tying its new terms to the scars on her arms and the maze topiary that now grew at the glade’s center, so no future disruption could ever erase it again. [634]
As the golden light faded and the maze hedges shrank back into the small clipping still pressed to her scar, Lira smiled, knowing she had done what so many thought impossible, mending the rift between worlds for every generation that would come after her. [678]
