[15] I watched it settle onto the rusted perch beside the window, its talons scraping iron oxide flakes that drifted down like dead leaves. [38] My atlas lay open on the table, its pages a tired geography I had edited for years, removing phantom islands and shifting borders with a dry comment always ready on my tongue. [170] The bird had been my scout for two winters, tracing watersheds on wing while I charted conjectural headwaters in ink. [191] The dog-eared phrasebook had belonged to a linguist who vanished here before me, and the question he’d marked had become my quiet obsession. [214] Rivers flowed into every map I corrected, but their origins always lay in blank spaces, deferred by better cartographers than me.
