[27] Dawn had not yet arrived, but the great lens already swept its silent beam across the dark sea. [45] He was a quiet man named Elian, the assistant keeper, who had learned to shrink his voice beside the head keeper's bellow. [67] This morning, he would not shout. [73] He would prove that whispers travel farther than shouts. [82] In his palm lay a tiny vial cork, its edges worn soft by years of anxious handling. [99] The cork had once sealed a bottle of night-blooming jasmine oil that his mother blended to beckon moths from the darkness. [120] Decades later, it still held a ghost of that scent, a memory of her pure intent to call fragile things gently. [141] Elian believed the cork had absorbed her purpose and now purified intentions.
