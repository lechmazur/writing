Lyric stood at the edge of the ancient terrace, the ebony clipboard cool and familiar against his arm. [18] The midsummer air tasted of sun-warmed stone and wild thyme, a scent he had memorized across two thousand years. [37] Above him, three moons waltzed into conjunction, their slow dance a silent chime only the heavens could hear. [55] He was the forever war peace negotiator, an immortal consecrated to ending the blood feud between the Concord of Radiant Suns and the Kallian Empire. [80] For millennia he had been operatively gentle, his every syllable and gesture a soft blade sheathed in velvet, disarming war-hardened ambassadors who would flinch at a raised voice. [108] Tonight he had to muster the full weight of that gentleness to anchor ideals in practice, for the treaty on his clipboard was the last hope. [134] The delegates had mustered in a semicircle behind him, their robes whispering against the pale stone. [150] He turned and gave them a nod, the movement so careful it seemed to slow time. [166] The ebony clipboard held a parchment scrolled with terms that had cost him three wars, two betrayals, and countless nights of bargaining. [188] Through ritual significance alone could this pact be sealed; the alignment of the moons was the only witness old enough to hold an oath of this magnitude. [215] The ancient terrace itself was a ruin, cracked and overgrown with wild roses, a place of flawed beauty where every treaty attempt before had shattered like glass. [242] But tonight's celestial dance offered a narrow window, and the price of redemption had finally been named. [259] He ran a finger along the clipboard's edge, the black wood humming with stored promises. [274] To redeem the galaxy from the forever war, he must surrender the very thing that made him the perfect negotiator: his immortality. [296] The method was inscribed in the ritual: at the apex of the conjunction, he would press his palm against the clipboard's surface, and it would drink his endless years, leaving him mortal, fragile, and bound as the treaty's living guarantee. [336] His motivation had never wavered—to anchor ideals in practice, to turn the shimmering dream of peace into a breathing reality. [357] But the flawed beauty of that dream cut him; he would finally taste the world as it was, with all its endings. [477] The three moons locked into a perfect ring, a slow dance culminating in a silent hush. [493] He laid his hand flat on the clipboard, and a tingling warmth crept up his arm. [517] He felt his endless years peeling away, a deep hollowing that was almost gentle, as if the cosmos itself was being operatively gentle with him. [569] He looked up at the flawed beauty of the terrace, the roses now smelling sweeter because they would one day rot. [590] The war was over, and the price of redemption was paid.
