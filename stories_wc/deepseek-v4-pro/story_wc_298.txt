Kael stepped deeper into the catacomb orchard, where twisted apple roots snaked between ribs and femurs as if the dead had been planted to nourish the trees. [27] The half-lit corridors glowed with phosphorescent fungi that painted his skin in moss-light, a faint blue trembling on his scarred knuckles. [48] He carried a burned tarot card, its edges curled and blackened, the image of two lovers charred but still legible beneath the soot. [71] That card was all that remained of his sister Lyra's original fate, severed six years ago in a desperate barter to save his life. [95] Kael's touch on the burnt paper was fiercely gentle, as though he could press the scorched fiber back into wholeness by will alone. [118] He had come here to the orchard's heart because he was a reckless idealist who believed that no barter was final. [139] He meant to patch the holes rotting through Lyra's soul before her spirit unraveled entirely. [154] For weeks he had been inlaying meaning everywhere along these bone-laced corridors, carving tarot sigils into ribs, pressing yew berries into eye sockets, whispering fragments of old songs into the loam. [185] Tonight, within the waning half-lit glow, he would trammel the very root that had swallowed Lyra's future, snaring it with a cord woven from her hair and his own. [214] He located a thick apple root coiling around a shattered pelvis, pulsing faintly with a sickly amber light. [232] Kael knelt, his resolve dusky as the fungus-glow began to dim, and laid the burned Lovers card over the root's knot. [253] With fiercely gentle fingers, he looped the hair-cord around root and card like a net meant to capture a stray breath. [274] The trammel tightened of its own accord, biting into bark. [284] He felt the root shudder as the barter of destinies rushed through the underground veins. [299] "I am here to patch the holes," he whispered, his voice carrying the dusky resolve that had driven him through years of searching. [322] The burned tarot card ignited in cold flame, not consuming but illuminating, revealing a tapestry of bartered threads woven through the orchard's deep dark. [346] He saw Lyra's thread, a frayed silver line yanked from a larger weave, and his own, a vibrant gold still whole. [367] Without hesitation, the reckless idealist reached into the vision and tore a piece of his golden thread free, offering it to the hungry root. [391] The root took it, and the trammel constricted, weaving the two threads together before snapping away the burned card into ash. [465] He had patched Lyra's soul, but the reckoning was that he must now bear the holes himself, his destiny forever bartered in gentle fragments. [489] He rose, fiercely gentle even in his own undoing, and left the card's ash in an inlaid hollow he had carved into a femur months ago—meaning laid into every gesture. [519] The catacomb orchard accepted the offering, roots snaking tighter around bones as if to remember the new bargain. [537] Kael walked back through the half-lit corridors, guided only by the dimming fungus and the weight of a patched, imperfect soul. [558] He had no regrets, only dusky resolve settling deep as the roots that held his traded thread. [575] Far above, beyond the catacomb's mouth, Lyra would wake from her long wasting, unaware that her brother had bartered his own completeness for her smile.
