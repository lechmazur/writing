The nectar dusk bled gold across the silent city, and Mara, the toll keeper of the Lightning Collection Tower, watched the empty bridge and spoke aloud to no one. [29] She could not keep the truth from her lips, not ever, and so she said, "No one will cross tonight, and I will sit here with the dead bees and the dark." [91] Mara lifted her eyes from the lacquer box in her lap, its surface inlaid with mother-of-pearl bees frozen mid-flight. [110] The box held the last true queen, a tiny golden speck that she had tended in secret since the drone engineers had abandoned flesh for circuitry. [136] She had been made toll keeper because her involuntary truthfulness made her useless for any job requiring pleasantries, and here her words could do no harm. [162] Yet when a child stepped out of the twilight and onto the bridge, Mara blurted, "You are the first living thing I have seen in a week, and you carry a matchbox that smells of phosphorus and old paper." [201] The girl, thin and sun-browned, held out the matchbox without flinching. [212] "I was told you would speak the truth," she said, "and the riddle inside this box says only a queen of nectar dusk can wake the hive." [239] Mara could not help herself; she laughed, a raw, truthful sound. [250] "Then you have found your queen," she said, opening the lacquer box to reveal the dormant golden speck nestled in a bed of wax. [274] The child stared at the queen, and Mara knew her own truth would spill out again, so she let it. [294] "I kept her alive hoping the drones would need her one day, because a tower that collects lightning should hum with more than silence, and a toll keeper's life is too small to lose its wonder." [476] The queen shivered, and in that instant the drone beehives below thrummed to life, their wings blurring into a song that swept through the tower and out across the city's dark grid. [573] "Your toll," she said, "is to carry this box to every dark corner and show them the riddle, so they remember that a hive never truly dies if someone keeps the queen."
