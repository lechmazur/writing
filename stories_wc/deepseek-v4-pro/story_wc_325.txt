[25] She was a spelunker by trade, yet every descent was a war against her own lungs, which clenched at the mere idea of enclosed spaces. [76] The codex was a research log from the Hadal Array, a forgotten undersea facility that once eavesdropped on ocean secrets through a web of undersea cables. [108] Lena could track invisible things—ghost currents of electricity, magnetic whispers of ancient transmissions looping through the cables like stranded songs. [126] She had inherited the gift from her father, the Array’s engineer, who taught her to feel what instruments missed. [142] Now she navigated the flooded labyrinth between tidal changes, the brief window when the sea retreated enough to let a human pass through a world once air, now mostly water.
