Liria moved through the umbral grotto with the quiet purpose of a committed caretaker, her bare feet silent on the cool stone. [22] The air hung in a vineyard hush, heavy with the scent of wet moss and the memory of souls. [41] Outside the grotto’s mouth, the world ended in a soft floor of cloud, stretching unbroken to the horizon like an infinite field of white grapes waiting for dawn. [69] She paused by the still pool that mirrored the cavern’s ceiling, where veins of phosphorescent lichen cast a faint, silvery glow. [90] From the pocket of her robe she drew an antique hairpin, its shaft darkened by centuries, its head a meticulous silver scallop shell. [113] She scalloped her thumb across the ridged surface, a kindly decisive gesture that soothed the ache of ages. [131] The hairpin was the one object she had carried from her final human life, the life that had ended after she forgave the unforgivable. [155] In that life, a beloved sister had betrayed her for an inheritance, leaving Liria to perish in a locked tower. [175] When the soul of that sister later passed through the grotto, wracked with guilt, Liria had greeted her with a gentle touch and a word of release. [202] She had meant it. [206] Yet forgiveness had not brought contentment. [212] Liria remained in the grotto, tending the souls that drifted up from the world below, guiding them toward their next rebirth with a steady hand and a heart that never turned weary. [244] She knew the cycle of rebirth intimately: each soul a droplet in a great river, forever rising, falling, returning. [263] But knowing did not quiet the question that stirred in her own core—where does contentment truly dwell? [281] When the question grew too loud, she would climb. [290] At the grotto’s edge, a gnarled tree twisted upward from a crevice, its branches piercing the cloud floor and vanishing into the quiet above. [314] Climbing trees to think better had been her method ever since she arrived; the act of rising, hand over hand, helped her untangle the threads of longing. [341] Today, she ascended quickly, her fingers finding hold on bark worn smooth by countless climbs. [356] She burst through the cloud layer and settled into a high crook, where the sky was a dome of deep indigo and the clouds below gleamed like a second ocean. [386] From this perch, she could watch the souls arrive. [395] They floated upward as soft motes of light, gathering at the grotto’s entrance before diving into the pool where their memories dissolved. [417] Liria observed one soul hesitate, a pale shimmer that seemed to look back at the world it was leaving. [436] She understood that hesitation. [440] In her palm, the hairpin felt warm. [447] She scalloped her thumb over it again, slower this time, feeling each groove of the shell-shaped head. [464] The motion unlocked a clarity she had not anticipated. [473] Contentment, she realized, was not something to be found like a hidden pool, but something she had been practicing all along. [494] It was the vineyard hush after the last soul was released, the precise weight of the hairpin in her hand, the way the tree accepted her weight without judgment. [523] It was the act of caretaking itself, the perpetual kindness offered without hope of return. [538] She smiled, and it was the kindliest decision she had ever made. [550] Leaning forward, she pressed the antique hairpin into a crevice of the branch, a deliberate gift to the tree that had held her. [573] Then she let go. [577] The fall was not a fall but a release. [586] Liria’s body dissolved into a cascade of light that mingled with the clouds, and for the first time she felt herself entering the cycle of rebirth she had so long tended. [617] The committed caretaker became the cared-for. [623] As her consciousness scattered, the last sound she perceived was the vineyard hush of the grotto, now holding the promise of her own return. [647] The tree held the hairpin in its wood, a small silver scallop shell shining under the indigo sky. [665] High above the clouds, the umbral grotto waited, patient and quiet, for the next keeper to climb. [682]
