The gargantuan shell spiraled upward from the abyssal plain, a nautilus the size of a fallen moon, its inner chambers threaded by an off-key wind that never rested. [28] Within its pearlescent walls, the insomniac poet sang half-sung lullabies that the wind snatched and warped into a shyly unstoppable current through the spiral. [52] She had not slept across centuries of longing, her eyes open maps of every star she had lost. [70] The galaxy postcard she kept, a rectangle of stiff luminescence depicting a spiral nebula the exact twin of her shell, had been a gift from a traveler who dissolved into the interstellar reefs before the armistice was even a whispered word. [109] The postcard throbbed with a distant, unreadable message, and in its gloss she saw her own elongated reflection: a gaunt, finned creature with gills that fluttered in erratic rhythm, a poet whose only remaining grammar was photosynthesis. [146] To photosynthesize pain into beauty had become her sole motivation, a process she rehearsed each century by crooning lullabies until the shell’s inner skin began to glow with a cold, reef-shadow joy. [178] Yet the blossom armistice, that final flowering truce between memory and marrow, eluded her, because she still clung to the postcard as proof that the vanished traveler had been real. [206] Tonight, under the slow pendulum of a deep-sea moon, she decided to ensconce the postcard where it could finally germinate. [226] She swam upward through the coiled hallway, her fins whispering, the off-key wind pushing her lullabies back into her throat until she hummed them silently. [251] At the shell’s apex, a secret whorl cupped a pocket of still water where light fractured into chromatic splinters. [270] There she ensconced the postcard, tucking it into a crevice lined with nacre finer than moth wing. [287] As her fingers withdrew, the off-key wind caught the half-sung lullaby she had been sheltering and blasted it through the shell’s entire architecture in one shyly unstoppable exhale. [315] The nacre ignited, and the shell began to bloom. [324] Anemones of every lost color erupted from the walls, soft polyps that opened like mouths remembering a song. [342] This was the blossom armistice: a thousand miniature flowers perching on the scaffold of centuries, each one a truce sealed between the silence of the abyss and the memory of galaxies. [373] The postcard dissolved into a spiral of light that threaded the new-born reef, no longer a relic but a seed. [393] For the first time across centuries of longing, the poet’s gills stopped fluttering and drew a deep, even breath. [412] The half-sung lullabies, no longer needed as alchemy, became simply joy, a reef-shadow joy that dappled her skin in jade and violet and let shadows exist without sorrow. [439] She had photosynthesized the pain, yes, but more, she had ensconced the very act of letting go, and in that quiet hollow, the armistice flowered. [463] Sleep, that elusive visitor, found her curled exactly where the light spiral cradled the shell’s apex, her fins tucked like folded poems. [485] The off-key wind softened into a lullaby of its own, a melody that had always been inside the shell waiting for the right throat to release it. [512] Anemone tendrils brushed her eyelids, dusting them with bioluminescent pollen that tasted of forgotten star-routes. [527] The poet dreamed, a new sensation, of a traveler made of nebula gauze, extending a hand that was also a familiar glyph from the postcard’s flip side. [552] That glyph spelled ‘armistice’ in a language that required no translation, just the blossoming of what had been armoured. [571] When she woke, centuries later or perhaps only a tide’s turn, the shell was no longer a hollow monument but a living library of all the pain she had sung into beauty. [603] The reef-shadow joy had settled into a permanent twilight, a state of grace where light and shade no longer struggled but danced. [625] The insomniac poet, now a well-rested keeper of the blossom armistice, still sang half-sung lullabies on occasion, but only to remind the shell of its shyly unstoppable power to transform. [655] And the galaxy postcard, now pure light threaded in the whorl, continued to photosynthesize whatever stray sorrow drifted in from the deep, turning it into petal and coral and the soft off-key hum of a universe at peace with its own echoes. [697] She never counted the ages after that, for in every exhale the shell whispered the word she had once only dreamed: blossomed. [719]
