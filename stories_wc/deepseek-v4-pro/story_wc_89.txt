The fortune read: "The bond you seek is not lost, but waits in the furrows of your own marrow." [72]
She had always been a beast whisperer, her soul attuned to the silent languages of claw and feather, to the whispers of fur and the confessions of scales. [100]
But the phoenix was different—a creature of fire and rebirth who had shared her hearth and heart until the predestined morning when it combusted into a new dawn and rose, leaving her with only ash and the echo of a song. [142]

To reforge a bond beyond death, she must shed her human form and become something else entirely—a creature capable of ascending into the phoenix's fiery retreat, a beast forged of longing and starlight. [210]
The orchard offered its gift: rare spices hung in the air—cassia buds, star anise, and threads of saffron—their scent a language older than sorrow, a hymn of forgotten gardens. [241]
She recalled the fortune cookie's second, hidden message, printed in tiny script on the reverse: "Conjure via the scent of rare spices." [263]
Summoning every thread of her paradoxically steady courage, she began. [273]

The smoke did not dissipate but gathered like a second orchard of shifting, aromatic stars. [349]
From its center, the phoenix's voice, a remembered flame, whispered: "You must become the beast you wish to follow." [368]
A reluctant metamorphosis—that was the price. [375]
