<story>After the marquee darkens and the ticket window clicks shut, I wake to my second life between velvet seats and cobwebbed spotlights.[22]
They call me the encore-seeking stage ghost, but I am only a listener with a stubborn appetite for repeats.[41]

Here the pears chime when brushed, and the plums hum a low note that sours if your heart lies.[102]
I am synesthetically blessed, cursed, saved, depending on your taste for paradox: I hear color, I taste footsteps, I see the pitch of silence.[126]
The orchard is tuned to me, or I to it, because time here is a fractured timeline pruned into spirals.[146]
Dates grow like fruit, futures like blossoms, and the props of discarded scenes dangle as scarecrows to frighten off despair.[166]
I come because a girl in the wings, Rowan the assistant gardener, has started pocketing the trowels and talking to the exit signs.[189]
She tends the maze by day, she waters this stage by night, and lately she thinks possibility has died.[208]
I want an encore for her, not applause but a reprise of courage, and I know how to lure possibility from the brink of despair.[233]
I do it by producing echoes, coaxing the orchard to play back a tone of her better hours.[251]
Echoes are seeds here, and seeds are time, and time is thread, and thread can intertwine if you hum it steady enough.[273]
I carry a hand-painted tarot coin, a souvenir once tossed from a balcony into my final curtain.[290]
One side shows The Star, silvered and chipped; the other is a blank that accepts the day's light and keeps it safe.[312]
I flip it at the path where the maze tightens, and the coin ticks as if counting breaths for a drowning stagehand.[334]

Rowan enters after midnight, hands cracked, eyes steady from crying the day's last tears before the new ones assemble.[398]

Behind the corridor is the old prop shed, its roof caved, but inside waits the blueprint she once sketched for a community nursery built from junk.[495]
She had abandoned it during the winter of gray budgets, when forms refused signatures and the city ledger gave the theater a shrug.[518]
Now the paper smells like cinnamon bronze, the scent of courage to me, and the notes I coax sound as violet as summer dusk.[542]

I am no savior, but I know my lines, and the orchard knows its cues, and together we can move a curtain of weather.[711]
Morning comes with the crew and their coffee, and Rowan brings a ladder, and the hedge-shadows brighten as if lit from memory outward.[734]
