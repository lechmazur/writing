Elara had never learned to breathe in small spaces. [9]
Her father, the bunker builder, carved tunnels into mountains like a man hollowing out fear itself. [25]
He believed the world above would crumble; Elara believed it was the world below that was already crushing her. [44]

Elara would press the doll's cold cheek against her own whenever the corridors narrowed. [93]
The bunker stretched six levels deep, and each level held its own silence. [106]
Elara had made it her purpose to collect the flavors of different silences. [119]
There was the dry, mineral hush of Level One. [128]
The wet, dripping quiet of Level Three, where condensation wept from pipes. [140]

She carried a notebook filled with descriptions no one else would understand. [235]
Silence, she had decided, was intangibly tangible — you could hold it the way you held breath, feel its shape without ever closing your hand around it. [261]
