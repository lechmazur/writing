[40] Today, his work was interrupted by a memory: a faint, persistent static shock from the lab’s experimental interface three days ago, a tingle in his finger that had lingered long after the current was cut. [68]

[110] The pill was meant to grant temporary, safe neural communion; now, the recall suggested its pathways were tangled, its connections unstable, a dangerous illusion of understanding. [132] He had to know exactly what had gone wrong, not just for his own peace of mind, but to illuminate the hidden pathways the corporation wanted to keep dark. [151]

He sat at his central console, the muscovite geode sliver he always kept there gleaming under a focused beam. [190] The recall was the problem; devising a way to trace the pill’s flawed architecture in his own mind was the task. [204] His method, born of his artistic intuition and scientific rigor, was to work through somatic markers. [219] The static shock wasn’t just a memory; it was a physical echo, a somatic brand left on his nervous system.
