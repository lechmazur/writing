<story>Elena Vasquez had not spoken her own name aloud in eleven days, which suited the solemnity of her work aboard the Archives of Meridian--a zeppelin library that drifted along the scarred coastline, collecting the histories that burned too hot for state repositories. [43]

The encryption was not alphabetical. [141]

It was spatial--each symbol corresponded to a location along the coast, and Elena realized the diary was a map disguised as confession. [164]
