<story>Maren had carried the crocodile's tooth for three years, sewn into a leather pouch against her sternum, where it pressed against her ribs each time she breathed. [27] Her grandmother had pulled it from a riverbed in the delta country and told her it would keep the dead from clinging. [43] What her grandmother hadn't explained was that the tooth didn't repel them—it attracted them, drew them close enough to speak. [63] And Maren, the lost soul collector, had learned to listen. [72]

She stood now at the lip of Echo Gorge, where the rock walls curved inward like cupped hands. [86] The gorge was famous among travelers for its acoustics—sound didn't just bounce here, it multiplied, split, reassembled itself into something almost living. [110] A whisper could become a choir. A scream could become music. [120] Maren had come because the gorge held more than echoes. [128] It held the residue of every voice that had ever passed through, layered into the stone like sediment. [144]

In the hush of a hidden sunrise, she settled on a flat stone and unstrapped the instrument she'd carried from the market in Sedara—a small harp, its frame slightly warped from years of neglect. [183] The merchant had laughed when she bought it, said it was firewood with strings. [197] But Maren knew that perfection left no room for the dead to enter. [210] The misaligned harp, with its uneven tension and bent tuning pins, produced notes that wavered and split, notes that created cracks in the ordinary world. [240] Through those cracks, the spectral confessions came. [249]

She plucked the first string. [254] The note bent sideways, and the gorge took it and stretched it until it sounded like someone exhaling after holding their breath for decades. [283] The air around her cooled. A shape condensed—not a body exactly, but the outline of one, translucent as river ice. [305]

"I pushed him," the shape said, and its voice was the voice of the gorge, multiplied and softened. [322] "My brother. He fell from the ridge and I didn't reach for him. I watched." [341]

Maren was modestly fearless—she did not pretend the dead couldn't touch her, and she didn't pretend their words couldn't wound. [366] She simply sat with it, the way you sit with a wound you know will scar but not kill. [385] "I hear you," she said. [391]

She played again. [395] Another note stumbled from the harp, and another shape rose from the gorge's depths, this one smaller, trembling. [416]

"I stole the bread from my daughter's plate," it confessed. [429] "She was so thin. I told myself she wouldn't notice. She noticed. She always noticed." [450]

These were the spectral confessions—the unspoken truths the dead carried, heavier than stone. [469] They came to Echo Gorge because the gorge held their voices, amplified them, refused to let them dissolve. [490] They came to Maren because she was the one who pursued them, not with force but with patience, with that crooked harp and her quiet presence. [519]

She had been collecting lost souls for six years now, traveling from village to ravine to forgotten temple. [541] She'd heard confessions of murder and neglect, of jealousy that had calcified into cruelty, of love withheld when it mattered most. [566] Each one landed in her chest like a stone dropped into still water. [581] She carried them. That was the work. [589]

But lately she'd begun to feel the weight of them, the accumulated sorrow pressing her spine into a curve. [611] She had started forgetting how to laugh, and laughter—she was certain of this—was the missing element. [633] Not laughter as denial, but laughter as release, as the sound a soul makes when it finally unclenches. [656] To integrate laughter into healing was her motivation now, though she didn't yet know how. [676]

The third spirit appeared in full, a woman with hollow eyes. [691] "I never told my mother I forgave her," she said. [703]

Maren plucked the misaligned harp, and the note that emerged—wavering, imperfect, almost comically off-key—struck something in the gorge that made the stone hum. [735] And in that hum, Maren felt it: a tremor at the corner of her mouth, involuntary and aching and luminous. [757] The hollow-eyed woman saw it and smiled, and the smile was the first thing about her that wasn't translucent. [781]

The hidden sunrise broke through, spilling gold across the gorge. [795] The spirits faded, but the echoes remained—layered now with something warmer, something that bent the way the harp's notes bent, imperfect and alive. [827] Maren touched the crocodile's tooth through her shirt and stood. [840] There were more voices to pursue. [848] And for the first time in years, she wanted to hear them. [860]</story>
