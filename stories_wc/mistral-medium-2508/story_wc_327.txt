The copper-veined storyteller, Veyra, had spent thirty years tracing the edges of silence in the Canyon of Endless Echoes, where the wind carried voices that no living throat had shaped. [31]

Her hands, lined with thin rivers of oxidized metal, moved with quiet articulation as she adjusted the flawed diamond at her throat—a stone said to curse those who listened too deeply. [55]

The diamond was not hers by choice, but by inheritance, passed from a line of translators who believed texture could be rendered into sound if only the right resonance was harnessed. [80]

The towers of bone rose around her, the remains of some ancient leviathan’s spine, their hollow chambers humming with the last gasps of a dying language. [105]

She had come here not to mourn the language, but to catch its final breath before it dissolved into the canyon’s hungry echoes. [127]

Above her, a flock of starlings wheeled in sudden, shifting patterns—no mere chaos, but a script written by unseen hands. [145]

Veyra knew the method: the birds’ flight was a cipher, their turns and dives mapping the contours of sounds too fragile for human ears. [165]

She pressed the diamond to her lips and exhaled, watching the birds fracture and re-form as her breath fogged the cursed stone. [183]

The canyon answered—not with words, but with the *texture* of them, the grit of consonants lost to time, the silk of vowels unspoken for centuries. [206]

It was working. The diamond was harnessing the echoes, pulling them through the patterns of the flock like a net through water. [228]

But the language was dying for a reason. Some sounds were not meant to be revived. [242]

A tremor ran through the bone towers, a groan like a voice waking from a thousand-year sleep. [257]

Veyra’s fingers tightened around the diamond as the echoes twisted, no longer passive reflections but something *alive*, clawing at the edges of the canyon’s silence. [283]

The starlings scattered. The patterns collapsed. [288]

She should have stopped then. But the copper in her veins burned with the same hunger that had driven her ancestors—translate it *all*, even the untranslatable. [313]

The diamond pulsed against her palm, warm as a heartbeat. The echoes coalesced into a single, shattered word, a name that had not been spoken since the language’s birth. [337]

The canyon *inhale*—not wind, but the sound of a thousand throats drawing breath at once. [351]

Veyra understood too late: the diamond had not been cursed for those who listened. It was cursed for those who *answered*. [372]

The bone towers began to sing. [377]

Their voices were the texture of crumbling parchment, the sound of a blade dragged across old wounds. [390]

She tried to run, but the echoes wrapped around her ankles, thick as chains. The copper in her veins turned to ice. [407]

And echoes, after all, are just sounds that have forgotten how to die. [543]
