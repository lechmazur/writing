<story>On the orchard planet of Pomarae, floating rock arches drifted above the apple groves like slow blue thoughts. [18] Elan was the village blacksmith, a meek man whose hammer spoke louder than his voice. [33] He lived in the season the elders called the time between understanding and confusion, when the harvest failed and no one knew why. [56] The orchards had turned to rubble after a quake split the arches, and the people slept too deeply, as if dreaming had become a sickness. [81] A traveling magician arrived, offering bartered awakenings: for the right price, he could rouse a sleeper, but each waking cost something dear. [103] He traded in memories, in years, in the colors a person loved most. [116] Elan's wife, Mira, had fallen into that endless sleep, and the magician named his fee: the blue from Elan's eyes, the azure he had inherited from his mother. [144] Quietly determined, Elan refused to surrender his color like a coin. [155] He suspected the magician's power lived not in him but in the magician's silk, a shimmering cloth he draped over each sleeper before the bargain. [180] So the blacksmith made a deliberate plan, slow and careful as cooling iron. [193] Beneath Pomarae's groves ran abandoned mine shafts, where miners once chased the glowing ore that kept the arches afloat. [212] Using those forgotten tunnels, Elan moved unseen below the magician's tent, listening through the rock for the secret of the silk. [233] Night after night he carried his anvil-callused patience down into the dark, learning the magician's rhythms. [249] He overheard the truth: the silk did not wake people at all. [261] It stole what it was paid, then merely let sleepers stir for an hour before they sank again, hollowed. [280] The magician fed on the slow surrender of a whole village, harvesting its will the way blight harvested its fruit. [300] Elan's motivation hardened into something molten: he would coax resilience from rubble, teach his neighbors to wake themselves. [318] From the mine's glowing ore he forged a single bell, tempering it with the azure defiance he had refused to sell. [339] He poured into the metal every quiet refusal of his life, until the bell rang with a color the silk could not steal. [362] On the night of the great market, the magician spread his silk over Mira and reached for his payment. [381] Elan stepped from a mine shaft hidden beneath the tent and struck the bell. [395] Its blue tone rolled outward, threading through every street and root and floating arch. [409] The sleepers stirred, and this time their eyes held their own colors, bright and unbartered. [424] The silk thrashed like a dying fish, finding nothing left to take. [436] The magician begged for one trade, then another, but the meek blacksmith only kept ringing. [451] Each toll said the same quiet word: no. [459] Mira woke fully, gripping Elan's hand, and around them the village remembered how to want things again. [476] Stripped of its hunger, the silk dissolved into ordinary thread, and the magician fled into the orchard dark. [494] In the seasons that followed, the people learned the lesson the bell had taught. [508] No one could awaken them but themselves, and resilience could be coaxed even from rubble. [523] They cleared the fallen arches, replanted the broken groves, and hung Elan's bell in the tallest standing arch. [541] When the wind moved through the floating stone, it rang with that stubborn azure note. [556] Elan returned to his forge, still meek, still quiet, but no longer mistaken for weak. [571] He had lived through the time between understanding and confusion and forged, from both, a deliberate defiance. [588] And every harvest after, the apples grew bluest nearest the singing arch.
