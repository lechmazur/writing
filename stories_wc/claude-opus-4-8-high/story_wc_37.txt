<story>In the mirror world, every sunrise had been stolen and folded away, leaving the land to drown in endless night. [20] I am Ysolt, the scattered memory keeper, and my mind is a basket of broken threads. [36] Each fragment I hold belongs to someone else, for I gather the recollections others have lost. [52] They sent me to the alchemical orchard greenhouse, the last warm place in a dying realm. [68] Beneath its glass dome, trees of copper and amber once distilled light into fruit. [82] Now the branches hang grey, and the soil forgets how to bloom. [94] My task was simple and impossible: to rekindle the realm before the night swallowed it whole. [110] At the orchard's heart stood a mica window, its thin sheets glittering like layered frost. [125] The old gardeners said it remembered every dawn the realm had ever known. [138] But memory alone cannot bind light; it must be sung back into being. [151] So I knelt among the dying roots and began the only craft I trusted: weaving dawns through forgotten songs. [170] Each melody I pulled from my scattered hoard had once belonged to a mother, a sailor, a child. [188] As I hummed, the mica window drank the sound and shivered with pale color. [202] Thread by thread, the songs braided themselves into ribbons of light. [213] I gathered those ribbons as a weaver gathers wool, and began to swaddle the nearest sapling in warmth. [231] The little tree drew the woven dawn into its bark, and a single amber bud opened. [247] It was small, but in the endless night, even one waking bud felt like a sunrise. [263] Yet I faltered, for each song I gave away thinned my own fragile mind. [277] A memory keeper who spends her hoard risks forgetting even her own name. [290] Still, I sang the lullabies of strangers, the work chants of vanished towns, the harvest tunes no one recalled. [309] And as the orchard brightened, something unexpected happened beyond its glass. [320] People came, drawn from the dark like moths toward a remembered hearth. [332] They recognized the songs, though none could say from where. [342] An old man wept at a tune his grandmother had once hummed. [354] A widow joined her voice to mine, and the harmony knits communities together where fear had scattered them. [372] I understood then that my emptying mind was not a loss but a gift returned. [387] Every memory I released found its rightful owner among the gathering crowd. [399] The mica window now blazed with a hundred layered dawns, each pane a different morning reborn. [415] Children pressed their palms to the glass and sang back what they had just learned. [430] Their fresh voices wove new dawns I had never carried, dawns the realm had not yet dreamed. [447] Light spilled from the greenhouse and crept across the mirror world's frozen fields. [460] Where it touched, the soil remembered green, and distant villages lit their long-cold lamps. [474] I felt myself growing lighter, hollowed of the burdens I had carried for so long. [489] It was a bound release, the loosening of a knot tied across countless lonely years. [504] When the last song left me, I could no longer recall my own childhood. [518] But the orchard was ablaze, the trees heavy with humming fruit, the people singing as one. [534] The widow took my hand and asked my name, and I smiled, for I had forgotten it. [551] So she gave me a new one, sung softly, the way you cradle a newborn against the cold. [569] In the mirror world, dawn was no longer a thing stolen but a thing we wove together. [586] I had emptied myself to fill a realm, and through forgotten songs, the night finally broke.
