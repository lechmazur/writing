[349] She had promised to sandpaper their rough memories smooth, and here, finally, was the surface to work. [453] The ice warmed beneath her hand, and the swirl loosened, no longer trying to subsume her. [469] Sefa pulled her up, and through the touch Mira felt the keeper's dread soften into something like hope. [487] They climbed back to the fortress as the rain thinned, leaving the dunes bright and washed. [503] In the kitchen, Mira cooked a single bowl of broth, the recipe her brother had loved. [519] Each spoonful tasted of him, but the bitterness was gone, replaced by something sturdy and warm. [535] She had kept the promise from their unspoken dream, sanding the rough grief until it could be carried. [553] The fortress, brittle from centuries of stored sorrow, felt a little less likely to crack. [568] Sefa sat across from her, taking a careful sip, and her eyes filled with borrowed memory. [584] "You could teach this," the old woman said, "and empty the pavilion pool by pool." [599] Mira thought of all those frozen souls, each rough memory waiting for a patient tongue.
