<story>After the last lighthouse keeper abandoned the coast, the arboretum perched on its seaside cliff became the only thing still tending the dark. [23] Naila tended it now, the way she tended everything since the wedding. [35] She had been a guest when the drone struck the open courtyard, and she walked out of the smoke while others did not. [58] In her pocket she carried a crumpled photograph of the bride and groom laughing, the only thing she had grabbed before running. [80] The paper had softened from handling, its edges gone gray, but the faces still grinned at a future that never arrived. [101] People found their way to her along the cliff road, the sleepless and the haunted, because word spread that she was quietly talented at one rare thing. [128] She could teach a panicking body to freeze its spiral before it dragged the mind under. [144] She did it through somatic markers, small physical anchors she helped each visitor discover in their own flesh. [162] A thumb pressed to a wrist, the cool ridge of a fern leaf, the weight of both heels against stone. [182] "Find the pearl of stillness," she would say, "the one calm grain you can return to when everything else churns." [202] One evening a young man named Soren climbed to the gate, shaking, certain the world was ending again. [220] He had survived his own catastrophe and now mistook every loud gull for a returning machine. [236] Naila walked him among the salt-bent trees, pressing his palm flat to a smooth-barked trunk until his breathing slowed. [255] "Feel the bark, name three things, let your shoulders drop," she instructed, and his racing pulse began to settle. [274] He asked her how she stayed hopeful when she of all people had reason to despair. [290] She drew out the crumpled photograph and let him look at the laughing couple. [304] "I do not pretend the world is kind," she said. [314] "I only want to anchor optimism in realism, so it does not blow away the first hard wind." [332] She explained that false hope shatters, but hope braced against truth can hold. [345] The photograph reminded her that joy had been real, even if it had been brief. [360] That was the grain she returned to, the small unbroken thing inside the wreck. [374] Soren studied the gray faces and understood that her stillness was not numbness but discipline. [389] Below them the abandoned lighthouse stood dark, no longer warning ships, its work inherited by these patient trees. [407] Naila had decided that someone must keep a light, even a quiet living one. [421] Over the weeks Soren returned, learning to freeze his panic at its first tremor instead of its peak. [439] He began guiding newcomers himself, pressing their hands to fern and bark and stone. [453] The arboretum filled, slowly, with people who had learned to locate their own pearl of stillness. [469] They did not forget what had been taken from them. [479] They simply refused to let the taking become the whole of who they were. [493] On clear nights Naila walked the cliff path alone, the crumpled photograph warm in her pocket. [509] She pressed her own heels into the stone, naming the sound of the surf, the smell of pine, the steadiness of her breath. [532] The teacher still needed her own somatic markers, and she was not ashamed of that. [547] Realism told her the dark was vast and indifferent and would not soften. [560] Optimism told her that one tended grove on one wounded cliff could still teach a frightened body how to be calm. [581] Between those two truths she planted her life and let it grow. [593] And in the keeperless dark, her quiet, living light held. [603]</story>
