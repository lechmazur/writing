<story>At the tilt of the earth toward spring, Mara climbed the zephyr bluff in mist. [15] She had been a burned spy for three years, her name struck from every ledger, her face forgotten by the agency that once owned her. [40] The bluff held a silver hush, as though the world had agreed to listen and say nothing back. [58] In her coat pocket she carried an engraved pillbox, its lid etched with two crossed reeds, a gift from the handler who had betrayed her. [83] She had come to read the codes in accumulated snow. [93] Years ago, agents who could no longer trust radios or paper had pressed their messages into the drifts here, stamping letters with boot heels and gloved knuckles, trusting the cold to keep them. [126] The drifts on the zephyr bluff never fully melted, and beneath each thaw new symbols surfaced like the rings of a frozen tree. [149] Mara knelt and brushed the top layer aside, prudently daring, knowing one wrong footfall could erase a sentence written by a dead friend. [172] The message she sought belonged to Iversen, the only colleague who had refused to sign the report that burned her. [192] Before he vanished, he had promised to vouchsafe the truth that would clear her name, hidden where only she would think to look. [215] She had not come merely to fix what was broken in her file. [228] She had come to find where her unused courage waited, the years of caution she had spent hiding instead of acting. [249] The mist parted in a slow breath, and there in the snow were the crossed reeds, the same emblem as her pillbox. [271] Below the symbol ran a line of pressed dots and dashes, brittle with frost. [285] She read them slowly: a bank, a city, a name that proved the handler had sold the network for money. [305] Iversen had not run; he had buried the evidence here and let the winter guard it until she was brave enough to return. [328] Mara opened the engraved pillbox, expecting the cyanide tablet the agency issued to every field officer. [344] Instead she found a tiny coil of microfilm, slid there long ago by Iversen's careful hand. [360] He had vouchsafed the proof twice over, once in snow and once in steel, so that no single thaw could erase her redemption. [383] The silver hush deepened, and Mara understood that fixing what was broken had never meant restoring the past. [401] It meant carrying the truth down the bluff and forcing it into the light. [415] She photographed the snow with a small camera, then memorized the message in case the mist turned to rain. [434] The tilt of the earth toward spring meant the drifts had only days left, and the codes would soon run away as water. [457] She had timed her return precisely, prudently daring once again, arriving while the evidence still held its shape. [475] For three years she had told herself she lacked the nerve to confront the people who had erased her. [494] Now she saw that her courage had not died; it had waited here in the cold, patient as Iversen's message, until she came to claim it. [520] She closed the pillbox over the microfilm and pressed it to her chest. [533] The zephyr that gave the bluff its name rose then, thin and cold, scattering the mist into ribbons. [551] Below her the valley appeared, gray and waking, roads thawing toward the city where the handler still walked free. [570] Mara stood, brushing snow from her knees, and the silver hush released her like a held breath finally let go. [590] She began her descent toward spring, no longer burned but tempered, carrying a dead friend's faith and her own reclaimed nerve. [611]</story>
