[30] Now humankind spoke in gestures and glances, and the old languages lay folded away like winter coats. [47] Iren herself was seasonally mute, her voice surrendering each autumn and returning only with the thaw. [63] So she had learned to speak through kinetic sculpture, building machines that moved and rang instead of arguing. [81] On the bluff stood her greatest work, a tower of suspended bells and turning arms. [96] Wind threaded the structure and coaxed it into slow, deliberate music. [107] Iren called these performances symphonies of truth, for each note answered a question the world could no longer phrase aloud. [127] Her motivation was simple and stubborn: to preserve the color of wonder before silence drained it gray. [144] For that, she needed one missing voice. [151] At the tower's heart she fitted an iceberg fragment, carried up the slope wrapped in wool and patience. [186] As the bells turned, their hammers grazed the frozen surface, and it began to glisten in the thin sunlight. [205] Each drop that melted released a sigh of older air, tuned by Iren to a particular pitch. [222] She worked with muted vigor, her hands quick yet her face calm, conserving every motion. [237] Autumn had already taken her speech, so she could not call out to the figures gathering below the clouds. [256] They came because the bells reached them, climbing through fog like remembered names. [269] Children arrived first, drawn to the spinning arms and the shivering, sunlit ice. [282] Iren watched their eyes widen, and that widening was the color she fought to keep. [297] Wonder, she believed, was the one truth that needed no translation. [387] But the fragment was melting, and Iren knew her instrument could not last. [400] Each note spent a little more of the ancient ice. [410] So she chose to teach instead of merely perform. [479] Iren stood back, breathless with that muted vigor, and let them play. [491] She could not speak until spring, but she did not need to. [503] Her symphonies of truth would continue through kinetic sculpture long after she fell silent for the season. [520] The wonder she had guarded now lived in many small hands. [531] Below the bluff, the clouds glowed with the last melted light of the fragment. [545] Iren raised one hand, and the children answered with a chord. [556] It was enough. [559] In a world after words became unnecessary, she had found a language that could still glisten. [575] The harmonious bell tuner closed her eyes and listened to the future ring.
