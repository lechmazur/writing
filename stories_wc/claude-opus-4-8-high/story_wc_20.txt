<story>After the search party disbanded, Mira returned alone to the rain-washed foundry. [12] She was a balanced alchemist, trained to hold opposites without letting either burn her. [26] Tonight the balance felt like a lie. [33] Water threaded through the broken roof and pooled around the cold crucibles. [45] Her brother Aldous had vanished here a month ago, chasing a process he called factually mystical. [61] He meant that the magic he studied obeyed laws as strict as any furnace, measurable and merciless. [78] In her palm she carried his last possession: a zodiac coin, twelve signs ringing a single eye. [95] The coin was cracked, split clean across the constellation of the Scales. [107] Aldous had always said names had weight, that to speak a thing truly was to lift its whole history. [126] She had laughed at him once, and that laugh was the regret that now glowed in her like banked embers. [146] She had come not to mourn but to redeem the moment she had dismissed his life's work. [163] Kneeling by the largest crucible, she laid out her tools and a small dish of gold dust mixed with lacquer. [183] This was kintsugi, the art of mending breakage with gold so the wound becomes the most luminous part. [201] But Aldous had taught her that intentions could be poured like metal, that kintsugi intentions could rejoin more than ceramic. [221] To mend the coin, she had to mean the mending; she had to understand the weight of his name. [240] So she spoke it: Aldous. [245] The foundry answered with a tremor, rainwater shivering into rings. [255] She learned the only way the mystical permitted: by exploring limits, pressing the coin's halves together until her fingers ached. [275] Each time she failed, the gold refused to hold, sliding off like water from a name said carelessly. [293] She pushed further, deeper into the cost, recalling every promise she had broken with him. [308] The Scales were his sign, the emblem of balance she had inherited and he had envied. [324] He had broken the coin himself, she now understood, to leave her the heavier half to carry. [341] Naming the weight aloud, she felt the regret turn luminous instead of bitter. [354] The gold caught, threading the crack with a seam brighter than the original stars. [368] In its mended face she saw not Aldous restored, but a message etched where the fracture had been. [386] He had crossed a limit no search party could follow, into the law beneath the laws. [402] His final note, written in light, said simply: balance is not stillness, it is carrying both halves. [419] Mira closed her hand around the warm coin and let the rain wash her face. [434] She had not found his body, and she no longer needed to. [446] By meaning the mending, she had redeemed the careless girl who once laughed at weight. [461] The factually mystical world had given her exactly what its laws allowed: not a brother returned, but a brother understood. [481] She stood in the rain-washed foundry until the crucibles gleamed like patient eyes. [494] Then she pressed the zodiac coin to her lips and spoke every name she had ever wronged. [511] One by one, she lifted their histories, feeling each true weight settle into her balanced hands. [527] The regret remained, but it shone now, a seam of gold along the cracks of who she had been. [546] An alchemist's true work, she finally knew, was not to erase the break. [559] It was to mend with intention until the wound became the proof of love. [573] Mira walked out into the storm, lighter for carrying more, and finally able to say his name without breaking. [592] Behind her, the foundry's last ember dimmed, and the rain kept its quiet, golden vigil. [607]</story>
