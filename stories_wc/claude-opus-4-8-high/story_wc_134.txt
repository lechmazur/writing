<story>Mira had foreseen every visitor to the musical crystal cave for forty years. [13] She knew their faces in the chiming dark before they ever ducked beneath the entrance. [28] Yet after the pilgrim arrived, the clairvoyant who didn't see it coming finally met her blind spot. [45] He came at dusk, robe gray as ash, and the crystals greeted him with a low, surprised hum. [63] Mira had not glimpsed his arrival, and the absence frightened her more than any vision ever had. [80] "You feel them too," he said, meaning the unseen forces that drifted between the singing walls. [96] She nodded, though she had spent her life pretending the cave's magic was only her gift. [112] The pilgrim explained that he had walked for years to find a single cave pearl, born grain by grain in the dripping dark. [135] Such pearls, he claimed, could inhabit a person's shadow and magnify everyday magic into something vast. [151] Mira laughed, but the sound came out steadily unpredictable, wobbling between hope and dread. [165] Together they descended to the cave's lowest gallery, where the crystals played their brightest, darkest chords. [181] There, resting in a stone cup of black water, lay the pearl, pale and faintly glowing. [197] As they leaned close, their two shadows fell across it, overlapping into one larger darkness. [212] The pearl brightened, and through overlapping shadows the unseen forces poured upward like water finding a crack. [229] Mira gasped as visions returned, flooding her mind with every future she had ever lost. [244] She saw the pilgrim's true purpose at last: he meant to let the forces inhabit him forever. [261] He wanted to carry the cave's small wonders into the gray towns beyond, to magnify everyday magic for people who had forgotten it. [284] But the pearl could only bind to one shadow, and now their shadows were tangled together. [300] "It will choose," the pilgrim whispered, his voice trembling in the bright darkness. [313] Mira understood then why she had never foreseen him. [322] The pearl had already decided, long ago, that she would be its keeper. [335] Her own future had been the one thing hidden from her, because the choice was hers alone. [352] She looked at the pilgrim, weary and faithful, and she thought of the gray towns starving for light. [370] For forty years she had hoarded the cave's music for herself, calling it a gift instead of a duty. [389] So she stepped back, pulling her shadow away until only his remained over the pearl. [404] The glow surged, and the unseen forces flowed into the pilgrim, settling into his outline like ink into cloth. [423] He wept, and where his tears struck the floor, tiny chimes bloomed in answer. [437] "Why give it up?" he asked, for he had seen her longing too. [450] "Because magic kept in a cave is only a secret," she said, "but magic carried out becomes a song." [469] The pilgrim climbed toward the dusk, his shadow now humming faintly with borrowed light. [483] Mira stayed among the crystals, and for the first time their music sounded entirely new. [498] Her gift was gone, yet she felt unburdened, free at last to be surprised. [512] In the years that followed, travelers spoke of a gray pilgrim whose shadow made flowers sing and lamps burn warmer. [532] And in the musical crystal cave, the clairvoyant who once saw everything learned to live within the steadily unpredictable dark. [552] She still kept the empty stone cup, its black water reflecting the bright darkness overhead. [567] Sometimes a new cave pearl would begin to form there, slow and patient as a held breath. [584] Mira tended it without foresight, trusting the unseen forces to choose their next wanderer. [598] When pilgrims arrived, she welcomed them, no longer the keeper of fate but the keeper of wonder. [615]</story>
