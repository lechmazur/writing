<story>Mira had been an aviation meteorologist for thirty years before the string broke. [13] The string was the cable tethering her sky gondola to the old observatory tower. [27] When it snapped one dusk, the gondola drifted free into the dusty starlight above the plateau. [43] She did not panic. [47] She had spent her whole career learning that the air keeps records, and she trusted it now. [64] Beside her sat a brass telescope, dented and warm, that had belonged to her brother Tom. [80] Tom had been a balloonist, and he vanished in a sky like this one twenty years ago. [97] His balloon was never found, only the empty coordinates where it should have been. [111] Mira had built her life around a single stubborn idea: that empty spaces remember. [125] She wanted to prove it, to show that absence was not erasure but storage. [139] To do this, she had invented her own quiet science: categorizing clouds by emotion. [153] Grief clouds were flat and gray, holding their weight without releasing it. [165] Joy clouds piled upward, ridiculous and bright, daring the sun to climb them. [178] And then there were the quietly explosive clouds, the ones that looked calm but carried thunder spooled inside. [196] Those were the clouds that reminded her most of grief: spooled wholeness, a complete storm wound tight and waiting. [215] As the gondola rose on a warm updraft, Mira raised the brass telescope to her eye. [231] Through it she did not search the stars but the dark gaps between them. [245] Tom had taught her that the spaces between stars were not nothing; they were rooms. [260] Tonight, drifting where he had drifted, she meant to memorialize him properly at last. [274] Not with a stone, which marks a place, but with the air, which carries everything everywhere. [290] She found the coordinates by instinct, the empty pocket of sky her instruments had recorded twenty years before. [308] There, in that nothing, a cloud was forming. [316] It was one of the quietly explosive kind, calm-skinned, humming with held lightning. [329] Mira understood then that the empty space had been keeping something all along. [342] Moisture that was once breath, warmth that was once a man, gathered and spooled into wholeness. [358] The plateau below had forgotten Tom, but the air had not. [369] She lowered the telescope and let herself simply watch. [378] The cloud brightened from within, a slow pulse, like a heartbeat learning its own rhythm again. [394] Then it released, not violently, but as one long exhale of light across the dusty starlight. [410] Rain fell upward first, caught in the gondola's lantern, then drifted down toward the plateau like an answer. [428] Mira wept, and her tears joined the same circulating water that had once been her brother. [444] This was her proof, written in cloud and rain instead of stone. [456] Empty spaces remember; they hold what we lose until we are ready to read them. [471] The broken string no longer frightened her; the air had always been her tether. [485] She read the wind the way she read clouds and turned the gondola toward the tower's distant lamp. [503] Catching a current of joy clouds rising, she let them carry her home. [516] Before she descended, she aimed the brass telescope once more at the emptied coordinates. [530] The space was clear now, calm, its long-held storm finally spent and free. [543] She whispered goodbye, and for the first time the word did not feel like losing. [558] It felt like filing something carefully where it would always be found. [570] The next morning, the meteorologists at the tower logged an impossible rain over dry land. [585] Only Mira knew it was a memorial, and that the sky had agreed to keep it. [601]</story>
