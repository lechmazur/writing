<story>Mira had always drawn what others overlooked. [7] As a natural history illustrator, she rendered beetles and ferns with a patience that bordered on devotion. [24] But her truest obsession could not be pinned to a page. [35] She wanted to find the place where echoes are born. [45] The librarian-priests of the upper towers told her it lay above the city, where the rooftop canals wove between spires like silver thread. [68] To reach it, she would have to cross the entire rooftop canal system before sundown. [83] That was impossible, the priests admitted, unless she borrowed time. [93] They gave her a pendant shaped like an infinity symbol, warm as a held breath. [108] It sculpts time itself, they said, but only across a giant's nap. [120] Beneath the towers slept the Mountain Giant, and while it dreamed, the pendant could stretch each minute into hours. [139] When the giant woke, the borrowed time would be reclaimed all at once. [152] Mira climbed at dawn, sketchbook strapped to her back, and stepped into the first canal-boat. [167] She closed her fingers around the pendant and felt the world slow. [178] Water that had rushed now crept; gulls hung frozen between wingbeats. [189] She poled through the high channels, and time obeyed her like a tide held back. [204] In the slowness she sketched everything: lichen blooming on copper roofs, a heron mid-stoop, the architecture of stillness. [222] Hours of her own seemed to pass within a single suspended heartbeat of the city. [237] By midday she reached the heart of the canal system, where eight channels met beneath a hollow tower. [255] From its open throat came a sound she recognized: the beginning of a melody that never resolved. [272] It rose, gathered, promised an ending, and then broke off into silence. [284] An interrupted cadence, hanging forever on the edge of completion. [294] Mira understood then what echoes truly were. [301] They were not reflections of finished sounds, but the unfinished ones, the cadences that no one had let resolve. [320] Each echo waited in this hollow tower to be born into completion. [332] Within the tower hung a cage of woven light, and inside it the trapped melody beat against the bars. [351] To let the echo be born, she had to uncage it. [362] But the lock was old, the latch intricate, and her time was running thin. [376] Far below, she felt the giant stir in its sleep. [386] Mira gripped the infinity pendant harder, borrowing more time than the priests had warranted. [400] The pendant grew hot, its endless loop glowing white. [409] Stillness deepened until even her own pulse hesitated, and in that sculpted hour she studied the latch like a specimen. [429] She drew its mechanism in her sketchbook, and in drawing it, understood it. [442] Her hands moved; the latch fell open; the cage of light unwound. [454] The trapped melody surged free and found its final note at last. [466] The interrupted cadence resolved, and the resolution rang outward across every tower as a single, perfect chord. [483] Every unborn echo in the city was born in that instant, completed all at once. [498] And the giant woke. [502] Borrowed time rushed back like water through a broken dam. [512] The world snapped into speed: gulls dove, canals roared, the suspended afternoon collapsed into a single breath. [529] Mira clung to the boat as the channels surged beneath her. [540] The pendant cracked, its infinite loop finally closed, its borrowed hours spent. [552] She did not mourn it. [557] In her sketchbook lay the drawing of the latch, the heron, the hollow tower, the architecture of a paused world. [577] She had illustrated not a creature but a moment, the exact place where echoes are born. [593] And as the city sang its newly completed chords back at her, Mira finally heard her own work answered. [612]</story>
