<story>The platform hung between two thunderheads, a single floating rock carved into a tea ceremony platform, suspended in the saturated hush that comes only as a breath between storms. [48] She had climbed here through the eye of the weather to perform a single, impossible task during a held breath. [68] Her teacher had warned her that the runes of release would only answer in that fragile pause, when the sky stopped to inhale. [113] Years of terror had left her half-statue, and she had come to shed that crust at last. [130] Her motivation was not to forget the old wounds but to transform scars into stories, to make every glassy ridge a sentence she could finally read aloud. [216] Scholarship had failed; the grand grammar of runes refused to come. [280] She remembered counting the seconds between lightning and its growl, certain the numbers told you how far the gods were standing. [301] These small, foolish certainties had once made the world bearable. [311] Now they loosened her fear, and the quartz slowed its advance. [322] Mira lifted the cup and drank, and the bitter tea tasted of childhood courage. [336] She pressed the crow quill's trimmed tip against the largest scar on her forearm, the one shaped like a river. [356] Instead of a rune, she wrote a word. [364] Then another, and the scar accepted ink the way dry earth accepts rain. [377] She was no longer carving spells; she was carving sentences, telling each scar what it had survived. [457] Her superstitions had not been wrong, only incomplete; the crow feather did carry wishes, but only when you wrote them down. [478] When the last scar became a line of small, steady letters, Mira set down the quill. [494] Her arms were bare now, lettered like a manuscript, every wound a chapter she could speak without flinching. [512] She breathed out at last, and so did the sky. [522] The second storm broke over the platform, but its rain only washed the table clean. [537] Mira tilted her face upward, no quartz forming, no fear left to harden. [550] She had walked into the breath between storms a frightened girl and would walk out a scholar of her own history. [571] The crow quill, its trimmed tip worn smooth, she tucked behind her ear like a child keeping a lucky charm. [591] Some superstitions, she decided, deserved to be kept.
