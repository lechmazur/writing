[40] There she gathered sea glass, smoothing each frosted shard until it caught what little light remained. [56] Her village feared fire, for a blaze had once swallowed their homes, and so lanterns were forbidden after dark. [75] Yet the children still cried for light, and Mira longed to share light without using fire. [91] So she practiced the slow craft of gem polishing, believing the answer waited inside the sea's discarded glass. [109] As memories resurfaced of her mother humming beside the same tide, her hands moved with patient certainty. [126] Her mother had once whispered that the calmest water holds a pearl of stillness, a quiet that could steady any frightened heart. [148] Mira would weigh each polished piece in her palm, judging which fragment might carry that stillness best. [165] The mist around her felt like a zephyr bloom, soft petals of air drifting through the cavern's serpentine bends. [184] Night after night she polished, and slowly each shard grew reflectively vibrant, glowing with borrowed moonlight. [200] But moonlight alone was thin, and the children needed something brighter than a fading silver gleam. [216] One evening, weighing a curved green shard, Mira understood her mother's true meaning. [229] Stillness was not the absence of motion but the gathering of scattered light into one calm surface. [246] If she arranged the polished glass around a single pool, the water itself could become a mirror. [263] She spent the next days fixing each reflectively vibrant piece along the grotto's quiet basin. [278] The arrangement caught the dim sky and multiplied it, each fragment passing brightness to the next. [294] When the moon rose full, the pool flared into a soft constellation that needed no flame. [310] Mira hurried to the village and led the trembling children down through the misted, serpentine passage. [326] They gasped as the grotto opened into a chamber bright as dawn, yet cool and safe. [342] No fire could touch them here; only the calm pearl of stillness shimmering in the pool's perfect face. [360] A small boy reached toward the light, and his fear dissolved into wonder. [373] Mira watched their faces glow, the earthbound star watcher finally bringing the heavens within reach. [388] As more memories resurfaced, she felt her mother's hum threading through the gentle, blooming air. [483] There she continued her gem polishing, refining new shards for travelers and distant villages alike. [498] She taught them to weigh each piece, to feel which held the deepest, calmest glow. [513] With every lesson, more memories resurfaced, knitting her grief into something luminous and shared. [527] The grotto became a sanctuary, its pool a steady pearl of stillness that never flickered or scorched. [544] Children grew into watchers themselves, learning the slow patience of polished sea glass and gathered light. [560] Mira understood at last that her mother had not feared the dark, only wished to honor it. [577] To share light without using fire was to trust the world's own quiet brilliance.
