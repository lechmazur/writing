Queen Maelis had stopped believing in her crown long before the soothsayer's final warning. [13] "Climb to the cobalt grotto above the clouds," the old woman had rasped, "or the dreams will hollow you out." [32] The disenchanted queen laughed then, a lacy, dangerous sound, all silk over knives. [45]

Yet the dreams came again that night. [52] In them, the sky bled starlight, silver fluid weeping from punctured constellations, pooling at her bare feet. [70] She woke gripping her sheets, certain the bleeding starlight meant something her waking mind refused to name. [86]

So she went, because mystery was the only sovereign she still obeyed. [99] She left the palace before dawn, trading her gown for climbing leathers, telling no councilor where the road led. [117] The path threaded upward through pines that leaned like courtiers whispering treason. [129] By midday she reached the ridge, where the ember grotto glowed against the rock, its mouth lit by coals no hand had fed in centuries. [154]

Inside the ember grotto, heat breathed against her face, and the warmth loosened something cold she had carried since her coronation. [175] On the cavern wall, someone had painted the very image from her dreams: a wounded sky, weeping light, and beneath it a figure climbing. [199] She traced the figure's outline and found it crowned. [208] It was her. [211]

The recurring dreams, then, were not madness but instruction, painted here by hands long turned to dust. [228] To unravel the mystery, she would have to climb higher still, past the ember grotto, into the cobalt grotto the soothsayer had named. [251]

The second ascent punished her. [256] The ridge narrowed to a spine of wet stone, and the clouds rose to swallow her boots, then her knees, then her breath. [278] She climbed anyway, fingers bleeding, the lacy menace of the wind threading through the rocks like a song meant to lure her over the edge. [304] "I am not afraid of you," she told the mountain, her voice softly commanding even here, where no subject could hear. [325]

The mountain did not answer, but it let her pass. [335]

She emerged above the clouds at dusk, and the cobalt grotto opened before her like a held breath. [353] Its walls were blue beyond blue, glittering with veins of mineral light, a hollow of sky trapped in stone. [372] And at its center the sky truly bled. [380] A wound in the heavens hung low above the grotto, leaking starlight that dripped and hissed against the cobalt floor. [399]

Maelis understood then why she had been called. [407] The crown she despised was not a prize but a duty: a queen was meant to mend the sky when it tore, and the line of mending-queens had failed for generations, choosing comfort over the climb. [442] Her dreams had been the wound calling to the last of her blood. [455]

She approached the bleeding light, and it sang the lacy, menacing song the wind had sung, promising her oblivion if she touched it. [479] She touched it anyway. [483] The starlight flowed into her hands, cold then scalding, and she pressed it back toward the wound the way one presses a palm to a cut. [508] The sky resisted; her arms trembled; the menace sharpened to a scream behind her teeth. [524] "Hold," she commanded the heavens, softly, the way she had never dared command her court. [539]

And the heavens held. [543]

The wound sealed with a sound like a sigh, and the bleeding stopped, the leaked starlight settling into the grotto walls until the cobalt blazed with new constellations. [571] Maelis fell to her knees, emptied and remade. [580]

When she looked up, the night had fully come, and the air had warmed with the first true breath of summer. [600] Below her, in the valley between the clouds, tiny lights began to rise. [614] She and the silent mountain seemed to share a single exhale, and in that shared breath she spotted the first fireflies of summer, climbing the dark exactly as she had climbed the ridge. [646]

She laughed again, but the menace was gone from it now, replaced by something quieter and truer. [664] The dreams would not return; their mystery was answered, their debt paid. [677] Tomorrow she would descend and wear the crown she had finally earned, not as ornament but as oath. [695] Tonight she watched the fireflies, the mended sky, and the ember grotto glowing far below like a coal that had waited centuries to warm a willing queen. [722]

She had stopped believing in her crown. [729] Now, above the clouds, she believed in herself. [737]
