[63] The canyon walls held memory the way old houses hold cold. [74] Years ago, this had been a research outpost, and the river below once turned turbines that powered her first laboratory. [94] That laboratory was gone now, abandoned like every dream she had ever filed away unfinished. [109] Mara had a single motivation left: to become the archeologist of abandoned dreams, excavating the futures people had buried inside themselves. [130] The broken periscope mirror was her shovel. [137] She had salvaged it from a sunken submarine, then learned that its fractured surface did something no intact mirror could. [203] They had not spoken since their father died, since the night they argued over which of his promises had been real. [254] She would modify the angle of the cracked glass by exactly four degrees so the moonlight struck both their faces at once. [276] Then, through synaptic plasticity, the entangled image would rewire whatever scar their grief had left behind. [292] Idris looked tired but willing. [297] "You still think we can fix a memory like a bridge," he said. [310] "I think we can excavate it," Mara answered, kneeling to position the mirror. [342] Mara felt the ground tremble beneath her knees, though she could not tell if it was the earth or her pulse. [363] Then they were both inside the same lost night, standing in the kitchen where their father had once knelt to fix a toy. [468] The synaptic rewiring was happening in real time, two grieving brains relearning the same evening together. [484] When the moon shifted and the alignment broke, the vision released them, gasping, back into the cold canyon air. [503] Idris reached across the silver stone and took her hand, the first time in three years. [519] "You found it," he whispered, "the dream we both abandoned." [529] Mara held the broken periscope mirror against her chest, understanding at last what kind of archeologist she had become. [548] She did not dig through soil but through the soft, plastic tissue of remembrance, recovering futures people had given up for lost. [570] Above them the canyon glowed, and the river that had once powered her vanished laboratory still murmured its old, faithful current.
