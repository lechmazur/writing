<story>Mira had abandoned the city's old reactor years ago, but the abandoned control room never quite abandoned her. [18] She returned during the ice melting, when the glaciers above the valley loosened their grip and the world began to drip. [39] A contemplative glassblower by trade, she had hauled her portable furnace through the dead corridors, its orange breath the only warmth among the frostbitten dials. [64] Mira was thoughtfully spirited, the sort who laughed at silence and answered echoes. [77] She had come here for one stubborn reason: to craft safe zones for inquiry, glass domes where children could ask dangerous questions without freezing or fear. [103] The control room, with its high ceiling and sealed shell, was the perfect prototype. [117] Working alone made her chatty, so she narrated her chores to the empty consoles, granting each a grand and ridiculous weight. [138] "Behold," she announced, "the heroic sweeping of the floor, where dust meets its ancient doom." [153] "And now, the legendary stoking of the furnace, a labor sung by no one but me." [169] This dramatic narration of mundane tasks kept the loneliness politely outside the door. [182] On the central desk lay the one object she never narrated: a cracked leather bookmark, soft as a confession. [201] It had belonged to her brother Tomas, the engineer who once ran these very panels before the evacuation. [219] When the meltwater warnings came and the others fled, Tomas had stayed to seal the cores, and he had not come out. [241] Mira had blamed the room ever since, and blamed herself for not staying with him. [256] For years her grief had hardened into something glacial, a private winter she carried wherever she went. [273] Now, as the real ice melted overhead, she felt the strange beginning of a thawing heart. [289] She fed the furnace, gathered a glowing gob of molten glass, and began to blow. [304] "And here," she whispered, abandoning the theatrics, "I make the first dome, for Tomas." [318] The bubble swelled, trembling and clear, catching the drip of meltwater that had begun seeping through the ceiling seams. [337] Each droplet struck the warm glass and hissed, as if the room itself were exhaling a long-held breath. [355] She shaped the dome through the night, and inside it she placed the cracked bookmark, sealing it within the wall like a relic. [378] It was a question made permanent: what is worth staying for? [389] By dawn the dome stood finished, a small transparent room within the larger dead one, warm and humming. [407] Mira realized the tone of the whole place had shifted, a weighty lightness she had not felt since before the floods. [428] The grief remained, heavy as ever, but it floated now, buoyed by something like purpose. [443] She decided she would not abandon the control room again. [453] Instead she would fill it with domes, a forest of glass safe zones for inquiry, where the curious could gather and wonder aloud. [476] She imagined children pressing their palms to the warm walls while the glaciers wept outside. [491] To them she would narrate the founding of this strange academy with all the grandeur it deserved. [508] "Behold," she practiced, "the place where no question was ever left to freeze." [521] The contemplative glassblower set another rod into the fire and watched it brighten. [534] She thought of Tomas, who had abandoned his own escape to shield strangers he would never meet. [551] His sacrifice had once felt like a wound; now it felt like an instruction. [565] Stay, build, and let the warmth do its slow, certain work. [576] She laughed softly, the sound carrying through the domes like a vow. [588] Outside, the ice kept melting, surrendering drop by patient drop. [598] Inside, so did she. [602]</story>
