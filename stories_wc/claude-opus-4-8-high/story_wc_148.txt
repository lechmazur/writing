<story>My father was the boy who cried wolf, and I am the silence he left behind. [16] They sent me to the lighthouse prison off a jagged reef, where the sea keeps its own confessions. [34] Here the copperlight of dusk pools in every corridor, a repose so warm it almost forgives. [50] I came chasing hush, that absolute quiet my father's lies had stolen from our village. [65] He shouted wolf until no one believed him, and when the real teeth came, the whole valley paid. [83] I inherited his curse: every word I speak sustains impossibilities, conjuring what should not be. [98] If I describe a candle, it lights; if I name a wolf, it pads down the stairwell. [115] The wardens feared this gift, so they kept me in the chapel cell, where a confessional rots against the wall. [135] From it I pried the lattice, that carved screen through which sinners once whispered their hush. [151] Tonight I must escape before they find the bodies of the two guards I could not save. [168] I had whispered a storm to break my chains, and the storm, being impossible, devoured them whole. [185] Now their absence ticks like a clock, and the dawn patrol will count heads at six. [201] The prison is a labyrinth of salt-eaten stairs, and I mean to thread it with my own story. [219] If I narrate a path, the path appears; my voice becomes the thread, the maze, the way out. [237] But spoken words roar, and roaring wakes the wardens, so I need a quieter craft. [252] So I hold the lattice to my lips and breathe my tale through its tiny holes. [268] Filtered through the screen, my words shrink to whispers, and whispers still sustain impossibilities. [282] I whisper a doorway, and a doorway hums into the stone, soft as a held breath. [298] I tell of a stair that climbs to the lamp room, and the stair unspools beneath me. [315] Each invention costs me, for the curse demands belief, and belief tastes of my father's shame. [331] Up I go, threading corridor after corridor, my story laid down like rope behind me. [346] At last I reach it: a narrow door left ajar for decades, its paint scuffed to bare grain. [364] The wardens never noticed how those scuffs form a map, faint scratches charting the reef beyond. [380] Some forgotten prisoner clawed his own escape into the wood, a story older than mine. [395] I press the lattice to the door and whisper myself small, small enough to travel through the scuffs. [413] The impossibility takes hold, and I thin into the grooves, sliding through the scuffs on that narrow door. [431] On the far side I unfold onto the gallery, where the great lamp turns in copperlight repose. [448] Below me the jagged reef bares its teeth, and the sea exhales its endless hush. [463] I whisper one last impossibility through the lattice: a thing that can hover above the waves. [479] The air thickens, and a small raft of light begins to hover beyond the rail, patient and waiting. [497] I climb the rail, my voice nearly spent, my father's curse finally bending toward mercy. [512] Behind me, dawn the color of old coins crawls across the cells, and the patrol bells begin. [529] Soon they will find the bodies, and they will scream wolf, just as my father once did. [546] But no one will believe a lighthouse can birth a man who travels through scratches. [561] That is the gift my father left me: a world so used to lies it cannot recognize a true escape. [581] I step onto the hovering light, the lattice still warm against my chest, and I let go. [598] The reef falls away, the prison shrinks, and at last I drift into the only hush I ever chased.
