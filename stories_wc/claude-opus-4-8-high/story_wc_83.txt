[52] She had come here hunting a single, impossible idea: to find where unused potential pools. [67] Every life cut short, she believed, left behind a residue of things that never happened. [82] The vows unspoken, the children unborn, the long marriage unlived all had to drain somewhere. [97] Her instrument for the hunt was a geode crystal, dull as a stone outside, but split open it revealed a cavity of violet teeth. [121] The crystal responded to absence the way iron responds to a magnet. [133] She modeled the dead through perturbation theory, treating each lost future as a small disturbance rippling against the smooth equations of the living world. [157] If she summed the corrections correctly, the geode would glow toward the reservoir where all that wasted possibility had gathered. [177] Tonight the great resonance engine had finally fired. [185] She had one narrow window to act: while the circuit breakers cooled, before the station's safeties rearmed and locked her out of the core. [228] As she neared the core chamber, the air grew strange, intangibly tangible, a pressure she could feel against her teeth yet never quite touch. [252] This was the pool she had hunted for so long. [262] The reservoir of unlived life shimmered at the chamber's heart, a slow vortex of pale light. [278] She lifted the geode, and the crystal drank. [286] What poured into her then was not memory but possibility itself, raw and unmediated, an unfiltered awakening. [303] She felt the wedding that should have been: the dance, the gray hairs, the grandchild on her knee. [321] She felt every guest's unlived decades arrive at once, a thousand parallel lives flooding a single body. [338] It was unbearable, and it was glorious. [345] Tears ran down her face while she laughed, caught in a bleak exhilaration she had not felt since the morning of the crater. [368] Here was proof that nothing was truly destroyed, only displaced, pooled, waiting. [478] The geode burned cold in her palms, demanding an answer. [488] She thought of her guests, frozen at the table, forever mid-toast. [500] They would not want her to hoard their ghosts. [554] Somewhere, a stranger would marry braver; somewhere, a child would be born who otherwise wouldn't. [569] The safeties rearmed with a hard, final clang.
