<story>After the energy beings arrived, the roads emptied, and only a drifting roadside poet still walked them, reciting verses to no one. [22] He called himself Ferenc, though names had grown soft and negotiable since the luminous visitors hung above the world like patient lamps. [44] They did not conquer; they simply asked questions, and the questions reorganized everything a man thought he knew. [62] Ferenc carried one strange possession: a frayed coil of rope, knotted from his own prison escape years before the beings came. [83] He kept it not from sentiment but from curiosity, for the rope had once measured the exact length of his desperation. [104] His true hunger, the one that moved his feet, was to discover the texture of time itself. [121] He suspected time had a surface, like cloth, and that he could read it by uncovering layers, peeling each hour to find the older hour beneath. [147] One night the beings summoned him, not with voices but with a pull, toward a terra-cotta grotto glowing faintly under starlight. [168] The clay walls were warm, the color of dried blood and autumn, and the stars pressed close as if curious too. [189] Inside waited a single energy being, a column of restrained light that flickered with what Ferenc could only describe as humor. [210] It was ethically playful, the way a clever child tests a rule to learn its real shape. [227] Poet, it said, you wish to feel the texture of time, and we can grant it, but every gift here is also a trap. [251] We offer impossible choices, because only impossible choices reveal a soul's grain. [263] It showed him the rope, his own rope, hovering and unknotting itself slowly in the air. [279] Each knot, the being explained, was a year, and the pattern of knots was the only record left of the men he had abandoned during his escape. [306] Choose, the light murmured: untie the rope and you will feel time's full texture, every layer at once, an ecstasy without limit. [328] But in untying it you erase the pattern, and those forgotten men vanish from memory forever, even your own. [347] Keep the rope knotted, and you keep them, but you will never touch what you crossed a ruined world to find. [368] Ferenc understood the cruelty and admired it, for the choice was not between good and evil but between two loves. [388] He sat on the warm clay floor and studied the rope the way he studied everything, by uncovering layers, slowly turning each frayed strand. [412] Beneath the fibers he saw the faces, the names, the small debts of mercy never repaid. [428] The being waited without impatience, since it lived outside the very texture he longed to feel. [444] At last Ferenc spoke, his voice carrying the passionate subtlety of a man who has decided quietly and completely. [463] I will keep the knots, he said, because the texture of time is only worth knowing if someone remains to remember it. [485] The men in this rope are my texture; their pattern is the cloth I would have peeled away to nothing. [505] The energy being flared, delighted, because he had refused the obvious hunger and chosen the harder, quieter one. [523] Then you have already touched it, the light said, for grief is time made tangible, and you have held it without flinching. [545] The rope reknotted itself and dropped into his palm, warmer now, as if the clay had breathed into it. [564] Ferenc left the terra-cotta grotto as the starlight thinned toward dawn, carrying his ordinary, extraordinary burden. [580] On the empty road he resumed his reciting, and now each verse named one knotted man aloud. [597] The energy beings listened, learning at last that some textures are felt only by those who refuse to unravel them. [617]</story>
