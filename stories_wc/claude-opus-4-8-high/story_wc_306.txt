<story>After the campfire's last spark dies, I climb the breezy lighthouse at dawn to begin my strangest contract. [18] They call me a conscience for hire, the voice you rent when your own has gone silent. [35] My client is the keeper, a woman who has bolted herself to certainty like a ship to a too-short rope. [55] She wants me to argue against her, to welcome nuance into certainty before it strangles her. [71] At the top, the lantern room is hung with wind chimes she has tuned to her own moods. [89] I learn quickly that she speaks rarely, preferring the method of deciphering wind chime selections. [104] When she lifts a particular chime to catch the gust, she is asking a question only the air can answer. [124] This dawn she chooses the brass one, low and certain, meaning: tell me I am right. [140] I am sincerely insincere by trade, agreeing with whatever pays while meaning none of it. [155] So I tell her she is right, and watch her shoulders sag with disappointment. [169] That is not the conscience she hired, she says, and presses something cold into my palm. [185] It is a hollow mirror shard, glass that reflects no face, only the doubt behind it. [201] Look, she insists, and I look, seeing nothing of myself, only the questions I have spent years selling away. [220] She explains that the shard was her father's, the keeper before her, who drowned because he never once doubted the tide. [241] Certainty killed him, she says, and she is terrified of inheriting his blindness. [254] Now I understand the contract: she wants what I have forgotten how to feel. [268] The wind shifts, and she reaches for the iron chime, the one that asks whether she should extinguish the lamp forever. [289] I catch her wrist before she can ring it. [298] For the first time my agreement is not for sale. [308] I tell her the truth, that I do not know if she is right, and that the not-knowing is the honest part. [330] She begins to weep, but it is the loosening kind, the cloak of doubt settling over her like shade after a cruel noon. [353] Under that cloak, she is suddenly safe to be uncertain, a protected vulnerability she has never allowed herself. [371] Together we unhook each chime from the rafters, retiring the oracle that made her decisions for her. [388] She will choose with her own flawed judgment now, weighing the tide instead of trusting it. [404] When she offers my payment, I hand it back and keep only the hollow mirror shard. [420] I am a conscience for hire no longer, because for the first time my own has answered without an invoice. [440] The sun breaches the horizon, and the abandoned chimes hang silent, no longer pretending to know the future. [458] She asks how she will live without their answers, her voice small in the brightening room. [474] Slowly, I tell her, the way everyone does, by guessing and grieving and guessing again. [489] It is a poor comfort, but it is real, and she nods as though real is enough. [506] Descending the breezy stair, I look once more into the glass and finally see a face, blurred and unsure, and entirely mine. [528] Outside, the campfire's grey ash has cooled to nothing, and the morning smells of salt and beginnings. [545] I keep the shard in my coat, a reminder that the most honest mirror shows you what you do not yet understand. [567] Behind me the lighthouse turns its newly doubtful eye across the water, warning ships not that the rocks are certain, but that the dark is wide. [593] And for once, I walk away believing something I actually said. [604]</story>
