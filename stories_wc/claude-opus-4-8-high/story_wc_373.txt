<story>My father ran moonshine along the coast, and I learned to read silence before words. [15] They called me the moonshine runner's daughter, as though I had no name of my own. [31] Tonight I carried his antique watch fob in my fist, its brass face worn smooth by three generations of nervous thumbs. [52] The fob was the price of entry to the pirate market hidden beneath the rotting pier. [68] Down there, between barnacled pilings, lanterns swung like drowned stars. [78] I had come to thwart a man named Cobb, who sold secrets the way other men sold rum. [96] Cobb had promised my father safe passage, then sold his routes to the revenue men. [111] So my father drowned, and his fob came back to me wrapped in a stranger's handkerchief. [127] The market kept a strange law called a truce of hush. [138] From dusk until sunrise, no blade could be drawn, no debt collected, no voice raised above a whisper. [156] Within that hush, enemies traded elbow to elbow, curiously steadfast in their mutual quiet. [170] I moved through stalls of stolen clocks and pickled eyes, listening. [181] My true purpose was stranger than revenge. [188] I had come to capture the sound of trust breaking. [198] Cobb believed the hush protected him, and his belief was the lock I meant to pick. [214] I found him at a table of green glass, counting coins by candlelight. [227] "The runner's girl," he murmured, not surprised. [234] "I owe your father nothing the hush will let me pay." [245] I set the watch fob on the table between us. [255] "I only want to trade," I whispered, matching the market's law. [266] He reached for the brass, and his fingers trembled despite themselves. [277] I worked through false starts, offering him deals I let collapse on purpose. [290] First I offered silence for silence, then gold I did not have, then the names of men who hunted him. [310] Each false start loosened him, taught him to lean toward me, to believe. [323] With every collapse he trusted the next offer more, the way a gambler trusts the turning card. [340] The hush wrapped us both in its comforting unease, a blanket that smelled faintly of rot. [356] At last I leaned close, as if to seal a true bargain. [368] "Take the fob," I breathed, "and tell me where you buried my father's still." [382] He smiled, certain now, and opened his hand to accept. [392] That was the moment I had hunted all night. [401] Inside the fob's hollow back I had hidden a tiny brass reed, a thing that sang when air rushed past. [421] When Cobb's breath gusted out in relief, the reed gave a thin, breaking note. [435] It was the exact sound of trust collapsing, captured and held. [446] I had not come to kill him; the hush forbade it, and I am no killer. [462] I had come to record his certainty, so that I might play it back. [476] For Cobb's secrets were sold on faith, and faith, once proven hollow, is worthless. [490] I would carry that broken note to every buyer he had, and let them hear how easily he could be led. [511] By the time the eastern pilings turned gray, the market began to fold its lanterns. [526] The truce of hush was ending, and with it, Cobb's safety. [537] He understood too late what he had handed me. [546] "Give it back," he said, forgetting to whisper, his voice cracking into the dawn. [560] But the sun was already a red line on the water, and the law of silence was lifting. [578] Knives could be drawn now, debts collected, and every cheated buyer would soon learn his price. [594] I climbed the slick ladder toward the pier as the first gulls cried. [607] The watch fob hung warm against my chest, heavier with his ruin than with brass. [622] I had not avenged my father with blood, only with the truth of a single false breath. [639] Behind me, beneath the rotting boards, the market dissolved into morning and accusation. [652] I walked home along the brackish coast, listening, as he had taught me, to the silence after.
