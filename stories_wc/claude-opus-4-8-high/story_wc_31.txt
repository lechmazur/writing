<story>Mira lit the last pier lamp the night the town released its sky lanterns. [14] As the late-shift lamplighter, she signified the festival's start by touching her long brass wand to each wick along the boardwalk. [35] The hush over the harbor felt like a meteor passing—everyone breathing low, waiting for light to leave their hands. [55] Mira had a stranger reason to be near the water tonight, one she kept tucked behind ordinary truths. [73] She always hid behind the truth—telling people exactly what was real so they would never suspect what she chased. [93] Her obsession was simple and impossible: she wanted to discover the color of sound. [107] Years ago, a diver had told her of a forgotten library sealed inside a sunken ship off the third buoy, where, he swore, words still glowed. [133] He had given her a single relic before he vanished: a hotel skeleton key tag, brass and barnacled, stamped with the ship's old name. [157] Tonight, with the lanterns rising to mask the surface in drifting amber, no one would notice her slip beneath. [176] She finished the last lamp, set down her wand, and dove. [187] The cold pressed the festival noise away until only her heartbeat remained. [199] She did not swim blindly; she moved by wandering purposefully, following the tilt of old masts she had memorized from charts. [220] The skeleton key tag warmed against her palm as she neared the hull, as if it recognized home. [238] A door, half-eaten by salt, gave way, and she pulled herself into the forgotten library inside the sunken ship. [257] Air pockets clung to the ceiling, and she rose into one, gasping in a bubble of ancient breath. [275] Shelves of waterlogged books leaned in every direction, and across their spines ran a soft, breathing glow. [292] The diver had not lied: these were luminous vows. [301] Each book held a promise someone had spoken aloud and then sunk here to keep, and the words shone in colors she had never seen. [326] When she touched a page, a vow hummed through her bones, and she finally understood what she had crossed the water to learn. [349] Sound had a color after all—a low promise glowed deep blue, a fierce one burned orange, and a tender one flickered the pale green of new leaves. [377] She drifted from shelf to shelf, listening with her eyes, and the meteor hush of the place wrapped around her like the lull above the harbor. [403] Then she found one book darker than the rest, its glow guttering, and the key tag in her hand grew cold. [424] It was the diver's own vow, the last he had made before the sea kept him, and it had begun to fade for want of a witness. [451] She realized the vows survived only when remembered, that to signify a promise was to keep its color alive. [470] So she read his words until they brightened, a steady silver, and she swore to carry their sound back into the world. [492] Her air thinning, she pressed the skeleton key tag against his book like a seal, then turned and rose toward the amber-lit surface. [515] She broke into the night exactly as the final lanterns reached their peak, the sky humming a color she could now name. [537] Mira climbed back onto the pier, picked up her wand, and walked the boardwalk again, this time relighting the lamps that the wind had stolen. [562] Each flame she set down now seemed to signify a vow of its own, a small luminous promise against the dark. [583] She no longer hid behind the truth, for the truth itself had finally turned the strange color she had always wanted to hear. [606]</story>
