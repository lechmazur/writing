<story>Mira piloted her gondola along the suspended rails of fog, a quiet operator of the only craft that crossed the drifting prism terrace. [23] The terrace itself hung between mountains, a glass platform that split dawn into ribbons of color. [39] Mira was forever in transit, ferrying passengers who arrived too loud and left, she hoped, a little gentler. [57] Her single purpose was strange but stubborn: to teach silence how to comfort. [70] She believed quiet had been blamed too long for loneliness, when really it only needed instruction. [86] Around her neck hung a storm glass vial, its feathery crystals predicting weather she could not yet feel. [104] When the crystals climbed, a passenger's grief was rising; when they settled, calm was near. [119] Mira worked by monitoring noise levels, reading the hush and clatter of those she carried like a tide chart. [138] Her method was fluidly logical: each silence had a cause, and each cause a remedy she could pour into the air. [159] One evening a boy boarded clutching a ticket no one had punched. [171] He had run from a station below, where a train had carried his mother away. [186] He would not speak, and the storm glass vial bristled white with the size of his fear. [203] Mira poled the gondola onto the terrace, where the prism floor scattered the last light into trembling colors. [221] She did not rush to fill the silence with chatter, as the loud ones always begged her to. [239] Instead she listened, monitoring the noise levels of his breathing until she found the rhythm under the panic. [257] Far below, a final train released its whistle, and the sound rolled up the mountains in a long bronze arc. [277] In the quiet that follows a train whistle's echo, something in the boy loosened. [291] That hush was not empty; it vaulted upward like a cathedral with no walls, only height. [307] Mira knelt and held the vial between them, letting him watch its crystals begin to dissolve. [323] "Listen," she said, "the quiet is not what took her; it is the room left for her to be remembered." [343] Her words seemed to catalyze a change, and the boy exhaled a question instead of a sob. [360] He asked where the gondola went, and she told him it went wherever the silence still needed teaching. [378] Slowly the crystals in the storm glass vial sank into a clear, still solution. [392] The weather inside him was turning, and Mira felt the terrace steady beneath their feet. [407] She poled them onward, the prism floor drifting through bands of violet and rose. [421] The boy began to speak of his mother, naming small things: her laugh, her cold hands, her songs. [439] Mira said nothing, but she nodded, letting each memory settle into the vaulting hush around them. [455] This was the lesson she practiced on every crossing, fluidly logical and patient. [468] Grief was loud, and silence could learn to hold it the way the terrace held the light. [485] By the far edge of the terrace, another gondola waited to carry the boy down to relatives. [502] He stepped across, then paused, and asked if the silence would follow him home. [516] Mira pressed the storm glass vial into his palm, its solution now perfectly clear. [530] "When the crystals return," she said, "remember that they always dissolve again." [542] The boy held it like a small calm star and stepped into transit toward the valley below. [559] Mira watched until the other gondola shrank into the dusk, then turned her craft back toward the rails. [577] The terrace drifted on, scattering colors no one but she remained to see. [590] She did not feel alone in it. [597] The hush had learned, at last, how to comfort, and it kept her good company across the quiet miles. [616]</story>
