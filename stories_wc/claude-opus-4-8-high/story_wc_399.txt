<story>The murmuring pavilion stood at the edge of the dunes, its silk walls breathing with a whisperfield calm. [18] Inside it, Selka waited, the half-masked line walker whose face the desert had never fully seen. [34] She had come after the prophet arrived, when the sands still held the warmth of his footsteps. [51] Her task was to preserve the echo of wisdom he had left before it dissolved into the dry wind. [70] The prophet had spoken only once, then walked beyond the ridgeline, leaving his words to those who could hold them. [90] Selka was strangely honest about her own failings, admitting she had forgotten half of what she heard. [107] That honesty was why the elders trusted her, for she never embellished a teaching she could not recall. [125] Beneath the dunes lay the kinetic parapet, an ancient engine of moving stone that answered footsteps with memory. [143] When a walker paced its hidden length, the parapet shifted, and the sand above opened into conjured clearings. [161] These clearings were rooms of stilled air where spoken words could settle and be kept like seeds. [178] To work the parapet, Selka could not look directly at it; she had to walk through peripheral awareness. [196] If she stared, the stone froze, sensing the greed of a gaze that wished to own rather than carry. [215] So she let her eyes drift toward the horizon and felt the parapet move at the corner of sight. [234] The pavilion murmured around her, repeating fragments of older lessons in voices long since buried. [249] She breathed slowly, matching the whisperfield calm that kept her steps even and her mind unhurried. [265] With each pace, a clearing bloomed below, and she spoke the prophet's words into its waiting silence. [282] 'Mercy outlasts the wall that forgets it,' she recited, unsure if those were the exact syllables. [298] Being strangely honest, she added aloud that she might be wrong, and the clearing held the doubt too. [316] The parapet seemed to approve, for honest words rooted deeper than polished ones in the conjured ground. [333] Walker by walker, generation by generation, the clearings had become a buried library of careful voices. [349] Now it fell to Selka to consecrate this newest clearing, sealing the prophet's teaching against time. [365] She knelt at the pavilion's center, where a thread of cool sand marked the line she must walk. [383] Removing half her mask was forbidden, for the line walker's hidden face kept the ritual impartial. [399] The mask reminded her that she carried wisdom for others, not glory for the face beneath. [415] She rose and walked the line, eyes soft, letting the parapet read her through peripheral awareness alone. [432] The stone answered, grinding softly, and the conjured clearing below sealed itself with a sound like a sigh. [450] To finish the rite, she spoke a final vow, promising to return and tend what she had stored. [468] Her motivation had always been simple: to preserve the echo of wisdom so the young could one day hear it. [488] The desert forgot kings and wars, but the parapet remembered every honest word ever set within its clearings. [506] When the next prophet came, perhaps centuries hence, these voices would still be waiting beneath the dunes. [523] Selka stepped from the line, her work done, the pavilion still murmuring its ancient gratitude. [538] She touched the half-mask once, a small private gesture, and felt the calm settle deeper inside her. [555] Outside, the sands smoothed over the conjured clearing, hiding it as though nothing had ever opened there. [572] Yet Selka knew the truth lay safe below, kept honest, kept whole, kept patient. [586] She walked into the dunes, half-masked and unhurried, a keeper of echoes in a forgetting world. [602]</story>
