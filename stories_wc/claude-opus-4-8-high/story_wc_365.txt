Mira had always called herself a sensory map maker, though no guild recognized the title. [14] She charted not roads but feelings, the taste of rust, the weight of silence, the precise temperature of grief. [31] Her workshop was an abandoned spaceport repair bay on a moon nobody visited anymore, where cranes hung like sleeping insects above her. [53] The murmur of half-remembered dreams clung to the place, leaking from the cryo-pods that had once held travelers between the stars. [73]

She came here because of the green glass shard. [81] It had arrived in her pocket without explanation, a sliver of bottle-colored crystal that hummed when she slept. [99] Each night it whispered the same fragment: a truth her mother had withheld, dying before speaking it. [116] Mira's whole vocation, mapping what others felt, had grown from that single absence, the unspoken thing she could neither taste nor weigh. [138] So she had come to the only machine still breathing in the bay, a battered weaving loom of light that the old pilots had used to navigate. [163]

The loom did not move ships. [169] It moved minds, threading awareness through parallel universes, letting a navigator borrow the eyes of their other selves. [188] Mira intended to use it differently. [194] She would weave between worlds until she found the version of her life where her mother had spoken, and there she would finally learn the weight of the withheld truth. [222]

The trouble was the loom's nature. [228] Its engineers had described it as cyclically spontaneous, meaning it followed a hidden rhythm of chaos, calm bursts and sudden surges that no one could fully predict. [254] Every seventh pulse it would lurch, and an untrained mind could be flung loose, scattered across a thousand unfinished lives. [273] Mira braced the green glass shard against the loom's intake and let it read the dream it carried. [291]

The bay dissolved into thread. [296] She felt herself stretched, a single sensation drawn through countless rooms that were almost her workshop but never quite. [316] In one world the cranes were painted red. [324] In another the cryo-pods were full, and travelers watched her pass with patient eyes. [338] In each, she searched for her mother, following the shard's hum like a scent on cold air. [355]

She found her on the ninth world. [362] Her mother sat mending nets of light, younger, unhurried, alive. [372] "You came a long way to ask," the woman said, and Mira realized the truth had never been a fact at all. [394] It was a feeling, and feelings were the only territory Mira knew how to map. [409]

"You withheld it to protect me," Mira said, tasting the answer before her mother confirmed it. [425] "I was leaving for the deep crossings," her mother answered. [434] "I could not tell my daughter I might never return, so I said nothing, and the nothing became your whole life's work." [457] The weight of it settled into Mira at last, heavy as a planet, light as forgiveness. [474]

Then the seventh pulse came. [479] The loom surged, cyclically spontaneous as warned, and the threads yanked taut to scatter her. [495] Mira understood in an instant that knowing the truth was not enough; she had to carry it home. [513] So she gripped the green glass shard and used everything her craft had taught her. [527] She redirected the surge, mapping the sensation of her own workshop, the rust, the silence, the cold familiar grief, and steered the loom's chaos toward that single anchor. [554]

The worlds unraveled around her, but her sense of home held like a rope. [568] She felt her mother's hand release her, gently, the way it should have the first time. [584] The bay reassembled, cranes gray again, pods empty again, the murmur of half-remembered dreams thinning to ordinary quiet. [603]

Mira sat on the cold floor, the shard now dull and silent in her palm. [618] The truth had weight, and she could measure it: not the burden she had feared, but the density of love disguised as absence. [641] She rose and began a new map, one not of grief but of the long way her mother had traveled to keep her safe. [664] Outside, the dead spaceport waited, but Mira no longer needed to leave to find anything. [680] She had woven between worlds and brought back the one thing no machine could chart, and finally, she had somewhere to put it down. [705]
