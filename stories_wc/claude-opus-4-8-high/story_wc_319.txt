[239] But doubt is my oldest companion, and I had crossed dimensions to make friends with uncertainty. [255] "If you erase the boundary," I warned, "the light has nowhere to end, and a thing that never ends cannot be loved." [398] Pell wept, not from fear now, but from the relief of a doubt she could finally hold. [415] "It is allowed to not know," she said, testing the words like new gloves. [446] We waited together, then, through the rest of the veil of sleep, while the mist settled like patient snow. [465] She left the pendant in the frozen garden, no longer a weapon against doubt but a small lantern for it. [485] When she woke, she would carry the garden inside her, exactly as I had taught.
