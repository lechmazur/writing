<story>Mira was the only intrepid orchard pilot brave enough to fly when gravity inverted. [14] For one hour each cycle, the orchard planet loosened its grip, and everything that was rooted strained toward the sky. [34] Floating rock arches drifted between the trees like the bones of forgotten giants, and the only sound was a whirling silence that pressed against her ears. [60] She flew not to harvest but to discover the source of resilience that let these orchards thrive where nothing should. [80] Her father had carried a broken arrow tip on a cord around his neck, claiming it pointed toward an answer he never lived to reach. [105] Now the relic hung at Mira's throat, and during the inversion it grew strangely cold. [120] With the ground no longer trustworthy, she had learned to fly by navigating by water flow, following the rivers that rose from their beds and braided upward into shimmering ropes. [150] Water always knew the true direction of things, even when the sky and the soil traded places. [167] Today the floating streams converged toward a single arch larger than the rest, a stone halo wreathed in upside-down blossoms. [187] Mira guided her small craft along the rising water, the arrow tip burning colder with every meter. [204] As she passed beneath the great arch, the whirling silence deepened into something almost solid. [219] Then she heard them: voices, thin and overlapping, a gathering of echoes drifting out from the hollow center of the stone. [240] They were the recorded breaths of everyone who had ever flown here before, including, she was certain, her father. [259] The echoes were not random; they were instructions, fragments of warning and welcome layered across centuries. [275] Following their murmured directions, Mira eased her craft into the arch's core and found a vein of water that did not rise but spun in a perfect, patient circle. [304] At its heart floated a seed the size of a fist, pulsing with light. [318] She understood at once that this was what accelerates plant growth across the entire world, the buried engine of every harvest. [339] The seed drank the inverted rivers and exhaled life so fierce that orchards could survive on a planet that should have been barren stone. [363] Here, at last, was the source of resilience her father had chased. [375] The arrow tip leapt from her chest and clung to the seed, and the broken edge fit a notch in the seed's shell exactly. [399] It had never been a compass at all, but a key, and her father had died searching for the lock. [419] When the tip seated home, the gathering of echoes swelled into a single chord, and her father's voice surfaced clear among them. [441] He said only her name, then, gently, that resilience was never a thing to be taken but a thing to be tended. [463] Mira had come prepared to carry the seed away, to plant her own fortune somewhere safe. [479] Instead she left the arrow tip in its notch, completing the key, letting the wound in the metal mend the wound in her heart. [503] The hour of inversion was ending; she felt the rivers shudder, ready to fall. [517] She turned her craft and rode the descending water back through the floating arches, navigating by its flow as gravity reclaimed the world. [540] Behind her, the great seed bloomed brighter, fed now by both water and remembrance. [554] The blossoms righted themselves, the trees settled, and the planet exhaled into its quiet, growing dusk. [570] Mira landed among her orchards as the whirling silence softened into wind. [582] She had discovered the source she sought, and chosen to guard it rather than own it. [598] Tomorrow the harvest would come, sweeter for being shared, and the intrepid pilot would fly again.
