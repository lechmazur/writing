<story>Mira had served the icebound spire above the clouds for nine years, polishing its silver railings while the wind sang through frost. [22] She was an ascending servant, promoted floor by floor, each rank earned through patience rather than ambition. [39] Today the old elevator carried her toward the master's vacant study, ten floors above her usual duties. [56] As the elevator climbs ten floors, the morning light fractured against the ice walls, throwing pale colors across her uniform. [76] She watched the world unfold beneath her through scattered light, the clouds glowing like spilled milk. [92] Mira was warmly inquisitive by nature, the sort who opened drawers not to steal but to understand. [109] The master had been an airship captain once, before grief grounded him in this frozen tower. [125] He had vanished a week ago, leaving only silence and the faint smell of pipe smoke. [141] The elevator shuddered to a stop, and Mira stepped into the study with her dustcloth ready. [157] On the desk lay the airship captain's goggles, their lenses cracked and dull. [170] Beside them sat a leather book bound with a small brass lock. [182] She knew she should only clean, yet the lock invited her curiosity like an unanswered question. [198] The clasp gave way easily, and inside she found page after page of meaningless symbols. [213] It was an encrypted diary, written in a cipher she could not begin to read. [228] Disappointed, she lifted the goggles to wipe the desk, and the cracked lenses caught the window's glare. [245] Through the fractured glass, the diary's symbols rearranged, the captain's secret revealed only through scattered light. [261] Mira held the goggles steady and let the words unfold across the page. [274] The captain had written of his lost crew, of an airship swallowed by a storm above these very clouds. [293] He had built the spire to remember them, encoding his sorrow where no casual eye could pry. [310] Yet the later entries surprised her, for they spoke not of despair but of small mercies. [326] He recorded the way frost bloomed like flowers, the warmth of tea, a servant's quiet kindness. [342] Mira realized the diary was his attempt to seek out the joy beneath anguish. [356] Each coded line traced a man learning to live alongside loss instead of drowning in it. [372] The final entry was dated the day he vanished, and it named her directly. [386] "If Mira finds this," it read, "then she possesses the only eyes patient enough to see." [402] He had left the goggles for her, knowing her warmly inquisitive habit would lead her upward. [418] The captain had gone walking onto the highest balcony, choosing to join his crew among the clouds. [435] Mira felt grief, but it arrived with a strange affectionate detachment, like mourning a story already finished. [452] She had not loved him, exactly, but she had understood him, and that felt like enough. [468] She sat at his desk and continued reading, the goggles pressed gently to her eyes. [483] Outside, the clouds drifted in slow gold rivers, and the spire hummed in the cold. [498] She decided she would learn the cipher fully, transcribing each entry so the joy might survive him. [515] It seemed the truest service she could offer, higher than any floor she had climbed. [530] For the rest of that winter she ascended daily, the elevator carrying her through scattered light. [546] She would put on the captain's goggles and watch his sorrow unfold into something gentle. [561] The icebound spire above the clouds no longer felt like a tomb. [573] It felt like a lantern, holding one man's stubborn hope against the endless white. [587] And Mira, the ascending servant, kept reading, content to seek the joy beneath anguish for as long as the lenses held. [608]</story>
