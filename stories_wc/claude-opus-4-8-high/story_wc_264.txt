<story>After the last forest burned, the sky kept the color of cooling ash, and the freight lines still hummed. [19] Mira was a dreamless freight guard, hired to watch the cargo that rolled across the emptied world. [36] She never slept truly, only blinked, because sleep had stopped delivering dreams to anyone after the fires. [53] The company never explained the cargo to its guards; they only handed her keys and a route into the gray. [73] The train carried one strange freight: a divergent archive, sealed in iron drums. [86] Scientists had called it that because it stored not facts but possibilities, every road the world might still take, recorded through wave functions that shivered when observed. [113] Because of this, the archive was occasionally everywhere, leaking into the air, so that two stations might receive the same sealed drum at once. [137] Mira's only company was a chess clock frozen at midnight, a gift from a passenger she could no longer picture. [157] Its hands had stopped the night the forests went, and she kept it because stopped time felt safer than the burning kind. [179] She wound it sometimes from habit, though the hands never moved. [190] For months she had ridden beside the singing drums, never daring to listen closely. [204] One night the train stalled beside a glowing dune in bloom, where pale flowers pushed through luminous sand. [222] Mira had never seen anything alive grow since the ash, and she stepped down into the soft light. [240] A voice rose from the drums, fractured and tuneful, a lullaby missing half its notes. [255] The archive was singing the futures it held, and the broken melody seemed to charm the flowers wider open. [274] Mira understood then why she had been hired without instructions: not to deliver the archive, but to plant it. [293] Her true task was to cultivate the sparks in the unlit corners, the places the fire had left without a single possible tomorrow. [316] She pried open a drum, and the wave functions spilled out as cold blue motes. [331] Each mote was a possible world, flickering between being and not-being until her gaze fixed it into the sand. [350] She thought of all the corners the fire had hollowed, the dark towns where no child would ever choose a path. [371] Where she looked, a flower bloomed; where she turned away, the possibility scattered back into everywhere. [387] The motes drifted everywhere at once, and she felt dizzy holding so many almost-worlds in her tired eyes. [405] Mira realized she could only save what she chose to witness. [416] So she walked the dune slowly, naming each spark she wanted to keep. [429] Doubt slowed her hands, for choosing one world meant letting a thousand others fall back into nothing. [446] A world with rivers, a world with green, a world where someone still dreamed. [460] The lullaby steadied as she worked, its missing notes filling one by one. [473] By the time the eastern ash paled toward something like dawn, the dune had become a field of fixed and certain blooms. [495] Mira looked at the chess clock and saw its hands had finally trembled past midnight. [510] Time had agreed to move again, because there was now a future worth measuring. [524] She thought of the passenger who had given her the clock, and understood now that he had been a guard before her, planting his own quiet fields. [551] The work would pass to another someday, this gentle planting of tomorrows across the unlit earth. [567] Beyond the dune the rails ran on toward the next dark place, waiting for her lamp. [583] That night, for the first time since the last forest burned, Mira closed her eyes and dreamed. [600]</story>
