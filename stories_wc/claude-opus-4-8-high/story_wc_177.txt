Mara could see only the before and the after, never the now. [12] The doctors named her condition foresight hindsight only, as if a label could soften the vertigo of living between two tenses. [33] She had come to the vine-draped bunker hidden in a rewilded forest because its old maps promised an answer. [52] Ivy had cracked the concrete, and birch roots fingered through the ceiling like patient questions. [67] Her motivation was simple and impossible: to discover why some paths choose their walkers. [81] On the central console lay a wafer seal, thin as a communion host, stamped with a sigil that had outlived its government. [103] The seal guarded a decaying directive, a command left running long after the hands that wrote it had turned to soil. [124] It still whispered through the bunker's speakers in a voice both precisely playful and unbearably old. [140] Walk where you are wanted, it teased, and Mara understood the riddle was meant for her alone. [157] Outside, the barometer fell when the storm approached, and the forest hushed as though holding a single breath. [175] Mara had learned that her only way forward was through interpreted dreams, the lone bridge across the missing present. [194] So she lay on the cot beneath the breathing vines and let sleep take the place she could never feel awake. [215] In the dream a corridor unrolled, and each tile lit only when her foot was already lifting from it. [234] She saw the directive as a child's chalk arrow, redrawn nightly by something that refused to let the line fade. [254] The arrow spoke with the speaker's playful precision: I am not the path, I am the wanting that wears it. [274] Mara realized then that a path was not stone but appetite, a route hungry for the right walker to complete its meaning. [296] The decaying directive had once chosen soldiers; now, half-rotted, it chose anyone whose grief matched its frequency. [313] Her grief, she saw in hindsight, had been broadcasting for years, a beacon she could not hear yet always carried. [333] Thunder cracked above the canopy, and the dream-corridor flickered toward its end. [345] She woke with the answer pressed against her tongue and the wafer seal trembling in the storm-charged air. [363] Lightning lit the bunker, and in foresight she watched her own hand break the seal a moment before she chose to. [384] The wafer crumbled, and the playful voice softened, freed at last from the loop it had been ordered to repeat. [404] Thank you, it said, for choosing back, and the directive at last fell silent into compost and rain. [422] Mara understood she had not been picked by the path; she had agreed to it, and agreement could be revoked. [442] To transcend the loop, she only had to want something the directive could not name. [457] She wanted the now, the unbearable middle she had been denied her whole strange life. [472] The storm broke fully then, washing green light through the vines, and for one impossible heartbeat she felt rain on her hands as it fell. [497] Not before, not after, but during, the cold drops striking and the present finally arriving. [512] It lasted only a breath, ethereal and gritty as wet ash, then vanished back into the seam of tenses. [531] But it had been enough to prove the question's answer. [541] Paths choose their walkers, she finally knew, only because walkers first whisper that they are willing to be chosen. [560] Mara stepped out of the bunker into the rewilding rain, leaving the broken seal among the roots. [577] She could still see only the before and the after, but now she walked unwanted by any directive, gloriously unchosen. [597] And the forest, patient and playful, let her go. [606]
