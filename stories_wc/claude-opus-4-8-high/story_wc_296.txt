<story>After the magic trick failed, Brenna stopped pretending she was anything but a guarded druid. [15] The illusion was supposed to summon her drowned brother's face from the water; instead, the lake answered with something colder. [35] At the heart of the cursed lake spun a swirling orchard vortex, trees torn from soil and turning in slow, patient rings. [57] Brenna walked across the ice that the failed spell had laid like a road, her staff tapping a war-drum rhythm against the frozen surface. [81] She had always been the one who finds lost things-rings, children, the names the dead forget-yet she had never found herself. [102] That was why she had come: to discover what mirrors don't show. [114] The orchard called to her through expanding circles, ripples of cold air that widened outward and pulled her into its turning center. [136] Each ring she crossed peeled away a layer of her guard, and she let it, because some doors only open to the unarmored. [159] At the vortex's eye stood a single apple tree, and bound within its trunk, frozen mid-breath, was her brother Cael. [179] His lips were parted around a frozen confession, the words he had drowned trying to speak the night the lake took him. [201] Brenna felt the old warlike fury rise, but here, in the orchard's still center, it cooled into a strange serenity-a calm sharpened to a blade's edge. [227] In her palm she carried the orchard's price: a flesh-eating plant seed, black and twitching, hungry for living warmth. [246] The vortex had told her the truth in dreams-only by planting the seed in Cael's frozen heart could the ice unbend and release his last words. [272] But the seed would devour whatever flesh it touched, and Cael, half-flesh, half-frost, might not survive the trade. [290] She pressed her hand against the bark, and for the first time in years the guarded druid spoke aloud. [309] 'I came to see what no mirror would show me,' she whispered, 'the part of you that blamed me, and the part of me that fled.' [335] The expanding circles tightened around her now, the orchard demanding its answer. [347] She knelt and pushed the flesh-eating plant seed into a crack at the base of the trunk. [364] It rooted instantly, drinking the frost, and the ice began to unbend with a sound like breaking lutes. [382] Cael's chest shuddered; the frozen confession cracked free of his lips. [393] 'It was not your fault,' he breathed, the seed's tendrils already curling up his arms. [408] 'I jumped to find the ring you lost-the one Mother gave us-and the lake kept me for it.' [426] So this was the lost thing she had always feared to find: not an object, but his forgiveness. [444] The seed fed, and Cael did not scream; instead he smiled with that same warlike serenity she now wore. [463] 'Let it take me,' he said. [469] 'A body is only a mirror, and you came to see past mirrors.' [482] Brenna wept, but her hands did not shake as she helped the vines do their work. [498] When the last of his flesh was gone, the swirling orchard vortex slowed and settled. [513] The cursed lake unfroze in expanding circles, ring after ring, until the trees stood rooted once more on solid ground. [533] Where Cael had stood grew a new apple tree, its fruit dark and gleaming, each one holding the ring she had lost as a child. [558] She plucked a single apple, slipped the ring onto her finger, and felt the magic trick that had failed finally complete itself in a way no illusion could. [586] The guarded druid lowered her staff and let her shoulders unbend at last. [599] She had come to find what mirrors hid, and found it was not a face but a peace-fierce, quiet, and entirely her own. [622]</story>
