<story>Mira catalogued stolen things for a living, tracing each artifact back to the hands that first loved it. [18] As a provenance researcher, she believed every object carried a destiny perforated by the choices of its owners. [36] The latest object was impossible: a transparent violin carved from ice crystal that refused to melt. [52] It had arrived at the velvet observatory at midsummer, wrapped in moss that whispered to her. [68] Mira had been accidentally telepathic with plants since childhood, ever since a fever left her hearing the green murmur of roots. [89] The moss told her the violin belonged to an empire that wished to own every song ever sung. [107] Her employer, the Cartographer of Estates, wanted proof of ownership before the promise lost its glow. [123] That promise was simple and cruel: deliver the violin, and Mira would inherit the empire's archives, every secret she had ever craved. [145] But the moss had also shown her a dream, a small one, of fields where music grew wild and unowned. [165] Now she had to weigh that dream against an empire, and she had only the night to decide. [183] She brewed coffee beneath the observatory's plum-colored dome, for she read the future in coffee stains. [199] The first stain bloomed like a mulberry bruise, an ache shaped exactly like regret. [213] In its dark edges she saw the empire's vaults, endless and airless, every melody pinned like a butterfly. [231] She poured a second cup, and this stain spread into open meadows, where the violin's ice would thaw into rivers. [251] The moss pressed closer, urging her to propagate something living from the frozen instrument before dawn. [267] Plants, she realized, did not hoard; they multiplied and gave themselves away. [279] The violin hummed when she lifted it, a sound like a glacier remembering it was once a song. [297] Her provenance training told her the truth the empire feared: the instrument had no single owner. [313] It had been carved by a hundred anonymous hands, each adding a note, each surrendering their claim. [330] Its destiny was perforated on purpose, full of holes so that anyone might pour their breath through. [347] If she handed it over, the perforations would be sealed, the holes filled, the music silenced forever. [364] The third coffee stain dried before she could read it, and its blankness frightened her most. [380] A future with no shape meant a future she still held in her own trembling hands. [396] Midsummer light slid violet through the velvet curtains, and the moss began to wilt with worry. [412] Mira understood that the promise glowed only while she stayed obedient and silent. [425] So she carried the ice violin out beneath the open dome, into the warm midsummer dark. [441] She drew the bow once, and the note that rose was not hers but everyone's. [456] As the crystal warmed, it wept clear water into the soil at her feet. [470] The moss drank the melt and sang back through her mind, telling her the song was now a seed. [489] From the dampened earth, a thin green shoot rose to propagate the music she had freed. [505] Each leaf unfurled a different note, and the meadow of her dream began, impossibly, to grow. [521] The empire would arrive at dawn to find only a garden that could not be archived. [537] Mira's promise dimmed and went dark, and she felt the mulberry ache of everything she had refused. [554] But the plants whispered that some destinies are meant to stay full of holes, breathing and unfinished. [571] She knelt in the singing garden, a provenance researcher who had finally traced an object home. [587] Home was no vault and no empire, only the open hands of anyone willing to listen.
