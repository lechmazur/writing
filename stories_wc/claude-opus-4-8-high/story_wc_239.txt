<story>I am the end of history witness, the last reader in an ancient temple library lost in time. [18] Here the silence is not empty but full, a resounding hush that presses against the stacked centuries. [35] I came to unravel cosmic illusions, those bright lies the universe tells itself to keep spinning. [51] My only tool is a softcover dictionary, its spine creased, its pages soft as moth wings. [67] With it I name things, and to name a thing is to begin to unmake its disguise. [84] The library keeps a law older than its walls, called spiral arbitration. [96] Every dispute, every contradiction, must descend a winding stair of judgments, each ruling tighter and truer than the last. [115] I am both plaintiff and judge, arguing the cosmos against my own doubt. [128] To begin, I practiced the method the old priests left behind: journeying inward while walking out. [144] I walk the corridors toward the distant door, yet each step sinks me deeper into myself. [160] My footsteps are wanderingly exact, drifting along no map yet landing on the precise stone each time. [177] This all happens across a universe's hiccup, that single stutter between one breath of creation and the next. [195] In that gap, time loosens, and the library floats unanchored, a held note no one releases. [211] The first illusion I tried was distance. [218] The dictionary defined it, then unsaid it, and the far door slid suddenly under my hand. [234] The second illusion was self, the belief that I stood apart from the shelves I read. [250] Spiral arbitration demanded I rule on that claim, and I could not. [262] Each judgment spiraled down to a single tighter question: who is the one who witnesses? [277] I opened the dictionary to the word unify, and the soft page seemed to breathe. [292] There the definition told me what every illusion had hidden. [302] The witness and the witnessed were never two; the cosmos and I were one argument folded into a seeming pair. [322] To unravel the illusions was simply to unify them, to let plaintiff and judge collapse into the same quiet breath. [342] The spiral of arbitration reached its center, where no further ruling was needed. [355] I had walked all the way out, and arrived all the way in. [368] The far door stood open onto the same library, now seen without its veil. [382] As the end of history witness, I understood my task at last. [394] I did not exist to record the final page, but to dissolve the difference between reader and book. [412] The universe's hiccup began to ease toward its second breath. [422] Soon creation would resume, and the temple library lost in time would slip back beneath ordinary hours. [439] I closed the softcover dictionary and set it on the central lectern, its work complete. [454] Around me the resounding hush deepened, no longer the silence of waiting but of fullness arrived. [470] I stepped through the open door and felt the corridor become my own chest, breathing. [485] Outside lay no exterior, only more of the one thing that had always been here. [500] My wanderingly exact path had carried me to the only address that mattered: everywhere and nowhere at once. [518] The hiccup passed. [521] Stars exhaled back into their courses, and history began again, unaware it had ever paused. [536] But I remained, knowing the secret the spiral had taught, that judgment and mercy, seeker and sought, are forever a single sentence. [558] Whoever next opens that softcover dictionary in the temple library will find one new entry, written in my hand. [577] It reads: to unify is to remember you were never divided. [588] And in that remembering, every cosmic illusion quietly, gratefully, comes undone. [599] The hush remains, resounding still. [604]</story>
