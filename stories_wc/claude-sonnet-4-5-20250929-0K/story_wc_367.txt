The Emerald Lighthouse had stood for three centuries, its green glass walls refracting starlight into patterns that sailors once mistook for omens. [23] Now, in an age when the last print newspaper had yellowed into museum archives and information traveled only through screens and whispers, Kael climbed its spiral stairs with a glass lantern wick cradled in his weathered hands. [61] He was the laughing storm herald, appointed by a council that no longer existed to perform a duty no one remembered needing. [84]

[110] The routine felt otherworldly, a ritual plucked from some parallel dimension where logic bent differently. [248] It had quoted him, though he'd never spoken to any reporter: "When the green light fails, so fails the boundary between what was and what must be." [276]
