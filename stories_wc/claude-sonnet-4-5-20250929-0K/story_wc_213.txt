[89] Up here, two miles above the valley settlements, the air moved in patterns most meteorologists never witnessed. [106] Mara had learned to read them through patient observation, tracking microclimate variations that warned of the violent updrafts that could shred gliders and topple the valley's wind turbines. [134] Her job was to sound the alarm when those invisible threats materialized—but only when truly necessary. [151]
