The zephyr bluff stood wrapped in mist at the tilt of the earth toward spring, when ice began its slow retreat and the world held its breath between seasons. [31] Marcus Kaine climbed the rocky outcrop with careful steps, his scarred hands gripping stone made slick by melting frost. [49] A burned spy—discarded by his agency after a mission gone catastrophically wrong—he had nothing left but the silver hush of this remote place and a promise he'd made to a dead friend. [80]

In his pocket rested an engraved pillbox, its surface worn smooth by anxious fingers. [94] Sarah had pressed it into his palm three years ago, her blood already pooling on the safehouse floor. [112] "Fix what's broken," she'd whispered, and he'd assumed she meant her bullet wounds, but her eyes had gestured toward the window, toward the world beyond. [136] Inside the pillbox: coordinates and a date—today's date. [146]

The bluff overlooked a valley where snow had accumulated in strange, deliberate patterns throughout the long winter. [163] Marcus had been watching for weeks from the village below, noting how the drifts seemed almost intentional, how they persisted in specific formations while surrounding snow melted away. [189] Codes in accumulated snow—it was the kind of dead drop only Sarah would devise, prudently daring enough to hide intelligence in plain sight where satellites couldn't penetrate the seasonal chaos. [217]

He'd been afraid to look, afraid to find where unused courage waits in the hollow spaces of a broken man. [237] After the fire that had scarred his body and ended his career, Marcus had retreated into silence, into the silver hush of isolation. [260] But Sarah had believed he would return to himself, had trusted him with something that mattered enough to die protecting. [281]

[306] A map, he realized, showing not geography but relationships, networks. [317] The trafficking ring they'd been investigating hadn't died with Sarah; it had metastasized, spreading through legitimate businesses and government offices. [336] The snow-codes documented everything: routes, players, payment structures. [345] She'd spent her final winter creating this archive, trusting the season itself to preserve her evidence. [361]

But spring was coming early. [366] Already, the southern exposures were melting, erasing crucial sections. [376] Marcus had perhaps three days before it all vanished. [386]

He descended rapidly, his heart hammering with purpose he hadn't felt since before the fire. Emma studied his scarred face, the intensity burning through his careful reserve. [491] "Show me," she said simply. [496]

[587] Fixing what's broken, he understood now, meant recognizing that some fractures made you stronger in the places you'd split apart. [608] The codes would reach the right people.
