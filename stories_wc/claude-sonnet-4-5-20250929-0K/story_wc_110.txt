[25] Mira worked the night desk at the forecourt's concert hall, a quiet registrar who processed ticket exchanges and lost item claims in the hollow hours when performances ended and patrons drifted home. [58] Tonight, she studied the projector lens someone had left behind, turning the heavy glass circle in her hands, watching how it bent and multiplied the light into ambiguously clear patterns—neither sharp nor blurred, but something in between.
