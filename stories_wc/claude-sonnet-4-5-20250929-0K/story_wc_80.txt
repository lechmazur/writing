[23] The tide had claimed this part of the estate years ago, when the river changed course and the drawbridge fell into permanent disuse. [48] Now, twice daily after the distant drawbridge rose for phantom ships that would never come, the current slackened enough for Marcus to pursue his peculiar work. [74]

He carried his grandfather's antique traveling inkwell in his breast pocket, feeling its familiar weight against his heart. [92] The brass had oxidized to a green patina that reminded him of everything the water had taken—not violently, but with patient, inexorable absence. [114]

Marcus had been hired as a shore surveyor three years ago, tasked with mapping the coastal erosion that threatened the old estates. [135] But somewhere between measuring loss and documenting decay, his mission had transformed into something the firm would never understand. [153] He had become an archaeologist of abandoned dreams, cataloging not what remained but what the departures meant. [170]

[194] Seaweed draped over the marble columns like mourning veils. [251] The mechanism still worked perfectly despite its age, releasing just enough ink for his careful script. [267]

On a small square of archival paper, he wrote: "The Whitmore twins held their debut here, 1847. [284] No one remembers they existed. [289] Catherine loved astronomy. [292] Elizabeth bred roses." [295]

He folded the note precisely and worked it deep into the foundation crack, sealing it with marine epoxy. [312] This was his method, had been for eighteen months now. [323] Placing notes in foundation repairs, leaving traces where the house itself would remember even after human memory failed. [341]

Marcus moved through the water-logged space with stoic sensitivity, documenting everything while feeling everything. [355] His colleagues thought him cold, meticulous to the point of mechanical. [367] They couldn't see how each measurement pierced him, how every notation of subsidence and structural failure was a small death he absorbed. [388]

[435] He died penniless in the servants' quarter. [443] His music was the ghost the house tried hardest to keep." [455]

[508] Love letters, confessions, accusations, all the words someone had hidden beneath the floorboards rather than deliver.
