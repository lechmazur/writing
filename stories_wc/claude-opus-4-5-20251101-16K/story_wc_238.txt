Vera Sinclair spread copper wire across the archive floor, her movements precise despite the tremor in her hands. [17] For fifteen years she had written the gossip column that burned through reputations like brushfire, and for twelve of those years she had spoken with the dead—a natural progression, really, from exposing secrets to excavating them from beyond. [54] Tonight the two callings converged in this dusty sheet music archive, where Eleanor Marsh's compositions sat moldering in cardboard boxes, waiting for someone to remember their composer. [78]

Outside, the orchard held its breath. [84] Tomorrow the chainsaws would arrive, and the apple trees Eleanor had planted would become stumps, then mulch, then memory. [101] But tonight belonged to the hush between what was and what would be destroyed. [115]

Vera pulled the antique perfume bottle from her coat pocket—cut crystal, amber residue still clouding its interior. [131] She'd found it in Eleanor's estate sale years ago, bought it out of guilt she couldn't name, kept it on her nightstand where its faint jasmine ghost accused her nightly. [159] Now it would serve as the anchor. [166]

She connected the copper coils to a hand-cranked generator, positioning the perfume bottle at the spiral's center. [181] The induced currents would flow through the wire, creating a field that spirits could inhabit like water filling a vessel. [200] Imperfect science for imperfect miracles—Vera had learned long ago that the dead didn't return clean and whole. [216] They came in fragments, in whispers, in sensations that felt like almost. "Eleanor." [257] Vera's voice cracked. [260] "I need to reconnect with you. [265] Not for a column. [269] For something I should have said before they put you in the ground." [282]

[311] Eleanor Marsh looked as she had in the photograph that accompanied Vera's career-making article: severe bun, eyes like winter ponds, mouth set against a world that had already begun its betrayal. "A river," Eleanor interrupted, "doesn't apologize for flooding. [424] It simply moves to its next path." [431]

Eleanor's form flickered, stabilized. [443] "Then be the stone that diverts the water. [451] You cannot un-drown what drowned, but you can build a bridge for those still standing on the bank." [469]
