The forgotten library existed in the space between remembering and forgetting, its shelves dust-laden with texts no one had sought in centuries. [24] Vera, the shard-mirror archivist, moved through these aisles with fragments of old mirrors woven into her coat, each piece reflecting a different version of herself—some younger, some older, some from lives she hadn't yet lived. [63] Her work was preservation, cataloging exiles and their eventual returns, annotating margins with observations only she could decipher. [79]

It was at the hush as an empty playground swing drifted in the breeze outside—that particular stillness of late afternoon—when the boy arrived. [103]

He was seventeen, maybe eighteen, with the hollowed look of someone recently cast out. [117] Vera recognized it immediately. [121] She had worn that same expression when her own exile began, before she understood it could be reversed. [138]

"They sent me here," he said. [144] "Said you could help." [148]

Vera walked backwards toward her reading desk, a nervous habit she'd never managed to break. [163] The mirror shards on her coat clinked softly. [171] "Help with what, exactly?" [175]

"Getting back. [177] They—my family—" [180]

"Sit." [181]

Vera poured tea from a pot that always seemed warm, its ceramic belly painted with equations no mathematician had ever solved. [203] When the boy had drained his cup, she took it from his hands and studied the leaves at the bottom. [222]

"By analyzing the geometry of tea leaves, I can read what you've been writing on your own soul," she said. [242] Her finger traced a spiral pattern. [248] "This angle here—you believe the exile is permanent." [257]

"Isn't it?" [259]

She reached across her desk and lifted an object from its velvet cradle: a small glass sphere containing what appeared to be an afternoon shadow caught in glass—the silhouette of a tree, frozen in amber-gold light, permanently suspended in its late-day form. [306]

"This shadow belonged to my daughter," Vera said. [315] The words came out rough-edged, defensive, though she was offering him something precious. [327] "She was exiled too. [331] Thirteen years ago. [334] I kept this shadow to remember the last afternoon we spent together." [346]
