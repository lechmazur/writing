[67] The network of memory-keepers who preserved the true histories—the ones before the Directorate's revisions—was crumbling. [84]

Mira pressed herself into an alley's shadow, her hand closing around the small brass key in her pocket. [102] It was unremarkable: stamped with the faded logo of Pacific Bell, a phone company that hadn't existed for sixty years. [122] The Directorate had erased it from official records, just as they erased everything inconvenient. [136] But the infrastructure remained—old lines, forgotten booths, dead drops connecting a world the regime pretended never existed. [154]

She was carefully resourceful by necessity. [160] When you had nothing, you learned to see potential in everything. [171]

[228] The resistance had taught her this language: how dusk painted directions in water, how the angle of light revealed paths invisible at any other time. [253] It was a code written in ephemera, impossible to intercept because it existed only in moments. [269]
