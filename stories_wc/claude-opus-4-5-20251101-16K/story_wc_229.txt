[20] For nine days, the coast had held its breath, and now the exhale came—cool, merciful, carrying the promise of something she had waited for since her father disappeared into the fog three months ago. [55] She climbed the spiral stairs to the lantern room, each step an ache she had learned to love. [70] As assistant lighthouse keeper, she had spent years in her father's shadow, watching him believe that volume was the answer to everything. [90] Bigger lights. [92] Louder horns. [94] He shouted at storms and bellowed warnings at ships that could not hear him over their own engines. [112]

[144] She had felt it in the way her father's body finally relaxed in sleep only when she hummed, never when the wind screamed. [168]

[225] Her mother had called it a beckoning oil. [233] "Not a command," she had explained to young Maren. [242] "An invitation. [244] It purifies intentions.
