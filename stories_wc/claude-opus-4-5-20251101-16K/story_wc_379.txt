Maren had learned long ago that discernment was not about what you chose to take, but what you chose to hear. [21] As curator of Finch & Found Thrift, she spent her days sorting through other people's abandoned stories, recognizing which objects carried weight and which were simply things. [47]

Every morning for three years, the residents of Oleander Court had gathered among the terra-cotta pots and climbing jasmine, bringing coffee cups and folding chairs and fragments of memory. [142] Letters accumulated behind the slotted brass mouth—first a trickle, then a stream. [154] Official notices. [156] Personal correspondence.
