[24] Vela had tended this place for seven years now, a shadow-laced peacekeeper sent to the edge of the world because she was too strange for anywhere else. [50]

[72] The woman's daughter had brought it, asking Vela to do what she was rumored to do: reconstitute what had been shattered. [93] Not the crystal itself, that was merely a vessel. [102] But the memories, the final thoughts, the goodbye that her mother never spoke aloud. [115]

This was her calling, the courageous anomaly she had become. [125] Others in the Peacekeeping Order mediated disputes, calmed riots, negotiated treaties. [135] Vela mapped empathy onto action in ways they couldn't understand: she restored the broken connections between the living and the lost. [155]

[178] Seventeen mirrors of varying sizes, their glass rippled with age, their frames ornate with tarnished silver. [193] Through them, Vela had learned to see what others couldn't. [203]
