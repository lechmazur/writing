[27] Time moved differently here, measured not in days or seasons but in the descent of burning lights that streaked across the polar sky. [49] She was what the old texts called a moonstruck dreamer, one whose mind had been touched by celestial madness and could never again be satisfied with earthbound concerns. [76]

[101] The stars had shifted in the centuries since its forging, yet Maren found it still served its purpose. [117] By tracing the path of falling stars with its sighting vanes and calibrated rings, she could follow each burning trail to its source and destination. [141]

Tonight, as every night, she watched for the first streak of fire. [153]

[168] This was her curse and her calling, to hear the voices that had never found their way into the world. [187] A painter who had seen colors that had no names but died before her brush could render them. [204] A poet whose verses had calcified in his throat, turned to stone by fear. [218] A composer whose symphonies had played only in her mind while her hands remained paralyzed at the keys. [235]

"I hear you," Maren whispered back, her breath fogging in the frozen air. [340]

This was her motivation, the purpose that kept her standing beneath the archway night after endless night: to free trapped creativity from its prison of light. [365] Too many visions died with their dreamers, too many stories went unspoken, too many songs dissolved into silence.
