[106] Mr. Elias Thorne answered, his eyes sharp behind wire-rimmed glasses, exuding a mysterious familiarity as if he'd been expecting her all along. [133] "Sugar?" he echoed with a faint smile, handing over a small tin. [146] "Borrow it as long as you like; neighbors share more than sweeteners in places like this." [250] He was patiently nimble, his fingers deftly arranging fragile manuscripts while he waited for her to grasp the nuances, never rushing the unraveling of linguistic mysteries. [284] During one session, as twilight bled into night, Elara spotted an antique desk seal on his writing table, its brass handle worn smooth, embossed with a cryptic sigil that resembled the building's faded cornerstone emblem. [340] Thorne lifted it gently, pressing it into a blob of red wax to demonstrate, the impression forming a perfect, enigmatic seal.
