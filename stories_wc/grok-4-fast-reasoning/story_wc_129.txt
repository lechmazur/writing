[28] Beyond the murmur of lost languages, in an era where ancient tongues echoed only in the wind's faint sighs, Elara had arrived as a stranger, her bold sympathy drawing her to the orchard's reclusive inhabitants like a moth to faint glows. [58] She yearned to graft herself onto this community, to weave her solitary craft into their intricate webs of kinship, those invisible threads binding families across generations in quiet rituals of shared memory.
