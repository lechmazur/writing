Lani, a pacific conflict zone translator for the regional ceasefire team, waited there because the corridors outside were full of men who no longer trusted the languages they spoke. [56]

Lani had promised Mako she would read them, because she recognized the pattern of fear in the way the ink swelled where tears had touched it, and she felt the same fear in her own throat. [231]

When Mako finally stirred, confused and thirsty, she placed the brain coral fragment on his bedside table and told him it was from the broken reef, but that reefs heal by grafting living polyps onto bleached stone. [577]
