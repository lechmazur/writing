I was a stunt double named Mara Kells, hired to fall off roofs and burn cleanly, yet I feared heights, fire, and even applause, and I stayed employed only because I was firmly adaptive, learning new ways to hide my shaking in plain sight. [74]

I did not win by being fearless; I won by being seen, and the crew, hearing how he used our panic and bodies, turned on him, while I signed my real name on the call sheet, took the child's hand, and stepped into a future that I authored. [619]
