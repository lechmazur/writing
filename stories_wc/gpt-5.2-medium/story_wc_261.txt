<story>After the anesthesia wears off, Mara wakes in the quiet tower at dawn, listening to the city far below as if it were under snow. [25]The room has a meteor hush, the kind that makes every thought sound like a pebble tapping glass. [43]On the bedside table sits her frosted hourglass, its sand blurred white, a gift from the surgeon who promised time would smooth the incision. [67]Mara is a closure seeker who opens wounds, and she cannot let smoothness be the final story. [84]She wants to learn to read the stories in scars, not just the neat ending stitched over them. [102]When the pain creeps back, she flips the hourglass and watches the grains drift, counting breaths until her fingers stop shaking. [123]A cup of coffee has gone cold beside it, and the brown rings on the saucer are the only maps she trusts. [145]She was trained, before the accident, to diagnose memories by examining coffee rings, because trauma always settles in circles. [190]She peels back the gauze a thumb's width, enough to see the fresh seam, and the sight makes her swallow hard with satisfaction and dread. [215]The tower keeps a greenhouse on its upper stair, and for reasons no doctor can name, the anesthesia left her accidentally telepathic with plants. [239]From the window box, the ivy murmurs in chlorophyll syllables that the scar is not only hers, that it is a door. [261]Mara presses her palm to the leaves, and the ivy shows her a memory of something bright being dragged underground, a displaced miracle. [284]The thought tastes like copper in her mouth, and she knows why the tower feels so clean and so wrong. [304]Years ago, a healer died here, and the last blessing meant for the street below was hidden in the foundation to keep it from being stolen. [330]The plants have been drinking that light ever since, but the neighborhood has been growing sicker, and Mara's own wound refuses to close. [353]If she can move the miracle back where it belongs, perhaps she can also move her story from rupture to meaning. [554]They did not steal it, they insist, they only caught what fell, the way leaves catch ash after a meteor breaks apart. [604]Dawn has fully arrived, and the city below lies pale and waiting, while her reopened wound leaks warmth down her skin like ink. [627]She sets the jar beside a rooftop planter, tips the hourglass, and lets its last white grains mark the moment she cracks the wax seal. [706]The light runs outward in branching lines, and for a breath the neighborhood exhales, windows fog clearing, coughs easing, and even Mara's pain quieting to a distant pulse.
