He slid his kayak down the damp ramp, speaking to the empty air in a merrily melancholic tone, as if the gulls were old friends who understood jokes that ended in silence. [234]

Lita jogged down and pressed a thermos into his hands, saying her father still owed Theo for showing him safe channels years ago, and that debts should not rot like old nets. [367]
Theo wanted to refuse, but the warmth of the metal made him gravitate toward the idea that people were more than their worst day, and he heard a thin strand of faith flicker in his chest. [403]
