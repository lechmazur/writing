The rain had been falling for three days straight when Elara Voss stepped into the hush of the old library, its stone steps slick with runoff and the scent of wet parchment clinging to the air. [23]
She wasn’t supposed to be here—not after the heart attack, not with her father’s debts still unpaid, not when every spare moment should have been spent grading papers or calculating odds for the next underground poker game. [58]
But the library’s mica-pane lanterns flickered in the dim, casting fractured light through their translucent windows, and something about the way the glow fractured into rainbows against the shelves made her pause. [94]
Her father had always said probability was just a language for the universe’s cruelest jokes, and tonight, she was fluent enough to hear the punchline. [123]

Elara adjusted the strap of her satchel, her fingers brushing the edge of a crumpled subway ticket tucked into the pocket. [146]

“Widen perspectives responsibly,” he’d say, right before losing another hand. [508]

And now, with the rain still falling outside and the lantern’s glow fading, Elara understood what she had to do. [745]
She would pay his debts, but not by playing the games he’d lost. [761]
