Dr. Elias Veyne stood at the bow, his breath misting in the cold air, the emerald swatch card clutched tightly in his gloved hand. [42]

Elias had come to recapture not just her, but the truth she’d sought: the Garden of Forgotten Names, a place where lost identities bloomed like flowers in the dark. [119]

The ship’s abandoned attractions loomed around him, their colors faded but still blissfully aberrant—clown faces with too many teeth, mirrors that warped reflections into impossible shapes. [152]
He ran his fingers along the swatch card, its fabric smooth and unnaturally cool, as if it had been woven from the same fog that swallowed the *Mariposa* every night. [186]
Liora had left it behind in her haste, or perhaps as a clue, its emerald hue a beacon in the gray. [208]

Memories altered with each step Elias took deeper into the ship. [222]
The carousel’s music played in his head, not as it was now—silent, its horses frozen mid-gallop—but as it had been when Liora danced beneath its broken lights, laughing as the world bent around her. [264]
He could almost see her, her hair a wild halo, her eyes reflecting the same unkindled solace he felt now: the quiet ache of being free yet bound by what was lost. [301]

Through narrative threads, he pieced together her path. [313]

One card caught his eye—the Two of Cups, split down the middle, its halves held together by a single thread. [365]
Liora had always said the chimera twins weren’t two people, but one soul fractured by time. [391]

The ship’s lower decks were a labyrinth of forgotten stalls, their wares rotting under layers of dust. [415]
A jar of fireflies, their light long extinguished, sat beside a ledger of names—visitors who had come and never left. [445]
Elias traced his finger down the list until he found hers, scrawled in her hurried hand: *Liora Veyne, seeking the garden where names grow back.* [478]

The deeper he went, the more the air thickened, not with fog, but with something older—something that remembered. [504]
The funhouse mirrors showed him glimpses of Liora, not as she was, but as she might have become: a woman with two faces, one smiling, one weeping, both staring back at him with his own eyes. [550]

At the heart of the ship, he found the garden. [583]

"You came," she said, not turning around. [651]

The chimera twins weren’t a myth; they were a choice. [744]

"Some solace isn’t meant to be kindled," she murmured. [801]
