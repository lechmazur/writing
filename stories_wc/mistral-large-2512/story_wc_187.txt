The stormlit observatory perched above the clouds like a forgotten god’s watchtower, its brass domes streaked with rain that never quite dried. [18]
Inside, the air hummed with the weight of centuries, thick as the fog that had, only hours before, become solid enough to carve. [43]

She had come here not to record the present, but to salvage the past—before it dissolved into the kind of nostalgia that hadn’t even happened yet. [110]

Elara stepped onto it, her boots clicking against the glassy expanse, and the stones beneath her feet murmured—not in words, but in the echoes of footsteps long gone. [182]

That was the trouble with collective memory: it was never whole, only fragments stitched together by those desperate enough to believe in patterns. [255]

“What if the fog didn’t solidify on its own?” [438]
The forecourt’s crystals pulsed faintly, as if in response. [450]
