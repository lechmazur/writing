[17] Elara pressed her palm against the cold glass and counted her heartbeats, as she always did, each pulse a quiet metronome against the stillness. [41] One hundred twelve per minute tonight, elevated but steady, a rhythm she had monitored since the day the county clerk handed her a death certificate bearing her own name. [69]

[90] By the time the state corrected the mistake, Elara had already attended her own memorial, watched her mother weep over an empty casket, and felt the strange vertigo of surviving a death that was never hers. [125] Something in her had fractured that day, not dramatically but subtly, like hairline cracks in old glazing. [142]

[160] The telescope at the room's center pointed upward through the open aperture, but Elara was not here for stars. [240] Elara recognized it immediately because she understood what it meant to exist in the space between recorded death and actual life. [260]
