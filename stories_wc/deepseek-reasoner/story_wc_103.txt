[44] She had come to study its impossible, non-Euclidean pathways, a natural masterpiece that humbled her own work. [65] In her satchel, wrapped in velvet, was her most precious artifact: a halo fragment, a sliver of polished, celestial metal that glowed with a soft, internal light. [93] It was a key, she believed, to perceiving temporal layers. [104]

[137] The world did not just brighten; it deepened, revealing histories etched into the very air. [155] But the effect was too potent. [161] A wave of energy from the fragment resonated with the garden’s own latent power, and Elara was thrown not backward, but *sideways*. [187] She was caught, retrocognitively trapped within a single, repeating moment from the garden’s past. [207]

[246] Her only company was a series of fading photographs, ghostly images that manifested on the bark of the trees around her. [271] They were echoes of visitors past, their smiles and glances bleaching away into nothingness as she watched, a stark chronicle of erasure.
