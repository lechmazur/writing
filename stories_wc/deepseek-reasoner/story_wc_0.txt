[76] Her motivation for coming was to strip needless drama from the event, to process it as data—a defined volume of water, a quantifiable force of wind. [106] It was how she preprocesses reality, a habit of mind that translated chaos into manageable, sterile inputs. [128]

[155] She righted a stool and sat, the act feeling both futile and necessary. [304] But one wing, largely intact, had been caught on a thorn of a surviving rose bush. [325] With a tenderness that surprised her, Aris peeled the wing from the thorn, finding it left behind a microscopic trace of its essence. [356] This dust from a butterfly wing was the artist, and the flood silt was its canvas. [707] The drama she had tried to strip away was never needless; it was the point. [726] The flood was a full stop, but in its aftermath, the sentence was continuing, written in dust and light.
