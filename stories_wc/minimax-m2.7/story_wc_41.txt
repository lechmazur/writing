[28] Here, among rusted metal and ghostly fog forms, the junkyard prophet moves, his steps tracing the thin line between the seen and the foreseen. [52] His mind is precognitively forgetful; tomorrow’s visions slip away like water through a sieve, yet the echoes of the past cling to his ribs. [76] He carries a piece of plain chalk, its pale surface a silent promise that he can draw the shape of things that never were. [100] To locate the hidden source of the sound he seeks—the faint, nostalgic hum of growing older—he arranges driftwood as directional markers, each upright stake a compass pointing toward memory. [152] He draws a circle on the wet earth with the chalk, a ring meant to trap the echo, to hold it fast before the fog can swallow it. [180] The chalk line glows faintly, a fragile barrier against the tide of longing that pulls at his heart with each wave of remembered sound. [204] He steps between the markers, counting the rhythm of his own breath, while the fog sculpture garden seems to inhale and exhale with the pulse of the unseen videotapes. [233] The hush is broken only by a distant creak, a sound that the prophet knows is the echo of his mother's voice, captured on a tape left to rot among the rusted relics. [354] The player crackles, and from its speakers spills the soft, measured rhythm of a heartbeat growing slower, each thump a reminder that time erodes even the strongest walls. [382] He stands amid the fog, the sound washing over him like a tide that has waited centuries to reach this shore, and he feels the pull of his own aging echo. [413] The memory of his mother's lullaby, now replayed, mingles with the creak of the garden's metal armatures, forming a chorus that the junkyard prophet can no longer ignore. [510] He steps back, feeling the weight of the tide and the echo settle within him, and he knows that his precognitively forgetful mind will one day forget this moment, but the echo will remain, a stone in the current. [617] In his pocket, the plain chalk warms against his skin, a silent reminder that even the most fleeting echo can be drawn into permanence.
