As the city's first preventable death investigator, she had learned to keep her pulse watchfully calm even when artifacts spoke louder than autopsies. [36]

She carried no flashlight; the lantern's wick, newly trimmed, awaited a spark that might reverberate across centuries. [104]

When she struck a match, the flame caught with a sigh that sounded like a name—Elias. [222]
