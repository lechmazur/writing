She had come here to retrace the path her mentor once walked, believing that purpose hides in the pauses between heartbeats, in the drifting resonance of memory and glass. [88]  
Below, the river whispered over stones, a sound like distant applause for a performance only the stars could see. [103]

The kiln had been her cradle, the place where sand became breath, where color learned to sing. [131]
