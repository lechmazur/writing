Inside, Chef Marisol knelt beside the last living vine, her tongue still tingling with the memory of her grandmother’s final meal—saffron, sea salt, and the soft apology for leaving too soon. [52]

She understood then that the seed was not for eating but for planting, and that pruning was not destruction but a quiet refusal to let ruin have the final word. [125]
