At the heart of the drowned garden, stone palms open above coral beds, and Aurele the living statue listens to every pulse of thought the sea still keeps. [23]  
Its bronze irises are flecked with calcified algae, yet they track the single foxglove-stained glass ampoule now drifting through the cracked dome like a crimson comet, each bloom inside preserved in warp of light and salt. [42]  
Only after the secret—that no city truly forgets, it merely folds its memories into the architecture—has been spoken aloud do the currents shift, carrying the ampoule to the statue's pedestal, a place no fish has touched in seven shipwrecked centuries. [65]  
Aurele had once been a guardian, sculpted to embody unflinching certainty; touch my stone, citizens were told, and doubt evaporates. [86]  
But telepathy betrayed the promise: tumbled private fears, joy, unspoken pities, all threading through its petrified nerves until certainty cracked harder than marble. [108]  
Now it kneels, incisively kind, knowing rigidity wounds more truthfully than any blade. [121]  
The ampoule knocks once, twice; a leaf of stained foxglove rattles inside like a tiny heart. [137]  
Aurele sends thought, warm as silt: Drink me? [144]  
Response blooms, not from the glass, but from the city itself—garden walls humming with the memory of how every seed here was first planted by the very people who fled it when war came. [174]  
Cascading revelation: the garden was never abandoned; it was left like a will, hope encoded into tendrils of kelp and memory. [196]  
Aurele lifts the ampoule with fingers that cannot bend yet ache with antique mercy. [215]  
A hairline fracture in the bronze wrist—here is the structural weakness—awaits thread. [228]  
From the ampoule’s mouth spills one glowing filament: foxglove essence mixed with distilled recollection. [246]  
Aurele thinks, I am statue, therefore conduit; statue, therefore bridge between what was and what insists on becoming. [269]  
With motion too slow for eyes of fish yet too deliberate for tides, the statue threads the filament through its own break, weaving sap of flower and memory into calcified sinew. [300]  
Each stitch sparks; color invades grey stone in muted bursts of mauve and violet—animate lifeless becoming simply alive. [325]  
Another revelation cascades: the city’s last gardener, fleeing, did not abandon hope; they entrusted it to the one figure unable to leave, knowing only stillness could welcome nuance into a certainty. [361]

To welcome nuance into a certainty, one must allow certainty to admit it was incomplete. [456]
