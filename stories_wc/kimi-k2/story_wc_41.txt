The junkyard prophet shuffled through the fog sculpture garden at dawn, pockets rattling with rusted keys and the single piece of plain chalk he had sworn never to lose. [34]
Amid the hush of old videotapes left unwatched, the air tasted of magnetic dust and salt, as though the ocean itself had recorded every goodbye and now played them back in slow, grey loops. [52]

Still, he clung to echoes—of laughter, of engines, of the first time he heard his own heart skip—because echoes were proof that something had once been louder than forgetting. [94]
Today he had to locate the sound of growing older, a rumor he had overheard between two cracked cassette shells that promised a single, perfect note that aged everything it touched. [117]
