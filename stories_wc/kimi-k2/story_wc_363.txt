She carried no loom now; the warping frame had been sold seasons ago to buy passage for a single word. [66]
That word—"return"—was meant for her brother, conscripted beyond the empire's farthest border, a place maps forgot. [86]
Yet couriers had failed, pigeons had fallen, and the word had lodged inside her like a swallowed thorn. [104]
So she walked the orchard each dusk, listening for any echo that might recommence the broken conversation. [123]

She tucked it into a pouch over her heart, determined to open it, though she knew no fire or blade could melt such resin without burning the message inside. [218]

The monks warned her the puzzle had no picture on the pieces; whoever solved it would see only what they most needed. [300]
Lira paid with her mother’s silver thimble, last keepsake of bloodline and craft. [318]

Hours slid into days; the hush thickened, and the orchard’s forgotten fruit ripened and fell unheard. [380]
