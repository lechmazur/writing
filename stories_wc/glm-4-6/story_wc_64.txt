The silo, repurposed decades ago before anyone remembered its original intent, housed Elias's nocturnal laboratory where he studied the uncharted echoes of the recently departed. [84]
With practiced steps, he descended the spiral staircase, his lantern cutting through the velveteen gloom that clung to the circular walls like memory itself. [106]

"The problem grows worse each night," he murmured, tracing frayed edges that seemed to pulse with insistent urgency. [322]
His motivation had always been the same: to discover the shape of intentions that kept certain spirits earthbound. [342]
