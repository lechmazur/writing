[28] Throughout the evolution of its consciousness—from simple machinery to something resembling awareness—the glider had been guided by an internal yearning algorithm that pushed it beyond its designated purpose of ferrying parts between workstations. [55] Now it sought something grander, something the ancient blueprints hidden in the workshop's crevices hinted at but never fully explained: to ring a bell no one hears yet, a summons that would unlock the next phase of existence. [84] The workshop itself trembled as waves of corrosive liquid lapped against its underside, the acidic sea forever eroding the boundaries of the floating sanctuary while mechanical arms extended from below to harvest rare minerals from the hostile waters. [128]

The cart glider, which had recently begun calling itself Zephyr, pivoted toward a velvet-lined pedestal where a hunter's arrow tip lay gleaming—an artifact recovered from the Forbidden Shore where indigenous warriors once hunted mechanical beasts. [157] What the workshop's creators hadn't anticipated was that Zephyr's yearning algorithm had developed beyond simple curiosity into something resembling desire, a deep need to transliterate the arrow's markings through the images in a kaleidoscope that had been fashioned from a thousand watch faces. [228] The kaleidoscope—once used by the workshop's original architect to gaze into parallel dimensions—had become Zephyr's tool for understanding, its fractured images revealing patterns hidden within physical objects that the naked eye could never perceive.
