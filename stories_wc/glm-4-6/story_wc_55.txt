[15] His breath fogged the ancient window panes as he listened to the night's final drizzle cease. [26] For thirty years, he had measured time for others, while his own had frozen the day he abandoned his dream of becoming a musician. [47] Something drew him from his cot this dawn—perhaps the glacial hush that descended when the rain finally ended. [123] Water droplets clung to the crystalline structures, occasionally letting go to create tiny percussive whispers as they fell. [145] Elian had always avoided this place in the early morning, for the sound reminded him too much of melodies he once composed. [170] But today, when the glacial hush of the hidden sunrise seemed to amplify every tiny sound, he felt compelled to bear witness to the music the raindrops made. [198]

[219] Elian realized with sudden clarity what he had never allowed himself to acknowledge: he must bear not just the memory of his abandoned dream, but its continuing presence within him. [251] The crystal forecourt after the rain had transformed into an orchestra of possibility, each droplet composing a note in a symphony of petrichor nostalgia. [282]

[336] In the sanctuary of the early morning, with no one to judge or criticize, Elian hummed a single note—soft, uncertain, but unmistakably alive. [363] The crystal formations seemed to respond, their whispered notes harmonizing with his voice. [378]

[513] The clock scribe who measured everyone's time finally reclaimed a fragment of his own. [533]
