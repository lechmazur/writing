[15] Elias, known to the few who bothered to remember as the junkyard prophet, moved through the haze with a purpose that felt both ancient and urgent. [178] His method was simple, yet absurdly difficult: arranging driftwood as directional markers, a ritual to locate the echo he so desperately needed to hold on to.
