<story>Iven the mirror maker arrived at the ruined orchard district upon the shimmer of twilight frost. [16] Centuries of thick fog had swallowed the trees, leaving only the silhouettes of pear and quince like drowned sailors frozen mid-breath. [37] He had come to recreate something lost, and under his arm he carried the astronomer's broken sextant. [54] Weeks earlier, the astronomer Pell had come to him trembling, confessing that a dream had escaped her skull during sleep and now wandered the fog, stealing warmth from the orchard. [84] She had smashed her sextant against the floor in despair, unable to chart where the runaway had gone. [102] Iven, firmly grounded in the old craft, believed a mirror could catch what instruments could not. [118] He set the sextant upright in the frost, its cracked arc pointing skyward like a rib bone. [135] Beside it he laid a silvered glass, polished that morning in his workshop under the glass mountains whose blue peaks never softened, never forgave. [159] Pell arrived as the fog thickened, her lantern bruising the white air. [171] I feel it near, she whispered, my dream is circling the orchard. [183] Iven nodded and explained the method: they would capture it through synchronized breathing, erasing the boundary between dreamer and mirror until the runaway mistook the glass for its own home. [213] The sextant, though broken, still remembered angles; it would mark the moment their breaths aligned. [228] They knelt among the splintered trees. [234] In, out, she breathed, and he matched her count. [243] The fog seemed to listen. [248] Frost sharpened on the black twigs; the twilight deepened into lavender; somewhere a pear fell that had no business falling after a century of silence. [273] Iven watched the mirror and saw, inside it, not his reflection but a field of running horses made of pale smoke. [294] Pell's breath caught. [297] That is mine, she whispered, that is what I dreamed the night it got loose. [312] Do not break rhythm, Iven said, and the sextant's broken arm gave a small metallic chime, finding an angle it had not held in years. [337] Their two breaths became one long tide. [344] The boundary between woman and mirror maker thinned; the boundary between mirror and fog thinned also; soon Iven could not say whose lungs drew the cold. [370] The horses inside the glass slowed. [376] They turned, curious, toward the warmth of the shared exhalation. [386] Iven felt the temptation every mirror maker fears: to let himself dissolve into the reflection, to stop being a man and become a silvered plane. [411] But he was firmly grounded, root in stone, and he held. [422] The dream stepped into the mirror. [428] Pell gasped, and the sextant's arc slipped down with a soft tick, closing the measurement. [443] Iven clapped a velvet cloth across the glass before the horses could wander out again. [458] The fog loosened its grip on the orchard for the first time in centuries. [472] Somewhere a thrush, long thought extinct in this district, tried a single note and gave up, uncertain of its own throat. [493] Pell wept, touching the sextant. [498] You cannot repair it, Iven told her gently, but you can read it one last time; it has just measured the distance a dream travels when it runs. [526] She nodded, set the instrument upright in the frost as a small monument, and wrapped the cloth-bound mirror in her coat. [547] Together they walked out of the orchard, their steps syncopated, their breaths still matched from habit. [563] Behind them the fog began to lift in slow panes, as if something had finally given it permission to end. [583] In the far distance the glass mountains caught the last twilight and rang, faintly, like a tuning fork struck in another country. [605] Iven knew then that every boundary he had ever drawn was only a thin silver he could choose, nightly, to erase and recreate. [628]</story>
