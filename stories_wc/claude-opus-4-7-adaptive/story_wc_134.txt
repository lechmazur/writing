<story>Mira had foreseen every visitor to the musical crystal cave for forty years, every pilgrim's name humming into her mind before their boots crossed the threshold. [26] She was the clairvoyant who didn't see it coming. [35] The pilgrim walked in at dawn, a thin woman with river-mud on her knees and a cave pearl cupped in her palms. [57] Mira felt nothing, no premonition, no bell-chime in the marrow, only the ordinary chill of the crystal corridors. [75] "You weren't on my tongue this morning," Mira said, startled by her own silence. [89] "No," said the pilgrim. [93] "I came to quiet the noise, not add to it." [103] The cave sang as they walked deeper, each crystal humming a note tuned by water and time. [120] Mira led by habit, one hand trailing the wet wall, but her second sight felt like an unlit lamp. [139] She had always known the future through overlapping shadows, the way a torch threw three versions of a visitor and she would read which one stepped forward. [166] The pilgrim cast only one shadow, steady as a post. [176] "What is the pearl?" Mira asked. [182] The pilgrim opened her hands; the cave pearl glowed with a soft interior weather, bright darkness pooled inside it like ink lit from within. [206] "My grandmother grew it in a spring," the pilgrim said. [216] "She said unseen forces inhabit small things if you ask them politely." [228] "Her gift was to magnify everyday magic, the kettle's whistle, the cat's half-purr, the coin that falls upright." [246] "The pearl is what she left me when the attention got too loud." [259] Mira understood, then, why she had not foreseen this woman. [269] A pearl that amplified small magics would also swallow large ones, including the reach of a cave-oracle's mind. [287] It made her steadily unpredictable, hour by hour, the way a candle makes a room both warmer and harder to read. [308] They reached the resonance chamber, where the crystals sang in thirds and every breath came back doubled. [325] The pilgrim knelt, laid the pearl in a shallow of water, and the whole cave dimmed a half-shade. [343] "I want to leave it here," she said. [351] "Somewhere the noise won't follow." [356] Mira hesitated. [358] The cave had always been hers because she could see every thread of it; without her gift, she was only a woman standing in a musical dark. [385] Yet the dark was bright in a new way now, lit from the inside by the pearl's patient glow. [404] Mira found she could hear things she had always looked past: the slow drip that tuned the D-flat column, the small beetle tapping rhythm against a stalagmite, her own breath keeping time. [436] The unseen forces the pilgrim spoke of did not arrive; they revealed themselves, the way stars do after sunset. [455] "Stay until it settles," Mira said. [461] The pilgrim nodded and sat on the wet floor like a patient animal. [474] For three days they kept watch. [480] Mira's visions came back in pieces, then not at all, then in strange angles she had never read before; futures that flickered like the shadows here, overlapping and shy. [509] She learned to read them again, slower, guessing. [517] On the fourth morning the pilgrim rose, bowed to the pearl where it sat humming softly with the cave, and walked out unburdened. [540] Mira stayed behind with the thing that had blinded her. [550] She had expected to resent it. [556] Instead she found herself kneeling beside the small bright dark, listening. [567] The pearl magnified whatever was near: the old hum of the crystals, yes, but also the quieter magic of being present without knowing what came next. [593] A clairvoyant who could not see, Mira thought, might finally learn to witness. [606] She stayed to inhabit the cave properly for the first time, shadow among its overlapping shadows, a woman learning that the future was steadily unpredictable and therefore, at last, worth listening for.
