<story>The lighthouse orchard perched above the shifting ice floes, its salt-bitten apple trees leaning south as if listening for thaw. [20] Ishmael kept the lantern lit and the fruit pruned, a shadow-laced peacekeeper who had come north to stop being needed. [40] He carried a broken crystal ball fragment in his coat pocket, the only thing left from a war he had refused to fight twice. [64] The shard warmed when strangers approached, a calmly intuitive little weather vane of other people's intentions. [80] One gray morning, the ice brought him a girl. [89] She arrived balanced on a floe that should have split beneath her, a courageous anomaly in a landscape built to drown children. [111] Ishmael hauled her up the switchback path, past the apple trees, into the warm round room beneath the lamp. [130] She said her name was Ven and that she was looking for the rest of herself. [146] From her wrist she produced a second crystal shard, twin to his own. [159] "You've been carrying a piece of me," she said. [168] During the weighted breath before reply, Ishmael felt every old reflex rise: deny, deflect, disarm. [183] Instead he set his fragment beside hers on the table, and the two edges sang faintly, recognizing each other. [202] Ven explained she had been piecing her memory back together for six years, via glimpses in vintage store mirrors. [221] In small coastal towns, she said, the mirrors in second-hand shops held stranger histories than their new ones. [239] She had seen her own mother in a gilt-framed oval in Reykjavik; her brother in a silvered hallway piece in Nome. [260] Each glimpse returned a sliver of who she had been before the accident scattered her. [275] But the final shard, the one that could reconstitute her wholly, had lodged itself in the pocket of a man who hated reflections. [298] Ishmael studied her. [301] He had taken his piece from the ruins of a command tent, believing it was strategic intelligence, a cracked oracle. [321] Instead it had taught him to feel what others felt before they said it, and that skill had undone him as a soldier. [344] He had come to the orchard because apples asked nothing of him and the ice kept most visitors away. [363] Yet here was a person he had accidentally been carrying his whole exile. [376] "If I give this back," he asked, "what becomes of me?" [387] Ven touched the fragment gently, not taking it. [395] "You've spent years learning to map empathy onto action," she said. [406] "That was my gift, before I broke. Now it's yours. The shard only amplified what you already were." [424] He thought of villagers he had refused orders to displace, of a prisoner he had walked free, of this lighthouse he tended for nobody. [448] Each act had felt like intuition answering a call he could not quite hear. [462] Perhaps it had always been her, paging him through his pocket. [473] He slid his shard across the table. [480] The two pieces trembled, rose an inch, and knitted themselves into a single clouded sphere that pulsed once and went clear. [501] Ven drew a long breath as if surfacing from deep water. [512] Color returned to her hands; her eyes lost their borrowed distance. [523] "Thank you," she said, and the words did not feel like closure. [535] Outside, the floes groaned, rearranging the coastline. [542] Ishmael expected to feel emptied, a shadow without its lining. [552] Instead he felt the orchard, the lamp, the ice, the woman, each distinct and near. [567] The gift had not left with the glass; it had only stopped being borrowed. [581] Ven wrapped the whole sphere in linen and offered it to him. [593] "Keep it," she said; "you'll meet others like me, and you'll know what to do." [608] He took the bundle, calmly, intuitively, and set it beside the lamp where the beam would find it every turn. [628] Below the cliff, the ice kept shifting its mind. [637] Above, the apples held.
