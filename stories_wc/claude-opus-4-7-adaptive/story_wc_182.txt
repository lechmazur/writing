<story>The bookstore coiled through the mountain like a held breath, its staircases spiraling between shelves that hummed with an unheard symphony only Ilen could almost detect. [26] As an arcane linguist, she had trained her ear for meanings that lived just beneath language, and the mountain-store spoke constantly. [47] She had come searching for the Library of Unlearned Lessons, a rumored vault of the knowledge the world had refused to absorb. [69] Her only map was a botanical diary left by her grandmother, its pressed leaves marking pages where a traveler's footsteps had once stalled. [92] Grandmother had been carefully resourceful, the kind of woman who could make a compass from regrets. [108] Ilen followed the leaves upward, past the Atrium of Contradictions and the Hall of Unread Letters. [124] Three days after the time traveler arrived, the bookstore began rearranging itself faster than its ancient wards allowed. [142] He called himself Marren, though the diary's margin notes named him differently in four unfamiliar scripts. [158] Marren claimed he ran rescue missions across centuries, pulling people and ideas from collapses before history swallowed them. [176] The Library of Unlearned Lessons, he said, was his richest source and his most dangerous one. [192] Ilen distrusted him immediately; he spoke every tongue without feeling any of them. [205] Still, his arrival had destabilized the mountain, and she needed the Library before its shelves folded beyond reach. [223] They struck an uneasy pact on the seventh landing. [232] He would guide; she would translate the stacks' whispered indices, which no ordinary scholar could parse. [248] Ilen worked the way her grandmother had taught her: by researching gaps, tracing what the catalog refused to mention. [267] Missing entries were footprints; silences were maps. [274] She cross-indexed the diary's pressed flora against the store's botanical wing, discovering a stairwell where a fern should have been filed but wasn't. [297] Behind it, a doorway exhaled cold light. [304] The Library of Unlearned Lessons was smaller than legend promised. [314] A single circular room, its shelves lined with books whose titles changed when she looked away. [330] Each volume contained a lesson humanity had failed to learn -- warnings unheeded, mercies untaken, songs unsung. [346] Marren reached for the shelves with the hunger of a man who had done this before. [362] Ilen finally understood her grandmother's warning. [368] He did not rescue lessons; he harvested them, carrying them backward to sell at moments when their absence had already shaped the world. [391] Every rescue he boasted of was a theft arranged in advance. [402] She stepped between him and the shelves, diary open. [411] "We renegotiate," she said. [415] Marren laughed, but his laughter bent in the strange acoustics of the room, revealing notes of fear. [432] The Library responded to language the way other rooms responded to keys. [444] Ilen spoke to it in the tongue beneath tongues, the grammar her grandmother had stitched into the diary's margins. [463] She did not demand; she proposed. [469] Let the lessons remain, but let them be readable -- let the world relearn what it had refused. [486] The shelves shivered. [489] Titles steadied. [491] A draft carried dust from the stacks outward through the mountain, settling onto every untouched page. [507] Marren's hand closed on empty air; the books refused his grip as strangers refuse a lie. [523] He vanished the way arrivals do when their trajectories are rewritten. [534] Ilen descended slowly, listening. [538] The bookstore no longer hummed unheard; its symphony had found a room willing to resonate, and readers far below were pausing mid-sentence, feeling something old return. [564] She pressed a fresh leaf between the diary's pages -- a sprig from the doorway fern -- and wrote a single line in her grandmother's script. [588] The lessons are home; the reader must do the rest. [598] Somewhere in the mountain, a page turned itself. [606]</story>
