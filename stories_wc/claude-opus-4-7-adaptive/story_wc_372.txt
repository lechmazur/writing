Hollis Varn climbed the glass stair to the frozen library above clouds, where silence had the color of quartz. [19] As orderly records chief, he had come to measure what remained. [30] The archive held the last luminous vows of a civilization that had spoken its promises into light, and those promises were failing. [52] Hollis set his case on the reading table, its hinges stiff with altitude. [65] He was a gently meticulous man, and he unpacked his instruments the way a surgeon lays out instruments for a dying friend. [87] A brass resonator, a tuning fork of silvered bone, a notebook with a pencil tethered by red thread, and, last, the magic lantern slide. [111] The slide was the reason for his pilgrimage. [119] Each vow, when spoken by a lover or a king or a child, had been captured in a pane of this same enchanted glass, and each pane sang in its own register. [151] His task was to measure them by sampling frequencies before the final thaw reached the upper stacks. [168] Above him, the dome of ice showed the sun as a pale, uncommitted coin. [181] The clouds below looked solid enough to walk on, but Hollis knew better than to trust what merely seemed true. [201] He lifted the first slide and slid it into the lantern. [212] A woman's face bloomed against the frost, her lips parting around a syllable he could not hear, and the resonator stirred with a low A flat. [238] He wrote the number, watched the frequency drift, and wrote again. [249] She had promised to return; the pitch wavered as if she were still keeping her word. [265] Slide after slide, the shelves emptied onto his table. [274] A coronation oath in B major, shrill as unshed tears. [284] A bedtime promise, low and round, the kind a parent repeats until it becomes the child's heartbeat. [301] A treaty sworn between two old enemies, its two voices tuned to a trembling third. [315] Each slide flared, hummed, and dimmed while illusions faded to memory, the bright image on the lantern wall going gray at its edges and then going nowhere at all. [344] Hollis had been warned this might happen. [351] The vows had been sustained for centuries by belief; with belief gone, the panes were shedding their frequencies like snow. [371] His job, officially, was preservation of data. [378] His private purpose, the thing that had brought him through three seasons of wind to this high quartz hush, was different. [399] He had come to conjure meaning from the edge of oblivion, to give the lost promises one last listener before they were only numbers in a ledger no one would open. [430] He worked through the night without noticing the hour. [439] When the lantern warmed the air, small threads of mist rose from the shelves, and the mist smelled, faintly, of every room in which a promise had been uttered: wax, wet wool, bread, iron. [473] Near dawn he reached the last shelf, the one marked with his own family's crest. [488] He had not known it was here. [495] The slide showed a young man, thin and grave, swearing to a woman that he would not forget her. [514] The resonator found the pitch, a hesitant E, the pitch of his father's voice before Hollis had been old enough to keep it. [537] He wrote the number. [541] Then, because the record would outlast him and no one else was in the library, he spoke aloud the words the young man on the slide was mouthing, matching pitch to pitch until the pane brightened once more. [579] The image steadied. [582] For a moment that was not long but was not short either, the vow burned plainly on the frozen wall, and Hollis understood that measurement alone could not have saved it. [613] A witness was needed. [617] He closed the case gently, leaving the slide in the lantern, so the light might last until someone else climbed the stair. [639] Outside, the clouds were thinning toward morning. [646] He descended with his ledger full, carrying, in careful columns, the frequencies of every remembered promise. [662] Somewhere below, a child was learning a word. [670] He hoped the child would mean it. [677]
