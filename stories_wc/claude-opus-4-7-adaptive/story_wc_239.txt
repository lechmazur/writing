The temple library had forgotten which century it belonged to, and so had I. [14] They called me the end of history witness, though no one who coined the title had ever survived to confirm it. [35] My duty was to watch the last filament of time fray and, if I could, to unravel cosmic illusions before the loom went dark. [59] I carried only a softcover dictionary, its spine creased into a soft hinge by decades of thumbing. [76] In a place where reality forgot its grammar, a dictionary was both weapon and compass. [91] The library breathed in a resounding hush, the kind of silence that pressed against the ears like deep water. [110] Shelves spiraled upward beyond sight, each ring a court, each court an argument, each argument a century trying to outvote the next. [132] This was spiral arbitration: the sacred method by which the cosmos judged itself, winding its contradictions tighter until some central truth was squeezed out like oil. [158] I had arrived across a universe's hiccup, that strange stutter when causality coughed and let a stray pilgrim slip between its teeth. [180] In that narrow gasp of possibility, I meant to climb the spiral and read the final verdict before it was pronounced. [201] The first shelf held a book arguing that love was arithmetic. [212] The second held one arguing that arithmetic was love. [221] Between them, a thin cord of dust balanced the dispute. [231] I opened my dictionary and looked up both words; the definitions had begun to braid. [246] Higher up, I found a monk who had died mid-sentence, his final word half-written, wandering toward a conclusion he never reached. [267] I finished the word for him, wanderingly exact: each pen stroke patient, each letter sure, guesses steadied by the weight of my book. [290] The shelves rustled approval; the hush deepened into something almost musical. [301] As I climbed, I understood the temple's trick. [309] Its method was journeying inward while walking out: each step along the ascending gallery carried me further into the coils of my own suspicion. [333] I began to remember arguments I had never had, losses that belonged to no life I had lived. [351] The librarian appeared on the seventh coil, a figure bound in translucent robes of index cards. [367] She asked what I hoped to do with the verdict once I held it. [381] To unify the arguments, I said; to lay the cosmos's quarrels against my dictionary and see which words the universe had been mispronouncing about itself. [406] She smiled with the patience of a verb awaiting its subject. [417] Then unify yourself first, she said, for the witness is also the witnessed. [430] I opened the dictionary to the word I, and the page was blank. [443] Every other term was defined by its neighbors, yet the self dangled, unsourced. [456] The hiccup in the universe trembled; I felt it near its end, the gasp almost exhaled. [472] So I wrote my own definition into the blank, carefully, wanderingly exact, letter by letter, until I formed a sentence that described no one and therefore everyone. [499] The shelves unspiraled. [502] Arguments folded into one another like hands finally recognizing their own fingers. [514] The illusions I had come to unravel turned out to be definitions quarreling over a single noun that had always been us. [536] History did not end; it remembered that it had never begun in pieces. [549] The library exhaled the hush back into ordinary air, and the ancient stacks settled around me like patient grandparents. [568] I descended, though descent and ascent had become the same gesture, a single breath performed inward and outward at once. [588] The librarian handed me the softcover dictionary once more, though I had never put it down. [604] Go witness somewhere else, she said, kindly; the end does not require an audience, only a reader. [621] I walked out of the temple library and into a morning that had learned my name while I was away. [641]
