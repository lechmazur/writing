The floating rock dojo hung above a world that could not decide what it had been. [16] Below, history rewrote itself in slow pulses, coastlines contracting and expanding, wars gaining and losing their dead. [33] Mira climbed the last stone step carrying a jar. [42] Inside the jar floated a small brain, pale as boiled milk, tagged with a paper label that read simply: DUPLICATE. [63] She had come for the custody hearing. [70] Master Oda waited cross-legged on the dojo's one flat outcrop, his robes stirring in a wind that did not exist anywhere else. [92] Behind him knelt the other Mira, the clone, hands folded like a child at prayer. [107] "You both claim the brain," Oda said, and his voice sliced through pretense the way wet paper tears along a fold. [128] "Only one of you can keep it." [135] Mira set the jar between them. [141] She had been the ego death survivor; the clone had been grown from a tissue sample taken before her collapse, a duplicate of who she had been on her steadiest morning. [172] After the dissolution, after the long white nothing, Mira had returned with most of her memories and none of her feeling. [193] Food had no taste. [197] Her daughter's laugh was arithmetic. [202] The brain in the jar was a spare, cultured from the same tissue. [215] A technician could graft it into either woman. [223] The clone wanted it because she had inherited Mira's habits but not her history. [237] Mira wanted it to vivify the parts of herself that had died when the rest of her dissolved. [255] "State your claims," Oda said. [260] The clone spoke first, her voice measured. [267] "I can feel," she said. [272] "Every morning I wake and the light has weight." [281] "I would continue her life as she would have wanted." [291] Mira's turn. [293] "I am the one who survived," she said. [301] "I walked out of the fire of ego death, and I am trying to come back." [317] "I need the brain to defeat the numbness." [325] Oda listened, then placed both hands flat on the stone. [335] "A custody battle resolves through complementary opposites," he said. [344] "Neither of you alone holds the answer." [351] "Feeling without history is sentiment; history without feeling is accounting." [361] He turned the jar so the label faced both of them. [372] "The duplicate is neither of yours until you agree on what it is for." [386] Outside, a river below the dojo reversed, then ran forward again, carrying barges that existed in contradictory centuries. [404] Mira looked at the clone and saw, with something faint but real, her own steady morning face. [421] The clone looked back and saw the exhaustion she had been spared. [433] "You were me before the collapse," Mira said. [441] "And I am you after it." [447] "Between us there is a whole person, but the brain in the jar is only one." [463] The clone nodded slowly. [467] "Then we split the use, not the tissue." [475] They proposed it to Oda together: the duplicate would be vivified in a shared vessel, a neutral body cultured to receive them both, one carrying memory, the other carrying feeling, neither swallowing the other. [509] Oda's attention sliced through the pretense of their compromise, testing for the lie of a woman wanting to disappear by dividing herself. [531] He found none. [534] "Granted," he said. [537] "On one condition." [540] "You return here each season while history is still unstable, and you prove the arrangement has not become a new numbness." [561] They agreed. [563] That evening, the technicians began the graft, and the label on the jar was peeled away, because DUPLICATE was no longer the right word for what the brain was about to become. [595] Below the dojo, a century changed its mind again, and a small battlefield unmade itself into a wheat field. [614] Mira felt the sun on the stones. [621] It had weight. [624] She did not yet know whose feeling that was, hers or the clone's or the newly vivified third thing growing between them, but it did not matter. [651] For the first time since the dissolution, something inside her agreed to stay. [664]
