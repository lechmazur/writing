When the anesthesia wore off, Ilse found herself lying among violet trees whose bark pulsed with a soft ultraviolet light. [20] She did not remember arriving. [25] She rarely remembered arriving anywhere anymore. [31] In her fist, damp with cold sweat, she clutched a pewter mask strap, the only thing she had carried out of the white rooms. [55] The surgeons had promised it would be her last operation: a small rewiring behind the ear, a correction for the correction, they said, to quiet the echoes the program had installed in her nerves. [89] She was, in their careful paperwork, a survivor of enhanced interrogation. [100] In her own paperwork, which she kept in her body, she was something else entirely. [115]

The forest around her was not a forest she knew. [125] The trees leaned in the violet wavelength, and moss beneath her hands shimmered like struck flint. [141] Yet her limbs knew the ground with uncanny precision; she could feel the slope of the root beneath her hip to the tenth of a degree, could sense the breath of wind across the small hairs of her neck as data, not sensation. [184] That was their gift, the only one she had not refused: she had been made proprioceptively enhanced, every joint a reporter, every muscle a witness. [209] She knew where her body was, always. [216] What she did not know was where she was. [225]

She pressed the pewter strap to her lips. [233] The metal tasted faintly of someone else's fear. [241] It had once buckled a mask to a face that was not hers; a man named Corven, whose interrogation she had been made to assist in the final week before her defection. [273] She had kept the strap because she could not keep him. [284] When her pulse surged now, she did not see his face. [295] Instead, her left hand spasmed toward her throat, her breath shortened into fours, her shoulder blades drew together as if bracing against a wall she could not see. [323] This was how she remembered: through the body, through trauma responses she had learned to read like letters. [341] A sparrow landed on a violet branch, and she flinched, then catalogued the flinch. [355]

The forest, she understood slowly, was a penance garden. [364] She had heard the rumor in the recovery ward: certain nations, ashamed at last, had planted ultraviolet forests in places where black sites once stood, ground soaked with what could not be washed away, the light calibrated to the wavelength at which human skin could not lie. [411] Ultraviolet penance, the orderlies called it, half a joke, half a prayer. [423] The trees did not forgive. [428] They only recorded. [431]

She stood, and her knees knew the angle of standing before she had committed to it. [447] She began to walk between the luminous trunks, and with every step her body produced a report: here, a memory of kneeling; here, a memory of being told to count. [477] She did not fight the reports. [483] She had come here for a reason she could barely articulate, a private arithmetic she had been trying for two years to solve. [506] She wanted to discover the mathematics of mercy. [514] How much grief did one debt require? [521] How many nights of shaking hands equaled one afternoon in which Corven had been made to speak? [538] She did not believe in forgiveness, which felt to her like a currency other people printed. [554] She believed in equations. [558]

At the center of the forest she found a clearing where the violet light pooled like water, and she sat at its edge and braced her palms against the humming earth. [589] The trauma came through her shoulders first, then her jaw, then finally the hot clear grief she had been too efficient to weep. [612] She let the numbers run. [617] Each tremor was a term; each held breath, a coefficient. [627] She had no proof the math would close, only the strap in her hand and the ultraviolet patience around her. [647]

When at last she looked up, the trees had not moved, and yet she felt herself, for the first time since the rooms, both displaced from every country and rooted in this one hour. [681] It was not forgiveness. [685] It was, perhaps, a remainder small enough to carry. [694] She tied the pewter strap around her wrist, stood on legs that knew exactly where they were, and began walking out of the violet light toward whatever country came next. [724]
