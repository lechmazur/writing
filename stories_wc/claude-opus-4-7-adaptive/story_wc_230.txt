Mira tended the hanging orchard alone, her ropes singing in the ravine wind. [13] The trees grew sideways from the cliffs, roots clawed into basalt, fruit swaying above a river that had not stopped raging since the empathy virus passed through three years before. [43] She had come here because the pigs liked the slope, and the truffles liked the pigs, and she liked neither company nor lies. [66] That last preference was no longer a preference. [74] It was a symptom. [78] The virus had not killed many. [84] It had only rewritten a small loop of cortex so that every infected mouth produced what the speaker actually believed. [104] Doctors called it involuntary truthfulness. [108] Survivors called it the unbearable courtesy. [114] Marriages dissolved. [116] Governments thinned. [118] Mira, who valued evidence and mistrusted sentiment, had thought herself well-equipped, and had been wrong within an hour of waking. [138] So she retreated to the ravine, where the storms occluded the sky most afternoons and she could speak only to her two pigs, Ada and Bayes, whose feelings she could measure but not wound. [172] The orchard hung from iron pitons her grandfather had hammered into the rock when truffles were currency. [189] Now they were only food, and Mira was only a forager, and the world above the canyon rim had grown quiet in a way she did not investigate. [217] Then the climber came. [221] He arrived in the second week of autumn, lowered on a borrowed rope, a leather satchel pressed against his ribs. [241] He introduced himself as Olen and asked, with the practiced softness of someone selling something dangerous, whether she was the woman who had been a physician before the orchard. [270] "I read anatomy for two years," she said, hating the precision the virus forced from her. [286] "I never practiced." [289] "I am a truffle forager." [294] "I find your face hopeful in a way that worries me." [305] He smiled, undeterred, and opened the satchel. [312] Inside, wrapped in oiled silk, was an antique glass eye, milky at its edges, the pupil painted a startled blue. [332] A smuggled relic, he explained, taken from a sealed museum in the lowlands; the customs runners had wanted nothing to do with it after they learned what it was rumored to do. [364] "Rumored where," she asked. [368] "In the textbooks they pulped," he said. [375] "The ones the Academy discredited a century ago." [383] "Sympathetic optics." [385] "Humoral occlusion." [387] "A glass lens placed against the sternum to interrupt the speech of the soul." [401] Mira laughed once, sharp as a struck flint. [409] "You believe that." [412] "I believe my sister stopped eating because every honest person at her table told her exactly what they thought of her." [433] "I believe she is alive because a woman in Petrel sold her one of these and she now hosts dinners where her guests can lie politely again." [460] "I believe I would like to leave one with you." [470] The wind shifted. [473] A late plum dropped from a branch above and burst on the ledge between them. [488] She wanted to argue from the textbooks she still trusted. [498] She wanted to send him up the rope. [506] Instead she heard herself say, "I am lonely in a manner that frightens me, and I would like to host a dinner." [528] That was how the orchard became, that winter, a place people came to be untrue. [543] Mira wore the eye on a cord against her sternum, and found that whether the cure was physics or theater, her tongue obeyed her again. [568] She could compliment a stew she found dull. [576] She could tell a grieving man his wife had spoken of him kindly when she had not. [593] She could cultivate, for the strangers who climbed down to her ledges, a small psychological safety: a room where their faces were not examined, where their words could carry the soft fictions that hold a life together. [630] She knew the relic might be only glass. [638] She knew her own willingness to deceive again might be the cure, and the eye merely its excuse. [656] The discredited textbooks were probably wrong about everything except the wanting. [668] Storms battered the ravine through the long months. [676] The orchard swung and held. [681] Mira poured wine for her guests, lied beautifully, and felt, for the first time since the virus, the fractured grace of being a person among persons, none of them required to be entirely seen.
