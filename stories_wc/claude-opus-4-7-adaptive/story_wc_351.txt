The canyon held its breath under a full moon, and Iona Vance descended into it carrying a broken periscope mirror wrapped in felt. [23] She called herself a helix-minded engineer because her thoughts spiraled: every problem doubled back on itself, strands of logic pairing with strands of feeling until she could not tell which was which. [55] Tonight, the pairing mattered. [59] Tonight, she meant to modify the dead. [66] The mirror had belonged to her brother Theo, salvaged from the submarine he never came home from. [83] Its silvering was cracked across the middle like a frozen river, and when she tilted it she could still see the shape of his last glance, the sliver of sky he had watched before the hull gave way. [121] That, at least, was what she told herself. [129] Memory, she had learned, was chaotically precise: wrong in its dates, exact in its ache. [144] She had spent three years building the lattice she now carried in a cooled steel case. [160] It was a device that worked through synaptic plasticity, coaxing neurons to rewire around an external signal the way ivy rewires around a trellis. [184] Fed the right input, a brain could be taught to remember what another brain had known. [200] Fed the wrong input, it could be taught to forget itself. [211] The canyon floor was chalky and silvered, a bone laid flat under the moon. [225] She found the altar she had chosen months ago, a sandstone shelf worn smooth by water no longer here. [244] She set the mirror upright. [249] She set the lattice beside it. [255] She set her own head between them. [262] She had come here during a period of profound transformation, the kind the journals warned would take anyone who undertook such work. [284] Her skin felt borrowed. [288] Her hands, as she threaded the electrodes along her temples, belonged to the girl she had been before the Navy's letter, and the woman she was after it, and someone she had not yet met. [323] Three selves wiring one head. [328] To become the archeologist of abandoned dreams: that was how she had written her purpose in the flyleaf of her field book. [350] Everyone left dreams behind, but no one went back to dig. [361] Theo had wanted to build cities under the sea. [370] Their mother had wanted to paint weather. [377] Iona's father had wanted to sing and had instead sold insurance for forty years. [391] Somewhere, those intentions still lay, compressed into the silt of lived lives. [404] Iona meant to exhume them. [409] She meant to wear them. [414] She activated the lattice. [418] The mirror caught the moonlight and split it, throwing a fractured oval onto the canyon wall. [434] Within that oval her own face wavered, then Theo's, then both, overlaid. [446] The tone of the night shifted into a quaking reverie: the stone seemed to hum, the air to breathe in her lungs rather than the other way around. [474] The entangled memories arrived not as pictures but as pressure. [484] She felt the cold of a pressure hull. [492] She felt a paintbrush she had never held. [500] She felt the weight of a ledger closed at five o'clock for the thousandth time. [515] And beneath all of it, like a ground note, she felt her own small dream, the one she had buried under engineering because engineering was the family's idea of safety. [545] She had wanted to write. [550] Just that. [552] To write sentences that held canyons inside them. [561] The lattice hissed a warning. [566] She was overloading. [569] If she pushed further, the plasticity would run away from her and she would become a library without a librarian. [589] She pulled the electrodes off. [594] She did not weep. [598] She picked up the broken mirror and looked into it one more time, and this time she saw only herself, older than she expected, her eyes carrying new weather. [627] At dawn she climbed out of the canyon with the mirror under her arm and the lattice powered down. [646] She had not resurrected Theo. [651] She had not become her mother. [657] She had done something smaller and stranger: she had remembered that other people's unfinished wanting was not hers to finish, and that her own was still unburied, still breathing, still there. [688] She drove home. [691] She opened a blank page. [696] She began. [698]
