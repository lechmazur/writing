<story>The workshop glowed topaz under a sky that was losing its stars one by one. [15] Invo, the letterpress operator, worked in the interval between vanishings, that narrow suspension when one constellation had already gone dark and the next had not yet winked out. [43] He called these moments his working hours. [50] On the press bed lay a single halo fragment, curved like a parenthesis broken off a larger sentence. [68] He had bought it from a pilgrim who could no longer feel pity, and who claimed the fragment had drunk the emotion out of him. [93] Invo believed this. [96] He believed, in particular, that emotions obeyed arithmetic, and he meant to prove it. [110] His motivation was old and patient: to understand the mathematics of emotion, the way grief divided, the way tenderness multiplied, the way courage subtracted itself from fear. [137] For years he had tested hypotheses on himself by habitually borrowing personalities from anyone who came to his door. [156] A grieving widow's posture, a gambler's laugh, a child's careless certainty. [167] He wore them like ink-stained aprons, then hung them back on the rack when he was finished. [184] Tonight he had borrowed the pilgrim's flatness, the hollow discipline of a man without pity, hoping that wearing a wound would show him its shape. [209] The halo fragment hummed when he laid a letter beside it. [220] This was the experiment: to print small broadsides, each carrying a phrase composed through deliberate ambiguity, and fold the fragment's charge into the ink. [244] Readers, encountering the phrases, would feel an emotion that was not quite their own. [258] He called the effect transposed empathy. [264] One soul's feeling routed through another's body, measurable by how long the reader stood still afterward. [280] The ambiguity was essential. [284] A sentence too clear would instruct; a sentence too vague would slip past. [297] Only a phrase poised between two meanings could disorient the reader enough to let a borrowed feeling settle where their own had been. [320] He set the type now, piece by piece, under lamplight that turned every brass slug to honey. [337] The chase locked with a small, decisive click. [345] Above, a star went out over the western roofline; the next had not yet followed. [360] He had a minute, maybe two. [366] Invo inked the rollers, laid the paper, and pulled the lever. [377] The halo fragment brightened; the press sighed. [384] When he lifted the sheet, the phrase on it read: She will not remember whether she forgave him. [402] He read it once and felt nothing. [409] He read it twice and felt the pilgrim's hollow widen inside his ribs, then narrow into something he had not packed for: a grief that belonged to a woman he had never met, routed through a man who could no longer host it, delivered to him by a curl of paper. [460] The workshop tilted. [463] This was lucid turbulence, the state he had been chasing for years: awareness perfectly intact while the interior weather reorganized itself. [484] He could name every tool on the bench and still not say whose hand was reaching for them. [502] He worked through it. [506] He measured the duration of the sensation, the decay curve of the borrowed grief, the moment his own quiet returned. [526] He recorded the numbers in a ledger whose margins were already crowded with earlier pulls. [541] A pattern was forming. [545] Sorrow, when transposed, lost fourteen percent of its weight. [554] Joy lost almost none. [558] Pity, apparently, could be distilled out of a person entirely and pressed into a curve of bone-colored light. [576] Another star vanished. [579] Invo set a fresh sheet on the tympan. [587] He had begun to suspect that his borrowed personalities were not disguises but experiments on a single hypothesis: that there was no private self beneath the borrowing, only the traffic of feelings between bodies, and the press was simply the first instrument honest enough to measure it. [634] If so, he was not cryptic by temperament but by necessity. [645] A man made of other people's weather could only answer questions with weather of his own. [661] He inked the rollers again. [666] Between the vanishing of one star and the arrival of the next absence, he pulled the lever, and the halo fragment, patient as arithmetic, gave him the next equation to solve. [697]</story>
