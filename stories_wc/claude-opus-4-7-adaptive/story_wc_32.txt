Alessio had failed at alchemy for nineteen years. [8] In the ember nexus at twilight, where three roads of coal-dust met beneath a sky the color of bruised peaches, he knelt with the only inheritance his uncle had left him: a broken bishop's crozier. [43] The shepherd's hook was snapped clean at the curl, its gilt flaking like molted feathers, and the shaft bore a hairline crack that no solder had ever closed. [71] He was, by every measure, a hopeless alchemist. [79] His ledgers proved it; his scars proved it; the villagers who crossed themselves when he passed proved it most of all. [100] Still, he came here each dusk to try again. [109] He had been raised to become a bishop himself, trained in Latin from age four, promised a small diocese by a cousin who collected favors the way other men collected coins. [140] That destiny had felt like wet wool pulled over his face. [151] To interrupt a stifling destiny, his uncle had once told him, you do not need to shatter the world; you need only refuse one step of it, cleanly, on purpose. [181] The old man had said this while dying, and Alessio, stoically sensitive as only a disappointed son can be, had not wept until three days after the funeral, alone, by a forge he did not yet know how to light. [221] Now he knew how to light it. [228] The ember nexus bloomed around him, small vents in the earth exhaling orange breath, each coal a paused heartbeat. [247] He placed the broken crozier across the central vent. [256] His work, tonight, was to vivify it: to coax whatever residual authority slept in the wood and gilt to wake, stand, and walk away from him as a living thing, so that he might be unbishoped forever by the visible desertion of his symbol. [300] Absurd, his neighbors would say. [305] Blasphemous, his cousin would say. [310] Alessio said nothing and opened his notebook. [317] He worked through successive approximation, the only method a hopeless man can trust: each attempt a rung, each failure a measurement, the truth assembled the way a blind mason assembles a wall by feeling where the last stone sat wrong. [357] First pass: salt of lead, three drops of his own blood, a whispered name from the baptismal register. [375] The crozier warmed but did not stir. [382] Second pass: he doubled the blood, halved the salt, sang instead of whispered. [395] The hairline crack widened; a curl of smoke rose that smelled improbably of lilacs. [409] Third pass, fourth, seventh. [413] By the eleventh attempt the gilt began to flow like honey, pooling and re-forming, each pool a slightly better approximation of a shepherd's hook than the last. [440] He understood then what his uncle had meant about creative destruction. [451] To make the staff live, he had to keep breaking it; each repair was a smaller ruin, and each smaller ruin was closer to the shape the staff had wanted all along, before a man had carved it into an office. [492] The tone of the air changed. [498] A frozen bloom settled over the nexus, frost flowering on coals that still glowed beneath, petals of ice holding their shape above fire, as though grief and warmth had agreed to share a single surface. [533] Alessio did not smile. [537] He only watched, and adjusted, and watched. [544] At the thirty-first pass, when the penny dropped, he felt it in his teeth before his mind. [561] The crozier rose. [564] It did not leap; it stood, the way a calf stands, negotiating with its own weight. [580] Then it walked, three uncertain steps, and turned to regard him with the attention of something newly alive and already disappointed in its maker. [604] He bowed to it. [608] The staff bowed back, gilt rippling, and then walked east, into the deepening twilight, down a road Alessio would never follow. [629] Behind it the frost melted; ahead of it the embers dimmed. [640] He closed the notebook. [644] His cousin's letter, arriving next Tuesday, would find no bishop-in-waiting, only a man in an apron, soot on his hands, quietly alive. [666] He gathered his tools as the last coal went dark. [676] The hopeless alchemist walked home through fields that suddenly seemed, for the first time in nineteen years, to belong to him. [697]
