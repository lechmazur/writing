<story>Mara arrived on the lavender bluff the night the forests began voting. [12] She was what the old catalogs called a luminous skeptic: skin faintly phosphorescent from a childhood accident, mind stubbornly unconvinced by miracles. [34] The aurora above her rippled in green and rose, a terrestrially cosmic banner stitching sky to tundra. [51] On her index finger she wore a tin compass ring, dented, inherited, older than any satellite map. [68] It had never pointed north. [73] That was the problem she had come to solve. [82] Down in the taiga, results were tallying. [89] Every eleven years the forests held elections, the root-networks trading chemical ballots through fungal wires, choosing which species would govern rainfall, which would surrender to fire. [115] Humans had learned to listen in, but Mara listened differently. [125] She sensed the vote through taste changes on her tongue: copper for oak majorities, anise for the birches, a faint tin-and-honey tang when the pines pushed a coalition. [153] Tonight her mouth held something new, something she could not name. [164] She had come here for Ilan. [170] More precisely, she had come to learn why some people carry weather with them, and Ilan was the best specimen she had ever catalogued. [194] Wherever he walked, barometers trembled. [199] Rain followed his grief; warm fronts chased his laughter. [208] The journals called it coincidence. [213] The luminous skeptic in her agreed, mostly, but the tin ring on her finger had begun to spin whenever he entered a room. [236] He was walking up the slope now, lantern swinging, hair silvered by the aurora. [250] "You made it," he said. [255] "The forests are still counting." [260] "I can taste them arguing." [265] She held up her hand; the ring ticked once, twice, then settled, its needle aimed squarely at his sternum. [284] "It keeps doing that," she said. [290] "Your grandmother's ring." [293] "My grandmother's ring has never agreed with magnetism, gravity, or good sense." [305] Ilan smiled the tired smile of a man who had been somebody's experiment before. [319] He sat beside her in the lavender, which crushed and released a scent like old radio static, the smell she always associated with futures that had not arrived. [347] "Ask it," he said. [351] So she did. [354] She asked the ring why storms loved him, and the ring answered by tasting, strangely, of rain on hot pavement, then of snowmelt, then of the ozone that follows lightning. [384] A picture converged in her mouth the way an image converges in a developing bath. [399] She understood, finally, that the compass was not a thing her grandmother had made. [413] It was an emergent compass, arising wherever enough small instruments agreed: her luminous skin, his weather, the voting roots, the aurora's iron whisper. [436] None alone pointed anywhere. [440] Together they converged on a direction the planet itself seemed to want. [452] "North is a story," Ilan said softly. [459] "The real needle is all of us leaning the same way at once." [472] She tasted the new flavor again, and this time she named it: a ballot cast by every living thing for the same quiet outcome, a vote to continue. [500] The forests had elected stillness this cycle, a fallow term, a breath. [512] Ilan's weather, she saw, was not his at all. [521] He was simply porous; the planet's moods passed through him the way light passes through certain old glass. [539] Her skepticism did not dissolve, it never did, but it made room. [551] "I came to prove you weren't special," she admitted. [560] "I'm not," he said. [564] "None of us are, which is the good news." [573] The aurora dipped low enough to touch the lavender, and for a moment Mara's phosphorescent hands matched its color exactly, as though her body were a small outpost of the sky. [604] Somewhere below, a final tally of oaks whispered through her molars. [615] She slipped the tin compass ring off her finger and set it in the grass between them. [632] It spun once, slowly, and chose no direction at all, which was, she realized, the only honest answer it had ever given. [654] They sat together while the forests finished their election, two instruments among many, converging briefly with the weather and the light, nostalgic already for this future they were, improbably, still inside.
