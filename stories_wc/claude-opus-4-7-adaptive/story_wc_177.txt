Mira could see only forward and back, never the now. [10] The present was a blind spot in her sight, a doorway she walked through without memory. [26] This was the gift and rot of her kind: foresight and hindsight only. [39] She had found the bunker by accident, or by the accident that paths sometimes make when they choose their walkers. [59] It crouched beneath a hill that had once been a parking lot, now rewilded, its concrete skin stitched over by moss and the green insistence of vines. [86] Birch and hazel had grown through the old fencing; foxes slept where guards had paced. [101] Inside, on a steel desk furred with dust, waited a folded order bearing a wafer seal the color of dried blood. [122] The seal was intact, but the directive it bound had begun to decay, its ink blooming into mildew, its words loosening from their intentions. [146] A decaying directive, Mira thought, is still a directive; it only means the command is hungry. [162] She did not break the seal. [168] She slept beside it instead, because her kind read the present through interpreted dreams, triangulating the unseen now from what was and what would be. [193] In her first dream a fox wore a courier's coat and laughed at her, precisely playful, its teeth counting her ribs like beads on an abacus. [219] "You want to know why some paths choose their walkers," the fox said. [232] "Ask the seal what it remembers before it was wax." [242] She woke with the smell of ozone in the bunker and knew the storm was approaching. [258] Thunder braided itself into the canopy above; rain began its slow argument with the leaves. [273] In the second dream the wafer seal spoke, not in words but in a lineage of hands. [290] She saw a chemist pressing it, a courier sliding it beneath a door, a soldier weeping over it, and finally, an empty room where the directive had been filed and forgotten by an agency that itself had since dissolved into rumor. [331] The seal remembered every palm that had carried it, and each palm had been chosen, not choosing. [348] In the third dream she walked a path through the rewilded forest and felt it pull, not guide, pull, the way water pulls a stone downhill. [374] The path had a hunger too, older than the directive, and the directive had only been the path's attempt to speak aloud. [396] She understood then that paths choose their walkers because paths are themselves unfinished thoughts, reaching for feet that might complete them. [417] When she woke the storm had arrived in full, shaking the vines, rattling the old ventilation shafts like loose teeth. [437] She took the folded order in her hand and considered the wafer seal one last time. [453] To break it would be to accept the directive, to obey a dead hand that had chosen her across decades of moss. [475] To leave it would be to let the path continue walking her the way it had walked every hand before. [495] Her gift showed her both futures with ethereal clarity and gritted certainty: in one she completed the errand and became another palm in the lineage; in the other she burned. [525] Mira, who had lived her entire life between memory and prophecy, decided to do the one thing her kind was said to be incapable of. [550] She chose the present. [554] She pressed her thumb against the wafer seal, not to break it, but to answer it, to tell the path that a walker could speak back. [580] The seal warmed, then softened, then dissolved into her skin like a name being spoken for the last time. [599] Somewhere, a directive finished decaying, and the path exhaled. [608] Outside, the storm peeled back, precisely playful in its retreat, as if a joke had been shared with the sky. [628] Mira stepped into the rewilded forest and, for the first time, saw the now: wet leaves, her own breath, a fox watching her without a coat. [654] She had not escaped the lineage; she had transcended it, becoming a walker who at last walked herself. [672] Behind her, the vine-draped bunker sighed shut, its long directive finally obeyed by being refused. [687] Ahead, a path waited, new, uncertain, and for the first time in her long doubled life, choosing nothing, asking only to be walked. [710]
