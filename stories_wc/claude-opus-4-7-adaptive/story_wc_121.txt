<story>Mira walked an inch above the ground, a gravity defier in a century that no longer believed in wonders. [19] She came to the glowing viaduct after rain because the stones remembered things the sky had tried to wash away. [39] In her satchel lay an ancient clay tablet with runes, cold as a dropped coin, heavy as a vow. [58] The runes, she had learned, were not a language but an engine, a map generated through procedural generation each time a reader's eyes fell on them. [84] No two scholars had ever seen the same tablet twice. [94] Tonight the runes promised a doorway between the drop and the ripple, that impossibly thin timeframe when a raindrop meets a puddle but the circle has not yet begun to spread. [125] Mira needed that sliver because inside it she was periodically eternal, an impossible creature with enough time to retrace the wrong turns of her own life. [151] Her motivation was simple and terrifying: to conquer the labyrinth of self, the corridors of guilt and pride she had built since she was small enough to float above her mother's kitchen. [183] She waited on the viaduct as lamplight trembled in the puddles, each bulb a small sun being slowly strangled by its own halo. [206] The world held that darkening brightness only wet cities know, where every glow admits the night pressing against it. [225] A drop fell from the eave above her. [233] Mira opened the tablet. [237] The runes rearranged themselves, summoning a corridor from her own memory reshuffled into something new. [252] She stepped in between the drop and the ripple, and fractured timelines bloomed around her like pages torn from a single book and sewn into many. [278] In one, she had never left home; in another, she had never spoken to her sister again; in another, she had flown too high and forgotten how to come down. [308] Each branch was a door; each door was a question; the labyrinth was her. [322] She walked inside herself, weightless, as the city paused with a raindrop suspended like a jewel behind her shoulder. [341] Her gift, her curse, was that the further she went, the brighter the corridors burned, and the darker the spaces between them became. [364] She met a younger Mira clutching a broken cup and told her, truthfully, that no one had been angry; she found a future Mira and listened without interrupting. [392] She knelt in a hallway where her sister's last letter lay unopened and read it this time, aloud, trembling. [411] Every step was a retracing, but the tablet refused to repeat geometry, so no remembered room welcomed her back unchanged. [431] Procedural generation is merciful, she discovered, because it will not let the mind grind the same grief into the same groove. [452] Hours passed inside her eternal instant, then years, then something that was neither. [465] She understood that being periodically eternal meant only that she would be granted these drops between ripples, these cracks between seconds, until she chose to stop running. [492] At last, at the labyrinth's center, she found no monster, only a chair and a window and a woman who looked like her. [515] The woman said, you do not have to defy gravity to be lifted. [528] Mira set the tablet down on the chair; the runes exhaled and went quiet. [542] She stepped back into the world just as the ripple began to spread, a silver ring widening from the drop she had bested. [565] Her feet touched the viaduct stone for the first time in her life. [578] The lamps kept their trembling halos; the night kept pressing in; yet the brightness, though darkening, now held steady, as if something inside her had decided which of all her timelines to keep. [611] She walked home on the ground, and the ground, astonished, held her. [623]</story>
