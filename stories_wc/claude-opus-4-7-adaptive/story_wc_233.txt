Queen Ilene had stopped believing in her own crown three winters ago. [11] Since then, the same dream visited her every night: a cobalt grotto above clouds, its walls weeping light, and a voice she almost recognized. [35] The court astrologers called it bleeding starlight, a phenomenon only recorded twice in three centuries, and always before ruin. [54] After the final warning from her seneschal - who begged her not to leave the keep unguarded - she went anyway, to unravel the mystery of recurring dreams that had hollowed her sleep into an echoing room. [89] She took no retinue. [93] She took rope, a lantern, and a knife whose weight she liked. [105] The path began at the ember grotto on the ridge, a shallow cave where pilgrims had once set coals to beg warm summers. [128] The coals were cold now. [133] Lace of frost covered the altar stones, pretty and predatory, a lacy menace she could not name but could taste on her tongue like iron. [158] From there, the only way upward was to climb. [167] She climbed at dusk. [171] The rock was kind at first, then miserly, then cruel. [181] Handholds shrank. [183] Wind teased her cloak like a bored cat. [191] She did not look down because looking down was a bargain she had already refused. [206] Halfway up, she met the boy. [212] He sat on a ledge as if he had been waiting for decades. [225] "You are late," he said, softly commanding, in the tone of someone whose patience was not patience but rule. [244] She knew the voice. [248] It had spoken in every dream. [254] "Who are you?" she asked. [259] "The part of the grotto that remembers you." [267] They climbed the rest together. [272] He moved as if the mountain were listening. [280] When the cliff opened into the cobalt grotto above clouds, she understood why her dreams had looked drowned: the whole chamber was submerged in blue, sky pooled inside stone, and above, through a wound in the ceiling, the night sky bled starlight in slow lacing threads. [326] The threads pooled on the floor in bright wounds. [335] "This is what your grandmother closed," the boy said. [344] "This is what your mother forgot to guard." [352] "This is what you dreamed because the bargain is coming due." [363] "What bargain?" [365] "A queen every century." [369] "To keep the starlight from bleeding through." [376] Ilene laughed, and the laugh surprised her by being small. [386] She had expected grand fury. [391] Instead she felt relief - this was why she was disenchanted, why crown and court had tasted of ash; some part of her had always known she was collateral. [419] "I won't," she said. [423] "Then the sky falls in." [428] She looked at the boy, who was not a boy, and asked, "Is there a third door?" [445] He tilted his head. [449] "There is always a third door." [455] "But it must be opened by two who share the same breath at the same moment." [471] "What moment?" [473] "A shared exhale spotting the first fireflies of summer." [482] She laughed again, and this time it was bigger. [491] It was almost midsummer. [495] Below, the valley had been stitched with green for weeks. [505] She had come, unknowingly, on time. [511] They sat at the grotto's lip. [517] The clouds beneath them turned to pale fields. [525] The first firefly rose from the slope - a single, stubborn green spark - and without speaking, she and the boy exhaled together, once, seeing it. [549] The starlight stopped bleeding. [553] It did not seal. [557] It did not heal. [561] It braided - the threads gathering into a lace that was no longer menace but pattern, something worn, something carried. [580] The boy faded the way dreams fade when you finally name them. [592] Ilene descended alone. [595] Her crown felt different on the ridge: not lighter, not heavier, just hers. [608] She passed the ember grotto and lit the old coals with her lantern because a queen who had unlearned enchantment might still keep a fire for pilgrims. [635] She thought of her grandmother, who had climbed this path in secret, and her mother, who had not. [653] She thought of the daughter she would now consider having. [663] The dreams did not return that night, or any night after. [674] But every summer, when the fireflies rose, she went to the ridge and exhaled into the dark, alone, and somewhere above the clouds, the starlight held its lace. [702]
