<story>Tova came down into the catacomb orchard the way she did everything: too fast, too bright, too willing to trade herself for a chance. [24] Down here, fig trees and blackthorn grew from the chests of sleepers, their roots snaking between ribs and hipbones like patient fingers sorting old mail. [49] She carried one thing that mattered: a burned tarot card, its edges blackened, its face a Fool scorched nearly faceless. [69] The Broker lived at the orchard's heart, and the Broker bartered destinies. [81] Tova meant to patch the holes in her soul, even if the thread was someone else's life. [98] Half-lit corridors glowed with lanterns of amber resin, and within that glow every footfall sounded like a confession. [116] She had been called reckless at the surface, idealist at the seminary, fool in the market, and she had accepted all three coins and spent them. [142] The Broker was an old woman with hands worn gentle, the kind that could set a broken bird or snap a neck with equal tenderness. [167] She sat on a stool between two pear trees whose roots cradled a child-sized skull. [182] "You brought me a dead card," the Broker said. [191] "I brought what's left," Tova answered, laying the burned tarot card on the offered palm. [206] It had been her mother's, drawn from a fire she hadn't been fast enough to stop. [222] The Fool, half-erased, stood at her mother's edge still stepping off the cliff. [235] The Broker turned it over, reading the char like braille. [245] She had a habit of inlaying meaning everywhere: in ash, in root-shape, in the grain of a listener's silence. [264] "You want someone else's end," she said. [271] "I want her years back," Tova whispered. [278] "Not possible, but I can trammel a stranger's fate to yours, braid their remaining days into your shortfall." [296] "They would sleep here, beneath a tree, feeding leaves while you walked the sun." [310] Tova swallowed; the orchard smelled of turned soil and quiet apples. [321] She had come ready to say yes, because yes had always been her country. [335] Yet the half-lit corridors around them trembled with small lives: moth-wings on resin, a sleeper dreaming his teeth into roots. [355] Each sleeper, she realized, had once come down for the same bargain. [367] "Whose years?" she asked. [371] "A baker's son upstairs, sixteen, careless on rooftops." [379] "He will fall next Tuesday; take his fall for him, and he lives while we split his surplus." [397] Tova laughed, a small dusk-colored sound. [403] The idealist in her had not expected a child. [412] The reckless in her had not expected to care. [421] "Switch it," she said, voice finding a new, dusky resolve. [431] "Trammel me to the boy instead; let my remaining days catch him when he falls." [446] The Broker's hands paused over the card. [453] "That isn't patching your soul; that's giving it away." [462] "No," Tova said; "the holes were always shaped like other people." [473] She thought of her mother, the Fool stepping, the fire she had not outpaced. [487] Recklessness, she understood now, was only idealism that had stopped apologizing. [498] The Broker weighed her with eyes the color of wet bark, then laid the burned tarot card at the foot of a young apple tree whose roots had already chosen a place for Tova's ribs. [533] "Done," she said; "when he slips, you will be the hand." [544] Tova climbed back through the half-lit corridors with a steadier heart. [555] The orchard exhaled behind her, inlaying meaning everywhere as it always did: in the scuff of her boots, in the tremor of resin-light, in the softness of a stranger's unspoken Tuesday. [586] On Tuesday she stood below a baker's roof and held out fiercely gentle hands. [600] The boy fell; she caught; her reckless arms buckled into an embrace that does not ask forgiveness, only offers. [619] Her spine took the cobbles so his did not. [628] In the breath before dusk she felt the holes in her soul close from the outside in, patched not by bargain but by the simple shape of a boy still breathing. [659] Somewhere below, a young apple tree opened its first flower. [669]</story>
