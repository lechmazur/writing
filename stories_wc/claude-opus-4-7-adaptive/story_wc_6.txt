Mira walked down to the obsidian lagoon beneath the dunes during the migration of clouds. [15] Above her, gray shoals slid eastward in their slow, annual drift, and the air carried that kelp-drift peace she had only ever found here. [39] She was a firefighter, and she was the arsonist's daughter, and the lagoon did not care which mattered more. [58] In her pocket sat a foreign coin drilled and strung on red thread, the only thing her father had left her before the courts took him away. [85] He had told her it was a charm against bad burns, and for years she had despised the joke of it. [106] Now she wore it because the weight on its red thread reminded her, gracefully alert, of every door she had kicked through. [128] The lagoon's water was a polished black mirror, and around its edge lay black sand from the volcanic beach a hundred miles south, hauled here by the same currents that hauled the clouds. [161] Mira knelt and let a handful of it run through her fingers. [173] She had come to graft something onto her own life, the way orchard hands grafted living wood to living wood. [193] She wanted to bind the part of her that had carried gasoline cans as a child to the part of her that now carried hoses, and she wanted the seam to hold. [225] Beside her sat Aunt Vesna, who had raised her after the trials, and who had once told her that scars can sing if you let them practice. [252] That sentence had become Mira's whole motivation, the reason she walked into smoke instead of away from it. [270] Three nights ago she had pulled a boy from a burning kitchen on Halver Street. [285] His father, panicked and ashamed, had set the fire himself for the insurance, and when Mira understood this in the smoke she had felt something that surprised her. [313] Not anger; not even the old, familiar nausea of recognition. [323] She had felt an unyielding compassion, steady as a hand on a shoulder, and she had carried both the boy and the trembling father out into the cold street. [352] She had not known that was the shape inside her until the smoke pressed it into a form. [370] Now, at the lagoon, Vesna unwrapped a small clay bowl. [380] Mira poured the black sand into it and laid the foreign coin on top, the red thread coiled around it like a sleeping vein. [404] Above them, the migration of clouds slowed, as it always seemed to over the lagoon, as if the sky paused to listen. [426] Mira spoke her father's name aloud for the first time in a decade. [439] She did not forgive him. [444] Forgiveness was a different country, and she might never reach it. [455] But she said his name and let the sand swallow it, and she felt the graft take, the wild rootstock of her childhood joined to the cultivar of who she was choosing to become. [489] Vesna pressed the coin back into Mira's palm and tied the red thread again at her wrist. [506] Charm against bad burns, she said, and this time the joke did not sting. [520] Mira watched the lagoon return her face, gracefully alert, salt-bright, unburned. [531] Somewhere along Halver Street the boy was eating breakfast with relatives; somewhere a courthouse was preparing a docket for his father. [552] Mira would testify on Monday, gently and without flinching, because that was also the work. [567] Compassion did not mean lying. [572] It meant carrying everyone out, including the truth. [580] The clouds resumed their migration. [585] Vesna gathered the bowl, and the two of them climbed the dunes back toward the road, the obsidian lagoon dimming behind them like a closed eye. [611] On her wrist the red thread held, and Mira walked into the rest of her life with a small, foreign weight at her pulse, certain at last that her scars were beginning, very quietly, to sing.
