In the cobalt observatory, autumn wind slipped through the copper seams and made the dome hum like a struck bell. [20] Mira, the court's galaxy-chart embroiderer, climbed the spiral stair with a folded silk sky under her arm. [37] She was famous for two things: stitching constellations that aligned with a queen's horoscope, and laughing at the wrong moments. [57] The second habit disguised her wisdom as foolishness, and that disguise had kept her alive through three regimes. [75] Tonight, ten days before the coup everyone pretended not to see, she had come to listen for unheard stories. [94] The observatory was the only place in the capital where silence still echoed. [107] Everywhere else, rumor filled the air so thickly that no truth could draw breath. [121] Here, under the blue dome, the echoed silence let stray voices become audible. [134] At the dome's center burned the flame from the eternal candle, the one gift the old astronomers had smuggled out of the Library before it fell. [160] The candle never shortened; its wax renewed itself from the possibilities it had not yet lit. [176] Mira set down her embroidery, unrolled it across the floor, and knelt as if praying to a map. [194] The silk held the autumn sky she had observed across forty years, each star sewn with a thread dyed from a different season. [217] She had come, she told the apprentice who let her in, to "fix a wrong stitch"—a foolish errand, obviously, for a gown no one would wear. [244] The apprentice, a boy named Ren, laughed politely and left her to the dark. [258] Alone, Mira did what she had never told anyone she could do. [270] She lifted the flame from the eternal candle with a pair of bone tweezers and placed it on the constellation of the Weaver. [293] The flame did not burn the silk; it listened. [302] A candle that fed on adjacent possibilities could whisper the things that almost happened, and the things about to occur. [322] Through adjacent possibilities, the flame threaded its light between the stitched stars, showing Mira the versions of tonight she had not yet chosen. [345] She heard a senator's wife practicing a eulogy for a king still alive. [358] She heard generals synchronize their pocket watches in three separate wings of the palace, ticks falling into a single, terrible rhythm. [379] She heard the queen's physician mix a sleeping draught and call it tea. [392] This, then, was the conspiracy, and it was not one plot but seven, braided so tightly that unraveling them required a needleworker's patience. [415] Mira had always suspected that coups were embroidery in reverse. [425] You found the knot, you teased it loose, and the picture came apart thread by thread. [441] She began where the tension sat highest: the synchronized watches. [451] If the generals moved at dawn, she would give them a dawn that arrived ten minutes early, or not at all. [472] With the flame still resting on the Weaver, she plucked one gold thread from her silk and rewove it two stars to the left. [496] In the adjacent possibility, the palace clocks drifted. [504] She pulled another thread; the physician's tea steeped too long and turned bitter enough to warn the queen. [522] She pulled a third, and the senator's wife wept into her pillow, ashamed of a eulogy she no longer needed. [542] Each stitch rearranged, the observatory's hush seemed to deepen, as though the building itself approved. [557] Ren returned near midnight with a cup of cider and found the embroiderer giggling at her own needle, muttering about a crooked star. [580] He left quickly; fools made him uncomfortable. [587] He would not remember, later, that the foolish old woman had been alone in the one room where silence could still be read. [610] Mira lifted the flame back into its candle, which accepted it like a mother accepting a sleeping child. [628] She rolled up her sky, tucked it under her arm, and descended the spiral stair humming an off-key lullaby. [647] Ten days later, the coup did not happen. [655] The generals' watches disagreed by a full quarter-hour; the queen woke with a bitter tongue and suspicious eyes; the senator's wife burned her speech. [679] In the cobalt observatory, autumn leaves drifted against the door, and the eternal candle burned on, a little brighter for the stories it had finally been allowed to tell. [708] Somewhere in a tower she had never visited, a boy named Ren woke from a dream he could not recall, certain only that he had once laughed at a woman he should have bowed to. [743]
