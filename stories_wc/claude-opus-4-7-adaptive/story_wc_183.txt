The ship was called the Velouria, and she had been drifting in the foggy bay for thirty years before Mira found it. [22] Mira was the chimera twin discoverer — the woman who, three winters ago, had cataloged a pair of children born sharing one heart and four tongues, neither human nor not. [51] That discovery had made her famous and unwelcome. [59] The journals called her work blissfully aberrant, a phrase she had begun to wear like a coat against the cold. [79] She had come to the Velouria because the twins, before they were taken from her, had whispered the same dream into both her ears: a garden of forgotten names, and a ship that knew the way. [115] The gangplank groaned. [118] Inside, the carousel had stopped mid-gallop, and confetti lay frozen in the rigging. [131] Fog moved through the funhouse mirrors as if browsing them. [141] In the ticket booth she found an emerald swatch card — six shades of green clipped to a brass ring, each labeled in a child's handwriting: moss, envy, pond, lung, parrot, mother. [172] She slipped it into her pocket. [178] She had come to recapture something she could not quite name, and the card felt like a key cut for a door she had not yet seen. [205] The Velouria offered no captain, no crew, only attractions. [214] In the hall of mirrors, Mira watched her reflection split into the twins and join again. [230] In the wax museum, the figures wore her mother's face. [240] She did not weep; weeping was for people whose memories sat still. [252] Hers had begun to alter the moment she stepped aboard, and she felt it as a slow rearranging, like furniture moved at night. [275] The sword swallower in her oldest memory now wore an emerald sash she had never seen before — until she pulled the swatch card out and matched the green exactly to "parrot." [306] Memories were being repainted from her pocket. [313] She understood then how the ship worked. [320] It was not a vessel but a loom, and it told its passengers stories until the stories became the passengers themselves. [341] Through narrative threads — strands of confetti, banners, the long ribbons left by departed dancers — the Velouria stitched newcomers into the fabric of its missing crew. [366] That was how the garden of forgotten names was reached: not by sailing, but by being woven. [383] She walked the deck, deliberately, letting the threads catch her sleeves. [394] A streamer wrapped her wrist; a tassel found her ankle. [404] With each touch, a name surfaced in her — Anselm, Pia, Doro, Klee — names she had never learned but now recalled having loved. [426] Her own name began to soften at the edges. [435] She thought, with a terror that surprised her by being gentle, this is unkindled solace. [450] The phrase rose unbidden, as though stamped on the underside of her tongue. [463] A peace without fire. [467] A rest that did not warm but did not harm. [477] She had spent her career lighting lanterns inside the bodies of strange children to prove they were people; here, finally, was a comfort that asked her to prove nothing. [506] The chimera twins, she knew now, had not been kidnapped. [516] They had walked here ahead of her, the way certain people walk into snow without coats. [532] She kept moving. [535] The threads tightened, and her steps became choreographed, and the choreography became hers. [548] She entered the bow garden — for there was a garden at the bow, hedged with cotton candy turned to coral — and saw the names hanging from every branch like fruit. [578] Her twins stood among them, holding hands with three hands. [588] They smiled at her with each other's mouths. [596] "You came," said one. [600] "We saved you a green," said the other, and reached for her pocket. [613] Mira gave them the emerald swatch card. [620] They tore "mother" from the ring and pinned it to a low branch where her name had been waiting, blank and patient. [642] She felt herself become a thread among threads — held, knotted, finally still. [654] The fog over the bay parted just enough to show the shore, and the shore looked very far, and not at all like home, and entirely like one. [682] This was trapped freedom: she could not leave, and for the first time in a long ferocious life, she did not want to. [705] The Velouria turned in the tide, blissfully aberrant, drifting nowhere with all its passengers found. [720]
