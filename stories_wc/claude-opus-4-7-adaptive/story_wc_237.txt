<story>The platform hung between two storms, a disc of basalt tethered by nothing the eye could see. [17] Mira, a budding rune scholar, knelt at its center where the tea set waited. [31] Above, the first cloudbank had just drained itself; below, the second was still gathering teeth. [46] This hour was what her grandmother called a breath between storms, and it was the only hour the ceremony could be held. [96] Beside the cups lay a crow quill with a trimmed tip, black as a starless sea. [112] Her teacher had forbidden writing here. [118] Runes that crystallize when stressed were dangerous on a platform that floated only because the wind agreed. [135] But Mira had come with a purpose her teacher would not approve: to transform scars into stories. [266] She lifted the crow quill and pressed its trimmed tip into the inkwell. [467] It drew out the silenced sound and set it on her skin in neat, readable lines. [483] When she released the breath, the rune was finished, and the crystallized ink settled into the scar as though it had always belonged there. [507] The burn was no longer a wound she had hidden; it was a sentence she could read aloud. [525] Thunder coughed to the west: the second storm was finding its voice. [537] Mira poured the second cup and bowed to the steam, as her grandmother had taught her. [553] She did not name the storm. [559] She drank slowly, feeling the new line in her palm thrum against the ceramic like a small, warm syllable. [578] The platform steadied under her, and the saturated hush of the evening turned from weight to blanket. [595] She had one story written; many scars remained, and many ceremonies. [606] When she stood, the crow quill went into her sleeve with the reverence of a teacher's name. [623] The next storm was already lowering its brow; she would ride the platform down before it broke.
