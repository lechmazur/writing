<story>The sky was wrong the morning Ivo climbed the icebound spire. [11] Clouds lay beneath him like spilled cream, but above, three suns burned where there should have been one, and the stars refused to set. [35] It was the signal every apprentice of wonder waited for: the hour when the world unlocked its own back door. [55] Ivo was a thief by trade and a chivalrous one by choice. [67] He had never robbed a crying man, never lifted from a widow, and always left a carved wooden token where a purse had been. [91] Tonight he meant to rob the Archivist of Hours, who lived at the spire's peak and collected minutes the way other men collected coins. [115] In Ivo's satchel clinked thirty-two chess pieces, each one hand-carved, each hollow, each hiding a secret folded small inside. [136] Some held confessions, some held maps, one held a lock of his dead sister's hair. [151] The plan was simple: swap the Archivist's chess set for his own, piece by piece, and in the swapping amend a single line of history. [176] The Archivist had recorded his sister as a traitor. [185] She had not been one. [190] That was the theft he had come to commit. [199] The spire's ice groaned under his boots as he climbed the outer stair. [212] Wind carved his cheeks; the wrong sky threw three shadows from his single body. [226] He felt the old sacredly playful thrill rise in him, the grin he always wore when trespassing on something holy. [246] His master, long dead, had taught him that wonder was a kind of prayer and mischief a kind of psalm. [266] To steal from a god, she had said, you must first be charmed by him. [281] At the top, the door stood open, which was either an invitation or a trap, and Ivo had learned to treat the difference as decorative. [306] Inside, the Archivist sat at a board of black and white glass, playing against no one. [322] He did not look up. [327] "You are late," the Archivist said, "by forty-one seconds." [336] Ivo drew out his pocket sundial, flipped open its lid, and watched the gnomon cast three shadows that all disagreed. [356] "The sky is wrong," he answered, "so time is negotiable." [366] The Archivist smiled without warmth and gestured at the board. [376] "Play, then," he said. [380] "Each piece you take, I answer a question." [388] "Each piece I take, you forfeit a year of your life." [399] Ivo sat, hiding his true purpose behind the mask of the game. [411] He did not need to win. [417] He needed only to touch every square. [424] Move by move, when the Archivist blinked or glanced at the impossible sky, Ivo slipped a carved piece from his satchel and exchanged it for the glass one on the board. [455] Each swap was quick, a magician's flourish dressed as a loss. [466] His pieces felt warm in his palm, confession-heavy, history-heavy, sister-heavy. [476] He played with cautious abandon, losing knights he loved, sacrificing bishops, taking pawns only when the Archivist's gaze wandered. [495] By the endgame, Ivo had given up nine years of his life and every piece on his side of the board had been replaced. [519] The Archivist laid down his king with a sigh that did not sound defeated. [533] "You did not come to win," he said. [541] "I came to amend," Ivo admitted. [547] He lifted the sundial lid and set it on the board beside a carved white queen. [563] "Open them all," he said. [568] The Archivist did, one hollow piece at a time, and as each secret unfolded the room grew colder and truer. [588] When he reached the queen that held the lock of hair, he stopped. [601] "She was my apprentice once," he said quietly. [609] "Then help me correct the record," Ivo said, "and I will give you back the years." [625] Outside, the three suns began, at last, to agree. [634] The Archivist took up a quill; the spire's ice sang a single clean note. [648] Ivo watched the ink move, feeling the old thrill of a locked door opening, and understood that he had not come merely to steal a name back. [675] He had come to embrace the unknown with open arms, as his master had taught him, and to learn again that wonder was the only worthy apprentice. [703] When he descended the spire, the clouds had closed. [712] The sky was right again. [717] In his pocket, the sundial lid clicked shut on a single, honest shadow. [730]</story>
