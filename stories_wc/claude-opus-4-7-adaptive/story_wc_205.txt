On the baroque asteroid of Vellemir, where gilded domes curled like frozen smoke and every balustrade wept moss, Ilya Vask walked with his coat pockets full of shards. [28] He had been, in another century, the voice of a revolution that failed so completely its very name had been scrubbed from the ledgers. [52] Now he was a defunct revolutionary, and poetry was all the insurrection left to him. [67] This was after time became visible, when the long storms unfurled their copper threads across the sky and every passing hour hung in the air like tinsel. [94] Scholars had found that during such storms, mirrors showed not reflections but the accumulated minutes of a room, layered and translucent, so that to look into glass was to drown in one's own prior selves. [129] Ilya had devised a simple remedy: covering mirrors during storms with black linen, the way old households once draped the dead. [150] The shards in his pocket were the reason he could not bear to see his past selves anymore. [168] They had come from an excavation on the asteroid's third tier, where the academy of Vellemir had unearthed a sealed chamber belonging to the Drevani, the civilization that had ruled before the failed revolt and vanished afterward. [205] In the chamber lay a crystal vase, broken into seventeen pieces, and every scholar on the rock had an opinion about what the fragments meant. [230] The archaeological dispute had split the academy into two camps. [240] One side argued the vase was ceremonial, proof that the Drevani had been a priestly people of solemn rites; the other insisted it was domestic, evidence of ordinary tenderness, a household object shattered by household grief. [276] Ilya, who had been invited as a consultant for his knowledge of the old languages, refused to sign either report. [296] He kept one crystal vase shard, smuggled it home in his lining, and set it beside his writing desk where the lamp caught its edge. [321] He was poetically inclined; he had always preferred metaphors to motions. [332] And that shard, when the storms came and time unfolded visibly in the windows, spoke to him in ways the committees would never accept as data. [358] He pressed his thumb to the edge until it bled, and in the bead of blood he saw the entire dead civilization not as artifact but as appetite. [386] So he set himself the task that would occupy what remained of his life: to reimagine an entire civilization from a single fracture of glass. [411] Not reconstruct; reimagine. [414] He would write the Drevani into being as poets write beloveds, giving them quarrels, lullabies, bad weather, better jokes than they ever had. [437] Each night he draped the long mirror in his study with the black linen, for he could not write while a younger Ilya sat half-visible in the glass reciting the manifestos that had killed his friends. [473] The shard, however, he left uncovered. [479] It held no reflection, only a narrow spike of lamplight that bent as he wrote, as though nodding. [497] By the second winter he had invented for the Drevani a grammar with two past tenses: one for what had been, and one for what ought to have been. [526] He wrote their harvest songs in the second tense only. [536] The academy, when his manuscript circulated, was appalled. [544] Both camps accused him of forgery, which he accepted as the truest praise his work could receive. [561] A young archivist came to his door one storm-black evening to return the pages. [575] She saw the covered mirrors, the uncovered shard, the old man with ink on his cuffs, and she did not give back the manuscript. [599] She asked, instead, in what tense he had written his own life. [611] Ilya looked at the shard, at the long burnished sorrow of its edge, and answered: "In the second. Always, now, in the second." [634] She stayed until morning, copying lines by candlelight, and when the storm finally lifted she folded the black linen from the mirrors and carried the manuscript out past the domes into the waking tiers of Vellemir. [670] Ilya did not watch her go; the mirror had uncovered itself of its own will, and there, layered in glass, stood every self he had ever been, applauding. [698] For the first time in a century he did not turn away. [710]
