Mira counted raindrops the way her father had once counted chips. [11] After the heart attack, he lived in the reading room of the old Whitlock Library, an indulgence the trustees allowed because he had donated every winning before the losing began. [41] Midsummer rain washed the tall windows, and the stacks smelled of wet clover carried in on someone's shoes.
