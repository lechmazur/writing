My father robbed coaches on the old toll road until a constable's pistol ended his career the winter I turned seven. [21] He left me little except a tin box of foreign paper money, bills he could not spend in any town that knew him. [44] Francs, rubles, a folded yuan note, a drachma so old it crumbled at the corners. [59] I kept the box for thirty years without opening it, the way some men keep questions. [75] I became, against every prediction, a founder. [82] Across my startup's rise and fall, five years almost to the day, I wore suits and learned the polished calm investors reward. [104] We built software that mapped property lines, a quiet thing, useful to surveyors and ranchers who marked their holdings through territorial markers: cairns, painted posts, the rusted wire of a grandfather's fence. [136] The work, I told myself, was the opposite of my father's. [147] He had crossed boundaries; I drew them. [154] At the peak we raised thirty million, hired forty people, and printed our logo on water bottles. [171] At the trough, eighteen months later, I laid off thirty-eight, then myself. [183] The board offered me a severance in three currencies, as if the joke of international failure required multiple punchlines. [202] I drove east into the dry country, chasing nothing in particular, the tin box rattling on the passenger seat. [221] Somewhere past the second dead town I saw the circus train. [232] It sat on a spur the rails no longer served, eight cars bleached to bone, elephants and clowns fading under thirty summers of sun. [256] I stopped because I had nowhere to be, and because the tracks, curling back toward the highway, seemed to answer a question I had carried since childhood: why do some paths circle back? [289] Inside the last car a man sat on an overturned crate, hands folded, perceptively calm, as if he had expected me at that hour. [313] He was old in the way ropes are old, still useful, still sound. [326] "You're the boy with the box," he said. [334] I did not ask how he knew. [341] In the dusky harmony of that car, where sunset came through the slats like a tuned chord, explanations felt rude. [361] He said he had known my father, had ridden with him once, then left the trade to tend animals for the circus that eventually died here. [387] "Your father tried to dissuade me from going straight," he said, smiling. [399] "He said honest work was slower robbery." [406] "He was wrong, but not by much." [413] I set the tin box on the crate between us. [423] The foreign bills lay inside like pressed leaves, each from a country my father had never seen. [440] "He took these off a banker," the old man said, lifting the drachma with a fingernail. [456] "Kept them because they looked like maps of places he'd never have to answer for." [471] I thought of the territorial markers I had turned into software, the way my product had let buyers trace every fence back to its first stake. [497] My father would have hated it, or loved it, or broken in and stolen the servers. [513] Either way, his path and mine had converged at a rusted circus train, and I understood, finally, what I had come to learn. [536] Paths circle back because we carry the center with us. [546] Wherever I went, I was the son of a man who took what did not belong to him, and I had spent five years building a polished calm around that fact, a suit over a scar. [582] The startup's rise had been inheritance in new clothes; its fall had been inheritance remembering itself. [598] The old man watched me understand. [604] "Keep the money," he said. [609] "Spend one bill a year, somewhere you have no business being." [620] "It's the only inheritance that teaches." [626] I walked out through weeds that had grown waist-high around the wheels, and the rails, rusted but unbroken, pointed back the way I had come. [651] I drove home with the tin box on the seat, quieter now, and in the morning I began again, more perceptively calm than before, knowing any road I built would loop. [682]
