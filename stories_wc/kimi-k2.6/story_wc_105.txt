[74] She heard colors faintly, a synesthesia that had worsened since the diving sickness took her middle ear. [89] To her, the forge's embers hummed a low bronze chord, while the bioluminescent algae tracing the shipwrecks screamed in a silver sheen she could almost taste behind her teeth. [118] This affliction made her invaluable and isolated, for she designed implants that did not merely amplify sound but preserved its emotional residue with terrible clarity. [141] Mira's hands moved while cells divided in the petri dishes warming near the ash-pan, the living substrates that would become the next generation of cortical receivers. [167] Her motivation remained singular and consuming: to find the sound of trust breaking, the precise frequency at which faith became fracture, so she could finally understand the moment her sister had whispered betrayal into a microphone seventeen years ago.
