The loop pedal priest knelt in the chapel of the dimensional anchor, his fingers hovering above the steel enclosure where stillness had begun to unravel. [25] Around him, silence no longer rested; it frayed at the edges like old canvas, and he could hear the dimensions grinding against one another in frequencies that his loop pedal alone could capture. [58] Father Caius had spent seventeen years learning that silence was not merely absence but architecture, and here at the anchor he decorated with silence the way other priests adorned altars with gold. [90] Now that careful construction was collapsing, thread by thread, into a hum that vibrated in his molars. [107] He pressed the footswitch, recording the decay into channel one, layering the silence so he could study its erosion through rhetorical devices made finally audible. [132] Before him, strapped to the obsidian plinth, Brother Soren convulsed with a rhythm that matched the grinding of worlds. [151] The young monk's possession was three-quarters complete; Caius had perhaps ten minutes before the entity wearing Soren's face would finish memorizing human physiology and seal the breach from the inside. [181] The Church assumed that possession was a spiritual problem, but Caius had come to illuminate hidden assumptions, chief among them that a soul could be stolen rather than merely translated into another syntax. [214] To plumb the exact depth of what crawled behind Soren's eyes, Caius needed more than exorcism; he needed to make the invisible argument visible. [238] From his cassock he withdrew an artist's palette with one color, a bruised violet he had mixed from the residue of failed exorcisms and the dust of collapsed portals. [267] The color was not paint but condensed contradiction, the visual frequency of muffled clarity, and he dipped his right thumb into it sparingly. [290] He smeared the violet across Soren's forehead in the shape of a question mark, because the entity expected crosses and commands, not an interrogation made flesh. [316] "What room does a shadow reserve for the light that casts it?" Caius asked, his voice dry and measured, each word a rhetorical device designed to fracture the invader's certainty. [346] The entity screamed, but the dimensional anchor absorbed the sound, warping it into the lo-fi register of a confession heard through a wall. [369] Caius stomped the pedal, looping the scream beneath a layer of chapel bells he had recorded the previous morning, creating a counterargument in pure tone. [394] Silence, he had always maintained, was the only honest rhetorical device; it carried no bias but revealed every structure built upon it. [416] He decorated with silence now, punching in a measure of dead air that made the grinding dimensions stumble, their syncopation broken by negation. [439] Soren's body arched, and the violet mark began to glow, illuminating the hidden assumption upon which the entity had based its invasion: that this dimension was a place rather than a sentence. [471] Caius saw the comprehension flicker through the alien presence like a subtitle half-submerged in heavy static. [487] He pressed another switch, sending the layered loops cascading backward through the chapel's speakers, a palindrome of muffled clarity that unraveled the stillness into active and final refusal. [515] The possession could not complete in a space where silence itself argued against occupation. [529] As the violet light consumed the last of the intrusion, Soren collapsed into true unconsciousness, and the dimensional anchor settled with a sigh that Caius's final loop caught and held. [559] He sat back on his heels in the hush, the artist's palette empty now, his argument concluded not with banishment but with quiet clarification. [583] Outside the thick steel walls, the worlds turned quietly, having heard, if not understood, the logic of a priest who preached only in the grammar of gaps. [610]
