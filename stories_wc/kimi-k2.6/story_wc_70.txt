[25] At the tower's base, in a brass booth that had once glowed with violet indicator lights, the candid toll keeper counted the evening's passage not in coins but in footsteps. [159] He had spent the week in miserable silence, afraid that his involuntarily truthful tongue would ruin the sacrifice the queen drone had made. [359] "Open it only when you know," he said, his voice tight with the strain of speaking only truth around something so fragile.
