Kael drifted through the mechanical rainforest, his boots crunching against fallen copper fronds that had long since ceased conducting sunlight. [20] He had been a stray philosopher ever since the Academy of Iron Logic cast him out for dreaming too loudly during lectures on fixed certainty. [45] That was before the sixth sense awakened in him, the one that heard thoughts as clearly as rainfall on hollow tin and saw probabilities where others saw only fact. [74] Now, whenever he slept, he translocated during sleep, waking beneath different synthetic canopies with no memory of travel except the oil stains on his coat. [99] Each dawn he rose and listened to the sound of wind through different trees to orient himself in the shifting labyrinth of brass and bark. [124] The bronze birches whispered in high, anxious frequencies that made his fillings sing, while the ancient gear-oaks groaned in deep, ponderous rhythms that settled his bones. [150] By comparing their voices like a musician tuning against reference tones, he mapped his nocturnal wanderings across the metal wilderness. [170] He was searching for something he could not quite name, driven by an insatiable need to rediscover the wild frontier of his mind. [193] The scholars had scrubbed that territory clean with their sterilized theorems, but out here, among probable miracles, thought grew tangled, feral, and gloriously unsafe. [217] Three mornings ago, he woke clutching a small gear between his palms, its teeth bitten with rust and its center radiating a warmth that felt like nostalgia. [244] It vibrated with a hum of insight, a resonant frequency that made his teeth ache with almost knowing and his vision flicker with equations half remembered. [270] He pressed it to his ear and heard not the clicking of machinery, but the echo of an idea he had forgotten he possessed before his expulsion. [297] The gear wanted to return somewhere sacred, and his translocations were pulling him closer with every restless sleep. [315] Kael began to follow the sound of wind through different trees as a deliberate method of divination rather than mere navigation. [336] Where the steel palms clattered fastest against their own riveted trunks, the hum of insight grew louder in his sternum and the air tasted of ozone and revelation. [364] Where the tin willows sighed through perforated leaves, the vibration dulled to a murmur, warning him away from dead ends of circular reasoning. [387] He let the forest conduct him like a symphony of compass needles, sleeping when tired, waking when the small gear grew hot against his bare skin. [413] On the seventh transit, he opened his eyes inside a hollow cathedral of roots where no photon should ever reach from the world above. [437] Yet the darkness was alive with motion, silver oil running in channels like bioluminescence, and he understood with sudden clarity that he had come to unravel the boundary between himself and the wilderness. [470] The small gear leaped from his open hand and floated toward a central spindle that turned without power, drawing threads of light from the surrounding dark. [496] As it meshed into place with a sound like a key entering a lock that had been waiting centuries, the mechanical rainforest shifted its posture, inhaling. [522] Every tree was a neuron he had abandoned during his academic imprisonment, every translocation a synapse firing in the dark of his subconscious. [545] The sixth sense had not been a gift from outside but a fracture in the wall of his own perception, letting his sleeping mind roam the architecture of his forgotten intellect. [576] The hum of insight swelled into a deafening chorus, and he realized the probable miracle standing before him: the frontier had never been lost, only overgrown by neglect. [604] He had simply been walking through it all along, mistaking his own vast, untamed cognition for an external wilderness of iron and chlorophyll. [627] The sound of wind through different trees resolved into a single, coherent voice, his own, speaking after years of enforced silence and self-doubt. [650] Kael the stray philosopher sat down among the threading roots, no longer translocating during sleep, for there was nowhere else he needed to go. [674] The small gear turned steadily in its cradle, and the mechanical rainforest breathed around him, wild and orderly and finally, irrevocably his. [696]
