[46] The tower rose from basalt like a warning finger, its beam sweeping across black water in meaningless intervals that matched no ship's need. [123] Sailors whispered that it devoured light and destiny alike, swallowing the future into its obsidian throat until both grew dark. [143] Elara believed otherwise; she sensed a latent radiance buried deep inside its crystalline fractures, a captive star waiting for the right mechanism to set it free. [272] She disassembled the wooden hemispheres to reveal a clockwork heart that ticked backward when spun, counting down instead of up. [292] Under the slow exhale of low tide, when the rocks revealed their gleaming bones and the brackish air grew still and heavy, she finally understood its language.
