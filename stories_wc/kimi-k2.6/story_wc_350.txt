[28] Once, those reverberations had coordinated planting across the plains, but now they only keened in disconnected monologues, isolating the silos further. [21] Maren was empathetically decisive by nature; she wept easily at children's rhymes, yet when she found the tarnished cuckoo clock weight buried in a crate of donated sheet music, she knew exactly what to do with it. [37] The weight had vanished from the tower's carillon thirty years ago, silencing the cuckoo that once signaled the midsummer summit between the grain crews.
