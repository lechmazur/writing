[28] Her hands, delicate yet calloused from years of crafting intricate scripts, trembled slightly as she adjusted the zinc-etched postal plate fragment before her. [49] The fragment, once part of a grand postal system now lost to time, bore faint etchings of a forgotten route. [72] Elara had discovered it while piecing together a shredded treasure map found under the floorboards of her studio. [105] The map, yellowed and brittle, hinted at a place where lost conversations went—a place she desperately sought. [138] The oasis that once sustained Verdania had dried, leaving the city reliant on artificial light and recycled air, and Elara felt the weight of silence pressing on her. [183] She believed the map, if restored, would lead her to the whispers of the past, the unspoken truths that had vanished with the oasis. [226]

[259] Her art wilted near dishonesty, the gilded leaves curling and browning at the slightest hint of deceit. [292] This had made her both revered and isolated, for few in Verdania dared to speak truthfully in a city where survival often demanded compromise. [399]
