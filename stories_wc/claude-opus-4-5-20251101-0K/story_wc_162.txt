[28] She was the corn maze architect's lost child, though "lost" implied someone was still searching. [42] After the butterfly effect—that singular moment when her father's prize labyrinth collapsed into chaos because a moth landed on the wrong stalk—no one came looking anymore. [66]

Wren had been seven when the maze swallowed her whole, and she emerged here, on floating glass, seventeen years older in body but forever suspended in that threshold moment. [93] Now she understood necessary distances: the space between who searches and who waits, between one world and whatever this fractured place claimed to be. [115]

[126] Wren pressed her palm against the wooden bench bolted to the crystal floor—salvage from some crashed vessel that had drifted too close to the prism's gravity. [252] CAVOK, CB, METAR, TAF—abbreviations that meant nothing to her but everything to whoever had charted courses through ordinary skies.
