[18] Mira knelt at its center, her fingers trembling over fragments of porcelain and lacquer—the remnants of masks she could no longer make whole. [40] She had been a restorer once, before the accident that shattered not just her hands but her certainty. [56] Now she accumulated silence instead, gathering it like dust in the hollow spaces where her craft used to live. [72]

[94] Its knots held more than rope—they held whispers, confessions, the final words of drowning men that the ocean had refused to release. [116] Mira understood now why the spirits remained restless. [125] Their secrets had never been heard. [130]
