[35] Her azure coat billowed against the wind, the color marking her as one of the lighthouse engineers who kept the northern shipping lanes navigable through the dark months. [60] Tonight, however, she had not come to tend the light. [70]

[132] Legend called it the Source Tone, and those who heard it were granted sonic absolution, their accumulated griefs and guilts dissolved into pure resonance. [156]

Vera had always been logically emotional, a trait her mother called her greatest strength and weakness intertwined. [171] She felt everything deeply but filtered each feeling through rigorous analysis, understanding that emotions were data points as valid as any measurement. [192] This duality now served her well as she processed the news that had arrived that morning: the Consortium of Sound Merchants had discovered the chapel's location and dispatched a recovery vessel. [264] The chapel's seal could only be opened by her key, but the Consortium would simply blast through the walls if denied entry. [286] They wanted to commodify the Source Tone, to record and sell it, ignorant that doing so would shatter its power into meaningless fragments. [308]
