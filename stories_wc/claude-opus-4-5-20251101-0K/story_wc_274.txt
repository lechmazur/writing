[17] Mira Chen stood alone in the Archive of Preserved Minds, her breath visible in the perpetual cold that kept three thousand frozen consciousnesses viable. [40] As cryogenic archivist, she had tended these crystalline chambers for eleven years, cataloging the dead-but-dreaming, never once questioning the official histories etched into their memorial plaques. [93] The dangerous liaison letter detailed everything: the selection process that chose which minds deserved preservation, the bribes from wealthy families, the dissidents quietly thawed and discarded when their stored memories proved inconvenient to the state. [159] It named the poet in Chamber 2,847, whose official cause of preservation failure was listed as equipment malfunction. [175]
