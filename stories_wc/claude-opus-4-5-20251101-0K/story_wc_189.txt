[23] Above her, the grotto's ceiling opened to the night sky, a ragged wound in the earth through which starlight fell like scattered salt. [45] She had spent forty years documenting the creatures that lived in transition—organisms that belonged neither to sea nor to land, thriving in the spaces between. [71]

[92] "You want to know if your daughter will survive the surgery," the chronicler said, not as accusation but as acknowledgment. [112] The man's jaw tightened. [116] He had come seeking something he could not name, following rumors of an old woman who could foresee what others could not. [137]

[155] It had belonged to her grandmother, who had belonged to her grandmother, passed down through generations of women who understood that time was not a river but a series of pools, each connected by narrow channels where everything changed. [195] The jade was warm against her palm, warmed by her body, by the bodies of all who had held it before. [216]

"I cannot tell you what will happen," she said, turning the cicada so it caught the starlight. [233] "But I can show you something." [240]
