The resonance chamber hummed with frequencies older than memory, its copper walls singing sympathetic harmonics to every note that Elias Wren coaxed from broken mechanisms. [28]

He was a music box repairman, though that title felt small for what he truly did. [42] His fingers knew the language of tiny gears and rusted springs, the whispered secrets of automata whose voices had fallen silent. [61] For forty years, he had listened to their confessions and restored their songs. [74]

[130] It had arrived three days ago, wrapped in moth-eaten velvet, accompanied by a note written in his grandmother's hand—impossible, since she had died two decades past. [157]

"Mend what isn't torn," the note had read. [165] "Tether the light before midnight claims it forever." [173]

[230] The light within it flickered, dimming each night as darkness crept closer to consuming it entirely. [244] To reclaim the light stolen by midnight—that was the task the universe had set before him. [259]

He understood now why it had come to him. [268] His gift had always been hearing the songs things wished to sing, even when they had forgotten the words. [285]

[302] Around it, he arranged seven music boxes, each representing a note in the scale his grandmother had taught him as a child. That was the lie. [473] That was not what this work was for. [480]
