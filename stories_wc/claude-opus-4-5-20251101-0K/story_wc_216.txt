[26] Inside, Vera worked as a borrowed-time timekeeper, one of the few remaining mortals who could sense temporal decay without instruments. [46] She had been given seventeen extra years after her scheduled death, purchased at great cost from the Temporal Preservation Council, and she spent each borrowed day cataloging anomalies in the damaged time capsule that occupied the silo's core. [103] Now a "7" meant profound grief, and "12" signified unexpected joy, and the old mathematical formulas that had governed time measurement became poetry. [126] Vera lived in this strange aftermath, where clocks displayed feelings instead of hours, and her job was to interpret what the damaged capsule had been trying to preserve before the change. [156]
