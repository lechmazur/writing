Maren kept the toll at the lightning collection tower, where copper veins channeled storm-fire into the city's reservoirs. [18] After the blackout, the tower stood dark, its glass capacitors empty as translucent honeycombs, drained of their amber glow. [37] She was born unable to lie—a condition the physicians called involuntary candor, though Maren considered it simply the truth sitting closer to her tongue than to most. [65] It made toll-keeping lonely work. [70] Travelers preferred pleasantries to precision, and Maren could offer only precision. [81] The blackout had lasted three days now. [88] Below the tower, the city's drone beehives had failed—the mechanical swarms that pollinated the rooftop gardens and produced synthetic nectar for the populace. [112] Without power, the drones died in their tech-hives by the millions. [123] The gardens would follow soon. [128] And then the people. [132] A figure climbed the tower's spiral stair as dusk settled, painting the sky in amber and bruised violet. [150] Nectar dusk, the old beekeepers called it—the hour when honey-scent hung heaviest in the cooling air, when the world tasted almost sweet. [173] "Toll," Maren said, though there was nowhere to go now that the city had stopped moving. [189] The woman at the gate was young, dusted with golden pollen that wasn't synthetic. [203] Real pollen. [205] Maren hadn't seen real pollen since she was a child, since before the drones replaced everything wild. [222] "I'm not here to cross," the woman said. [230] "I'm here to deliver." [234] She placed a matchbox on Maren's iron desk. [242] Maren picked it up. [246] Inside, scratched into the faded cardboard, a riddle: "What hive has no queen, what sweetness endures when the wire is cut?" [267] Maren's throat tightened. [270] She knew. [272] She'd always known, but the truth wouldn't stay buried. [281] "The lacquer box," she said, the words pulled from her like thread from a spindle. [296] "In my quarters." [299] "The red one with the gold cranes." [306] "I've been keeping it for someone, but I never knew who until right now." [320] The woman's eyes sharpened. [324] "You know what's inside?" [328] "I know it hums," Maren said, hating how honesty amplified everything. [339] "I know I've kept it warm against my chest on cold nights because it seemed alive." [355] "I know I've never opened it because I was afraid of spoiling the wonder." [369] "Wonder," the woman repeated, her voice strange. [376] "The committee sent me." [380] "They want to engineer it." [385] "Replicate it." [387] "Make it a system." [391] "Something efficient." [393] "They want to drain it," Maren said, her voice cracking. [403] "Like they drained the lightning." [408] "Like they drained the real bees and replaced them with drones." [419] "Everything becomes a collection tower to them—something to take from until nothing's left." [433] The woman's face changed. [437] Resignation, or recognition. [440] Perhaps both. [442] "I can't lie to you," Maren continued, the truth spilling like nectar dashed from a frame. [458] "I've kept that box for eleven years." [465] "Not because I was told to—because every morning I'd hold it and feel something impossibly gentle and alive, and I'd think: this is what routine should feel like." [494] "Not dead ritual." [497] "Wonder." [498] "Living wonder." [500] Silence pooled between them. [504] Outside, the capacitors caught the last of the fading light and glowed like amber before surrendering to dark. [522] "The riddle," the woman said quietly. [528] "The answer?" [530] "Memory," Maren said. [533] "The last organic queen." [537] "The last living hive." [541] "Kept in a lacquer box by a woman who couldn't lie about why she held onto it." [558] The woman reached for the matchbox, as if to take it back, but Maren's hand closed over it first. [577] "Tell them I died in the blackout," Maren whispered. [586] "Tell them the box was empty." [592] "Tell them whatever lie you need." [598] "I can," the woman said, and something passed between them—not a lie, but a kindness. [614] "I can do that." [618] Maren led her through the dark tower to the box. [628] The queen hummed inside, soft as a prayer, sweet as nectar dusk. [640]
