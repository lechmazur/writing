The wolf-boy's son had come to the lighthouse prison off the jagged reef chasing hush. [14] Not silence—hush was different, older, the shape truth took when it finally came to rest. [28] He'd spent all his years in the wake of his father's lie, the famous cry that had summoned a real wolf down upon the village. [53] Nobody had believed anything he said after that. [61] His truth was always haunted by his father's invention. [70] Now he needed to thread this labyrinth with his own story, before they found the bodies. [86]

The door was narrow, left ajar for decades, its wood marked with the scuffs of all who'd pushed through before him. [107] He entered through the scuffs, reading the marks like braille, like promises. [119] Each scuff a word. [123] Each word a confession. [127] This was how you threaded a labyrinth—you passed through what others had left, learned the passage by their marks. [147]

Inside, the lattice from the confessional leaned against the curving wall. [158] They'd brought it here when the old chapel on the mainland was stripped, repurposing sacred things for secular uses. [177] It was just wood now, just a grid meant to separate penitent from priest. [191] But it had sustained impossibilities. [196] All those years of whispered sin, all that weight of confession pressed through its grain—it should have cracked. [215] Instead it held. [218] It held, dense with the things people told it when they thought no one else could hear. [235]

He pressed his palm flat against the lattice. [243] The wood was warm. [247] Copperlight from the dying sun filled the stairwell with a strange, heavy repose—still, weighted, patient. [263] The light made everything seem like it was holding its breath. [274]

Below him, in the cells that the sea had slowly begun to claim, the bodies waited in their hush. [293] Two guards. [295] Five prisoners. [297] The bodies would be found in three days, when the supply boat finally came. [311] But he was here now, chasing the hush that came before discovery, when truth was still possible to shape. [330]

He took the lattice down from the wall. [338] It was lighter than it should have been, light with all it had held and released over the years. [357] He strapped it to his back and began to climb. [367]

The lighthouse prison spiraled above him, staircases tightening like the throat of a confession he'd refused to make his whole life. [388] At the third landing, the stairs simply ended. [396] A gap hovered in the air above a forty-foot drop to the flooded chambers. [410] Whoever had removed these stairs had not expected anyone to cross again. [422]

But the scuffs on the narrow doorframe told a different story. [433] He passed through them too, bracing his hands where others had braced, hauling himself over the gap. [450] A father had come this way, perhaps—another liar chasing his own hush, his own peace. [466]

He unstrapped the lattice and laid it across the gap. [476] The wood that had sustained impossibilities held him as he crossed. [487] He did not hover over the drop. [494] He walked on what others had confessed, and it bore his weight. [506]

At the top of the lighthouse, the copperlight gathered into one strong beam directed at the darkening sea. [524] He could see the reef below, the white water breaking, the dark shapes of what waited in the lower chambers. [544] The sea would take its time revealing them. [552]

He sat in the beam's warmth with the lattice across his knees. [564] The lighthouse kept its hush—no guards alive to tend it, no prisoners left to make noise. [581] The hush he'd been chasing was his to keep now. [591] He had threaded the labyrinth with his own story. [600] Not his father's lie, not the village's suspicion, not the cry that had started everything. [615] His story. [617]

Below, the bodies waited in their lockers and pools. [626] Above, the light turned slowly outward. [632] The lattice was warm with copper repose, and the wolf-boy's son began finally to whisper the truth to the only listener left—the patient, sustaining hush. [658]
