Vesper arranged lead type backward in the composing stick, her fingers knowing each letter by touch alone. [17] The topaz workshop glowed amber beneath constellations that were vanishing, one by one, each star fading like a word struck from some celestial proof. [114] It was not theft but transposition—empathy inverted, reassembled, made useful. [125] She felt their patterns, not their pain. [132]
