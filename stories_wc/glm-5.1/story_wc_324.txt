Mira traced hearts for a living — not the rhythm of blood, but the invisible threads binding one spirit to another. [18] She was a contact tracer for hearts, mapping who loved whom, who still called out across the years. [33] She was persistently curious, a trait that served her work well, though it occasionally led her into places she wasn't expected. [51]

[73] Mira had come because of a signal — something flickering through a decades-old hearing aid she'd found among her grandmother's effects. [92] The device shouldn't have been transmitting, yet when she held it to her ear, she caught pulses: faint, rhythmic, unmistakably human. [111]
