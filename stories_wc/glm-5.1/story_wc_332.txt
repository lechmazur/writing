Maren pressed her palm against the watercolor palette, its thirty-two dried wells still holding traces of pigment she had not mixed with water in seven years. [24] The clock above the Archival Desk read 3:47 AM, the hour when the whispering archipelago's islands seemed to murmur most urgently to each other across the dark channels. [48] As the odd-hour librarian of Starling Island, she tended the Liminal Collection: books that only held stories between midnight and dawn, their pages releasing salt-scented fog and fragile illuminations that would evaporate in daylight. [78]

Tonight, the registers showed an anomaly. [84] A dream had escaped the Circulation Vault. [91] Not just drifted loose—dreams did that constantly, which was why the library employed her—but purposely fled. [106] The Frey Manuscript, which contained Maren's own cipher of longing, lay open on its cradle, its coded marginalia rearranged into a message she had never wanted to read: GONE TO WATER. [132]

She clutched the palette against her chest, its surface warm despite the vault's chill, and descended to the photography lab. [149] Here, between the stacks and the sea, she practiced her craft: painting with light. [162] She set her tripod by the eastern window and loaded the modified plates—glass sheets treated with photochemical emulsion that could hold more than mere image. [183] Through sunrise photography, she had learned to vitalize what the waking world considered lost. [195]

The concept was simple, even if the execution demanded everything. [204] Dreams dissolved at first light, their luminous absence leaving only ache. [214] But if you could capture that moment when darkness yielded, when the horizon breathed its first pale secret—could freeze it in silver and halide—then the dream's essence could be preserved and read. [241]

She worked in the hush of improbable beginnings, that trembling space where the impossible consents to exist. [255] Three mornings ago, she had nearly captured the recurring vision of a girl diving from Carillon Rock, her small body caught between air and water, suspended forever in the arc of departure. [283] The girl was Irin. [287] The girl who, awake, would not speak her name or explain why she had stopped coming to the library, why she had stopped being Maren's daughter and become instead a creature of tide and wind. [317]

Now Irin's dream was running toward the water—really running, not merely drifting. [328] Through the window, Maren watched the horizon thicken into a gray that promised frequency. [340] She prepared her plates and placed the watercolor palette beside the camera, though she could not explain why. [355] The palette was her mother's. [360] It had no place in this work. [367] And yet her fingers kept returning to those crusty wells, as though the dried cerulean and madder might call back what the waking world had claimed. [390]

Light crept over the eastern island, and with it came the dream: Irin manifested against the brightening sky, translucent and falling, her hair streaming backward as she dove once more toward the sea. [418] Maren's hands moved—aperture, focus, timing—and the plate captured the luminous figure perfectly. [432] But something was wrong. [436] The image held the girl's outline but not her essence. [446] Maren felt the familiar vacancy opening in her sternum, the bright hollow of losing. [459]

Absently, she dipped her thumb into the palette's cerulean well and pressed it to the plate. [472] The color caught. [475] Worse—it spread, seeping through the silver halide and staining Irin's silhouette with the same blue that filled her daughter's remembered eyes. [494] Maren added madder to the curve of the diving figure's foot. [502] Naples yellow at the hair's streaming edge. [508] She painted with light and leftover pigment, with sunrise and memory, and the dream on the plate began to vitalize—not merely preserved but trembling, almost breathing, her daughter caught forever in the moment of choosing. [538]

Through the glass, the actual dream across the channel faltered in its flight. [548] It turned toward the window. [553] Irin's face, even translucent, wore an expression Maren recognized. [562] Not forgiveness. [564] Not yet. [567] But acknowledgment. [569] A cipher of longing decoded at last: I hear the color you're calling back to me. [581]

The sun broke fully over the whispering archipelago, and the dream dissolved, but its vitalized form remained forever painted on glass. [599] Maren held her daughter up to the light. [606]
