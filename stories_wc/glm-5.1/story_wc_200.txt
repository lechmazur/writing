In the hush of a hidden sunrise, when Echo Gorge held its breath between night and day, the lost soul collector began her descent. [24] She carried only two things that mattered: the misaligned harp strapped across her back, its strings warped from decades of canyon humidity, and the crocodile's tooth that hung at her throat—a relic from the first soul she'd ever freed. [64] Below, in the gorge's deepest crevasse, spectral confessions already drifted upward like morning fog. [78] Fragments of regret. [81] Whispers of things undone. [85] The dead always spoke in confession; it was the collector's task to listen. [98] She was modestly fearless about this work. [105] Not brave in the way of warriors who sought applause, but brave the way roots are brave—pushing quietly through stone because growing was all they knew how to do. [135] She settled on a flat rock near the gorge's narrow stream and unstrapped the harp. [150] Its frame was ancient gum wood, its strings refusing to hold true pitch. [163] When she plucked them, the notes came out distorted, bending strangely off-key, as if the music itself were limping toward something it could not quite reach. [189] This was the method. [193] To pursue lost souls not with commands or prayers, but through the distorted notes of the misaligned harp. [211] The wrongness in the sound was actually the point. [220] Lost souls recognized wrongness. [224] They had lived inside it. [229] The bent notes called to them like a familiar language, and one by one they materialized along the canyon walls—translucent figures caught mid-confession. [253] "I never told my daughter I was proud of her," murmured a woman in a faded housedress. [270] "I laughed at a funeral once and never forgave myself," said a man whose outline flickered like a dying bulb. [290] The collector listened. [293] She always listened. [296] That was the first half of her work. [304] The second half was harder. [309] She set down the harp and touched the crocodile's tooth at her throat. [322] The tooth was rough and yellowed, and it had been given to her by an old woman by a river long ago, with instructions that seemed absurd at the time: when you have heard everything they cannot forgive themselves for, make them laugh. [365] The dead took themselves so seriously. [371] Their confessions wrapped around them like burial cloths. [379] They held onto guilt as though letting go meant disappearing entirely. [390] The collector understood luminous ache—the way beauty and sorrow could share the same breath, the way light arriving through canyon walls at sunrise could make you homesick for a place you'd never been. [424] So she told them the absurd truth: that their confessions, which felt so heavy to them, were exactly what made them human enough to release. [449] "To laugh at a funeral," she said to the flickering man, "means joy persisted." [463] "Even there." [465] "Even then." [467] Something shifted in his translucent chest. [473] She turned to the woman. [478] "Your daughter learned you were proud from everyone except you." [488] "That's not failure." [491] "That's comedy." [493] "The cosmic kind." [496] The gorge erupted. [499] Not in ridicule, but in the startled laughter of souls who had finally heard their own stories told without reverence. [519] The sound bounced from wall to wall, Echo Gorge doing what it was named for, multiplying the laughter until it became indistinguishable from the sunrise spreading across the canyon rim. [549] Spectral bodies rippled. [552] Edges softened. [554] The laughter was integrating into the healing—each chuckle dissolving a shard of shame, each snort of recognition unknotting a confession that had been held too long. [581] One by one, the figures lifted. [587] Not ascending solemnly, the way paintings depicted, but tumbling upward like leaves caught in autumn wind, still chuckling at themselves. [607] The collector watched, her modestly fearless heart doing what it always did—holding steady, holding space, holding the quiet knowing that every soul she freed left a small luminous ache in her own chest where they had been. [645] She touched the crocodile's tooth again. [651] Tomorrow, there would be more confessions. [657] Tomorrow, she would pluck the misaligned strings again, and pursue them further into the gorge and further from themselves, until the laughter came. [680] The hidden sunrise finished its quiet work. [687] Echo Gorge exhaled. [690]
