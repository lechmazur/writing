[69] Her method was heat-mapping emotions, reading the residual warmth that human presence left on stone walls and sandy floors. [88] Every feeling had a temperature, a hue on her calibrated lenses. [100] Joy registered in amber waves. [105] Loneliness pooled in deep ultraviolet. [111] And something new had appeared in the deepest chamber—a phenomenon her instruments could only describe as factually mystical, reading as both impossible and undeniably present. [137]

This was the world after humans had learned to hibernate, an adaptation that had saved the species but created long seasons of darkness where only the mapmakers remained conscious. [167] Maren's motivation had always been to become the voice of the unknown, to speak for the spaces between mapped territories and documented feelings. [192] The cave network held miles of unmapped passages, and during hibernation season, things moved through the dark that no waking human had ever witnessed. [218]

[266] The fossil helped her transition between the mapped world of consensus reality and the unmapped territories she explored alone. [288] When her mind resisted what her instruments revealed, touching the fossil's pressed veins grounded her enough to record the impossible. [312]
