The alchemical orchard greenhouse had not known sunrise in seven centuries. [12] Its glass dome, fouled with luminescent soot, held eternal night within which trees of quicksilver bore fruit of condensed moonlight. [32] Sir Aldric stood motionless among them, a withdrawn knight whose oath had died with his order, whose hands had forgotten the grip of purpose. [53]

He had come seeking the orchard's deepest fruit: the Unmade Peach, said to hold possibility's raw shape before it hardened into what the world called real. [78]

Aldric knelt where the oldest quicksilver trunk split the greenhouse floor. [91] Roots of crystallized amber twisted through broken flagstones. [100] There, wedged between root and stone, lay an abandoned marionette theater program, its parchment foxed and curling. [118] He had found it three days prior and had not moved since. [129]

The program listed a performance titled "The Architect's Dream," with stage directions that were not directions at all. [149] They were factually mystical sequences, precise geometric instructions that corresponded to the greenhouse's growth patterns. [165] Petal fall angles. [169] Root bifurcation ratios. [173] The tree positions formed a choreography the program named "The Seventh Allegiance." [187]

Aldric traced the patterns with a finger, and the orchard responded. [198] A low hum rose through the glass. [205] Silver leaves shivered in sequence, propagating a wave that traveled from the distant walls inward, each tree's tremor triggering the next through forgotten patterns embedded in their alchemical composition. [235] The whole greenhouse was a puppet theater, its strings made of resonance. [248]

Reality shifted. [251]

The air tasted of copper and starlight. [258] The quicksilver trees bent as if pulled by unseen hands, their branches reaching toward the center where a shape flickered, the outline of a peach that existed only as potential, flickering between peach and leaf and light and nothing. [296]

His breath came fast. [300] Shivering bliss climbed his spine, the old knight's discipline warring with wonder, and wonder was winning. [318] The program had shown him: to reach the Unmade Peach, he must participate in the pattern. [335] Not merely observe but join the dance the orchard was always performing. [349]

Aldric gripped his left gauntlet. [355] With deliberate pressure, he pressed the clasp and felt it unsnap. [367] The steel fell away, revealing a scarred hand pale from years inside armor. [381] He laid the gauntlet on the ground. [389] Then the right. [393] Piece by piece, he unsnapped the buckles of his breastplate, each release sending tiny shocks through the humming air, each piece of armor placed carefully along the pattern the program had drawn. [429]

The abandoned program fluttered though no wind moved. [438] Its stage directions glowed faintly, and Aldric understood: the marionettes were not performed for an audience. [458] They performed the audience into being. [465] The knight had been the puppet all along, his each step through the greenhouse following forgotten patterns laid down before his birth. [488]

As reality shifted again, the stone floor softening, the dome's glass becoming sky, he spoke the words written beneath the final diagram on the program. [515] A murmured allegiance, not to king or order, but to possibility itself: [529]

"I yield my shape to what remains unshaped." [540]

The Unmade Peach solidified. [544] Silver fruit hung in silver air, trembling with every form it could become. [559] Aldric reached with bare hands, and the fruit dissolved at his touch, pouring through his fingers not as juice but as light that entered his skin and reorganized what it found there. [592]

His scars shone briefly. [596] His hands no longer ached. [603] The shivering bliss peaked, rang through the orchard like a struck bell, and Aldric laughed, a broken sound becoming whole. [625]

The program crumbled to dust. [631] Its patterns had served their purpose: his passage was complete. [644] He stood in endless night, unarmored, no longer withdrawn, holding within himself the shape of everything he might yet become. [668]
