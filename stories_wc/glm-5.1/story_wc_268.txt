Tolla was the smallest titan ever recorded, standing barely chest-high to the marble columns, yet she bore the old blood unmistakably. [18] Her eyes held starlight; her voice carried the resonant depth of mountain roots. [30] She was regally unpretentious, wearing simple linen rather than ceremonial armor, eating humble porridge from a wooden bowl in the ancient temple library lost to time. [56]

Centuries she had waited here, watching seasons blur, learning patience across the pause of shifting constellations. [70] The temple had been abandoned long before she arrived, its priests fled or dead, its archives forgotten by the world above. [92] But the books remained, thousands upon thousands of volumes, their pages thick with scholarship and, hidden within, something else. [112]

She found the first cryptic message twenty years into her exile. [122] A marginal notation in a treatise on celestial mechanics, written in an iron gall ink so pale it seemed more ghost than script. [146] "The crown sits on borrowed brows." [153] Each subsequent discovery revealed more, fragments tucked into theological debates, philosophical discourses, bestiaries. [168] Always the same hand, the same ink, always pointing toward a truth she had suspected since childhood. [184]

Someone had known. [187] Someone had foreseen the usurpation and left this trail of evidence, scattering it across disciplines where only the most devoted scholar would find them all. [215] The messages spoke of the false queen's origin, an enchantment woven with the rune of drift, allowing a mere court advisor to shift her appearance and memory into that of the true heir. [248] An illusion sustained by blood and deceit. [255]

Tolla's sister. [257] The real princess. [260] Dead these three hundred years, murdered on the night the pretender took her face. [274]

Tonight, the constellations completed their pause, froze in their momentary stasis, an occurrence that happened once each millennium. [292] The rune of drift would be at its weakest. [301] The illusion could be sundered. [306]

Tolla took the iron gall ink pellet from her pouch. [314] She had dissolved countless such pellets over the centuries, using the ink to annotate her own findings, to trace the pattern the hidden messages revealed. [340] But this last pellet she preserved whole. [349] It had come with the final message, tucked into the binding of an ancient atlas. [365] "When stars still, break the pellet upon the reflection of the false one, and speak the unmaking." [384]

She climbed the temple's highest tower. [390] From here, she could see the distant capital, its lights glittering against the dark. [406] In the reflecting pool at the tower's center, the stars hung motionless. [418] The constellation of the Heir stood directly overhead, her constellation, the one that marked true lineage. [435]

The pretender would be holding court now, drinking from the ceremonial cup, wearing the ceremonial crown. [452] Confident. [453] Secure in her stolen skin. [459]

Tolla crushed the pellet between her fingers and let the dark ink fall into the pool. [475]

The surface rippled with light, and for a moment she saw the throne room clearly, the false queen laughing, her courtiers bowing, the whole glittering facade built on murder and magic. [508]

Then Tolla spoke the words she had assembled across the centuries, the final meaning of every hidden message, every cryptic fragment. [533]

"Sunder." [534]

The word was not loud, but it carried the weight of patient centuries. [548] In the pool, she watched the pretender's face crack like porcelain, watched the truth beneath emerge, old, twisted, terrified. [570] The court screamed. [573] The mask fell away entirely. [579]

The false queen collapsed. [583] It was a graceful motion, strangely dignified, as though she had always known this moment would come. [603] The crown rolled free, spun across the floor, and stopped. [615]

Tolla watched the distant city dissolve into chaos, then quiet. [627] The library behind her had begun its own slow collapse, shelves leaning, pages drifting like leaves. [645] The temple had held together only long enough to serve its purpose. [659]

She was small. [662] She was tired. [666] She was done waiting. [672]

Tolla descended the stairs and began the long walk home. [685] The ancient library crumbled behind her, stone sighing into dust, its columns bowing like penitents. [704] She did not look back. [709] The fragmentary messages had served their purpose, and now the books could return to the earth from which their parchment came. [734] The rune of drift faded from the night sky as the constellations resumed their slow celestial dance. [752] A new age began with a diminished titan's quiet footsteps, leaving behind the graceful collapse of everything that had sustained her through the long wait. [778]
