Maren had always been willingly perplexed by the cards, choosing uncertainty over the cold comfort of fixed futures. [18] Her fingers, stained black as a moonless sky from decades of onyx-ink readings, trembled now above the dolls' tea set saucer -- a miniature porcelain thing, its crack running like a river tributary through painted roses. [52] In the midnight glade above the clouds, where pale birches grew from cloud-stuff and the air tasted of stars, everything waited. [73]

Hours after the birth, she had climbed here, leaving her daughter sleeping in a hospital bassinet far below. [91] She had kissed the baby's forehead and whispered a question: where does the time we lose accumulate? [108] She needed to know. [112] Her own mother had vanished when Maren was seven -- not died, but vanished, the way a word vanishes from your tongue when you need it most. [139] One birthday she was there, tracing Maren's small hand onto the lace tablecloth, a family custom that captured each year's growth in slow-spreading ink. [162] The next morning, she was gone. [168]

So Maren read the cards, but they always showed the same spread: the Cracked Cup, the Steeple, the Mother Walking Away into Cloud. [190] Tonight, after holding her newborn and feeling time already slipping through her grip like water, she had come to unfasten the mystery once and for all. [217]

The glade existed between moments -- she had found it years ago in her readings, a place where lost time pooled like rainwater in leaf-cups. [242] Moonlight fell through the birches, silver as old coins, and the air breathed milk and remorse: cloudbound repentance rising like fog from all things left undone. [268]

She arranged seven cards around the cracked saucer, pinning each corner with a smooth stone so the wind wouldn't scatter them. [290] Then she unfastened the leather cord from around her wrist -- the one her mother had tied there when Maren was born, the one that had never been removed. [319] It fell away like a held breath released. [327]

"I'm here," she whispered to the clouds below, the glade above, the time between. [341] "Tell me where it gathers." [346]

The cards began to move -- not from wind, but from something beneath them, something rising through the porcelain's wound. [366] The crack in the saucer widened, and through it came not breaking but blooming: light the color of old birthday tablecloths, cream and soft, stained with traces of hands spanning decades. [397] Her mother's hands. [400] Her grandmother's. [402] Her own, from every birthday she'd ever had, each outline a different size, a different shape, a record of becoming. [422] They rose from the fissure and hung in the glade's still air like an archive of growth. [440]

She understood then, with choking beauty pressing hard against her ribs, that lost time didn't accumulate in some distant hoard. [460] It gathered here, in the rituals themselves -- in the tracing, the tying, the careful naming of each finger as ink marked cloth. [483] Every year her mother had pressed Maren's hand to linen and drawn round each finger with slow, deliberate care, she'd been marking time exactly so it couldn't be lost. [512] The tracings were not records of what had passed. [521] They were the destination. [525]

And the crack in the saucer? [532] It had appeared the night her mother disappeared -- Maren had found it split on the kitchen table, the tiny tea set she'd played with as a child, as if even small things couldn't hold that kind of leaving without breaking. [570] But the crack hadn't destroyed the saucer. [577] It had opened it -- an aperture for what needed to pour through. [589]

Maren reached into the light and found her mother's last hand-tracing, the one from that seventh birthday, the final proof that love had been there, had been real, had been enough. [619] She pressed the traced outline to her chest and for one suspended moment felt the touch again: palm warm against her cheek, lips brushing her hair, the voice she'd been seeking in cards and ink saying simply, Keep tracing. [655] Keep holding. [657] Don't let time be a thief. [663]

When she descended from the glade, dawn was breaking in ribbons of gold along the horizon. [679] In the hospital room, her daughter stirred, small fists rising toward the light. [692] Maren unwrapped the perfect hand, pressed it flat to the clean white blanket, and with the onyx ink that still stained her fingers -- ink that had always known more than she allowed herself to see -- traced each finger, each tiny crease, each unfolding line. [732] A new outline on a new cloth. [739] A new year begun. [743]

Time didn't accumulate in some far place. [750] It gathered wherever love paused to notice it. [758] She was willingly perplexed no longer. [764]
