[67] She knew every corridor, every passage where sound bent and folded back on itself. [80] She knew where echoes lived and where they went to die. [90]

[137] The harmonic caves, which had once resonated with the music of water and wind, now rang with a wailing static that burrowed into Rillon's skull. [160] She retreated to the deep chambers where she had always worked, where the oldest ice grew in translucent sheets along the walls. [181] Here, the temperature held at the threshold of stillness. [190] Here, before the storm, she had always been able to think. [201]

[222] She could not remember what quiet felt like, though she had spent her whole life in pursuit of it. [240] For Rillon had always been searching for the quiet in the storm, not just in the caves, but in herself.
