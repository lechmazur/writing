The cursed orchard clung to the cliffside like something that had been planted in spite and maintained in exhaustion. [18] Vem had not asked for this duty. [25] She had inherited it from her grandmother along with the creeping rot in her left knee and a disposition that made strangers regret asking directions. [49] But travelers kept coming, kept getting lost in the labyrinth of switchback paths and dead-end gullies that wound between these cliffs, and they kept needing saving whether Vem wanted the trouble or not. [82]

She cracked open another small nut from the twisted trees. [92] Inside, the kernel bore veins and whorls that no natural seed should hold—a forgotten blueprint pressed into the flesh of the nut itself. [115] Her grandmother had called them path-seeds. [121] The trees drew the labyrinth's shape from the stone and recorded it in their fruit, year after year, generation after generation. [142] Most people had forgotten what they were for. [150] Vem wished she could. [154]

Three travelers had stumbled past her gate at noon, young and laughing, heads full of wherever they thought they were going. [174] She had watched them turn into the lower maze and said nothing, because they had not asked. [191] By late afternoon, when their voices began to carry that particular thin edge of fear, she had started her rounds. [211]

The mechanical birds patrolled above, brass wings catching the dying light. [221] They were not malevolent, exactly, but their copper talons could sever rope and their whirring presence drove people deeper into the maze in panic. [245] Vem had learned to read their patterns, to know which paths they favored and which they abandoned as dusk settled over the cliffs. [267] She had also learned to hate the sound of them. [276]

Her method was simple and strange. [282] She could not touch the fog that rose from the cliffs each evening—the cold breath of the frost to come, she called it. [305] Contact with bare skin would leave the marks unreadable to anyone but her. [318] Instead, she held her cupped palm above the fog and let the shape in the nut's kernel transmit itself downward. [338] The impression transferred without touch, leaving luminous tracings in the vapor that pointed toward safer passages. [354] She worked offhandedly exact, her hand steady at precisely the height her grandmother had taught her, the pacing of her steps matched precisely to the intervals between waystones she could not see. [384] No effort, no thought. [388] Just the mechanical execution of duty she resented. [396]

She assembled the path in sections—five impressions here, seven there, a long curve marking along one goat trail that would lead them past the nest of the most territorial bird. [426] The frost would form tonight, the temperature already dropping in a way her bad knee felt hours before any thermometer confirmed it. [448] Once the frost settled, the fog impressions would freeze into meaningless glitter, and the travelers would be trapped until spring, if they survived that long. [473]

Their voices echoed closer now, one of them crying, another calling out names that received no answer. [489] Vem scowled and moved faster, not out of concern but out of impatience. [501] This was the graceless part of the work: no grand rescue, no dramatic descent into the maze with a torch raised high. [522] Just an old woman in a cursed orchard, pressing patterns into fog because no one else remembered how, and no one else could be bothered to learn. [548]

The trio appeared at the base of the cliff path, faces pale and one limping along with the help of the other two. [569] The luminous lines glowed before them, plainly visible in the thickening fog, tracing a route that wound upward through the rocks and past the orchard gate. [595] They hesitated, then followed. [599]

Vem turned back toward her gate. [605] The mechanical birds swept overhead, indifferent to her, intent on patrolling boundaries they had been built to maintain. [623] By morning the frost would come and the path-seeds would be scattered for the next batch of fools. [640] She would be ready, and she would resent every moment of it. [652]
