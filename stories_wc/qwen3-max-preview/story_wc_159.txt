Each step echoed on the pearlescent floor, a rhythm older than the clouds beneath her feet, older than the museum of extinct feelings where she now walked—not as visitor, but as last curator. [42]
Her duty: to preserve the sound of forgotten names, not by inscription or chant, but by the echo of a long-forgotten song that only the wind remembered. [67]

She carried no lantern, no torch; she lit her path by listening, by letting memory glow where silence pooled darkest. [112]

It had belonged to her grandmother, who whispered that the crack wasn’t damage, but an opening—a mouth for ghosts to breathe through. [172]
The caretaker believed her. [175]

There, beneath a skylight woven from spider-silk and starlight, she’d suspend the cracked chime and wait. [220]
Not for wind—that came reliably, sighing through the library’s porous architecture—but for resonance. [236]

So she tended the emptiness like a garden. [444]
