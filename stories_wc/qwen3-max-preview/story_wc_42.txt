It whirled madly even in zero gravity, defying entropy, a blur of silver against the obsidian void beyond the glass dome. [38]

The paradox was her compass: changelessly constant, like the spinning hand that never slowed, never wavered, yet never marked a single hour. [145]  
It was the universe’s own metronome, ticking through dimensions she could barely perceive, its motion neither progress nor decay, just pure, luminous ache. [171]

She had spent years repairing cameras not to document the world, but to reframe it—to show how light bends around grief, how silence echoes louder than screams. [265]  
Now, in this silver tower above the boiling clouds, she would attempt her final repair: not of glass or circuitry, but of time’s own architecture. [292]
