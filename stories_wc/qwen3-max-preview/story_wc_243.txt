Her motivation was simple, sacred: to preserve the weight of small moments — not the grand declarations, but the hush between heartbeats, the way tea steam curled around a confession, the tremble before a kiss. [99]  
That was why she carried walnut ink — not for permanence, but for depth; its sepia tones held sorrow like a vessel, and joy like a secret. [122]
