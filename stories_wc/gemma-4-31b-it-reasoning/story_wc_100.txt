[54] The library was a cathedral of sodden parchment and coral-crusted arches, a place where time moved like syrup. [71] The mysterious package arrived after a century of waiting, delivered by a bioluminescent courier who vanished into the dark before Silas could offer a single word of thanks. [98] Inside the lead-lined crate sat a broken lighthouse lens, a shattered eye of obsidian and diamond that seemed to pulse with a forgotten frequency. [121]

[139] He longed to give rest a permanent address, a sanctuary where the fragmented spirits of the deep could stop their endless, circling wanderings. [161] For eons, the city had been besieged by psychic distress signals—shrieks of longing and fragmented, watery pleas that rippled through the trenches like sonic scars. [185] These signals hunted the quiet, creating a chased stillness that kept Silas in a state of perpetual, jittery alertness. [203] The silence was never true; it was always running from the noise of the lost. [217]

[240] This quiet obsession drove him to believe that the broken lens could be revitalized into something more than a ruin. [259] He did not want to guide ships to shore; he wanted to guide the lost to their final chapter. [277]
