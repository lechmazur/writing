[240] He understood that to wake this sound, he needed to understand the specific geometry of that pain. [258] From his leather satchel, Elian retrieved a porcelain insulator, a ribbed, white relic from an age of electricity. [276] It was a hard, inorganic thing, yet it possessed a purity of resonance that the surrounding organic decay lacked. [326] He did not simply observe the sculpture's trauma; he mirrored it in his own body, curving his spine to match the cedar's lean, slowing his heart to match the slow decay of the grove. [374] The atmosphere remained intensely quiet, the kind of silence that rings in the ears like a distant bell. [458] The sound was not born of air, but of the release of a thousand years of structural agony. [476] He strummed again, faster this time, finding the harmonic nodes where the porcelain and the petrified wood converged. [547] Elian wept, not from sorrow, but from the sheer physical weight of the sound.
