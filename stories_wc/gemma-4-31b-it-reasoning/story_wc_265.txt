[84] This erratic displacement was not a curse, but a symptom of the era after the sixth sense awakens. [152] He believed that by navigating the forest's chaos, he could find the point where geometry failed and intuition took over. [247] It was carved from a strange, petrified wood that mimicked the shape of a cog. [262] Elias treated this object as a talisman, a physical anchor in a world of shifting coordinates. [480] He felt the boundaries of his identity dissolve, the structured thoughts of the philosopher giving way to a primal, untamed awareness. [501] The process of unraveling was both terrifying and liberating. [510] He saw the Mechanical Rainforest not as a prison of metal, but as a mirror of the structured mind. [529] By breaking the gear's cycle, he was breaking the cycle of his own conditioned perceptions. [592] He no longer needed to jump through space because he had finally arrived at the center of himself. [610] The gear fused with the machine, and the machine fused with his consciousness. [623] He stood still, a bridge between the organic and the synthetic. [634] Elias breathed in the scent of ozone and ancient loam, knowing that the miracle was no longer probable, but present.
