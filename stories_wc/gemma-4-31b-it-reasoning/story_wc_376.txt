[106] Despite his prestige, Elian was haunted by a singular quest: he wanted to find where lost conversations go. [229] He possessed a zinc-etched postal plate fragment, a relic from the old world's communication system. [244] To accompany him, he carried a pale morning-glory in a leaden pot. [256] This was no ordinary plant; it was a genetic anomaly that wilts near dishonesty. [556] The words spoke of a love that had been too heavy to voice, an apology for a departure that had never been explained. [579] As Elian read, he realized that the city had not died because the water vanished, but because the people had forgotten how to be true to one another. [607] He sat amidst the echoes for hours, understanding that the greatest treasure wasn't the map, but the courage to be seen. [628] He left the Archive not with gold, but with a renewed commitment to honesty. [642] He returned to his studio, not to illuminate scrolls for the few, but to write letters of candor to the many.
