[17] Below, the world was a churning grey sea, swallowing the contested lands between the Lowland Confederacy and the Sunken Citadel. [37] This crumbling perch, The Watcher’s Spire, was their designated neutral territory, a place too high and too exposed for treachery. [58] A furrowed hush settled over the scene, broken only by the sigh of the wind through eroded crenellations. [75]

In his gloved hand, Elias worried a cold piece of brass: an antique drawer pull shaped like a sleeping lion. [95] It was his anchor, a relic from a negotiation years ago that had collapsed into tragedy, its weight a constant reminder of the cost of a single miscalculation. [123] He was a prisoner exchange negotiator, a dealer in fragile lives, and his motivation was simple, if severe: to recycle mistakes into wisdom. [146]

The protocol for this exchange was archaic, deliberately so, designed by paranoid minds who trusted the heavens more than each other. [167] Every step had been governed by synchronizing through moon phases, a celestial timetable immune to sabotage. [183] The initial offer on the new moon, the counter during the first quarter, and now, the final transfer under the pale gaze of the waxing gibbous. [208]

[229] She held a heavy chain leading to a hooded figure who shuffled at her side. [242] Beside Elias, his own charge, a boy from Kaelen's faction, shivered against the cold. [256]

They stood for a long moment in that space between apology and forgiveness, a chasm far wider than the parapet itself. [276] The Confederacy had issued a formal regret for the border skirmish, but the Citadel's wounds were too fresh for it to be anything but ink on paper. [302] This exchange was the only tangible thing, the only possible step forward. [313]

[349] "The moon is at its zenith," he said, his voice low, careful not to let the wind snatch it away. [368]

[396] Kaelen guided her prisoner forward as Elias did the same, the two captives meeting in the middle of the narrow walkway. [459] It was the key, a unique object from a neutral, bygone era, its authenticity guaranteed by its tragic and well-documented history—his history. [482] A soft click echoed in the damp air.
