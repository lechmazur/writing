[67] Elara’s motivation was a constant ache in her chest: to translate distance into closeness, to somehow bridge the chasm between her desolate present and a past she only knew from stories. [102]

[157] For weeks, she had tinkered with the hand-cranked player she'd salvaged, and today, its horn finally coughed to life. [291] She remembered a fragment of a story, a whisper about 'groove-writing,' a method for hiding messages not in the audio, but within the very shape of the carved tracks. [323] The cylinder wasn't a recording; it was a riddle. [332]

[365] It took days, her eyes straining in the flickering lamplight, Barrow’s quiet presence her only anchor. [726] The riddle wasn't about finding what was lost, but about nurturing what was left.
