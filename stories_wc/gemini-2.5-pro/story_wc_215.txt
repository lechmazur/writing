Elias had not spoken in a decade, yet the silence of the scorched wasteland was now louder than any city he had ever fled. [22] It was the silence of ghosts, shimmering in the heat haze where the apiaries once thrived. [36] He, an astonished recluse content in his desolation, found his world undone not by a sound, but by the sight of them. [57] They had flickered into existence a year ago, pale afterimages of a kinder time, and his quietude had curdled into perpetual shock. [78]

[94] She drifted through the skeletal remains of their abandoned beekeeping oasis, her form translucent against the cracked earth. [112] Today, her spectral drift had a purpose that pulled at the edges of his calm. [127] His gaze followed her, a tranquilly perceptive study of her sorrowful mime. [292] Sunlight, harsh and white, fractured through the colored panes, casting splintered blues and reds across his weathered hands. [310] One panel depicted a queen bee, another a field of clover. [320] It was their whole story contained in a fragile vessel. [330] He carried it to the stone bench where they used to watch the sunset burnish the sky. [347] Lyra’s ghost settled across from him, her form wavering, her eyes fixed on the box. [363]
