Elias stood on the cobalt terrace at dusk, the world bleeding from blue to bruised purple. [16] A sieved hush had settled over his life years ago, the kind of quiet that remains after a scream has been stolen from the air. [39] In his palm, cool against his skin, lay a single, unmoving watch hand. [52] It was all that was left of Anya. [60]

He was a black market genealogist, a trafficker in forbidden bloodlines and buried histories. [75] His particular, damning talent was that he could also manufacture false memories, weaving comfortable lies to shield clients from the barbs of their own ancestry. [99] This attribute, once a source of pride, now felt like a curse etched into his soul. [115] His motivation was singular and sharp: to discover the weight of a withheld truth that had festered under the weight of unspoken words for five long years. [141] He needed to quell the ghost that lived in the silence. [152]

His work for Anya had been simple, at first: trace her lineage back before the Archives erased it. [169] He had found the truth—that she was heir to a dissident line—and the danger that came with it. [186] He'd offered her the escape hatch, a fabricated past, a new set of memories to overwrite the perilous reality. [205] She had refused. [208] Two weeks later, she was gone, and this sieved hush was all he received in her place. [226]

He left the terrace and made his way down the crumbling steps to the coast. [240] His method was strange, a penance born of desperation: by sifting through driftwood carvings along a forgotten shore, he hoped to find some echo of her. [266] Anya had loved this place, her hands always busy shaping the sea-cast wood into twisted, silent figures. [284] He believed a part of her, a final thought or feeling, might be imprinted on one of them. [302]

The waves whispered against the shingle, a sound like sandpaper on bone. [314] He moved slowly, his fingers tracing the contours of waterlogged timber, each piece a potential vessel for the past. [333] He wasn't just searching for an object; he was panning for a feeling, a resonance, the psychic signature of her last moments. [353] Most pieces were inert, smoothed into anonymity by the tide. [363] But his guilt-ridden heart insisted, pushing him onward through the twilight. [374] It was the only way to quell the acidic burn of his tempered regret. [388]

He almost missed it. [392] It was a small piece of oak, darker than the rest, nestled between two stones. [407] There was no intricate carving, only a single, deep gouge down its center, as if made by a nail dragged with immense pressure. [428] As his thumb settled into the groove, the world tilted. [438]

It wasn't a memory that flooded him, but a terrifying, structured absence. [450] He felt the hole where Anya's life should have been, a void not created by death, but by a chillingly precise excision. [470] This was the withheld truth, heavier than he could ever have imagined. [481] Her enemies hadn't just killed her; they had used a power that manufactures false memories on a terrifying scale, plucking her from the minds of everyone who had ever known her. [511] They had scrubbed her from the world's recollection, leaving behind only this hollow, sieved hush. [527] His 'unspoken words' with her family weren't them hiding details; it was them not remembering her at all. [546]

He staggered back to the cobalt terrace, the piece of wood now clutched in his hand alongside the watch hand. [565] The silence was no longer a mystery; it was a weapon, and it had been used to unmake a person entirely. [585] His guilt was for leading her to this fate, but the horror was in its totality. [600]

He looked from the driftwood to the small metal hand, the only two artifacts of a ghost. [617] He could not bring her back. [623] But he possessed the same terrible attribute that had erased her. [634] He could manufacture false memories, but he could also restore true ones. [646] His work would no longer be about unearthing the past for a fee, but about painstakingly rebuilding it. [663] He would quell the silence not by forgetting, but by forcing the world to remember. [677] Starting with her family, he would begin to sift the truth back into the world, one carefully seeded memory at a time.
