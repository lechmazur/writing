During the lull of distant thunder, when the next peal hung like a blade that had not yet fallen, Ilyan returned to Edrig, the time-frozen village that had exiled him. [30]
He had once been its chess tutor, schooling bakers' sons and council heirs to see sacrifice before glory, until the mayor's daughter misread his lesson and opened the sealed tower. [60]
From that tower unfolded the platinum labyrinth, a shining engine of walls no larger than a crown, and from its heart spread the woven hush. [85]
Every rooster's crow, kettle song, sneeze, argument, and footstep had been caught in silver stillness, leaving Edrig beautiful and unbearable. [105]
For twelve years Ilyan wandered the borderlands teaching bandits endgames for bread, carrying a pocket set whose pieces he touched whenever guilt rose. [128]
He had come to forgive himself for past mistakes, if Edrig would live. [141]

Now she stood in the square at seventeen forever, arm raised toward the first bright wall of the labyrinth as if welcoming a star. [284]
