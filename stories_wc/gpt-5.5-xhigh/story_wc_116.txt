Beneath Aurel, under cisterns cracked like old teeth, there endured an overgrown library hidden in catacombs of lost knowledge. [19]
Roots threaded its ceilings, pale fish swam its flooded aisles, and mushrooms lifted dust from shelves as softly as monks turning pages. [41]
Its chronicle keeper, Senn, kept no throne list, no victories, no accusations, only dates of rain, rot, hatchings, and human footsteps. [62]
He had chosen the work to witness change without judgment, because judgment, he believed, was how dead cities kept demanding obedience. [83]
Across the drift of untold seasons, his ink grew thin, his hair grew fern white, and the library learned his slow step. [105]
Senn, as the keepers said, grips reality loosely, not from madness, but from a tenderness that let impossible rooms pass without alarm. [127]
When a catalog of extinct birds began laying warm blue eggs, he simply wrote: first chick opened one eye at moonrise. [148]
When a staircase forgot up and down, he marked the day and set a lantern at each end. [166]
In the eastern stacks, behind atlases of countries that never forgave their borders, Senn kept a well-worn origami swan. [185]
It had been folded by the first keeper from a treaty, then refolded by every keeper after each flood, fire, or forgetting. [207]
On ordinary nights it looked like paper, but in emergencies its cryptic folds opened into dark instructions for escape. [226]
The instructions always led, by ways no shovel could copy, to the obsidian refuge along the river. [243]
That refuge was a black glass chamber older than Aurel, half cave and half listening ear, where water spoke in pebble clicks. [265]
Within it lived what the keepers called regenerative silence, a hush so deep that broken fibers remembered how to braid themselves. [286]
Senn had entered it only once, as an apprentice, and had come out hearing moss grow under stone. [304]
For many years, the library needed no refuge. [312]
Its crumbling peace held: pillars sighed, vellum molted, the river rose and withdrew with the patience of an old animal. [332]
Then, after a season without stars, people from above found the burial stair. [345]
They came carrying hooks, lamps, and royal permits mildewed beyond authority, hungry for weapons rumored in the lost knowledge. [364]
Their leader was Mara, Senn's last apprentice, who had left because she wanted histories to condemn the cruel and absolve the harmed. [386]
Now she looked older than her leaving and more tired than her anger. [399]
Senn welcomed them with tea brewed from clean condensation and recorded: Mara returns with twenty searchers and no songs. [418]
She read the line over his shoulder and snapped the book shut. [430]
"You still refuse to say whether a thing is right." [440]
Senn touched the swan in his sleeve, feeling one crease tremble like a trapped vein. [455]
"Rightness is a blade," he said, "and I was asked to keep a window." [469]
Mara ordered her searchers to pry open the sealed index of catastrophes. [481]
The moment iron touched the lock, the catacombs answered with a groan, and aisles folded inward like lungs taking fright. [501]
Books burst their bindings; maps peeled into flocks; the river, suddenly silver with stirred memory, climbed the steps toward them. [521]
The searchers shouted for blame, but blame could not hold a ceiling. [533]
Senn unfolded the origami swan until its wings became corridors of script, each crease naming a motion too small for muscles alone. [555]
To save the living and the records together, he would have to burrow via cryptic folds in a well-worn origami swan. [576]
He pinched the left wing, bowed his head through a paper shadow, and the floor softened into layered dark. [595]
Mara seized his belt before doubt could seize her, and the searchers followed, crawling under shelves through passages no wider than regrets. [617]
They emerged inside the obsidian refuge as the river hammered outside, blind but musical. [631]
Silence gathered around them, not absence, but a patient pressure, knitting cut hands, flattening warped pages, cooling fear without erasing it. [652]
In that regenerative silence, Mara began to weep because no verdict arrived, only the sound of water making room for stone. [673]
When the flood sank, they returned through the swan's last crease to a library changed beyond repair. [690]

He opened his chronicle to a clean page, while the searchers waited for anger, praise, or law. [733]
Senn wrote only what had happened: Index lost, reeds arrived, Mara stayed to count new shoots. [749]
Mara read it twice, then sat beside him, and for the first time she did not close the book. [768]
Above them, Aurel continued to fall. [774]
