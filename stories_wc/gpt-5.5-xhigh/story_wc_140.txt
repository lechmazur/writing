At low tide, the port city smelled of coins, kelp, and diesel, and the stacked containers rose like a metal cliff around the harbor. [24]
By high tide, water filled the alleys between them, turning the towering labyrinth of shipping containers into a floating map nobody had drawn twice. [48]
I lived inside that map because the city had misplaced me among its dead. [62]

No correction came, so I carried no name, only a dock pass with the photograph rubbed pale by salt. [101]
It suited me, almost, because after being declared gone, my eyes began to filter reality differently. [117]

I built it by collecting fragments of overheard stories across the rhythm of tides. [206]
