Mara Venn was the labyrinth maintenance worker for the tower gardens, which meant she swept sand from false turns, replaced bent hinges in dead ends, and apologized to visitors who found childhood memories filed under the wrong hedge. [38]
Every morning she oiled the brass arrows that pointed nowhere, trimmed the yews that whispered weather, and poured measured tears from the gutters into jars marked for the city's Office of Quantified Sorrow. [71]
Only the trees made her restless. [77]

To read that testimony, he wrote, one must deconstruct the maze without cutting it down, by mapping scattered dreams. [370]
That was maintenance, Mara decided, only grander. [377]
