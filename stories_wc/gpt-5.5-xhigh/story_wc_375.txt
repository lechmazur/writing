<story>After the last echo of laughter fades, Tovin Vale stayed alone beneath the mourning lamps of Mirrorglen. [17] The subterranean crystal cave held the sound a moment longer in its violet ribs, then surrendered it to dripping dark. [37] By trade he was a charcoal-fingered portrait sketch artist, paid to catch the faces of miners, brides, and the newly dead. [58] By curse or blessing he was mnemonically gifted, unable to forget a cheek scar, a swallowed sob, or a number glimpsed backward in glass. [82] That night he had drawn old Jassa smiling for her wake, and the mourners had laughed because she used to laugh first. [104] Now their warmth was gone, and an auroral sorrow moved through the cave, green and blue along the quartz ceiling. [124] Tovin packed his charcoal, but the wall behind the dais clicked. [135] A broken combination lock dropped from the frame of Governor Pell's official painting and struck the floor at his feet. [155] Its shackle had been cut, yet its tumblers showed 3-1-8, the very sequence his mother had whispered before the wardens took her brushes. [178] Tovin remembered being eight, remembered her paint-stained hands lifting that same portrait, remembered the warning she pressed into his ear. [198] When the cave forgets how to breathe, rectify what they corked. [209] For twenty years he had obeyed the council, making likenesses in soot while every brighter line in him tightened like a stopped bottle. [232] They said color tempted madness, because his mother had painted imaginary dawns no cave child was meant to desire. [251] Yet inescapable callings do not ask permission; they return as knocks, echoes, and locks falling open after funerals. [269] Tovin lifted the governor's painting from its hooks. [277] Behind it lay a narrow iron door, not larger than a coffin lid, and around its seam the crystals had blackened. [298] The broken lock had been a decoy or a promise; no remembered combination could mend teeth sheared clean away. [317] A strip of oilcloth was tucked in the hinges, covered in his mother's small, urgent hand. [333] Not the dial. [336] The image. [338] Give the Lung a face. [343] Beneath the note slept twelve corked vials of pigment, colors Tovin knew only from forbidden stories: dawn rose, storm gold, sky blue. [365] He almost laughed then, but the cave answered with a hollow sigh from somewhere deep behind the iron. [383] The North Lung, the ancient vent that drew fresh air through hidden shafts, was closing, and Mirrorglen was breathing its own fear. [405] To rectify a broken machine, the council would send riveters, priests, and laws. [418] To rectify this, Tovin understood, required a painting with something behind it. [430] He opened the first vial to uncork suppressed creativity, and the smell of linseed and mineral light rose like a permitted memory. [452] His hands shook until he drew Jassa's laughing eyes on the iron. [464] Then came the baker with flour in his beard, the twins who sold candles, the quiet warden who had once looked ashamed while confiscating brushes. [489] Tovin painted them not as portraits pinned in stillness, but as a crowd turning toward an unseen morning. [507] His mnemonic gift guided every eyelid and wrinkle, but his suppressed creativity finally spilled between them as ribbons of impossible sky. [528] The cave brightened, reflecting his colors through a thousand facets, and sorrow spread overhead like an aurora that had learned mourning underground. [550] People returned, drawn by the glow, and nobody spoke when they saw their own faces opening across the forbidden door. [570] Councilor Renn reached for Tovin's wrist, then stopped, perhaps remembering the stale air in his grandson's cough. [587] The iron warmed. [590] Behind the painting, something shifted, first a pebble fall, then a groan like a giant waking thirsty. [607] Tovin added his mother last, not as he had seen her dragged away, but as she had been at the hidden frame, smiling fiercely. [631] The pigments ran together around her hair, making a crown of green fire. [644] With a crack, the painted door split along the blackened seam, and cold wind burst from behind it, smelling of snow Tovin had never touched. [669] Lamps guttered; mourners wept; crystals rang in notes too clear for laughter. [681] The North Lung opened, and the whole cave inhaled. [690] In the rush of air, the governor's old canvas fell face down, revealing on its back one final line from Tovin's mother. [712] A calling buried is not buried alive; it is planted. [722] Tovin picked up the broken combination lock and slipped it into his pocket, not as evidence, but as a relic of failed silence. [745] By dawn, or what the cave called dawn, children were pressing colored dust into his palms, asking for skies.
