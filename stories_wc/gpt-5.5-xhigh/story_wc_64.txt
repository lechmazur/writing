Mallow Greaves was the blithe cemetery gardener of Wickmere, a man who whistled while clipping yew and called each grave by name. [22]
His cheer was not mockery; it was a lantern he carried through the village's velveteen gloom. [38]
Every dawn, his sheds, seed trays, and burial ledgers stood tenderly organized, because disorder, he believed, made sorrow shout. [57]

It was brass and empty, except for a folded note smelling faintly of dust, cloves, and rain. [94]
"Meet me where grain once slept, after the chess clock runs down," the note said in Edda's careful hand. [113]
Edda had been buried three days, so Mallow did not argue with the invitation. [127]

He had come not for treasure, nor for spectacle, but to discover the shape of intentions. [272]

The board of sound showed a game unfinished in life and continued in regret. [451]
