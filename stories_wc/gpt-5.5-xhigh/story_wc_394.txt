On the third deck of a gears-and-cogs workshop floating on an acid sea, Tallo adjusted the wing screws of his cart and called the sunrise a leaky brass egg. [29]
Nobody contradicted him, because he was the pioneering cart glider, the first fool to believe a delivery trolley could skim between rust towers if given canvas, nerve, and insults. [58]
His companions admired this with the usual irreverent awe, which in the workshop meant saluting him, stealing his biscuits, and placing bets on his obituary. [83]
Throughout the evolution of that bobbing factory, from four lashed boilers to a city of chimneys, Tallo remained steadily curious about one absurd vacancy in the world. [110]
The vacancy was sound. [114]
Not ordinary sound, for the place shrieked, clacked, burped steam, and cursed in thirteen trade dialects. [130]
Tallo meant the sound predicted by the yearning algorithm bolted under his ribs, a nest of brass mothwheels that twitched whenever history approached an undiscovered longing. [156]
The algorithm insisted that he must ring a bell no one hears yet. [169]
The bell itself hung unfinished above Dock Nine, huge as a moon, tuned not to present ears but to some future organ the acid sea was busy inventing in its stubborn little monsters. [202]
Every season, translucent crabs crawled up the pontoons with more elaborate feelers, tasted the workshop's shadows, and fell back hissing like philosophers denied tenure. [226]
Tallo sketched them all, not tenderly, exactly, but with the respect one gives a miracle that also eats boots. [245]
His missing key arrived inside a relic net hauled from the green foam after an acid squall. [262]
Among warped helmets and prayer spoons lay a hunter's arrow tip, black glass fused around a core of silver, older than the sea's poison and sharper than professional advice. [291]
When Tallo held it near his chest, the yearning algorithm rattled so hard that three screws escaped and a nearby priest converted to mechanics. [315]
The tip had belonged, the archivists said, to surface hunters who once followed birds across dry continents before the continents got ideas above their station and dissolved. [342]
Tallo saw another history in it, a straight desire to cross distance, condensed into a point. [358]
He ground the arrow tip into a stylus and fitted it to his grandmother's kaleidoscope, a contraption of mirrored tubes, stained mica, and one trapped starling feather. [385]
By turning the tube toward the sea, he could transliterate the crabs' color pulses through the images in a kaleidoscope into punch marks on a bell score. [412]
The patterns were not music yet; they were grammar for bodies still under construction. [426]
The workshop council laughed until Tallo projected the translation onto the boiler wall. [439]
There, the crab pulses described ballast leaks, anchor rot, and a migrating reef of acid sponges chewing toward the pontoons. [459]
That stopped the laughter, except for old Foreman Vesh, who considered doom a fine lubricant. [474]
Repairs would take weeks, and the reef would arrive by dawn. [485]
Tallo offered a worse plan, naturally. [491]
He would load the unfinished bell onto his winged cart, glide to the outer beacon where the crabs nested, and strike the score with the arrow stylus as clapper. [520]
No human would hear the result; that was the whole splendid joke. [532]
If the algorithm was right, the crabs' unborn inner combs would feel it as instruction, not command, and swarm where their own survival pulled them. [557]
Vesh called this irresponsible, which everyone recognized as permission. [566]
At dawn the cart shot from Dock Nine, wheels screaming along copper rails before the wings caught furnace wind and lifted bell, man, and bad idea over the acid sea. [596]
Below, the sponge reef opened yellow mouths, admiring him in the nutritional sense. [609]
Steam blistered his goggles; one wing rib snapped; the algorithm kept tugging him toward the beacon with the calm optimism of a drunk saint. [633]
Tallo landed sideways, which counted, bolted the bell to the beacon, and set the hunter's arrow tip against its silent lip. [654]
He struck once. [657]
Nothing happened to human hearing. [662]
To the sea, however, the blow unrolled as blue pressure, silver taste, and a map. [677]
Millions of crab larvae turned beneath the waves, their new combs trembling, and poured toward the reef like living punctuation correcting a disastrous sentence. [701]
They ate the sponges, anchored the pontoons with crystal threads, and ignored all applause, since applause had not been invented for them. [723]
When Tallo returned, scorched and grinning, the council asked what the bell had said. [737]
He shrugged, patted the cart, and answered, "Mostly, hurry up, you gorgeous idiots." [750]
Generations later, when workshop children grew delicate combs behind their ears, they heard old echoes from Dock Nine and learned that yearning is not hunger for what exists, but courtesy to what is still becoming. [785]
