<story>After the last forest burns, the rail lines keep running, because ash weighs less than memory and somebody must invoice the dead. [22] Maro is the dreamless freight guard on the night convoy, stamping crates of rainseed, bone flour, and bottled birdsong. [41] He sleeps sitting up but never dreams, so the children in the refugee cars ask him to watch theirs. [60] He says yes, though he sees only a black platform, a lantern, and tracks crossing themselves like guilty fingers. [79] On the seventh crossing beyond the cinder cities, the convoy reaches a glowing dune in bloom. [95] Flowers of glass push from the sand, ringing softly whenever the wind forgets its own name. [111] There, in the baggage car, he finds a chess clock frozen at midnight inside a crate marked ORCHARD. [129] No hand can move its buttons, yet both faces tick backward in his pulse. [143] Beside it waits a woman made of old station announcements, her mouth opening in many towns at once. [161] I am Elsbet, she whispers, and I am occasionally everywhere, which is lonelier than being nowhere. [177] Maro raises his rifle, but the barrel fills with moths, and the moths hum a fractured lullaby. [194] Sleep, little ember, sleep in the coal, they sing, not together, not apart, never whole. [209] Elsbet tells him the crate is not cargo but an entrance to the divergent archive, where every rescued possibility is shelved. [230] After the forests died, she says, the world chose one dry road and called the others myths. [247] But choices leave sparks, and I need a guard to cultivate the sparks in the unlit corners. [264] Maro thinks of the children, their brows damp with borrowed dreams, and of his own empty nights. [281] What grows from sparks, he asks. [287] Forests, answers Elsbet, if someone charms them through wave functions before the archive collapses into a single burned page. [306] She opens the chess clock, and midnight spills out, not darkness but all midnights braided together. [322] Maro sees a thousand trains: one carrying apples, one carrying coffins, one where he laughs with a wife he never met. [343] In another, he dreams so fiercely that flowers burst from his eyes and root in the upholstery. [360] The archive shudders, and beyond its shelves a devouring silence gnaws, the last simplicity wanting every story flat. [378] Elsbet cannot touch the silence, because being occasionally everywhere has thinned her into rumor. [392] Maro can touch it because he is stubbornly here. [401] He winds the frozen clock with the key from his freight ledger, and each turn costs him a memory of trees. [422] First goes the smell of pine rain, then shade on his mother's apron, then the green word green. [440] The clock starts, one face for loss, one for chance, their needles arguing. [453] Maro steps into the divergent archive. [459] Its aisles are made of unopened doors, and behind each door an unburned forest breathes in a different grammar. [478] He hears children laughing in cedar, wolves praying in fern, rivers counting tadpoles like coins. [493] The silence follows, smooth as a white sheet pulled over a cage. [505] To fight it, Maro does not shoot. [512] He sets the clock between the aisles and listens for the half-rhythms beneath its doubled ticking. [528] Elsbet taught him the charm: name a thing, let it be two things, then love both until they interfere. [547] Through wave functions, she said, tenderness can choose without killing the unchosen. [559] So Maro remembers the children one by one, not as passengers, but as possible forests. [574] Nia with the cracked flute becomes birch light; Sol with the coughing lamb becomes moss after thunder. [591] The twins who sell buttons become two oaks grown from the same acorn and disagreeing beautifully. [607] Each remembered child sends a spark into a dark shelf, and each spark opens like a seed. [624] The silence recoils, not wounded, but confused by abundance. [633] It had expected one answer from a man without dreams. [643] Maro gives it a nursery. [648] The glowing dune outside blooms higher, glass flowers filling with sap, roots breaking the dead rails. [664] In the refugee cars, sleeping children murmur leaves into their blankets. [675] Elsbet smiles from every window and no window. [683] The chess clock reaches one minute after midnight, and the archive exhales all its divergent branches into the world. [702] Maro falls asleep at last on the baggage floor, ash in his hair, soil under his nails. [719] He dreams of guarding a forest train that carries no freight except dawn. [732] Somewhere, a lullaby mends itself, and for once its pieces keep time. [744]</story>
