<story>After the carnival leaves town, Mira Venn returns to the abandoned spaceport passenger terminal with a survey pack and the careful hunger of a curious civil engineer intern. [28] For three nights the midway had glittered beyond the fence, and its generators had hidden the old building's smaller sounds. [48] Now only wind moves through ticket lanes, boarding tubes, and cracked skylights, whistling like a crowd that remembers departure. [67] Mira has come because the city council wants demolition numbers, but she wants to puzzle out new equations for hope. [87] Her mentor says structures fail when loads forget their paths; Mira suspects neighborhoods fail when grief has nowhere lawful to travel. [108] The terminal once received settlers from orbital ferries, then refugees from burned coastlines, then nobody. [123] Each absence left barricades, signs, and locked glass. [131] Inside, she studies the departures hall by lantern, mapping puddles from roof leaks and the thin rivers of sand blown under the doors. [154] The place is a maze of intentions: queues bent to calm panic, ramps tilted for luggage, murals promising Mars, arrows pointing toward collapsed runways. [178] Near a fountain dry as bone, her boot clicks on brass. [189] She kneels and finds an explorer's sextant piece, a curved limb etched with star marks, snapped from some child's carnival prize or an ancestor's kit. [214] It fits her palm like a question measuring both heaven and ruin. [226] Mira is functionally creative, a phrase her supervisor uses when her odd sketches keep meeting code. [242] The fragment gives her an angle instead of a souvenir. [252] Holding it against the moonlit skylight, she notices its arc matches the old ceiling trusses by rhythm, not size. [271] The roof has not merely leaked; it has been trying to draw a map. [285] Each puddle occupies a former waiting area, each sand ribbon stops at a kiosk base, and every stain leans toward the central fountain. [308] Mira spreads her plans across a baggage carousel and compares them with the sextant curve, turning intention into gradient. [327] The original designers had meant people to flow gently from fear to departure; later guards had meant them to bottleneck for inspection. [349] Carnival crews, seeking shortcuts, had opened service shutters and wedged doors with painted boards, accidentally offering a third design. [368] Mira sees that the building is not dead; its purposes are arguing. [380] At dawn her estimate is due, and at noon a sandstorm is forecast to scour the low district where carnival tents stood yesterday. [403] Families who earned the carnival's last coins sleep in parked buses beyond the fence because the shelters are full. [422] Mira calls her supervisor, reports instability, and asks for permission to keep the terminal standing one more day. [440] He hears only liability and says no. [447] She thanks him anyway, then begins to disobey with professional calm. [458] Using the sextant piece as a gauge, she marks angles on the floor where ramps can channel wind rather than fight it. [480] She drags queue rails into chevrons, unbolts drain grates, and tapes cracked panels into scoops that aim leaks toward the fountain basin. [502] From there, old graywater pipes descend to thermal sumps beneath the concourse, sealed since the last orbital ferry departed. [521] If water reaches them cleanly, small turbines in the maintenance manual might wake emergency batteries that everyone assumes are scrap. [541] If wind is guided through the boarding tubes, dust should settle in cargo pits instead of choking the hall. [560] If frightened people are given visible paths, they may become a load the building can bear. [576] By late morning, the first buses nose toward the fence, drivers blinking at the intern in a hard hat waving a carnival flag. [599] Mira does not promise safety; she points to taped arrows, open gates, and the fountain, speaking as if explaining a bridge to gravity. [622] They enter. [624] The storm arrives behind them, brown and enormous, and the terminal shudders like a ship remembering oceans. [641] For minutes there is only impact, dust, coughing, and the stern music of materials accepting stress. [657] Then, through redirected flows, the building begins its quiet arithmetic. [667] Rain scraped from the roof runs down Mira's scoops, spins the sump turbines, and lifts a thread of yellow light along the departures board. [691] Wind diverted through tubes drops its sand in the cargo pits with the soft hiss of poured grain. [709] Children follow glowing arrows around the fountain, laughing once, then growing hushed beneath the stars painted on the sextant in Mira's hand. [731] When the storm passes, nobody applauds, but people are asleep under intact trusses, and the abandoned spaceport passenger terminal breathes. [751] Mira writes no demolition estimate, only the first clean equation for hope: intention, redirected, can resurrect shelter. [768]</story>
