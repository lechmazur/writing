She was known in the valley as a mellow music therapist, the sort who could turn a panic tremor into a rhythm and a rhythm into breath. [53]

This eclipse day, she had been asked to help the children keep calm while the sun disappeared over the ridge. [96]

Mira liked that shelter because it made every whisper rounder, as if the mountain itself were listening before deciding what to answer. [145]
