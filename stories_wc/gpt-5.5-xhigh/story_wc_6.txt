<story>During the migration of clouds, when whole white herds crossed the coast toward the mountains, Nara Vale kept watch at the dune station. [23] She was an arsonist's daughter firefighter, a title the town spoke like an accusation and a prayer, depending on who needed saving. [45] Her father had burned the old cannery in a drunk season of rage, and the blaze had kissed Nara's throat before neighbors dragged her out. [70] Years of skin graft and breath lessons left a silver ridge under her jaw, tight as a tide line, but her eyes stayed gracefully alert. [95] Nara trained herself to hear danger without letting it sour into suspicion. [107] That was her unyielding compassion: not softness, people learned, but a hand that reached through heat and came back with someone breathing. [129] On calm evenings she walked to the obsidian lagoon beneath the dunes, where the water lay black and polished between mats of eelgrass. [152] The place held a kelp-drift peace, a slow breathing of salt, flies, and far surf that seemed older than blame. [172] In her coat pocket she carried black sand from volcanic beach coves west of town, grains heavy enough to settle any shaking hand. [195] Beside it hung her talisman, a foreign coin drilled and strung on red thread, given by the surgeon who had taught her to hum before she dared speak. [223] He had said the coin would count her breaths if she swung it like a pendulum. [239] Nara used it now while teaching burned children by the lagoon, because she wanted to prove that scars can sing. [259] Not beautifully at first, not loudly, but honestly, the way wind finds holes in shell and makes them flutes. [278] The alarm bell broke that lesson. [284] Across the ridge, cloud shadows galloped over brown grass, and under them a red seam opened where lightning had touched a fisherman's shed. [307] The children froze, each looking at Nara's scar before looking toward the smoke. [320] She smiled, clipped on her mask, and handed the red thread to little Sami, who had not spoken since his own house fire. [343] When the coin swings, count three and breathe out, she told him, and keep everyone singing until I return. [362] Then she ran. [365] At the shed, flames raced not inland but downward, licking the dry roots that stitched the dunes over the lagoon. [385] If the roots burned through, the dune face would collapse, trapping the children below and pouring smoke into the hollow. [405] The engine crew was minutes away, so Nara took the old beach shovel and attacked the nearest dark bank. [424] Black sand hissed over flame, not enough to conquer it, but enough to write a cool crescent around the weakest slope. [445] She thought of her father's hands, how they had once made fire for supper and later made it a weapon. [465] Hatred would have been easier than remembering both. [473] A cough answered from inside the shed. [480] Nara kicked the door and found Old Tovin, the watchman who had testified against her father, pinned beneath a fallen spar. [501] For a breath, his fear saw not a firefighter but the arsonist's blood. [514] For the next breath, Nara saw only a man being burned. [525] She slid under smoke, wetted her gloves in a puddle, and lifted the spar while the skin along her graft pulled tight. [547] Tovin screamed, then crawled, and she crawled after him, nudging his boots whenever panic made him stop. [564] Outside, the crew arrived in a growl of pumps. [573] They cut the fire from above while Nara and the children below answered each other with a ragged tune. [592] Sami held the coin high, the red thread bright as a vein, and his small voice kept time for the others. [613] By dusk, the migrating clouds had gone purple, and the lagoon mirrored them as if the sky had been laid in black glass. [636] Tovin, bandaged and ashamed, tried to thank her, but Nara asked him to thank the children first. [653] His apology came clumsy, like driftwood bumping stone, yet Sami nodded, still clutching the coin. [667] Nara gathered a palmful of cooled black sand and pressed it into the wet ash at the dune's wounded foot. [687] Tomorrow they would graft marram grass there, green strips over the burn, roots braided like patient fingers. [704] Tonight she asked the children for the song again. [713] They began uncertainly, but Nara joined, her scarred throat rough, tender, and steady. [726] The note wavered across the obsidian lagoon, touched the coin, and returned in a thin silver ring. [743] Tovin wept without hiding. [747] Nara did not forgive the fire, or her father, or the town's old cruelty all at once. [764] She simply kept singing, because compassion, like the sea, did not loosen stone in one tide. [780]</story>
