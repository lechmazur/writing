<story>At the center of Veyr, under streets padded with ash, the cathedral floated around a dimensional anchor called the Nail. [20] Its bells never rang, because sound could jar the Nail and loosen the worlds threaded through its eye. [38] Father Orrel, the loop pedal priest, served there by kneeling before a silver foot switch and singing prayers into layered rounds. [59] He was famous for the way he decorates with silence, leaving deliberate gaps between holy phrases until listeners heard their own fear answering. [82] Tonight those gaps were failing. [87] A girl named Sera lay on the altar grate, wrists glowing with geometry, while a homeless intelligence pressed inward through her breath. [109] The canons called it possession, but Orrel knew that word hid an assumption, that a body was a house and spirits were thieves. [132] His duty was to illuminate hidden assumptions before the possession completes, because unnamed premises were doors no exorcism could close. [152] Beside Sera sat his only other tool, an artist's palette with one color, a damp white made from powdered anchor stone. [173] The pigment did not banish demons; it revealed what every witness believed was already true. [188] Orrel dipped one finger and drew a ring around the loop pedal, then another around Sera's lips. [205] The white lines shone with muffled clarity, as if moonlight had been wrapped in wool and taught to speak. [224] In that subdued glow, the entity inside Sera smiled with her mouth and whispered, I am the rightful occupant. [243] Orrel pressed the pedal and recorded his own breath, four counts in, four counts out, making a soft wall. [262] Then he began to plumb the voice, not with hooks or relics, but through rhetorical devices sharpened by mercy. [281] If you are rightful, he asked, what is a right without a giver? [294] If you are occupant, what room did you build? [303] If she is a vessel, why does the vessel remember her mother's hands? [316] The loops repeated each question beneath the next, until the chamber seemed filled with patient judges wearing cotton masks. [335] Sera's fingers unclenched, but black script crawled from her nails toward the Nail, seeking the hinge between dimensions. [353] Orrel had only minutes before the possession completes and the intelligence could use her as a living key. [371] He painted the single color across his own tongue, accepting the taste of chalk and old stars. [388] Years earlier, he had believed faith was volume, a tower of certain answers, and he had shouted a sick man deaf. [409] Since then he had learned that truth often entered as unraveled stillness, the quiet after certainty lost its thread. [428] So he lowered every loop except the breath, decorating the cathedral with silence until even the black script paused to listen. [449] The entity laughed without sound, and the Nail trembled, showing brief rooms where other Veyrs drowned, burned, or never were born. [470] Orrel spoke anaphora into the pedal: Not your hunger, not your title, not your borrowed mouth, not your borrowed wound. [490] The phrase circled Sera like a rosary made of refusals. [500] He turned to chiasmus, because inversion could expose a lie's spine. [511] You do not possess her because you know her; you know her only because you possess her. [528] The white rings flared. [532] Sera coughed, and in the cough came a child's word, Pap, followed by a sob too small for any ancient thing. [553] The canons behind Orrel began chanting commands, but their certainty fed the intruder, making Sera's shadow stand taller. [571] Orrel raised one hand for silence, and because he decorated with it so well, they obeyed. [587] He asked the final question softly, using aporia, the sacred art of confessed uncertainty. [601] What if rescue is not eviction, but invitation back to the one assumption you erased? [616] He touched the palette to Sera's brow, leaving the last white thumbprint like a small shut eye. [633] Inside the mark appeared a scene everyone had ignored: Sera inviting the intelligence in, not from consent, but loneliness, after the cathedral took her father for anchor duty. [661] The hidden assumption broke, and with it the bargain. [670] The entity shrank from conqueror to echo, unable to claim a home built on grief it had merely copied. [689] Orrel gave the breath loop one final tap, and the layered air became a cradle rather than a cage. [708] Sera inhaled alone. [711] The Nail steadied. [714] Morning found the cathedral unchanged, except for one white palette, empty at last, and a priest sitting beside a waking girl. [735] When she asked whether she was clean, Orrel shook his head, not denying her, but refusing the old frame. [754] You are here, he said, and let silence finish the blessing. [765]</story>
