At the magnetic nexus at twilight, compass needles spun like anxious insects, and Selren Voss set his chessboard on a slab of blue iron. [24]
He had once been the capital's most thoughtfully precise tutor, able to turn a pawn lesson into a philosophy of mercy. [45]
Now he was an exiled chess tutor with dust in his cuffs, paid in roots, rumors, and the occasional apology. [65]

Selren told himself exile suited him because difficulty was the only landscape where his mind still found flowers. [108]

The place belonged to no kingdom, only to magnets, migrating birds, and the old road between the mountain cities of Aster and Mor. [154]
Every nineteen years, when the moon began swallowing the sun, both cities renewed the eclipsed covenant there, sharing water rights, ore rights, and mourning rites. [179]
This year their envoys faced each other across the iron basin and said nothing. [193]
Silence at the nexus was not empty; it gathered, bent, and rang beneath the stones. [208]

"You taught princes to read boards," she had written, touching the scar on her throat where smoke had stolen her voice. [249]
"Teach these envoys to read what they are not saying." [259]
