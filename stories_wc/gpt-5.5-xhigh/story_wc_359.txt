<story>At the moment a sculptor's chisel first strikes marble, every bell in Nivalis is required to hold its tongue. [19] The law was older than the city, older even than the ice cavern under it, where silence had been carved by centuries into a sculpted hush. [45] Only I was allowed to disturb it, because I was the prism-song bell ringer, synesthetically blessed and therefore suspect. [64] Colors rang in my teeth, notes opened like flowers behind my eyes, and lies always tasted of dull pewter. [83] That gift had earned me neither wealth nor ease, but tonight it might save the charter that kept children fed on more than porridge and fear. [109] Above me, in the civic square, Master Ila bent over the veiled statue commissioned by the magistrates. [126] They wanted a monument to industry, not amazement, and had declared wonder ornamental unless someone could certify wonder as essential before dawn. [148] Ila, who had taught me to listen to stone, had delayed her first blow until the aurora climbed low enough to touch the glacier. [172] When her chisel finally rang, the cavern answered by not answering. [183] The hush around me settled, loosened, settled again, an oscillating repose that made each breath feel borrowed. [200] I stood beside the central bell, a clear cone of ice veined with trapped starlight, and unrolled the amber music sheet. [221] It had been pressed from resin before the northern forests drowned in frost, and every note inside it wandered like a preserved moth. [244] To ordinary eyes it was blank; to mine it smelled blue, hummed gold, and warmed my palms with green instructions. [264] My task was not to make noise, but to unfetter the testimony sealed inside the sculpted hush. [281] If the testimony emerged, the charter stone in the square would glow, and the magistrates would have to maintain the wonder schools, lantern festivals, and sky-watching rations they despised. [310] If I failed, Nivalis would become efficient, dim, and obedient. [320] I raised the mallet, but my hands remembered my brother Tovin, who had vanished here attempting the same rite three winters ago. [342] People said the cavern took him because he rang too loudly; I knew it took him because grief had made him deaf to color. [366] He had wanted wonder to prove our mother's death meaningful, and the hush accepts no bargain made from hunger. [385] So I lowered the mallet and waited until my pulse stopped pleading. [397] The aurora filtered through the glacier roof in bands of violet, lime, and rose, each color carrying a path my body could hear. [420] The amber music sheet asked me to travel through the colors of the aurora without moving my feet. [438] Violet first: I struck the bell so softly the sound appeared as a cool spiral around my throat. [456] The hush leaned close, and I saw Tovin kneeling within it, not trapped, but listening. [471] Lime next: the second strike tasted of spring melt, and the cavern walls showed children in the wonder school learning the constellations by touch. [495] Rose last: the bell did not ring outward, but inward, and the amber sheet flared as if dawn had been folded into it. [518] Then the sculpted hush spoke in many small silences. [527] It showed me fishermen steering home by a green sky, nurses calming fevered babies with glass bells, miners surviving weeks underground because one painted a sunrise on a helmet. [556] Wonder was not decoration; it was the muscle by which despair lifted its head. [570] The magistrates had measured bread, coal, and hours, but never the courage that let a hand reach for them. [589] I understood, and because understanding has weight, the bell bowed toward me. [601] Tovin's shape lifted one palm, asking release, not rescue. [610] I struck once more, no harder than a snowflake landing on wool, and whispered, 'Be unfettered.' [626] The hush opened like a clenched hand. [633] Above, I felt Ila's marble accept its first true line, not a monument to labor alone, but a figure looking up with frost on its lashes. [659] The charter stone awakened in the square, casting amber light through the streets, and even the magistrates fell quiet because the quiet no longer belonged to them. [686] When I climbed from the ice cavern, dawn had not broken; it had been certified. [701] Ila met me beside the statue, her chisel nicked, her eyes bright with the calm terror of making. [719] 'Did it answer?' she asked. [724] I touched the amber music sheet, now clear as ordinary glass, and heard from the waking city a hundred children laughing in colors no decree could ration.
