During a sky lantern release, when the harbor held a meteor hush and every boat chain seemed afraid to clink, Mara Venn began her late-shift pier lamplighter round. [28]
She loved that hour because lamps answered her pole with gold throats, yet she had taken the work for a stranger hunger: to discover the color of sound. [56]

All he left Mara was a brass hotel skeleton key tag stamped LIBRARY, though no key dangled from its ring. [97]

Most citizens believed the colors signify forgiveness, grief, or luck, but Mara suspected they formed a map. [141]
Her suspicion sounded foolish, so she kept it folded inside routine, the safest lantern a lonely woman owned. [159]

Its word, LIBRARY, filled with blue fire each time the gong sounded. [194]

She had passed it for six years and never noticed the hotel crest carved upside down on its plate. [257]
The ring of the skeleton tag fit a crescent notch, and the lock yielded without a key, as if recognizing family. [278]
A stair dropped through damp iron into the wreck of the Aureate, a packet ship that had carried the Mariner Hotel library when the hotel burned. [304]

A note written by her father lay atop it: Sound keeps its color where a promise is neither broken nor spent. [384]
Mara touched the page, and the drowned room answered with a chord she saw as green, violet, and tender yellow. [404]

I will tell Mara why I went down, it read, but the ink after that had been scraped thin. [450]

She had found sound's color, not as one shade, but as a vow made bright enough to guide the living. [753]
