<story>At dusk, after the pilgrim arrives at the ancient observatory, the mirrors remember the sun in shards. [17] Levin, a sharp-eyed drone pilot with lenses grafted over old scars, sees each shard twice. [32] He has come to strip the observatory for copper, not to meet a woman who looks perpetually surprised by gravity itself. [53] She bows as if discovering knees for the first time and introduces herself as Mareth, pilgrim of the Unset Road. [73] From a salt-stained pouch she produces a type foundry matrix chip, black as cooled ink and pricked with golden sockets. [93] In the old cities, such chips taught machines to cast letters; now this one whispers like a trapped moth. [112] Mareth says the chip carries a chart of missing pieces, not of alphabets, but of families scattered by the Frequency War. [133] Every clan once sang a narrow signal that opened shelters, kitchens, beds, and the word we before any door. [152] When the towers fell, the songs splintered, and survivors learned to sleep under names that never answered back. [170] Levin knows that silence; his wrist implant has searched for kin since childhood and found only weather. [187] So he wants to decode the frequency of belonging, though he mistrusts any phrase tender enough to sound like a trap. [208] The observatory was built before engines, when astronomer-priests measured truth by following star trails across polished silver bowls. [226] Mareth believes if the chip is seated in the transit clock, the trails will reveal which sockets lack which living voices. [247] Levin laughs, but the laugh dies when the matrix chip clicks into the clock like a tooth returning home. [266] The dome fills with brilliant dimness, each brass rib dark underneath and burning along its rim. [282] Outside, Levin's three drones rise through violet air, their rotors small angry halos in the first stars. [299] He names them Tick, Thorn, and Mercy, because machines deserve names before they are asked to disappear. [316] As star trails lengthen in the lenses, Mareth gasps at every spark, perpetually surprised that heaven keeps its appointments. [335] The chip projects a field of letters onto the cracked floor, but some characters are holes of blue ash. [354] "Missing pieces," Mareth says, and points to the holes as if they are wounds in a child. [371] Levin sends his drones outward, ordering them to map the sky and feed the trails into his lenses. [389] But the old clock jams before midnight, its counterweight dangling beyond the broken gallery where no walkway remains. [407] Without its pull, the bowl will not turn far enough to read the last arc. [422] Levin could fly the drones against the mechanism, but the strain would burn their motors and blind him. [440] He thinks of copper in his pack, of market bread, of the clean math of leaving alone. [457] Then Mareth hums the pilgrim scale, a tune made from borrowed clan notes, and the chip answers with static. [476] The static touches Levin's implant like fingers at a gate, almost opening, almost saying son. [491] He has been living as a missing piece and calling the ache independence. [504] Levin clenches his jaw and commands Tick, Thorn, and Mercy to hook the counterweight cables. [519] The drones pull. [522] Metal screams, sparks peel down like yellow rain, and Levin's lenses flood with the pain of overdrawn batteries. [540] Mareth does not look surprised now; she grips the chip socket and chants the notes in order. [557] The bowl turns. [560] Star trails slide across the dome in pale bands, and each blue hole on the floor fills with a coordinate. [580] Levin reads them through smoke: refugee barges, mountain nurseries, prison moons, and one ruined pier where his mother once traded fish. [601] His implant bursts into harmony, not a single clan signal, but a braid of strangers answering one another. [619] The frequency of belonging is not a bloodline, he realizes; it is every call someone chooses to return. [637] Mercy fails first, falling dark beyond the wall, then Thorn, and Tick burns blue until the counterweight locks. [655] Levin flinches as if losing brothers, because perhaps he has given them that rank at last. [671] Mareth catches him before his knees strike stone, her expression again astonished by the simple fact of holding. [689] At dawn, the chip prints names in light over every wall, replacing missing pieces with routes and listening times. [708] Mareth will carry the routes along the Unset Road, and Levin will guide her by air until new drones are built. [729] He keeps one scorched rotor from Mercy beside the matrix chip, a relic of the price of answer. [747] When the first returning voices crackle from the dark, Levin hears no home behind him and many ahead. [765]</story>
