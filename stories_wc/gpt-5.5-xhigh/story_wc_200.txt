In the hush of a hidden sunrise, when light entered Echo Gorge only as a pale seam above the black cliffs, Mara Esh carried her harp to the riverbed and listened for the dead. [34]
She was the district's lost soul collector, a title larger than her shoulders and smaller than her work, for she did not trap spirits; she persuaded them to remember the road home. [66]
The gorge answered everything twice, once in stone and once in sorrow, so a lie spoken there returned as a bruise and a truth returned as rain. [93]
That morning the echoes had begun to laugh. [101]
In the village below, broken bones had failed to set, fevered children woke quoting strangers, and widows heard jokes from mouths that had been sewn shut for burial. [129]
Mara had come to collect the source, not to silence it, because her master had taught her to integrate laughter into healing before pain hardened into law. [156]
Villagers called her modestly fearless because she blushed when thanked, apologized to nettles she brushed aside, and still entered places where priests refused to whistle. [181]
At her belt hung a crocodile's tooth, honey brown and curved, drilled through the root and threaded on blue cord. [201]
It had belonged to Nilo, the old bone setter, whose jokes once made patients laugh so hard their clenched muscles surrendered splints and stitches. [225]
Three nights ago, Nilo's buried voice had crawled out of Echo Gorge, bright with mirth and rotten with guilt. [244]
Mara knew his laugh, and underneath it she heard the luminous ache of a man who had died before forgiving himself. [265]
She knelt on a slab where water once ran and set the harp across her knees. [281]
Its frame was willow, its strings gut and silver, and its bridge deliberately crooked, for spectral confessions came clean only through the distorted notes of a misaligned harp. [309]
Straight music flattered the dead; crooked music made them answer. [319]
Mara plucked the first sour interval with the crocodile tooth, and the gorge shook loose a man's whisper. [337]
"I told them the bridge would hold," the whisper said. [347]
Another note bent upward, and a woman's voice sobbed that she had hidden medicine from her sister. [363]
The spectral confessions rose around Mara like moths made of breath, each drawn to the wrongness in the tune. [382]
She accepted them gently, naming each soul, tying each truth to a strand of red thread until the air cleared enough for Nilo's laugh to strike. [408]
It came from the eastern cleft, where the old maps named it echo gorge in small frightened letters, as if capitals might wake the stones. [432]
Mara stood and began to pursue the laugh between boulders silvered by the secret sun. [447]
Her fear walked with her, but it walked behind. [456]
When she reached the cleft, the laugh became Nilo himself, translucent, potbellied, with river mud in his beard and his kind eyes snapped shut. [480]
"Little collector," he said, "you should not chase a joke that has learned to bite." [494]
"You taught me jokes were splints for the heart," Mara answered, lifting the tooth. [508]
Nilo flinched at the charm, and the gorge poured out his final confession. [521]
Years before, during the flood, he had led twelve children across a rope bridge, telling them a ridiculous story about a crocodile who wanted dentures. [548]
They laughed, and because they laughed they did not hear the first rope snap. [562]
Nilo had thrown them forward, one by one, until the current took him; ten lived, two did not, and he had spent death believing laughter had masked disaster. [590]
Mara let the confession finish before she played again. [599]
This time she tuned nothing true; she let the harp wobble like a wounded knee, then struck the crocodile tooth across three strings. [623]
The ugly chord became the very laugh the saved children had carried home, frightened, grateful, alive. [639]
It was not clean happiness. [644]
It was grief with breath in it. [650]
Nilo opened his eyes. [654]
From the village road above, voices answered: old patients, grown children, the two dead ones also, all laughing softly within their tears. [675]
Mara stepped close enough that his cold passed through her ribs. [687]
"Healing is not forgetting the wound," she said. [695]
"It is teaching the wound that breath can still come." [704]
Nilo's face crumpled, then eased, and for the first time his laugh did not echo; it entered the threads Mara held, warming every confession tied there. [731]
The hidden sunrise spilled fully over the rim, and the gorge shone as if sorrow itself had learned to give light. [751]
Mara collected Nilo last, not in a cage, but in the tooth's small hollow, where his laughter rested like medicine waiting for a careful hand. [776]
