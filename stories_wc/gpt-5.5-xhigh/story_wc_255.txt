After the phoenix rose above Vey and left the eastern roofs feathered with fire, no one in the city woke simply because morning came. [24]
Sleepers opened their eyes only through bartered awakenings: a memory for a voice, a sorrow for a skill, a buried truth for a child's next breath. [50]
Those bargains were made in Maera's glassblower's courtyard, where furnace heat trembled in the air and broken panes flashed like captive wings. [72]
Maera had lost her speech to smoke, so she dealt by communicating through plant arrangements, setting rosemary for remembrance, nettles for pain, and small white onions for honesty. [100]
Elian came there carrying his brushes and the concertina reed tongue that had survived when his father's instrument melted in the rising. [122]
He was a colorblind painter, known for portraits in soot, ash, and borrowed labels from apprentices who told him which jar held red. [145]
Most people pitied him, but Elian had learned to want another thing: to find beauty in imperfection, in bubbles inside glass and faces pulled sideways by grief. [172]
His little sister Nara lay in ash sleep behind Maera's kiln, breathing as lightly as a moth under a cup. [192]
She had watched the phoenix too directly, and her waking price had not yet found a shape. [209]
On the first noon after Elian arrived, Maera placed a cracked basil pot between two thorn branches, then laid a wilted violet across them. [233]
Elian read the sentence by stem and angle: Bring a broken sweetness through danger, or the girl will keep dreaming. [253]
The courtyard petitioners laughed because he answered with plants instead of speech, nudging a chipped aloe toward a rampant weed and setting his reed tongue beside them. [280]
In Maera's grammar, it meant: let a scar learn to feed itself. [292]
That was Elian's innocently cunning gift; he seemed guileless enough to touch nettles barehanded, yet he arranged meanings no merchant could untie. [314]
A broker named Savid, whose left eye had awakened after he traded away a lover's lullaby, offered Elian a clean bargain. [335]
Let the ash awaken your colors, Savid said, and the overflow will awaken Nara. [349]
Elian imagined sudden crimson pouring into every rose and wound, perfect at last, and felt a small terror for his own hand. [371]
If color arrived like a king, would he still notice the kingdom of cracks, fingerprints, warped mouths, and smoky light? [391]
He refused, not because he despised color, but because Nara deserved more than to be ransomed by his hunger. [410]

He fitted the concertina reed tongue into a narrow glass tube Maera had blown with one deliberate blister near the lip. [452]

The pots answered, leaves shivering though no wind crossed the walls. [483]

The barter had not bought perfection; it had awakened the courtyard to the use of flaws. [693]
