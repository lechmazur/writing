After the sixth sense awakens, everyone in Veer can hear direction. [11]
North clicks like beetle jaws, east rings like glass rain, and the forbidden south gives a low, persuasive purr. [30]
I was paid to map those sounds through the mechanical rainforest, where copper vines climbed steel trunks and orchids opened with the soft ticking of clocks. [56]
Then the stray philosopher arrived beside my survey fire, barefoot, gray coated, and carrying a small gear on a thread around his neck. [79]
He called himself Oren, though he admitted names were only fences built by people frightened of weather. [96]
By dawn, he had vanished from his blanket and reappeared asleep in the crook of a tree thirty meters above us. [117]
When I shouted, he woke, blinked at the gears of the canopy, and said, "A mind like mine translocates during sleep when it finds an open gate." [144]
That sounded impossible, but in Veer probable miracles were ordinary business if they repeated twice and left scorch marks. [163]
Oren had come to hire me, not with money, but with the promise of the hum of insight. [181]
He said the hum lived in the oldest regulator tree, a machine grown before machines learned shame, and it could open the wild frontier of the mind again. [209]
At first I thought "your" meant mine, the way philosophers snare listeners, but Oren tapped the small gear and corrected me. [230]
"Not yours alone," he said, "but everyone who has forgotten how to wonder; I came to rediscover the wild frontier of your mind." [253]
The gear, he explained, had once turned inside that tree, until someone removed it and the forest began to dream in circles. [275]
Dreaming forests are dangerous because paths repeat your regrets, and rain falls upward whenever you lie. [291]
I agreed because my own sixth sense had woken wrong; it heard only the locked rooms inside my skull. [310]
For three days we walked under brass leaves and piston ferns, following the sound of wind through different trees. [329]
Wind in ironwoods gave a courthouse drone, wind in glass palms chimed like nervous teeth, and wind in rubber cedars swallowed footsteps. [351]
Oren listened to each note, then unraveled the knots of direction the forest had tied across our path. [369]
Whenever he slept, I tied one end of my measuring cord to his ankle and the other to my wrist. [389]
Still, he translocated before midnight, dragging me through a curtain of fern teeth into a clearing that had not existed at sunset. [411]
At its center rose the regulator tree, taller than weather, its bark a lattice of bronze ribs around a heart of green light. [434]
The hum of insight trembled from it, not loud, yet intimate, as if a bee had entered every thought and found honey there. [457]
I nearly wept, because the locked rooms in me answered with hinges I had never heard. [473]
Oren stepped forward to return the small gear, but roots like segmented snakes lifted from the mud and barred him. [493]
The tree spoke in ticks: "What enters me must give up the map it loves most." [509]
I knew Oren would surrender philosophy, his last fence, but he surprised me by offering sleep. [525]
"Without sleep," he said, "I will stop translocating and finally remain responsible for where I stand." [541]
The tree shivered, and the sound of wind through different trees gathered around us like a jury made of seasons. [561]
Ironwood drone, glass palm chime, rubber cedar hush: each strand crossed the hum, and together they began to unravel my certainty. [582]
I saw that my locked rooms were not prisons built by grief, but shelters I had mistaken for home. [601]
To restore the gear, someone had to enter the heart and remember a path no ordinary sense could trust. [620]
Oren could not, because he had given up the one map he trusted, the sleeping shortcut through places. [638]
So I listened beyond north, east, and the purring south, into the new sense that had frightened me for months. [658]
It did not point outward; it pointed into possibility, where old habits curled like sleeping animals. [674]
I entered the bronze ribs, placed the small gear in the green socket, and felt the rainforest inhale. [692]
Every tree began to move, not walking, not marching, but remembering how forests sway before anyone names them machines. [711]
The hum rose through my bones, and my mind opened past its fences into hills, storms, and unlicensed stars. [730]
Oren laughed like a man finding an address under his own skin. [742]
Dawn found us where we had begun, beside the dead survey fire, with no scorch marks except the bright ones behind our eyes. [765]
In Veer, that was evidence enough. [771]
