In Tern, in the years after violence becomes impossible, a quarrel could still bruise a person; it only had to do it without fists. [24] No knife would cut living skin, no hand would close hard enough to strike, so the harbor learned softer cruelties: liens, vows, silences, and love offered like a hook. [53] Mara, the most graceful dock worker on the sublittoral fringe, stepped among wet ropes and ankle high anemones as if the tide had trained her bones. [79] Men cursed when she outlifted them; she answered by setting their crates down gently, never where their toes waited. [98] Women called her eel girl, half insult and half blessing, and she tucked both meanings under her cap. [116] At low blue tide, the disappearing island of Ardent shoaled beyond the outer posts, carrying orchards, black goats, and a chapel whose bell rang underwater. [141] By dawn it would be gone, leaving only foam and arguments. [152] Dockmaster Senn met Mara beside the winch house, coat spotless above boots that had forgotten honest mud. [169] In his palm lay the pirate's whalebone scrimshaw, a curved white sliver etched with ships burning from a time when burning people had still been possible. [195] Captain Orris had carved it from a dying whale and bragged that it unlocks guarded hearts better than any blade. [215] "Take it," Senn said, closing her fingers around the bone before she could refuse, though the law made coercion impossible and therefore polite. [238] "Ask Ardent's ferryman why the island flees us, and make him answer." [250] Mara felt the scrimshaw warm, not like fire, but like an animal recognizing a cage. [265] She hated Senn for needing her and hated herself because part of her wanted to be needed. [282] Once, as a child, she had sung in sleep and drawn whales under the pier, vast backs gliding through moonlit green. [303] Her mother had shaken her awake and whispered that hiding was safer, but safety was not the same as life. [323] The reason to leave hiding, her mother said, was to find freedom in embracing one's true nature, even if every neighbor called that nature monstrous. [348] Mara boarded the skiff to Ardent with the scrimshaw in her teeth and anger braided through her tenderness for the islanders. [369] The ferryman, Old Pell, waited at the surf line, a small man with eyes barricaded by years of watching homes vanish. [390] "Back, Tern dog," he said, and offered her a cup of pear cider with the same motion. [407] "Bite me after I drink," Mara said, accepting it. [416] That was how people spoke now, when no one could break a jaw: every kindness wore its helmet. [434] She touched the scrimshaw to Pell's wrist. [441] His face crumpled before his voice did, and Mara hated the bone for stripping him so naked. [458] "Ardent is not fleeing you," Pell said. [465] "She is molting." [468] He pointed to the chapel bell below the clear water, where chains of coral and bronze ran from the island's reef to Tern's pilings. [492] "Senn calls them moorings, but they are promises signed by hungry folk; Ardent cannot shed her shore until someone dares detach them by the call of a whale from the deep." [523] Mara looked at the island's cliffs and saw, beneath moss and goat paths, the slow flex of living hide. [542] Ardent was a whale calf grown around soil, hiding from the old pirate wound in its mother's bone. [560] The scrimshaw shuddered in Mara's hand like a guilty tooth. [570] When she returned, Senn waited with clerks and ribboned contracts, all of them armed with lawful concern. [587] "Do not make me your villain," he said softly. [596] "If Ardent leaves, Tern starves by winter." [603] Mara pressed the scrimshaw to his chest, and his guarded heart opened with a soundless snap. [619] Senn gasped, then whispered, "I am tired of loving people by locking the doors." [633] "Then help me open one," Mara said, furious enough to cradle his elbow when he swayed. [649] Together they waded into the sublittoral fringe, clerks shouting statutes from the pier and island children shouting insults worse and more useful. [671] Mara set the pirate's whalebone scrimshaw against her throat. [680] She did not sing prettily; she sang as herself, salt rough, enormous, afraid, and free. [695] From the trench beyond the harbor came an answer, by the call of a whale from the deep, and every contract chain loosened like a forgiven fist. [722] Ardent detached with a sigh that lifted boats, goats, and arguments together. [734] Senn clung to a piling, weeping with rage and relief, while Pell spat cider at him in blessing. [752] Mara rode the outgoing swell until her feet left the dock's remembered path and found, below it, the wider road of water. [774] Behind her, Tern cursed her name and began, at last, to learn hunger without chains. [789]
