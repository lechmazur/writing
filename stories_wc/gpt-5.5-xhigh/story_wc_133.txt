After the water wars, Morrowfen became a tidal island, a hump of salt-bitten stone joined to the mainland by a causeway that vanished whenever the moon tugged hard. [28]
No one came there without wanting something buried. [36]
Elian Voss, an insomniac painter with cracked blue knuckles and winter-open eyes, wanted to thresh meaning from experience, which sounded noble until he said it before breakfast. [63]
He was wryly earnest about this habit, apologizing with a bow and adding that sleep was for people who trusted their memories. [85]
His memories did not deserve trust. [91]
