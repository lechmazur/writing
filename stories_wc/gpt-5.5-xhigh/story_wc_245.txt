When she turned it slowly, its needle pricked her skin and pointed not north, but need. [83]

No one in Low Bell crossed those trees, because their leaves sang above hearing and made ordinary thoughts crack like thin ice. [126]
Mira welcomed what could not be heard. [133]

The forest's ultrasonic speech pressed against her palms in sparks, each spark a syllable too high for ears. [176]

Mira waited until the sky held still, then pressed the platinum ring to the black soil and began to dowse. [286]

She knelt and touched the moss where the taps pointed, and the moss gave her a note like blue fire. [326]

Some notes carried Tomas's laughter; others carried his fear, which smelled of wet wool and old ink. [361]

"You found the stillpoint," he whispered. [456]
