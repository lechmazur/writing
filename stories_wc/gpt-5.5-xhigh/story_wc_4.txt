<story>The lavender field tucked behind rusted factory ruins of Vale Mill waited where rain chimed through broken vents like a secret apology. [22]
No one in town lingered there willingly, because the mill had taken three workers and left everyone else carrying unfinished goodbyes. [43]

She was perceptively calm, the sort of calm that noticed which widowers joked too brightly and which children avoided silver whistles. [86]
The case had belonged to her mother, a surveyor who measured canals, playgrounds, and once the crooked route of a runaway goat. [108]
Inside it, Mira kept tissue, transparent paper, trimming knives, and black-and-white photographs donated grudgingly from kitchen drawers and funeral boxes. [128]
She had promised the council a memorial, but privately she wanted to transmute pain into a masterpiece no one could own alone. [150]
Her method began via arcs in black-and-white photographs, though she explained it so gently that even skeptics leaned closer. [169]
In each picture, a curve appeared: a crane boom, a bent lunch pail handle, arms raised around an anniversary cake. [189]
When she overlaid the curves on the old factory map, they met beyond the loading dock, beneath the wild lavender. [209]
The meeting point was not magic, probably, but Vale Mill had always enjoyed pretending its practical things wore little hats. [229]
Mira dug there with permission, tea, and three elderly volunteers who argued over spades as if choosing violins. [247]
Under a mat of roots, they uncovered a brick channel, dry as toast, leading from the engine house to the field. [268]
Etched in the bricks were initials of workers, some dead, some old, some belonging to families who no longer spoke. [288]

Some refused, and she never pressed; her gift was to let silence settle until it opened a small side door. [346]

Widow Arun gave Mira a biscuit tin filled with pictures and said the mill had swallowed her singing. [386]
Mira placed each sorrow in the dove gray map case, not as evidence, but as seed. [402]

Those paths crossed the channel and curved outward, away from blame, toward every street in town. [437]
