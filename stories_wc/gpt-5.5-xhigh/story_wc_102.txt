After the past life memory surfaces, Mara returns to the twilight viaduct in bloom, where wisteria hangs like smoke caught in purple bells. [23]
She is a camera obscura artist, known in harbor markets for crawling inside her black tent and painting the world upside down. [45]
People hire her for likenesses, but she is spiritually curious enough to believe a face is only the porch of a soul. [67]
For years she has carried kwan yin's tear, a clear bead from a ruined shrine, mounted as the lens of her portable box. [90]
Through it, light softens, colors confess, and the breathing of her sitters changes the projected image in ways ordinary glass never allows. [112]
Mara has learned to heal by revealing through breathing changes: fear makes the picture shudder, grief dims the edges, forgiveness draws hidden lines bright. [136]
On the night the memory came, during a monastery vigil, she saw herself as a young signalman named Ren, running along this same viaduct through rain and sparks. [164]
Ren loved a flower seller named Lio but died sealing a cracked arch before he could deliver the letter in his coat. [186]
Mara woke with river mud in her mouth, Lio's name under her tongue, and one question that felt larger than death. [207]
She had come to find where unused love is stored. [217]
The viaduct now carries no trains, only walkers, vines, and the thin electric lamps that blink on at dusk. [236]
Mist lifts from the river, making every arch appear half remembered, while bees still wander the blossoms as if evening has not been announced. [260]
Under the seventh arch, where Ren's hands had packed mortar around stones, an old man is trimming moonflowers. [278]
He introduces himself as Lio Venn, keeper of the city gardens, and Mara nearly drops the camera box. [296]
"My grandmother named me for her grandmother," he says, studying her shock with kind, tired eyes. [312]
"She hated this bridge because a signalman promised to meet her here and never came." [327]
His breath catches after he says it, not dramatically, but as if a small hook has snagged inside his ribs. [347]
Mara asks whether he will sit for a portrait, and he agrees because the fog has made him lonely. [366]
She sets the tent facing the river, fixes kwan yin's tear into the aperture, and invites him into the dim red chamber. [388]
The outside world appears on the canvas wall inverted: blooming viaduct above, river below, Lio's silver head rooted in sky. [408]
"Do not pose," Mara tells him. [414]
"Just breathe as the memory asks." [420]
The first inhale turns the projected flowers gray, and his held breath darkens the seventh arch into a bruise. [439]
When he exhales, a bright seam appears between two stones, thin as a lit fuse. [454]
Mara feels Ren's panic rise, but she slows her own lungs until the image steadies. [469]
Together they follow the seam outside, Lio with his pruning knife and Mara with the box hugged to her chest. [489]
Behind a curtain of blossoms, the mortar has cracked around a rusted signal pin. [503]
Lio pries gently, and a tin packet slides out, wrapped in oilcloth that smells faintly of rain. [520]
Inside waits a letter, browned but legible, addressed to Lio's ancestor and signed by Ren's unsteady hand. [537]
It says he was not fleeing her, nor ashamed, but going under the arch to brace the failing stones before the workers' children crossed at dawn. [563]
It says love unused is not dead; it hides where courage interrupts it. [576]
Lio reads until his breath breaks, then reads again until it changes. [588]
The hook in his ribs loosens audibly, a wet softening, and color returns to his mouth. [604]
Mara sees, with foggy lucidity, that this is the healing she had sought: not a miracle imposed, but a truth making room in the body. [629]

"My grandmother used to call a soul like yours a pilgrim of sparks," Lio whispers. [666]
"Jumping from darkness to darkness, carrying warmth it never got to spend." [678]
Mara touches kwan yin's tear, and the bead is warm as skin. [690]
She understands that unused love is stored not in heaven, nor in graves, but in any place where someone still needs the story completed. [714]

"Keep painting," he says. [734]
"I think Ren finally delivered it." [740]
