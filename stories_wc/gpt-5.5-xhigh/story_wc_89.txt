After the phoenix rises, the fairy-tale orchard blooming under permanent moonlight becomes a place where promises can hear their own names again. [22]
Mara Vey comes there barefoot, carrying only a cracked bone flute, a pouch of spices, and the fortune from a cookie she has folded until it is soft as skin. [52]
People in seven villages call her a beast whisperer, but tonight every fox, dormouse, griffin, and silver cow falls silent before she speaks. [75]
They know grief by smell, and hers has ripened past sorrow into something luminous and dangerous. [91]
At the orchard center waits the phoenix, newly risen from blue ash, its feathers combing sparks through blossoms that never close. [112]
The bird was once Arel, or held Arel, or burned around the last breath of Arel; Mara no longer trusts mortal categories. [134]
He had been her beloved and her truest companion on the long roads, the one person who answered her whispers without needing fur, feather, or fang. [160]
When raiders killed him at winter dusk, she asked the beasts to carry him back, but death had already made a country of him. [184]
The fortune from the cookie had found her the next morning inside a market stall she did not remember entering. [204]
Its paper read, What love cannot follow, love must become. [214]
For three months she hated that line. [221]

To reforge a bond beyond death, the moonward dead could not simply be summoned; the living half of the bond had to change shape enough to meet them. [277]
She wanted Arel returned as he had been, laughing, scarred, smelling of rain, not herself remade into a stranger. [296]
Still, her hands were paradoxically steady as she untied the spice pouch beneath the phoenix tree. [312]

From the pouch she poured saffron red as comet dust, black cardamom from caves under the sea, and star anise harvested from a giant's wedding cake. [362]
The aromas rose in rings, and each ring carried a memory: Arel feeding apples to thundermares, Arel teaching her to dance with one boot missing, Arel dying without letting go of her sleeve. [395]
The orchard leaned inward, branch by branch, as if the universe had become a small room lit by one patient lamp. [416]
Every animal breathed with Mara. [421]
