At the summit of Mount Orrin, the celestial mapping observatory rang softly in the wind, its brass domes turning all night like patient, listening shells. [25]
Lio Vey worked there as the last lucid dream chronicler, charting not only comets and eclipses but the dreams people brought him in fear. [49]
