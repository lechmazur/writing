[19] The mysterious lighthouse on the rocky island had been her prison and her laboratory, its beam sweeping the black water while she dismantled chronology itself. [35] Tonight, under the slow exhale of low tide, the ocean retreated to reveal the hunger stone that local legend claimed had last surfaced during the famine of 1642. [58]

[84] She had spent decades reverse-engineering the puzzle of an ancient yoyo she had discovered in the lighthouse's cellar, studying how potential energy slept within the spinning disk, how descent could be transformed into ascent through stored momentum. [115] The hunger stone, she realized, was not merely a marker of starvation but a key—its grooves matching the brass axle of her yoyo artifact perfectly. [140]

Her motivation had always been singular: to step beyond fate's design. [153] The lighthouse kept time for ships, but it also trapped her in a predetermined existence, every rotation of the lens another tick toward an inevitable death she had seen inscribed in her father's water-damaged almanac. [181] Now, with the tide withdrawing its protective veil, she could initiate the sequence she had theorized but never dared attempt. [202]

[249] As the low tide completed its exhale, revealing the full expanse of rocky island normally submerged, the stone began to absorb the moonlight rather than reflect it. [276]
