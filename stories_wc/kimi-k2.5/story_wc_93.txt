[19] He had survived the Great Smoothing, that day when friction deserted the world and left every surface slick as glass. [47] His curse arrived unbidden: futures unfolded behind his eyes before they existed, each vision burning like collapsed star dust in his skull. [218] His motivation had never wavered: to understand the sacred patterns required seeing how time flowed when physical laws broke. [236] The eels knew this secret; they slid through water and air alike without resistance, tracing geometries that existed outside normal space. [275] Now he placed the final stone—a white quartz painted with spiral galaxies—at the sequence's terminus.
