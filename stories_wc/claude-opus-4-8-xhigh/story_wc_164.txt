<story>Maren was a sea-foam recollectionist, gathering forgotten moments the way tide pools gather scattered light. [15] She worked inside an iridescent cave within a giant crystal geode, where every wall threw back colors that did not quite exist. [37] Her one tool was a shard of crater glass, smooth and dark, melted long ago when something fell from the sky. [58] Pressed to the eye, the crater glass transliterates emotions, turning feeling into faint script she could read along its surface. [78] Lately, the recollections arrived broken, full of missing pieces, like songs with their middle verses torn away. [95] Her true task, the one she rarely confessed, was to discover why some paths disappear. [110] People came to her remembering a journey but not its road, a marriage but not its first hello. [128] The geode hummed with a cheerful gloom, bright walls cupping a sadness that somehow felt like company. [145] Each dusk a migrating bird passed over the geode's open crown, a thin gray traveler bound somewhere it never named. [165] As it flew, it released a handful of seeds, which fell glittering into the cave below. [181] Maren had learned that the lost fragments could be recovered via the scattered seeds dropped by a migrating bird. [200] Each seed held one missing piece, a sliver of a road that had been quietly deleted from the world. [219] She gathered them quickly, for everything had to be read before memory fades into the cave's permanent dark. [237] Tonight a fisherman had come, weeping over a daughter whose face he could picture but whose name had vanished. [256] Maren cracked the first seed and pressed the crater glass to her eye. [269] Script bloomed across the glass: a kitchen, a song, a small hand learning to braid rope. [285] But the path that linked the man to that kitchen was simply gone, a corridor with no doors. [303] She moved to conjecture, the recollectionist's last resort, weaving guesses where the truth refused to stay. [319] If the road vanished, she reasoned, then someone or something had walked it backward, unspooling it as they went. [338] She cracked another seed, and another, racing the dark, lining the cave floor with bright recovered shards. [355] Pattern by pattern, the missing pieces fit, and a single shape emerged from them all. [370] Every erased path led toward grief too heavy to carry, and the bird had been mercifully lifting those roads away. [390] It migrated not across countries but across sorrows, plucking the cruelest stretches so the people could keep walking. [408] The daughter, Maren now understood, had drowned, and the road to that knowing was the one the bird had taken. [428] Here lay the recollectionist's terrible choice, glittering on the cave floor in a hundred honest pieces. [444] She could return the road, and the name, and the full weight of the loss with it. [461] Or she could let the seeds dim and leave the fisherman his soft, nameless ache. [476] The crater glass warmed in her palm, transliterating her own divided heart into trembling, contradictory lines. [492] She thought of her own missing road, the one she had paid the bird to erase, years ago. [510] That was why some paths disappeared, she finally knew; people asked the sky to take them. [526] Maren gathered the seeds into her cupped hands and walked to the geode's bright mouth. [541] She would give the fisherman the choice she had never given herself. [553] At dawn she offered him a single seed, glowing faintly, the road folded inside like a sleeping animal. [571] He held it a long moment, then closed his fist and spoke his daughter's name aloud. [587] The remembered road rushed back into him, terrible and complete, and he wept without trying to stop. [604] Above them the migrating bird circled once, then flew on, lighter now, leaving Maren her cheerful gloom. [621]</story>
