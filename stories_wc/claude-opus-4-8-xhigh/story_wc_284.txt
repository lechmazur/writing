<story>They called me burned, which is the trade's gentle word for discarded. [12] When the agency burns a spy, it does not light a match; it simply lets the wrong people learn your name. [33] So I came alone to Zephyr Bluff, where the mist hangs like a silver hush over the sea. [51] It was the tilt of the earth toward spring, that uncertain hinge when frost loosens but does not yet release. [71] My old handler, Verrick, had taught me how the agency talked when it could not be heard. [88] Codes in accumulated snow, he called it: footprints pressed in patterns, drifts shaped by a quiet hand, messages the next thaw would erase. [111] I climbed prudently daring, hugging the rocks where the wind could not throw me, yet refusing to turn back. [130] Near the summit, in a hollow the sun had not reached, the last snow of winter lay like a thin white page. [152] Across it ran the marks I had both hoped for and dreaded. [164] Three short ridges, a swirl, a single deep print: Verrick is alive, and Verrick is sorry. [180] He was the one who had let my name slip, fixing a leak in his own career by sacrificing mine. [200] I had come not for revenge, but to learn whether what was broken between us could ever be mended. [219] A figure waited beneath the leaning pine, its hood beaded with mist. [231] Verrick looked older than the years allowed, as if guilt had aged him faster than time. [247] He held out an engraved pillbox of silver, its lid worked with the shape of a wren in flight. [266] I knew it at once; it had been my mother's, lost when the agency stripped my house bare. [284] "I kept it safe," he said, "so that one day I might vouchsafe you the truth it guards." [302] Inside, instead of pills, lay a curl of microfilm and a folded slip of paper. [317] The film named the men who had ordered my burning; the paper held my own clearance, signed and sealed. [336] "You can be restored," Verrick murmured, "but only if you carry this down alive." [350] It was not absolution he offered, but a chance to repair the world he had helped to fracture. [368] I understood then what I had truly climbed for. [377] Not the codes, not the box, but the place where my unused courage had been waiting all winter, banked like coals beneath the snow. [401] I closed the pillbox and slid it into my coat. [411] "Why now?" I asked. [415] "Because spring is coming," he said, "and the snow that carried my messages will soon be gone." [432] "Some debts must be paid before the thaw, or they vanish unpaid." [444] The mist thinned around us, and the bluff held its silver hush, as though the whole hill were listening for my answer. [466] I could have left him to the cold, turned my new courage only toward my own escape. [483] Instead I offered my arm to steady the man who had broken me. [496] Fixing what is broken, I had finally learned, sometimes means lifting the one who broke it. [512] We descended together into the loosening world, our boots breaking the soft crust at every step. [528] Below, somewhere, the men who had ordered my ruin waited to be named, and now I would name them. [547] Behind us, the last snow began to weep its codes into nothing, but I no longer needed the snow. [566] I carried the message myself, in the one place no thaw could reach: the steady, unfrozen courage I had at last climbed the bluff to claim. [592] The mist closed behind us, keeping its silver hush, and spring, for the first time in years, felt like something I deserved. [614]</story>
