<story>Mira was a budding rune scholar, quietly deliberate in every stroke she carved. [72] Mira's single obsession was to find where shadows go at noon, when they shrink to nothing and seem to vanish entirely. [93] Her teacher had vanished the same way, mid-sentence, at the stroke of twelve. [106] He had left behind one object: a clockwork automaton key, cold and intricate, its teeth shaped like tiny runes. [168] Each night she studied the key by lamplight, tracing its runes and copying them onto wax. [184] Slowly she discovered the method her teacher had favored: change came not by force but by closing loops. [227] She practiced patiently, sealing one loop after another, feeling the observatory hum in answer. [241] On the seventh night, the orrery's gears shifted, and a hidden keyhole opened in its heart. [257] Mira fitted the clockwork automaton key inside and turned it, slow and certain. [270] The brass spheres spun, the ceiling parted, and the sky filled with a model of noon, bright as a remembered day. [291] In that artificial light, every shadow in the room shrank, then folded into the keyhole itself. [307] Mira finally understood where shadows went at noon: not away, but inward, drawn into the sealed sleeping place behind the world. [328] There they waited, like everything else awaiting an awakening, for someone to close their loops. [343] Among the gathered shadows stood one shaped exactly like her teacher. [354] He had not died at noon; he had been pulled inside, mid-lesson, before he could finish teaching her. [372] Quietly deliberate, Mira lifted her stylus and began to trace the broken circle his shadow carried. [388] It was the very rune he had been drawing when reality first bent. [401] To free him she would have to relearn his unfinished thought and close the loop he never could. [449] The shadow thickened, gathered weight, and stepped forward as her teacher, blinking in the lamplight. [464] He looked older than the memory of him, yet his first words were the end of his old sentence. [483] "Always finish what you start," he murmured, finally completing the lesson the bending had interrupted. [498] Together they turned the key once more, and one by one the trapped shadows woke, returning to the people who had lost them at noon. [523] The floating isle steadied, the spoons across the worlds straightened, and reality remembered its proper shape. [539] Mira set down the clockwork automaton key, no longer a budding scholar but a true one. [555] She had learned the secret her teacher prized most: that every awakening, sealed or freed, is simply a loop waiting to be closed. [578] Below the observatory, noon arrived without stealing anyone, and the shadows stayed exactly where they belonged.
