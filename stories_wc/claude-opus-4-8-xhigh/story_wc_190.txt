<story>Caelum poled his gondola along the skyway, drawing it toward the drifting prism terrace. [14] He had ferried mourners here for thirty years, and he knew the geometry of grief. [29] The terrace hung between two cliffs, its glass panels catching the last light and splitting it into trembling colors. [48] Below, the valley railway carried its evening train, and Caelum waited for the moment he trusted most. [65] He worked only in the quiet that follows a train whistle's echo. [77] In that hush the passengers stopped weeping aloud, and silence had room to do its work. [93] At his belt swung a storm glass vial, its crystals shifting like weather caught in liquid. [109] He read it the way other men read faces, fluidly logical, translating its blooms into the mood of whoever stepped aboard. [130] Tonight a single passenger waited at the lower dock, a girl no older than twelve, clutching a folded ticket. [149] She was in transit between the orphanage she had left and the aunt she had never met. [166] Her noise was wrong; she chattered, laughed too sharply, filling every gap with sound. [180] Caelum's craft depended on monitoring noise levels, and hers told him she was terrified of the quiet itself. [198] The storm glass clouded, dense and gray, confirming the storm she carried inside. [211] He helped her aboard and poled them out over the gulf, saying nothing. [224] "Why won't you talk?" she demanded, her voice cracking against the vaulting hush of the terrace. [240] He had learned long ago why silence frightened the grieving. [250] When his own wife died, the quiet had felt like punishment, an empty room shouting her absence. [267] So he had made it his work to teach silence how to comfort, to soften its edges until it held people instead of accusing them. [292] The train whistle rose from the valley, long and silver, then faded. [304] In the quiet that followed, Caelum uncorked the storm glass vial. [315] He had discovered, years before, that the open vial could catalyze a change in the air around it. [333] The crystals, exposed, drew the noise from a person and rendered it visible, blooming in patterns only stillness could complete. [353] He set it on the rail between them and waited. [363] The girl quieted, watching the cloudy fluid begin to clear. [373] As her breathing slowed, the crystals untangled, growing into delicate ferns of frost that climbed the glass. [390] The terrace itself seemed to drift higher on the silence, its prisms scattering the dusk into a slow rain of color. [411] "It's beautiful," she whispered, and for the first time her voice was small without being afraid. [427] Caelum nodded, and let the quiet answer for him. [436] The vial had not changed her; it had only shown her that stillness could be full instead of empty. [455] She studied the frost-ferns, then the colors falling around them, and slowly she stopped trying to fill the air. [474] The silence settled over them like a blanket warmed by a body, and Caelum recognized the comfort he had spent a lifetime teaching it to give. [500] He poled toward the far cliff where the aunt would be waiting. [512] The girl gathered the vial gently and held it out to him. [524] "Keep it," he said, breaking his own silence only once. [534] "You're still in transit," he added. [540] "You'll need to read your own weather." [547] She took it, and the storm glass, cradled in her warm hands, stayed perfectly clear. [562] At the dock a woman stood, hands trembling, and the girl stepped off without a word, carrying her quiet with her. [583] Caelum watched the reunion fold into stillness, two strangers learning the same lesson he had learned. [599] Then he turned his gondola back toward the drifting terrace, alone again in the vaulting hush, content. [616]</story>
