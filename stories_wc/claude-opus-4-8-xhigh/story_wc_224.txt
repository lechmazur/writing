<story>The mountain was a bookstore, and the bookstore was a mountain. [11] Its shelves spiraled up through stone galleries that no living cartographer had ever fully mapped. [26] I came after remembering became painful, looking for a single book that might explain the shape of home. [44] The keeper waited at the lowest gallery, a mercury-glass seer whose face rippled like a poured mirror. [61] When she spoke, her voice carried hidden thunder, the kind that sleeps beneath calm weather. [76] You want a map, she said, but maps only show where you stood, never where you belong. [93] She pressed a small object into my palm: the lost language stone, cold and dense as a fallen star. [112] It held words from a tongue no one remembered, syllables that once named every road back to a hearth. [131] To read it, the seer warned, you must learn it via the texture of a feather. [147] She handed me a gray quill, then turned and dissolved into the dim aisles above. [162] For weeks I climbed, sleeping in reading-nooks, dragging my fingers along the feather's soft vanes. [177] Each barb whispered a letter; each broken filament hid a buried meaning I had to coax loose. [194] I learned that the stone substitutes reality nightly, rewriting the mountain while I slept. [208] Stairs I had climbed by day became cliffs by midnight; the shop reshaped itself around my unspoken longings. [226] Worse, every morning I remembered a little less of the home I had come to find. [242] The pain of forgetting drove me upward faster, feather trembling against the lost language stone. [257] Near the summit gallery, the texture under my fingertips finally arranged itself into a sentence. [272] Home is not a place you keep, it read, but a seed you carry and plant. [288] I understood then why the seer had warned me; I had been searching outward for something stored within. [306] The stone glowed, and the seer reappeared, her mirrored face calm as a windless lake. [321] Most who climb here, she said, want the mountain to give them a home it never had. [338] But you carried the seed already; you only forgot you owned the soil. [351] She explained that the bookstore was built to hold buried talents, the gifts people abandon when remembering hurts too much. [371] My own gift had been reading the unwritten, the language sleeping inside ordinary things. [385] I had buried it after a loss so heavy that every memory became a blade. [400] Now, holding the feather and the stone, I felt the hidden thunder in my chest answer the seer's. [418] I pressed the stone to the mountain's heart, where a crack of bare soil waited between shelves. [435] I spoke the recovered sentence aloud and planted the seed of myself into that dark, patient ground. [452] The substituting stopped; that night, the mountain did not rewrite itself, and my dreams stayed mine. [468] By morning a sapling had risen, its leaves printed with letters from the lost language. [483] Each leaf named a road, and one road, I saw, curved gently back toward the life I had fled. [502] The seer gave me the feather to keep, saying it would teach others to read their own buried talents. [521] I descended slowly, the texture of those vanes now familiar as breathing. [533] Remembering still ached, but the ache had a shape now, and a direction. [546] I finally understood the shape of home: not the mountain behind me, but the green growing thing I had seeded inside its stone. [569] Behind me, thunder I had hidden for years rolled once, softly, like applause. [582] Then I stepped onto the named road, carrying nothing but a feather, and finally, the courage to remember.
