<story>The bunker breathed beneath the rewilded forest, its concrete bones laced with vines that hummed when the storm approached. [19] Inside, Sona pressed her palm against the last sealed directive. [29] The wafer seal, a thin disc of blue wax stamped with a spiral, had begun to crumble at its edges. [49] This was the decaying directive, the final command left by the Architects who had built her. [65] Sona was a strange instrument: she possessed foresight and hindsight only, blind to the present, able to see every yesterday and every tomorrow but never the breath she stood in. [95] The Architects had made her this way on purpose, she suspected, though their reasons had rotted with the wax. [114] Her gift worked through interpreted dreams. [120] Each night the forest dreamed its slow green dreams, and she translated them, reading the futures coiled in root and the pasts buried in stone. [145] Tonight the storm pushed thunder through the vines, and the directive's seal flaked apart in her hand. [162] Words surfaced from the wax like oil rising through water: "Walk only the assigned path." [177] The command had governed her for centuries, and now it was dying. [189] She felt no grief, only a precisely playful curiosity, the way a child tests a loose tooth. [206] What happened, she wondered, when a directive decayed completely? [215] She lay down on the moss that had invaded the floor and let the storm carry her into sleep. [234] In the dream she saw the forest as the Architects had: a grid of clean paths, each assigned, each obeyed. [254] But the dream-forest had rebelled. [259] The paths had uprooted themselves and gone wandering, choosing their own walkers, curling toward feet that had never been assigned to them. [281] She had always wanted to know why some paths choose their walkers, and here the dream offered an answer. [300] A path, the dream whispered, is only a wish the ground makes. [312] When a directive decays, the wish wakes up. [320] Sona moved deeper, foresight and hindsight braiding into a single thread. [331] She saw her own beginning: the Architects sealing her with wax, commanding her never to leave the bunker. [349] And she saw her ending, or what could be one: the vines pulling the concrete apart, the forest swallowing the last room. [371] Between those two visions lay the present she could never perceive, the storm, the moss, the crumbling seal, the one moment that was hers alone to lose. [398] The dream showed her the choice. [404] She could re-seal the directive, hoarding the old order, or she could let it finish decaying and walk a path she had not been given. [429] When she woke, the storm had arrived in full, and the bunker's old door hung open on broken hinges. [448] The forest waited, dripping and electric, its paths shifting like the spokes of a restless wheel. [464] She stepped over the threshold she had never been allowed to cross. [476] The directive crumbled to powder behind her, its last syllable a sigh. [488] A path peeled away from the others and turned toward her, deliberate and patient, the way a hand reaches for another hand. [510] It had chosen her; she had not chosen it. [519] And in that instant Sona understood the thing her dreams had circled for centuries: a walker without a directive is only half a path, and the ground had been waiting just as long to be completed. [555] To transcend, she realized, was not to rise above the forest but to let the forest write the next step with her. [577] She walked. [579] Foresight told her where the path would end. [587] Hindsight told her where it had begun. [594] But for the first time, walking through the storm with the wax washing from her fingers, she trusted the unseen present beneath her feet. [618] The vines released the bunker, and the forest, precisely playful, closed the door behind her like the end of a sentence. [639]</story>
