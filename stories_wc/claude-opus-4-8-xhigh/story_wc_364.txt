<story>Eldas was a meek blacksmith, the kind who apologized to his own anvil. [13] He lived on Pomarin, an orchard planet riddled with floating rock arches that hung like broken halos over dead fields. [33] Once the arches had cradled apple light; now they cradled only dust and the memory of harvests. [50] The orchards had collapsed into rubble after the great quake, and most farmers had fled to the orbital docks. [69] Eldas stayed, quietly determined to coax resilience from rubble. [78] His secret was a length of magician's silk, blue as a defiant noon, traded years ago for the last of his iron. [100] The silk performed bartered awakenings: lay it over something sleeping, name what you would surrender, and the dormant thing would stir. [121] Every awakening cost a memory, and Eldas had been paying in pieces. [133] He worked the abandoned mine shafts beneath the orchards, where the old quake had buried the deepest roots. [151] In those tunnels, the rubble was warm, and the silk hummed against fractured stone. [165] Each night he descended with a lantern and laid the cloth across a seam of broken loam. [182] His method was deliberate, never greedy, because the silk punished haste with confusion. [195] Eldas lived between understanding and confusion now, his past thinning each time he bartered. [209] He had forgotten his mother's face to wake a single seedling. [220] He had surrendered the smell of rain to revive a fallen pear tree. [233] Still he climbed back up, and still he hammered nothing on his cold anvil, listening for green sounds. [251] One morning a girl arrived from the docks, sent to collect the abandoned forge for scrap. [267] She found Eldas asleep, the blue silk folded in his lap like a captured sky. [282] When she shook him, he could not remember the word for hello. [294] Yet he smiled and led her down the mine shafts, lantern trembling. [306] There, the deepest seam pulsed with stubborn roots he had woken one memory at a time. [322] Tendrils had threaded the rubble into a living lattice, refusing to die. [334] He pressed the silk into her hands and whispered the only barter he had left. [349] He would surrender his name so the whole orchard might wake at once. [362] The girl protested, but his eyes held that azure defiance, calm and unbreakable. [375] She laid the cloth over the trembling lattice, and he spoke. [386] His name left him like a last spark leaving a dying coal. [398] Above them, the rubble groaned, and the floating arches drank a sudden flood of green. [413] Branches climbed the broken halos, blossoms bursting against the azure morning. [424] The orchard planet remembered itself, fruit swelling where only dust had been. [436] When the light settled, the blacksmith stood blinking, a man without a past. [449] He did not know the girl, nor the silk, nor why he wept. [462] But the girl took his hand and led him into the sunlight. [474] Farmers were already returning, drawn by the impossible green spreading across the arches. [487] They asked who had done this, and the girl pointed to the meek man at her side. [504] He had no name to give them, so they gave him one. [516] They called him Orchard, and they handed him a hammer to begin again. [529] He took it, quietly determined, though he could not say why the weight felt like home. [545] The silk lay spent in the dirt, just blue cloth now, its magic bartered to the last. [562] Orchard picked it up, deliberate and gentle, and tied it to the tallest floating arch. [577] It snapped like a banner over the reborn fields, a flag of azure defiance against forgetting. [593] He did not remember the cost, but the planet did, and the planet bloomed. [607]</story>
