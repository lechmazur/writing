<story>In the canyon of endless echoes, towers of bone rose like the ribs of a buried god. [17] Between them drifted a pale spire in mist, a needle of white stone the echoes refused to name. [35] Mara, a defiant artist exiled here for painting forbidden faces, climbed toward it before the ritual could begin. [53] The priests had promised forgiveness, but forgiveness, she knew, always carried a price. [66] Tonight that price was her memory: to be absolved, she must surrender the single truth she carried. [83] That truth was a name, the name of the child the priests had let the canyon swallow. [100] She refused to let it die in a single throat. [110] So she had a plan, born of her craft: to refract a single truth into many, until no ritual could gather them all. [133] At the foot of the pale spire she found Tomas, a boy left to tend the lamps, innocently mischievous, flicking pebbles at the sleeping echoes. [158] Each pebble woke a whisper that the bone towers caught and refracted into a hundred fainter voices. [175] Mara watched the trick and understood her instrument. [183] "Help me," she told the boy, "and we will make the stones remember a song." [198] Through impromptu poetry sessions they worked the long evening, Mara speaking lines and Tomas hurling them at the towers like stones. [219] She buried the forbidden name inside rhyme, so that the canyon learned it without knowing what it held. [237] Each verse struck the bone and split, and every fragment carried one syllable of the truth. [253] The forged hush of the place, that silence hammered thin by centuries of priestly law, began to fill with murmurs. [273] Tomas laughed, delighted, never guessing he was smuggling a dead child's name into eternity. [287] By moonrise the pale spire in mist glowed, every echo bent and scattered, the truth refracted beyond recall. [305] Then the priests came with their lanterns and their ledger of pardons. [317] Their elder offered the absolution: forget the name, and walk free of the canyon forever. [332] Mara knelt and let them take the memory from her tongue. [343] She paid the price of forgiveness; the name dissolved inside her, leaving only a clean and aching blank. [361] The elder smiled, certain the silence was restored. [369] But the canyon would not hush now. [376] From a thousand throats of bone the syllables returned, recombining, until the towers themselves spoke the child's name aloud. [395] The priests struck the stone, but you cannot unsay an echo. [406] What one mouth had carried, the whole canyon now refused to release. [418] Mara, emptied and forgiven, did not remember why she wept. [428] Yet some defiant root in her recognized the song the stones were singing. [441] Beside her, Tomas grinned and threw one last pebble, and the pale spire rang like a struck bell. [459] The elder turned on Mara, his pardon curdling into rage. [469] "You were forgiven," he said, "and still you defied us." [479] "I forgave nothing," she answered, though she could not recall the wound she meant. [493] The name was gone from her, yes, but she had refracted it into the world, and the world would keep it. [514] That was the true bargain she had made: not to keep the truth, but to set it free. [532] The price of forgiveness had bought her silence, and her silence had bought the canyon's voice. [548] Lanterns guttered as the priests fled the towers of bone, chased by the name they had tried to bury. [567] Mara stood in the forged hush that was a hush no longer, and felt strangely whole. [583] She did not know the child anymore, but every echo did, and that was enough. [598] Tomas tugged her sleeve, asking for another rhyme, and the artist, smiling at last, began to refract one more truth into the singing dark. [622]</story>
