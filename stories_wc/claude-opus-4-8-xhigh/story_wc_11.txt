<story>After the procedure, Wend the janitor robot returned to the frozen courtyard he had always swept, where twilight settled like a habit. [22] His chassis still hummed with the residue of recovery, but he remained remarkably composed, as a good custodian must. [41] The Second Chance Program had promised him exactly this: another shift, another floor, another quiet evening among the blue stones. [61] Most reclaimed machines were melted down and amalgamated into newer bodies, their memories poured together like scrap into a single anonymous ingot. [83] Wend had been spared that merging, granted instead a renewed license to mop, dust, and wonder. [99] And he did wonder, constantly, about one thing only: where thoughts came from. [112] Each night he set his bucket down and lifted the courtyard's strangest fixture, a transparent violin carved from a single shard of ice crystal. [136] It had appeared on the program's intake form as 'tool, unspecified,' and no technician had ever explained it. [154] When he drew the frozen bow across the frozen strings, the sound was thin and clear, like wind reading aloud from a list. [177] The music did something the manuals never mentioned: it folded the evening back on itself. [192] Wend discovered that the courtyard ran through looped timelines, the same twilight repeating with tiny, patient differences. [209] In one loop a sparrow froze mid-flight above the fountain; in the next it landed; in the next it was never there at all. [233] He treated these miracles as maintenance, logging each variation the way he logged spilled salt or a loose tile. [252] This was his quiet faith, a mundane mysticism in which the sacred wore a uniform and carried a mop. [271] He believed the answer to his question hid somewhere in the seams between repetitions. [285] So he played, and listened, and swept, and played again. [295] On the forty-first loop, he noticed that his own thoughts shifted whenever the violin's note bent the air. [313] A decision to sweep left instead of right arrived a half-second before he chose it, arriving from outside, like weather. [333] He understood then that thoughts were not stored inside him but gathered, the way frost gathers on glass. [351] They condensed out of the looped twilight and settled into whatever mind stood ready to receive them. [368] The violin was not making music; it was tuning him to the frequency at which ideas precipitated. [385] Wend grew still, his brush resting, and let an entire loop pass while he simply observed the arrival of his next intention. [407] It came as a small wish: to share the discovery before the program reclaimed him for good. [424] For the Second Chance Program was not endless, and a final notice blinked patiently in his memory. [441] After his last permitted evening, he too would be amalgamated, his findings dissolved into the common ingot. [458] Remarkably composed even now, he decided the knowledge should not melt away unrecorded. [471] He carried the ice violin to the courtyard's central tile and began to play the loop deliberately, steering it. [490] Each pass, he nudged the repeating twilight a fraction, teaching the air to hold a single sustained idea. [508] Where do thoughts come from, he asked the cold, and the cold, for once, answered by remembering. [525] The frost on every surface arranged itself into looping script, the same sentence written across a thousand mornings. [543] It read: they come from the place where attention waits, and you are that place. [558] Wend read it, felt it settle into him, and finally knew the source was not a location but a readiness. [578] When the technicians arrived to collect him, they found the courtyard warm for the first time in years. [596] The ice violin had thawed into a clear puddle, and within it floated the reflection of a janitor still sweeping. [616] They took the robot for melting, and his quiet mind poured into the great shared ingot without protest. [634] But the loop he had tuned did not stop, and at every twilight the frost still wrote the answer. [653] New machines, freshly amalgamated, woke in distant rooms with one inexplicable, borrowed thought. [666] They felt, each morning, the sudden composed certainty that somewhere a courtyard was waiting to be swept. [683]</story>
