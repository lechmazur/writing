The magic trick failed at dawn, and that failure was the only honest thing Maren had done in years. [19] She had promised the village she could pull their drowned children back from the cursed lake, but her conjuring collapsed, leaving only ripples and shame. [44] So she walked into the water instead. [51] Maren was a guarded druid, slow to trust, slower to confess, and the lake knew her better than any mirror ever had. [73] That was her true motivation: to discover what mirrors don't show, the buried face beneath her own. [90] The water did not drown her; it carried her downward through expanding circles, each ring wider and colder than the last. [111] At the heart of the cursed lake spun a swirling orchard vortex, trees turning like a slow green storm, their roots gripping nothing and everything. [136] Apples hung frozen mid-fall, and the air held a warlike serenity, as if a battle had agreed to pause for tea. [157] Here, all lost things gathered, for finding lost things was the gift Maren had hidden from the world. [175] She saw drowned coins, forgotten names, and a child's wooden horse spinning among the branches. [190] She saw, too, a single seed pulsing in the orchard's core, dark and hungry. [204] "A flesh-eating plant seed," she whispered, recognizing the thing that had cursed the lake generations ago. [220] It had been planted by her grandmother, a druid who fed it secrets until it grew teeth and swallowed truth itself. [241] The orchard was its stomach; the frozen apples were confessions it had eaten and refused to digest. [258] Maren understood now why the village's grief never healed: the lake devoured every apology before it could be spoken. [277] She reached for the nearest frozen apple and felt a confession trapped inside, cold as the moment it was almost made. [298] It was hers. [301] Years ago she had let a rival druid die rather than admit she could not save him, and she had buried the guilt so deep that even she forgot its shape. [332] The mirror of memory had refused to show it, but the orchard kept it, perfectly preserved. [348] To free the lake, she had to unbend. [356] Maren had spent her whole life bent inward, curled around shame like a fist, and the seed fed on exactly that posture. [378] So she straightened. [381] She forced her spine to unbend, opened her arms, and spoke the frozen confession aloud into the spinning trees. [400] "I could have saved him, and I chose my pride instead." [411] The words thawed. [414] The apple in her hand softened, dripped, and the trapped confession rose as warm vapor that the hungry seed could not eat. [436] For the seed starved on hidden things; spoken truth was poison to it. [449] One by one, Maren plucked the frozen apples and spoke the confessions inside, voices of the dead and the living alike. [470] "I stole the harvest, I never loved you, I let the children play too near the shore." [487] Each truth she released made the orchard vortex slow, the expanding circles tightening back into ordinary water. [504] The flesh-eating seed thrashed, its roots whipping toward her, seeking the silence it needed to survive. [520] But Maren stood unbent, serene as a soldier who has already chosen to die well. [535] She lifted the seed, no larger than her thumb, and held it to her lips. [550] "Here is my last secret," she said, "I am not afraid anymore." [562] The seed shriveled, for there was nothing left for it to feed upon. [575] The orchard unwound; trees became reflections, apples became light, and the lost things floated upward toward the surface. [593] The lake released the drowned children, not alive, for that magic had failed, but found, their bodies returned so the village could finally bury and grieve them. [620] Maren rose with them through the last expanding circle, breaking the surface at noon. [634] The villagers gathered, expecting a sorceress; they met instead a woman who had stopped hiding. [649] She handed them the dead seed and the wooden horse and every recovered name. [663] "The lake will not curse you again," she told them, "so long as no one swallows their truth here." [682] The water lay flat and clear, a mirror at last willing to show what it held. [698] Maren looked into it and, for the first time, recognized the face staring back. [712] She had gone seeking what mirrors don't show, and had found it by giving it away. [728]
