<story>The feral library guardian had not slept in a hundred years, yet she dreamed without end. [16] She prowled the stacks on all fours, her spine arched like a question no one would answer. [33] Beyond the last shelf lay a frozen garden in mist, where statues of forgotten readers waited under glassy frost. [52] She was dimensionally homesick, aching for a country that survived only as a smell and a slant of impossible light. [72] Her work was to wait, for the library kept its own slow patience, and she had learned to interiorize exteriors. [92] By drawing the cold garden inward, she made a private weather of fog and lucidity inside her ribs. [110] Tonight the veil of sleep parted, and a man fell through it onto the marble floor. [126] He had crossed from a waking world into hers, still clutching a cultist's pendant in his trembling fist. [144] The pendant was a black disc rimmed with silver, engraved with the boundaries of light, the lines where brightness was forbidden to pass. [167] She circled him, low and bristling, scenting fear and old incense. [178] His sect had taught him to fear the dark between dimensions, to seal every uncertainty behind ritual lines. [196] But the pendant had dragged him here instead, to the one place where light and shadow were still negotiating their treaty. [217] "Where am I?" he whispered, his breath fogging in the garden's spilling cold. [230] "Between," she rasped, her voice unused, rusted at the hinges. [240] He flinched from her, then from the statues, then from the silence itself. [253] She understood him at once, for she too had once tried to wall the world out. [269] The library had cured her of that, teaching her instead to make friends with uncertainty. [284] "Your pendant," she said, "draws hard lines around the light, but light is a friend, not a fence." [302] He looked at the disc, at the boundaries his sect had carved into it, and his certainty wavered. [320] She did not pounce; she had learned to wait, the way the frozen garden waited for a spring that came in centuries. [342] Together they sat among the misted hedges, and she taught him her one trick. [356] She showed him how to interiorize exteriors, to take the vast cold inside and let it become a quiet room. [376] The fog in his mind cleared into that strange foggy lucidity she lived within, where nothing was certain and nothing was feared. [398] He confessed, then, that he too was dimensionally homesick, that no world had ever felt like his own. [416] Maybe, she offered, home was not a place but a way of holding the unknown without clenching. [433] He turned the pendant over, and with her claw she scratched a single gap into its perfect ring of boundaries. [453] Through that small opening, a thread of garden light slipped in, and the frost began, very slowly, to glisten. [472] The statues did not move, but they seemed less like prisoners now and more like patient guests. [489] When the veil of sleep began to draw him back, the man did not resist. [504] "Come visit," she said, "the door is only a thought wide." [515] He promised, and he left the pendant in her keeping, a gift and a question both. [531] Alone again, the feral guardian wore the broken ring against her chest and felt the cold garden breathing inside her. [551] She was still homesick, still uncertain, but the uncertainty had a face now, and a name, and a promise to return. [572] So she did the thing she did best, the thing the whole library was built to do. [589] She curled beneath the misted shelves, between the boundaries of light, and began, contentedly, to wait.
