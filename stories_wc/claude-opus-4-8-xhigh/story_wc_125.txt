<story>After the energy beings arrived, the roads emptied of everyone but me. [12] I am a drifting roadside poet, and I had learned that wandering was the only honest profession left. [30] The luminous travelers did not conquer; they simply offered the texture of time, a gift I could not stop craving. [50] They claimed that time had layers, like lacquer, and that a patient soul might peel them back and taste each one. [71] To discover the texture of time became my whole motive for walking. [83] They told me the lessons waited in a terra-cotta grotto beneath the desert stars. [97] I carried only one possession of weight: a frayed coil of rope. [109] Years ago that rope had lowered me out of a prison window, knot by knot, into a freedom I never fully trusted. [131] I had written every poem since on the strands of that escape, unable to forget the men I left behind. [151] When I reached the grotto, starlight poured through its mouth and lit the orange clay like a slow ember. [170] An energy being waited inside, a shimmering lattice that hummed with an ethically playful warmth. [185] It did not demand; it teased, daring me to earn my answer by uncovering layers of my own past. [204] Lay the rope upon the floor, it murmured, and read the pattern your life has braided into it. [222] I obeyed, coiling the frayed line across the terra-cotta in a spiral that caught the starlight. [238] Each fray, the being said, is a moment you chose yourself over another. [251] I touched the first frayed spot and felt the texture of that night flood back. [266] There had been only enough rope for one descent before a guard would find it. [281] Impossible choices had stacked themselves like the grotto's strata: save myself, or wait and free a friend who might be caught beside me. [304] I had chosen myself, and the texture of that choice was rough, eternal, unkind. [318] The being glowed brighter, neither praising nor condemning, only inviting me deeper. [330] Now choose again, it whispered, and offered me the impossible once more. [342] I could keep walking the world to taste time forever, or I could feed the rope into its light and relive the escape, this time waiting for my friend. [371] One path promised endless wonder; the other promised a single redemption I could never confirm had worked. [388] The grotto held its breath; the stars pulsed in their ancient pattern overhead. [401] I understood then that the texture of time was not a flavor to collect but a weight to carry. [420] I lifted the frayed coil and pressed it into the shimmering lattice. [432] Light unspooled through every fiber, peeling back the years by uncovering layers I had buried beneath my wandering songs. [451] I stood again at the prison window, knot by knot, the cold air sharp against my hands. [468] This time I waited, gripping the rope until my friend's frightened face rose into the dark. [484] We dropped together, the frayed line screaming, and we both struck the ground breathing. [498] Whether the memory was true or merely the being's playful mercy, I would never know. [513] When the grotto returned, the rope was gone, and so was its hold on me. [528] The energy being thinned into a quiet glow, satisfied, ethically content with the choice it had only ever offered, never forced. [549] I walked back into the starlight, no longer a drifting poet of regret but of release. [565] I had wanted to taste time and instead had learned its truest texture: the grain of a choice you finally let yourself make. [588] The roads were still empty, but the pattern of my steps had changed. [601]</story>
