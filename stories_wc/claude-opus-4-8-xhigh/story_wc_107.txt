<story>The ice was melting when Soren returned to the abandoned control room, his furnace already glowing behind him. [18] Once this room had governed a dam, its dead consoles staring out over a valley now loud with thaw. [37] Soren was a contemplative glassblower, and silence suited him the way water suits a riverbed. [52] He set his tools on a console, tucking a cracked leather bookmark beside them like an old friend. [70] The bookmark had belonged to his sister, who once read aloud here before the engineers fled the rising water. [89] She had asked too many questions for the men who ran the dam, and they had taught her silence instead. [109] So Soren came each spring to honor her by building what she never had: a safe zone for inquiry. [128] He lit the furnace and began, as always, to narrate. [138] "Behold," he announced to the empty dials, "the gathering of the molten gather upon the blowpipe!" [154] His voice filled the room with weighty lightness, turning a simple chore into a small, sincere theater. [171] "Witness now the turning," he proclaimed, rolling the glob so it would not droop, "lest gravity claim its prize." [190] This was his method, thoughtfully spirited, for keeping despair from settling in the cold corners. [205] Outside, the glaciers wept down the mountain, and meltwater hissed against the warm stone of his workbench. [222] He blew gently, and a bubble swelled into the dim air like a held breath learning to release. [240] These globes were his safe zones, transparent domes under which a person might sit and ask anything at all. [259] He placed each finished sphere along the consoles, until the dead controls glittered with fragile, questioning light. [276] For years he had told himself this ritual was enough, that grief could be tended like a furnace. [314] That afternoon a girl wandered in, drawn by the firelight, mud on her boots from the melting trail. [332] She studied the glowing spheres, then asked the question his sister had been forbidden: "Why did everyone leave?" [488] Soren laughed, surprised by the sound, and the abandoned control room seemed suddenly, stubbornly alive. [503] He understood now that his glass was never meant to preserve grief, but to shelter wonder. [519] Outside the ice kept melting, unbothered, indifferent, and somehow generous in its surrender. [532] Inside, he gathered another gather, lifted the blowpipe, and resumed his grand narration.
