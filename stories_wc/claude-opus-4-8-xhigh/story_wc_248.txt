<story>They called me the lending library arsonist, and the name still burns when I whisper it. [16] I set that fire to erase a promise I had broken, then I vanished, going off the grid into mountains no map admitted. [39] But promises, like embers, refuse to die quietly. [47] So I came here, to the lost civilization archive buried beneath the ash plains, where my friend Saro had begged me, years ago, to follow her one day. [75] She had vowed to preserve a dying culture's memory, and I had vowed to help her; instead, I had burned the very building that held her research. [102] Now Saro was gone, and only her trail of artistic creations remained, a deliberate path she had laid for the one mind clever enough to read it. [129] I am analytically intuitive, a man who reads patterns the way others read faces, and Saro had counted on that gift. [150] The first creation waited at the archive's mouth: a faded tapestry fragment pinned to stone, its threads spelling a direction only I would recognize. [174] I traced its frayed weave with shaking fingers and felt the volcano of my guilt soften into something almost like prayer. [195] Deeper in, lamplight revealed murals she had painted to portray the lost people: their festivals, their farewells, their long migration toward the sea. [218] Each scene pointed onward, a brushstroke arrow, a hidden grammar of color guiding me toward the heart of the ruin. [238] I followed the trail with tears stinging, decoding the rhythm of her images, until the corridor opened into a circular vault. [259] There hung her final work: a vandalized self-portrait, the painted Saro slashed across the eyes, as if she had wounded her own likeness on purpose. [284] I nearly broke, thinking vandals had reached this sanctuary, but my analytic instinct caught the truth beneath the gashes. [303] The cuts were precise, intentional, forming letters where the canvas peeled back to expose words written underneath. [320] She had defaced her own face to plant a hidden message, knowing only my eyes would search past the ruin for meaning. [342] I leaned close and read: If you came, then you kept your word at last. [357] Below that line, instructions led to a sealed alcove where she had hidden the true archive long before my fire could reach it. [380] I pried the seal loose and found scrolls, recordings, and woven records of the lost civilization, every fragment she had saved from my flames. [404] She had forgiven me before I had even begged, trusting that the man who burned books might one day rescue a forgotten promise. [427] The volcano in my chest erupted, not with rage now, but with a tenderness so fierce it left me kneeling among her treasures. [450] I understood the cruelty of her riddle at last: she had made me wound my own pride along the path, so that finding her trust would burn brighter than my shame. [481] I gathered the tapestry fragment, the scrolls, and the slashed portrait, and I carried them back toward the world I had fled. [503] I would rebuild the lending library I had razed, and fill its shelves with everything Saro had entrusted to my flawed hands. [525] On its first wall I would hang her portrait, wounds and all, and beneath it I would write the promise I finally meant to keep. [550] The arsonist would become a keeper of flames meant only to give light. [563] And somewhere in the ash, I swear the lost civilization stirred, as if a forgotten people had been promised, at last, that they would not be forgotten. [590] I lit a single candle and began to read aloud, so the silence would learn their names again. [608]</story>
