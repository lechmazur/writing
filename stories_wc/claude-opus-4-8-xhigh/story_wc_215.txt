<story>The recluse had outlived the bees, the orchard, and nearly her reason for staying. [14] Her home was an abandoned beekeeping oasis stranded in a scorched wasteland, where bleached hive-boxes leaned like tombstones over cracked irrigation channels. [36] For years she had spoken to no one, and no one had come. [49] Then ghosts became visible. [53] It happened gradually, the way dusk gathers—first a shimmer at the well, then a translucent figure standing among the dead lavender. [75] She was not afraid; she had always been tranquilly perceptive, content to watch. [88] The dead could not speak, she learned, but they lingered with purpose. [100] She read them through contextual clues: the direction of a gaze, the tilt of a shoulder, the way a phantom hand hovered over a particular hive. [126] Most drifted on within a day or two. [134] One did not. [137] The woman who appeared at her threshold wore the same sun-faded apron Maren herself owned, and the recognition struck her like cold water. [160] It was her sister, Ysolde, dead twenty years, returned without a word. [172] Maren stood astonished, the long-buried grief surfacing all at once. [182] They had built this oasis together, two girls coaxing bees from a dying land, bound by a promise neither had ever spoken aloud. [205] That promise had tangled itself into a covenant: to keep the hives alive, and to keep certain words forever unsaid. [225] The night before Ysolde left for the cities and never returned, they had quarreled in silence, each refusing to be the first to speak. [249] Maren had sealed her unsaid apology inside a stained glass box, vowing to open it only when her sister came home. [270] Now her sister had come home, and the box waited on the mantel, its amber and cobalt panes still catching the cruel desert light. [294] Ysolde's ghost gazed at the box, then at Maren, and the meaning needed no translation. [309] Open it, the gaze seemed to plead. [316] But Maren hesitated, gripped by the very motivation that had ruled her hermit years: to preserve the weight of unspoken words. [337] If she broke the silence now, she feared the covenant between them would dissolve, and her sister would have no reason to stay. [360] So they lived together in that mute eloquence, the living woman and the dead one, communicating only through small gestures. [380] Ysolde would point to the empty hives; Maren would understand, and clean them. [393] Maren would set out two cups of water, and Ysolde would bow her head, grateful for the ritual if not the drink. [415] Days folded into weeks, and the recluse grew strangely whole, as though presence alone could mend a sundered thing. [434] Yet she noticed her sister fading, the cobalt of her eyes thinning toward gray. [448] Through contextual clues again—the lengthening of Ysolde's gaze toward the horizon, the way she pressed her transparent palm to the sealed box—Maren understood the truth. [475] The unspoken words were not a tether; they were a debt. [486] Ysolde had returned not to remain, but to be released. [496] The covenant they had tangled in pride could only be untied by the very confession Maren had hoarded. [514] On the last evening, when the wasteland burned orange and the ghost was little more than a held breath, Maren lifted the stained glass box. [539] Her hands trembled as she opened the lid for the first time in twenty years. [554] Inside lay a single dried sprig of lavender and a slip of paper bearing two words: I'm sorry. [572] She did not need to read them aloud; she had only to let them exist in the open air. [591] Ysolde's gaze softened, and a slow, weightless smile crossed her vanishing face. [603] The covenant loosened; the unspoken finally spoken by being shown. [613] As the last light failed, the ghost dissolved into the warm dark, leaving behind a faint hum—the sound, Maren was certain, of bees returning. [638] In the morning, a single living blossom had opened beside the well. [650] The recluse knelt before it, tranquil and perceptive as ever, and understood that some words weigh the same whether spoken or simply set free. [674]</story>
