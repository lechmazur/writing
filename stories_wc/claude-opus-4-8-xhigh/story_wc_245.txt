<story>Mira had never seen the ultrasonic forest, but she could feel it singing against her teeth. [16] She was a mapmaker who drew worlds she would never witness, blind since birth, guided by stranger senses. [34] Her skin absorbed languages the way roots drink rain. [43] When she pressed her palm to bark, the forest's ultrasonic chatter seeped through her pores and unfolded into words she suddenly understood. [65] On her finger she wore a platinum compass ring, its needle warm, restless, alive. [79] It had belonged to her mother, who had vanished into these trees a decade ago, chasing what she called the hidden stillpoint. [101] The stillpoint, her mother's letters insisted, was a place where all the world's grief held its breath. [118] Mira had come to find it, not to map the land, but to navigate the uncharted emotional depths her mother had drowned in. [141] She wanted to understand the woman who had loved a silence more than her own daughter. [157] The compass ring did not point north; it pointed toward feeling. [168] So Mira raised her hand and began to dowse, sweeping the air, letting the needle tug her between the humming trunks. [189] The ground answered in vibrations she catalogued like coastlines. [198] Soon she noticed the notes. [203] Scattered through the canopy hung a trail of musical notes, single tones pressed into the air like dewdrops, each one leading deeper. [225] Her mother had left them, Mira realized, a melody broken into breadcrumbs. [237] She followed, humming each tone as she found it, stitching the fragments into something whole. [252] The work took her across the pause of shifting constellations, that long held breath when the old stars set and the new ones have not yet risen. [279] In that interval the forest quieted, its ultrasonic roar thinning to a whisper. [292] Note by note, the hidden melody assembled inside her chest. [302] It was not a song of triumph. [309] It was a lullaby, the one her mother used to hum, transcribed into the trees so a blind daughter could read it with her ears. [334] The compass ring grew suddenly cold, its needle settling, still. [344] Mira stopped. [346] She had reached the stillpoint. [351] It was not a clearing or a shrine, but a small hollow where the forest's relentless sound cancelled itself into perfect silence. [373] Here her skin absorbed nothing, because here there were no languages, only stillness. [386] For the first time in her life, Mira heard her own pulse without translation. [400] She understood, then, why her mother had stayed. [408] The stillpoint did not erase grief; it let you finally hear it clearly, undisguised by the noise of coping. [427] In the center of the hollow lay a small leather map, blank, waiting. [440] Her mother's last cartography: an empty page, because some territories could only be drawn by the one standing in them. [460] Mira knelt and pressed her palm flat against the blank leather. [471] No words rose into her skin. [477] Instead something rose out of her: every unspoken ache, every year of anger softening into ache, every ache softening into the simple shape of missing someone. [503] She wept without sound, and the silence held her like a hand. [515] It was a quiet blaze, this grief, burning without consuming, warming without scorching. [528] When the new constellations finally rose, their faint song returning to the forest, Mira stood. [543] She had mapped nothing on paper, yet she had charted the one country that mattered. [558] Slipping the platinum compass ring from her finger, she laid it in the hollow where the needle could rest forever. [578] She no longer needed it to find feeling; she had learned to dowse with her own heart. [595] Humming her mother's recovered lullaby, Mira walked back through the ultrasonic forest, leaving the hidden melody trailing behind her like light. [616]</story>
