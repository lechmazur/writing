<story>The orchard waited to die. [5] The county had marked every apple tree with red ribbon, and tomorrow the saws would come. [21] In that hush before the felling, I drove to the sheet music archive at the orchard's edge. [38] I am Mara Quill, the gossip-column exorcist. [45] I write three hundred words on celebrity hauntings each week, and on quieter nights I evict the actual dead. [64] People assume the two jobs cancel each other, but ghosts gossip too. [76] The archive was a cold brick building stuffed with brittle scores. [87] A widow had hired me, paying in an antique perfume bottle she swore was haunted. [102] She wanted me to reconnect with her late husband, the archivist, who had not crossed over. [118] "He owes me an apology," she said, pressing the bottle into my palm. [131] The glass was clouded violet, the stopper shaped like a closed iris. [143] Inside slept a finger's width of bergamot and grief. [152] I told her grief was a tuning fork; strike it once and the whole room sang. [168] She did not laugh. [172] They never do when the metaphor lands. [179] The archivist's name was Edgar, and Edgar had loved this orchard more than his wife. [194] He had spent thirty years cataloguing songs nobody played, while the trees outside fruited and rotted. [210] Now both man and orchard were scheduled for removal. [219] I did not work with chalk or salt. [227] I worked through induced currents, looping copper wire across the steel shelving and feeding it a low hum from a car battery. [249] Ghosts are static; give them a circuit and they speak. [259] I uncorked the perfume bottle. [264] The bergamot rose, and the wire began to whisper. [273] Edgar arrived as a draft between the folios. [281] The current caught him, organized him, the way a magnet sorts iron filings into a pattern that almost resembles a face. [302] "You smell the bottle," he said. [308] "She kept it on the windowsill. [314] I broke it once and glued it badly." [322] So that was the haunting: not the bottle, but the crack. [470] "Tell her I planted the orchard the year we married, and I confused the tending for the loving," he said. [490] "Tell her the apology is overdue and the interest is unpayable." [615] "He glued it badly on purpose," she said, "so I'd always know where he'd been." [630] I left her the bottle and kept the wire.
