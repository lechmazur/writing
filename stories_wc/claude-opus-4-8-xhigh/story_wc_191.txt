<story>The lore keeper Vesper had outlived three generations of glass cities, yet the old legends still pressed against her ribs like trapped birds. [23] The one she loved most spoke of the stairway of ascension, hidden behind a kaleidoscopic waterfall where light still fractured into colors the world had since abandoned. [50] She had been wisely adventurous all her long life, never reckless, but never still. [64] So at dusk she decided to sally from the archive-tower, her boots whispering across floors that remembered when machines still sang. [85] The waterfall waited at the city's drowned edge, its spray bending into prisms that smelled of rain and forgotten neon. [105] She stepped through the curtain of shattered light and entered the hidden orchard behind the kaleidoscopic waterfall. [122] Inside, silver trees bore fruit of soft circuitry, and the air hummed with a nostalgia so dense it felt like memory made weather. [145] At the orchard's heart stood a wall, blank and gray, where the legends promised the stairway would reveal itself. [164] Vesper understood the old method now: the way was not built but uncovered, found through the peel of paint layers revealing hidden murals. [187] She drew a ruby flake from her pocket, a sliver of ancient pigment-stone that her predecessors had guarded for centuries. [207] Pressed to the wall, the ruby flake bit into the surface and began lifting the topmost coat of dull paint. [227] Curl by curl the gray sloughed away, and beneath it bloomed a mural of a coronation never completed. [245] There sat the unclaimed throne, painted in golds the colony had outlawed, waiting for a ruler who had never come. [265] She peeled deeper, and a second layer surrendered, showing the same throne abandoned, vines crawling over its arms. [283] Each stratum told a chapter, and Vesper, the lore keeper, finally read with her hands what no book had preserved. [303] The founders had built a throne so sacred no one dared sit, and so the kingdom governed itself into ruin and then into wonder. [327] The final mural was no painting at all but an opening, where the stripped paint revealed actual steps climbing into starlight. [348] The stairway of ascension had been hiding under varnish and propaganda the whole forgotten while. [363] Vesper hesitated, knowing the legends warned that only the patient should climb, and only until sunrise. [379] After dawn, the orchard would seal, the waterfall would still its colors, and the stair would sleep another hundred years. [399] She climbed, each step lighting beneath her like a memory recalled, the futuristic glow warm with the ache of older days. [420] Higher she went, past murals that drifted free of their wall and floated beside her like patient ghosts. [438] At the summit waited the unclaimed throne itself, real and humming, neither gold nor ruin but quiet possibility. [456] Vesper realized the throne had stayed unclaimed not from neglect but from a wisdom passed down like her own. [475] To rule was to end the story; to leave it open was to let every dreamer keep climbing. [493] So the wisely adventurous keeper did not sit. [501] Instead she set the ruby flake upon the cushion as her record, a small red witness that she had come and chosen restraint. [524] The eastern sky paled, and the orchard began to fold its colors inward. [537] Vesper descended swiftly, the stair dimming step by step behind her until sunrise touched the highest mural and dissolved it. [557] She slipped back through the kaleidoscopic waterfall as its prisms thinned into ordinary water. [571] Back in the archive-tower, the lore keeper opened a fresh page and wrote the legend true at last. [589] The stairway was real, the throne endured, and the ruby flake now waited there for the next wisely adventurous soul brave enough to climb and humble enough to leave the throne unclaimed.
