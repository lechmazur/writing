<story>After the resuscitation, I woke with light leaking out of me. [11] The doctors called it a lingering luminance, a glow my dead skin refused to surrender. [26] They photographed me to study it, and that is how I learned I was photographically cursed. [42] Every image developed showed not my face but the face of whatever feared me most. [57] My whispering advisor arrived the night I left the hospital, his voice never rising above the hush of a turned page. [78] He pressed a dream dictionary into my hands and told me to read it backward. [93] Its paradoxical notes claimed that fear sleeps where it is born, and that it was born inside a place built to delight. [115] There was only one such place near the river: the abandoned carnival funhouse. [128] My motive was simple and savage: to trick fear into revealing its weakness, so my glow might dim and let me rest. [150] We crossed the fairgrounds at midnight, where rotted cotton-candy stalls oozed a sweetness so foul it pulled me closer. [169] Everything here held that repulsive allure, beauty curdled into something I could not stop touching. [184] The funhouse door wore a single ornament: an opera cloak clasp, silver and shaped like two clutching hands. [202] My advisor whispered that fear had worn it once, as a costume, before the carnival died. [218] Inside, the mirrors had not forgotten how to shine. [227] They caught my lingering luminance and threw it down the corridor, multiplying me into a hundred ghosts. [244] Somewhere in that gallery, fear waited, and I meant to make it unbosom its secret. [259] The dream dictionary had instructed me to speak only contradictions, for fear cannot survive a sentence that argues with itself. [279] So when the thing uncoiled from the deepest mirror, I greeted it warmly. [292] It wore my resuscitated face, but rotting, lovely and obscene at once. [304] 'You are the brightest dark I have ever met,' I told it, 'and the safest danger.' [320] The figure flinched, its certainty cracking on the paradox. [329] 'Speak true,' it hissed, 'or lie completely, but do not braid the two.' [342] I laughed and pressed harder, telling it that I had come to leave and would never depart. [359] The mirrors dimmed; the hundred ghosts dropped to ninety, then fifty, then one. [372] My advisor whispered the final line from the dictionary's last impossible page. [384] 'Fear dies,' he breathed, 'only when something glows brighter than it does.' [396] Then I understood why I had been kept alight. [405] I tore the opera cloak clasp from the door and pinned it over my heart, where the luminance pooled strongest. [425] The silver hands clamped, and my glow surged through them like blood remembering a wound. [440] I blazed. [442] Fear shrank from me, bleached and trembling, its weakness finally unbosomed: it could not bear to be outshone. [460] 'You tricked me,' it wept, dissolving into the cold glass. [470] 'I only told you what you taught me,' I answered. [480] When dawn pried into the funhouse, my advisor was gone, his pages scattered like molted feathers. [496] I walked out wearing the clasp, dimmer now, almost human. [506] The lingering luminance had not vanished; it had simply learned to be mine, a lamp instead of a leak. [525] A child photographed me at the gate, curious. [533] The picture, when shaken into being, showed only a face: mine, and unafraid. [546] I keep the clasp shut over my heart always, a small warm tyranny holding the light in place. [564] On nights the river fogs, the funhouse glows faintly, as if remembering the brightest thing it ever swallowed. [582] And the dream dictionary, water-warped and useless now, still falls open to one impossible line: to be unafraid, become the thing fear flees. [605]</story>
