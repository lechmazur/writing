<story>Riven had not dreamed since the last forest burned. [9] They called him a dreamless freight guard, and he wore the title like ash on his collar. [26] His freight was a single crate, and the crate held a divergent archive. [39] Inside, every choice the world had refused still hummed, branching softly through wave functions that never agreed on a single past. [60] The archive was occasionally everywhere, leaking into rain barrels, cracked mirrors, and the breath of sleeping children. [77] Riven's orders were simple: carry it across the dead country and never let it open. [92] He had surrendered his dreams to a recruiter who swore that a sleepless man cannot be tempted by the unborn worlds he hauls. [115] He walked the wagon road by night, humming a fractured lullaby his mother had left unfinished. [131] The melody broke in the same place each time, as if grief had snapped a string he could not find. [151] His feet still remembered orchards that no longer existed, and the ache kept him moving. [166] On the seventh night he crested a glowing dune in bloom, where pale flowers fed on buried light and swayed without wind. [188] The petals shone because the archive's leakage had taken root here first, and the sand still remembered being a seabed. [208] There a woman waited beside a chess clock frozen at midnight. [219] "You guard the unchosen," she said, "but you have stopped choosing yourself." [231] Her name was Sable, and she tended the dune's blossoms like a gardener of impossible springs. [247] She meant to cultivate the sparks in the unlit corners, the half-lives the fire had missed. [263] "Open the crate," she charmed, her voice the very lullaby he had never been able to finish. [280] Riven trembled, for charm was the one weapon his dreamless armor could not turn. [294] For years his orders had been a wall, yet her song slipped through every gap in the mortar. [312] The chess clock, she explained, had stopped the instant the last tree fell, freezing every game humanity might still play. [332] To restart it, the archive had to breathe, scattering its branching paths through wave functions back into the living world. [352] "If you keep it sealed, nothing new is born," she sang, "and the dark stays merely dark." [369] Riven thought of the forests, of the dream he had traded for duty, of his unfinished song. [386] He understood at last that he was not a guardian of the future but its jailer. [402] So he knelt among the glowing blossoms and pressed his thumb to the iron latch. [417] The crate exhaled, and the divergent archive poured out as a tide of soft, contradicting lights. [433] Each spark drifted toward an unlit corner, settling into dunes and barrels and the sleeping mouths of children. [451] The chess clock shuddered, and its frozen hands lurched past midnight into a trembling new minute. [467] As the archive emptied, Sable bloomed brighter, becoming the everywhere it had once only occasionally been. [483] "Now you may dream," she whispered, and she folded into the wave of light like a tide returning home. [502] Riven lay back on the warm dune as the lullaby completed itself in his chest, every missing note finally found. [522] After the last forest burned, the world had assumed all seeds were only ash. [536] But sparks are stubborn, and possibility is the one fire that feeds on darkness. [550] Riven closed his eyes, and for the first time in years a dream arrived, green and impossible and quietly growing. [570] In it the dunes wore forests again, every burned trunk re-rooted from a remembered possibility. [585] And somewhere in the unlit corners, new sparks answered the lullaby, learning at last how the song was meant to end. [606]</story>
