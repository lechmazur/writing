<story>The carousel halted, and the kinetic lagoon above the clouds forgot which hour it was supposed to be. [18] Mira, a resolute scavenger, felt the stillness arrive like a fracture running through glass. [32] For years the carousel had churned the floating tides, spinning water into slow, luminous wheels that drifted between the cloudbanks. [52] Now everything hung motionless, and time itself grew chronologically confused. [62] She could not say whether she had arrived yesterday or whether yesterday was still coming. [77] Mira had come to the lagoon with enormous ambitions, but the salt mists had eroded them slowly, the way they erode the painted horses on the carousel. [104] Once she had wanted to map the entire sky-sea, to name each current and chart each cloud. [121] Now she wanted only one thing, and it frightened her more than the silence. [135] She had come to negotiate with her own ghost. [144] The ghost lived in the unmoving water, a younger Mira who still believed in everything. [159] To reach her, Mira had to assemble a key by collecting fragments of overheard stories that floated through the lagoon like seeds. [181] Travelers passing on the gondola-clouds always shed words, half-confessions, jokes, regrets, and these settled on the surface where she could gather them. [203] She scooped a sentence about a lost sister, a phrase about a burning house, a whisper of someone's first love. [223] Each fragment, strung together, became a sound the ghost would answer. [234] In her satchel she carried her only treasure, a disposable camera wheel salvaged from a wreck of an old amusement barge. [255] It held thirty-six exposures, thirty-six chances to keep a moment from dissolving. [267] When the last fragment clicked into place, the water rippled and her ghost rose, dripping light. [283] You stopped the carousel, the ghost said, not as an accusation but as a fact. [298] I had to, Mira answered; it was spinning my whole life away, and I could not hold a single year in my hands. [321] The ghost studied her eroded face, the lines that ambition had carved and then abandoned. [336] We wanted to chart the sky, the ghost reminded her, gently. [347] I want only to forgive you now, Mira said, and to be forgiven. [360] That is the negotiation. [364] Regret moved between them like the lagoon's halted tide, heavy and unmoving. [376] The ghost reached for the disposable camera wheel and turned it once in the silver air. [392] Take a photograph of me, the ghost said, so you stop chasing what I was. [407] Mira lifted the camera, and her hands shook with all the years she had misplaced. [422] She pressed the button; the shutter snapped; and the sound traveled across the still water like a small, clean bell. [442] When she lowered the camera, the ghost was already thinning into mist. [454] You kept the moment, the ghost whispered; now you can let the rest go. [468] Mira advanced the camera wheel with her thumb, the soft ratchet the only motion in that fractured stillness. [486] Thirty-five exposures remained, enough for whatever life began after this halted hour. [498] She understood, finally, that her ambitions had not died; they had only changed shape, the way water changes when it stops spinning. [520] She would no longer chart the sky. [527] She would gather the small, true moments and keep them, one frame at a time. [542] The lagoon remained motionless, but Mira no longer felt trapped by its stillness. [555] She slung the camera over her shoulder and stepped onto a drifting cloud, ready to overhear the next story. [574] Behind her, the carousel stood quiet, its painted horses facing a sky that had finally agreed to wait. [592] And for the first time, the confusion of hours did not frighten her. [605]</story>
