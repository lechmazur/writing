<story>Mira had always been the wistful loom spinner of the forgotten orchard, where apple trees still bloomed beneath a canopy of bamboo. [22] Deep in the bamboo forest, the stalks swallowed every footstep, leaving a raveling silence that frightened ordinary travelers away. [41] She had not come for the apples. [48] She had come to finish a tapestry that would carry a message across an impossible distance, to a brother lost beyond the edge of the living world. [75] Years ago, before the silence thickened, her brother Tomas had promised to return when the orchard bloomed again. [93] He never did, and the loom had gone quiet for a decade. [105] Then, at a dusty rummage sale in the last village before the forest, Mira found an old box of jigsaw puzzle edges. [127] The pieces were only borders, no centers, yet when she fitted them together she saw they spelled the map of a route no road had ever traced. [154] Through those jigsaw puzzle edges she finally understood how a thread could be sent where feet could not follow. [173] The frame of the puzzle was the frame of her loom; the empty middle was the distance she must weave herself into. [195] At the orchard, she strung the loom and began to recommence the work her grief had abandoned. [212] Around her neck hung a single tree sap amber bead, hardened a thousand years ago around a captured breath of light. [233] It was stoically sparkling even in the dim green light, as if it remembered a sun the forest had forgotten. [253] Her grandmother had taught her that such a bead could anchor a message, for amber holds whatever it loves without ever letting time corrupt it. [278] So she wove the bead into the tapestry's heart, and the threads drank its light. [293] Through the hush of time's unraveling tapestry, the orchard began to shimmer, each blossom flickering between bloom and bare branch. [313] Mira understood the cost the puzzle's edges had hidden in their hollow center. [326] To send a message across impossible distance, the weaver must spin herself into the weft, thread by thread, until nothing of her remained on this side. [352] She did not hesitate. [356] A latent sacrifice had been waiting in her since the day Tomas left; she had simply been searching for the loom that could spend it well. [382] Hour by hour she fed her own warmth into the pattern, and the raveling silence began to reverse, knitting itself into a single, traveling word. [407] The word she carried was not spoken but woven: that he had never been forgotten, that love could still cross where bodies could not. [431] As the last of her unspooled into thread, the amber bead pulsed once, becoming a lantern to carry her woven voice down the route the puzzle had revealed. [459] The bamboo bent aside like curtains, and the tapestry lifted from the loom, drifting through air no bird had ever crossed. [480] Where Mira had stood there remained only the warm bead, settling into the moss like a fallen star. [498] Far beyond the edge of the living world, in a place where roads dissolved, Tomas felt a thread brush his hand. [519] He had wandered the gray distances for ten years, unable to find his way back, certain he had been forgotten. [539] Now the tapestry wrapped around him, warm with his sister's woven warmth, and he read her message in every knot. [559] She had spun the route home and become the road itself. [570] Following the threads back, Tomas walked until bamboo parted and the orchard's blossoms touched his face once more. [588] He knelt where the amber bead glowed in the moss, and the silence that had ravelled the orchard for a decade finally let go. [612] Lifting the bead, he felt his sister within it, patient and whole. [624] He strung it on the silent loom and, with hands that had learned her craft in dreams, began to weave the answering thread. [647] If she could cross an impossible distance to reach him, then he could carry her message back into the living world, so neither sacrifice would be wasted. [674] And so the loom of the forgotten orchard sang again, and the wistful spinner's gift went on, thread answering thread, across every distance time had ever pretended was final. [703]</story>
