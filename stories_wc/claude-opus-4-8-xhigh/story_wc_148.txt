<story>My father was the boy who cried wolf, and I am the boy he never warned them about. [18] They sent me to the lighthouse prison off the jagged reef, where no one's lies could cost a flock, only the gulls and the tide. [43] Here the air keeps a copperlight repose, a slow warm dimness that pools on the stairs like honey gone to rust. [64] I came chasing hush, which is the only inheritance a liar's son truly craves. [78] All my life people leaned in for the sound of my voice only to brace for the lie they assumed would follow. [100] Silence, I decided, was the one thing no one could accuse me of inventing. [114] The keepers before me left a confessional lattice bolted into the lamp room wall, salvaged from a chapel the sea had swallowed. [136] Through its little wooden grid the light fell in soft diamonds, and I learned to press my face there and speak to no one. [160] It was a strange place, this tower, for it sustains impossibilities the way other walls hold heat. [177] Lamps that should have drowned still burned; a clock with no hands still kept the hour; and in the lattice, voices that had long gone quiet seemed to hover, waiting. [207] At the base of the stair was a narrow door, left ajar for decades, its paint worn to a confusion of scuffs. [229] I read those scuffs the way other men read scripture, tracing each scrape until the keepers who made them began to tell me things. [253] Through the scuffs on that narrow door I learned the labyrinth of the reef below, every false channel, every tooth of stone. [275] And I learned, too, what the last keepers had done before they vanished. [288] They had quarreled, three of them, over a wrecking lamp set false to lure a ship onto the rocks. [307] The scuffs near the hinge were a man dragging another; the marks low on the jamb were heels that did not want to go. [331] Somewhere beneath the cistern, the bodies still lay, and the mainland had never been told. [346] I understood then that I had arrived before they find the bodies, in that thin hush between a crime and its discovery. [368] A supply boat was due at the turning of the season, and with it, men who would search and judge. [388] When they came, they would find a liar's son alone above three murders, and they would write the easy story. [408] My father had taught me, without meaning to, exactly how that ending goes for our name. [424] So I resolved to do the only honest thing a doubted man can do: to thread the labyrinth with my own story. [446] Night after night I sat at the confessional lattice and spoke the truth into it, the whole of it, slow and plain. [468] I named the dead keepers, the false lamp, the dragging heels, the cistern stone. [482] The tower, which sustains impossibilities, took my words and would not let them fade. [496] In the copperlight the diamonds of the lattice began to hover off the wall, each one holding a held breath of what I had confessed. [521] This was the hush I had chased all my life: not empty, but full, a silence that remembered. [539] When the boat finally scraped the reef, the men climbed the stair through that narrow door left ajar for decades. [559] I said nothing, for I had learned the worth of my own voice. [572] Instead I led them to the lattice, where the hovering diamonds spilled the recorded truth in light across their faces. [592] They went down to the cistern and found the bodies exactly where my story had set them. [609] For the first time in two generations, a man of my blood was believed. [623] They left me the lighthouse, since a keeper who cannot lie is worth more than any lamp. [640] Now I tend the copperlight repose, and the hush around me hums with everything I have ever told it true. [660]</story>
