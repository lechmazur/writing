<story>Mira had foreseen a thousand futures, but never her own. [10] She read strangers' fates in the way light fractured through stone. [21] Her home was a musical crystal cave, where every stalactite hummed a different note when the wind slipped through. [40] She had settled there with one purpose: to magnify everyday magic, to take the small wonders people overlooked and make them sing. [62] Pilgrims climbed the mountain for her counsel, and she always knew their questions before they spoke. [78] After the pilgrim arrived that morning, everything she trusted began to fail. [90] He was old and barefoot, carrying nothing but a single cave pearl wrapped in gray cloth. [106] He set it on the stone floor and asked Mira to read its meaning. [120] She reached for her gift, expecting visions to bloom, but nothing came. [132] For the first time, the clairvoyant who saw everyone's path saw only darkness where the pearl sat. [149] Then the pearl glowed, not brightly, but with a bright darkness that deepened the shadows instead of banishing them. [168] The crystals around them slid their song into a minor key, and Mira felt unseen forces gathering, currents she had never sensed before. [191] "What is this?" she whispered. [196] The pilgrim smiled sadly. [200] "It is a seed of the things that move beneath all seeing," he said. [214] The cave's notes turned steadily unpredictable, each chord arriving wrong, each silence stretched too long. [229] Shadows from a hundred crystals lengthened and crossed. [237] Through overlapping shadows, something began to inhabit the room, a presence without shape, drawn by the pearl's slow pulse. [256] Mira understood, too late, why she had been blind. [265] The forces that hid from her were the very ones she had spent her life ignoring. [281] She had performed everyday magic for visitors yet never once let herself feel it. [295] The presence pressed close, hungry for a home; it wanted to inhabit her, to wear her sight like a borrowed coat. [316] Through the overlapping shadows it reached, and Mira finally saw, not the future, but the present, vivid and terrifying. [335] "You can refuse it," the pilgrim said, "but only by offering something truer than prophecy." [350] Mira looked at the cave pearl, formed drop by patient drop over ages, a slow miracle no one had watched being made. [372] That was the everyday magic she had always preached but never lived: patience, the quiet gathering of small things into beauty. [393] She knelt and cupped the pearl in both hands. [402] Instead of reading it, she simply listened. [409] The crystals' song steadied, then brightened, the bright darkness warming into something like gold. [423] The presence hesitated. [426] It had come to inhabit a hollow vessel, but Mira was no longer hollow. [440] She filled herself with the ordinary: the cold taste of cave water, the ache in her knees, the pilgrim's tired breathing. [461] The overlapping shadows pulled apart, and the unseen forces, denied a home, dissolved back into the rock from which they had risen. [483] When the cave fell quiet, Mira opened her eyes. [492] Her gift was gone; she could no longer see futures. [502] But for the first time, she could fully see now. [512] The pilgrim rose to leave. [517] "You magnified everyday magic at last," he said, "by becoming it." [528] He left the cave pearl behind, and the door of daylight swallowed his thin silhouette. [543] Mira held the pearl up to the humming crystals. [552] For the first time, the cave sang for her alone, and she finally heard every ordinary, unrepeatable note. [570] Outside, the wind shifted, and the mountain answered with a chord she had never charted. [585] She laughed, surprised, because surprise was now the truest proof that she was awake. [599] The pearl warmed in her palm, ordinary and infinite, like every life she had once presumed to read. [617] She closed her eyes, kept them closed, and chose to stay. [628]</story>
