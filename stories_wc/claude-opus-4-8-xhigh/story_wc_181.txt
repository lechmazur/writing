<story>The curator arrived bewildered, clutching a pistachio shell cameo blank in his gloved hand. [14] Around him, the astral orchard blossomed on its lonely asteroid, branches heavy with fruit that shone like swallowed stars. [33] He was named Ansel, and the woman who left him this place had been a stranger. [49] Her will arrived as a ledger, and she had instructed him to read everything between the first and last page. [69] The first page held a single line: "Inheritance does not flow forward; it pools wherever it pleases." [86] Ansel did not understand, which was, he admitted, his permanent professional condition. [98] As a museum curator, he catalogued objects whose meanings always outran him. [110] The cameo blank felt warm, as though something inside it were impatient to be born. [125] He pressed his thumb against the pale oval, and a memory that was not his rushed up his arm. [144] He saw the dead woman as a child, planting these luminous trees with seeds she had stolen. [161] The memory receded, and Ansel staggered toward the orchard's small stone cottage to steady himself. [176] Inside the cottage he found a cramped washroom, and there, as always, his mind cleared. [191] All his life, Ansel's breakthroughs had ambushed him over sinks and beside bathtubs, never at his desk. [208] Gripping the basin, he grasped what the ledger meant. [217] The cameo blank was a key, and the orchard was a vault of forbidden knowledge. [232] The dead woman had wanted to inquire into the architecture of time, and the fruit answered such questions. [250] Her inheritance was nonlinear, for she had willed her future into her past and the remainder to him. [268] Above the mirror she had scratched two words: simply complicated. [278] The phrase named the orchard exactly, a paradox grown small enough to hold in one hand. [294] Ansel returned to the trees, disquiet brightening into something almost like joy. [306] He pressed the cameo to a low-hanging fruit, and the blank began to carve itself. [321] A face emerged in the pale shell, and it was his own, older, weary, knowing. [336] The orchard, he realized, did not record the past; it remembered the future and handed it backward. [353] That was the forbidden part, the reason the woman had hidden it on an asteroid no map confessed. [371] To know your own ending was a gift that aged you the instant you accepted it. [387] The carved cameo showed Ansel planting new seeds, just as the woman once had. [401] He understood then that he was not the heir but the predecessor, the one who would seed her childhood. [420] The inheritance looped, generations feeding backward, each curator tending the orchard that produced its own founder. [436] Bewildered no longer, Ansel opened the ledger to its last page. [447] The final line read: "When you reach me, plant, then read this book again from the start." [464] Everything that mattered happened between the first and last page, in the orbit between command and obedience. [481] He returned to the washroom once more, because that was where he always thought clearly. [496] Staring into the spotted mirror, he chose to accept the loop rather than escape it. [511] Forbidden knowledge, he decided, was only forbidden because it refused to stay in line. [525] He walked out among the blossoming branches and knelt in the cold, silver soil. [539] From the carved cameo he pried three luminous seeds and pressed them down. [552] Somewhere in a younger orbit, a small girl would find them and begin the orchard. [567] The disquiet never left him, but it glowed now, gentle as the fruit overhead. [581] He picked up the ledger, turned again to the first page, and began to inquire once more. [598] The orchard listened, patient and bright, waiting to be inherited all over again. [611]</story>
