<story>The cobalt fortress wore its snow like a vow, blue walls swallowing the white until the whole structure seemed carved from frozen dusk. [23] Inside, I worked as the silent-choir bellhop, one of those who carry luggage and hymns we are forbidden to voice aloud. [118] For the fortress was a sanctuary, and sanctuaries always charge a toll no brochure lists. [133] Each morning was seraphically mundane: I polished brass, folded linens, and counted guests who never spoke above a whisper. [175] The mark was faint, a bruise of rust where countless soles had paused. [188] My regenerating fingers tingled against the stone, and I felt, rather than heard, a vibration humming beneath. [205] I knelt and brushed the dust away. [212] Beneath lay a loosened piano bench bolt, wedged into the mortar like a forgotten tooth. [275] The unpardonable silence, I realized, was not holy restraint but a wound dressed as virtue. [290] We sang with our hands so no voice could ever rise loud enough to question the rulers who kept us warm. [567] Guards came running, but they found not rebellion, only people remembering they had once been loud. [583] You cannot arrest an avalanche; you can only stand in its frostbitten, hopeful path.
