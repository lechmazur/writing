<story>Mira called herself an attentive mystic, though the title earned more scorn than coins. [14] Her brother Dane was firmly grounded, an engineer who measured the world in tolerances and torque. [86] She led him outside, where dawn had glazed his car window with feathered frost. [100] "I commune through the patterns in frost on a car window," she explained, pressing her palm near the glass without touching it. [122] "Frost remembers what the metal felt," she insisted, "and metal remembers its drivers." [135] From her coat she drew a glass prism and held it to the weak light. [150] Color split across the frost, and the feathered lines seemed, for a heartbeat, to rearrange. [165] Dane scoffed, but a shifted hush settled over the yard, the ordinary morning noises dropping away as if the world had inhaled. [187] Mira studied the crystals with the care of someone reading a face. [199] "This car carried fear last night," she murmured, "and someone gripped the wheel and prayed." [214] Dane's jaw tightened, because he had told no one about the deer that bolted onto the highway near midnight. [233] He wanted to believe his sister was guessing, reading the dents and the late hour like any clever fraud. [271] Yet Mira's gift lived on truthful lies, and she knew it better than he did. [342] "You almost died," she said softly, and that single fact was wholly real. [355] Dane sat down hard on the cold step, the fight draining out of him. [465] Maybe it was leftover body heat; maybe it was the residue of every fear he had carried into dawn. [484] Either way, Dane could no longer call it nonsense. [493] They sat in the misted arbor while the frost melted, two siblings holding a fractured rainbow between them. [511] Mira had set out to prove that objects absorb emotions, and she had won by lying her way to a deeper honesty. [533] The prism held their morning now, fear and relief and reluctant love, sealed in glass like light in amber. [552] And when the hush finally lifted and the birds resumed, neither of them could say where the lie had ended and the truth had begun. [577] Dane drove home with the prism on his dash, watching the colors swim each time he braked. [594] He would not admit he had become an attentive man, watchful for residue in ordinary things.
