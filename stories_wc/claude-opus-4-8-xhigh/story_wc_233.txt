<story>The disenchanted queen climbed before dawn, because the final warning had finally come. [13] For seven nights the same dream had unspooled behind her eyes, lacy and patient as a spider's threat. [31] She woke each time tasting ash and starlight, certain the answer waited above the clouds. [46] So she came to unravel the mystery, leaving her cold throne and colder advisors behind. [61] The ridge rose in jagged terraces, and at its lip glowed the ember grotto, an old furnace of stone that no fire-keeper now tended. [85] Smoke curled from its mouth like lace, beautiful and faintly threatening, beckoning her higher. [99] She climbed past it, palms scraped, breath thin, the warning of her seer echoing. [113] Go once more into the dream's country, the seer had said, and you may never return crowned. [130] But a queen who no longer believed in her own magic feared little. [143] At the summit she found the cobalt grotto floating above the clouds, its walls blue as drowned midnight. [161] This was the place from her dreams, exact down to the dripping veins of light. [176] Across the cavern ceiling, the stars themselves seemed wounded, bleeding starlight in slow silver drops. [191] Each drop struck the floor and rang like a tiny bell, and with each note a memory surfaced. [209] She remembered, suddenly, the girl she had been before the crown drained her wonder. [223] A voice answered from the bleeding light, softly commanding, neither cruel nor kind. [236] You have dreamed your way to me to ask why you cannot feel, it said. [251] The queen lifted her chin and demanded the truth she had climbed so far to claim. [267] The light gathered into a figure of woven starlight, lovely and edged with menace. [281] Long ago, it told her, she had traded her enchantment to spare her kingdom famine. [296] The grotto had kept her stolen joy locked inside its bleeding stars ever since. [310] The dreams were the bargain expiring, summoning her to either reclaim or surrender it forever. [325] To take it back, the figure warned, would cost the kingdom its long peace. [339] Here was the lacy menace beneath every gift: nothing returned without taking something else. [353] The queen stood between two emptinesses and understood the seer's final warning at last. [367] She would not unmake her people's harvest merely to soothe her own gray heart. [381] Yet she refused to leave wholly hollow, so she bargained with the softly commanding light. [396] Give me only one drop, she said, enough to remember how to feel small things. [411] The figure considered, then loosed a single bead of bleeding starlight into her cupped hands. [426] It cost the kingdom one night of summer rain, the smallest possible wound. [439] Warmth spread through her chest, faint but unmistakable, a thaw within long winter. [452] She descended to the ember grotto where her oldest gardener had waited through the climb. [467] He had followed her up the ridge despite the warning, unwilling to let her vanish alone. [483] Together they stepped from the smoke into the blue dusk settling over the valley. [497] Below them the meadow glittered, and with a shared exhale they spotted the first fireflies of summer. [514] For the first time in years, the small green lights moved her nearly to tears. [529] The single drop of starlight pulsed in her palm, mirroring their tiny living glow. [543] She was no longer wholly disenchanted, only careful, a queen who had learned the price of wonder. [560] The recurring dreams would not return, for their mystery was finally unraveled and answered. [574] Behind them the cobalt grotto sealed itself, its wounded stars knitting closed above the clouds. [589] And the queen walked home, softly commanding the night to keep one light burning for her.
