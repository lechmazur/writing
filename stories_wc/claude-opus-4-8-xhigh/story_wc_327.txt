<story>The last speaker of Vohrai was dying, and with her the language would die too. [15] Maren, the copper-veined storyteller, had walked for nine days to reach the canyon of endless echoes to record what remained. [35] The canyon was a place of towers of bone, ancient ribs of some forgotten leviathan thrust up from the salt. [55] Wind threaded between them and returned multiplied, so that a single word became a hundred whispers. [71] Maren was quietly articulate, choosing each phrase the way a mason chooses stone. [84] Her skin bore thin lines of copper that caught the light, a mark of her lineage of tale-keepers. [102] She carried a flawed diamond rumored to be cursed, a stone with a fracture running through its heart. [120] The Vohrai had used it to translate texture into sound, pressing it against carved tablets until the grooves sang. [139] That was her purpose: to translate texture into sound, to preserve the dying tongue not as words but as the music it had always been. [164] The old woman, Tehla, waited in a tunnel of echoes at the canyon's deepest point. [179] Her breath came shallow, and her hands trembled around the carved tablets she had guarded all her life. [197] "You came," Tehla said in Vohrai, and the tunnel repeated it, layering her voice until it sounded like a chorus of all who had ever spoken. [223] Maren knelt. [225] She pressed the flawed diamond to the first tablet's surface, dragging it slowly along the grooves. [241] The stone hummed. [244] Where the diamond's fracture met the rough texture, the sound bent and warped, producing a tone no smooth gem could make. [265] The curse, Maren understood now, was not malice but precision. [275] The flaw read the texture more deeply than perfection ever could. [286] But a single stone could not hold an entire language. [296] Maren needed to harness the canyon itself, its echoes, its towers, its restless air. [310] She had studied how the Vohrai once did this. [319] The answer lay in the sky. [325] Each dusk, a flock of pale birds rose from the bone towers, wheeling in patterns that the Vohrai had read like script. [347] Through the patterns in a flock of birds in flight, the old people had timed their recitations, letting the echoes fold one word into the next without collision. [375] Maren waited for the birds. [380] When they lifted, she watched their turning shapes, and she began to drag the diamond across the tablets in rhythm with their flight. [403] Tehla spoke each word a heartbeat before Maren's stone found it. [414] The tunnel caught both voice and tone, braiding them, and the birds shaped the timing so nothing was lost. [433] Across the death of a language, they worked. [441] Hours passed; the birds settled and rose again; Tehla's voice grew fainter. [453] Maren felt the copper in her veins warm, as though her ancestors leaned close to listen. [469] Word by word, texture by texture, the language poured from tablet to diamond to echo. [484] The flaw in the stone gave each sound its grain, its weather, its weight. [498] By the third dawn, Tehla could no longer speak. [507] She lay against the tunnel wall, her eyes on the wheeling birds. [519] "Finish it," she whispered, and the echoes carried the command outward until it seemed the whole canyon begged. [537] Maren pressed the diamond to the final tablet. [545] The fracture caught a groove she had not noticed, a spiral carved by a hand long dead. [562] The tone that rose was unlike any before, deep and many-voiced, and the birds above broke their pattern, scattering as if struck. [584] In that scattering Maren read the last word of Vohrai, the word for memory that survives its speakers. [602] She spoke it aloud. [606] The tunnel took it and would not let it go, folding it endlessly between the towers of bone. [624] Tehla smiled and was still. [629] Maren rose, the diamond warm in her palm, no longer rumored cursed but proven faithful. [644] She had harnessed the canyon, the birds, the flaw, and the dying breath of the last speaker. [661] The language was dead. [665] But its texture lived now as sound, circling forever in the tunnel of echoes, waiting for someone quietly articulate enough to listen. [687]</story>
