Mara hated tight spaces, which made her the worst possible spelunker for the drowned facility. [15] But she was the only one who could track invisible things, and the dead deserved a witness. [32] The research station crouched on the continental shelf, abandoned for forty years, reachable only between tidal changes. [49] When the currents slackened, the airlock corridor flooded shallow enough to crawl, and crawl she must. [65] She lowered herself through the hatch, breath rasping, the ceiling pressing close like a closing fist. [81] A crashing hush filled the passage: surf hammered the hull far above, then fell to a silence so total it rang. [102] Mara counted the rhythm and timed her movements to it, sliding forward whenever the sea exhaled. [118] Her gift had always been strange: she could sense the unseen, the residue of signals, the ghost-paths of vanished energy. [138] Here the invisible thing she tracked was current of another kind, the faint pulse still threading the undersea cables. [157] Those cables had been the facility's purpose, listening lines laid across the abyss to hear the planet's hidden voice. [176] When funding died, the staff were ordered out and the work erased, their names scrubbed from every record. [194] Mara had come to stand by the unremembered, to prove that someone, at last, had returned. [210] The corridor opened into the old archive, and she rose gasping into a bubble of trapped air. [227] Shelves leaned, swollen with brine, but one steel cabinet had kept its contents dry. [241] Inside lay a codex, the project's handwritten log, its pages bordered with painted illumination. [255] In the margin of the final entry, a single gold fleck caught her lamp and held it. [272] The fleck was no decoration; it was a code, a map drawn by someone who feared erasure. [289] She read the cramped script and understood: the cables still sang, carrying recordings the censors never found. [306] The scientists had hidden their final data in the signal itself, encoded to survive through phase transitions. [323] Liquid to vapor, current to heat, the message changed form yet never died, waiting for a reader. [340] Mara connected her receiver to the terminal the gold fleck indicated and let the dormant line wake. [357] Through her headphones rose voices, eleven of them, naming themselves one by one into the dark. [373] They had recorded their own names so that the deletion could never be complete. [387] Mara wept and copied every name, every reading, every farewell, into her own sealed drive. [402] The walls seemed to lean nearer, and her old fear clawed up, but she breathed through it. [419] The crashing hush above told her the tide was turning; the corridor would soon flood beyond passage. [436] She had minutes only, the narrow window between tidal changes already closing around her. [450] Cradling the codex against her chest, she dropped back into the choking tunnel. [463] Water surged at her heels, rising faster than she had planned, swallowing the way behind. [478] The ceiling shrank, the air thinned, and panic whispered that she would never get out. [493] Then she heard the recorded voices again in memory, eleven people who had refused to vanish. [509] If they could outlast forty silent years, she could outlast forty more meters of dark. [524] She pulled herself through the last hatch as the sea closed the facility forever behind her. [540] Surfacing under a grey sky, she clutched the codex and let the waves crash their hush around her. [558] The undersea cables would go on humming, but their secret now had a keeper. [572] Mara had returned, and she would publish every reclaimed name into the loud, remembering world. [587] The unremembered would be unremembered no longer, because someone, at last, had chosen to stand beside them. [604]
