<story>Della came to the pirate market hidden beneath the rotting pier an hour before midnight, carrying her father's antique watch fob in her fist. [24] She was a moonshine runner's daughter, raised on quiet engines and quieter promises, and she had learned to read a room by its silences. [48] Tonight the room was the underbelly of the pier, where smugglers traded by candle and code until sunrise, and where no honest sound went unpunished. [73] Her father vanished here three nights past, and the fob was all he left, its hollow case hiding a thumb-sized wax cylinder built to record. [98] Della's motivation was simple and terrible: to capture the sound of trust breaking, the exact moment a man named Corliss betrayed her father, so she could play it back and prove it. [130] The market ran on a truce of hush. [138] Every trader, thief, and turncoat agreed to one law: no name spoken, no deal recorded, no voice raised above the slap of the tide. [162] To break the hush was to invite the long knives, so Della moved like a held breath. [179] Corliss held court at the far stall, fat with stolen rum, and he was curiously steadfast in his kindness toward strangers, refilling cups, laughing low, remembering birthdays. [206] That steadiness was the unease that comforted no one; a man so reliably warm had to be hiding the cold. [226] Della needed him to confess the betrayal aloud while the cylinder turned, but trust does not break on command. [245] She tried to thwart his guard through false starts, easing toward truth and then retreating before suspicion could harden. [264] First she posed as a buyer of cane liquor, then as a courier, then as a grieving girl who knew nothing. [285] Each approach failed, and each failure taught her the shape of his fear. [298] On the fourth false start she set the watch fob on his table, face up, ticking, and watched his easy smile snag. [320] He knew that fob; he had given it to her father as a token of partnership, years before. [338] 'Where did you get that,' he murmured, and the warmth drained from his voice like tide off a sandbar. [357] 'From the man you sold to the harbor guards,' Della said, soft enough to keep the truce. [374] Corliss leaned close, and now his steadiness curdled into something raw, the confession crawling up despite him. [391] 'He owed me,' Corliss breathed, 'so I traded him for his debt, and I'd do it again.' [408] Inside the fob, the cylinder turned, and the sound it caught was not a shout but a whisper, the small dry crack of a partnership splitting. [434] That was the sound of trust breaking, and Della had it now, pressed forever into wax. [450] But Corliss saw her hand close over the fob, and his eyes went to the long knives leaning by the post. [471] He rose to stop her before the recording could leave the pier. [483] Della raised her voice for the first time all night, breaking the truce of hush on purpose. [500] 'This man deals with the harbor guards,' she cried, and the market turned as one, because a trader who sold a partner to the law could sell any of them next. [531] The truce did not protect informers, and in the surge of bodies that swallowed Corliss, Della slipped toward the rotting stairs. [552] She climbed until the planks thinned and the sky showed gray, the fob warm in her palm, ticking its faithful little heartbeat. [574] Sunrise met her at the pier's edge, and the unease in her chest settled into something almost comforting. [592] Her father might never come home, but his betrayer would, dragged into daylight by his own kind. [609] Della pressed the fob to her ear and listened, once more, to the small dry crack of trust breaking, and knew it had been worth every false start. [637]</story>
