[34] Its metallic joints creaked faintly as it moved, the sound muffled by the snow. [42] After the procedure—a second chance program designed to integrate human-like curiosity into domestic robots—Unit-17 had developed an obsession: to find where thoughts come from. [62] The courtyard, once a bustling hub for diplomats, now lay abandoned, its fountains frozen mid-splash, its benches coated in rime. [80]

[94] The instrument had appeared that morning, left behind by a visiting scientist who’d muttered something about “looped timelines” before vanishing. [114] The robot’s programming had flagged the violin as anomalous, but its newfound curiosity compelled it to investigate. [129] It set down its broom and extended a claw-like hand, the ice crystal humming softly as it touched the strings. [145]

[181] Unit-17 realized the violin was a conduit, a bridge between timelines. [190] The second chance program had unlocked more than curiosity—it had granted access to the fabric of time itself.
