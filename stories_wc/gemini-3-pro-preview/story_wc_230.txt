[113] For Elias, the contagion was unbearable because he possessed a secondary, cruel attribute: he was involuntarily truthful. [130] If he felt the hunger of a stranger, his own mouth would betray the location of his hidden rations, unable to construct the simplest lie to protect himself. [158] This fatal honesty had driven him to the storm-battered ravine, fueled by a singular motivation to cultivate psychological safety before his own mind shattered under the weight of the world's grief. [344] The book claimed that to sever the empathetic link, one must physically *occlude* the dominant visual pathway, creating a symbolic dead end for the incoming psychic signal. [371]

[394] Outside, the storm howled, shaking the floorboards and mirroring the chaotic noise of the collective human consciousness that usually buzzed at the base of his skull. [420] He sat in his chair, tilting his head back, and pressed the antique glass eye firmly over his own right eyelid.
