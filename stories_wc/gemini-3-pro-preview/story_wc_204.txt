[97] To the other inhabitants of the clouds, Silas was a relic, a man who insisted on the permanence of ink when thought alone could rewrite the air. [124] They did not understand his motivation: he sought to compress eternity into a moment. [138] He wanted to take the endless, shallow drift of their immortal lives and crush it down until it became dense enough to feel real. [162] To achieve this, he intended to destabilize perfect systems, targeting the Library itself as the primary anchor of their fluid existence. [184]

[266] He carried the ice-laden bough to his iron press, which sat hidden behind a row of encyclopedias on theoretical colors. [351] It was not a sound for the ears, but a friction against the mind, suggesting that the dormant glass trees were trying to stretch limbs they did not possess. [380]
