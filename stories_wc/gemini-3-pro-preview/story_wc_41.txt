[27] Here, the damp air clung to the skeletons of discarded washing machines and towers of tires, softening their edges until they resembled ancient, unknowable monuments. [73] He was a junkyard prophet, a ragged seer cursed with a mind that was precognitively forgetful. [121] The prophecy would arrive, and then, like a dream upon waking, it would slip through his mental grasp, leaving behind only a compulsion to act without knowing why. [149]

It was a specific, heavy time in the history of things: the story unfolded amid the hush of old videotapes left unwatched. [171] Thousands of black plastic cassettes formed the garden’s retaining walls, their magnetic ribbons decaying in silence, holding memories no one would ever view again. [195] The sheer weight of these suspended narratives created a vacuum of sound, a muffled stillness that pressed against Elias’s chest. [214]

His motivation for venturing this deep into the refuse was esoteric but consuming: he sought to capture the sound of growing older. [236] It was not a sound heard with the ears, but a vibration felt in the marrow, a frequency of entropy he had sensed in a future he had already forgotten. [267] To *locate* the exact epicenter of this resonance, he had to trust his hands rather than his failing foresight. [286]

[351] He began the ritual of arranging driftwood as directional markers on the wet earth. [365] The wood was pale and smooth, bleached by the sun and salt of a distant sea, standing out like bone against the dark, oily soil. [390]
