Silas believed that time had ceased to flow linearly since the arrival, pooling instead like water in these desert recesses, waiting for a hand to disturb it. [130]
From his tattered rucksack, he withdrew his most precious possession: a frayed coil of rope from a prison escape that had occurred just before the world changed. [157]
The rope was heavy with the memory of friction and fear, a tangible artifact of his liberation from a cell that no longer existed. [181]
