However, Elian was driven by a singular, desperate motivation: to discover the texture of time, convinced that if he could physically touch the chronal flow, he could tear down the flat images masking his life. [209]

From his satchel, he withdrew the artifact he had stolen from the grand cathedral, a jagged, luminescent object known as a halo fragment. [252]

Elian knelt amidst the thick vines, ignoring the phantom image of a rainy funeral that tried to block his view of the floor. [299]
