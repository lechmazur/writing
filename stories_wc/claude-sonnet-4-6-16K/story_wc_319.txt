The guardian had no name that she remembered, only habits: the circling of shelves, the cataloguing of breath, the particular way she bared her teeth at dust motes when they drifted too close to the spines. [36] But the shelves were gone now. [42] She crouched at the edge of the frozen garden, where the mist sat low and white against the black soil, and she waited. [65] The ice-locked roses held their shapes like arguments never finished, petals sealed mid-curl. [78] She had been waiting long enough that the cold had stopped meaning anything. [91]

Around her neck, on a cord of braided copper wire, hung the pendant she had taken from the cultist's body three seasons past. [114] It was a disc of obsidian etched with a glyph that, when she pressed her thumb against it, pulsed faintly at the boundaries of light: not producing illumination exactly, but disturbing the edges of brightness, the places where light ended and the dark began claiming territory. [160] She had not given it back. [166] She had not known to whom. [172]

She was dimensionally homesick in a way no language she had catalogued could properly name: a wrongness of air, of gravity's particular insistence, of the angle at which sound arrived to her ears. [205] Her home dimension was three shades quieter than this one and smelled of cold copper and old paper, and she missed it the way a tongue misses a lost tooth: constantly, automatically, without choosing to. [240]

Through the veil of sleep, she could sometimes almost reach it. [251] Not travel there. [254] Just sense it behind the gauze of half-consciousness, the way you sense a word before you can say it. [273] She had learned to linger at that threshold, the animal part of her brain still twitching while the library-keeper part grew still and took notes. [298]

The mist thickened around her. [303] She pressed the pendant flat against her sternum and practiced her method: interiorizing exteriors. [317] She breathed the frozen garden inward, pulling the mist through the aperture of her attention until it lived inside her chest rather than outside her body. [343] The calcified roses became interior structures. [349] The silence between ice crystals became the silence between her thoughts. [360] Outside became inside, and the boundary between self and garden blurred, and this was, strangely, a steadying thing. [378]

This was how she made friends with uncertainty. [386] Not by resolving it. [390] Not by finding the door back home. [397] But by letting the not-knowing occupy real space within her, giving it a room, shelving it between loss and patience where it could stop arriving as a surprise. [425]

The pendant pulsed once, sharply. [430] She lifted her head. [434]

At the far edge of the garden, where the mist thickened into something almost architectural, a shape stood precisely at the boundaries of light: at the line where weak grey dawn ended and shadow still held its claim. [472] It was small. [475] It was looking at her. [480]

She waited. [482] The feral part of her wanted to charge; the library part catalogued: bipedal, uncertain posture, probably lost. [499] The pendant had gone cold against her skin. [507]

The shape took one step forward, into the light. [516] A child. [518] Barefoot on the frozen ground, wearing a coat two sizes too large, moving with the particular unsteadiness of someone traveling through the veil of sleep without knowing they had crossed it entirely. [550] A sleepwalker. [552] A traveler without a map. [557]

The guardian understood that expression. [562] She did not bare her teeth. [568] Instead she sat down fully on the cold ground, lowering herself until she was less frightening, and she let the mist gather around them both like a shared room in a library neither of them had built. [605] She interiorized this too: the child's lostness, the open question of what happened next, the specific weight of a moment that could resolve in several directions. [631] She shelved it. [634] She made space for it. [639]

The child sat down across from her on the ice-hardened earth, and neither of them spoke, and the frozen garden held them both in its grey and luminous quiet. [668] She waited to see what the uncertainty would teach her this time. [680] The pendant rested warm against her chest, no longer pulsing, no longer reaching toward other dimensions or other lives. [699] Just present. [701] Just here. [703]
