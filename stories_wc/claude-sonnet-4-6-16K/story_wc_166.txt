Seren had chosen the exile herself. [6] That was the part that stayed sharp, even after the nostalgia ban stripped her of the right to mourn it. [26] The Tribunal had outlawed backward-looking three winters prior, burning the calendars of old ceremonies, and so she could not grieve the shore she had left, nor the crowds who once watched her dance the tides. [61] She could only move forward, into the twilight garden, where the stars had begun their slow puncture of the darkening cloth above. [83]

She knelt between the silver-leaved sage and the black iris beds, and opened her palm. [98] A single moonstone cabochon dust speck rested in the crease of her lifeline, small as a stopped breath, luminous as an apology. [120] It was the last fragment of the ceremonial stone her teacher had pressed into her hands on the day she mastered the crossing wave, the heel-pivot at the tide's exact reluctant edge. [152] She had ground the rest into powder and scattered it over water she would never touch again. [169] This one speck she had kept, not out of sentiment -- the ban had made sentiment a crime -- but because it was data. [191] It told her where the light still pooled. [199]

The garden held its breath around her. [206] It had that quality after dusk, a stillness that was not absence but compression, every sound and scent pressed tightly into the hour like ink held at a pen's tip. [236] Seren had come here specifically for that compression. [244] She was a skilled tide dancer, which meant she had trained for twenty years to read the momentum of things, the surge and withdraw, the places where force gathered before it released. [276] But tide dancing required an audience, a shore, a ceremony, and the ban had dissolved all three by declaring the past off-limits and leaving practitioners like her standing in the present with no context and no stage. [313]

So she had fledged. [317] Not fled. [319] Fledged, the way a young wing learns the air by observing gaps between branches, by noting where the wind finds no resistance, by trusting that absence is a kind of instruction. [350] She had studied the garden's silences the way she once studied the ocean, and in the spaces between the star-clusters overhead, she had begun to see the structure of something older than ceremony. [383]

She rolled her sleeve to the elbow. [390] Her forearm lay pale beneath the stars, a strip of mortal skin, impermanent and warm. [405] This was the work she had come to do: to pen a cosmic verse on that skin, not with ink but with the moonstone dust, which, when pressed and breathed upon, left a temporary phosphorescent script. [441] Her teacher had called it reading yourself into the universe. [451] The Tribunal would call it nostalgia, a reference to old practice, a backward glance. [465] Seren called it the only honest act left. [473]

She pressed the speck to her inner wrist and drew. [483] The line came out of her the way the tide had always come, not willed but summoned, her body knowing the grammar before her mind caught up. [510] She felt herself spontaneously anchored, rooted through the knees into the garden's dark earth, as though the act of marking had nailed her to the present moment with a precision no law could manufacture. [544] The verse she wrote was not in any language the Tribunal catalogued. [556] It curved along the vein's blue road, looped at the pulse point, and ended in a symbol that looked like the gap between two stars, which was also, she understood now, the shape of a choice freely made. [594]

The garden exhaled. [597] Overhead, the stars continued their indifferent and magnificent work. [606] Seren stayed kneeling, reading the glow on her own skin until it faded, which it would, which was the point. [626] She was spontaneously anchored to now, not to before, and the verse she had written confirmed it: the exile had never been punishment. [649] It had been the gap she needed to observe in order to finally fledge. [663] She pressed her glowing wrist to the cool earth once, leaving a faint print among the roots, and then she rose. [684]
