Maren carried the seed the way a soldier carries a blade — close to her ribs, never touching skin. [18] The orchard at the lake's heart spun in a slow, furious spiral, apple and thorn trees locked in perpetual rotation above black water that had not been still in living memory. [49] The druid had come here for the same reason she came everywhere: to find what had gone missing. [67]

The seed was flesh-eating, small as a molar, and it pulsed with a faint warmth that unsettled her through three layers of leather wrapping. [91] The ritual was supposed to be simple. [98] Place the seed at the vortex's edge, speak the fourteen names of the lake's dead, and the water would show her own face from the inside — what mirrors couldn't show, the hidden architecture of a person. [134]

After the magic trick fails, the world tends to go very quiet. [146]

The fourteen names dissolved midway through, swallowed by the spinning air, and the seed burst its wrappings and sank straight into the water without ceremony. [171] Maren watched it go with the flat expression she had perfected over years of guarding herself from anyone who might notice how carefully she watched. [196] The orchard continued to spin. [201] The lake did not care about her preparation. [209]

She had always found lost things — coins in grief, children in fog, names that families had stopped speaking aloud. [228] It was not a gift she had chosen so much as a gravity, a pull toward absence. [245] She understood, standing at the black rim of the vortex, that she had been using it to avoid the one thing she could not locate in any field or ruin: herself. [276]

Maren stepped in. [279]

The cold hit like a war beginning. [286] She moved through expanding circles, the method she knew from tracking — start wide, spiral inward, let the pattern do the thinking. [307] Each pass brought her closer to the vortex's eye, where the trees grew oldest and the water ran shallow over pale stone. [329] The serenity she wore was not peace. [336] It was the silence of a drawn bow, the particular stillness of someone who has decided to move. [354]

At the center, half-submerged and perfectly preserved inside a column of ice that the spinning water somehow never touched, was the frozen confession. [377]

She recognized the voice before she read the words, because the voice was hers — twelve years old, carved into the ice in a child's uneven script, the thing she had said to her mother the morning before the lake took her. [418] I don't need you. [422] Four words. [424] The kind that become load-bearing walls the moment they are spoken. [435]

Maren had spent a decade and more finding lost things for other people because she could not afford to find this. [456]

The seed had done its work after all. [464] It had not shown her face in the water. [473] It had grown down through the dark and reached this column and held it intact, preserved, waiting — a plant that ate flesh now holding bone. [498]

She pressed both hands against the ice and let herself unbend. [509] Not collapse, not break — unbend, the way a branch releases snow in spring, slow and deliberate and loud with its own relief. [531] The words on the ice did not disappear. [539] She had not come here to erase them. [547]

She had come to look. [552]

What mirrors don't show is not ugliness. [559] It is the specific shape of a wound that the face has learned to work around, the compensation that becomes the character, the guarded thing that guards until it forgets what it is guarding. [593]

The orchard turned. [596] The water held its cold. [601] Maren stood at the center of the cursed lake and saw, for the first time without flinching, exactly what had gone missing. [623]

She left the ice intact and walked back through the circles, outward this time, carrying nothing she had not already owned. [644]
