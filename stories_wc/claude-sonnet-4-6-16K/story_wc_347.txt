The orchard clung to the cliff face like a regret -- crooked black trees, fruit that tasted of old promises, and Maren at the center of it, tending what she had never asked to tend. [34] She had inherited it from her grandmother, who had inherited it from someone who no longer had a name. [53] The curse was simple and terrible: the trees would bear fruit as long as someone stayed to tend them, and the fruit, bitter as it was, kept the travelers alive who wandered into the cliffside labyrinth below. [90] Maren stayed because no one else would. [97]

The labyrinth itself was a thing of ungainly grace -- all wrong angles and sudden vistas, corridors of stone that opened onto sheer drops, dead ends that smelled of pine and salt wind. [129] Copper-feathered mechanical birds patrolled its passages, gear-eyed and relentless, programmed to grind smooth any marking left on the walls. [148] Travelers who scratched arrows into stone found those marks erased within minutes, the birds descending with a sound like clocks being unmade. [170] But people kept entering the labyrinth, as people always do, and before the night frost formed -- turning the stone passages into mirrors of ice that made every direction look like an exit -- they needed to find the one true path out. [211]

Maren had found the blueprint three winters ago, folded into the hollow of the oldest tree -- a diagram of every corridor, drawn in a hand so confident it hurt to look at. [243] Whoever had drawn it had been offhandedly exact: measurements crowded the margins, distances given to the quarter-inch, the whole thing annotated as casually as a list of chores. [271] She had memorized it in a single night, lying on her back under the cursed branches, tracing the routes with her eyes until the sky went gray. [298]

Her instrument was a small nut -- a walnut, thumb-sized, worn smooth from years of handling. [313] In the cursed orchard she had learned, the way one learns things in such places, that the trees responded to pressure placed near them rather than on them -- hold the nut beside a trunk without touching the bark, and the tree would lean, barely, toward whatever direction occupied your thoughts most clearly. [365] The lean would hold. [369] The mechanical birds, calibrated only to detect markings on stone, paid it no attention. [383] She could assemble the route tree by tree, each one tilting a few degrees toward safety, the whole orchard becoming a crooked arrow that anyone who knew to look could read. [414]

It was graceless work. [418] The trees, already ugly with their curse, grew worse when they leaned -- hunched, off-balance, their bitter fruit swinging wrong. [437] But the blueprint had specified eleven trees along the western corridor -- the only passage that led to the cliff stairs and out -- and so she walked, and paused, and held the small nut near each trunk in turn, thinking the same word each time: out. [482]

Tonight there were two of them in the labyrinth. [491] She had heard them an hour ago, voices bouncing up through the stone channels -- a man and a child, the child asking questions the man had no answers for. [520] She did not love this work. [526] She had not chosen the orchard, or the curse, or the forgotten blueprint that had fallen into her hands as if it had been waiting for exactly the wrong person. [556] But the frost came when it came, and the labyrinth did not care what she had chosen. [573]

At the eleventh tree, she cupped the small nut close to the roots and thought her word with everything she had. [594] The tree shuddered. [597] Below, she heard the footsteps change direction. [604] A mechanical bird swept overhead on copper wings, scanning the walls for marks, finding none, moving on. [621] Maren stood in her lopsided orchard, on her crumbling cliff, and held the small nut against her palm until the voices below went quiet in the way that meant the cliff stairs had been found. [656] Around her, the trees straightened slowly, imperfect and black against the last pale light, and the first frost came down and made the whole terrible place shine. [683]
