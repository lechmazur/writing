The mist came low that night, settling into the eaves of Nora's workshop like a patient animal. [17] Stars pressed through gaps in the fog, small and exact, and she was grateful for their company. [34] She had not spoken to anyone since the afternoon, since Marcus had sat across from her kitchen table and explained, without flinching, that the sanctuary donations had been redirected for two years. [67] He had smiled while he said it. [74] She kept returning to that smile. [80]

She set her lamp on the workbench and uncovered the chameleon. [91] The little creature was a veiled chameleon, a rescue from a collector whose permits had lapsed, and it had arrived three weeks ago with the final centimeter of its tail tip gone, sheared by a closing cage door. [132] The injury was clean, at least. [138] Nora had been coaxing the tissue, monitoring regeneration, logging small mercies. [149] Tonight she needed to check the suture line before the animal entered its next shed cycle. [165]

She lifted it gently, and it gripped her finger with its slow, deliberate feet. [179] Its colors were doing something quietly vivid in the lamplight, a shifting of teals and golds that had no name in any language she trusted right now. [209] The tail tip, blunted and healing, curled against her wrist like a comma at the end of an unfinished sentence. [230]

Nora had spent ten years building convictions about the people she chose. [242] She believed in reading character the way she read animals, by patience, by accumulation of small signals, by trust in her own observations. [266] Those convictions, she understood now, were more fragile than she had allowed. [278] Marcus had been good at surface. [284] He had shed whatever made him legible and shown her only what she was already disposed to believe. [302] She had wanted a competent partner. [308] He had performed competence. [312] The sanctuary's animals had not suffered, he had been too careful for that, but the money was gone, and with it her certainty that she knew what she was looking at when she looked at a person. [353]

The chameleon's eye tracked a moth near the lamp. [362] Nora watched it and did not move. [369] This was the part of her work she had never been able to explain to people who did not already understand it: the necessity of shimmering stillness, of making yourself small and present without pressure, so that a frightened creature could remember you were not a threat. [416] It required something close to the erasure of ego. [425] She was not sure she had that in her right now. [436]

But she stayed still anyway, and the chameleon stayed calm, and she thought about what it meant to reconstruct a version of herself that could hold two things at once. [467] She did not want to become hard. [474] Hardness was easy and it was a lie, another kind of surface. [486] She also did not want to remain permeable in the way she had been, open past the point of reason. [506] There was a balance somewhere, she believed, between self-respect and compassion, between the wisdom of what she now knew and the refusal to let that knowledge calcify into bitterness. [537]

By shedding layers, the romance of her own good judgment, the flattery of having been chosen by Marcus as his alibi, she might find that balance. [565] It would not feel like gain. [571] It would feel, for a while, exactly like loss. [580]

She set the chameleon back on its branch and made her notes. [592] The tail tip was granulating well. [598] In another few weeks, the tissue would be unremarkable, carrying only the faintest seam to mark where something had been severed. [619] The animal would not grieve it. [625] The animal would simply be the animal it now was. [635]

Nora capped her pen and looked out through the workshop's open door at the mist and the stars beyond it. [655] She was not the animal. [660] She would grieve. [663] But she thought she understood, finally and without comfort, that grief was the honest beginning of whatever she intended to reconstruct. [684] She turned off the lamp and let the stars do their quiet, exact work. [698]
