After the tower falls, Mira tends her roses. [8]

The communications relay had stood at the canyon rim for sixty years, threading messages between settlements across the baked red flats. [29] Its collapse three weeks ago had taken with it the last reliable proof that the outside world still held its shape. [50] Refugees arrived at the dome's airlock in clusters now, eyes carrying that specific hollowness that follows not just loss but the loss of the idea of order. [79]

Mira let them rest inside her glass-domed enclosure, among six hundred varieties of desert rose arranged in spiraling rows. [98] The dome caught the afternoon sun and held it, so the light inside was neither harsh nor dim but something between — a tranquil blaze that made everything look important without making anything look urgent. [134] She moved between the beds with a watering can, speaking quietly to each plant in turn. [150]

This was her method: inculcating plants with values. [158] Her father, who had tracked fugitives across four territories for thirty years, had called it nonsense. [174] She had watched him say so while cleaning his rifle over a kitchen table strewn with bounties, and she had decided then, at age nine, that his world and hers would not overlap. [209] Plants, she believed, absorbed the intentions pressed into the air around them. [221] She spoke patience to the pale-petaled climbers. [228] She spoke endurance to the deep-crimson sprawlers near the south wall. [239]

The south wall had a patch near the base — a strip of heavy canvas cut from an airship ballast sandbag scrap she had salvaged from the canyon debris years ago. [272] The original glass had cracked in a windstorm. [280] The canvas let no light through, which meant the roses beside it grew slightly slower, leaning outward toward brightness. [299] She spoke extra words to those ones. [306]

Her father's hunting knife still hung from her belt in its worn leather sheath. [320] She had carried it since his funeral, not as a weapon but as a reminder of what she had chosen not to become. [344] When refugees arrived looking frantic, sometimes looking dangerous, she would touch the handle once — not to draw it, but to feel the decision under her fingers, the choice to keep it sheathed. [378] The engraved quiet of that gesture steadied her more than any blade could. [391]

She was senselessly perceptive in ways that unnerved people who did not know her. [405] She could read the specific texture of a person's silence and know whether they were afraid or merely tired or concealing something sharp inside themselves. [431] The woman who arrived that Thursday carried three kinds of silence simultaneously. [443] Mira gave her water first and roses second. [451]

"I don't need flowers," the woman said. [458] Her name was Dar. [462] She had been a systems engineer on the tower. [471]

"They need you," Mira said plainly. [477] "Sit with them a while." [482]

Dar looked skeptical but sat. [487] The dome held its golden stillness around her. [495]

What Mira was building — what she had always been building, even before the tower fell, even before her father's name became a legend in the wrong kind of stories — was a thread. [530] A living thread of tended things running through the chaos that periodically swept the flats. [545] Every rose she kept alive was a knot in that thread. [556] Every stranger she steadied inside the dome was another. [565] She had no illusion that the thread would hold everything. [575] She only believed it was necessary to weave continuity through chaos, the way water moves through cracked rock: not forcing, not stopping, finding the line of least resistance and following it faithfully. [608]

Dar stayed four days. [612] On the last morning she helped Mira patch a new crack near the dome's apex with strips of mesh and adhesive, working in companionable silence. [638] The roses around them opened in the morning heat, red and amber and a white so bright it looked slightly fictional. [660]

Mira touched the knife handle once, feeling the engraved grain of it. [672] She kept it sheathed. [676] She spoke to the roses. [681]

Outside, somewhere in the distance, another structure was probably falling. [691] Inside the dome, something else was learning to grow. [700]
