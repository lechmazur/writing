Maren had been trained to scream without meaning it. [9]

That was the job description, loosely. [15] Crisis simulation: she learned to clutch her arm as though shrapnel had found it, to weep for cameras while technicians adjusted lighting and federal contractors checked boxes on clipboards. [44] She was good at it because she understood the grammar of grief without having lived it. [60]

Then the refinery fire in Galveston took her brother, and she learned the difference. [74]

The interregnum arrived that autumn -- no sitting government, a constitutional gap that left the nation moving on momentum alone, like a top still spinning after no one's hand was near it. [108] Maren did not call it a crisis. [114] She called it a pause she could use. [122]

She carried the chessboard in a canvas sack. [130] Someone had pressed it into her hands at the funeral -- a family heirloom from a colleague of her brother's. [150] All the major pieces remained, kings and queens and their attendants, but every pawn was gone. [166] The board always struck her as the truest image of the interregnum: authority still present but the ordinary people stripped away, nothing left to sacrifice. [191]

The whispering gallery at the civic hall had been open and unguarded for three weeks. [206] Security contracts had lapsed in the governmental vacuum, and the great domed room sat empty, its curved limestone walls patient and waiting. [228] Maren arrived at dusk, when the light came in obliquely and left shadows that seemed to generate their own quiet glow -- what she had started calling the nomadic penumbra, that wandering hem of half-dark which belonged to no surface for very long. [271] She liked the word nomadic. [276] She had been living it for two years. [284]

She set the chessboard on the bench at the gallery's edge and removed her shoes. [299]

Her motivation was simple and strange: she wanted to become the historian of small gestures. [314] Not the refinery fire, not its smoke column visible from sixty miles, not the Senate hearings. [330] She wanted to record the way her brother stirred his coffee counterclockwise, the way he touched the bridge of his nose when he was uncertain about something. [358] History had no room for those. [364] She intended to make room. [369]

She began to walk. [373]

The method had come to her by accident, pacing a hotel hallway during another simulation job she could no longer stomach. [394] She had noticed that certain steps, taken in certain patterns, formed letters when she imagined them as marks on a floor. [415] In the whispering gallery she extended it: she moved along the curved wall and her path -- heel, ball, pause, pivot -- spelled names. [439] Her brother's name first. [443] Then the names of the fourteen others she had found in the fire's report, people nobody was delivering speeches about. [463]

The gallery did what galleries do. [469] It carried her footfalls around the dome and returned them to her changed, softened, as if the room were gently answering. [491]

She had a friend -- benevolently ruthless, the kind of person who arranged necessary pain with genuine love -- who told her she needed to unbind herself from the actor's contract, the unspoken one that said her suffering had to be legible and performed. [533] Maren hadn't argued. [536] She had simply come here instead of answering. [544] The walking was the answer. [549]

When she reached the spot where the acoustics converged, she stopped. [560] She whispered her brother's name into the wall. [568]

The name came back from across the dome, faint and rounded and altered, as if the room had held it briefly and returned it gentler than she had sent it. [598] She stood in that luminous absence -- the exact quality of a space where something important had been and was no longer, still warm from the having-been -- and felt the binding begin to loosen. [633]

She picked up the chessboard on the way out. [643] The pawns were still gone. [648] But she thought she understood now what the board was actually showing her: not loss, but the shape that loss makes -- an outline precise enough to prove something was there. [678]

She would write it all down. [684] Not as a crisis. [688] As a record. [691]

Maren walked into the evening where the nomadic penumbra was already moving on without her, as it always did, toward whatever light came next. [715]
