She had watched the last star of the old cosmos blink out, and that was the credential no one else could claim. [22] Maren carried it the way she carried the softcover dictionary -- worn at the spine, tucked against her ribs, a second heartbeat. [43] The temple library had no door she could remember opening. [53] It existed somewhere between the universe's last exhale and its next one, a pocket stitched into the pause -- what the old astronomers had called a universe's hiccup, that involuntary spasm of spacetime when cause and effect briefly forgot their arrangement. [93]

The shelves rose without ceiling. [98] Scrolls and tablets and bound folios leaned into one another like exhausted travelers, and the air between them held a resounding hush -- not silence exactly, but the weight of everything that had already been said pressing down on everything that hadn't. [139]

She opened the softcover dictionary to no page in particular and read the word she found: arbitration. [156] Below it, in an edition that had no business containing such a definition, someone had written in a cramped hand: the negotiation between what is real and what the cosmos merely performs. [188] She recognized the handwriting as her own, from before she had been born into this role. [204]

The spiral arbitration had been the old method, used when two truths occupied the same space and refused to yield. [224] It was not a debate. [229] It was a walk. [233]

Maren began at the library's outer edge, where the shelves were newest and the ink still smelled of intention. [252] Her task was to unify -- to take the cosmic illusions that had calcified into doctrine and the raw, unwitnessed facts she carried in her chest, and press them until they resolved into one coherent thing. [287] The method demanded she journey inward while walking out, which meant she moved toward the library's far exits while her attention folded steadily back toward her own center. [315]

It was wanderingly exact, this walk. [321] Her feet chose paths that looked random -- left at the astronomy folios, right past the crumbling lexicons of dead languages -- and yet each turn brought her closer to the precise geometric heart of the spiral. [356] She was not lost. [360] She was describing a shape that the library had always been. [371]

The cosmic illusions she needed to unravel were not lies, exactly. [382] They were conclusions the universe had reached too quickly, stories it had told itself across eons to avoid examining its own foundations. [404] Chief among them: that history had ended. [411]

Maren had witnessed the end, yes. [417] She had stood at that threshold and watched the final event collapse into itself like a wave discovering there is no more shore. [440] But witnessing was not confirming. [445] The softcover dictionary, she now understood, had always been trying to tell her this. [459] Its definitions were not fixed. [464] Every word in it spiraled outward from a root meaning no one fully remembered, and every definition contained, inside it, a small door. [487]

She reached the outermost exit of the temple library -- a threshold of pale stone worn smooth by feet across a universe's hiccup, across this single elastic instant that had lasted long enough to contain her entire journey. [524] She did not step through. [529] She stopped, turned, and looked back at the spiral she had walked, which was visible now in the faint phosphorescent dust her steps had disturbed. [554]

There it was. [557] The shape she had made was not a path to an ending. [569] It was an argument, wanderingly exact and irrefutable, that endings and beginnings were the same arbitration conducted from different vantage points. [590]

She lifted the softcover dictionary. [595] She pressed it, spine-out, into the empty air before her, and felt the resistance of the illusion -- solid, ancient, certain of itself. [617] Then she breathed, and the book passed through, and she felt the two truths unify in her sternum like a tuning fork finally struck. [641]

The resounding hush deepened and then lifted. [648]

The library exhaled. [651]

History, it turned out, had not ended. [658] It had simply been waiting for someone wanderingly exact enough to walk all the way out of it and still arrive at the center. [682]

Maren stepped through. [685]
