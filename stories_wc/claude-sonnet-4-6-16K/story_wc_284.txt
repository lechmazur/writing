Elena Marsh had been burned two years ago, her cover shredded like paper in rain, her network gone before dawn. [20] She had not worked since. [25] The agency called it retirement; she called it erasure. [34] But the note had come, slipped under her door in February's last cold hour, and it had brought her here: Zephyr Bluff, where the mist pooled between limestone shelves and the first warm wind of the tilting year moved like a rumor through the pines. [79] She pressed her back against a rock and listened to the silver hush of it. [94] No birds yet. [97] No voices. [99] Only the pale wind and the slow drip of snowmelt from the overhang above her. [114] She was prudently daring by old habit -- careful enough to arrive an hour before the contact window, bold enough to have come at all. [138] Most burned officers stayed burned. [143] They tended their grievances and their gardens and let the world's machinery grind on without them. [159] Elena had done that for twenty-two months and found it worse than any field assignment she had ever accepted. [178] She pulled the pillbox from her coat pocket. [186] It was small, silver, engraved along its lid with a compass rose so fine the lines looked drawn by a needle. [207] Her handler, Orin, had given it to her the day before Prague -- not as a tool but as a token, he'd said, for the day you need to remember you chose this. [239] She had carried it ever since, empty, a reminder of what had been broken. [253] Now she pressed her thumb across the engraving and studied the accumulated snow banked against the bluff's stone face. [272] The code was there. [276] She had to look for it the way you look for a face in a crowd -- not hunting, just open. [296] Three drift-lines where the wind had shaped the snow into ridges, then a gap, then two more. [313] A pause in the pattern. [318] Then five, clean and deliberate. [323] Someone had guided the snow with a board or a rod during the night, nudging accumulation into signals the casual eye would read as weather and the trained eye would read as coordinates. [356] Three-one-two. [357] Pause. [358] Two-five. [359] She committed them to memory and said nothing aloud, because the mist carried sound in strange directions on Zephyr Bluff and she had not survived this long by trusting acoustics. [389] A figure emerged from the tree line. [396] Not Orin -- Orin was dead, she knew that now, had known it since the note arrived in handwriting that was almost his but carried the small compensatory loops of someone working from a copied sample. [431] This was someone younger, careful in the way new officers are careful, which is to say visibly so. [449] She let him come to her. [455] He stopped two meters away and looked at the pillbox in her hand. [468] "You kept it," he said. [473] "I keep everything that still works," she said. [481] He hesitated, parsing that, then reached into his jacket. [490] He produced a folded document, plain paper, and held it toward her with the particular angle that meant it had been sanitized of prints. [514] "They want to vouchsafe you a second operational window," he said. [525] "Not reinstatement. [527] A single correction. [530] The network Prague destroyed -- there is one thread still viable. [540] One name that never surfaced. [545] You are the only person who knows the original context." [555] Elena looked out at the mist, at the pale codes dissolving now in the morning's first warmth. [572] She thought about twenty-two months of gardens and grievances. [581] She thought about what Orin had meant by the day you need to remember you chose this. [598] She had always believed that unused courage did not disappear -- it only settled, the way sediment settles in still water, waiting for current. [621] This was current. [624] She took the document. [628] She opened the pillbox and set it on the snow between them, compass rose facing up, and let it serve as the only ceremony the moment deserved. [655] "One thread," she said. [659] "Then we fix the whole broken thing or we leave it alone forever." [672] The young officer nodded. [676] The mist shifted. [679] Somewhere below the bluff, the earth tilted one more degree toward spring, and Elena Marsh, burned and careful and daring, walked back into the work that had never quite let her go. [711]
