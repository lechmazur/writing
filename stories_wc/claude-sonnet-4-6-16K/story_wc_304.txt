Maren had learned to read the aurora like static. [9] She would stand at the grotto's open edge, lucidly watchful, scanning the charged curtains of light for the faint repetitions, the stutter-patterns that told her something was approaching. [37] Tonight, the green sheets overhead pulsed with a rhythm she recognized: someone was almost done. [52]

The grotto hung suspended above the cloudline, anchored to an iron spike driven into the mountain's throat. [69] Stone bowls held burning oils; the light they cast was amber and intimate, barely touching the aurora's cold grandeur beyond the rim. [91] Maren set her tools on the preparation table and waited. [101]

He arrived the way they all did lately -- carried by the wind service, strapped to a cedar litter. [119] His name was Corso, and he had asked to spend his final hours here rather than in the valley clinics below. [140] He was eighty-one years old and entirely lucid, which made him harder to comfort and easier to trust. [158]

"You do hair," he said, studying her kit. [166]

"And everything after," she answered. [171]

Corso had been a signal engineer once, spending decades finding patterns in static -- pulling coherent messages from interference, locating the one true frequency beneath all the noise. [198] His hands still moved as though tuning invisible dials. [207] Maren recognized the habit; she had it herself, though she worked in faces rather than frequencies. [223]

She began. [225]

Beautician and mortician share the same fundamental act: they infuse the body with the memory of itself. [242] Maren mixed pigment and oil while Corso watched the aurora with half-lidded eyes, and she worked color back into his papery skin as though painting a fresco on a wall that might come down at any hour. [279] Each stroke was deliberate. [283] Each stroke was also, in its own way, a kind of listening. [295]

"I have something," he said, and pressed an emerald chip into her palm. [308] It was a circuit fragment, she realized -- old, the kind used in early signal hardware, filed smooth on one edge and still faintly iridescent. [332] "Found it in my first transmitter," he said. [340] "It caught a transmission once that nobody else could explain. [350] Beautiful noise. [352] I kept it because it felt like proof of something." [362]

Maren held the chip to the aurora light. [370] The green above matched the green within it precisely, as though they were two iterations of the same pattern. [389] She understood then what he was really asking her to do. [400]

She was not here only to prepare him. [408] She was here to capture the last spark before oblivion closed over it -- to hold the frequency of who he was just long enough for it to become something permanent. [438] That was the true work, the thing the valley clinics could not offer: not preservation, but witness. [455]

Maren placed the emerald chip at the center of his preparation cloth, where it would stay through everything that followed. [475] Then she returned to his face, working with a quietness that matched the grotto's own. [490] The aurora moved above them in slow waves, and the static she had learned to read resolved, as it always did near the end, into something almost like a melody. [520]

Corso crossed the threshold of stillness just before the aurora faded to white. [533] It was not a dramatic moment. [539] It was the way a signal finally clears: no fanfare, no distortion, just the clean sound of what was always there. [560]

Maren sat with him in the radiant hush that followed. [570] The grotto swayed. [573] The first pale light of something beyond dawn gathered at the eastern edge of the sky, and she pressed two fingers gently to the emerald chip, which had grown warm. [603]

She would finish her work now. [609] She would infuse the last of the color, close what needed closing, and send word down to the valley. [628] But for this moment she remained exactly where she was -- lucidly watchful, finding the pattern in the silence, holding the frequency of a life just long enough to know it had been real. [661]

The chip stayed warm in her hand all the way to morning. [673]
