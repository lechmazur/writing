The parapet belonged to neither kingdom. [6] That was the point. [10] Maren had chosen it herself three months ago, after the failed exchange at the river bridge, after the shot no one admitted firing, after the boy in gray never came home. [89] He had taught her that the space between an apology and forgiveness is not empty. [104] It is the most crowded room you will ever stand in. [115] She understood that now. [119]

The two delegations were somewhere below, in their separate staging tents, each insisting the other should climb first. [137] Maren had stopped caring about symbolic precedence at the river bridge. [148] What she cared about now was the timing. [156] Three prisoners on each side, and both delegations willing to talk only because the moon would be full in four hours. [177] That part had taken her six weeks to engineer. [186] She had learned from the failed exchange that no agreement brokered in the dark held past morning, and no agreement brokered in harsh noon light survived the walk back to camp. [217] The negotiation needed a shared witness, something neither side owned. [227] So she had begun synchronizing through moon phases, scheduling every contact, every courier drop, every preliminary signal fire to align with the lunar calendar both cultures already used for harvest and war. A figure climbed the parapet stairs behind her, boots deliberate on wet stone. [348] She did not turn. [352] "They are ready," said the liaison from the eastern delegation, a man named Sorel whose stillness she had come to read as caution rather than hostility. [378] "One more minute," she said. [406] She was uncommonly thoughtful by nature, her old commander had once said it as a criticism, as if considering consequences thoroughly was a liability in a field that rewarded speed. [436] She had spent years trying to think faster. [444] The boy in gray had been the cost of that effort. [590] She placed the drawer pull in her coat pocket, where it rested against her hip like a small, cold reminder. [610] She had not earned forgiveness yet.
