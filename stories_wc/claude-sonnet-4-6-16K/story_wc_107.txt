The glassblower Maren had claimed the abandoned control room three weeks into the thaw, when the river ice began splitting its first long cracks against the dam's concrete face. [29] The room still held its old instrumentation -- dials frozen mid-reading, toggle switches locked in positions that no longer meant anything to anyone. [51] She had carried her portable torch and tank up four flights of stairs, and now the blue flame threw dancing shadows against the banks of dead monitors. [78]

She worked with what she called thoughtfully spirited intensity -- not frantic, not lazy, but present the way a river is present: moving, aware, inevitable. [102] She was making glass orbs, small enough to cradle in both palms. [114] Each one would anchor a corner of the inquiry spaces she was building with a collective downtown -- rooms where anyone could walk in, sit down, and ask the questions they had been taught to swallow. [149]

"Now," she announced to the empty room, her voice low and theatrical, lifting a fresh gather of molten glass from the furnace, "the artist begins the sacred rotation." [177] This was her method, the one her old teacher Dov had mocked and then quietly adopted himself: she narrated every step as though the task were operatic, as though turning a blowpipe were a deed worth witnessing. [214] It kept her honest. [218] Mundane work narrated aloud became work she could not sleepwalk through. [229]

From her breast pocket she drew the cracked leather bookmark, creased and split along its spine from years of handling. [249] Dov had pressed it into her hand the last day she had seen him, when his own hands were already losing their steadiness. [271] "Mark your place," he had said, which had seemed obvious and then had not seemed obvious at all. [289]

She set it against the edge of the console and watched the molten gather glow. [304]

Outside, the dam's overflow channel ran loud with meltwater, a sound like continuous applause from an audience that did not know the show. [328] The ice that had locked the river for four months was surrendering in sheets, and the water underneath was cold and astonished and free. [352] She had timed her residency here deliberately, choosing the thaw because the season matched what she needed to do inside her own chest. [375]

She had grown careful in the years since Dov died -- careful in a way that had curdled into avoidance. [394] She had stopped taking students, stopped answering the letters from community organizations, stopped believing that the work could hold open a space large enough for real uncertainty. [421] She had, in short, abandoned hope as a working material. [431]

But the inquiry spaces had called her back. [439]

The collective had asked for something glass, something that would catch light differently at different hours and remind visitors that the room's meaning was not fixed. [465] She had said yes before she understood why. [473]

"She breathes," Maren narrated, exhaling steadily into the blowpipe, the gather expanding into a luminous sphere. [489] "The glass takes the breath of the maker, and the maker is reminded that she has breath to give." [508] The orb swelled slowly, catching the blue flame light and holding it like a private sun. [524]

The cracked leather bookmark caught her eye from the console edge. [535]

Mark your place. [538]

She understood now that Dov had not meant a page. [548] He had meant the specific territory of her own capacity -- the place in herself where attention and care and the willingness to be uncertain could coexist. [574] She had left that place. [579] She was being asked to return to it. [587]

She rolled the blowpipe slowly, watching the glass breathe. [596] The sound of ice breaking apart outside moved through the concrete walls like low music, like something enormous deciding, finally, to let go. [619] The room hummed with the old ghosts of its purpose -- decisions made here, power managed, crises navigated -- and she felt suddenly that it was right to be making something fragile in a place that had once been designed for control. [659]

The orb was nearly finished, flawless and warm. [667] She would make eleven more, one for each corner and window ledge of the inquiry space, and when the light came through them in the morning, the room would feel the way a question feels before it hardens into an answer. [708]

She held the glass up, and her heart -- long packed in its careful frost -- moved. [723] Not dramatically. [725] The way ice moves when the temperature finally tips: a slow, structural yielding, a thaw that is also a kind of relief. [747]

She set the bookmark inside her breast pocket, close to that slow warmth. [760]

"And now," she said quietly, rotating the pipe with steady hands, "she continues." [773]
