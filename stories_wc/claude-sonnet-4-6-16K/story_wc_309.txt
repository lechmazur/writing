The Automated Councils had been running creative work for three years before Ossel admitted he could not compete. [18] Other mages had upgraded their sigil-engines, outsourced their inspiration to the humming brass looms that churned out spells by the cartload, precise and loveless. [42] Ossel had not. [45] He had simply grown quieter, had walked the lower markets less, had let his license lapse into the category the Councils labeled "underperforming practitioners." [69] He did not argue the designation. [75]

What he carried instead was a garden folly keystone, a wedge of pale limestone he had pried from the collapsed arch of an old ornamental ruin in a valley he had once loved. [110] The stone was unremarkable except for one thing: it glowed. [120] Not brightly, not usefully, just a faint and steady luminance that refused to leave it regardless of how deep he wrapped it in cloth or how many years passed. [149] The Councils had tested it and found no utility, no extractable power. [161] They had handed it back to him with a shrug. [171]

He kept it strapped against his sternum, under his coat, close enough that in cold weather he sometimes felt its warmth. [193]

The icebound spire was twenty thousand feet up, above the cloud layer, which meant it was reachable only by mist walking, the old slow method no one bothered with anymore because the brass looms had generated seventeen faster alternatives. [232] Ossel still knew it. [236] You dissolved yourself into any moisture in the air, became direction instead of body, and drifted until you chose to cohere again. [258] It was imprecise. [261] It accommodated impossibilities the way water accommodates the shape of whatever holds it, which is to say without judgment and without preference. [283] You might arrive slightly wrong, missing a tooth, an eyelash, a memory of something minor. [298] Ossel had lost the name of a dog he had owned as a boy. [312] He no longer remembered which mist-walk had taken it. [322]

He arrived at the spire's entrance platform just as the sun crested the cloud ceiling below him, and the clouds turned gold and flat as hammered metal. [349]

Inside, the spire was all ice-locked stone and silence. [359] The automated age had not bothered to reach this high. [369] There were no looms here, no councils, no designation of underperforming. [380] There was only the slow tick of frost expanding in hairline cracks, and the way the light came through the translucent ice-sealed windows, dim and brilliant at once, as if brightness had agreed to be humble. [416]

He climbed to the top chamber and sat down on the bare floor. [429]

Then he reached into his coat and began to unstrap the keystone from the leather harness he had sewn for it years ago, the buckles cold and stiff. [458] It took longer than it should have. [465] His fingers were not what they were. [472] But eventually the stone came free and he held it in both palms, and its lingering luminance came up to meet the dim brilliance falling through the ice-sealed walls, and for a moment the room held two kinds of light that had no business being equal and yet were. [521]

He had not come to pray. [527] He was not built for ceremony. [533] He had come because he needed to remember that a broken arch in a forgotten garden could produce something that outlasted its own purpose, something the brass looms could measure but not replicate, not because the technology was insufficient but because replication was not the point. [579] The point was the original leaving of light in a thing. [590] The point was the stone not knowing it was supposed to stop glowing. [603]

He sat there for an hour while the clouds moved below him and the light in the chamber slowly shifted. [623]

When he finally stood to leave, he did not restrap the keystone. [635] He set it on the floor in the center of the room, where its glow would linger in the ice-sealed dark for however long stone could remember being loved. [664]

Then he dissolved into the thin cold moisture of the high air and drifted, slower than necessary, all the way down. [685]
