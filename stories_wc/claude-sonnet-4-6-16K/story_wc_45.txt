After the substitute body was found stiff in the cradle, Maren did not weep where the village could see her. [20] She had always been the forgiving skeptic of Dunmere -- the woman who dismissed old stories as pretty nonsense yet held no grudge against those who believed them. [47] But now she palmed the small object found tucked beneath the false infant's tongue: a single baby tooth, faintly iridescent, too hard to be any ordinary child's. [74] A changeling's baby tooth, according to every tale she had once dismissed. [86]

She walked the hedge maze at dusk, one hand trailing the dense green walls until her fingers found the hidden gap she had discovered years before. [112] Beyond it lay the secret garden hidden behind a hedge maze -- her garden, her laboratory, her confessional. [129] Climbing roses threaded iron armatures she had bent herself. [138] Lanterns hung in the elder trees, and long worktables held the tools of her real trade: splints, wrapping linen, the smooth hardwood mallets used in bone setting. [165] She had learned the craft from her grandmother, who had learned it from hers, and Maren had added what the new anatomical texts taught, marrying instinct with diagram. [193] That marriage of old knowledge and new method was the garden's whole spirit -- nostalgic innovation made visible in every limb she had ever straightened. [217]

She set the tooth on the central table and lit the lanterns. [229]

The substitute body had resembled her daughter Cass in every surface detail: the widow's peak, the three freckles below the left ear. [251] But Maren, even in grief, had noted the wrongness in the bones. [263] A forgiving skeptic does not abandon evidence; she simply waits for more of it. [277] The limbs had been too symmetrical, the joints too clean, as though someone had attempted a replica without access to the imperfections that accumulate in a life. [304] Bone setting had taught her that -- that fractures heal with callus, that stress leaves marks, that a body is a ledger of its own history. [329] This substitute body had no such history written in it. [339]

She yearned for Cass with a precision that felt surgical. [349] Not the wide ache of grief but a narrow, located wanting, like a tongue probing the exact socket of a missing tooth. [371] She understood the metaphor as soon as she thought it and looked at the tooth on the table. [389]

It was shrewdly innocent, that small curved thing. [397] It presented itself as ordinary -- milk-white, small enough to rest on a thumbnail -- yet it carried inside its enamel a faint tracery of lines she recognized from the anatomical plates: growth record, calcium history, a biography compressed to the scale of a seed. [440] Someone had left it deliberately. [445] Not a threat. [448] An address. [450]

Maren understood then that her skepticism had always contained a cleft in certainty -- a hairline fracture she had plastered over with rationalism, the way a careless bone setter plasters over an improperly healed break. [484] The old stories were not literally true the way a diagram was true. [497] But they were structurally true, the way a well-healed bone is true: load-bearing, functional, shaped by real forces. [515]

She had made a mistake years back. [522] A girl brought to her with a complicated forearm fracture, set in haste during a difficult winter, and Maren had accepted a good result when she should have sought a perfect one. [554] The arm had healed crooked. [559] The girl had grown up and become Cass's mother -- Maren's daughter-in-law, gone now in the fever year. [576] All of it connected, all of it her responsibility to carry. [587]

She did not want to bury the mistakes. [595] She wanted to turn mistakes into constellations -- to map them honestly against the dark, to let them become navigable. [614]

She pocketed the tooth. [618]

The garden held its breath in the way old places do when someone finally names the thing that has lived there unnamed for years. [642] Tomorrow she would follow where the tooth led, through whatever gap in the real world a changeling's address might open. [662] Tonight she realigned the lanterns into a new pattern above the worktables -- something between a star chart and a lesson plan -- and let herself believe, with full eyes open, in the structure hidden beneath the surface of ordinary things. [701]

That was close enough to faith for a forgiving skeptic to carry. [713]
