Mira arrived at the blooming fortress at dusk, the jasmine-wrapped battlements turning gold as the last light pooled at the horizon. [21] She had been walking for three days, chanting low syllables to keep her legs honest - "forward, forward, forward still" - a habit she had developed crossing deserts where the mind tried to convince the body to stop. [57] She was a wandering fireworks designer, and she carried her trade in a battered case strapped to her back. [76] Inside: fuses, powder, wire, and the notebook where she mapped trajectories like a cartographer of temporary light. [93]

The fortress gates stood open, the iron long since eaten by rust and ivy. [107] Inside the courtyard, jasmine and night-blooming cereus climbed every wall, their white petals just beginning to open in the cooling air. [128] Someone had tended this place, or at least had once. [138]

She found the fire spinner's kerosene rag knot near the central fountain - still damp, still faintly fragrant, abandoned perhaps that very morning. [160] Someone had been here. [164] Someone who worked with fire differently than she did, someone who danced with it rather than launching it skyward. [183]

Mira set down her case and let herself go quiet, filtering the courtyard through ambient awareness - the way she had learned to read a launch site not by examining it inch by inch but by letting it speak all at once. [224] The direction of the breeze. [229] The echo of the fountain's drip. [235] The weight of silence where a person might recently have stood. [246]

The mosaic floor of the courtyard told her the most. [256] It had been there for centuries, blue and gold tiles arranged in spiraling patterns, but someone had recently disturbed a section near the eastern wall. [281] Crouching, she found it: a note in mosaic, not written but assembled, individual letters formed from colored tile fragments pressed into the gaps of the ancient work. [308] It read: THEY SAY TONIGHT HAS NO STARS. [316] MAKE YOUR OWN. [319]

She sat back on her heels and laughed - a small, serious laugh. [331]

During the hush of a night with no stars, the cloud-cover was absolute, and the darkness that settled over the fortress was the kind that pressed against the eyes like a hand. [363] It was the kind of dark that told you: nothing matters, nothing will come, give in. [379] She had crossed deserts knowing that voice. [386] She knew it was a liar, but she also knew it was patient. [399]

She picked up the fire spinner's kerosene rag knot and held it a moment. [413] Whoever left this was gone, but they had left it like a permission slip. [427] She set it back down gently. [433] She had her own tools. [438]

She began chanting again, softly - "burn, rise, burn, rise" - as she moved through the blooming courtyard, laying fuses between the flowering beds, wedging mortars into the mosaic cracks with a designer's precision. [470] The jasmine and cereus would not catch; she knew fire well enough to give it a path that kept the blooms safe. [492] That was the whole art of it: destruction and beauty choosing the same moment to exist, each one making the other more true. [515]

Her motivation was not joy exactly. [521] It was something more stubborn - to defy the gravity of hopelessness, which pulled downward with the particular patience of a thing that believed it would eventually win. [548] She did not believe it would win. [555] She believed this because she had been in enough dark places to know that dark places end. [572] And when they did not end on their own, you lit something. [584]

She connected the final fuse. [589] She stood in the center of the courtyard, in the center of the mosaic, where the ancient tiles spiraled inward toward a sun no one could see right now. [618] She lit the match. [622]

The fireworks tore upward with a sound like tearing silk, and the cloudbase above the fortress blazed violet, gold, white - illuminated from below, glowing like a ceiling someone had painted for exactly this occasion. [656] In the light, the blooming fortress became impossible and perfect, every petal lit, every mosaic tile shining, the note in the floor reading its four words to no one and everyone. [687]

She was already chanting for the walk ahead. [695]
