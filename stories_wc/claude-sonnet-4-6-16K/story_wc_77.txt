After the resuscitation, Mara could not stop seeing the light. [10] It clung to surfaces like residue, a lingering luminance that coated doorframes and spoon-backs and the sleeping faces of strangers on the bus. [33] Her doctors called it a side effect. [40] Creel, her whispering advisor, called it an invitation. [48]

She met him at dusk outside the abandoned carnival funhouse on Route 9, the one shuttered since the flood year. [68] The structure leaned into the hillside as if ashamed of its own geometry, its painted clown faces blistered into expressions no designer had intended. [92] There was a repulsive allure to it, the way a wound draws the eye before the mind can object. [111]

Creel was photographically cursed, which meant every camera that caught his image returned someone else entirely - a stranger's face where his ought to be, some composite of unmet people. [140] He wore this fact the way he wore his opera cloak, as evidence that the visible world owed no one accuracy. [161] The cloak's clasp was a tarnished silver bee, wings spread mid-flight, and Mara had long suspected the clasp was older than Creel himself. [184]

"You died for four minutes," he whispered, not unkindly, as they crossed the threshold. [198] "Fear saw the inside of you. [204] Now we go in to see the inside of fear." [214]

His method was unusual, as all his methods were. [223] He produced a small dream dictionary from inside the cloak, its margins dense with his paradoxical notes. [240] Opposite "mirror" he had written: the thing that shows you nothing is the only honest glass. [256] Opposite "falling" he had written: you land where you were always going. [268] Opposite "funhouse" he had written only: go. [275]

The interior smelled of mildew and old electricity. [283] Panels of warped mirror lined the central corridor, each one distorting a different truth. [297] In one, Mara appeared eight feet tall and needle-thin. [306] In another, she was a smear, barely human. [314] The lingering luminance she had carried since the hospital seemed to intensify here, as though the funhouse recognized it, as though this place had been dark long enough to be hungry for what she carried. [349]

"To trick fear," Creel whispered behind her, his breath barely touching her ear, "you must let it believe it has already won." [371] "You unbosom yourself to it - you give it everything it expects to find - and in that moment of satisfaction, it drops its guard." [394] "That is when you see the seam." [401]

She understood, or began to. [406] Fear had seen her open on a table, had looked directly into the stopped machinery of her heart. [424] Fear believed it knew her completely. [430] That was its error. [434]

She walked deeper, past the tilting barrel room, past the corridor of spinning discs. [448] Each mirror she passed reflected that strange light back at her, amplified, until the whole corridor pulsed with it. [467] She thought of Creel's paradoxical notes: the thing that shows you nothing is the only honest glass. [484] She stopped before the largest mirror, cracked down the center, its silver backing peeling at the edges. [501]

She let herself look afraid. [506] She let her face do the thing it had been doing alone in the hospital bathroom at 3 a.m. [525] She unbosomed every trembling inch of the four minutes she had been gone, laid it all against the cracked glass like an offering. [548]

The light around her flared. [553]

And there it was, in the mirror's ruined surface: fear's own face looking back, not at her, but past her, at the light. [576] It was afraid of the light. [582] Of course it was. [586] Fear lived in the space where illumination ended, and she had come back from that space carrying something fear could not follow. [608]

Creel touched the silver bee clasp at his throat, a small satisfied gesture. [621] "There," he whispered. [624]

Mara pressed her palm flat against the cracked glass and felt the cold of it travel up her arm and dissolve before it reached her chest. [650] The funhouse settled around them, its warped wood exhaling. [659] The painted clown faces outside, she imagined, were blinking in their blistered way. [672] The lingering luminance did not leave her. [679] It had never been a symptom. [685]

She understood now what Creel's dictionary had known before she asked. [696] She had come back from the dark carrying the dark's only antidote, and fear, confronted with that paradox, had shown her exactly where it kept its one unlocked door. [725]

She would not forget. [729]
