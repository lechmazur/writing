Marcus Veil had given away everything twice. [7] His third fortune dissolved into a free clinic in Managua, his second into wells dug across the Sahel, his first into a childhood that was never really his to spend. [37] Now he owned only what fit inside a canvas bag: a change of clothes, a tin of sardines, and a mason jar sealed with wax that held eleven lightning bugs, each stoppered in its own small glass vial, each still glowing with a patience that embarrassed him. [84]

He had planned to reach the ferry landing by nightfall. [94] Instead a rockslide redirected him, and the detour dropped him onto a black sand beach at the foot of Kilauea's southern flank, where the volcano breathed orange at the treeline and the ocean swallowed its heat without complaint. [132]

The sky above was doing something slow and enormous. [141] A partial lunar eclipse had begun, and Mars hung close beside the moon, so that the two bodies seemed to lean into each other like partners in a dance neither had chosen but both knew well. [177] The celestial arrangement pressed a vibrant hush over the beach, the kind of silence that isn't empty but rather full -- stuffed with the sound of lava ticking against cooled basalt, with the low pull of tide, with the charged breath of a world mid-sentence. [221]

Marcus sat on the black sand and opened his bag. [231] He didn't mean to reach for the jar first. [240] His fingers simply found it, the way a tongue finds a sore tooth. [253]

The lightning bugs had been given to him by a girl named Pilar in Costa Rica, the daughter of a family he'd once funded without meeting. [279] She had pressed the jar into his hands and said, in the flat tone of a prophecy mistaken for instruction: keep them until the mountain drinks the moon. [307] He had laughed and thanked her and almost entirely forgotten it. [318] He hadn't forgotten it entirely. [323]

The volcano pulsed. [326] The eclipsed moon turned the color of old copper. [335] Without deciding to, Marcus uncorked the first vial. [343]

The lightning bug drifted out and rose, a single comma of green light ascending toward the red sky. [361] He opened the second, then the third, not out of ceremony but out of something he couldn't name -- a feeling like following a corridor that had always been there. [390] One by one the bugs lifted free, until eleven points of living light climbed the dark air between the ocean and the volcano, weaving patterns that corresponded to nothing and to everything. [422]

A whispering hush moved through him then, not from outside but from the center of his chest, a hush that felt textured, granular, like the black sand beneath his palms. [452] He had spent thirty years pressing money into the hands of strangers, trying to understand what it felt like when a thing changed -- not before, not after, but during. [481] What was the grain of it. [487] What was the weave. [491]

He had never found it in the clinics or the wells or the signed checks. [506] He found it here, watching the bugs move against the copper moon while the lava field cracked and resettled behind him, while the ocean kept its enormous patience ahead of him. [537] He found it in the understanding that he had orchestrated nothing -- that the rockslide had come, the detour had come, the eclipse had aligned without his consent -- and that he had nonetheless been the hinge on which all of it turned. [578] He was a man who gathered accidents the way certain stones gather heat, and what gathered around him completed itself, whether or not he intended it, whether or not he was watching. [610]

Pilar's prophecy had needed no interpreter. [616] It had only needed him to arrive, which he had done by stumbling, which was the only way he had ever arrived anywhere. [639]

The last lightning bug climbed until it joined the stars, or seemed to. [652] Marcus sat with his empty jar and his empty bag and the black sand teaching his palms its fine, volcanic grammar. [673] The dance of the celestial bodies continued without pause, indifferent and gorgeous, offering nothing but itself. [689]

He thought: so this is what transformation feels like from the inside. [701] It feels like sitting very still while everything moves. [710]
