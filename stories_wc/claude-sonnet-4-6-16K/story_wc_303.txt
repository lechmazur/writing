The velvet observatory smelled of pressed roses and old starlight. [10] Mira Voss had been here three midsummer days already, and still the ice crystal violin said nothing she could prove. [30] She set her coffee cup on the marble sill and waited. [41] The stain it left was her method, the only honest cartography she trusted. [54] In the brown ring's uneven edge she read the shape of what was coming: a corridor, a choice, a door that swung both ways. [78] She had learned, over eleven years of provenance research, that the future was not a locked room but a perforated thing, full of holes where light and consequence leaked through equally. [109]

The potted lemon tree in the corner shifted its leaves. [119] Mira felt the thought before she heard it, that low green murmur that had embarrassed her since childhood, the accidental telepathy that no specialist had ever quite explained. [147] The tree was telling her the violin was afraid. [156]

She crossed to the instrument case. [162] The violin lay on black velvet like a sleeping animal, entirely transparent, carved from a single formation of ice crystal that had, by all rights, never existed. [189] Ice crystal did not hold. [194] It did not persist through centuries. [200] Yet here it lay, solid and cold and singing faintly in the midsummer heat, its structure too perfect to be anything but deliberate. [223]

The empire had commissioned her to confirm the violin belonged to the Aldric dynasty. [237] A clean chain of custody. [242] Six hundred years of legitimacy, documented and sealed, and the empire's cultural ministry would use that document to propagate the claim across every archive, every academy, every velvet-draped hall of learning from the capital to the coast. [279] The dynasty's story would become the only story. [287] The violin would anchor it. [292]

Mira poured a second cup. [297] She watched the stain. [301] The brown ring spread into the shape of a woman playing music in a low stone room, surrounded not by empire but by a village so small it had no name on any map she had catalogued. [338] The stain darkened at the center. [344] The woman's face was upturned, and even in the imprecise language of dried coffee, Mira recognized the expression. [362] It was not performance. [366] It was prayer. [369]

The lemon tree murmured again. [374] It had felt the violin's fear more precisely now. [383] Not fear of her hands, but fear of silence. [392] Fear of being assigned a history that was not its own. [403]

Mira sat down on the observatory's velvet bench, the midsummer sky enormous and indifferent through the dome above her. [422] She thought about the dream she had carried since before she understood its weight: a small research institute, her own, built on honest work. [446] The empire's commission would fund it entirely. [453] One signature, one clean report, and she could stop tracing other people's objects and begin building something that belonged to her. [474]

She weighed this against what the coffee stain had shown her. [485] A dream against an empire. [490] It was not a fair scale. [496]

A provenance researcher's duty was to the object's truth, not to the convenience of its current holder. [513] She had said this at every lecture she had ever given. [524] She had believed it the way she believed in midsummer and in the slow patience of growing things. [542]

She picked up her pen. [547] The promise had not yet lost its glow, and she was running out of time to waste on cowardice. [566] She could write two reports. [571] She knew that. [574] She was smart enough to construct the lie. [582]

The ice crystal violin caught the afternoon light and scattered it across the velvet walls in cold, fractured color. [601] The lemon tree went quiet. [606] Even the plants, it seemed, would not tell her what to choose. [618]

She began to write the true report. [625] Word by careful word, she let the violin's real origin propagate outward from her pen, through the document, into the record that would outlast the empire's need for it. [654] A perforated destiny, she thought: hers had always had holes in it, places where the planned-for future simply fell away and left her standing in the open air. [682] The dream would have to wait. [688] The violin had told her everything, and she had no gift for unhearing. [701]
