The cobalt dock glistened after the rain, every plank wet and dark as the inside of a closed eye. [18] Maren came there because she had nowhere else to go with what she almost knew. [34] She had been carrying the half-spoken secret for three weeks, the words living in her throat like a stone that would not drop and would not rise. [61] The secret concerned her brother, and the canal, and the night he disappeared, and she could not finish a single sentence about it to anyone who asked. [88]

The guardian was already there. [93]

He was always already there. [98] Nobody in the district had a name for him, only a habit of not looking directly at him, the way you avoid looking at something that makes you feel too clearly understood. [131] He sat on the dock's edge with his legs folded, wearing clothes the color of the canal itself, and he was spiralingly calm in the way deep water is calm, revolving around a center that was perfectly, terrifyingly still. [170]

Maren sat beside him because something in her chest told her to. [182]

He did not speak. [186] He never spoke. [189] That was the thing people said about him in low voices, and that was also, she realized now, exactly what she needed. [213] No question. [215] No sentence waiting for her to complete it. [223] Just the wet planks and the cobalt sheen of the canal surface and the smell of rain still lifting off the stone. [244]

From his coat pocket he produced a dental mirror, the small angled instrument with its long handle, and she almost laughed at the strangeness of it. [271] But he tilted it without looking at her, catching the single lamp that burned at the far end of the dock, and he aimed the reflected point of light at the row of old warehouse shutters lining the far bank. [308]

Then he began to weave. [313]

That was the only word for what his wrist did: a slow, deliberate weaving motion, guiding the mirror's beam from shutter to shutter, each angled slat catching and redirecting the light, creating patterns with shutters that cascaded down the row like a conversation between closed doors. [356] The reflected points appeared on the canal's surface one by one, tiny and precise, until the dark water held a field of them. [379]

Stars. [380]

Her brother had gone into the dark, and she had been treating that darkness like a wound to be covered, a sentence to be left unfinished because finishing it might make it real. [477] But here was the guardian, patient and wordless, teaching the darkness around her the value of the stars still moving inside it. [500]

The silent empathy of it undid something in her chest. [510]

She did not cry. [514] She sat inside the stillwater tension of the moment, feeling the secret rise and rise in her throat, hovering at the very edge of being spoken, and she understood finally that she did not have to speak it, that he had already heard it in the way she sat, in the angle of her shoulders, in the particular silence she carried.
