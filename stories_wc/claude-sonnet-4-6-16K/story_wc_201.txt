Mara woke when the anesthesia wore off, not to pain exactly, but to the forest's pressure against every nerve ending she owned. [22] The trees here glowed indigo-white, ultraviolet light bleeding through bark as though the wood itself were burning in a frequency no ordinary eye could stand. [47] She could stand it. [51] That was part of what they had made her. [60]

After the interrogation -- after what they called "enhanced" and what she called by its older, plainer name -- the program had offered her a choice: prosecution or modification. [87] She had chosen modification because it sounded like survival. [96] What they gave her was proprioceptive enhancement, a rewiring of the sensory maps that told the body where it lived in space. [118] She could feel the soil density through her boot soles. [128] She could feel the angle of every branch above her, the weight of UV-saturated leaves pressing light downward like a kind of judgment. [151]

She called this place the penance forest, not because anyone had named it so, but because the light made everything she had done visible to her, layered and precise. [180]

The pewter mask strap was still in her coat pocket. [190] She had carried it for eleven months. [197] It had belonged to the man in the third session -- the one whose face she could no longer reconstruct from memory alone. [219] The strap had come loose when he shook his head and she had pocketed it without thinking, the way you pocket a coin, the way your hands do what your mind refuses to authorize. [253] She did not know why she still had it. [262] She suspected she was waiting for it to tell her something. [273]

She pressed her spine against a tree and felt the bark's grain through her jacket, through her skin, all the way to the vertebrae. [297] To brace herself this way had become ritual: solid contact, solid geography, the body located in space before the mind attempted anything more ambitious. [321]

The memories came back through the body first. [329] Her jaw tightened -- that meant the cold room. [337] Her shoulders rose toward her ears -- that meant hours sitting in the same position while questions were asked in one language and she answered in another. [363] Her left hand pressed flat against her thigh -- that meant she had been on the other side of the table. [383] She had been the one asking. [389]

This was how she remembered: not in images but in postures, not in sounds but in the shape her nervous system made around them. [413] The enhanced proprioception had not created this mechanism. [421] It had only sharpened what trauma had already built -- the body's precise archive of everything the mind tried to misfile. [441]

She had come here to work something out. [449] She thought of it as the mathematics of mercy, which sounded grandiose but felt, in practice, like a very small and specific problem: was there a threshold beyond which mercy became impossible, and if so, where exactly was it, and was she already past it, and did the location of the threshold change depending on which side of the table you had sat on. [513] She did not think mercy was simple. [520] She thought it was structural, like a proof, and that if you could find the correct axioms you could derive it even from ugly premises. [545]

The ultraviolet light pulsed gently between the trees, neither warm nor cold, and she understood that this was why she kept coming back. [568] The forest did not forgive her. [574] It did not condemn her. [579] It simply irradiated everything equally, the way mathematics did, without preference for the theorem or the variable or the error in the working. [602]

She pulled the pewter mask strap from her pocket and held it in the pale violet dark. [619] It was a small thing, six centimeters of oxidized metal. [629] She felt its weight with a precision most people reserved for instruments. [641] She did not know the man's name. [648] She knew the strap's mass, its temperature relative to her palm, the exact pressure required to hold it without dropping it. [669]

She thought: start there. [673] Start with what you can measure. [679] She pressed her back harder against the tree, felt the whole forest lean into her spine, and began. [697]
