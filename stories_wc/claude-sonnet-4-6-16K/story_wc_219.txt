Dr. Maren Solis had designed the experiment herself, which was the problem her department never forgave. [16]

She called it an artificial pilgrimage, a term that earned her three rejection letters and one committee hearing before anyone agreed to fund it. [39] The premise was simple enough to sound insulting: that the chemistry of homecoming, the oxytocin cascade, the cortisol drop, the particular warmth that floods the chest when a known place reasserts itself, could be manufactured without any authentic history at all. [74] She believed this because she had no choice but to believe it. [86] She had not been home in eleven years. [94]

The tower stood at the edge of the university's old botanical grounds, rainwashed and pale in the April light, its limestone face still wet from the morning's downpour. [124] Swallows cut arcs near the upper windows. [131] Everything smelled of opened earth. [136]

Maren arrived first, as she always did, holding the piece of fly amber she carried as a professional habit rather than a sentimental one. [160] She told herself this often. [165] The amber was a Cretaceous specimen, a small fly suspended mid-gesture inside the golden resin, its wings still angled as though it had been interrupted mid-thought. [191] She used it during lectures to illustrate the idea that preservation was not the same as return. [207] The fly was not home inside the amber. [215] The fly was simply stopped. [220]

She had made first contact with her subject three weeks ago, a terse email she now regretted for its clinical tone. [241] The subject, a man named Aldric who had spent six years abroad in fieldwork he described only as exhausting, had agreed to participate with a brevity that suggested he understood clinical tones very well. [279] They had spoken twice by phone since then, and each conversation had the texture of two people carefully not saying the real thing. [304]

Maren looked down the sculpture path that curved away from the tower through overgrown yew and elder. [321] The sculptures were old faculty commissions, abstract bronzes mostly, now wrapped in ivy and lichen, their original shapes readable only as suggestions. [345] Someone had walked the path recently, probably before the last rain, and their footprints were still pressed into the dusty grit between the stones, half-filled with pale water. [373] She followed them without deciding to, her own steps converging with the ghost of whoever had passed before. [390]

She found Aldric at the third sculpture, a figure that might once have been a woman or a wave, now barely distinguishable from the hedge behind it. [419] He was looking at the footprints too. [426]

He was not what she expected, though she had expected nothing precisely. [438] He had the quality she thought of privately as luminous skepticism, a brightness in the attention that refused to soften into either faith or cynicism, that held everything at the exact distance required to actually see it. [474] She recognized it because she had spent years cultivating the same thing and wondered, momentarily, if it showed on her face the way it showed on his. [501]

They walked without preamble toward the tower. [508]

"You think you can make me feel like I have come home to a place I have never been," he said, not unkindly. [530]

"I think the body does not verify," she said. [539] "I think recognition is chemistry. [545] I think if the sequence is right, the conclusion follows." [555]

"That is academically rebellious," he said. [561]

"It got me three rejection letters," she said. [569]

He almost smiled. [572] The spring air moved through the overgrown path and the wet tower caught the afternoon light and held it in that particular amber-bronze way that old stone does after rain. [604] Maren closed her fingers around the specimen in her pocket, the small fly fixed forever at the edge of motion, and felt the familiar argument stir in her chest: that preservation was not return, that stopped was not home. [644]

But here, where their separate trajectories had finally converged, where the dusty footprints and the wet stone and the quiet that hung between them had the weight and color of bronze, she was no longer certain the argument held. [685]

She was no longer certain that was a problem. [694]

The tower door opened easily, as though it had been waiting with patience rather than neglect, and they went in. [714]
