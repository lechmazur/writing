Mira kept her secrets the way the cavern kept its trees - in absolute darkness except for what she chose to illuminate. [20]

The orchard had no name on any map. [28] Forty trees, maybe more, grew from the mineral floor in gnarled columns, their roots threading between stalagmites, their pale branches angled upward toward stalactites that wept water in slow, patient drops. [61] The dripping nourished everything. [65] Mira had found the place six years ago, a week after Dalen left - not died, not disappeared entirely, but left in that particular way that erases the future without erasing the person - and she had recognized in the orchard's quiet persistence something she was not yet able to name. [114]

She returned after the orchard grew silent beneath the moon, when the bats had settled and the last leaf had stopped its trembling, because silence was the only condition under which the mathematics became visible. [148]

From her coat pocket she withdrew the glass eye pair. [158] She had taken them from a taxidermist's workbench years before the orchard, years before Dalen, when she was still learning what her hands could do. [180] They were brown, not her own color, and slightly larger than human eyes required. [194] She had enchanted them gradually, the way she did everything - not by forcing them open to new purpose but by suggesting possibilities until the glass agreed. [220] She was a gently inventive woman, this conjurer, and her magic bore that quality: patient, curious, never quite finished. [238]

She pressed the glass pair against her closed eyes and looked at the mosaic stepping stone. [254]

It lay at the orchard's center, the single flat surface in an otherwise uneven floor, and someone long before Mira had pressed colored tiles into it - blue, ochre, a deep arterial red. [286] Time and foot traffic had chipped the pattern into something partially illegible. [298] But through the glass eyes, the chips became a language. [308] The missing fragments did not reduce the message; they were the message. [320] Each gap was a coordinate, each crack a variable, and together they described via the chipped patterns on that mosaic stepping stone what she had spent years trying to quantify: the exact shape of losing someone who was still, technically, alive. [360]

Ambiguous loss, one grief book had called it. [368] Mira had rejected the term at first because ambiguous implied the loss was unclear, and to her it was devastatingly clear. [391] Dalen was gone from every place that mattered while remaining present in every legal and biological sense. [407] The ambiguity was not in the loss itself. [415] It was in the mathematics - in how you calculated the weight of an absence that carried no death certificate, no ceremony, no agreed-upon moment of ending. [444]

The chipped mosaic told her something new tonight. [452]

She traced the pattern with one careful finger, and through the glass eyes she watched the gaps and the surviving tiles dovetail - edge fitting against absence, presence locking against void - into a shape that resembled a proof. [490] Not a simple equation but a recursive one, where grief fed back into itself and emerged, fractionally smaller, on the other side. [514] The orchard dripped. [517] A drop struck a pale leaf above her and ran down the branch in a thin silver thread. [535]

She understood, in that moment, that the mathematics of emotion did not resolve. [548] It accumulated. [550] Every loss was added to the running total, and the running total was not a burden but a kind of ballast, weight that kept a person from floating away from their own life entirely. [584] Dalen had taken something. [588] The stone confirmed it. [592] But the taking had also, incrementally, made her more herself - more certain of what she valued, more deliberate in what she chose to grow. [618]

She tucked the glass eyes back into her pocket. [627]

The orchard was not optimistic in any showy way. [637] It did not bloom dramatically or perfume the cavern air with false sweetness. [649] It simply persisted in the dark, fed by what fell from above, growing toward a light it could not see but had apparently decided to trust. [675]

That, Mira thought, was the variable the grief books always omitted: the small, stubborn coefficient of continuing. [692]

She straightened, smoothed her coat, and walked out the way she had walked in - quietly, carrying her count, already planning what to conjure next. [717]
