The orchard had not known daylight in three hundred years. [11] Silver blossoms hung from apple trees like lanterns that forgot their fire, and the moon -- enormous, close, permanent -- pressed its pale face against the sky as though it had nowhere else to be. [44] Maren had grown up being told this was unnatural. [53] She had grown up being told a great many things. [63]

She stood at the orchard's heart, holding a strip of paper so small it seemed laughable against the enormity of what she carried in her chest. [89] The fortune had fallen from a cracked cookie at her brother Solus's funeral dinner, sliding across the lacquered table until it stopped beneath her palm: You will speak to what cannot answer. [121] Listen anyway. [123] She had folded it into her coat pocket and kept it there for eleven months, through winter and the cruelest kind of spring, waiting for the moment it would make sense. [154]

After the phoenix rises. [158] That was the phrase the elders used for the interval between one impossible thing and the next. [175] Solus had died a beast whisperer, the last in their line -- or so everyone assumed. [190] But Maren had always been able to hear the low grammar of animals, the vowel-sounds of wolves and the consonantal clicks of beetle wings. [214] She had simply never told anyone. [220] His death sealed her silence into something heavier than stone. [230]

Tonight the phoenix had risen. [235] She had watched it from the orchard's edge: a column of orange and gold erupting from the mountain's shoulder, silent as a word you cannot say aloud. [262] The ash had not yet finished falling when she unclenched the fortune from her fist and read it again by moonlight. [283]

She reached into her satchel for the bundle of spices -- dried dragon-tongue root, powdered star anise carried from the continent's far rim, a thumb-sized knot of black cardamom that smelled like incense burned inside a heartbeat. [319] The elders called this combination a cartography of the in-between. [329] She had stolen the recipe from Solus's journal, understanding it only after his absence made everything he'd written luminous with meaning. [350]

She crushed the spices between her palms and raised her hands to the silver dark. [365] The scent unfurled -- ancient, layered, almost gravitational. [372] It was via this that she intended to conjure him: not his body, not his ghost, but the specific frequency of trust that had lived between a beast whisperer and the creatures he loved. [406] The bond, she believed, did not require a living vessel. [416] It only required a voice willing to hold both ends. [426]

The orchard responded first. [430] The blossoms shivered on branches that had no wind to blame. [441] Then, from somewhere between the oldest trees, she heard it -- the low, tidal murmur of the great stag Solus had named Kael, dead two seasons before her brother. [469] Kael stepped forward into the moonlight, and he was not quite real, and he was not quite gone. [487]

Maren felt the metamorphosis begin. [492] It moved through her the way cold moves through water: not violent, but total. [506] Her hearing deepened past anything human. [512] The orchard's every creature -- moth and fox and the slow dreaming worm -- began transmitting in a language she could read without learning. [534] She did not want this. [539] She had never wanted to be the last of anything, to be appointed by grief to carry a gift forward. [559]

But she was paradoxically steady. [564] The change terrified her and she did not flinch. [573] She held the spice-scent cupped between her palms and kept her eyes on Kael's luminous face, and she spoke the first words a beast whisperer speaks: not a command, not a plea, but an acknowledgment. [608] I see you. [611] You are not only the past. [617]

Kael lowered his enormous head. [622]

The fortune fluttered from her fingers. [628] She did not retrieve it. [633] It had served its purpose, the way a key serves its purpose -- not by staying in the lock, but by turning it. [655]

The moonlight held everything in its enormous, unhurried glow, and the orchard breathed, and Maren breathed with it, changed and continuous, the bond reforged across the one border that had always seemed absolute. [688]
