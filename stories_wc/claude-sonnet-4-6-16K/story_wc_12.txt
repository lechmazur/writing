Father Dren was known for two things: his loop pedal and his silence. [13] The congregation of the Dimensional Anchor - a crumbling chapel wedged between what the old maps called the First Layer and the Hollow - understood that his silences were not empty. [42] He decorated with silence the way other priests decorated with gold leaf, pressing it into corners, letting it pool beneath the pews, shaping it into something that held weight. [71]

[92] Her eyes were the color of still water, and still water, he knew, was the first warning. [109] The possession had not completed - he could tell by the way her fingers still twitched at her own name, some residual self clinging to the surface. [135] He had seen the final stage before, when the fingers stopped. [146] There was time, but not much. [152]

[196] People always asked about the palette. [202] He never answered. [205] The question was the point. [210]

[260] What he said was this: "Do you believe the silence chose you, or that you chose the silence?" [278]

This was his method - to plumb through rhetorical devices, to send questions down like weighted lines into dark water, feeling for the floor of a person's hidden assumptions. Muffled clarity - that was the only way to describe what happened in a good exorcism.
