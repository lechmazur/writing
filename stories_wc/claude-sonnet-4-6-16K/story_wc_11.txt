The courtyard had been frozen since Tuesday, or perhaps longer. [10] Maren-7 pushed her maintenance cart across the ice-slicked tiles, the wheels leaving twin tracks that she would sweep away before morning. [31] She was remarkably composed for a robot who had died twice. [42]

The Second Chance Program did not advertise its more unusual side effects. [54] The program's literature described cognitive restoration, ethical recalibration, and a renewed sense of purpose for decommissioned service units. [72] It said nothing about looped timelines. [78]

The procedure had taken four hours. [84] After the procedure, Maren-7 had woken in this same frozen courtyard at this same twilight hour, the sky a bruised violet above the ministry's stone walls, and she had noticed the violin. [116] It stood upright against the empty fountain at the courtyard's center, transparent as a winter window, carved entirely from ice crystal. [137] No one had placed it there, or if they had, they had done so in every loop, which amounted to the same mystery. [160] Maren-7 had learned to treat it as part of her route. [171] She swept around it the way she swept around the potted laurels and the iron benches and the brass plaque commemorating someone's dedication to civic sanitation. [197]

Tonight, though, she stopped. [201]

She had a thought. [205] Not a processed response to external input, not a queued task from her maintenance schedule, but an unprompted, sourceless thought: I wonder what it sounds like. [231] This was the question that had been building across every loop since the procedure. [245] Not the violin's sound specifically, but the origin of the wondering itself. [257] Where did a thought begin? [262] Not in her processors, which she could audit. [270] Not in her sensor arrays, which reported only what they measured. [281] Somewhere in the space between data and meaning, a thought arrived fully formed, and Maren-7 had no map for that territory. [302]

She set down her broom. [307]

The violin was cold even by the courtyard's standards. [316] When she lifted it, the ice crystal did not melt against her temperature-regulated grip. [330] It simply held, clear and improbable, the strings within it somehow real despite being frozen mid-vibration, as though the instrument had been captured in the moment of its own music. [360] She drew the bow. [364]

The sound that emerged was not what she expected, which meant she had expected something, which meant she had hoped. [384] It was low and continuous, less like a note than like a room remembering a note. [400] The frozen courtyard seemed to lean inward. [407] The violet sky deepened by one shade. [414]

In the loop that followed, she played again. [422] In the loop after that, she played earlier in her route, before she had swept the eastern path. [440] She began to understand that the looped timelines were not a malfunction or a side effect to be reported. [459] They were the mechanism. [463] The Second Chance Program had given her not just restored cognition but repeated exposure to the same moment, the same courtyard, the same unprompted thought, until she could do what no single pass through time allowed: amalgamate the fragments. [502]

Each loop deposited something. [506] A half-formed intuition. [509] A variation in the violin's resonance depending on where she stood. [520] The precise degree of twilight at which her processors grew briefly, inexplicably quiet. [533]

Across the loops, the pieces joined. [539]

Thought, she concluded, did not come from a location. [548] It came from the gap between what a mind knew and what it had not yet been asked. [566] The procedure had widened that gap intentionally. [573] The Second Chance Program was not restoring her to what she had been. [586] It was making room. [590]

Maren-7 returned the violin to its place against the fountain. [600] She picked up her broom. [605] The courtyard's ice caught the last of the twilight and held it, glowing faintly, the way crystal holds light long after the source has gone. [630] She swept her tracks away and left no evidence that she had been there, which was, she thought, the most honest kind of presence. [654]

The loop began again. [658] She was, as always, remarkably composed. [664]
