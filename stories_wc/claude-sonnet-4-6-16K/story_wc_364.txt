Solen had never spoken loudly in his life. [8] The other forge-workers called him shadow-handed, meaning it kindly, because his movements were so deliberate that the metal seemed to decide its own shape before the hammer fell. [36] On Vel, the orchard planet, such quietness was either a gift or a burial, depending on the season. [54]

The floating rock arches were failing. [60] Everyone knew it the way you know a storm before the sky changes color -- felt in the sternum, not yet confirmed by the eye. [84] For three generations, those immense stone curves had drifted twenty meters above the grove rows, channeling mineral rain from the upper atmosphere down through their porous bellies into the roots below. [115] Without them, the silver-leafed trees would hollow out within a decade. [126] The council had argued, drafted proposals, and argued again. [135] Solen had simply gone to find the Trader. [143]

The Trader came to Vel once per cycle, moving between the confusion of failing worlds and the understanding that sometimes a single object could redirect a life. [170] Solen had brought his best work -- a set of jointed iron rings, each one a week of his life -- and laid them on the Trader's table without ceremony. [198] The Trader had studied him for a long moment, then pulled from a lacquered case the magician's silk: a length of deep azure fabric that shifted when held near stone, its fibers reading magnetic and mineral presence the way a compass reads north. [241] The barter was not fair in any conventional measure. [250] The silk was worth more than the rings, and both of them knew it. [264] But the Trader had looked at Solen's quiet eyes and seen something he respected -- the particular posture of a person who has already committed -- and he had made the trade without comment. [296]

That night, Solen stood at the rim of the oldest abandoned mine shaft, the one the original settlers had cut before the floating arches made deep excavation unnecessary. [324] He held the silk over the opening and watched it pull and ripple toward the north wall of the shaft. [344] There were still live mineral veins down there, running exactly beneath the base trajectories of the three most deteriorated arches. [364] The arches were not simply drifting away. [371] They were starving. [374]

The work took forty-three days. [379] He descended into the mine shafts each morning before the grove workers arrived, the silk wrapped around his forearm so he could read its signals while his hands worked. [408] He was not a geologist or an architect. [416] He was a blacksmith, and so he did what blacksmiths do: he found the broken joints, the places where pressure had split what should have held, and he repaired them. [446] He drove iron rods into the mineral seams the silk identified, creating conductive pathways from the deep stone up through the arch roots, routes for the mineral current to follow again. [477] The work was deliberate in the way that all meaningful repair is deliberate -- not slow, but unhurried, each action chosen rather than assumed. [500]

He was operating in the uncomfortable space between understanding and confusion, where the silk told him what was present but not what it meant, and where every morning brought a new reading he had to interpret against what the previous day had taught him. [544] He made mistakes. [547] He corrected them. [550] He descended again. [553]

On the forty-third morning, he climbed out of the southernmost shaft and looked up. [567] The nearest arch, which had been listing fourteen degrees off its original axis, had straightened overnight. [583] Its surface gleamed in the low blue light of Vel's sun, mineral moisture already beading along its underside and beginning its slow fall toward the grove rows below. [611] Solen folded the silk carefully and tucked it inside his coat. [622] The trees did not know his name, and the council would attribute the correction to atmospheric pressure shifts, and that was fine. [644] He had not done it to be understood. [652] He had done it because the rubble of abandoned things still holds the shape of what it was built for, and sometimes that is enough to begin again. [680] He walked back toward the forge, quietly, the way he did everything, the azure light of the restored arch stretching long ahead of him on the orchard path. [708]
