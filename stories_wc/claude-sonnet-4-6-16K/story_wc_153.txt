Sera came to the mangrove labyrinth at low tide, when the roots arched from black water like the ribs of something enormous and patient. [24] She carried one thing: a shard of a gargoyle's rainspout, gray and porous, the broken mouth still curved into a snarl. [45] She had pulled it from a demolished cathedral a decade ago, not knowing then that it held the property of drawing voices from the air. [70]

The spirits started singing before she cleared the first bend. [80] Their music was not beautiful -- it was the sound of things remembered wrong, half-syllables stretched over reed-thin harmonics -- and she walked into it the way a person walks into cold water, choosing discomfort because the alternative is standing still forever. [120]

She was a medium, though she hated the word. [129] It suggested a middle thing, neither here nor there. [138] She preferred to think of herself as a finder, someone who located the precise moment before a decision collapsed into consequence. [159] Tonight, she needed to find that moment for herself. [168]

Her mother had left a box under the floorboards: forty-three unsent letters, each addressed to Sera, each written in the months before she disappeared. [192] Forty-three attempts at explanation or apology, all of them sealed, all of them kept. [206] The keeping was the thing that undid her. [214] If you seal a letter and hide it, you have not forgiven yourself. [227] You have only deferred the sentence. [233] Sera needed to find the moment her mother sat with pen lifted, deciding not to send them. [250] Not to accuse. [253] To understand. [255]

Deep in the labyrinth, where three channels converged and the mangrove canopy sealed out the sky, there was a structure -- a rusted observation box left by some surveyor a century past, its single window still intact. [291] The spirits circled it, their singing rising into something almost unbearable. [302] Condensation beaded on the glass. [307]

Sera pressed the gargoyle shard flat against the pane and waited. [318] This was her method, the one she never explained to clients: the shard could evoke impressions stored in moisture, in the breath that had once touched glass, in any surface that remembered warmth. [351] She moved it slowly, and letters formed -- not carved, but cleared, as though fingers were brushing the fog from the other side. [373]

I am trying, the condensation read. [379] I do not know how to be your mother and also be this ruined. [393]

Sera pressed her forehead to the cold window. [401] The apology had always been there, waiting in the space between her mother's hand and the mailbox, in the held breath that never fully released. [426] She had carried that unspoken weight as her own guilt for years, the way children do, believing absence is their fault. [447]

The moonlight broke through a gap in the canopy. [456] Where it touched the shard in Sera's hand, the stone grew warm. [468] The hairline crack along its jaw began to knit, slowly, the way a wound closes when you finally stop pressing it. [489] She had read about this property in her grandmother's notes: the shard heals in moonlight, and something in the healing carries over. [511] Sera felt it move through her palm and up her arm like a slow current. [526]

The spirits quieted. [529]

She stayed until the condensation dried and the glass went dark and still. [542] She did not find what she expected -- not her mother's voice, not a scene, not the answer to why. [561] She found something smaller and more necessary: the exact texture of the moment before deciding, the way it feels to love someone and still not know how to reach them. [591]

She carried the shard out of the labyrinth the way she had carried the letters home. [607] Carefully. [608] Without pretending they were lighter than they were. [616]

At the treeline, she sat in the full moonlight and took out the first letter, the oldest one. [634] She broke the seal. [638] She read her mother's handwriting -- rushed, tender, terrified -- and felt nothing close to healed. [652] She felt close to honest instead. [658]

In the mangrove labyrinth behind her, the spirits resumed their off-key singing, and she let it carry her the rest of the way home. [682]
