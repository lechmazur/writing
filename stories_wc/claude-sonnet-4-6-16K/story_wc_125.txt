The energy beings arrived three weeks ago, and nobody agreed on what to call them. [15] Some said angels, some said mathematics made visible, some said nothing at all because language felt suddenly embarrassed of itself. [35] Maren had her own word: witnesses. [41]

She was a drifting roadside poet, the kind who sold hand-lettered verses from a folding table at truck stops and crossroads, who slept in her car when the motels ran too expensive and in the motels when loneliness ran too deep. [82] When the beings appeared — luminous, slow-turning, hovering at the edges of perception like the last word of a song you almost remember — she did not run. [108] She drove toward them instead, following their drift south until she found the terra-cotta grotto carved into the base of a chalky hill, its walls the color of old blood and warm bread. [141]

She arrived at night. [145] The grotto opened like a mouth mid-sentence, and starlight pooled inside it in ways that defied the geometry overhead. [164] The beings circled the entrance slowly, their glow pulsing in something close to a rhythm, close to a breath. [183]

Maren pulled from the back seat a frayed coil of rope. [194] It had belonged to a man named Solis, who had used it to climb down three stories of prison wall seven years ago and had pressed it into her hands at a roadside in Tucson without explanation, the way people sometimes give poets the things they cannot carry. [242] The rope was ugly, practical, storied — the kind of object that had already made its most important decision. [260]

She brought it into the grotto. [266]

The walls were layered, she noticed — paint over plaster over carved sandstone over something older still, each stratum a different era's idea of shelter. [290] She began to run her fingers along them, by uncovering layers she had learned she could sometimes feel the texture of time directly, the way a geologist reads pressure in stone. [321] This was not metaphor. [325] Since the beings arrived, sensation had grown more honest. [334] Touch told you things. [338]

Beneath the topmost layer of ochre wash, her fingernails found a pattern — spirals repeating at precise intervals, a grammar of circles pressed by hands long still. [364] She traced them until her heart steadied. [371]

That was when the choice appeared. [377]

Not announced, not dramatic. [381] Just suddenly present, the way a cliff edge is present when fog lifts. [394] The beings outside were offering something — she understood this the way you understand cold, through the body before the mind. [414] They were offering to absorb her, not destroy her, but dissolve the boundary of her, so that she could feel time not as a river but as a fabric, could touch its every thread simultaneously. [449] She would become exactly what she had always wanted: a creature made entirely of texture and witness. [466]

The rope lay coiled at her feet. [473]

And here was the impossible choice, the kind with no clean seam: becoming what you most desire by ceasing to be the one who desired it. [499] She was ethically playful by nature — she had always believed that the right question mattered more than the right answer, that morality was a kind of creative practice, a living improvisation rather than a fixed score. [535] But even she found no playfulness here. [542] Only weight. [544]

She picked up the rope. [549] She held the frayed end, the end that had scraped brick and caught a man's desperate hands on his way to freedom. [571] She thought about Solis, who had made his impossible choice and handed her its evidence without apology. [588]

She set the rope down again, gently, like a period at the end of a sentence she respected. [606]

Then she stepped toward the grotto's mouth. [613] The beings drew near, patient as epochs. [620] She did not surrender. [624] She reached out one hand and pressed it flat against the nearest one, the way she had pressed her palm to the grotto wall, by uncovering layers, by choosing contact over dissolution, texture over transcendence. [659]

It pulsed. [661] She felt years like grain in wood. [668] She remained herself — smaller than she had hoped, larger than she had feared. [681]

Behind her, the spiral pattern on the ancient wall seemed, briefly, to continue. [693]

She took out her notebook. [699] She wrote one word, then crossed it out, then wrote it again. [711] The beings waited. [714] The stars did not move, but the light they made did, pooling and shifting in the grotto like something almost ready to speak. [737]

Maren stayed until dawn, which was the only answer she had, and which, for now, was enough. [754]
