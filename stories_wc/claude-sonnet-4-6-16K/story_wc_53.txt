The carousel stopped three days ago, or perhaps three years. [10] Maren had stopped trusting the difference. [16]

She moves through the kinetic lagoon with practiced silence, her boots finding purchase on the wet membrane of cloud-crust that holds the water aloft. [40] The lagoon churns and tilts, its surface always migrating north by northeast, pulling debris in slow ellipses around the old carousel platform. [62] When the carousel ran, it generated enough centrifugal drag to keep the whole system stable. [77] Now it stood still, and the lagoon was slowly unwinding itself toward dissolution. [90]

Maren didn't mind dissolution. [94] She had been dissolving for years. [100]

From her jacket pocket she pulled the disposable camera wheel -- the thumbscrew advance mechanism she had salvaged from a camera body that had fallen from somewhere lower, somewhere real. [129] She turned it between her fingers as she walked, the clicking teeth counting nothing. [143] It was the only timepiece she trusted precisely because it measured nothing at all. [157]

She was chronologically confused in the clinical sense: her neurologist, back when she had one, had called it temporal dissociation following the incident. [180] What that meant, practically, was that Tuesday could feel like 1987, that the smell of salt water could convince her she was nine years old, that she sometimes reached for a phone she hadn't owned in a decade. [218] The lagoon's constant motion helped. [223] When everything shifted, her own shifting felt less like damage. [233]

She made her living by collecting fragments of overheard stories. [243] Travelers passed through the lagoon on the cloud-ferry, and Maren drifted close, her head angled, her notebook open. [261] A woman describing her daughter's wedding. [267] An old man mapping his brother's death. [274] Teenagers arguing about a band that had broken up. [283] She stitched these pieces into longer narratives, sold them to the memory brokers who worked the lower platforms. [301] They never asked where the stories came from. [309] Everyone up here was assembled from scraps. [316]

But today she wasn't working for the brokers. [324]

Today, in the stillness after the carousel halted, she was looking for herself. [337] Not her present self -- she had resigned herself to this version, this resolute woman who moved through cloud-water with certainty because certainty was a posture she had mastered. [365] She was looking for the ghost of the self she had been before ambition had worn smooth, before every plan had been ground down by years of postponement until nothing remained but the grooves where the plans used to be. [405]

She had wanted to be a photographer. [412] That was the eroded thing. [417] She had owned thirty cameras, had studied composition and light until she could read a room the way a musician reads a score. [440] And then the incident, and then the temporal confusion, and then the long series of smaller surrenders, each one feeling like practicality, until practicality had consumed everything she had meant to become. [472]

The ghost appeared, as ghosts do, in the surface of the water. [484] She was younger in the reflection -- not dramatically, maybe seven years -- and her eyes held something that Maren recognized the way you recognize a language you used to speak. [513] Expectation. [514] The ghost was still expecting something good to arrive. [523]

Maren crouched at the lagoon's edge, the cloud-crust compressing softly under her weight. [536]

"I'm not coming back to where you are," she said. [546] "That's not the negotiation." [550]

The ghost rippled but held its shape. [557]

"What I want," Maren said, "is for you to stop pulling." [568] "Every time I almost settle, I feel you." [576] "The weight of what you were planning." [583] She turned the camera wheel in her fingers. [591] Click. [592] Click. [593] Click. [594] "I kept this because you would have used it." [603] "I'm keeping it for you." [608] "But I need you to let the rest of it go." [619]

The lagoon drifted northeast. [623] The carousel platform receded behind her. [629]

She had collected enough of other people's stories to know that regret didn't dissolve -- it transformed. [645] It became the medium you moved through, the cloud-crust under your feet, the thing that held you up precisely because it refused to be nothing. [670]

The ghost did not answer. [675] Ghosts rarely did. [678]

But the water stilled, briefly, in that fractured way that the lagoon allowed -- a breath between movements, a held chord -- and Maren took it as terms accepted. [705]

She stood. [707] She walked north by northeast. [712] She kept the wheel. [716]
