Queen Mara had stopped wearing her crown three seasons ago, not out of rebellion but out of exhaustion. [18] The gold felt like a hand pressing down on her skull, and she was already weighted enough by the dreams. [38] Every night the same vision: a grotto burning cobalt above the clouds, and starlight that moved like blood through a wound. [59]

Her advisors had issued warnings in sequence, each more dire than the last. [72] After the final warning — a sealed letter threatening her removal from the throne if she left the palace grounds again — she laced her boots before dawn. [98]

She was disenchanted with ruling, disenchanted with stone corridors and petitioners and the careful theater of power. [115] But she was not disenchanted with understanding. [122] The dreams were pulling her somewhere real, and she meant to follow. [134]

The ridge above the Ashwood took four hours to climb. [144] She went alone, which scandalized no one because no one knew. [155] The path was faint, threaded between dark pines, and the air grew thinner and sweeter as she ascended. [173] She had brought no torch. [178] The ember grotto on the ridge was said to glow on its own — an old shepherd's claim she had dismissed for years, until the dreams began showing her its exact silhouette. [209]

By the time she reached the upper elevation, clouds had pooled below her like a white inland sea. [227] The world below disappeared. [231] She stood on the edge of that erasure and breathed. [241]

The grotto was ahead, its mouth carved into basalt, its interior pulsing with a deep amber warmth she could feel against her face from thirty paces. [267] But it was the sky above her that stopped her feet. [278]

Starlight was bleeding. [281]

That was the only word for it. [288] The stars above the cloudline were not points but slow rivulets, luminous threads running downward as if the sky had been cut along invisible seams. [313] The light gathered at the grotto's lip and pooled inside, shifting from amber to cobalt, cobalt to violet, a wound that had learned to be beautiful. [339]

She moved into it. [343]

The cobalt grotto above the clouds was smaller than the dreams had suggested, but more honest. [359] No mythic grandeur, just ancient rock and this impossible radiance soaking the walls. [372] The air held something like intention. [378]

Then she heard the exhale behind her. [385]

She turned, softly commanding in the way that years of rule had made involuntary — shoulders settled, chin level, voice already forming authority before she had decided to speak. [413] A girl stood at the grotto entrance, fourteen perhaps, wearing the rough wool of a ridge shepherd. [430] She had followed the same faint path, apparently, drawn by something she could not name either. [446]

They stared at each other across the bleeding light. [455]

The girl pointed upward. [459] The first fireflies of summer had appeared above the grotto's mouth, drifting through the starlight threads, their small green pulses tangling with the larger luminescence in a way that made the sky look like it was breathing. [496] Queen Mara exhaled slowly, and the girl exhaled with her, that shared breath a kind of language neither had planned. [516] In that shared exhale spotting the first fireflies of summer, something in Mara's chest loosened — the tight narrative she had built around her dreams, around her purpose, around the lonely sovereignty of knowing things no one else could see. [555]

The girl sat down on the grotto floor without asking permission. [566] Mara sat beside her. [570]

The bleeding starlight continued its slow descent, lacy and menacing in the way that beautiful dangerous things always are, gorgeous precisely because they could ruin you if you stared too long. [601] Mara understood now that the dreams had not been warning her. [612] They had been inviting her to this exact stillness, this stone floor, this girl who had climbed the same dark path for reasons she would probably never fully articulate. [641]

The mystery of the recurring dreams was not a puzzle to be solved. [654] It was a direction. [658]

She had followed it after the final warning, past every boundary others had drawn for her, up through the dark and cold, into the cobalt light above the world. [687]

She was disenchanted with ruling. [692] But here, in the ember grotto's glow, with fireflies threading through starlight and a shepherd girl breathing beside her, she found herself enchanted with something else entirely. [719]

She did not know what to call it yet. [728] She supposed that was the point. [734]
