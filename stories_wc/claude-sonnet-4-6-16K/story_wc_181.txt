Maren had not expected the orchard to be in bloom when she arrived. [13]

The asteroid was supposed to be barren -- that was what the inheritance papers said, the ones she received three years before her grandmother died and seven years after her grandmother's own grandmother had filed the original deed. [50] Nonlinear inheritance was simply complicated: property that passed forward and backward through a bloodline like water finding its own level, arriving in the hands of whoever the orchard chose. [79] Maren had never heard of land choosing its heir before, but the papers were notarized, and the orchard was blooming, and she was here. [103]

The trees were silver-barked, their blossoms the color of light seen through closed eyelids. [117] They grew in rows across the asteroid's curved surface, roots gripping the rock as if they had always meant to be there, as if the loneliness of this small spinning world had called them into being. [153] Maren walked among them with her curator's instinct -- catalog, assess, preserve -- though nothing she had curated before had ever breathed back at her. [176]

She carried the object with her everywhere: a pistachio shell cameo blank, smooth and uncarved, small enough to hide in a closed fist. [199] She had found it tucked inside the inheritance papers, between the first and last page of a document that seemed to have no middle, only its beginning and its end pressed together like a held breath. [235] The shell was warm to the touch even in the chill between stars. [248] She did not know what it was for. [256] She did not know why she could not set it down. [267]

On her third night in the orchard, she went to inquire of the oldest tree -- a gnarled thing at the asteroid's northern tip whose blossoms were deepest gold. [295] She had read somewhere, in a text the university had locked behind seven permissions she had broken through one by one, that the orchard was a repository, and that its caretaker could ask it questions if they knew how to listen. [336] Pursuing forbidden knowledge had felt romantic before she arrived. [345] Now it felt simply necessary, the way breathing felt necessary, the way she could not stop turning the pistachio shell between her fingers. [368]

The tree did not answer. [373]

She went to wash her hands and think. [381]

The bathroom was a small pressurized module she had attached to the asteroid's maintenance hub, barely large enough to turn around in, lit by a single amber strip along the ceiling. [412] It was there -- as it had always been, in every bathroom she had ever stood in, since she was nine years old crying over a failed exam -- that the understanding arrived. [443] Having philosophical breakthroughs in bathrooms was her most reliable method, undignified and absolutely hers. [457]

The cameo blank was an unwritten question. [464]

The orchard was not a library. [470] It was a conversation that had started before she was born and would continue after she was gone, a sentence spoken across generations that did not move in order. [499] Her grandmother had received the deed after death because she had needed it then. [513] Maren had received it now because she needed it now, between the first and last page of her own bewilderment, standing in the middle of something she could not yet name. [544]

She pressed the cameo blank against the cold mirror. [553]

When she returned to the orchard, the oldest tree had dropped a single blossom onto the rock at its roots. [573] She picked it up. [577] It was warm, like the shell. [583] She placed the blossom against the blank's smooth face, and something -- not a voice, not exactly -- moved through her the way inheritance moves: sideways, quietly, without permission. [610]

She understood then that she was not the curator of the orchard. [622] The orchard was the curator of her. [629] It had been tending her questions for years before she thought to ask them. [643] It would tend them for years after she forgot she had. [654] This was simply complicated and also, somehow, brightened -- the disquiet of being known by something ancient and patient and indifferent to the order of time. [679]

Maren sat down among the silver roots and let herself be held. [691] She did not know what the cameo blank would eventually carry -- whose face, whose story, which page of which life. [711] She only knew that she was somewhere in the middle of it, and that the middle was enough. [729]

The blossoms fell around her like slow questions finding their answers. [740]
