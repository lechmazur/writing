The palace hung like a chandelier forgotten by god, its spires lashed to the cathedral ceiling of the world by chains of iron and amber resin, each stalactite a pillar older than the kingdoms that had rusted above. [38] Prince Edric had not bathed in eleven days. [46] His coat was torn at both elbows, his boots held together by copper wire he had stripped from a broken automaton, and his hair hung in salt-stiff ropes across his brow. [79] None of that mattered to him. [85]

What mattered was the egg. [90]

It rested in a nest of velvet scraps at the center of his workbench, a pale ovoid the color of fog before sunrise, speckled with rust-colored flecks no larger than a pinprick. [124] It had belonged to the Varenthian songbird, a species extinguished two centuries ago when the great gears of the Mechanist Lords had ground the last natural forests into lumber for their engines. [157] Edric had found it sealed inside a brass reliquary in the palace's deepest archive, preserved through some accident of cold and dryness, its contents unhatched and, possibly, still alive. [187]

He moved through the laboratory the way he moved through every problem: hypothesizing about everything. [202] He muttered questions at the ceiling, at the condensation dripping from the stalactite above him, at the egg itself. [221] If warmth could restart what cold had suspended, how much warmth, at what rate, applied to which surface? [239] If the shell was porous, had the embryo desiccated, or had the low humidity acted as a preservative? [257] If it had been preserved, what was the probability of a flawed genesis, a chick that emerged broken by its own long dormancy? [281] He wrote nothing down because the palace had run out of paper, but the hypotheses stacked in his mind like cards, trembling. [304]

The mechanical world above did not care about trembling. [313] The gears did not care about eggs. [320] The Mechanist Lords had sent Edric to this suspended palace as a soft exile, believing a prince who wept at the sound of music was no prince at all. [351] They were not wrong that he was unsuited to their world. [362] They were wrong that his unsuitability was a flaw. [371]

He had assembled the incubation apparatus himself, piece by piece, over nine days. [384] He pulled warmth from the palace's steam pipes, filtered it through a copper coil, and directed it into a ceramic chamber lined with goose down salvaged from a ruined mattress. [416] To assemble something with no blueprint, no precedent, nothing but the stubborn architecture of hope, was the only act of governance he had ever found meaningful. [444]

On the eleventh night, the egg trembled. [451]

Edric pressed his ear to the ceramic chamber and heard it: a sound like a fingernail dragged once across silk. [471] Then silence. [473] Then again, a little harder. [478] His breath fogged the air. [483] The palace swayed almost imperceptibly on its chains, as it always did when the underground winds moved through the cavern below, and the motion felt, in that moment, like the world leaning in. [518]

The shell split along its longest axis just before dawn. [528] The chick that emerged was small, wet, and asymmetrical, one wing slightly shorter than the other, its beak crooked by some pressure of the shell or the centuries or both. [560] It was a flawed genesis, exactly as he had hypothesized it might be. [573] It was also alive. [577]

It opened its beak and produced a sound that had not existed in the world for two hundred years. [596] Not beautiful in the way that mechanical music was beautiful, precise and repeating and cold. [611] Beautiful in the way that error is beautiful, the way a cracked bell still rings. [626] Bitterly sweet, that sound, because it meant the world had once held this and had chosen gears instead, and yet here the sound was, insisting. [653]

Edric sat on the floor of his laboratory, his torn coat around his knees, and wept in a way that was not grief and not joy but the exact seam between them. [687] Through the discovery, he understood at last what he had been trying to do, not restore a dead thing, but prove that beauty did not ask permission to return. [718] The crooked bird called out again into the cold air of the suspended palace, and somewhere far above, a gear, stressed past measure, cracked cleanly in two. [747]
