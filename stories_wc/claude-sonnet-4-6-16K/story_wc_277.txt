Vera Aldridge did not inherit her family's newspaper empire graciously. [10] She accepted it the way a planet accepts a moon -- not by wanting, but by the mathematics of gravity. [29] The ink-stained legacy of the Aldridge Press had circled her entire life, and she had long ago mapped the orbits of regret that came with it: the summers lost to board meetings, the letters her father never wrote because he was always writing for everyone else. [75]

The journalist called on a Tuesday, when the clouds were low and Vera was already climbing. [91] The observatory sat above the weather, above the valley's gossip, above everything her family had built their fortune reporting. [110] She came here devotedly, recording each night's coordinates in a ledger she kept sealed with a brass clasp, a habit so faithful it had become its own kind of worship. [140] Tonight, she tucked her phone into her coat pocket and pressed upward through the iron staircase, the journalist's words still vibrating in her ears. [164] He claimed the Aldridge Press had suppressed a story thirty years ago -- a story about her father -- and that he had documents to prove it. [189]

She did not panic. [193] She observed. [195]

At the top, the dome was already open. [203] Cold air poured in like a second sky. [211] Vera pulled from her breast pocket the rosewood pen nib her father had given her at fourteen, the day he told her she had a writer's eye even if she preferred stars to sentences. [245] She never used it to write. [251] She used it to think, rolling the warm grain of wood between her fingers the way other people count beads. [271]

The journalist had been specific. [276] He said the Aldridge Press had run a story in 1993 crediting a whistleblower's leak to an anonymous source. [295] He said her father had invented that source. [303] He said the leak had actually come from her father's own competitor -- a man who later went to prison for fraud -- and that protecting the source had protected a criminal. [333]

Vera turned the pen nib in her fingers and looked up at Jupiter, steady and indifferent. [349] She was devotedly observant enough to know when something did not fit the sky it was placed in. [367]

If her father had fabricated an anonymous source to protect a fraudulent man, then the Aldridge Press would have eventually benefited from that man's silence. [392] But the Press had run three major investigations into financial corruption in the nineties, two of which directly targeted associates of the very man the journalist named. [419] No one protects their alibi and then systematically dismantles his network. [430] The geometry was wrong. [434]

This was proof by contradiction. [439] The journalist's premise, if true, required her father to be both complicit and his own adversary. [455] You could not hold both stars in the same hand and call it a single constellation. [471]

She did not dismiss the journalist. [477] Hidden truths had a way of hiding inside almost-truths, and she had not climbed above the clouds simply to confirm what she already believed. [501] But she understood now that she would need to intervene -- not to protect her father's reputation, which could survive scrutiny, but to find whatever small, frightened truth was actually orbiting at the center of the journalist's claim. [538]

She pressed the rosewood nib against her palm and felt its familiar point. [551] Her father had taught her that a nib is nothing without pressure. [563] You have to lean in. [568] You have to mean it. [573]

The echo of the dome carried a low sound across the telescope housing, solemn and musical in the same breath, and she found herself almost wanting to laugh. [601] Here she was -- heiress to ten thousand column inches, standing above the clouds, solving her dead father's riddles with a piece of wood and a planet for a witness. [630]

She pulled out her phone and called the journalist back. [640] "You are missing a document," she said, when he answered. [650] "I know because the story you are telling is impossible without it." [662] "Meet me tomorrow." [665] "Bring everything." [667]

She hung up and looked again at Jupiter, which had not moved, which never hurried, which kept its faithful orbit regardless of what the newspapers said. [693] The clouds below shifted and parted, and for a moment she could see the valley's lights -- small, warm, and entirely unaware of what was coming. [718]
