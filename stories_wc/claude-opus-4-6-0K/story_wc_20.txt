[50] Now they left with nothing but wet boots and the quiet consensus that some people choose to stay lost. [68]

[89] She was known in the valley as the balanced alchemist, a title she had earned not through temperance but through an exacting commitment to equilibrium — every transmutation she performed required something given for something gained, every healing demanded an honest reckoning with harm. [128] The balance was not metaphor. [133] It was factually mystical, written into the substrate of the world as surely as gravity or grief. [148]

[180] On her workbench lay the zodiac coin — a disc of pale electrum stamped with all twelve signs in a spiral that wound inward toward a blank center. [207] It had belonged to the boy. [213] He had arrived at her door eight months ago carrying nothing else, saying his name was Fen, and she had not asked further questions. [236]

That was her failure. [240] She had not asked because she understood the weight of names — how a name could be a brand, a sentence, a cage built from two syllables. [263] Her own name, Maren, meant "of the sea," and she had spent thirty years landlocked in this mountain foundry, transmuting base metals and broken things, as if distance from the water could unmake the word. [299] She had assumed Fen carried a similar burden, that his silence was a kindness he offered himself. [316] She had respected it, and in doing so, she had failed to see the fractures running through him. [333]

Kintsugi. [334] The old art of mending broken pottery with gold, making the damage part of the beauty. [349] Maren had adapted the principle to her alchemical practice — healing shattered vessels, rejoining fractured alloys, filling the cracks with something more precious than what had been whole. [377] But kintsugi required you to first gather all the pieces, and she had not gathered Fen's. [394] She had let him keep his shards hidden, and when pressure came, he had simply come apart. [411]

She picked up the zodiac coin and held it near the furnace light. [424] The blank center where a thirteenth sign should have been caught the glow and threw it back, luminous and empty. [443] She understood now that the coin was a question Fen had been asking her all along, one she had been too balanced, too measured, too respectful of boundaries to answer. [472] Who am I if none of these signs claim me? [481]

Maren closed her hand around the coin and made her decision. [492] She would go where the search party had not — not outward into the hills, but inward, into the limits of her own craft. [515] She would push past the equilibrium she had always maintained, risk the imbalance, and forge something that could call him home. [536]
