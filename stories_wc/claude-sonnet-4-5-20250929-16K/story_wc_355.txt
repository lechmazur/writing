[28] She had never believed in violence, not even the violence of forcing open a lock. Her sister Elena had been a humanitarian aid worker, fluent in seven languages but increasingly unable to speak about what she witnessed. [250] The diary was her final possession, locked the day before she died, its contents a mystery that had haunted Marcella like an unresolved chord. [274]
