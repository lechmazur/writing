[24] She'd been trapped in this body for three years now, ever since the Acceleration—that terrible day when humanity's predicted future arrived forty years ahead of schedule, bringing technologies and transformations no one was prepared to handle. [56] Her legs had been crushed in the chaos of those first hours, but her mind had expanded beyond anything she'd imagined possible, receiving visions that branched and twisted like the maze gardens she'd once tended. [93]

[122] This fragment was all that remained of the place where she'd first received her visions, where she'd seen the future screaming toward them at impossible speed. [149] No one had heeded her warnings then. [156] They'd called her purposefully curious in the worst way—a meddler, someone who asked dangerous questions and chased answers no one wanted to hear.
