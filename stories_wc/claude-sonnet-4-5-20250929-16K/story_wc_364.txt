[23] Kael the blacksmith moved through the petrified groves with measured steps, his shoulders perpetually hunched, voice rarely rising above a whisper. [45] Others had fled when the Shattering came, but he remained, quietly determined to rebuild what catastrophe had claimed. [64]

In his calloused hands, he carried the magician's silk—a ribbon of impossible blue that had belonged to the conjurer who'd once made the stone arches dance. [90] The magician was gone now, dissolved into the same confusion that had seized the planet when its core inverted, turning fruit to glass and soil to ash. [116]

[139] Each exchange left him hovering in that terrible space between understanding and confusion, where he knew his purpose but forgot his past. [162] He no longer remembered his mother's face or his first forging, but he remembered this: resilience could be coaxed from rubble if one was deliberate enough. [188]

[234] The silk hummed when stretched between specific points, creating sympathetic vibrations that made the floating arches shimmer. [251]

[327] Did he have a sister? [332] What year was it? [337] The questions dissolved as quickly as they formed. [345] What mattered was the work. [350]
