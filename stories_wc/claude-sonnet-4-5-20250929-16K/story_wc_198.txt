[24] She'd played here every dawn for three years, her melodies drifting through the mist like forgotten prayers. [89] The radiant grotto glowed within, morning light filtering through cracks in the rock ceiling, turning mist into something luminous and alive. [110]

Inside, her breath caught. [114] The prism atrium - she'd forgotten this place had a name - hung suspended in the grotto's center, a geometric sculpture of glass and crystal that her grandmother had told her was built by monks centuries ago. [149] It caught the dawn and fractured it into rays that painted the wet stone walls with shifting rainbows. [168]

[331] Her childhood self had stumbled upon this place and fled, terrified by whispers she couldn't quite hear then. [497] She played through negative space, her saxophone defining voids where the whispered words tried to emerge and form meaning.
