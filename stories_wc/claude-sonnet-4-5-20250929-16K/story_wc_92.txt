The fort had always glowed at twilight, its crystalline walls catching the last rays of sun and holding them like a promise. [22] But tonight, as Mira completed her rounds as the vigilant lock sentinel, darkness swallowed everything without warning. [38] The unexplained blackout swept through the compound in a breath, extinguishing even the luminous stones that had burned steadily for three centuries. [60]

Perfect, she thought, a smile playing at her lips. [69]

For months, Mira had walked these corridors with the measured pace expected of her station, checking bolts and testing mechanisms while her mind worked through far more intricate designs. [98] Her calmly inventive nature had never been suited to mere guardianship, but the position granted her access to every locked room, every sealed chamber where the Council kept its secrets. [127] And secrets, she had learned, were merely puzzles waiting for the right key. [141]

[161] Here stood the sundial, commissioned by the previous Council but never completed—they'd abandoned it when the painter died, leaving it half-finished, its hour markers stopping abruptly at noon. [188] Most considered it an eyesore, a reminder of waste. [197]

Mira considered it the cradle of sparks. [204]

[218] In the darkness, she could feel what others couldn't see: the subtle variations in texture where she'd embedded her coded messages. [238] Each angle held meaning—thirty degrees for the armory schedule, forty-five for guard rotations, sixty for the location of the Council's private correspondence. [260] The half-painted nature of it provided perfect cover; she simply continued the "restoration" work, adding layers of meaning beneath layers of paint. [282]

[327] Each person had found their own quiet way to resist the Council's iron grip, and together they were building something that couldn't be locked away. [352]

[471] He'd always suspected her of something, though his imagination never stretched beyond thinking she might steal silverware. [488] The idea that his lock sentinel was calmly dismantling his entire world, one coded angle at a time, lived beyond his capacity for intrigue. [511]
