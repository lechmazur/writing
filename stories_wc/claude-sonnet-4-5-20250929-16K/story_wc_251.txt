[19] She had been the after-school chess coach for eleven years, committed to every child who shuffled through the doors, though lately she noticed how quiet they had become. [86] While parents said "She's doing fine," Marina heard: We've stopped expecting magic. [98] This attribute had made her lonely for most of her life, surrounded by the constant hum of unvoiced fears and desperate hopes, but now it served a purpose. [125]
