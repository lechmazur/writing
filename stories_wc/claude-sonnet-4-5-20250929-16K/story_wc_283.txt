[28] The building nestled against cliff walls like a secret, its shelves heavy with objects that carried the weight of forgotten moments. [121] She had found it wedged between shipping crates, labeled only with a date three decades past and a single word: unscramble. [143]

[165] "The last daughter of the cliff-born will seal the canyon when the eastern wind fails." [180] Her mother had been cliff-born. [185] Maren was the last daughter. [190] For twenty-eight years, this prophecy had shaped every choice, every path closed to her because she might one day fulfill those words. [212]

[334] Buttons of Return. [337] As the afternoon stretched, patterns emerged. [343] The buttons arranged themselves into something like language, like constellation maps of human decision. [357]

[444] Generations had heard only fragments, had scrambled the words until they became chains. [458] She thought of her own life, choices unmade, paths forbidden. [468] All because no one had taken the time to truly listen, to sort through the lost connections and find the original pattern. [490]
