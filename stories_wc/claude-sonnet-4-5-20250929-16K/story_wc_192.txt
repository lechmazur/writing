The sanctuary had been ash and charcoal for three years now, ever since Maya burned the last letter and scattered the remains across the blackened earth. [28] She called herself a tender shadow keeper, though no one was left to call her anything at all. [47] Under starlight, the scorched ground looked almost silver, almost beautiful, like the sanctuary it had become through her careful destruction. [67]

The doorknocker ring was the only metal that hadn't melted in the fire. [80] It hung on the door frame of what used to be a garden gate, leading nowhere now, just standing alone in the middle of the burned ground. [107] Maya touched it sometimes, feeling the cold brass against her fingertips, remembering how she had burned bridges carefully—not in rage, but with surgical precision. [131] Each relationship severed cleanly. [135] Each contact point cauterized. [139] No bleeding, no mess, just absence. [145]

She thought she had found peace in this loaded emptiness. [155]

Then Sienna arrived at the fading threshold. [162]

The boundary between Maya's sanctuary and the world beyond had grown indistinct over time, like watercolor bleeding into paper. [181] Sienna stood there, at the edge where ash met grass, neither fully in nor fully out. [198] Maya recognized immediately what she had once been: someone suffocating under the weight of too many connections, too many demands, too many versions of herself stretched across too many people's expectations. [228]

"I heard about this place," Sienna said, her voice barely disturbing the air. [240] "Where people go to disappear." [245]
