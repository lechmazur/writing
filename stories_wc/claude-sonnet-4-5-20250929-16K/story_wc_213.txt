[17] Mira climbed the spiral stairs each dawn, her worn boots echoing against ancient stone, to test the storm sirens that guarded the sanctuary from the violent weather patterns churning below. [45] She was nobody important—just the tester, the listener, the one who made sure the warnings would sound when zero hour arrived. [66]

But lately, she had stopped trusting the sirens. [74]

[92] Her notebooks filled with observations: temperature shifts at dawn, pressure changes before the afternoon squalls, the way moisture gathered differently on the eastern buttresses. [113] The patterns told stories the mechanical sirens couldn't hear. [122]

That vibration had revealed something: the tower itself was a instrument for reading the sky. [249]

[290] The rope connected to the bell mount, which connected to the tower frame, which extended deep into the cathedral's foundations, creating a vast antenna tuned to the atmosphere itself. [316]

Mira pressed her palm against the hemp fibers. [340] Through her hand came information the sirens would never register: a massive pressure inversion building in the clouds below, cold air sinking while warm air rose, creating a deadlock that would soon snap with catastrophic force.
