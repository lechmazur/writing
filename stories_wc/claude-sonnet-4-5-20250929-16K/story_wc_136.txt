[25] Her notebooks filled with analytically artistic renderings of streets, alleys, and hidden passages that others overlooked. [40] But tonight, at midnight—the hour that divides bad habits and new starts—she noticed something impossible. [56]

[83] She knelt, tracing one fissure with trembling fingers, feeling the certainty bloom in her chest despite her racing heart. [102] This was no coincidence; this was a map only she could read. [115]

In her pocket, she clutched the phantom train ticket punch her grandmother had given her before dying. [131] "Follow what others cannot see," the old woman had whispered. [142] The punch was brass and empty air—it had never belonged to any railway Mara could identify, yet it felt heavy with purpose. [165]

The arrows led her toward the abandoned district, where fog rolled thick between condemned buildings. [180] She dared not turn back, though every instinct screamed caution. [191] Her life had been spent mapping known territories, but this—this was the unraveling of mysteries she'd sensed beneath the city's skin for years. [213]

[229] Above, stars pierced through in impossible configurations, as if she walked beneath a starlit vault that existed outside normal geometry. [249] The fog didn't obscure; it revealed. [255] Between its currents, she glimpsed structures that shouldn't exist: bridges connecting nothing, doorways standing alone, staircases ascending into vapor. [272]

The pavement arrows continued, each crack more deliberate than the last. [284] Mara documented them in her notebook, sketching with quick, practiced strokes, her analytical mind cataloging even as her artistic sensibility marveled at their impossible beauty. The gates opened. [441]
