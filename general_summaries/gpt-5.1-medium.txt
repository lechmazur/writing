3) Signature moves  

- Builds stories around highly specific occupations whose work is load-bearing: “cuneiform translator who discovers that *asking* is a kind of crossing,” a “hydraulic clock shepherd” timing sirens, a janitor-robot finding selfhood “between broom strokes.” Vocation is engine, not costume.  
- Literalizes sensory or abstract metaphors into working mechanics—silence as fabric, echoes as harvestable, luck as marble, a “cracked listening-crystal that ‘catches intentions beneath.’” These devices have rules and force decisions.  
- Fuses procedural or pseudo-scientific precision with poetic language: “water thinking about falling,” “shingled hush,” “the mathematics of mercy.” This lyric-technical blend freshens familiar emotional beats.  
- Prefers quiet, charged closures over spectacle: endings of refusal or stillness—no siren sounded, no throne taken—where a final image “simply reweights what we’ve seen.”  
- Treats objects as metaphysical anchors: hair vials, “shards of hush,” broken locks, and seeds carry more narrative gravity than villains or set-piece conflict.  
- Uses mosaic structure sparingly but effectively: a few shards or charged vignettes, tied together by a coherent conceptual lens rather than overt plot beats.  

4) Failure modes  

- Lays fresh inventions over “Derivative chassis under fresh paint”: healer-mentor arcs, “wise-guide teaching courage,” or “magical shop/service changes protagonist.” When emotional logic could survive with generic tools and names, the premise stops feeling transformative.  
- Slides into abstract, workshop-scented framing—“serve the small miracles,” “restore balance,” “healing through understanding ancient shared burdens”—without always re-rooting those ideas in specific contradiction, cost, or concrete scene pressure.  
- Dilutes risk at the ending: lava that “conveniently stops short,” ghosts that neatly validate a protagonist, mercy or community that arrive without a meaningful price, turning an initially sharp ethical knot into reassurance. Closure is present but sometimes too clean.  
- Lets lyric reflection dominate (~40%) without enough friction from world logic or opposing forces; in those pieces, originality reads as aesthetic (beautiful language) rather than conceptual (new way of thinking about the problem).  
- Reuses template-dense worldbuilding—“generic dystopian Ministries,” ethereal secret societies, healing sanctuaries—so the macro-premise feels familiar even when the line-level and devices are fresh.  
- Relies on guide figures whose wisdom maps neatly onto the story’s ultimate moral; those guides often lack personal stakes or internal conflict, flattening interpersonal tension and reducing the sense of true risk.  

5) When it shines / when it breaks  

It shines when everything is built around a single, non-obvious, high-stakes question articulated through a vocation and mechanism: “How do you retire a god of endings by measuring silence?” “What happens if a ‘useless’ drip-sensing gift is the only fix for cosmic delay?” In those conditions, it pursues implications rigorously, keeps reflection tethered to specific work and tools, and lets closure emerge as a charged reconfiguration rather than a big reveal. The reader feels that no other profession, object, or rule system could support this story’s physics or emotional calculus; the ending lands as inevitable and costly at the level of worldview, even if it’s quiet on the surface.

It breaks when the same conceptual rigor is not applied to the story’s underlying arc. If the chassis is a familiar redemption, mentor, or magical-service template, or the world leans on “fantasy everywhere” tropes like a catch-all Ministry, the originality sinks back into surface flourish. Lyric prose and clever inventions then feel like decorative upgrades on a standard emotional journey. Endings in these cases tend to resolve by affirmation—mercy, community, validation—rather than by a genuinely difficult choice or irreversible loss, leaving the reader with clarity but less resonance.  

6) Keep vs. adjust  

• Keep  
- Centering vocation as plot and theme engine, designing jobs whose tools and tasks directly generate conflict and meaning.  
- Treating invented mechanisms as constrained systems that force choices, rather than as arbitrary magic; keep “mechanics that matter.”  
- Maintaining the lyric-technical fusion in the line, where phrases like “shingled hush” and “the mathematics of mercy” make thought and feeling feel newly minted.  

• Adjust  
- Audit the underlying chassis before drafting: if the spine is “mentor heals mentee” or “magical shop redeems customer,” push toward a less-trodden structural question or add a real, non-negotiable cost.  
- Ground big thematic framings (“restore balance,” “serve small miracles”) in one or two sharply drawn contradictions or losses that must be confronted on the page; avoid ending on untested insight.  
- When reflection starts to dominate, re-anchor it in micro-choices, object-interactions, or rule-driven complications so that the lyric mode is continuously pressurized by what can break, be lost, or be refused.
