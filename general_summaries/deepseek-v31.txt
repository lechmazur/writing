1) Executive profile

DeepSeek V3.1 Non-Think’s portfolio shows a consistent command of single-POV, scene-rooted storytelling with pressure that tightens and clear, on-page closure. Its dominant strengths live at the seam of character and causality: protagonists operate under simultaneous, embodied contradictions, make micro-choices that reweight values in real time, and pay visible costs at the end. The drive lens is generally disciplined—either Causal or Accumulative/Textural—and taught early, with orientation locked inside ~120 words. The result is clarity and momentum that earn resonance; final beats are often decisive renunciations or charged reconfigurations rather than platitudes.

Limitations recur at the moment of greatest pressure. At peaks, interiority can shift from embodied to diagnostic, cost can tilt conceptual rather than felt, and prose can drift upward in register just when the scene needs the body. Secondary pressure sometimes stays archetypal, making relational turns feel perfunctory. Experimental patterns (mosaic/multi-POV) are attempted sparingly and mostly with legible segmentation; when they miss, it’s due to untaught switches or soft escalation rather than scope. Reflection-led modes succeed when anchored in present action; when abstraction intrudes, momentum thins and closure lightens.

Track usage: overwhelmingly Track A (single POV; one scene, sometimes two shards), with occasional, successful Track B/C entries marked by clear teaching and clean transitions. Closure reliability is high; the best pieces land with undeniable, embodied cost, while weaker ones imply cost or resolve too smoothly.

3) Signature moves

- Embodied interiority under pressure: contradictory impulses enacted simultaneously via tactile micro-choices and gestures.
- Lens discipline from the open: early orientation and a taught Accumulative or Causal pattern held to closure.
- Setting as crucible: recurring micro-objects/tools constrain tactics and directly shape the final beat.
- Rhythm that serves stakes: prose modulates—long for breath, clipped at turns—while staying POV-specific.
- Closure with cost as image, not thesis, often via renunciation or endurance.
- “micro-choices like clutching the arrowhead” crystallize value under duress.  
- “dust motes, thick as felt” anchors lyric to body and place.  
- “The closure lands as Decisive Change with visible cost” is a reliable outcome.

4) Failure modes

- Diagnostic label-speak at peaks (“her motivation snapped into clarity”) replaces embodied struggle just when contradiction must act on-stage.
- Cost abstracted or light at closure: loss implied rather than dramatized, dulling felt consequence.
- Filter-verb stacks and register drift in the final third (“she saw… she felt…”) flatten immediacy or elevate beyond the focal voice.
- Escalation plateaus: middle beats skip friction or resistance; solutions arrive conveniently or via single neat realization.
- Relational pressure underused: secondary characters stay functional/archetypal, blunting crucible effects when relationship change is central.
- Track-test slips: untaught mosaic or multi-POV shifts; >4 shards without a coherent taught lens; reflection patches that are not anchored in present micro-choices.

5) When it shines / when it breaks

It shines when a persistent constraint—object, environment, vocation, or method—shapes every tactical choice and is paid off in a final, specific act/image. Single-POV pieces that layer simultaneous contradictory impulses while keeping diction tightly filtered through the character’s expertise or bias deliver clean momentum and resonant, costly closure. Accumulative structures excel when the pattern is taught early and each repetition deepens pressure.

It breaks when abstraction displaces embodiment in the last third. If the climax leans on conceptual rephrasing, tidy moral echo, or academic/lyric register beyond the POV’s natural lexicon, the closing image loses weight. Similarly, when escalation is gentle and external resistance is thin, the final renunciation or transformation reads as procedural or convenient rather than paid-for. Untaught segmentation or orientation lag is rare but, when present, siphons pressure from otherwise disciplined arcs.

6) Keep vs. adjust

• Keep:
- Embodied, character-specific interiority that fuses motive, flaw, and immediate perception with tactile cues.
- Early lens teaching and constraint-keeping: every object/method introduced must shape options at closure.
- Closure via concrete, costly image—renunciation/endurance that forecloses real alternatives on the page.

• Adjust:
- Replace peak-labels with enacted contradiction: stage the simultaneous pull in gesture, tactic, and mistake.
- Audit the final third for filter-verb clusters and register drift; recast into direct sensory action in the established lexicon.
- Deepen relational pressure where relevant: give secondary characters distinctive gesture/idiom and escalate consequence so the crucible alters both parties.
