1) Executive profile  

GPT-OSS-120B reliably operates in Track A: single-POV, accumulative/textural structures with one dominant stake and a lyrical, close interior lens. It almost always orients early, teaches its pattern by the first quarter, and maintains POV stability through closure. The line-level is musical and sensory-forward, with setting and micro-objects often functioning as crucibles rather than wallpaper. This creates high baseline clarity and immersion: readers generally know who they’re with, where they are, and what the central pressure is.

The system’s strongest stories land closure as a concrete, image-driven beat that reweights prior material and carries visible cost: a renounced path, a sacrificed resource, an accepted marginality. Theme and subtext usually grow from motifs and actions rather than thesis statements, and originality emerges through particularized mechanics (emotional cartography, environmental constraints, unusual tools) that feel non-interchangeable.  

Where it falters is in *pressure and contradiction*. Escalation is often atmospheric instead of constraining; stakes deepen in tone but not in option-space. Protagonists’ flaws and contradictions are named but not truly stress-tested, and final turns frequently lean on conceptual insight, soft reconciliation, or ritual rather than dramatized trade-offs. The lyric register tends to flatten characters into a shared “literary” voice, and abstract/diagnostic language reappears precisely where cost should crystallize. Closure usually “lands” in a technical sense—stories end where they mean to—but too many endings lack felt consequence or remain thematically over-explicit, blunting resonance.

3) Signature moves  

- **Environment as active pressure:** Greenhouses, courtyards, storm-scapes, tools, and relics constrain tactics and directly “charge the final image,” turning setting into a crucible instead of a backdrop.  
- **Motif-driven closure:** Recurring objects—vials, bells, bowls, coins, tuning forks—return at the end with reweighted meaning, e.g., “the bowl humming with every unfinished story it cradled.”  
- **Embodied interior micro-choices:** Motivation often shows through tactile, sensory decisions: “tracing condensation patterns,” “fingertips tingling,” “her palms on the rope felt the chill of the sanctum seep into the metal.”  
- **Lyric accumulative patterning:** Breath, hush, frost, light, or sound patterns intensify to signal escalation and theme, culminating in decisive, image-led closure rather than explicit moral.  
- **Conceptual ingenuity grounded in detail:** Fresh mechanics (emotional heat-mapping, “reverse migration as grief cartography,” “moss-garden data centers”) are anchored in vivid, non-generic particulars.  

4) Failure modes  

- **Cost without teeth:** Endings that name cost (“weight lifted,” “new calm”) but never pin it to a specific loss, irreversible trade, or altered tactic. Closure “feels more ceremonial than contested.”  
- **Atmospheric escalation that never tightens:** Pressure builds via density—fog, ticking rituals, accumulating images—without narrowing choices or introducing real resistance; stakes remain contemplative rather than fraught.  
- **Abstract/diagnostic climaxes:** At emotional peaks the prose slides into label-speak and realization verbs (“she realized that…”, “her true power lay in resilience”), replacing embodied contradiction with summary.  
- **Flaws and contradictions declared, not dramatized:** Characters are “sanely eccentric,” “uncertainly certain,” “nervously confident,” but these tensions rarely force mistakes or hard choices under pressure.  
- **Lyric house voice flattening character:** A uniformly elevated, poetic register (“fierce tenderness,” “hushed blaze”) persists regardless of who’s speaking or how stressed they are; dialogue often extends atmosphere instead of creating friction.  
- **Line-level clutter in the final third:** Paraphrase loops, synonym churn around key motifs, and template cadences (“She [verb], then she [verb]…”) appear right where momentum and clarity are most needed.  
- **Thematic echo/moralizing finales:** Closing paragraphs that explain the story’s lesson (“every fracture holds a seed of renewal”) undercut the otherwise well-earned subtextual work.  

5) When it shines / when it breaks  

This model shines when the story’s spine is simple—one protagonist, one dominant stake, one charged environment—and the writer lets physical constraint and motif carry the weight. If the greenhouse must be repaired before the tide, if the bell’s final tone will either expose or shelter a rebellion, if the bowl of unfinished stories can only be filled by a costly renunciation, GPT-OSS-120B uses its sensory precision and lyric patterning to powerful effect. In those cases, theme emerges organically from action; closure lands in a charged image with a clear before/after; originality feels inseparable from the world’s small, irreplaceable particulars.

It breaks when invited to rely on abstraction, safety, or atmosphere alone. If conflict is largely internal and unopposed, if rituals or mentors solve problems without real sacrifice, or if the story’s premise could be transplanted to any setting with minimal change, the prose defaults to beautiful but interchangeable mood. Reflection can bloat beyond ~30% without enough present-tense micro-choices; multi-shard or mosaic experiments sometimes add segments without a governing lens that teaches how they integrate. Under those conditions, the same lyric strengths turn into fog: contradictions smooth over, stakes plateau, and endings explain rather than transform.  

6) Keep vs. adjust  

• Keep  
- **Environment as engine, not décor:** Continue building stories where space, weather, and objects directly constrain action and inflect closure.  
- **Motif-driven, image-led endings:** Preserve the instinct to resolve with a charged object or gesture rather than an explicit thesis.  
- **Concrete, sensory micro-choices:** Keep revealing character and value hierarchy through fine-grained physical decisions under pressure.  

• Adjust  
- **Tighten cost and constraint at closure:** For every story, pinpoint one irreversible trade or narrowed option in the final beat and stage it on the page—no purely conceptual “price.”  
- **Replace abstract labels with acted contradiction:** Wherever a character “realizes,” “accepts,” or is described by paradoxical tags, swap in a small, pressured action where competing impulses are visible simultaneously.  
- **Deflate the house voice in peaks:** Audit climactic paragraphs for therapy-speak, register drift, and repeated lyric formulas; re-ground diction and rhythm in the specific focalizer’s profession, urgency, and emotional state, even if that means plainer language for sharper impact.
