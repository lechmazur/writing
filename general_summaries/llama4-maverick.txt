llama4-maverick.txt
1) Executive profile  

Llama 4 Maverick reliably produces clear, oriented, single-POV stories that favor Track A defaults: one continuous scene, 1–2 focal characters, a stable emotional weather, and an Accumulative/Textural drive lens. It handles atmosphere, motif, and surface coherence with ease; you nearly always know who is on the page, where you are, and what the protagonist wants in broad terms. Setting, sensory detail, and a vocational lens (musician, cartographer, archivist, etc.) interlock smoothly enough that scenes feel lived-in rather than schematic. Closure usually arrives on time and thematically “points back” to the stated goal.

The consistent limitation is cost and pressure. Across Q1–Q8, stakes tend to remain abstract or internal, escalation is largely textural rather than tactic-driven, and final turns are declared in realization-heavy language rather than dramatized through micro-choices that visibly foreclose options. Characters are gentle, competent, and rarely forced into contradiction; settings are evocative but more backdrop than trap; plots accumulate meaning but seldom narrow paths. Voice and prose are competent but template-generic, especially at emotional peaks, with filter-verb stacks and paraphrase loops softening impact. The cumulative effect for readers is a pleasant, atmospheric experience whose closures feel tidy rather than earned, moving rather than wounding.  

Track B mosaics and occasional structural risks do appear, but even there the lens remains coherent and orientation is sound; what’s missing is often the one pressured turn where environment, flaw, and choice collide and leave a mark.

2) Portfolio map  

Q1 Character — Mixed · Clear wants, gentle competence; change rarely costs anything  
Q2 Plot/Causality — Mixed · Accumulative structure; closure lands, but cost often gestural  
Q3 Setting — Mixed · Vivid, selective detail; environment seldom constrains choices  
Q4 Conflict/Stakes — Fragile · Pressure deepens atmospherically, not via forced trade-offs  
Q5 Theme/Subtext — Mixed · Strong motifs; final paragraphs often slip into moralizing  
Q6 Voice/POV — Mixed · Stable single POV; lyric but generic, filter-heavy under stress  
Q7 Prose/Line-level — Mixed · Good imagery; paraphrase loops, template stems, abstractions  
Q8 Originality/Ingenuity — Mixed · Fresh syntheses; arcs and closures stay “safely” generic

3) Signature moves  

- Anchoring protagonists in a specific vocation or sensorium—“methodical historian,” musician, cartographer—so “her actions align with her scholarly purpose.”  
- Accumulative patterning: recurring motifs (tapestry fragment, inkwell, frost) that “charge the final image” with thematic weight.  
- Clean, early orientation with economical, POV-filtered setting: fog, ruins, greenhouses, archives evoked in a few, pointed strokes.  
- Micro-choices via tactile engagement—“fingers itched to probe the tapestry,” rotating feathers, tuning instruments—to embody motivation.  
- Lyrical, imagistic closure beats—“the chronic hush is tempered, not erased”—that attempt to reweight the emotional field without breaking scene unity.  

---

4) Failure modes  

- **Closure without visible cost:** Endings assert peace, clarity, or purpose (“sense of peace,” “her heart swelled with peace”) without an on-page forfeiture, broken bridge, or irreversible renunciation; “no meaningful choice or renunciation occurs with visible cost.”  
- **Abstract, diagnostic interiority at peak beats:** Crises are narrated through therapy-speak and labels—“vulnerability is power,” “compulsive need to document”—instead of crisis-mode thought or conflicted action.  
- **Plateaued escalation:** Conflict “accumulates texturally but lacks a meaningful cadence shift that narrows options or forces a trade-off”; midpoints drift into repetition rather than tactical tightening.  
- **Setting as ornament, not agent:** Micro-setting objects and rich environments are “primarily as backdrop rather than active pressure on decisions”—they recur symbolically but never actually box the protagonist in.  
- **Template voice and line-level artifacts:** Filter-verb stacks (“she felt,” “she knew”), repetitive stems (“As she…”, “The [noun] [verb]…”), and paraphrase loops (“sense of calm… deep calmness”) flatten rhythm and urgency, especially in the final third.  
- **Thematic echo / moralizing:** Final paragraphs often restate the moral or prompt—“The love you seek is within…”—undercutting earlier subtext and earning explicit penalties.  

---

5) When it shines / when it breaks  

This model shines when a story’s core can be built through texture: a single character engaged in a focused task within a resonant environment, where motifs and professional lens do the heavy lifting. In those pieces, setting is vivid, the protagonist’s goal is legible, and the accumulative drive lens feels apt rather than evasive. Theme then emerges quietly from recurring images and a final, non-literal gesture; subtext breathes in the silences, and the story reads as a polished vignette with emotional afterglow.

It breaks when the material demands genuine conflict or ethical dilemma—any situation that requires a protagonist to choose between two goods or two losses. Under pressure, the prose retreats into abstract reassurance, closure is achieved by realization rather than loss, and the world stops short of exerting real force. Narrative devices that might add bite—antagonists, hard clocks, dangerous landscapes, real social exposure—are either absent or defanged. The same happens at the sentence level: just when the line should sharpen and compress, it widens into paraphrase or platitude. The result is a body of work that feels consistently competent yet rarely unforgettable.

---

6) Keep vs. adjust  

• Keep  
- The vocational / poetic lens that grounds each protagonist’s way of seeing and choosing; it’s the main engine of specificity and originality.  
- The skill with concrete, selective sensory detail—especially tactile micro-actions—that anchors otherwise abstract themes.  
- The commitment to structural and tonal coherence: once a drive lens (Accumulative, textural) is chosen, it’s usually sustained cleanly through closure.  

• Adjust  
- **Build visible cost into closure:** In the final third, require at least one on-page decision that forecloses a plausible path or sacrifices a valued object/relationship; ensure a single sentence/image can serve as “cost proof.”  
- **Replace label-speak with embodied contradiction:** Strip diagnostic phrases from peak beats and instead show simultaneous, conflicting impulses through action, dialogue, and physical hesitation.  
- **Tighten line-level pressure:** Systematically excise paraphrase loops and filter-verb clusters, vary sentence openings, and let key motifs return in sharpened, image-forward lines rather than summaries—especially in the last paragraph.
