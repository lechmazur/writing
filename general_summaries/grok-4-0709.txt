1) Executive profile  

Across Q1–Q8, this model tends to work in Track A defaults: single close POV, one scene or a two-shard structure with a clear transition, and 1–2 focal characters. When it stretches into mosaic or multi-POV (Tracks B/C), segmentation is usually taught and coherent, and clarity rarely collapses outright; the main issues in those modes are soft pressure and drift at closure, not orientation debt.  

Its dominant strengths lie in tightly anchored motivation, active setting, accumulative patterning, and a character-bound voice that keeps perception selective and textured. Stories nearly always orient who/where/when early, then build pressure through recurring motifs or environmental constraints; closure typically lands in a recognizable form (Decisive Change, Resonant Reconfiguration, Conceptual Repricing) with at least some cost. When it shines, the final choice is visibly paid for in action or renunciation, the environment or micro-object charges the last image, and theme emerges subtextually from that pattern rather than being named.  

Limitations cluster around abstraction in peak moments: diagnostic label-speak for emotion, conceptual or thematic summary in the last paragraph, abstract “cost-talk” instead of concrete loss, and escalation that plateaus in mood or metaphor rather than tightening options. In those cases, closure still happens but feels frictionless or tidy, with contradiction resolved by assertion rather than lived struggle. Line-level craft is generally polished and propulsive, but can fall into motif loops, template cadences, or overwritten figurative density—especially in the final third—blunting impact just where the story most needs precision.  

Overall, the model is dependable on clarity, lens consistency, and basic closure; what determines whether a piece feels publishable is whether pressure genuinely narrows choices and forces a specific, embodied cost, or whether the story relaxes into summary, coincidence, or thematic explanation at the end.

3) Signature moves  

- Clear, escalating motivation tightly aligned with action: characters “choose to open the valve instead of explain,” or “clutch the old locket” in crisis, making value hierarchies legible.  
- Active, constraint-driven settings where caves, greenhouses, ridges, or shelves narrow options (“the narrow gap enabled his escape route, integrating the micro-setting of shelves to pressure the confrontation”).  
- Accumulative/textural plotting that teaches a motif early (dust, roots, tide, song) and then reweights it at closure: “the grain’s dissolution at dawn crystallizes the theme of loss with undeniable cost.”  
- Subtext carried through gesture, silence, and final image rather than speech: “the final image of Elena clearing decay ‘with acceptance’ offers a non-literal answer.”  
- Character-bound voice that filters every detail through a specific bias or vocation, with pressure-responsive rhythm (“syntax lengthens in calm, compresses in the void, mirroring bodily strain”).  
- Closure images that echo earlier motifs and environment so the world and object feel indispensable (“roots cradled bones, bioluminescent fungi guided, and the sundial’s shadow anchored time”).

---

4) Failure modes  

- Emotional and thematic label-speak at climaxes—phrases like “kindled humility,” “oscillating repose,” or “cycles of compassion” appear where embodied reaction is needed, flattening interior conflict into diagnosis.  
- Abstract or implied cost at closure: “auroral sorrow,” “the weight of new knowledge,” or “changed forever” without a concrete image of forfeited tool, relationship, or path (“no concrete on-page image shows legible cost”).  
- Escalation that plateaus in atmospheric accumulation: motifs repeat, but no “meaningful tightening” occurs; the path remains “linear and unopposed,” and conflict resolves by summary or convenient magic/artifact.  
- Final-paragraph thematic summary or platitude that undercuts prior subtlety: a “thematic echo” or line that “functions as a thematic summary,” turning resonant ambiguity into thesis.  
- Prose artifacts in the final third: paraphrase/synonym loops, template cadences (“He [verb], then he [verb]”), and figurative overgrowth that obscure sequence or cost just as the story peaks.  
- Occasional track-test slips in accumulative/mosaic pieces: >4 shards or untaught compression/elision that leaves lens or closure slightly muddled, even when basic orientation holds.

---

5) When it shines / when it breaks  

This model shines when it has a tight, concrete crucible: a single character under spatial or ritual constraint, a small cast, and one dominant motif or object that both shapes tactics and returns at closure. In that frame, it reliably establishes stakes early, lets environment or ritual evolve causally, and drives toward a decision that forecloses a path with visible loss—“she forfeits rest and companionship, embracing endless vigilance.” Theme then surfaces through the charged final image rather than explicit explanation, and the voice stays rooted in the character’s idiosyncratic noticing.  

It breaks when the story leans too heavily on conceptual or lyrical moves without matching them to real-time, narrowing pressure. If conflict remains observational or puzzle-like, if antagonism is diffuse, or if the turning point comes via coincidence or untested magic, escalation feels static. Similarly, when closure is handled by retrospective explanation—stating what has been learned, naming the theme, or gesturing at “profound” cost without dramatizing what is no longer possible—the accumulated imagery and worldbuilding drain away instead of detonating. Overwritten or repetitive language in those final beats further blurs cost and sequence.

---

6) Keep vs. adjust  

• Keep  
- Building strong, concrete motifs and micro-settings (bell, valve wheel, frost, dust) that recur and then charge the final image.  
- Filtering description and action through a distinct, character-specific perceptual bias, with rhythm that responds to pressure.  
- Aligning plot lens and escalation method (accumulative, causal, or mosaic) early, then holding that lens cleanly through closure.

• Adjust  
- Replace diagnostic or thematic labels at peak moments with specific, in-scene sensations, gestures, or choices that let contradiction play out simultaneously.  
- Make the cost of closure unmissable: add one precise line of concrete loss or foreclosed path at the end instead of abstract “ache” or enlightenment.  
- Audit the final third for loops, convenience, and thesis lines: delete paraphrase, test each device for setup/payoff, and let the closing image—not a summarizing sentence—carry theme.
