1) Executive profile  

Across Q1–Q8, this model’s fiction is defined by tightly held lenses, immersive embodiment, and closure that usually lands with visible cost. It tends to work in Track A (single-POV, accumulative/textural) with frequent, well-handled forays into Track B mosaics and the occasional cleanly segmented Track C. Orientation (who/where/when) is usually solid by ~100 words, voice stays bound to a specific consciousness, and setting functions as constraint rather than wallpaper. As a result, readers are rarely disoriented; momentum comes less from big reversals and more from mounting textural pressure—motifs, micro-choices, and constraint-driven escalation that crystallize into a charged final image.

The model’s dominant strength is interiority under pressure: protagonists have concrete, idiosyncratic motivation and make visible micro-choices in consequential environments. Conflict is often internal but enacted in-scene, with “simultaneous contradictory impulses” driving choice. Theme and subtext emerge from this pressure rather than being declared. At its best, closure shows earned renunciation or endurance that forecloses real options and leaves a “reweighted” world behind.

Where the work slips, it tends to do so in a few predictable ways: escalation stalls into atmospheric accumulation rather than true tightening; cost at closure becomes conceptual rather than bodily or relationally paid; and line-level density drifts toward abstraction or “purple” register, particularly at emotional peaks. The model sometimes leans on stock arcs or tidy epiphanies, and a minority of endings explain their meaning with thesis-like lines. Even then, structural coherence and basic clarity generally hold; the failures are about depth and felt cost, not basic craft.

3) Signature moves  

- Embodied, character-specific noticing as engine: “molars ache,” “bare feet on cold metal,” “tides in teacups.”  
- Accumulative pressure via motif density and constraint (clocks, storms, rituals) instead of plot pyrotechnics.  
- Settings that actively press or enable choice: “the storm-damaged greenhouse… constantly constraining and enabling her choices.”  
- Renunciation/endurance closures where “final image reweights all prior grief” or “leaves the mending tool behind, hands empty now.”  
- Theme carried by recurring objects (shells, plates, roots, threads) that evolve functionally, not decoratively.  
- Lyric, voice-bound syntax that modulates with stress: “She wept. Not from awe. From recognition.”  

4) Failure modes  

- Escalation as atmosphere rather than tightening: “escalation relies more on accumulation than decisive tightening”; options never visibly narrow.  
- Cost at closure asserted, not paid: “cost remains diffuse,” “more conceptual than sharply felt,” or sentimental exits (“Go gently. You are loved.”).  
- Moments of register drift/abstraction—“spiritual dislocation,” “architecture of redemption,” “encyclopedic paragraphing”—that detach from embodied lens.  
- Template arcs and stock tropes (mentor/initiatory artifact/outsider-saves-world, “template heroics”) left untransformed.  
- Late or untaught structural moves in mosaics: >4 shards or segmentation that leads to “slightly strained transition legibility” or wandering focus.  
- Over-dense figurative clusters (“liquid starlight,” “auroral sorrow,” “screaming tenderness”) at high-pressure beats, briefly muddying clarity.  

5) When it shines / when it breaks  

The model shines when a single, specific drive is introduced early and then placed inside a physically consequential environment with a clear constraint or deadline. Under those conditions, its strengths—micro-embodiment, motif-based escalation, voice-bound lyricism—align. Pattern-teaching is early, orientation is crisp, and closure can land as a non-literal but inevitable image that “reweights all prior material with on-page cost.” It is particularly strong when conflict is both internal and externally tested: a character whose fear and resolve collide in an action that is materially shaped by place or tool (“valve wheel,” “lantern beam,” “ring of roots”).

It breaks when it trusts mood and diction to do the work of conflict. If there is no true mid-story pressure point that narrows options, tension remains a beautiful stasis. Endings then lean on conceptual insight or aphorism (“repair is not restoration, but reharmonization,” “unused love doesn’t vanish—it incubates”) instead of dramatized loss. Similarly, when a richly particular premise falls back on unexamined genre scaffolding, the singularity collapses: haunted lighthouses or end-times imagery remain generic if not re-specified through this character’s logic. Reflection-heavy or overly lyric passages can also overrun present-moment stakes if not anchored to micro-choices.

6) Keep vs. adjust  

• Keep  
- The disciplined use of sensory, embodied detail as the backbone of interiority and setting; it reliably grounds pressure and theme.  
- Early pattern-teaching of motifs and syntactic habits, then paying them off in closure through concrete, reweighted images.  
- Commitment to specificity and idiosyncratic synthesis—“acoustic archaeology,” “bonsai as archive,” “cartography of silence”—instead of quirky surface novelty.  

• Adjust  
- Sharpen escalation and cost: insert or clarify a beat where options truly narrow and proof the closing choice with visible bodily/relational consequence, not just conceptual language.  
- Scrub abstraction and thesis-speak from climactic and closing lines; replace “social-media-quote” insights with action, gesture, or charged silence that implies the same meaning.  
- Interrogate every trope and device for necessity: transform mentor/prophecy/haunted-object patterns so they arise from this world’s logic and this character’s flaw, or cut them in favor of more particular, riskier moves.
