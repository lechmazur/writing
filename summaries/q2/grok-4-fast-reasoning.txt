**Question 2 Editorial Brief: Plot, Structure & Causality | 600–800 word stories**

---

**1. Executive Insight: Central Pattern and Reader Impact**

Across this batch of 600–800-word fiction, nearly all stories operate under an **Accumulative/Textural (Track A) lens**, with a heavy emphasis on gradual density-building, poetic motif repetition, and a single-scene or two-shard arc. Mosaic (B) and solid Causal (C) exceptions exist but are always taught early and maintained with discipline when present. These stories frequently achieve closure as **Resonant Reconfiguration** or **Charged Poise/Earned Stasis**, in line with the rubric’s schema—closure is committed to the page, with cost either conceptually or emotionally legible. The strongest pieces deliver a decisive before/after: the protagonist’s stance, options, or self-understanding is irrevocably reweighted, and the ending crystallizes this through a charged image or action that can be physically or emotionally pointed to. Where the axis falters, closures are too often “conceptual”: cost is narrated, diluted, or deferred off-page, and meaningful escalation or narrowing of options stalls before the climax. Notably, orientation and pattern teaching are almost universally managed within the first 120 words (as required), unless deliberate disorientation is later paid off at closure. Overall, the grading evidence confirms that most pieces satisfy closure validity and cost under their own lens, but escalation and on-page sacrifice still frequently mark the difference between good and exceptional.

---

**2. Strength Profile: Recurring Behaviors Worth Preserving**

- **Early, Clean Orientation:** Nearly all stories anchor who, where, and when by ~120 words, e.g., “Orientation is clear within 120 words: frost world, Elara, after flood.” This ensures that any lyric surface doesn’t veer into disorienting abstraction.
- **Coherent Pattern/Lens Teaching:** The chosen structural lens—accumulative, mosaic, causal—is consistently taught early and seldom violated. E.g., “Accumulative/Textural lens holds...each returned village layer tightens the emotional pattern until the final image crystallizes the reconfiguration.”
- **Closure On the Page:** Most stories land a decisive on-page moment—no off-stage closure. “Closure is a Resonant Reconfiguration—her stance shifted with visible cost.” Clear transformation is anchored with a specific image or gesture.
- **Persistent Constraints Shaping Tactics:** The best pieces introduce an overt, scene-specific constraint—broken bell, dwindling light, oath, storm, etc.—that both motivates micro-choices and charges the closure. “The persistent constraint—frost timer, rising waters—shapes choices and charges the final image.”
- **Emotional and Conceptual Cost:** Even when not fully visceral, most pieces at least provide a clear emotional or conceptual price at closure: “cost is legible in tears and relinquished ambitions,” “her solitude exchanged for communal labor,” or “by foreclosing an old path, the character accepts ongoing risk.”

---

**3. Weakness Profile: Recurrent Failure Modes**

- **Closure Without Felt Cost:** A major theme—closures that recap resolution but do not show or dramatize a specific on-page sacrifice, risk, or renunciation. E.g., “cost remains conceptual—we’re told about ‘acceptance’ but don’t see what she surrenders.” This flattens emotional impact and suppresses high-band scoring.
- **Stalled or Soft Escalation:** Stories that “accumulate” motifs but fail to meaningfully tighten pressure—no tactical narrowing, no micro-turn, just ambient mood until the end, e.g., “the accumulation never tightens options under the Accumulative lens.”
- **Convenient or Coincidental Solutions:** Magic objects, messages, or disclosures appear without prior setup and resolve a core problem, e.g., “the closure’s resolution feels convenient… the artifact’s sudden arrival.”
- **Overreliance on Summary/Reflection:** Some texts skirt the BRR reflection ceiling, with more than 30-40% summary or outcome-telling, muting immediacy: “closure’s cost remains more stated than dramatized,” or “emotion arrives through abstract assertion.”
- **Orientation or Lens Ambiguity past Midpoint:** Rare but still present—if lens pattern is ambiguous beyond the midpoint or at closure, it’s penalized: “lens ambiguity past midpoint (−0.3) as accumulative/textural drive isn’t firmly taught early.”
- **Meta-Narrative and Editorial Intrusions:** Bracketed word count notes, “wait, that’s over—adjust” lines, or visible drafting artifacts (even briefly) puncture immersion and cap structure/causality score, e.g., “meta edits briefly disrupt cadence,” “Wait, that's over—trimming back” breaks causality.”

---

**4. Performance Boundaries: When the Writing Excels vs. Breaks**

These stories excel when their lens is tightly taught, pressure escalates through persistent structural constraint, and closure landings are sharpened by an exact, on-page moment of cost. They break down when conceptual closure is asserted without dramatized price or when tension builds only atmospherically and never meaningfully narrows options. Felt, legible cost—emotional, physical, or moral—at closure is non-negotiable. Sudden convenience, lingering abstraction after midpoint, unexplained rule-breaking, or orientation drift at closure are the most common sources of breakdown.

---

**5. Signature Moves and Blind Spots**

- **Signature Moves:**
  - Rapid, poetic orientation anchoring by ~80–120 words.
  - Oblique but consistent use of motif and cadence as plot pressure
  - Closure delivered through charged, physical or symbolic action/image.
  - Persistent (sensory, emotional, or environmental) constraint structuring scene tactics.
- **Blind Spots:**
  - Resting on conceptual/atmospheric closure at the expense of concrete, paid-for stasis.
  - Tendency toward “and then” sequence—events accumulate, but pressure does not intensify.
  - Moments where cost is summarized, not depicted (“the ache lingered” vs. a dramatized loss).
  - Occasional meta-structural artifacts that betray narrative immersion or causality.

---

**6. High-Band Signals (≈8.8+ Band): Distinguishing Strong from Exceptional**

- **Closure Effect and Cost Proof:** The exact moment of cost is spot-lit—“tears as ‘silent rivers’,” “coin discarded, path foreclosed,” or “petals scattered, new connection sealed”—and is unambiguously on the page.
- **Cohesive, Taught Lens at Closure:** The structure’s lens (accumulative, mosaic, causal) remains clean and unambiguous from midpoint to closure, and pattern breaks or cadence-shifts are paid off without contradiction.
- **Escalation and Constraint:** The story meaningfully intensifies or narrows options mid-scene—either through structural constraint (ticking clock, closing opportunity) or micro-choice that alters tactics/stance and gives closure its punch.

---

**7. Revision Levers and Keepers**

*Keep:*
- Early, specific orientation: anchoring who/where/when by ~120 words.
- Structural lens teaching: clear establishment of accumulative, mosaic, or causal pattern.
- Closure proof: decisive, on-page moment/image where transformation and cost are both pointed to.

*Change:*
- Commit to on-page cost: Dramatize sacrifice or trade-off through a concrete image or action in the closing passage; do not let cost remain abstract or off-stage.
- Escalate pressure: Make stakes tighten, not just accumulate—force a tactical narrowing or decision that forecloses old options.
- Eliminate convenience and meta-artifacts: Ensure late-provided solutions, objects, or rule changes are set up and that no editorial scaffolding surfaces in final lines.

---

**Evidence Note:** The above is drawn and synthesized from a breadth of convergent grader remarks, with quotes isolated to those specifically appearing in the commentary. Where evidence is thin (e.g., for some rare lens/drift or orientation missteps), this is flagged rather than extrapolated. Causal and accumulative mosaic patterns are only criticized when their teaching or closure is ambiguous to multiple graders. No penalty is applied for well-anchored multi-POV or multi-shard structures. All comments acknowledge the rubric’s prioritization of lens adherence, persistent constraint, escalation, and concrete closure cost.
