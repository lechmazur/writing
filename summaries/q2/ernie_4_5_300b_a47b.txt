**600–800 Word Story Evaluation — Plot, Structure & Causality**

---

**1. Executive Insight**

Across this cohort, the central structural pattern is the dominance of the Accumulative/Textural lens (Default Track A), with exceptions for stories that execute Causal (linear, because/therefore) logic or hybridize the two. Most stories rely on sensory patterning, motif recurrence, and careful image/intensity escalation rather than a classic plot twist or external reversal. This mode favors closure through Resonant Reconfiguration, Charged Poise, or, more rarely, Decisive Change, with cost often rendered through felt loss, physical sacrifice, or foreclosed options. Judging by the rubric, closure almost always lands on the page; the persistent challenge is calibrating cost legibility—converting abstract or conceptual shifts into palpable, crystallized loss or irreversible change. Orientation is generally solid, but some stories sustain minor penalties for slow anchoring or lens ambiguity at midpoint. The strongest work tightens narrative pressure through one meaningful deepening or cadence shift, threading constraints or dilemmas so that the final state cannot be confused with mere stasis. The few that falter typically do so by substituting atmospheric accumulation or repricing for concrete, dramatized sacrifice.

---

**2. Strength Profile**

- **Structural Coherence & Pattern Teaching**: Stories mostly teach their structural rhythm early—“the pattern of accumulated dust observations tightens coherently,” “the pattern of frost-reading is taught early and pays off.” Repetition of motifs (frost, light, map, thread, or silence) builds stakes toward a clear, earned cadence shift.
- **Alignment of Lens and Closure**: Accumulative/Textural pieces escalate via sensory or conceptual density, then land closure as a clear shift: “Closure lands as a Resonant Reconfiguration... Cost is legible in her tears and the choice to let memory flow rather than freeze.”
- **Closure with On-Page Cost**: Top stories consistently deliver a visible price—"the cost lands clearly: 'wondered if Lila would forgive him...betrayal'"; “the closure is legible in the final image of the new graffiti ‘Begin,’ reconfiguring Mara's options.”
- **Economy and Containment**: The best stories respect scope—"single scene, single POV, one cadence shift"—producing tension without overextending or fragmenting narrative focus.
- **Causal Tightness (in Causal/Hybrid Lenses)**: Where employed, because/therefore causality is handled with discipline: “Causal chain holds: cracked eye → mending → orchard revival… closure is a Decisive Change.”
- **Anchored Orientation**: Most stories pass the “who/where/when by ~120 words” test; “Orientation anchors early (‘under obsidian sky, Lira knelt by the pool’).”
- **Pressure through Constraint**: Persistent constraints or rules shape tactics and closure images—“the persistent constraint of the coachman’s curfew shapes all tactics,” “the orphaned pearl motif narrows options.”

---

**3. Weakness Profile**

- **Cost Not Fully Legible**: Repeatedly, cost of closure is more conceptual than felt—“closure lands, but the cost is more conceptual than visceral”; “the cost is implied rather than rigorously taxed on the page.”
- **Perfunctory, Soft, or Coincidental Escalation**: Rising pressure sometimes relies on “coincidence” (“the storm shifts a container to reveal the skeleton”) or "convenient oracle" interventions rather than protagonist action and constraint.
- **Late or Weak Orientation**: If “who/where/when” is not set by line 120, and not functionally disoriented to serve closure, stories risk both Q2 and Q3 penalties.
- **Lens Ambiguity at Midpoint**: Some stories execute poetic or symbolic drift, so “the lens (Accumulative/Textural vs. Situational) remains ambiguous past midpoint.”
- **Lack of Pressure Tightening**: Occasionally, stories “accumulate density without meaningful tightening”—reflective/atmospheric blocks do not force a choice or sacrifice.
- **Resolution by Assertion or Summary**: Endings sometimes declare insight or summary instead of dramatizing staked options—“closure is articulated rather than enacted on the page,” “the final ‘cycle’ is asserted, not tested.”
- **Convenient or Untested Mechanisms**: Device/ritual success or world resets arrive via “convenient magic,” “asserted rather than dramatized constraint,” or a “soft reset that clears problems without cost.”

---

**4. Performance Boundaries**

This writing excels when it aligns a single taught drive lens (Accumulative/Textural or Causal/Hybrid), keeps orientation rock-solid, escalates with pressure through a single meaningful tightening, and lands closure where cost is not only conceptual but rendered with consequential, felt, or visible stakes. The approach breaks when escalation softens into reflection/decorative collage, orientation is delayed without serving later closure, or closure’s price is only suggested (“she smiles, dawn breaks”) rather than locked by an irreversible renunciation, narrowing of options, or sacrifice. The presence of “coincidence complicates, not resolves” is a decisive fault line: well-structured stories make protagonist action the agent of change, not circumstance.

---

**5. Signature Moves & Blind Spots**

- **Signature Moves**:
    - Pattern escalation—motifs accrue, then fracture or reweight at climax.
    - Closure as a crystallized, charged image (broken pendant, burning fog, new stance).
    - Economy: stories are structurally self-contained, rarely exceeding two functional shards.
- **Blind Spots**:
    - Conceptual or emotional cost often substitutes for visceral, dramatized loss or foreclosed path.
    - Reflection blocks can cross the 20–30% threshold and risk sapping escalation.
    - Coincidental (or oracle-driven) reversals replace persistent constraint, dulling cost’s bite.

---

**6. High-Band Signals (≈8.8+)**

- **Coherence of Lens at Closure**: The story “teaches its pattern early and pays off,” maintaining a single lens (accumulative, causal, or hybrid) through closure with no ambiguity or drift.
- **Clear, Felt, or Visceral Cost**: “Closure lands on the page with visible or embodied price: bleeding hands, shattered flask, trembling; not just a reweighted worldview.”
- **Crisp, Final-Third Lines**: The charged cadence shift, unique image, or action (“she pressed her palm, and stepped inside”; “the map glowed in darkness, but her path was her own now”) that makes the before/after state unmistakable.

---

**7. Revision Levers and Keepers**

**Keep:**
1. Early, solid orientation anchoring (who/where/when) within 120 words.
2. The taught, consistent lens (accumulative, causal, or hybrid) maintained to closure.
3. Single, meaningful pressure tightening (cadence shift, forced action, or motif escalation).

**Change:**
1. Make cost proof explicit and embodied at closure—add a single sentence/image that dramatizes the price paid for the protagonist’s change of stance or irreversible loss.
2. Scrub for coincidence-as-resolution and reflect: does the protagonist act to resolve, or does a convenient circumstance intervene? If the latter, restructure to agency/constraint.
3. Review midpoint: Is lens (accumulative/textural or causal) still holding, with pressure intensifying? If motif accumulation has drifted into reflection, sharpen the stakes—add a present-moment dilemma or test.
