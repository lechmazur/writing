1) **Executive insight**

Across these stories, point of view is almost always formally sound: single-POV third limited (or first) is maintained with very little head-hopping, and the narrative stance typically stays within what the focalizer can plausibly know. Where the writing truly shines, it couples that structural discipline with a strong *perceptual signature*—the sense that each consciousness filters the world with its own biases, lexicon, and rhythms. In the top pieces (mostly Track A, a few B/C with clean segmentation), voice modulates under pressure: syntax and diction tighten, images sharpen, and any early elision is paid off on the page, so closure lands with emotional and conceptual cost. Mid-band work is technically correct but often generic in voice or uniformly “lyric,” while the low band tends toward purple abstraction, template repetition, or explanatory distance that blurs a singular mind.   

---

2) **Strength profile**

- **POV stability as default:** Nearly every story holds a single focalizer without head-hopping; even rare shifts (e.g., a closing lens widening) are usually cued and brief.

- **Strong perceptual signatures at the top end:**
  - “*spectral patterns*,” “*decay curves*” for the neutron-star researcher.
  - “*hands were permanent wrinkles of silt and sorrow*” for the undertaker.
  - “*reading dust the way others read books*” for the archivist.
  - “*reading the wind’s confession through instruments*” for the meteorologist.

- **Rhythmic modulation under pressure:** High-band prose varies sentence length to mirror emotional state—staccato in climaxes (“*She stopped. She could report. She should report.*”), long breathy lines for reverie.

- **Voice-as-character:** Syntax and diction reliably reflect the focalizer’s vocation and temperament:
  - Glassblowers speaking in heat and silica; cartographers in angles and flows; botanists in growth and decay.

- **Pattern-taught compression:** Many pieces introduce their stylistic compression early (“*time pooled like resin*,” “*moving stillness*”), then reuse and reweight these phrases at closure so lyric elision feels earned rather than evasive.

- **Clean dialogue when present:** In better stories, speech is sparse, goal-driven, and voice-distinct:
  - “*We don’t carry the weather… we are the compromise*” feels specific to that speaker.
  - “*You’re the one who says whispers carry further than screams*” carries subtext and relationship in a line.

---

3) **Weakness profile**

- **Generic or “committee” lyric voice in mid-band:** Some narrators lean on elevated but interchangeable diction (“*luminous echo*,” “*drifting resonance*,” “*quiet resolve*”) that could belong to any thoughtful protagonist, dulling POV specificity.

- **Abstraction under peak stress:** At climaxes, several stories default to thesis-like lines—“*vulnerability is not weakness, but the very force that allows light to pierce darkness*,” “*courage meant mapping empathy onto action despite fear*”—which step outside the focalizer’s lived moment and read like authorial commentary.

- **Template cadence and phrase-churn:** Repetitions of complex coined phrases without reweighting (“*nested patterns*” x9, “*pane of hush*” x7, “*sensitively perceptive*” x7) create mechanical, drumbeat rhythm and break the illusion of organic thought.

- **Filter-verb stacks and “helpful narrator” distance:** In the weaker stories, high-intensity beats are narrated as:
  - “*She felt, she realized, she understood*” or
  - “*He knew, he finally saw*”
  
  instead of being rendered through fresh, immediate perception. This blunts urgency and flattens psychic distance.

- **Occasional register drift:** Moments where narration slips into corporate/academic or workshop-abstract idiom (“*resonant reconfiguration*,” “*procedural generation,*” “*conceptual repricing*”) that doesn’t feel native to the focalizer’s lexicon.

- **Rare but notable focalization leaks:** A handful of pieces widen suddenly into omniscient summary at closure (“*the town began to heal*,” “*a child born under the double moons would soon dream*”) or dip briefly into another character’s inner state without structural cue, softening the single-POV contract.

---

4) **Performance boundaries**

This writing excels when the story is anchored in a concrete vocation or obsession (archivist, translator, mechanic, cartographer, tuner, cook) and the prose lets that vocation dictate sensory hierarchy, metaphor field, and rhythm. Under those conditions, even dense lyricism stays legible, and closing moves feel inevitable rather than explained. It breaks when pressure spikes and the voice either (a) floats up into abstract summary, or (b) falls back on repeated coined phrases and filter verbs instead of sharpening into more specific noticing. The more the line is asked to carry theme directly, rather than via embodied perception, the more the distinct voice collapses toward generic “literary” tone.

---

5) **Signature moves and blind spots**

**Signature moves**

- Habitual pairing of technical and lyrical diction inside one POV (“*hydraulic lime ratios*” beside “*hushed blaze*”).
- Recurring use of short, three-beat clauses at critical decisions (“*She climbed. She counted. She stepped.*”) to signal pressure.
- A strong interest in **silence** and **hush** as active forces; many narrators read quiet as text.

**Blind spots**

- Reliance on the same favored constructions across characters (“*quiet blaze*,” “*brazen calm*,” “*traveled stillness*”), risking meta-voice overriding individual POV.
- Trust in interior summary (“he realized…”) where the story could instead dramatize the perception that forces the realization.
- Occasional closing zoom-out into omniscient or essay mode that undercuts the hard-earned immediacy of the final image.

---

6) **High-band signals (≈8.8+)**

- **Coherent lens at closure:** Final images and lines are unmistakably filtered through the established perceptual signature, not generalized theme (“*the canyon didn’t change, but her map of it finally did*” rather than “she learned to live in the moment”).

- **Cost legibility without explanation:** We feel what the narrator loses or surrenders through concrete choice and sensory shift (a breath pattern, a tremor, a changed way of noticing), not via declared lessons.

- **Rhythmic intensification in the last third:** Sentences shorten or lengthen in ways that clearly map to emotional pressure, without resorting to repetitive stems or phrase-churn; read-aloud flow tightens instead of flattening.

---

7) **Revision levers and keepers**

**Keep**

1. **Vocational lenses:** Keep building POV around professions and obsessions—the archivist of smells, the glassblower of grief—because they naturally generate distinctive diction and noticing.
2. **Pattern-taught lyric devices:** Continue introducing key metaphors or phrases early, then reusing them at closure with shifted meaning; this is a major source of resonance when not overdone.
3. **Rhythmic modulation:** Preserve the habit of varying sentence length to track breath, effort, and danger; it’s a core strength in high-band pieces.

**Change**

1. **Tighten abstraction at peaks:** For each story, locate the 2–3 most intense beats and replace summary of insight (“she finally understood…”) with one fresh, sensory perception that *forces* that understanding on the reader.
2. **Cull repeated coined phrases:** When a neologism or epithet appears more than twice, ask if each repetition adds new weight. If not, cut or vary to avoid template cadence.
3. **Audit register for each POV:** Run a line pass looking for academic/therapy/committee terms that don’t match the character’s established idiom; either rephrase in their own vocabulary or ground the abstraction in a concrete image they would plausibly think.
