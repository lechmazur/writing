<question>3</question><grade>9.5</grade><comment>The setting is the story's greatest strength, a brilliantly realized and original space that is both atmospheric and causal. Every detail, from the pomegranates to the rusted steel, feels purposeful and integrated.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The dominant stake—understanding the prophecy's meaning for the city and Harel's duty—is signaled early and deepens effectively. The final reinterpretation of the prophecy is a decisive turn that reweights the stakes from destruction to preservation, with the cost being the burden of this knowledge.</comment>
