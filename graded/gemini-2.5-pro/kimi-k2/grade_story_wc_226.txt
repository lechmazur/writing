<question>4</question><grade>9.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Lysandra's creative freedom and redefinition of forgiveness versus the Council's oppressive penance—is signaled early and tested under escalating pressure. The line "...forgiveness was not something to be earned through erasure, but something to be claimed through the courage to remain whole" is a thematic echo that replaces earned resonance with a tidy moral, significantly reducing the subtextual power of the final image.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The story maintains a consistent, lyrical voice and a stable point of view. <question>7</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The prose is evocative and often beautiful, with strong imagery and rhythm. <question>8</question><grade>9.4</grade><comment>The core premise is highly original.
