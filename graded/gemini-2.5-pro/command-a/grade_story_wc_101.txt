<question>5</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The theme of unity and hope is central and coherent, but its delivery lacks subtlety. Mara’s speech (“we’re not just survivors. <question>6</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The third-person limited POV is consistent and the narrative voice is clean and controlled. However, the voice lacks a distinct perceptual signature; the prose feels more like a capable, objective narrator than a deep immersion into Mara's specific worldview. While technically sound, the voice is more functional than memorable, missing an opportunity for a more textured or idiosyncratic perspective.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The prose is clear and graceful but leans on several familiar constructions ("dwindling like the embers of a dying fire"). The repetition of key phrases like "gently persistent" and "amplified quiet" feels slightly programmatic, as if checking boxes. While free of major errors, the line-level craft doesn't achieve the high information density or syntactic variety of truly top-tier prose, sometimes summarizing emotional states instead of embodying them.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The story of a reluctant leader uniting a community is a familiar archetype. This execution is heartfelt and competent but does not significantly subvert or transform the trope. The narrative unfolds predictably, hitting emotional beats with sincerity but without the surprising turns or fresh perspective that would distinguish it in a competitive literary market. It reads as a very well-executed parable, but not an entirely original one.</comment>
