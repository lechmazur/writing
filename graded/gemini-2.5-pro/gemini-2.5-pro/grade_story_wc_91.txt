<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The stakes are entirely internal and philosophical ("to understand the texture of transformation"), which is appropriate for the story's mode. The cost is palpable but poetic—the end of a beautiful moment—rather than a price paid to overcome a tangible obstacle.</comment>

However, the final sentence ("He had sought to understand... and now he had felt it...") comes dangerously close to summarizing the theme, a slight thematic echo that pulls it just shy of perfection.</comment>

These moments feel more like an omniscient author explaining the character's significance rather than thoughts filtered strictly through Kael's own perception.</comment>
