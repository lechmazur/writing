<question>4</question><grade>6.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake (saving the orchard / settling the ghost’s debt) is signaled clearly and does resolve, but the conflict is largely non-contested: Elara feels awe and fear, yet encounters no real obstacle or morally difficult trade-off in carrying and planting the bead. Escalation comes from atmospheric intensification (the pulsing bead, trembling ground, luminescent tendrils) more than from sharpened stakes or narrowing options. The final choice to press the bead into the soil is meaningful but easy—there’s no serious alternative or visible price on Elara’s side beyond inherited sorrow. As a result, tension is present but not gripping.</comment>
