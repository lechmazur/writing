Closure is solid—a Decisive Change/RR where she gains the sought ability and settles into a new function—though the felt cost of that change is more implied than dramatized. <question>3</question><grade>9.0</grade><comment>The bone towers and canyon of echoes are vivid, sensorially grounded, and integrated into action: they turn wind into voices, vibrate with skimming tones, and function as the literal infrastructure of time-maps. The environment is not backdrop but constraint—movement risks sacrilege and temporal disturbance—so her stillness and reliance on sound are meaningfully shaped by place. At the end, the towers become “markers on a temporal map” and their echoes “roads between ages,” directly charging the final image. The specificity and consistency of this setting would be hard to replace without breaking the story’s logic and atmosphere.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—learning to navigate time without physical movement under a quasi-sacred constraint—is clear and present from early on. Pressure escalates texturally rather than via overt conflict: years of waiting, the rare signal from the birds, and the harmonic experiment that finally answers the wandering pulse. That said, antagonism is mostly abstract (taboo and time itself), so the piece lacks a sharp on-page test or explicit trade-off at the climax beyond understanding the pulse. The resolution feels earned but not hard-won; raising the sense of potential failure or risk in the moment of alignment would heighten stakes.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Themes of stillness-as-journey, reverence for history, and non-physical forms of movement emerge organically from the setting, the taboo on footsteps, and the final realization that “the most important journeys require no physical steps at all.” Recurring images—dust, echoes, inherited objects, bird patterns—quietly repriced by the ending give the piece conceptual resonance. However, the last few sentences lean into explicit statement (“She taught that…”) in a way that edges toward moral summary rather than letting images alone carry the weight. With a touch less didactic framing, the same thematic material could land more powerfully.</comment>

Rhythms are controlled, with enough variation in sentence length to avoid monotony even as some parallel structures recur for effect. The voice is more ceremonial than idiosyncratically character-specific, but within the chosen mode it is confident and precise.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The prose is generally tight, evocative, and information-dense; many lines do multiple jobs of worldbuilding, mood, and concept at once. Repetition of certain phrases (“through centuries of layered dust,” “canyon of endless echoes”) reads more as incantatory pattern than accidental looping, though there are a few near-duplicate sentiments that could be sharpened or cut. Figurative language is frequent but largely coherent and thematically aligned (stone/bone/dust/time), with little true purple sprawl. Line-level momentum holds from start to finish without scaffolding or confusing syntax.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The core idea—temporal navigation through harmonic stone-skimming, bird formations, and bone-tower echoes, all under a taboo against physical movement—is strikingly original and feels internally coherent. The final reframe of the pulse as compass and the canyon as a temporal map is a non-obvious yet earned development. While the emotional arc stays relatively serene rather than shockingly innovative, the world logic and imagery are memorably fresh.</comment>
