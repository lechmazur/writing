<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—getting a dangerous, coded letter out as an act of resistance and hope—is clear and conceptually potent. Internal conflict (fear of exposure, doubt, or reluctance) is largely absent, so tension is more atmospheric than visceral. As a result, escalation under the chosen lens feels like gradual thickening rather than a genuinely tightening squeeze.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of analog resistance, memory versus digitization, and “meaning in the margins” are woven through concrete motifs—chalk, margins, surface mail, the arcade, the clipboard—rather than bolted on. Rhythm and syntax vary enough to sustain momentum in a reflective, low-action piece.</comment>
