<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Althea will finally risk ending her immortality to learn what comes after goodbye—is clear early and steadily tightened as the mutiny escalates from anomaly to explicit offer. Her decision to merge and become the conduit is an earned resolution that forecloses the path of continued immortality with visible conceptual cost. The piece favors interior, philosophical tension over visceral jeopardy, which limits the visceral intensity but not the clarity of stakes.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The theme of rebellion against the “tyranny of continuation” and the value of uncertainty over eternal sameness is woven tightly through the mutiny, the amulet’s memory of death, and Althea’s final act. At times the language edges toward explicit thesis (“the endless parade of identical moments”), but it still arises from situation rather than didactic aside. The final conceptual repricing of immortality versus not-knowing has real resonance without collapsing into a neat moral.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is consistent, elevated, and confidently blends scientific and mythic registers in a way that feels like an extension of Althea’s vast perspective. Distance control is stable; we never slip into other minds or out of her frame. The main limitation is that the sustained high-register lyricism occasionally blurs emotional immediacy, keeping this just below exceptional.</comment>

Momentum survives, but a few moments feel more like concept-display than pressure-building. Tightening or varying texture in peak beats would lift this into the top band.</comment>

The premise’s implications are followed through to a bold, non-obvious endpoint (becoming the conduit for reality’s rebellion) without retreat into safety. It falls just short of 9+ because a few immortality-and-boredom notes echo familiar SF themes without quite transcending them at every turn.</comment>
