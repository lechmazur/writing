<question>2</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The structure is clean: discovery of the object → memory-reading investigation → reinterpretation → quiet epilogue with the letter and altered status quo. <question>3</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The lighthouse, opal dock, and mist are vivid, recurring, and tightly tied to mood and theme. Details like barnacled hull, alpine air, and the glass jar on his desk feel selective and purposeful rather than touristic. Environment doesn’t strongly constrain physical choices, but it decisively charges the emotional and thematic field, especially at closure.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the stranger’s final gesture will be understood and thus if their memory “lands” anywhere—is clear but largely internal and contemplative. Pressure deepens slightly as Cornelius focuses on the “gaps” and feels the echo pressing against his thoughts, yet there’s no genuine risk or alternative path beyond understanding vs. not understanding. His micro-choices (to engage the memory, to preserve the boutonniere, to treat himself as witness) are coherent but low-cost. As a result, conflict feels beautifully atmospheric but not strongly escalatory.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The theme of memory as witnessing, and of isolation transformed into a form of connection, is well integrated through images (light, mist, dock, edelweiss) and Cornelius’s actions. The final reconfiguration—gesture as gift rather than plea—works thematically and feels earned by the investigation. Overall resonance is real, but the explicit statements keep it below elite literary subtlety.</comment>

There’s no head-hopping or stance confusion; the narration’s certainty matches what Cornelius can infer. A touch of generic phrasing (“quiet testament,” repeated “softly persistent”) keeps the voice from feeling utterly singular but doesn’t materially harm it.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The fusion of an object-psychometry ability with a solitary lighthouse keeper at nautical twilight, focused on a single enigmatic gesture, feels fresh and coherent. The conceptual turn—reframing the gesture as a gift and the lighthouse as a custodian of memory—is non-obvious yet grounded in the established premise. It’s not radically innovative, but it is thoughtfully original and tonally unified.</comment>
