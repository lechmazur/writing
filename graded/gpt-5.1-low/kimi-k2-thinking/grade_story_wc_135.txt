The after-rain condition subtly shifts tone to renewal and makes the setting feel like a prepared page for new inscription. World rules around mist-as-memory are stable enough that the magic reads natural within this setting.</comment>

There’s no strong sense that a choice could go wrong or foreclose something vital; the process feels preordained. <question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The theme of silence/absence as a form of speech and meaning is woven tightly through object (valve), method (staggered intervals), and setting (mist, blank ivory stones), culminating in the line about “cartography of silence.” The story’s final images—silence as a “key,” the harbor as sanctuary for journeys “between what could be spoken”—reprice earlier motifs with real resonance. However, the text occasionally edges into explicit thesis statements (“Her gift was never the recovery of sound…”) that slightly undercut subtextual power. Despite that, the conceptual reframing of exile feels earned and lingers.</comment>

The staggered-interval method and the notion of “transcripts of silences” are especially inventive. A bit more friction or unpredictability in the process could elevate it further, but the imaginative vision is already distinctive.</comment>
