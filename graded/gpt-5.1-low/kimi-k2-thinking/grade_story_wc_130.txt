<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The piece reads cleanly as an accumulative, ritual-driven scene: each step with the glass eyes and mosaic builds toward the doorway image and conceptual closure about transforming loss. Closure lands as a resonant reconfiguration: loss is redefined as a question and a space that can hold grief. Stakes remain largely interior and abstract, but the structural throughline is solid and legible.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The cavern orchard is vivid and specific—stalactites feeding trees, silver droplets, obsidian and pearl fruit—and the setting’s logic remains consistent. Environment is more than backdrop: moonlight-as-liquid and the orchard’s “listening” roots directly participate in the ritual and final humming response. The glass eyes in the mosaic watching the orchard at the end give the place a charged, ongoing presence. It stops short of constraining concrete choices, but it powerfully charges tone and final image.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The central stake—whether Vesper can reconfigure her ambiguous loss into something bearable—is clear but remains low-tension and almost entirely internal. There is a mild escalation as the stone warms, patterns shift, and the doorway emerges, yet there’s little sense of possible failure, danger, or mutually exclusive outcomes. Her agency is steady but not tested; no option is decisively foreclosed on the page. The result is emotionally coherent but more meditative than pressured.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>The thematic spine—ambiguous loss, the urge to quantify emotion, and the persistence of love—is well seeded in concrete images like the eyes, the mosaic, and the doorway. However, the story repeatedly states its ideas (“mathematics of emotion,” “ambiguous loss,” “buried optimism”) and then explicitly answers them (“love persists, even when the formula is unclear”), undercutting subtext. The strongest moments of theme are imagistic—the half-open doorway, the eyes as witnesses, birdsong at dawn—but these are somewhat drowned by overt moralizing. Greater trust in implication over declaration would lift this substantially.</comment>

There are no POV slips or distance confusions, and the narrative modulation from hush to humming orchard is smooth. At times the register edges toward generalized “poetic” rather than sharply character-specific, which keeps it just shy of top-tier.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The prose is polished, syntactically varied, and rich with sensory imagery, and most sentences do more than one job. Despite this, clarity and momentum remain intact, and the closure image reads cleanly. Slimming a few redundant formulations would sharpen impact without sacrificing lyricism.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The fusion of cavern orchard, ritual mathematics, and taxidermist’s glass eyes as tools for processing ambiguous loss is striking and memorable. What holds it below the very highest band is the familiar, almost workshop-phrase articulation of the final insight (“love persists, even when the formula is unclear”), which softens the otherwise distinctive synthesis. Overall, it’s a strong, imaginative piece with a clear individual vision.</comment>
