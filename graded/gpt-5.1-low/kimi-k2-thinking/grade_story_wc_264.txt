A touch more tactile, non-visual sensory grounding could push this higher, but as-is it’s strong and thematically integrated.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether possibility/memory can survive the encroaching null-zones—is clear and escalates as the archive’s “cancer of nothing” spreads. However, conflict is almost entirely one-sided and abstract; there’s no real counter-force with agency, just entropy, and the guard never seriously wavers. The pressure deepens conceptually rather than through sharp reversals, which works but keeps it shy of top-tier tension.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Themes of memory versus forgetting, potentiality versus extinction, and the idea of dreams as infrastructural (a place where futures are born) are rich and organically threaded through archive, forest, and lullaby motifs. At times the language edges toward explicit thesis (“that memory is enough to grow from”), slightly undercutting subtextual power. Still, the thematic net is coherent, emotionally resonant, and emerges from concrete images rather than pure sermonizing.</comment>
