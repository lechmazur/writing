<question>4</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—balancing preservation of the amphitheater with commercial/modern pressures and physical safety—is clear, and the discovery/decoding sequence is a solid tightening. <question>5</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>Themes of reconciling past and future, reading compassion out of stone and mechanism, and treating heritage as a living system rather than a static relic are coherent and well-seeded (the chain, the “breath,” the flood as story). Yet the final paragraphs slide into explicit moralizing (“encrypted compassion was not a hidden code, but a shared heartbeat,” “testament to the power of measured, compassionate change”), which undercuts the otherwise earned resonance. Ambiguity and moral tension around profit vs. reverence are smoothed over rather than left provocatively live.</comment>

The elevated diction suits the quasi-mythic, fable-like tone of the piece, even if at times it edges toward abstraction. There’s good rhythmic variation in sentence structure, and the prose modulates appropriately between description and narrational summary.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>Line-level craft is generally strong: sentences are clear, varied, and information-dense, with many doing double duty for atmosphere and character. The ending leans on thematic abstraction rather than the sharpest possible concrete image, slightly dulling the closure beat. Still, there are no real loops, scaffolding, or figurative muddles that impede comprehension or momentum.</comment>
