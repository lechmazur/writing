<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Kael can locate and honor the “unused potential” before the window closes—is clear and does tighten around the time pressure and the discovery under the cathedral. However, the conflict remains largely internal and contemplative; there is little sense of a genuinely risky alternative path (e.g., weaponizing the energy, aborting the scan) that she must consciously foreclose. The result is powerful but somewhat one-note in terms of pressure, more meditative hunt than crucible.</comment>

Trimming such direct statements would let the subtext and motifs do more of the work.</comment>
