The cost is conceptual and emotional more than viscerally felt, which limits the ceiling.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Themes of impermanence versus technological preservation accrue naturally from particulars: the ephemeral bloom, the flare’s artificial daylight, the stored digital image, the “world built by human hands” underfoot. However, some late lines (“a fragile reminder of what the universe could not preserve,” “space age had given her tools… yet it could not preserve…”) border on explicit thesis statements, slightly flattening subtext. The piece invites reflection but doesn’t fully escape familiar formulations about transience.</comment>
