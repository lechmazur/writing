<question>2</question><grade>9.1</grade><comment>The accumulative structure is clean: climb → contact with tunnel/diamond → harnessing birds → consequential aftermath, with no orientation issues. Causality within the lyric mode is consistent—each move (“therefore” to the birds, then to the diamond, then to the memorial) follows from prior constraints. <question>3</question><grade>9.2</grade><comment>The setting is vivid, strange, and fully integrated: bone towers, canyon of echoes, tunnel of polished calcium, and spinebirds all constrain what is possible and how translation can occur. Sensory detail (“air tasted of fossilized thunder,” copper resonances) is precise and POV-filtered rather than touristic. The final bone-tower/canyon images reappear as witnesses, giving the world a subtle, charged presence in the closing beat.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—giving stone-grief a comprehensible voice before the language vanishes—is signaled early and tightened as speakers dwindle to “three… two… one.” Escalation comes through intensifying attempts (copper resonance → tunnel → birds-as-syntax) and the recognition that the perfect translation is unintelligible, a real conceptual defeat. Costs are clear: the language is irretrievably lost as a human inheritance, and she pays with exhaustion, isolation, and eventually her life. The conflict is more fated than agonistic, so tension is quietly existential rather than sharply contested, which slightly softens the sense of escalating jeopardy.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>Themes of language death, translation, memory vs meaning, and what constitutes a “voice” emerge organically from concrete particulars (stone texture, bird patterns, echoes) rather than abstract treatises. However, some lines state the thematic thesis too directly (“some languages die so completely they cannot be mourned,” “the curse had been a gift after all”), slightly reducing subtextual subtlety. Despite this, the emotional aftereffect is strong and conceptually rich.</comment>

The final turn from failed rescue of a language to the birth of a self-sufficient, earless voice is intellectually and emotionally non-obvious yet inevitable in hindsight.
