<question>1</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Mira is sharply sketched—her measured gait, talismanic curl, and attunement to breath all feel specific and embodied, and her profession is convincingly lived-in. The structure is accumulative but not vague; recurring motifs (cedar, roots, keepsake, eclipse) braid into a coherent before/after. The only thing holding it short of the very top tier is that the miracle’s mechanism remains deliberately mysterious, so the cost is mostly conceptual/emotional rather than sharply dramatized through altered options.</comment>

The keepsake curl and guitar function as micro-settings/tools that carry into and color the final image. This is rich, selective worldbuilding that supports tone and theme without devolving into static description.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—can music, timed to an eclipse, bridge dementia’s erasure—is clear and tightened by the looming totality window (“For ninety seconds, the duration of totality”). Pressure escalates from quiet preparation to the uncanny synchronization of Eleanor’s breath-sound with the A minor chord and the sudden clarity of her speaking Mira’s mother’s name. However, conflict remains mostly internal and situational rather than contested; nothing truly opposes Mira beyond time and illness, and her choices are not sharply traded off against visible risk. The result is moving but structurally gentle, with a single major deepening rather than a strong sense of mounting jeopardy.</comment>

That said, the last paragraphs lean into explicit statement and near-moralizing, explaining what the story already shows and slightly dulling the subtextual charge. The piece still resonates, but a lighter touch on the articulated insights would let the images carry more weight.</comment>
