Closure lands as an Earned Stasis/Charged Poise: Liora recommits to her burdensome post, uses her last nut differently, and we end on a reconfigured sense of the labyrinth as something that might “remember how to lead people home.” The cost—her continued isolation and delay of any solution beyond guiding—is legible, though not maximally exploited.</comment>

Details are economical but textured, filtered through Liora’s long familiarity rather than tourist cataloging. The final images—frost silvering wood and fruit, birds frozen, traveler walking free below—are strongly charged by earlier environmental rules.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Liora will risk breaking her mandate to guide travelers, and what that costs her—is clear, and the girl’s arrival functions as the tightening beat that tests her pattern of refusal. Her choice to use the last nut at the labyrinth’s entrance and walk away is a genuine renunciation that forecloses easier detachment, but the pressure around that moment could be sharpened. Overall tension accrues, but without a strong in-scene reversal or concrete complication beyond established burdens.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of caretaking as both imprisonment and quiet rebellion, systems built to guide becoming traps, and the redemptive power of small, precise acts recur organically through blueprint, nuts, impressions, and frost. There is no moralizing; instead, the ending invites reflection on unseen labor and the cost of holding hope in a decayed order. The one slightly overt line (“a trail of graceless beauty for those who needed it most”) still fits the earned pattern rather than reading as thesis-statement.</comment>
