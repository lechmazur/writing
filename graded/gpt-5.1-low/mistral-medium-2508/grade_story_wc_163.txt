Small objects-as-setting (feather, glass shard, lens) charge the final images, especially the echo between deep glow and surface fireflies. The world feels specific, not generic SF seafloor dressing.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elara’s isolation endures or is reconfigured by connection—is clear but very low-key. There is one main tightening: Kael’s arrival and shared witnessing of the respite, culminating in hand-holding and his departure versus her choice to stay. However, “until sunrise” doesn’t function as a live clock so much as a gentle boundary, and the cost of her choice is more implied than sharply felt. The conflict never quite spikes into a moment where she must visibly trade one value against another on the page.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of belonging, home-as-frequency, and the dialogue between surface and depths emerge organically from images (constellated algae, feather, jellyfireflies, glass shard) rather than explicit theorizing. The ending avoids platitude while still clearly implying her answer to the central question about where and how one belongs. A hair more friction or ambivalence around the decision would deepen the thematic charge further.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Line by line, the prose is dense with image yet mostly avoids purple excess; “her maps were alive, shifting with the tides” and “its glow seeping into his skin like ink” are strong, concrete metaphors. There’s little scaffolding or repetition and almost every line does at least two jobs (worldbuilds and characterizes, or advances mood and theme). At times the figurative load is high, but it stays legible and supports, rather than blurs, the closure.</comment>

The emotional arc—a quiet, cautious connection in a liminal hour—is more familiar, though executed with nuance. Overall, the synthesis of marine SF, lyric romance, and metaphysical cartography reads as distinctive and confident.</comment>
