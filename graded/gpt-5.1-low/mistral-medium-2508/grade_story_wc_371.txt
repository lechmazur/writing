<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—the city’s slide into unthinking adjustment vs. Liora’s refusal to look away—is clear and thematically rich. However, opposition is mostly atmospheric; Liora encounters almost no resistance, and her choices never truly endanger her or demand a visible sacrifice. The result is powerful in mood but relatively gentle in pressure compared to elite-level conflict density.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Themes of creeping normalization, the sacred in the ordinary, and “tenuous absolution” are woven through specific images (coins left at an empty bakery, crumbs in the soil, guards ignoring mint and thyme) rather than stated morals. Ambiguity around whether this new stasis is truly enough keeps the piece from sentimentality.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The synthesis of creeping-normality horror with miraculous bread and guerrilla gardening in a quietly uncanny city feels fresh and emotionally specific.
