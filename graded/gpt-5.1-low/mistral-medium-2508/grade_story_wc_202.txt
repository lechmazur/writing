<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Lior can find a meaningful anchor and re-engage with overlooked histories—is clear but kept almost entirely on an interior, conceptual plane. The resolution arises from a realization plus a small ritual act (fitting the lens into the coat) rather than a pressured choice with visible cost. As a result, tension is present but gentle, more contemplative than compelling.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>Themes of memory, overlooked histories, and the function of anchors are deeply woven through objects (lens, brush, coat contents) and Lior’s arc, culminating in the redefinition of anchors as instruments of return. However, several lines verge on direct moralizing (“The past wasn’t a place you left behind…”, “The best translations were the ones that changed you”), slightly flattening what the images already imply. The piece is thematically rich but would benefit from trusting its imagery a bit more.</comment>
