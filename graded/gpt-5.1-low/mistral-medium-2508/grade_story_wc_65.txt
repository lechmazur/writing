<question>2</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The structure is clean: one continuous scene, a clear progression from arrival to engagement with the spire to the completion of the predictive map. This is near-publishable level atmospheric work, just shy of utterly transformative.</comment>

At times, the register drifts into essayistic abstraction (“groundedly imaginative act,” “predictive modeling was complete”), slightly distancing us from embodied experience. Still, the voice is distinctive and controlled enough to be publication-ready with minor trimming.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>Line-level craft is generally excellent: sentences carry multiple jobs, imagery is mostly precise, and rhythm varies well. <question>8</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The core premise—an “ignorance cartographer” mapping not what is, but what might be, via an ice-ledger of choices—is fresh and memorable. The piece stops at a luminous vignette rather than pushing into a riskier or more costly turn, so its originality is more conceptual than dramatic. Still, it feels distinctive enough that it would stand out in a strong speculative slush pile.</comment>
