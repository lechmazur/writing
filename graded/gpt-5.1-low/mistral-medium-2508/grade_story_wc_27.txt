<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: boredom and doubt → hum from stones → contact → activation → others drawn → engines reignite. Structure as a single continuous scene is tight and legible.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>9.0</grade><comment>The abandoned spaceport is vividly and economically rendered—“rusted girders,” “cracked concrete,” margin-of-daylight light—and the bay’s physicality evolves as the metal wakes and ships lift. The stone circle and opera glasses function as charged environmental tools, shaping both tactics and the final image. Setting details are always filtered through Lena’s body and attention (bare feet, breath fogging), giving the world texture and bias. The final shadow stretching across the concrete elegantly recharges the opening image.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The core stake—whether the rebellion will stay dead in its lull or be reanimated—comes through clearly and escalates from private yearning to communal ignition. Pressure is mostly situational and internal, with the hum acting as catalyst rather than a true antagonist. Once Lena commits to the stones, events tilt in her favor without meaningful setback or visible sacrifice beyond risk and strain. The result is thematically satisfying but somewhat low-friction in terms of concrete cost and opposition.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of listening, memory, and finding meaning in wreckage accumulate organically via motifs (abandoned bay, forgotten stones, opera glasses repurposed). The idea that the rebellion is about refusing silence rather than clean victories is coherent and resonant. However, the story edges into explicit thesis statements—“The rebellion wasn’t about winning. It was about this…” and “meaning wasn’t found in clean victories”—which flatten some subtext. The emotional aftereffect is strong but could trust image over direct commentary more.</comment>

POV boundaries are clean and there is no head-hopping; even brief glimpses of the scout are strictly external. The prose achieves a confident texture that would sit comfortably in an elite market.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Line-level craft is strong: sentences are varied, taut, and information-dense, often doing double duty for mood and action. <question>8</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The fusion of rusted spaceport, mystical stone circle, and heirloom opera glasses gives familiar rebellion tropes a fresh, almost mythic slant.
