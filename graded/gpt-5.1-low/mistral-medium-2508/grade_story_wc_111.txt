The cost (abandoning the security of tallies and embracing a less quantifiable relationship) is legible, though gently rendered. <question>3</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The garden maze is vivid and specific—hedges that whisper, roses that mark where she wept, ivy spelling her name—giving the environment real texture and emotional charge. The recurring interplay of plants and glass charges the final image of paths shifting “to welcome the lost, the curious.” While the setting doesn’t impose hard logistical constraints, it strongly inflects meaning and atmosphere.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The central conflict—wonder and relational care versus quantification and extraction—is clear, with the elders’ accusation and later visit raising modest but coherent pressure. The ending still resonates, but it leans closer to parable than to ambiguous, open-ended literary complexity.</comment>
