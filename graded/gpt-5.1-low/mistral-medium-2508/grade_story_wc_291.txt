While the setting doesn’t drastically constrain physical options, it strongly shapes the story’s emotional and conceptual field.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elias will bear honest witness to Liora’s erasure despite knowing it changes nothing externally—is clear and appropriate to the scale. However, external opposition is minimal and he faces no truly wrenching choice between competing goods; the main risk is psychic strain. The result is a well-shaped but relatively low-intensity conflict arc.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically from particulars: a face-blind man as “portraitist of the invisible,” a dancer “erased,” a quarry that is a town’s wound, and the idea that the dead want witness, not vengeance. The final image of Elias carrying Liora’s name and walking toward a coughing child reweights the earlier material with a quiet, humane answer to the question of what he’s for. Ambiguity (no legal justice, uncertain impact) feels deliberate rather than evasive.</comment>
