<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—capturing the last breath of a language at potential personal cost—is clear and sharpened well when the echoes shift from passive to alive and the bone towers begin to sing. <question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically around translation, hubris, and what should remain unknowable, with the cursed diamond reframed as punishment not for listening but for answering. Recurring motifs—copper veins, flawed/perfected diamond, dancing starlings—shift meaning at the end from tools of mastery to witnesses and alternate language. There’s no moralizing; the piece trusts its images to pose complex questions about preservation versus erasure.</comment>
