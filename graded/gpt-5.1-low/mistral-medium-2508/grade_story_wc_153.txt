Closure lands clearly as a decisive change: she abandons the fetishized limbo and steps into a stance of readiness and openness, with the letter’s release as concrete act and cost. <question>4</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether to stay in the safe stasis of silence or risk genuine change toward her sister—is signaled early and sharpened by the spirits’ question and her repeated half-measures (evoking words that vanish, pressing the shard without drawing blood). The cost is the relinquishing of her cherished liminal state and the illusion of endless possibility, with no guarantee of response. <question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Themes of silence vs speech, healing vs enduring scars, and how apologies function even when unsent are woven through tangible motifs (water, mangroves, the shard, condensation letters) rather than abstract commentary for most of the piece. Subtext about self-forgiveness and relinquishing self-protective limbo accrues without heavy moralizing. The slightly aphoristic final reflections (“Some letters didn’t need to be sent… Some wounds didn’t need to heal…”) edge close to explicit thesis, trimming a bit of the mystery.</comment>
