<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she will surrender herself to the basilica/dream mechanism to save/reconfigure the isle and reach her daughter—is clear and deepens as the gem “bites” and she begins to be rewritten. Her final act of letting go is a real renunciation with cost, not a free reward. <question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of grief, memory, and transformation are woven through concrete motifs—the cranes, tea garden, dream-recording glass, and the isle’s “sinking” that’s actually unfolding.
