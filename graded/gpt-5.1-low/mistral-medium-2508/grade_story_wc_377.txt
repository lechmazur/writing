<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Under an accumulative/textural lens, pressure builds through the sculpture’s accelerating motion and the impending shatter of the ice, with the dominant stake being whether the trapped “songs” (truth/wonder) will be released. Her choice is essentially to continue her practice as before, with reaffirmed conviction rather than altered tactics. The piece is intentionally quiet, but the stakes feel more illustrative than biting.</comment>

However, a few explicit thesis-like lines (“The world didn’t need more noise. It needed more wonder.”) verge on sermonizing and reduce some subtextual power. More reliance on image over direct statement would deepen the lingering ambiguity and complexity.</comment>
