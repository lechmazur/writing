<question>3</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The greenhouse, cracked glass, wet soil, dusk, and storm are vivid and consistently filtered through the carver’s sensibility, creating a palpable, almost ritual space. The setting doesn’t tightly constrain physical tactics, but it powerfully shapes the emotional and spiritual terms of the scene and the closure image. Economy is good—no museum touring—though a hair more interaction with the physical constraints of the space could push it higher.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether he can transform his relationship to grief and pass that transformation to his daughter—is clear early and deepens rather than widens. Pressure escalates from the slipped chisel and cracked stone (embodied failure) to the father’s admonition and the new carving method, then to the choice to place the butterfly on the table. The cost is subtle but present: relinquishing his long-held solitary containment of grief to a shared, vulnerable practice. Stakes remain mostly internal and never feel life-or-death, which keeps this strong but not maximal.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The thematic arc—grief as hollows/space, the dead as active participants, collective mourning as door not wound—is tightly woven through concrete motifs: stone, butterflies, thunder, ribbons. They were the hands guiding the chisel…”), which slightly edges toward explicitness, but they’re still grounded in image and don’t collapse into sermonizing. Overall, the emotional aftereffect is earned and lingers.</comment>

POV boundaries are respected; there’s no head-hopping or unearned omniscience beyond the lightly mythic register that’s taught early. Rhythm modulates well with intensity, avoiding monotony.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The prose is rich—“hues of bruised violet,” “veined with starlight,” “wings whispering…like pages turning”—yet the figurative language remains coherent and aligned with the story’s motifs (stone, breath, books, weather). There’s a mild risk of over-lyricism, but it doesn’t obscure clarity or stall momentum, especially since the piece is fundamentally meditative. Line-level control at the ending (“door,” “unspooling toward the future”) is particularly strong and clean.</comment>

While elegiac magic-realist treatments of grief exist, this synthesis of objects and customs gives it a memorable identity. The risks are aesthetic rather than structural and largely pay off.</comment>
