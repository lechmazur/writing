Closure is a mix of decisive change and conceptual repricing: “Home” becomes direction rather than place, and she literally departs along that vector. World rules about time dilation and the station’s past are sketched lightly but feel coherent for this length.</comment>

However, opposition is mostly ambient (void, abandonment) rather than sharply dramatized, so the pressure feels more wondrous than harrowing. The cost of jumping (potential death, permanent severance from “home” as place) is only lightly implied, which softens the tension curve.</comment>

Still, the last images (a new anniversary beginning; “somewhere else, a hand reached for a sketchbook”) achieve a resonant, non-didactic echo.</comment>
