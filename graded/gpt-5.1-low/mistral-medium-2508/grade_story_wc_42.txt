The cost is primarily philosophical, but the before/after is unmistakable (from chasing stillness to moving with the spin). The observatory and its mechanisms also function as a charged object-as-setting that constrains and charges his choice.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The primary stake—whether he clings to repair and stability or risks letting the observatory/time itself reconfigure—is clear and escalates from odd malfunction to existential tremor. However, the external stakes (collapse, foundations shaking) are quickly transmuted into a near-bloodless “miracle,” so the sense of potential real loss dissipates rather than tightening into consequence. The internal conflict resolves convincingly but with relatively gentle pressure for this register of prose.</comment>

At times the diction and aphoristic cadence feel slightly more essayistic than tightly character-bound, but the piece has clearly committed to a heightened, mythic register and sustains it. Still, the synthesis of setting, vocation, and metaphysics yields a memorable and coherent vision.</comment>
