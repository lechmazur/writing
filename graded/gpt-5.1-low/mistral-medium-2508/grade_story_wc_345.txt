<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The orchard planet and arches are vividly realized—“fruit pulsed with starlight,” “ribs of a god”—and integrated into action (arches tremble at the bell, the soil hums with old explosions). Environment isn’t mere backdrop; the war-scarred soil and responsive arches shape what’s possible and what healing means. One could push the physical constraints of the arches/planet further into the characters’ tactical choices, but what’s here is strong and economical.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Veyth can transform from war-scarred ruin into a true “nebula of respite”—is clear, and there is one meaningful deepening when Liora realizes the nebula is a state to be orchestrated, not found. However, conflict remains largely soft; once Liora decides to help, there is little friction or reversal, and the rebuilding mostly proceeds in a gentle montage. External opposition (war-memory, a haunted landscape) stays atmospheric rather than actively constraining choices. As a result, escalation feels more like steady growth than tightening pressure.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Themes of collective care, chosen family, and building sanctuary “with” rather than escaping the world emerge organically from the orchard work, Kael’s ghostliness, and the repeated bell motif. Lines like “Some ghosts… are just family who forgot to leave” carry layered emotional and thematic weight without overexplaining. A bit more reliance on implication and less on declarative theme statements would raise the sophistication.</comment>

Rhythm modulates well between densified lyricism in key moments and clearer, plainer beats for exposition. The register is elevated but suits the speculative-fable mode without slipping into purple excess.</comment>

There are a few abstracted or slightly didactic lines (“That’s how collective effort works”) that flatten the otherwise rich texture, but they don’t significantly harm momentum or closure. Overall, information density and musicality are high without sacrificing clarity.</comment>

The final turn—that the sought-after nebula is what they are already building—lands as earned rather than gimmicky.
