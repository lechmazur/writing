Details are sensorial and selective (damp earth, crushed sage, overripe plums), always filtered through Elias’s attention. The only thing keeping this from a 9+ is that the environment constrains mood and metaphysics more than concrete tactical choices.</comment>

His final descent into the dark is a choice, but it doesn’t decisively foreclose a visible path; the student’s offstage status keeps pressure mostly hypothetical. The conflict is thematically sharp but never feels acutely inescapable on the page.</comment>

A couple of aphoristic lines flirt with overtness but remain grounded enough in concrete experience.</comment>

The way “appearing” is reframed as prerequisite to “vanishing” feels like a non-obvious yet inevitable insight from these particulars.
