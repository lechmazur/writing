A hairline limitation is that the broader world beyond orchard/city remains abstract, but for this length the focus is appropriate.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—can Elara repair a broken ritual and, through it, her own fractured sense of belonging—is clearly established and tightens with the discovery of the sundial and the approach of noon. However, the piece largely avoids friction or complication; there is no serious setback, competing demand, or moral trade-off that tests her resolve beyond quiet doubt. The conflict remains compelling but gentle, which caps the sense of pressure.</comment>

The emotional aftereffect is real but a little over-explained.</comment>
