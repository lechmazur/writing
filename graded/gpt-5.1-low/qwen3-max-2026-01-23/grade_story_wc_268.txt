<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—restoring truth by unmasking Valerius—is clear and consistent, and there is a conceptual tightening from private vigil to public exposure. The sunder moment is powerful but arrives without much sense that failure was ever a live possibility. As a result, the story feels more like a beautifully described foregone conclusion than a pressured struggle with visible reversals.</comment>

Still, the piece avoids sermonizing and earns a quiet emotional resonance through its final images.</comment>
