<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The canyon of bone towers, endless echoes, and layered dust is vivid, specific, and tightly integrated with the premise of resonance and archival memory. The stone arrowhead’s placement into the throne-as-archive is a strong object/setting fusion that reweights earlier details. A slight pruning of ornate description in favor of the most functionally charged elements would sharpen rather than merely beautify.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Corbin can unify and “appraise” the fractured Pulse without moving or violating his reverent code—is clear but remains mostly internal and low-friction. However, there is little sense of real jeopardy, competing goods, or possible failure; once the birds appear, events unfold almost ceremonially. The result is a beautiful but relatively unpressured escalation curve.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of reverence for history, stillness as a form of movement, and integration of past/underworld (dust) and sky/aspiration are coherently woven through bone libraries, the wandering Pulse, and Corbin’s final transformation. Some thematic statements verge on explicit thesis (“Navigation without moving required integration, not direction”), slightly flattening what the images already carry. Nonetheless, the closing cadence—Corbin as living compass, paths walked inward—lands with genuine, non-platitudinous resonance.</comment>

Rhythm and syntax are controlled, modulating from long, baroque sentences in descriptive passages to tighter lines at key turns (“A sharp cry split the air.”). There’s no POV leakage or awkward shifts, though the uniform loftiness occasionally risks smoothing over emotional jaggedness.</comment>

While not radically experimental in structure, the synthesis of elements reads as a confident, distinctive vision rather than a genre template.</comment>
