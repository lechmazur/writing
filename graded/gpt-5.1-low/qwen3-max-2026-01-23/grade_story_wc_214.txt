The environment rarely constrains concrete choices, though, so it’s more thematically than tactically active.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elian can find strength in vulnerability and belonging in stillness—is clear from early on. However, there is minimal sense of risk or competing impulses; the journey feels like an unopposed slide toward enlightenment rather than a hard-won stance. The resolution is earned but low-friction, with little visible price beyond a quiet emotional opening.</comment>

The insight feels sincere but somewhat generic and pre-articulated.</comment>

However, the narrative idiom sometimes reads more like an essayistic essay/narrator than like Elian’s distinct mental texture, blurring character-specific voice. <question>8</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The cosmic janitor in a defunct star-tracking station, using salvaged lantern glass to commune with meteors, is a fresh and appealing frame. However, the core moral—strength in vulnerability, stillness as fertile—is familiar, and the story doesn’t push it into particularly surprising territory beyond its graceful staging. Overall, the piece feels thoughtfully original in premise and texture, but thematically safe.</comment>
