Causality is soft but intact: Lyra’s prompt leads to revisiting family ritual, which in turn sparks the design solution. <question>3</question><grade>8.9</grade><comment>The greenhouse, dusk light, damp soil, and monarch-filled sky are richly rendered and consistently filtered through Elara’s sensibility as someone escaping stone dust. Setting moves beyond postcard: the greenhouse is tied to family rituals and spectral visitations, and the butterflies’ migration directly informs her understanding of grief’s shape. The final images—the greenhouse as sentinel and the monarchs as a “living constellation of hope”—recharge earlier details and charge the closure. A slight excess of atmospheric description in the middle risks saturation but doesn’t blur clarity.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how to represent communal grief after a mass death—is strong and present from early on, with the bridge collapse giving it tangible weight. Pressure does deepen when Lyra appears and demands she “retrace the path of memory,” forcing Elara into emotionally loaded recollection rather than abstract design. However, the conflict remains predominantly contemplative and harmonious; there is little escalation in the sense of options narrowing or values colliding, and no real risk of failure is felt in the moment. The resulting memorial concept is persuasive but arrives without much friction or sacrifice.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Themes of grief as connection, migratory patterns, and communal remembrance are tightly braided through the ribbon rituals, poem fragments, and monarchs, and the ending reweights earlier images rather than bolting on a moral. That said, some thematic statements verge on explicit thesis—“The shape of collective grief is connection—a migration toward light”—pushing the piece close to didactic where it could have trusted subtext slightly more. The overall emotional resonance is genuine and lingers beyond the final line.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is lyrically controlled, with a distinct perceptual signature in how Elara notices texture, light, and ritual objects. Psychic distance is managed well as the narrative slides between present sensation and memory without confusing temporal stance. There’s no head-hopping, and the spectral voice is integrated without explanatory clutter.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The prose is polished, varied in syntax, and often carries multiple loads (image, emotion, and theme) in a single sentence; lines like “thundering calm” and “silent majesty” are memorable. Information density is high without major repetition or scaffolding, and action/order remain clear despite lyricism. The main constraint at this level is a mild tendency toward over-saturation of figurative language and elevated modifiers, which occasionally risks purple prose without quite tipping into muddle. Tightening a few stacked metaphors or adjective clusters would sharpen impact further.</comment>

The final design—a circular path of poem fragments and resin “wings”—is a specific, non-obvious answer to the story’s question about collective grief. A few elements (e.g., the Auden/Rumi references) lean on familiar cultural touchstones, but they’re integrated well enough not to feel derivative.</comment>
