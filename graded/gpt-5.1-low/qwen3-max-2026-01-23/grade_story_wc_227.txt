<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Kael can create meaning before dissolution—is clear, and the external peril of the failing workshop sharpens that from the outset. The ultimate "choice" is an endurance/renunciation: he commits fully to the ritual and to accepting loss rather than scrambling for survival. Physical danger recedes somewhat into the background during the long ritual, so the sense of narrowing options is more internal than external, but it remains coherent.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The thematic throughline—breaks as sites of honored history, grief transmuted rather than erased—is powerfully realized through the kintsugi ritual and specific memories. However, lines like "Meaning had been conjured" and "Oblivion’s edge was no longer a void of terror" drift toward explicit thesis, slightly undercutting subtext. When the piece lets images (golden seams, calm sea, Braille-like breaks) carry the insight, it feels more truly literary.</comment>
