<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elara will submit to inherited strictures or act decisively for the land’s urgent recovery—is clear and paid off through her choice to unseal the amber herself. However, obstacles remain mostly conceptual; there is no strong counterforce or on-page consequence beyond internal and symbolic risk, which softens the sense of genuine peril. The result is thoughtfully tense but not quite bracing.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of tradition versus evolution, destiny as something consciously chosen rather than passively fulfilled, and interdependence with the land are coherently woven through objects (amber, kite, fern) and Elara’s past as an insurgent. With a lighter authorial hand and more trust in subtext, the same material could resonate more powerfully.</comment>
