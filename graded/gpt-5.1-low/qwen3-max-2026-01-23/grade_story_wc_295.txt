The only minor limitation is that external stakes stay modestly scaled to her psyche; everything important happens in her head and on the page of her journal.</comment>

The environment underwrites the tone of “midnight solace” and supports the metaphor of watchfulness, though it doesn’t sharply constrain physical options. Overall, this is rich, economical, and integrated scene-setting suitable for publication.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The central conflict—solitary, unsustainable vigilance versus the possibility of shared burden—is well established and escalates from weariness to crisis of meaning. The telegram functions as a genuine tightening/deepening beat that forces a stance change rather than a mere comfort. However, the emotional and practical price of her previous stance (what she risks in relinquishing it, or what damage it’s done) could be dramatized more viscerally. The resolution is earned but slightly too serene, with limited sense of ongoing tension or sacrifice beyond the insight itself.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of shared burden, sacred rest, and community across time arise organically from the variable-star metaphor, Kael’s mentorship, and the temple inscriptions. The piece risks sentimentality by assuring us that her words “would heal others” instead of trusting the images to imply that. The thematic power is strong but would benefit from more restraint and implication in the closing paragraphs.</comment>
