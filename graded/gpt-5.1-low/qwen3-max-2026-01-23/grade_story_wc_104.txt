<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—the survival of the seed vault versus demolition—is clearly signaled and linked to a real-time deadline, and there is a meaningful tightening when the bulldozer’s shadow "crept across the floor like a sundial’s hand." However, tension softens at the climax: the crew’s instant pivot from "indifference to awe" and the foreman’s immediate reverence make resistance feel too easily overcome, with little sense of potential loss once the vault is revealed. The result is effective but not fully harrowing.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of stewardship, legacy, and nonviolent impediment are woven consistently through the imagery of seeds, chains-as-roots, and the shift in the workers’ attitudes. That said, the closing lines tip toward explicit moralizing ("hope had been redeemed," "the harvest would begin today"), spelling out what the images already imply. This slightly flattens ambiguity and keeps the piece from a more open, haunting resonance.</comment>
