The recurring objects (pendulum, rake handle, ridge/tide) explicitly charge the final images and closure. <question>4</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The primary stake—restoring the clock and thus the community’s temporal order—is clear and established early, but the on-page opposition is relatively soft (grit in the pivot, emotional weight of legacy rather than a hard obstacle). Her choice to push the bob and then refine its swing, claiming her own inscription, resolves the conflict in a quiet, earned way. The cost is more ethical and ongoing than immediate, so tension remains more meditative than gripping.</comment>

Subtext is strongest in the juxtaposition of the pendulum’s swing, the rake’s carved ledger, and Liam’s silent nod. The ending still offers a clear thematic answer, but with less ambiguity and subtlety than the material could support.</comment>
