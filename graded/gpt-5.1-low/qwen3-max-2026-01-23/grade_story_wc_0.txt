<question>4</question><grade>7.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how Aris will relate to loss and meaning in the wake of the flood—is clear and present from the outset. However, the conflict remains internal and relatively low-friction; there is little real resistance beyond his own previous habit, and the cost of change feels gentle. The resolution is earned, but the sense of risk and sacrifice is muted for a piece aiming at the very top tier.</comment>

The emotional aftereffect is sincere but somewhat managed rather than allowed to bloom from implication alone.</comment>
