While the environment doesn’t strongly constrain physical choices, it absolutely frames and deepens the central contemplative action and final image (“The tide turned”). This earns a high score, just shy of 9 because setting is more atmospheric than tactically constraining.</comment>

The emotional escalation is thus more deepening than tightening, credible but gentle enough to limit the stakes band.</comment>

There is a mild risk of over-saturation—figurative language appears very frequently—but it rarely muddies clarity or feels like mere ornament. A top-tier venue might suggest slight trimming of abstractions in the final third, but the overall craft is high.</comment>

The core idea—that time’s meaning resides in attention to liminal moments—is not new, but the particularization here (anemones, barnacles, hermit crabs, cicada) makes it memorable.
