Closure lands cleanly as a Resonant Reconfiguration/Conceptual Repricing: she surrenders the tool that defined her and redefines mapping as composition rather than absorption. <question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The ultrasonic forest is richly rendered through non-visual senses (vibrations, pitches, textures) in a way that is tightly bound to this blind POV. Environmental features are not decorative: moss that chimes, mushrooms in C-minor, and the silent hollow sphere all constrain her tactics and culminate in the charged final image of the glowing ring on moss. World rules (sound as landscape; silence as compressed potential) are consistent and learned through her movement rather than exposition. The density of imagery is high but generally purposeful and oriented around her vocation and senses.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she can find a stillpoint stable enough to navigate overwhelming emotional depths—is clear and grows as the forest forces her into increasingly painful resonances. The major tightening is the forest turning explicitly into a crucible of personal memories, culminating in the choice to step into and then out of the stillpoint without retreating from feeling. The price of leaving the ring behind is conceptually strong but could land even more sharply with one more concrete sense of what continuing to absorb languages cost her. Overall, conflict is more contemplative than sharp, but the internal pressure is credible and accumulative.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically from particulars: sound as emotional cartography, silence as potential rather than void, and grief/joy as harmonics of the same note. The forest tests these ideas by embodying different emotional chords and forcing her to live them rather than name them. The one explicit articulation—“that grief and joy were not opposites, but harmonics of the same note”—skirts on-the-nose but is mostly earned by prior sensory work. The ending image of a new trail of notes growing from her original word nicely externalizes the shift without preaching.</comment>

The diction is elevated but coherent with the quasi-mythic register of the piece, and the brief remembered details (Prague widow, Icelandic log) are integrated smoothly. There is no head-hopping or stance confusion; psychic distance is stable and appropriate.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Line-level craft is strong: sentences are clean, largely free of redundancy, and often do double duty for atmosphere and character. A few formulations (“the zero before the song, the breath before the scream, the love before the loss”) approach purple and slightly over-explain what is already clear in context, but they don’t obscure closure. The final reweighting—her shift from absorbing to composing languages—feels both surprising and inevitable in hindsight.
