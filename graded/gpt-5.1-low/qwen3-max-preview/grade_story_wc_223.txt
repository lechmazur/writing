<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the narrator (and others) can reclaim/authenticate experience and gesture outside scripted power—is clear and steadily intensifies. The final refusal to bow and insistence on “listen” foreclose a path back to performance, though the price is conceptual (being truly seen, potential future conflict) rather than materially dramatized. Stakes remain somewhat abstract, but for a lyric piece they’re legible and coherent.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>Theming around absence, unsung lives, and unscripted truth is rich and consistent, with strong images like the pawnless board and the ring on a vacant square. The most resonant work happens in gesture (the widow’s unbinding, the non-bow, the sovereign’s terror) rather than in those declarative sentences. Overall, the piece invites reflection and lingers, but trims of explicit moralizing could heighten it.</comment>

Some thematic articulations are familiar in content, but the delivery vehicle is inventive and memorable.</comment>
