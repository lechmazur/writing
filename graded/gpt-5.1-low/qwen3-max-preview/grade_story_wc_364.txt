<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how Arlo will use up a finite, powerful resource and whether anything will replace it—is clear, and the silk’s depletion gives the narrative a built-in clock. However, pressure leading up to this moment remains mostly abstract; Arlo never seems tempted to hoard, despair, or quit, and no concrete opposition or loss tests his resolve on the page. As a result, escalation feels more like a graceful curve than a truly narrowing corridor of options.</comment>

Still, the closing images and structural echoes carry genuine emotional and conceptual aftereffect without resorting to outright moralizing.</comment>
