<question>2</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The structure is a clean single-scene, accumulative lyric where each return to ritual, compass, and sky builds toward the filament’s appearance. Closure lands via a resonant reconfiguration: the same silence that was oppressive becomes “companionable,” and witnessing seems to alter the anomaly. Stakes remain largely internal and reflective, which keeps the piece from feeling structurally bolder.</comment>

<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The observation deck, time-dilated sky, and “echoed silence” create a vivid, specific atmosphere woven tightly into her perception. The environment doesn’t just backdrop the story; the altered sky and deck’s stillness are integral to her ritual and the final image. While the setting doesn’t sharply constrain options, it meaningfully charges her choices and the ending.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she will surrender to fear/nostalgia or remain as an exploratory witness in a broken temporal world—is clear but mostly internal. Pressure modestly escalates from quiet ritual to the sudden appearance of the violet filament, which prompts a choice to engage and record rather than retreat. The sense of cost (isolation, pariahdom, estrangement from family) is present but mostly summarized, not actively pressured in-scene. As a result, the climax reads contemplative rather than sharply testing her resolve.</comment>

It was the choice to remain curious when the world lost its rhythm.” A few lines come close to explicit thesis-stating, but they’re grounded in concrete memory and action. The final image of companionable silence offers a resonant, non-didactic answer to the story’s questions.</comment>
