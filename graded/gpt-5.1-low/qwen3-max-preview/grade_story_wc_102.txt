<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she can locate and access the fate of unused love—is clearly established and followed through, with escalation in the shift from observation to pursuit to direct contact with the pilgrim and the tear. The piece largely avoids sermonizing; insight arises from enacted ritual rather than expository statement. Complexity surfaces in treating unused love not as waste but as an active, permeating chorus, which complicates conventional notions of loss. A few lines edge near pseudo-profound abstraction, but the line-level concreteness around them keeps the theme anchored.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The synthesis of camera obscura artistry, breath-based healing of absences, Kwan Yin’s literal tear, and a “pilgrim of sparks” collecting unused love is striking and memorable; it feels like a coherent cosmology rather than a collage of cool ideas.
