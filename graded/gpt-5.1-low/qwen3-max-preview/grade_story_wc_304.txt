<question>2</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The accumulative/lyric structure is clean: we move steadily from set-up of vocation and chip to the infusion and the final image of drifting lanterns, with a clear Charged Poise closure. Causality within the ritual is internally consistent and orientation is never in doubt. The main limitation is structural flatness: there is only a gentle tightening toward the infusion rather than a true turn or constriction of options. As a result, the piece feels like one beautifully sustained beat rather than a shaped arc under real pressure.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The skyborne grotto and aurora are rendered with striking specificity (“stalactites of condensed skylight,” “liquid radiance”), and the environment’s properties meaningfully relate to the chip and the drifting constellation of sparks. The setting strongly inflects tone and theme, and the aurora’s slowing at the end elegantly charges the closure image. What keeps this just shy of the very top tier is that the grotto rarely constrains her choices; it’s a gorgeously realized backdrop more than a full-on tactical constraint.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preserving a final lucid instant at the brink of oblivion—is clear and conceptually weighty, and the act of beginning the infusion functions as a soft tightening. Yet there is little genuine opposition or risk of failure; everything unfolds as intended, and her agency is never seriously tested. The tension is mostly conceptual and tonal rather than dramatized through reversals or trade-offs, so escalation remains modest.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Themes of death as art, the ethics of stalling oblivion, and awareness at the threshold are woven organically through vocation, ritual, and final image without explicit moralizing. The closing vision of joining a “silent constellation” offers a resonant answer to the opening premise. Some complexity is left on the table by the absence of any voiced doubt or counter-impulse about this practice.</comment>

There is no head-hopping and no register drift; the elevated language feels appropriate to the ritualized, sacred frame. The voice is strong enough for high-tier venues, though the unwaveringly lush register risks slight monotony.</comment>
