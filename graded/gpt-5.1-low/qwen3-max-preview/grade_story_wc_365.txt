Stakes remain focused and the before/after is legible: she moves from conjecture about a silenced truth to specific, costly knowledge of her mother’s sacrifice. The green shard functions as a micro-setting/tool that constrains how she moves between worlds and charges the final image when it cools in the ruined bay. Environment and recurring objects clearly shape options and reweight the ending, satisfying the high-band gate.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—uncovering the withheld truth about her mother and the nature of her own existence—is legible, and there is one meaningful deepening when she finds herself in a universe where she has been erased. The cost emerges mainly at the realization beat rather than through contested action. As a result, tension is solid but not gripping, with escalation more conceptual than felt.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of truth, sacrifice, divergence, and responsibility are strongly integrated, especially in the revelation that her mother anchored an “accidental” Elira at the price of her own reality. That said, the story leans into explicit thematic statement (“some truths don’t set you free—they make you responsible”) rather than letting subtext and image do all the work, which slightly flattens complexity. Even so, the final repricing of knowledge and selfhood has genuine emotional and conceptual resonance.</comment>

Occasionally, the diction edges toward abstract (“cyclically spontaneous failure”), but overall the voice feels controlled rather than generic.</comment>

Momentum holds through the transition and revelation without looped phrasing or structural clutter.</comment>
