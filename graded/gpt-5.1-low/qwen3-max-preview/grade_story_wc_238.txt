<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The central stake—whether they can salvage trust and shared art before the orchard (and by extension, a chapter of their past) is lost—is clear and emotionally felt. His accusation “You stole my music” and her correction “I stole your trust” sharpen the conflict nicely. Still, the confrontation resolves a bit smoothly; one harder refusal or moment of genuine risk (him nearly rejecting the manuscript or sending her away) would heighten cost and escalation.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Themes of imperfect miracles, flawed art, and the messy nature of forgiveness are tightly woven through objects (perfume, manuscript), profession (gossip/exorcism), and setting (archive/orchard as doomed legacy). A couple of lines (“Because miracles don’t wait for perfect timing”) flirt with on-the-nose commentary but are buffered by surrounding scene work.</comment>

Tone (“ethereal grit”) and worldbuilding risk inventive moves that mostly pay off.
