<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The drift-ice marina and surrounding Arctic world are vividly rendered (“floes still groaned like old bones,” “violet auroras”) and tightly filtered through Elara’s listening obsession. Environment constrains action—black ice causes the fall that triggers the chime; the floe enables her drift and isolation; the cold nearly kills her—and charges the final rescue image. The heirloom parasol functions as a micro-setting object, bridging marina, floe, and later hospital spaces. A slight pullback is that once she’s adrift, the physical mechanics of survival on the floe are compressed in favor of mood, softening some situational immediacy.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how Elara will relate to death and endings—is established early and tightened through the glacier-collapse sequence where she chooses the floe instead of immediate safety. That choice clearly forecloses a path and entails risk, while her internal need (understanding endings) conflicts with base survival instinct. The later refusal of euthanasia consultations is an earned stasis/renunciation that crystallizes the arc. The main limitation is that external conflict (disaster relief stakes, community danger) remains largely atmospheric rather than fully interwoven as a counter-pressure to her inward pull.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>Theme is rich and coherent: endings as thresholds, not discrete events, and the reframing of euthanasia from pure cessation to a sung transition. There is slight over-articulation in lines like “She realized then: she hadn’t come to hear endings,” which edges toward explicit thesis rather than leaving it fully in image and implication.</comment>

Rhythm modulates well between long, textured sentences and sharp beats (“She froze.”) at key turns. Occasionally the register skirts the edge of self-conscious poetics, but it remains coherent and legible throughout.</comment>

The story risks a highly stylized lyric mode and leans into its own weirdness rather than hedging, which pays off in the threshold/threshold-music reframing.
