Sensory and spatial details are precise, economical, and consistently filtered through the archivist’s ritualized, professional gaze.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—maintaining the precarious balance of her reversible exile through exacting mentorship—is clear, and the cartographer scene functions as a concrete test of that pressure. Still, the cartographer’s visible shift and the held shadow offer an earned, if quiet, sense of cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.9</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically from motif and action: resilience as missteps refused rather than apologized for, and the ethics of holding broken things without lying them whole. The closing addition—“not to cage, but to compass”—elegantly reweights the entire story’s stance on boundaries and care.</comment>

The emotional truth of combative mentorship and the refusal of sentimental wholeness grounds the fantasy conceit.
