Her agency is real (she chooses to merge absence rather than erase it), yet the lack of obstacles or real risk keeps tension modest. The resolution feels earned but low-pressure.</comment>

The prose modulates gently with the clock’s changing cadence, supporting an accumulative mood. What keeps it just short of top-tier is that the voice, though polished, is more generically lyrical than sharply idiosyncratic.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The fusion of archival practice with quasi-ritual temporal engineering—rewoven ceremonial rope plus vanished embassy seal in a clock tower—is an appealingly fresh angle. Structurally it’s fairly straightforward, so the boldness lies more in premise and objects than in narrative risk. With slightly more surprising complication or a sharper emotional cost, this could edge into truly standout territory.</comment>
