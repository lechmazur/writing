<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how to live with and transmute communal violence without replicating its methods—is clear and deepens as she moves from solitary ritual to receiving villagers, especially the child. The cost is psychological rather than situational, but the piece makes that cost legible in her unflinching coexistence with burning-house memories alongside the child’s wonder. Complexity is preserved: her surrender includes the cost of perpetual exposure to pain, and the story resists easy redemption of the village’s past.</comment>
