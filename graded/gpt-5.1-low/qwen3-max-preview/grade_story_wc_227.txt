<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The central stake—can she turn her own and the world’s fractures into sacred meaning before the workshop (and possibly she herself) is destroyed—is clearly established and escalates. Her agency is crucial: every escalation comes from her choosing to continue the ritual despite worsening conditions, culminating in the final choice to offer her abandoned self and tears. The main limitation is that external peril never quite forces a concrete tactical adaptation beyond “finish faster,” so the conflict stays slightly more symbolic than physically consequential.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The theme of making brokenness sacred—kintsugi as an ethic rather than an aesthetic—is threaded through concrete acts (filling scars with gold linked to precise failures and betrayals). However, a few lines near the end (“She hadn’t conjured an elixir…she’d conjured meaning”) move into explicit thesis territory, slightly reducing the power of the subtextual work that precedes them. Overall the piece still invites reflection beyond itself, with a rich, coherent metaphor field.</comment>
