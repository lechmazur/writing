Her decision to leave the piece, return to the villages, and reconfigure her practice is a genuine turn, but it comes with relatively little on-page cost beyond implied career/market sacrifices. Tension operates more as a meditative tightening than as a sharp narrowing of options.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Theme—the reimagining of survival and alliance as shared gesture rather than tech or treaties—emerges coherently from recurrent images of hands, lanterns, and driftwood. However, the piece sometimes states its thesis a bit too directly (“She would show them how to reimagine alliance—not as treaty or tech, but as touch…”), edging toward gentle sermon rather than fully trusting image and action. The whispered line “Simplicity is not absence. It is architecture.” similarly flirts with aphorism, though it is thematically apt.</comment>
