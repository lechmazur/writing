<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether he can keep joy’s memory from “rotting in silence”—is clear early and remains coherent, and the cost of failure (emotional petrification) is palpable. <question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of grief, memory, and the ethics of tending to what can’t be restored emerge organically from the concrete particulars of echo, leaf blower, and hourglass.
