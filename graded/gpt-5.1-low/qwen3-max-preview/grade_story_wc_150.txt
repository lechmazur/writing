<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether faith and communal orientation can be restored through this act of reassembly—is clear and coherently pursued. Her choice to root herself permanently is a meaningful renunciation, though it arrives as a serene inevitability rather than the end of a felt struggle. The piece succeeds as quiet, accumulative conflict but stops short of sharper costs or a live contest of options.</comment>

The resonance is real but somewhat over-explained, dulling the afterimage.</comment>
