The final image of the clipboard lifted into triple moonlight effectively charges the environment into a thematic agent.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elira can muster a more binding, embodied peace—reads clearly, but on-page opposition is minimal. There is a mild escalation from solitude and doubt to communal, risky commitment, yet no moment where refusal or sabotage genuinely threatens the outcome. As a result, tension is present but muted, more contemplative than pressurized.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Themes of the price of redemption, the binding power of ritual, and peace as an ongoing practice rather than a single victory are well integrated with the objects and actions on the page. However, several lines state the thesis quite directly (“Redemption, she had learned, was never free…”, “This… is not a treaty. The ending resonates, but its emotional and conceptual answer is a bit more told than it needs to be.</comment>
