The frosted hourglass and coffee rings function as micro-setting objects that charge the final image. Sensory focus (light shifting, cold glass, hum at the skull) is selective and purposeful, deepening the mood without clogging momentum.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant conflict—how Maren will live without the visible record of her scars and whether she can restart time after her mother’s death—is clearly articulated. Her final agency—turning the hourglass and choosing to inhabit both backward- and forward-facing passages—resolves that tension credibly. The piece remains quiet and could bear slightly sharper on-page cost or risk in the decision, but it still reads as a meaningful tightening for this length.</comment>

The “displaced miracle” and “two passages” formulations are clear, resonant answers to the story’s question about what becomes of suffering. There’s mild risk of over-clarity in phrases like “displaced miracle,” but they stop short of sermonizing.</comment>
