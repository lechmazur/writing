The “cool and faintly trembling” wall at the close neatly fuses setting with the story’s central question. One could push environmental constraint slightly further—have the dereliction impinge more directly on her safety—to reach the very top band.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Maren embraces or rejects her mother’s potentially ruinous discovery—is present early and clarified by the diary entries and film strip. The final choice to go deeper is clearly a renunciation of safety and normality, with an implied cost of obsession and isolation. The escalation is primarily internal and contemplative; a touch more external or temporal pressure could heighten the sense of narrowing options.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Theme coheres around legacy, obsession, and the idea that places “remember” and perhaps dream, with Maren’s inheritance of her mother’s work as the crucible. The closing act of walking deeper to “follow the sound of whatever the walls were dreaming” gives a resonant, non-literal answer to the central question. A few lines verge on direct statement (“The burden of legacy was not that it demanded continuation. It was that it demanded choice.”), but they stay just inside earned reflection.</comment>

The perceptual signature—acoustic-first, space-sensitive—is distinct and carries through to the end. Maren’s profession isn’t a gimmick; it drives method, imagery, and theme all the way to the final image.
