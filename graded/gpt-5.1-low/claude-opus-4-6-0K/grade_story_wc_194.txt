<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The core conflict—truth-telling versus dynastic loyalty and comfort—is clearly established and tightened as Elara moves from musing to a concrete plan of exposure. Escalation is primarily internal and intellectual rather than confrontational, which suits the piece but keeps tension at a simmer rather than a boil. <question>5</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>Themes of inherited guilt, silence as complicity, and the ethics of unraveling a legacy are woven tightly through recurring images: the crank that cannot play, standing waves used for eavesdropping, “eloquently silent” as both character trait and moral stance. The piece invites reflection on what it means to “end” a dynasty and whether that act is justice, betrayal, or both. The thematic web feels coherent and organically grown from particulars.</comment>
