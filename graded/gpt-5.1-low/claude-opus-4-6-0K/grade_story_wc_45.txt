<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The core stakes—rescuing the Harrow baby and, more deeply, reconfiguring Maren’s relationship to her own failure—are legible early and tighten as she moves from skeptical observer to active seeker. The maze traversal is summarized and largely frictionless, so external difficulty remains modest. Nonetheless, the final decision to “choose to believe it” in the face of ambiguity is a clear, earned stance crystallization that forecloses her prior posture of strict skepticism.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of faith versus certainty, grief as an un-set fracture, and the possibility of remaking guilt into “constellations” are woven through concrete images (bones, teeth, stars, thorns) and Maren’s craft rather than abstract sermonizing. A few lines tilt toward explicit articulation (“Permission to forgive herself,” “some fractures ask to be set with faith”), which slightly blunts the otherwise subtle resonance. Still, the ending’s quiet charged poise—hands on oak, listening into an unverifiable elsewhere—leaves a lingering, complex aftertaste rather than a tidy moral.</comment>

Psychic distance is well managed, tightening around key realizations without drifting into authorial omniscience.
