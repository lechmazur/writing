<question>3</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The setting is vivid, specific, and consistently filtered through Maret’s archivist sensibility: salt as both corrosive and preservative, tide marks on walls, bleached stone, and “Ivory Harbor after rain” as a blank page. The harbor/blank-page image charges the final moment and ties world, vocation, and theme together. The only ceiling is that the environment shapes meaning more than concrete tactical choices.</comment>

Her agency at the end (deciding to treat ruin as a starting point) is thematically strong but faces little resistance or dilemma on the page. <question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Themes of absence, preservation, and rebuilding meaning from fragments are woven tightly through plot, imagery, and Maret’s habits (“Every gap is a shape. The piece could reach higher if the personal cost of that stance were made slightly sharper and less serene.</comment>
