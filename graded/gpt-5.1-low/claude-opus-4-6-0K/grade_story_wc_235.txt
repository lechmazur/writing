<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Wren can manage her psychic disarray and mortality terror by narrativizing dreams—is clear from early on and tightens through the revelation of scrambled chronology and her escalating reliance on birch bark. However, conflict remains largely internal and textural; there’s no marked reversal or narrowing of options, only an intensification of the same strategy (write, sleep, watch younger selves). The piece resists simple morals—her strategy of refuge in dreams feels both sustaining and faintly self-erasing.
