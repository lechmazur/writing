<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elara can use the coin/orchard to transform splintered possibility into something bearable—is clear and threads the whole piece. Her agency shows in choosing to perform, to persist with this method, and to reach into the spiraling light, but opposition is mostly implicit (entropy, despair) rather than sharply felt in the moment. <question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically: unfinished stories, fractured time, and the possibility of weaving bridges rather than restoring wholeness. The piece resists a neat moral by accepting enduring fracture while finding a kind of grace in partial connection. A couple of sentences (“what has been is not gone but merely distributed across time’s broken mosaic”) edge close to explicit thesis, but they’re grounded enough in image not to feel like sermonizing.</comment>
