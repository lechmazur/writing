The scrimshaw and whale function as charged micro-setting objects that shape choice. The atmosphere of salt, rusted warships, and king tides sustains tone without feeling like a catalog.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The conflict is primarily internal—Maren’s reluctance to be everyone’s emotional repository versus her unsatisfied sense of self—but it’s sharpened by the ex-soldiers’ need and the metaphysical pull of Voss/whale. Stakes remain intimate yet consequential, and the price (abandoning her community role, entering the unknown) is legible. The pressure arc is subtle rather than jagged but still tight for this length.</comment>

The final image of Voss holding steady reweights the whole piece as a story about choosing oneself and answering a latent calling. One or two lines (“a woman done carrying everyone”) edge close to explicit thesis but are still grounded in character.</comment>
