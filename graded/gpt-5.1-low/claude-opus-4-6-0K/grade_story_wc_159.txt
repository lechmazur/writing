<question>4</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preserving the living sound of names against oblivion—is established early and returns at the climax when the lullaby activates the entire terrace. That said, conflict remains primarily conceptual and internal; there’s no on-page choice where she must forgo one concrete good to preserve another. The escalation is satisfying for a lyric mode but could feel slightly static to a reader seeking sharper dilemma or consequence.</comment>
