<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stakes (recovering her family’s stolen resilience and surviving a dangerous Turning flight) are clear and interwoven via the ticking-hour constraint. <question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of memory, injustice, and the artificiality of “resilience” are effectively embodied in the gathering of echoes and the buried treaty; the final line “But echoes remember everything” nicely reweights the entire journey. However, the moral calculus is fairly tidy—corruption versus excluded family—without much ambiguity or complicity shading Kael’s own position. The insight is earned through specific discovery, but the thematic articulation comes close to being on-the-nose rather than fully left in image and implication.</comment>

Still, Kael’s interior voice is not strongly idiosyncratic—another competent pilot could plausibly sound similar—so the perceptual signature doesn’t quite reach standout distinctiveness. Tense and stance remain consistent and support clarity throughout.</comment>
