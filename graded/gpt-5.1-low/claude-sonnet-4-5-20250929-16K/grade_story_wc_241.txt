Causality is modest but intact; nothing feels contrived or teleported. The only thing holding it out of the top band is that the central pressure never quite tightens into a true dilemma—her path forward, once glimpsed, is relatively unopposed.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The rainwashed library, skylights, candle lantern, and unmowed clover-lawn create a coherent, textured atmosphere that clearly reflects Mira’s mood. The lantern functions as a micro-setting object that recurs and travels with her decision. Setting is more tonal than constraining, which is why it falls just short of the very highest band.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The conflict is primarily internal: how to live with probability, uncertainty, and her father’s self-destructive contradictions, and what stance she will take as a teacher and daughter. Stakes are emotionally real—her father’s health, the meaning of his life’s work, and her own ethical vocation—but the pressure remains low-key and reflective. The rehab call and his request for the notebooks provide a gentle tightening that nudges her into a choice. Still, there’s little genuine opposition or risk to her decision, so escalation is more deepening than sharpening.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The thematic weave of probability versus lived uncertainty, knowledge versus behavior, and “lucid contradiction” is impressively integrated into every layer: the father’s gambling, the notebooks’ emotional labels, the proposed syllabus, and the closing recognition that “the mathematics wouldn’t save them.” The piece resists a tidy moral by allowing Mira to seek responsible engagement with uncertainty rather than simple repudiation of gambling or math. A few lines verge on thematic statement (“widen perspectives responsibly”) but they’re rooted enough in concrete circumstance to avoid sermonizing. The final image of remade, temporary world after rain reweights the prior material with conceptual and emotional resonance.</comment>
