<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The structure is clean: one continuous scene governed by the tide window and a sequence of small, causally linked interventions that culminate in a charged final image. <question>3</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The tidal arcade is vivid, tactile, and conceptually integrated: pillars, tidal pools, and starlight all inform the kinds of miracles she can orchestrate (e.g., the bioluminescent plankton photo, the time pressure from the returning sea). The environment genuinely constrains choice via the tide and shapes the social flow of tourists. The chess bishop also functions as a micro-setting object, charging the final image with “diagonal truth.” This is very strong, though one more moment where the physical risk of the tide impinges on her actions could push it even higher.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake is essentially whether Marina can engineer meaningful, serendipitous connections within a limited window, and the piece does show a series of successful, increasingly intricate manipulations. Because her tactics work smoothly and no real resistance or cost appears, tension stays gentle and contemplative rather than truly tightening.</comment>

There’s a mild tendency toward essayistic phrasing in a few explanatory sentences, but the POV distance and texture remain stable and persuasive throughout.</comment>
