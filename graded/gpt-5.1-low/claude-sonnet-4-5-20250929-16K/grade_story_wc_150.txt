<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Structure is clean: arrival and premise, exploratory wandering, discovery of the sundial, then reburial and communal restoration above ground. The piece uses an accumulative/textural drive, and the closure—a conceptual repricing of faith and interruption—lands clearly. <question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The orchard-catacomb setting is vivid and thematically charged: roots snaking through bones, bone-rich earth feeding trees, the pear-root chamber, and the displaced sundial all feel specific and lived. The final scene with the sundial among wild trees, branches casting “living” shadows, shows the setting directly charging the last image. A slightly more tactile sense of Mira physically working in that space under constraint could push this to the very top tier.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Mira’s desire to complete the rites and reclaim a sense of rooted faith—is clear, but external opposition is mild and the escalation is mostly conceptual. Her wandering and the sundial discovery deepen meaning rather than sharply narrowing options, and the priest’s resistance has already softened by the time he appears. The main tightening comes from revaluing the orchard/catacomb complex and choosing to reinstall the sundial above ground, but this doesn’t feel like a hard trade-off. Conflict is present and coherent, yet it never fully bites; the piece reads more as meditation than test.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of death nourishing life, faith as evolving rather than static, and diaspora seeking reconnection are well embedded in concrete images like roots piercing catacombs and the inverted sundial. However, the final paragraphs veer into explicit thesis statements (“Faith didn't need to be what it was—it needed to grow like these trees”), edging toward sermonizing rather than leaving more work to image and implication. The piece still resonates, but the moral is a bit too plainly spelled out for top-tier subtlety.</comment>

The priest’s single piece of dialogue feels natural within the story’s slightly elevated register and efficiently carries subtext about his lapsed faith. There are no POV slips or register jolts, though a touch more idiosyncrasy in Mira’s internal diction could sharpen her individuality further.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Prose is clean, precise, and rhythmically varied, with most sentences doing double duty for atmosphere and meaning (“Time had inverted this place—what was above had gone below, what was below had broken through above” is a good example). It stops just shy of the very highest band because some lines, especially in the thematic wrap-up, tell what the images already imply, slightly reducing density and surprise.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The fusion of orchard and catacombs, tree roots literally feeding on ancestors, and a seasonal sundial buried and then reinstalled is a striking, coherent conceit. The story offers a fresh angle on diaspora-return and faith narratives by rooting them in this specific, inverted ecology rather than generic nostalgia. The conceptual move that “interruption” can be transformation is thoughtful, if aligned with familiar lit-fiction concerns. It’s inventive and well-realized, though not so structurally or emotionally radical as to feel groundbreaking at the absolute top tier.</comment>
