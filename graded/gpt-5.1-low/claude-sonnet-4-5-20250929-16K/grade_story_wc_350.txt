Closure is strong and on-page: the previously silent silo offers a child’s song, and the others respond in harmony, creating a decisive before/after in community dynamics. Spatial relationships (tower broadcasting to seven silos) remain clear throughout, grounding the metaphor of isolation and connection.</comment>

The emotional and moral risk is clear, but the sense of possible failure or backlash remains somewhat muted; there’s little impression that anything worse than disappointment could happen. As a result, tension is steady and sincere rather than grippingly high-pressure.</comment>

The emotional aftereffect is warm and hopeful, but a bit tidy.</comment>
