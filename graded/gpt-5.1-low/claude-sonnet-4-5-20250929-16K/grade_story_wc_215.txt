The only thing holding this just beneath the very top tier is that the physical environment, beyond the box, doesn’t tighten tactical options in the climax so much as frame them.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake is conceptual—whether the covenant of remembrance will be honored—and it’s signaled and pursued clearly, with the puzzle of the box as the main pressure. Still, the conflict remains relatively low-friction; there’s little real risk or pushback beyond the implicit spiritual stakes, and Silas never seriously flirts with refusal. The resolution is earned but gentle, with more deepening than sharp tightening.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Themes of memory, labor acknowledgment, and the sacredness of unspoken words are woven tightly through bees, ghosts, the list of names, and Silas’s relationship to silence. Some thematic statements—“Some words gained power by remaining unspoken, held sacred in silence…”—veer close to overt essaying, telling the reader the thesis rather than fully trusting the images. Even so, the piece avoids simple moralizing and leaves a lasting conceptual and emotional resonance.</comment>

Overall, there’s high information density, minimal redundancy, and no significant artifacts or loops.</comment>
