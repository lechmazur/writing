The birch bark as “organic memory” neatly fuses setting with theme. The environment doesn’t strongly constrain tactics, but it does charge the final lamp-and-bark image effectively.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Mara can make sense of her shattered chronology enough to endure—is clear and thematically resonant. Pressure increases via cumulative anomalies (grave, out-of-order days, seeing herself from the ceiling), and her need to theorize becomes more desperate. However, there’s limited on-page confrontation or choice beyond continued documentation; the conflict remains largely internal and static in terms of options. The result is persuasive but relatively low-key in escalation for a “highest echelon” target.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Themes of narrative as survival mechanism, the tyranny of linear time, and “feral nostalgia” are woven through concrete images (grave, soaring dreams, scrolls) rather than abstract statement. Her jealousy of earlier selves and the idea that “only broken time reveals how desperately humans need their stories” avoid moralizing while offering conceptual bite. A touch less direct thematic labeling in places (“The distinction seems quaint now, almost charming”) would elevate it further.</comment>

Temporal uncertainty is rendered in a controlled way that’s legible yet disorienting in the intended sense.
