Closure lands on the page: the final glass cookie and released fortune concretize her new stance. Weather/light (dawn, frost, shimmer) track with and amplify the emotional arc. The environment doesn’t force hard tactical trade-offs, but it strongly charges the final images.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>Stakes are largely internal—will Lian remain trapped in longing or transform it into creative agency—and that core is clear but under-pressurized. There’s no real counterforce or possibility of failure on the page; once she begins, the ritual proceeds smoothly to success with no meaningful setback or sacrifice. The supposed temporal pressure (“Before exhaustion hits”) never bites into her options. As a result, escalation feels more like a steady amplification of imagery than a tightening of conflict with visible cost.</comment>

However, the final moral-like line—“Your heart is the baton; the world is your score”—edges toward overt thesis and slightly flattens the otherwise suggestive imagery. Subtext remains more atmospheric than conflicted, but the emotional aftereffect is coherent and non-trivial.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The third-person limited voice is lyrically consistent, with a distinct taste for reflective, musical metaphors that fit Lian’s artistic obsession. There’s no head-hopping or stance confusion, and the pattern of heightened language is taught early and maintained. At times the register nears uniform rhapsody, which slightly reduces modulation under peak moments but doesn’t break POV integrity.</comment>
