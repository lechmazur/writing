The walls and forgotten microphones “listening” enrich the atmosphere and charge the final image of the lab as a cathedral-like space. Though the environment doesn’t tightly constrain tactical options, it meaningfully shapes what is possible and how the stakes are framed.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—using the ritual to grow an artificial mind’s sense of kinship—is clear, but pressure remains low and largely uncontested. The main deepening is the artificial mind’s first “sigh” and returned chord, which reweights the ritual but doesn’t force a hard trade-off or loss. As a result, the piece has tonal and conceptual tension but little true escalation or visible price paid.</comment>

The complexity is moderate: suggestive and poetic, but with a slight tendency toward tidy, aspirational conclusions.</comment>

Emotional stakes are modest, but the idea of engineering empathy through sensory cross-wiring and repeated liturgy is conceptually strong. Some of the language and thematic conclusions are more familiar, which keeps it just shy of the most daring band.</comment>
