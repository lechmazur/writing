<question>4</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The core stake—whether he will finally face and correct a withheld truth that underpins his fraudulent trade—is clear but under-pressured. The carvings yield their message on first attempt, and the client’s stakes never actually test him. Subtext exists in images (tide, carvings, the watch) but is often overshadowed by explicit statement.</comment>

At times, the high-register, metaphor-heavy narration slightly outpaces what a person under real moral stress might indulge in, softening immediacy. Still, the voice is controlled enough to feel intentional rather than generically ornate.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The prose is polished at the sentence level with mostly clean syntax and some strong images, but it leans into purple excess and repetition. Momentum sometimes stalls under the descriptive load, especially mid-story. Trimming a layer of figurative language and consolidating repeated concepts would markedly sharpen impact.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The central conceit—a black market genealogist who fabricates noble pasts seeking redemption via a literal shore of driftwood memories—is fresh and memorable. The redemption arc itself follows familiar contours and the explicit moral aphorism tempers the daring of the premise. Overall, it’s an inventive frame executed with care, though not yet at a startlingly original thematic depth.</comment>
