<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—earning forgiveness for the canyon’s ancient wounds via art—is clear, and there is some escalation as the solitary mischief widens into a communal chorus and the canyon holds its breath before responding. The ritual being “imminent” adds a soft clock but doesn’t noticeably constrain tactics or force a hard trade-off. The emotional deepening works, but pressure never quite tightens into a true crucible.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Themes of forgiveness, shared memory, and refracted truth are coherent and carried through specific motifs (echoes, the spire, the mural, the chorus), and the final line “We are the echo” reweights prior images effectively. The emotional aftereffect is gentle and sincere but somewhat tidy; ambiguity or resistance would deepen resonance. Still, the thematic architecture is solid and integrated with the imagery.</comment>

Rhythm is generally well controlled, with longer, incantatory sentences suited to the ritual atmosphere. The diction sometimes trends uniformly high across all moments, which slightly flattens modulation, but not enough to break the spell.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The prose is lush and generally precise, with many lines doing double duty for mood and theme (e.g., the spire “splitting [light] into shards of cold silver”). Nonetheless, clarity remains good; syntax is clean, and the closure image is not obscured by figurative excess. Trimming a few redundant metaphors and abstract statements would raise density and make the strongest images stand out more.</comment>

The refract/echo motif is conceptually tight and used in several inventive ways. It stops short of a truly surprising moral or structural turn, but the imaginative world-building and method of atonement feel publication-ready.</comment>
