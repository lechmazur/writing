The main weakness is that opposition is soft and brief (the factions “demand” and “shout,” but don’t force tactical adaptation), making the resolution feel a bit frictionless for the scale of the stated conflict.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>Thematic through-line—healing numbness through action that holds complementary opposites, and reconceiving clones as partners rather than theft—is coherent and organically tied to the blade, brain, and factions. Yet the piece leans heavily on explicit thesis statements (“The method was simple yet profound,” “a testament that even after ego death, one can rebuild through complementary opposites”) that verge on didactic, especially in the final third. Subtext exists in the imagery (shared pulse, rising dojo) but is often overshadowed by overt moralizing.</comment>
