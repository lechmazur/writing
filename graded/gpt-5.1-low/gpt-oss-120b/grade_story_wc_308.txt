<question>2</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The accumulative, lyric structure is coherent: arrowhead discovery → appraisal ritual → bird-pattern reading → internalized map → inward turn. Environmental details like “centuries of dust” and the vibrating towers integrate with theme and closure instead of feeling like a postcard. A bit more interaction between physical constraint and moment-to-moment choice could push this higher.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>5.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—learning to navigate without moving—is stated early, but pressure around it never tightens; nothing concrete threatens her, the canyon, or the opportunity. The “cost” is mostly conceptual (choosing understanding over motion) rather than dramatized as a trade-off or loss. This keeps the conflict from escalating beyond a meditative quest, which limits narrative tension.</comment>

The resonance is real but not unusually complex.</comment>

At times, the voice lapses into generalized “epic” register rather than something idiosyncratically hers, which slightly flattens character-specificity. Still, control and cohesion are strong enough to feel publishable with light refinement.</comment>

Still, the mythic sensorium and specific magical methods stand out as distinct within the genre.</comment>
