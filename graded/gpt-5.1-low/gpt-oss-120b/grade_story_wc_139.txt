<question>2</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The story follows a clean single-scene structure: Mara waits, grief accumulates, the breach occurs, the ink writes a contract, and consequences ripple outward, ending in a conceptual reconfiguration of asylum. Causality is coherent under an accumulative/textural lens—the pressure of grief and waiting enables the breach, which enables the contract, which reweights asylum. Closure lands as a Conceptual Repricing rather than a decisive on-page event, and the cost is more abstract than felt, since the officials’ transformation is summarized rather than confronted. Stakes of failure (what happens if the ink doesn’t work or the officials reject her) are underdeveloped, softening the structural impact.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The funeral home prep room is vividly and economically evoked—lilies, incense, polished oak, and the sense of “whispering shadows” create an immediate, coherent atmosphere. The ink pot functions as a recurring object-as-setting, anchoring the scene physically and conceptually through to the final “clink.” Overall, the space feels specific and thematically tuned without slipping into static tour description.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—providing proof to secure political asylum—is stated clearly, and the internal process of waiting for the breach provides a modest tightening as grief thickens and the ink begins to glow. However, the conflict remains largely uncontested; no counterforce (internal panic, risk of exposure, or failure mode) meaningfully pressures Mara’s tactics. The climax (the ink writing the contract and the commission’s response) unfolds smoothly without a hard trade-off or visible loss, so the stakes feel conceptual rather than visceral. As a result, escalation is present but muted for a piece aiming at elite publication.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Theme—fairness as reciprocal covenant rather than legal formality, and death as a conduit for ethical rebalancing—emerges clearly from the ink’s function, Mara’s lineage, and the asylum frame. The notion of “mathematics of a fair trade” provides a strong thematic spine, and the redefinition of asylum as “a living covenant of reciprocity” neatly reweights earlier material. However, the story often states its ideas directly (“She understood that the fairness she sought could not be imposed by law alone...”), slipping toward essayistic exposition rather than letting theme arise solely from image and action. The resonance is real but somewhat over-explained.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The third-person close voice is consistent, controlled, and comfortably handles lyric abstraction (“liquefy rigid thinking,” “breach in perception”) without losing basic clarity. At times, the register leans toward generalized conceptuality, which slightly distances us from Mara’s embodied experience, but it remains internally consistent rather than drifting arbitrarily. There is no dialogue to test, but the narrative stance is stable and apt for the story’s aims.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.3</grade><comment>The prose is generally strong, with vivid images like the ink “swirl[ing] like a midnight river” and equations becoming “veins of gold,” and most sentences pull weight for atmosphere and concept. Nonetheless, rhythm and clarity survive these flourishes, and the final image (“twin vessels for change”) is clean and resonant. Line-level craft is above average but not yet razor-honed for maximal compression.</comment>

A few moves—the bureaucratic transformation on reading the contract, the dissolving “walls” of prejudice—touch familiar symbolic territory and are more on-the-nose than surprising. Still, the core vision feels distinctive and committed rather than generic or template-driven.</comment>
