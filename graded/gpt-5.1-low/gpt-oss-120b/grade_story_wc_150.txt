<question>2</question><grade>6.6</grade><comment>The structure is straightforward: a single throughline from entering the orchard to completing the altar, with the magical activation as closure. The environment subtly acts on the action—the roots recoil and then guide, bones rattle in time with a “distant, unseen clock”—and the restored altar catching dawn light charges the final image. The recurring interplay of roots, bones, and light strongly anchors both tone and theme.</comment>

Escalation is a gentle deepening (roots reacting, light growing, final chime) rather than a tightening that narrows options. As a result, the conflict feels contemplative and low-risk, limiting tension and emotional payoff.</comment>

The resonance is real but relatively safe and tidy.</comment>

At times the lyricism becomes slightly uniform in tone, blunting moments that might benefit from sharper or more varied texture.</comment>
