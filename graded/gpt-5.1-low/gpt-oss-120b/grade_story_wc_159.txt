<question>3</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The “opal library above clouds,” glass orchids, and museum of extinct feelings are vivid and coherent, with strong sensory specificity and consistent surreal rules. However, the environment rarely constrains choice; it mainly amplifies atmosphere and theme. Visually and conceptually rich enough to approach top-tier, but not yet structurally indispensable.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether lost feelings and names can be preserved against erasure—is mostly treated as a given rather than tested. Her agency operates through steady repetition, not through a pressured choice that forecloses a path; there’s minimal escalation beyond deepening the ritual’s description. As a result, tension is low and stakes feel abstract rather than viscerally tightening.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of memory, preservation, and the cyclical nature of loss are coherent and emerge organically from recurring images (orchids, names, echoes, dunes-to-bloom metaphor). At times, the piece edges toward explicit statement (“so the desert of lost emotions could one day bloom again”), slightly flattening subtext. Still, the overall aftertaste is emotionally specific rather than generic.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The third-person lyrical voice is consistent, with a distinct lexicon (“foxglove regret,” “pale light seeped through opal panes,” “deserted marvels”) and controlled, incantatory rhythm. However, the diction sometimes tilts toward self-conscious ornamentation, with repeated constructions and a narrow emotional palette of wistful solemnity. Strong and memorable, but the intensity of the register occasionally risks homogeneity.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Line-level craft shows clear care: images are often striking, syntax is varied, and the piece reads smoothly aloud. Some metaphors feel more decorative than necessary, which slightly blurs impact and saps momentum. Clarity remains intact, but tighter selection would sharpen the already strong visuals.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The central conceit—a caretaker in an opal cloud-library preserving extinct feelings via glass orchids and a cracked wind chime—is fresh and imaginatively coherent. Emotional arc (from regret to purposeful acceptance) is familiar, but the execution through these symbols and mechanisms is distinctive. With slightly more risk in conflict or formal structure, this could press into a higher originality band.</comment>
