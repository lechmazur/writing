<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The accumulative/textural drive is coherent: retrieve the slide → measure the silence → awaken the vows → arrive at a reconfigured relation to oblivion. The library and sky actively shape the climax: the hum of books powers the device and the clouds’ “shards of silver” reinforce and echo the vows. It stops just short of 9+ because the environment rarely constrains her options; it enriches and charges the final image more than it forces hard choices.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether meaning and memory can be stabilized against oblivion—is clear but largely internal and contemplative. There is a mild escalation as frequencies shift, the sky “dissolves,” and the light intensifies, but the process unfolds smoothly without a true threat of failure or trade-off. Her agency is real (choosing to persist, to measure exactly, to record and finally to speak the last vow) yet none of these decisions foreclose a meaningful alternative path. The piece functions more as an unbroken ritual than a pressure arc with visible prices paid.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Themes of memory, archival labor, and resistance to oblivion are woven through concrete images—the hum of frozen books, luminous vows as filaments stitching time, the ledger humming at the end. The final vow image is strong, but the spelled-out moral edges toward sentimentality and reduces ambiguity. Resonance is solid but not subtle enough for higher-band literary work.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The third-person limited voice is steady and lyrically controlled, with a distinct lexicon (“quartz hush,” “vaulted ceiling,” “glacial marble”) that matches the protagonist’s meticulous, ritualistic mindset. A slight sameness of tone across the whole piece keeps it from feeling fully modulated under peak pressure.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The prose is polished and mostly precise, with many lines doing double duty for atmosphere and theme (e.g., “turning invisible vibrations into a soft, trembling glow”). Still, line-level clarity is very high, and the closure image is sharp and legible rather than muddled. Trimming a few redundant metaphors would tighten momentum and lift it into a higher band.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The central conceit—measuring silence via the hum of frozen books in a cloud-top library, to awaken a lantern-slide of binding light—is striking and feels genuinely specific. The story commits to its aesthetic and conceptual logic without flinching, and the ending constellation-map of vows is memorable. Its main limitation is that the emotional calculus remains relatively straightforward; the piece takes formal and imagistic risks more than psychological ones.</comment>
