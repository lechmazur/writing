The coup remains off-page, so the decisive external outcome is deferred, but the narrative still lands on a clear before/after in knowledge and action (city ignorant vs chart in circulation). The environment doesn’t strongly constrain tactical choices, but it does meaningfully charge the final image of dawn on the copper dome and the stitched sky as proof. The “echoed silence” motif roots tone in the space and evolves with the emerging conspiracy.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—uncover and transmit the truth about the coup before it happens—is present and escalates conceptually as she discovers bribery, falsified votes, etc. However, conflict remains largely frictionless: the candle readily “obliges,” there’s no resistance from conspirators, and no on-page threat to the courier or to her work. The main tightening is informational rather than pressure-based; options don’t visibly narrow, and she never faces a real trade-off or risk. As a result, tension feels more contemplative than urgent despite the coup clock.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The themes of truth vs power, unheard stories, and wisdom disguised as foolishness are woven consistently through the image system (loom, flame, adjacent possibilities, silence). Some thematic lines veer toward explicit statement (“She smiled, knowing that unraveling conspiracies required not only sharp eyes but a heart that listened to the unheard”), slightly undercutting the piece’s otherwise strong subtext. Still, the stitched sky as proof of sewn truth is a potent non-literal answer to the story’s central question.</comment>

Overall, the perceptual signature—seeing thought and conspiracy as threads and patterns—is distinctive and stable.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The prose is polished, rhythmically varied, and information-dense; most sentences do double duty for mood and worldbuilding. There are no major loops or scaffolding repetitions; progression is generally clean. The final paragraphs land a clear, vivid image without figurative muddle, even if some lines could be sharpened by pruning adjectives.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The central conceit—an embroiderer using a flame that contains untold stories to map a coup through stellar embroidery in a cliffside observatory—is striking and memorable. While the conspiracy itself is somewhat familiar, the method of investigation and the attention to “unheard stories” and workers’ whispers as code give it a non-obvious angle. The piece takes stylistic risks with its lyric mode and conceptual toolset that mostly pay off without sacrificing clarity.</comment>
