<question>6</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>The third-person lyric voice is consistent, controlled, and suited to the contemplative subject, with some memorable turns (“each droplet a story of effort unseen”). However, reliance on similar constructions and repeated key phrases (“hidden patterns,” “demure audacity,” “moon paths,” “whispering parapet”) flattens rhythmic variety and can feel mannered. There is little modulation of distance or texture as the piece moves toward closure, so emotional intensity and sentence rhythm remain at one lush register.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The prose is often beautiful and image-dense, with strong sensory specificity, but it edges into purple and repetitive in places (“scintillatingly somber,” reiterating the same motifs without adding new nuance). Figurative language is mostly coherent, yet the accumulation of similes and personifications can feel over-layered for a 600–800 word piece. Overall readability and clarity remain solid, but a demanding editor would want pruning for concision and sharper turns.</comment>
