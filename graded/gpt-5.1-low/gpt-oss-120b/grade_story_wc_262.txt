<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The piece operates cleanly on an accumulative/lyric lens: each move (psychometry → hidden compartment → letters-as-map → river of light) intensifies the central quest. Closure lands with a resonant reconfiguration—she becomes the carrier of unused love—at a felt conceptual cost. The only limit is that obstacles are minimal, so the progression feels inevitable rather than pressured.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The projection booth and old movie palace are richly evoked and consistently threaded through the action (humming booth, sputtering projector, hidden compartment, “audience of shadows”). The setting also charges the final image, with the ancient projector screening freed love and the shadows applauding. What keeps this just shy of the very top is that the environment frames and amplifies more than it constrains concrete choice.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—locating and transforming unused love via unsent goodbyes—is legible and tied to her actions throughout. There is a single main tightening when the hidden compartment and letters appear, reweighting the quest from search to transformation. However, resistance and risk feel low: each new step yields without real friction, and she never has to trade one meaningful option against another. The cost is more conceptual/emotional than dramatically pressured on the page.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Themes of unrealized affection, unsent communication, and the possibility of transforming stored sorrow into light are woven deeply into objects and actions (film frames, letters, map-ink, river of memory). The ending’s conceptual shift—that the answer lies not in a single goodbye but in gathering and incubating them—reweights the entire piece without spelling out a moral. Recurring images (thunder/hush, light, constellations) support a lingering resonance about how love and farewell coexist. At times the symbolism trends dense and explicit, but it avoids outright sermonizing.</comment>

Patterned diction (“thunderous hush,” “whispered thunder”) feels deliberate rather than accidental. The lushness occasionally flirts with over-similarity of register, but control is maintained.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Line by line, the writing is vivid and mostly efficient, with many sentences doing double duty for atmosphere and concept. There is some accumulation of abstractions and elaborate images that could be pared without loss, which slightly flattens momentum. Still, clarity is preserved and the final images land cleanly.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The fusion of occult psychometry, a vintage projection booth, and the metaphysics of unused love feels genuinely fresh. Its one limit is that the emotional arc stays relatively smooth; a sharper, riskier contradiction in her relationship to this “unused love” could push it into truly exceptional territory.</comment>
