The final image of marble walls glowing with starlight and photons “dancing like a memory” over the crystal timer nicely charges the closure. <question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of wonder versus reason, preservation versus imprisonment, and attention to the “periphery of existence” are coherent and sustained through recurring images of echoes, reflections, and jars. Overall the thematic work is strong but somewhat tidy and declared rather than allowed to remain provocatively ambiguous.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The core conceit—a locksmith reverse-engineering a broken timer to harvest echoes via crystal and reflected light in a marble cavern—is strikingly original and conceptually coherent. Where it falls short of the very highest band is in emotional and dramatic complexity: the human dimension stays metaphorical and serene, without risking a sharper, more idiosyncratic conflict or cost. Still, as a lyric fable of perception and preservation, it offers a distinct and memorable synthesis.</comment>
