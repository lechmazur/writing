While the setting doesn’t tightly constrain concrete choices, it undeniably charges the narrative’s stakes and closure.</comment>

There’s no tested alternative (conceal vs reveal, resist vs comply), so tension remains mostly conceptual and atmospheric. As a result, escalation feels more like deepening meditation than conflict-driven narrowing of options.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of memory, emotional history, language of the dead, and archival responsibility are woven through recurring images (wax cylinders, children’s hands in soil, fruit, foghorn silence). More implication and slightly less explanation would elevate the resonance.</comment>

Though dense, the narration maintains clarity and an interior focus without slipping into omniscient explanation. The lack of dialogue suits the contemplative mode and avoids register clashes.</comment>

While not radically experimental in structure, the conceptual world-building and imagery show high originality.</comment>
