<question>1</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Maya’s motive (find the sister/name) is clear and consistent, and her emotional texture—uncertainty about her own memories, the pull between longing and fear of what she’ll uncover—feels grounded. <question>2</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The structure is clean: arrival on the ship → exploration seeded with memory clues → encounter with the sister → choice to reclaim versus accept erasure → partial restoration and departure. The conceptual cost of leaving the Garden’s “solace” is present but under-dramatized; the decision lands more as poetic logic than as a wrenching trade. Still, for this length the arc is tight, with no major orientation or motivation gaps.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The abandoned carnival ship in the foggy bay is vividly realized—the rusted Ferris wheel, funhouse mirrors, skeletal tent, and carousel all work together tonally. The setting is more atmospheric than constraining; it shapes mood and theme more than concrete tactical options. Even so, the ship and carnival logic feel integral rather than postcard-decorative.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—lose herself to the Garden’s erasure vs. reclaim a painful, unstable shared identity—is clear and escalates once the sister appears and reframes the Garden as a state of being. The pressure tightens when we learn the sister has been “trying to forget,” making Maya’s plea a genuine contest of values rather than a foregone conclusion. The climactic tactic (using invented/shared narrative to pull the sister back) is strong, but the price of that reversal (what they both give up) is gestured at more than felt. Stakes remain intimate and coherent, though a sharper sense of risk or potential failure in the closing exchange would heighten impact.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Themes of memory’s mutability, the ethics of remembrance vs. erasure, and narrative as a tool for reclaiming identity are well seeded (mirrors, posters, the Garden, “creating truth from the raw material of longing”). The emerald card and Garden imagery nicely embody the seduction and danger of “unkindled solace.” However, some lines drift into overt thesis (“As memories alter, they become unreliable narrators of our own lives”; “The Garden of Forgotten Names wasn’t paradise. It was erasure dressed in green.”), and the final declarative sentence (“These two had chosen to be remembered.”) tips into explicit moralizing. The piece would gain power by trusting its images and actions more and stating its insights less directly.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is lyrically inclined yet generally clear, with a consistent sensibility that marries decay, surrealism, and reflective thought. Dialogue between Maya and her sister is natural and subtextual enough to carry their differing stances on memory without sounding expository. There is a mild slide into essayistic omniscience in a couple of lines, but not enough to break POV control.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Line-level craft is strong: sentences vary in rhythm, images are often striking (“eyes watching her with blissfully aberrant joy, as if decay had freed them from the burden of motion”), and most lines do at least two jobs (worldbuild + theme, or mood + character). Figurative language is frequent but mostly controlled, with only occasional near-purple moments that don’t significantly cloud clarity. There are no major loops, scaffolding sentences, or mechanical artifacts, and the final images land cleanly. Slight pruning of abstract phrasing could sharpen the already-effective prose.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The fusion of an abandoned carnival ship, a Garden of Forgotten Names, and the idea of literally narrating a lost twin back into solidity is a fresh, memorable synthesis. The conceptual move from passive recollection to active story-weaving as salvific method is particularly strong. It stops short of formally radical or structurally risky, but demonstrates high-level imaginative coherence.</comment>
