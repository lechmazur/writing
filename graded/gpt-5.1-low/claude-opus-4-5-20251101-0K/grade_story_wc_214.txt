Interiority is specific (e.g., tracing meteors, feeling their journeys in her pulse) rather than generic. <question>2</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The accumulative structure is clean: nightly routine → lantern interlude → deepened realization → dawn, with no orientation or causality slips. Closure lands as an Earned Stasis/Conceptual Repricing where Mira’s refusal to leave and her reframing of “falling” as “flying” mark a clear before/after in how her solitude is valued. Cost is legible in the acceptance of permanent smallness and isolation, even if external stakes remain quiet.</comment>

The yellow lantern glass and the dome opening ground the cosmic imagery in tactile objects and spatial relations. While the setting strongly charges mood and theme, it rarely constrains concrete choices, which keeps it just shy of the top band.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The central stake is Mira’s stance toward solitude and insignificance, and that conflict is clear but largely internal and meditative. There is a modest escalation as meteors are reinterpreted—from death to arrival—and as she recognizes her inability to leave as a chosen rebellion rather than mere habit. However, there is little sense of narrowing options or a pressured decision point; the piece is more contemplative than dynamically contested.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of surrender, vulnerability as strength, and stillness versus speed are rich and coherent, well embodied in the recurring images of falling stars and stardust. However, they are stated too explicitly in places (“Not the mathematics of falling stars, but their meaning,” “Some things endured not by fighting but by yielding”), tipping into moralizing rather than trusting subtext. The final paragraphs in particular summarize the thesis instead of letting the images carry it.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is lyrical yet controlled, with a consistent contemplative cadence that suits Mira’s sensibility. Imagery like “the cosmos’s long exhale” and “the sacred art of flourishing stillness” gives the piece a distinct texture without losing clarity. There is no POV leakage or stance wobble, and the narration’s elevated register feels plausibly like the matured, long-isolated mind we’re inhabiting.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Sentence rhythm and paragraphing are strong, with clear, uncluttered syntax and few redundancies; most lines pull double duty for mood and character. The final image of the yellow glass as “a small captured sun” is vivid and well-earned.</comment>
