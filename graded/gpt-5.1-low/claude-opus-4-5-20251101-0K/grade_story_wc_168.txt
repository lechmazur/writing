<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether he can let go enough to scatter Celia’s ashes and what that means for his relationship to loss—is clear from early on. The cost of scattering is palpable, as is the quieter but significant renunciation of his fantasy that suffering needs translation. <question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Theme around the universality of pain, the futility of bargaining with the universe, and the idea of broken-but-held love emerges organically from the box, the scars, and the celestial imagery. However, several lines state the theme quite directly (“Pain wasn’t a language to be translated…”; “the universe didn’t make deals”), edging toward essayistic moralizing rather than trusting image and action. The complexity remains, but a slightly lighter touch on explicit thesis statements would strengthen the subtext.</comment>
