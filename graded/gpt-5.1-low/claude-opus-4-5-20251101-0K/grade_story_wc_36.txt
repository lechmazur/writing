Objects-as-setting (flute, driftwood markers) are integrated into both world rules and tactics. The environment quietly charges the final image: a silent range that now reads as “potential” rather than emptiness.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Mira will risk taboo to gain knowledge to prevent another catastrophe—is established early and escalates credibly through her decision to uncase and then play the flute. The major tightening is the moment she steps “in both places at once,” forcing her to confront the precise nature of their past mistake and, implicitly, her own survivor’s guilt. Cost is mostly conceptual and ethical (breaking an oath, re-opening trauma) rather than physically immediate, so the pressure never feels fully acute. Still, the final renunciation—re-casing the flute but carrying the discord insight—reads as an earned, consequential stance.</comment>

Some lines edge toward thesis-statement (“To harvest insights from failure…”, “Not emptiness. / Potential.”), slightly flattening subtext. Nonetheless, the ending reweights the silence and her role in a way that invites reflection beyond the scene.</comment>

The emotional arc—turning survivor’s guilt into a new, slightly heretical doctrine—is credible and not over-obvious.
