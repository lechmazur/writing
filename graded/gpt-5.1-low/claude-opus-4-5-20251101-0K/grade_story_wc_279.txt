<question>2</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The structure is clean: one continuous scene that tightens from arrival in the grotto to the mandala’s activation and the conceptual re-framing of the hush. Closure lands as a clear conceptual repricing: silence redefined as invitation, with a modest behavioral shift (she begins drawing on the walls) to embody it. Stakes remain somewhat abstract and philosophical, which limits the felt cost despite the elegant arc.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The grotto and its mist are vividly realized and integrated—acoustics, flaking tiles, damp stone, and the very existence of whispers there all meaningfully shape what’s possible. The tile as recurring object and the mandala as micro-setting both charge the final image of “watching the mandala breathe.” World details about the unlearning are economical yet evocative, giving the grotto’s persistence real weight. Setting is not just backdrop; it is the mechanism by which the hush is interrogated.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The central conflict—how to relate to the emergent hush—is clear, with the old man embodying surrender and Mira embodying an exploratory counter-move. Pressure escalates texturally as the mandala coalesces, the tile hums, and the whispers sharpen into near-coherence. However, the stakes never quite bite; there’s no on-page consequence for choosing wrong beyond an implied spiritual or existential misstep. The resolution feels more like graceful alignment than a choice extracted under meaningful duress.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of meaning, silence, and unlearning are well integrated into action and image, especially through the mandala and the reorientation of the whispers. That said, several lines verge on direct thesis statement (“Meaning was never in the noise,” “Silence as invitation”), edging into mild didacticism. The piece would be stronger if a bit more were left in subtext rather than explained.</comment>

Occasional figurative density brushes up against purple but generally remains purposeful and in-character.</comment>

Overall, the prose is polished and publishable, with only minor drift toward overt statement.</comment>

This feels like a strong, original take that could stand in a top-tier venue with slight sharpening of cost and ambiguity.</comment>
