Environmental features matter to action (path behind the waterfall, digging in disturbed soil, the ventilated glasshouse) and strongly charge the final image—“The waterfall continued its eternal fall… keeping time with nothing” ties atmosphere directly to theme. At times the description verges on lushness that slightly outruns immediate pressure, but it remains controlled and integrated into movement rather than stalling it.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake is Mira’s internal stance toward certainty versus uncertainty in planning, and it is clearly signaled and followed through. The story does escalate in a situational/accumulative way—from odd coincidences to a charged encounter with the compass and logbook and finally to her decision to build a probabilistic clock and stop counting time on the climb out. However, external conflict is virtually absent and internal pressure is relatively smooth; she faces little genuine risk or temptation to revert, so the feeling of cost and narrowing options is muted. The result is intellectually satisfying but emotionally low-tension.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The thematic spine—honesty of uncertainty versus the lie of precision—is tightly woven through clocks, the spinning compass, the storm, the greenhouse’s perpetual leaves, and the waterfall’s indifference to time. That said, the theme is sometimes stated too directly (“to befriend uncertainty in planning,” “A clock that displayed probability rather than precision”), edging toward essayistic moralizing rather than fully trusting imagery and action, especially in the final third.</comment>

There is no dialogue to test, but the narrative stance remains stable and plausibly limited to what Mira can infer, with no head-hopping or register breaks.</comment>
