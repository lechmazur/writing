Still, the key conflicts (corruption, exile) sit mostly off-stage in backstory, so the ending slightly reiterates an already-established state rather than marking a sharp on-page before/after.</comment>

There is a slight tendency to state philosophy outright (“She had learned long ago…”) instead of trusting subtext, which keeps this just short of top-tier subtlety.</comment>
