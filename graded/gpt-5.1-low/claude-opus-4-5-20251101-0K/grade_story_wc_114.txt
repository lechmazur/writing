<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The central stake—whether Cassandra will report the certain destruction of her employers or withhold it—is legible early and coherently tightened as she confronts the “weight” of that swallowed truth. <question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Themes of truth’s weight, engineered fallibility, and the ethics of silence versus prophecy emerge organically from Cassandra’s history, the weather systems, and the smudged illustration rather than from abstract lecturing. The final assertion that “some silences spoke louder than prophecy” edges toward explicit thesis, but the preceding scene work largely earns it and prevents it from feeling like a tacked-on moral. Dialogue between Cassandra and Mira is spare, natural, and layered with subtext, advancing both theme and conflict without over-explaining. Psychic distance remains stable, and the few more elevated abstractions feel earned by the character rather than imposed by an external narrator.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Line-level craft is strong: sentences vary in length and rhythm, and most images do at least two jobs (worldbuilding plus emotion or theme) without padding. Minor risk of over-lyricism is kept in check by concrete anchors like instruments, tea, and the smudged illustration, so clarity and momentum hold through closure.</comment>
