The only thing keeping this just shy of exemplary is that the environment constrains mood and symbolism more than concrete tactical choices.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the “lull” is death or a recoverable pause—emerges early and tightens around Maya’s need for meaning. The internal conflict is contemplative rather than sharp; there’s no explicit on-page moment where continuing to wait or giving up presents itself as a competing path she nearly chooses. As a result, the escalation feels steady and beautiful but not particularly fraught or costly.</comment>
