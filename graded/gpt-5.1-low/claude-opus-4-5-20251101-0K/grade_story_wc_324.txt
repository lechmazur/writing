The hearing aid functions as a micro-setting/tool with its own rules and sensory texture. <question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Margaret’s need to reconcile with Ruth and ease decades-old guilt—is clear and emotionally credible. Still, opposition is extremely soft: Vera encounters no resistance, danger, or serious doubt, and the outcome feels foregone once she hears Ruth’s pulse. The piece reads more as a beautifully rendered ritual than a conflict that narrows options with visible cost.</comment>
