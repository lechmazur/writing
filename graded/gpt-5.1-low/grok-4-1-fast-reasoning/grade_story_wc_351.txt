The cost of this choice (loss of prior self/normalcy) is more conceptually than viscerally rendered, limiting impact.</comment>

The final image of a helix-monument built from canyon silt ties setting directly into closure. It’s lush and purposeful, though adding one or two tactile, constraining details under pressure could push it higher.</comment>

Her success feels inevitable rather than contested, and no real option is foreclosed on the page except in conceptual terms. This keeps the experience texturally rich but dramatically soft.</comment>

Repetition of key terms feels mostly like purposeful motif rather than scaffolding.</comment>
