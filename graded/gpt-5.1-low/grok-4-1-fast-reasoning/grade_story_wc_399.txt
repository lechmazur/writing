While the environment doesn’t strongly constrain moral choice, it does constrain technique and charges the final image of the humming barrier.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.5</grade><comment>Stakes are clearly stated (wisdom will fade if not preserved), but conflict is almost entirely procedural and uncontested. Pressure therefore plateaus: there is a sense of importance but little escalation or genuine risk of loss. The final success is earned in process terms, not through a hard trade-off or visible sacrifice.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>Themes of preservation, periphery versus center, and truth’s fragility accumulate through images (edges, clearings, sidelong gazes) in an integrated way. There is some resonance in the idea that wisdom can only be approached indirectly, but the story tends toward gentle affirmation rather than complex tension. The ending lingers conceptually, yet avoids any uncomfortable ambiguity or counter-pressure.</comment>

Several adjacent sentences restate anxiety about preservation and calm in slightly different metaphors, creating minor momentum drag. Line-level craft is solid but would benefit from pruning and greater variation in image and cadence.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The conceptual blend of “conjured clearings,” peripheral consecration, and a kinetic parapet under dunes is fresh and memorable, not a standard fantasy template. The originality lies more in world-building and language than in character conflict or structural risk.</comment>
