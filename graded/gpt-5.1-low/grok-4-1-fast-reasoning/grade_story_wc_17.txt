Environment modestly constrains action (the ivy, the dark interior, the alcove, the hidden stone) and charges the final image of sketching under the altered sky. The only thing keeping this below the very top band is that environmental constraint remains soft—he never must choose tactically because of the world, only thematically in response to it.</comment>

As a result, the final vow feels earnest but not sharply tested on the page, which tempers the felt cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Legacy, complicity, and the ethics of listening (to walls, to history) are integrated through recurring images rather than bolt-on commentary. The piece invites reflection on how we map and exploit the earth, though it stops just shy of more ambivalent or conflicted moral terrain that might deepen its resonance.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The close third-person voice has a strong, consistent texture—lyric but not opaque—with a clear perceptual signature focused on sound and subtle vibrations. Psychic distance stays stable; we’re in his head without head-hopping or register whiplash. Occasional repeated phrasing (“legacy’s burden,” “ivy-veiled passage”) brushes against over-insistence but doesn’t seriously harm control.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The prose is generally tight, vivid, and information-dense, with many sentences doing double duty for mood and plot. Metaphors are mostly coherent and on-theme, and there’s little scaffolding or obvious looped phrasing. At times the language verges on purple or stacked (“emerald tendrils that pulsed like veins in the humid air,” “walls wept seismic sorrow”), and the repetition of key motifs can feel hammered rather than elegantly threaded. Rhythm and clarity hold throughout, but a light paring of intensifiers and duplicate emphasis would lift it further.</comment>

The idea of developing images “in echo” and of walls holding seismic memory is particularly memorable. Some beats (ruined estate, family secret) are familiar, but they’re significantly transformed by the conceptual frame.</comment>
