<question>2</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The causal spine is clean: routine → prophetic vision in the wick → decision to alter the beam → summoning of shadows → reconfigured dawn. Because opposition never materializes, the climax feels more like an unopposed activation than a pressured decision.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The Emerald Lighthouse, glass lantern, sea, and stars are sensuously drawn and integral to the premise; this could not be set in a generic room. The wick and beam are used as functional tools in the magic, not just décor, and the final image of the lighthouse as “eternal sentinel” under indifferent stars is thematically charged. Sensory detail is rich, though heavily visual and sometimes overstuffed. The environment shapes what is possible but doesn’t strongly constrain Elias—he moves through it with ease.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Elias choosing to unleash ancient shadows to reshape humanity—is huge but mostly conceptual, with no concrete counterforce. Escalation is primarily a matter of scale (from storm to world-reframing darkness) rather than tightening pressure or narrowing options. Once he sees the prophetic images, nothing resists his plan; his “fervent resolve” leads straight to success. As a result, the story reads more like an origin myth of a change than a conflict under strain.</comment>

The ending’s “dawn laced with eternal night” offers a resonant image but leaves the ethical stance underexplored. Complexity is present but not fully deepened by concrete consequences.</comment>

Some sentences restate content with mild synonym churn rather than adding new pressure. Despite this, causality and the final image remain legible, so the line-level excesses are more cosmetic than structurally damaging.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The fusion of a magical lighthouse weather-herald with a post-print, screen-saturated world and primordial shadows is striking and memorable. Elements like “Laughing Storm Herald” and news-beams across realms show genuine imaginative flair. The broader mythology (forgotten gods, ancient shadows) is more familiar territory and could be individualized further to reach truly exceptional originality.</comment>
