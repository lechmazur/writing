World rules about light-hoarding and blackout tests are consistent enough to support the premise without distracting exposition.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elara can use the blackout to seed a silent revolution against the Overwardens—is signaled early and followed through. Yet the conflict is curiously frictionless: no antagonistic presence appears on the page, guards are merely puzzled, and no action comes close to failing. The main “tightening” is the move from decoding the sundial to activating the vault conduits, but this doesn’t foreclose options or force a trade-off. As a result, tension is more implied than felt.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.3</grade><comment>Themes of hoarded power versus collective illumination and the idea of revolutions as quiet, infrastructural work (angles, codes, small objects) are integrated and coherent. However, the piece leans toward explicit labeling (“silent revolution,” “inventive calm fueling the hush”) rather than letting subtext fully carry the weight, which flattens complexity. Moral or emotional ambiguity—what this rebellion might cost or corrupt—is lightly touched, not probed.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is stable and mostly well-controlled, with a recognizable lyrical texture and consistent distance. At times the diction and metaphor density verge on purple and repetitive, softening impact and making sections feel more like motif recitation than lived perception. Still, POV boundaries are clean, and the narrative stance holds together to the end.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>6.0</grade><comment>The prose is generally grammatical, clear at the level of action, and often striking in individual images. Several adjacent sentences paraphrase similar ideas about hush, glow, and intrigue without adding new pressure or information. This ornamental accumulation slows momentum and slightly dulls the closure image, which leans on already-familiar metaphors.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The combination of a lock sentinel, phosphorescent fort, blackout-as-cover, and a revolutions-as-shadow-network encoded in sundials and quill holders feels fresh and conceptually appealing. The narrative arc itself—competent insider quietly seeding an uprising—follows familiar lines and lacks an unexpected moral or emotional turn that would make the whole feel inevitable and singular. Still, the worldbuilding and tactical ingenuity lift it above template territory.</comment>
