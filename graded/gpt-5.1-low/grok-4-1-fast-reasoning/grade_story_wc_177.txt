<question>5</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Themes of fate vs free will, entropy, and the symbiosis of paths and walkers emerge clearly from the events and recurring images of roots, vines, and loops. However, the piece frequently states its thesis outright (“Because walkers rot without choice’s illusion”), reducing subtext and leaving little room for reader inference. The ending reconfigures the premise nicely (paths now follow her), but the wisdom is somewhat over-explained rather than fully earned through dramatized conflict.</comment>

Rhythm and grammar remain clean, but a bit more restraint and variation would sharpen momentum and heighten key beats.</comment>
