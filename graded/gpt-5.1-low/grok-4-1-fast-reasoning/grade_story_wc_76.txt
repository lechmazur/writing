<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—defying a hereditary curse to rewrite the island’s fate at personal cost—is signaled early and tightened as Elias commits to the stone and yoyo. The price is unmistakable on the page (his body wasting, collapse amid gears), and his agency drives each turn. The main limitation is that opposition is largely systemic and internal; there’s little adaptive resistance beyond “the stone hungers,” so tension could be even sharper with more concrete on-page tests or micro-reversals.</comment>

The repeated language of “bleak exhilaration” and “latent radiance” risks slight over-signaling but stops short of moralizing. The piece invites reflection on whether liberation justified the cost without stating a thesis outright.</comment>

Tightening redundant modifiers and varying phrasing would elevate momentum and distinction.</comment>
