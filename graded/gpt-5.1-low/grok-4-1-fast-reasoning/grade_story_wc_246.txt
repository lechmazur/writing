However, the costs of revision are gestured at (“unforeseen alliances,” balance demanded) more than dramatized, so the ethical and emotional price stays somewhat abstract. The pen, gardens, and anomaly together function as a micro-setting that strongly charges the final image of a lingering, tamed disturbance. A slight overabundance of ornamental cosmic metaphors keeps it shy of the very top band, but the atmosphere is distinctive and purposeful.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—repairing the anomaly and reconfiguring her mistake—is clear, and there is at least one meaningful tightening as she moves from local fixes (seed isotopes) to deeper causal edits (Vega signal, mentor). Because each revision seems to improve conditions with minimal on-page pushback, pressure never quite peaks, and the ethical conflict remains more conceptual than felt. The final stance—continuing her work, newly at peace—lands, but with relatively modest tension preceding it.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of fallible agency, ethical revision, and finding meaning through “threshing” mistakes are well integrated with the pen and anomaly, and the closing image of “unbending” herself from regret is a strong thematic callback. However, some thematic language (“contingent relationships,” “lexicon of edits”) edges toward abstract thesis-speak instead of staying grounded in concrete conflict. The resonance is genuine but would deepen if at least one negative consequence of revision were specifically dramatized.</comment>

It stops short of exceptional primarily because some phrases repeat and the lyricism occasionally smooths over sharper emotional beats.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The line-level work is polished, with many sentences doing double duty for image and concept, and the piece reads smoothly aloud. Still, clarity is rarely compromised, and there are few, if any, true scaffolding or loop artifacts. Trimming a handful of metaphors and tightening repeated motifs would likely elevate it into a higher band.</comment>
