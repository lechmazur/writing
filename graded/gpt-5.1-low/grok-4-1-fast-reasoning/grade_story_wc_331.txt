<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether and how Eliza can reawaken analog memories and reach the story-realm without losing herself—is present and coherently built. However, that pressure is resolved fairly gently via a patron’s cough and her quiet refolding, so the cost and narrowing of options feel modest rather than acute. The conflict works for a lyric piece but stops short of a truly bracing test of her values.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Themes of analog memory, the materiality of stories, and the interface of chaos/order via hyperbolic geometry arise organically from Eliza’s actions and the library’s design. At times the language edges toward stating its thesis (“analog memories demanded physical resurrection, not simulation”) rather than letting implication and image fully carry it. The ending image—solitude, scarf around her neck, the library’s “curved potential”—does reweight earlier motifs in a satisfying, if a bit declared, way.</comment>

Rhythm leans toward long, similarly cadenced sentences, which creates a hypnotic effect but occasionally risks monotony and softens moments that might benefit from sharper, shorter beats. Still, the perceptual signature—scientific-poetic, mildly obsessive—is distinct and coherent throughout.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The prose is often striking, with many lines doing double duty for atmosphere and concept, and very few outright clumsy constructions. There is also some conceptual repetition around solitude, analog memories, and hyperbolic geometry that could be trimmed without loss. Overall it’s above-average line work that would benefit from selective cutting and a bit more variation in sentence structure and texture.</comment>
