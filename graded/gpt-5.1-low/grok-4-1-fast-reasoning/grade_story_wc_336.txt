That said, the setting rarely constrains choice or imposes risk; it mainly provides lyrical texture and raw material. The final image of Lirien on the “verdant perch” nodding valleyward reprises the setting effectively but without a new charge of danger or cost.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The stakes—loss of childhood awe and a graying world—are enormous, but the on-page conflict remains abstract and largely unopposed. Once Elowen appears, Lirien decides to help and the ritual proceeds flawlessly; his “pruning” of a frayed thread neatly stabilizes everything without resistance or sacrifice. Elowen’s doubt is invoked then instantly dispelled by the seed’s success, so no option is meaningfully foreclosed. The result is a pleasant, gently escalating sequence rather than true pressure.</comment>

However, the piece leans toward tidy moral clarity (guardian bestows cure; village is healed) with limited ambiguity or counter-pressure. At times, the diction edges toward homogenous solemnity, which flattens emotional modulation across beats.</comment>
