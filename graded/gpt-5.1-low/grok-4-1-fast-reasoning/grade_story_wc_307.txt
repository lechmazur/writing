<question>2</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>The structure is straightforward (arrival → trance → visionary lesson → post-trance understanding), with clean orientation and a coherent build under an accumulative/textural lens. However, escalation is mostly conceptual; there’s little sense of options narrowing or stakes concretely testing her—she does not yet choose the grotto or refuse it under visible cost. The world’s magical rules (floating rock, magnetic veins, trance visions) feel consistent within their own logic. At times the density of image flirts with decorative excess, but it never collapses basic clarity.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether and how Elara can achieve reversal—is signaled, but on-page pressure remains mostly contemplative rather than truly conflictual. The main tightening is her visionary exposure to the grotto and the encoded lesson in the ripples, which redefines what “reversal” means. Yet she isn’t forced into a difficult trade-off in scene; choosing patience over haste is framed as obviously correct, without real temptation or competing harm. The piece reads more like a prologue to the real ordeal than a self-contained test of resolve.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The theme of “to appear, first vanish” is clear and organically tied to the mechanics of the ritual and the grotto’s pull, not merely asserted. However, the story edges toward explicit statement (“You teach vanishing to appear”) and slightly undercuts subtextual power with on-the-nose articulation. Still, the reweighting of youth from cosmetic gain to existential surrender is effective and lingers.</comment>
