The recurring grotto/sea glass imagery meaningfully charges the final image of communal visits to the grotto’s edge. As a result, the escalation feels more like a smooth unveiling than a pressured test of her resolve.</comment>

The piece edges toward statement in its last lines but retains enough image-based resonance to avoid outright sermonizing.</comment>

Certain formulations and key terms recur without fresh development (“memories resurfaced,” “bloom,” “zephyr,” etc.), creating a sense of lexical loop and diminishing impact. Line-level craft is competent and occasionally striking but could benefit from tightening and more varied figurative strategies.</comment>

Nonetheless, the emotional and narrative arc—trauma transmuted into healing, solitary guardian becoming communal beacon—follows familiar patterns without a particularly non-obvious turn. The story feels gracefully executed but not quite surprising in either its choices or its final revaluation.</comment>
