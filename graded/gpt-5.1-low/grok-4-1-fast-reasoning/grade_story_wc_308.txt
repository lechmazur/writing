Closure is strong: the wandering pulse joins him and his body becomes an anchor while his mind roams, a decisive before/after with visible cost. The only limit is that opposition remains abstract—no new structural complication beyond endurance itself.</comment>

The closing image of his still body among bone towers with dust sifting down feels fully charged by earlier setting work. <question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—unlock the arrowhead’s nature and attain motionless navigation—is legible early and sharpened by his obsessive, centuries-long vigil. The climax, where the flock finally mirrors the spirals and Thren chooses to press the artifact to his forehead, is earned and clearly consequential. What keeps it mid–high rather than elite is that the conflict never quite tightens into a dilemma or sharp reversal; endurance is tested, but no serious alternative path or ethical tension challenges his course.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of reverence for history, endurance as devotion, and the fusion of self with accumulated memory emerge organically from the specifics of Thren’s ritual and the canyon’s imagery. The final reconfiguration—Thren effectively becoming another echoing relic while his mind roams—reweights the story’s earlier images without any moralizing statement. It doesn’t quite reach the most complex or surprising thematic territory, but it’s coherent, resonant, and emotionally honest.</comment>
