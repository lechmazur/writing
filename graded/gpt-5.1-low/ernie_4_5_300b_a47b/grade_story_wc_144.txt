<question>2</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: damaged drone → diagnosis via water → repair and recalibration → confirmation as the ship launches. <question>3</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The misty rail depot, anchor lift, and water basin are vivid, tactile, and well-integrated into Lira’s way of perceiving, with strong specificity (braille tool belt, prismatic hues on the walls). The basin in particular functions as a charged object-as-setting, constraining her diagnostic method and returning in the final image. Weather and industrial machinery shape tone more than tactics, but they are consistently threaded through action rather than as a static backdrop. This is high-level atmospheric work, just shy of the very top because the environment doesn’t significantly alter her options beyond the basin’s role.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake is clear—get HB-7 functional before/for the *Horizon’s Breath*’s ascent—but the pressure on that goal never tightens beyond a single, smoothly executed technical fix. Internal stakes (trusting the basin vs. logic, fear of failure) are implied but not dramatized in choice points. <question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Theme coheres around alternative forms of “seeing,” listening as perception, and the idea that calm, attentive craft can turn chaos into clarity. Some thematic points are carried in slightly declarative lines (“Listening’s a kind of seeing,” “truths no machine could hide”) that edge toward thesis statements. Overall, the piece invites reflection, but the moral complexity and cost are relatively low.</comment>
