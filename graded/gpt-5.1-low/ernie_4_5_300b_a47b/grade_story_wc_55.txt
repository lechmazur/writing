Sensory detail is selective and purposeful, especially smell and sound/its absence. The only ceiling comes from setting rarely constraining concrete tactics beyond mood and epiphany.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Lira will stay trapped in a self-eroding silence or renegotiate her relationship with the keep—is established and followed through. However, the encounter resolves with almost no real danger or cost; the bear is benign very quickly, which softens tension. The internal conflict progresses, but the lack of sharper consequence at the peak keeps escalation from feeling fully earned at a high level.</comment>

Overall, though, the piece leaves a coherent, reflective aftertaste rather than a neat moral.</comment>

There’s no head-hopping or stance wobble, and the narrative diction feels unified across the piece. At times the register feels slightly more ornate than Lira herself might think, but it still reads as a coherent literary voice rather than a mismatch.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The combination of a clock scribe, a singing crystal forecourt, and a rain-damp bear as emissary of an abandoned dream feels fresh and specific.
