Closure lands as a resonant reconfiguration: grief is re-priced as a bridge/door, and his future work is reoriented. The butterfly migration and lantern light evolving over evenings subtly track emotional progression. Setting strongly charges the final image (leaving the greenhouse with ribbon and internalized light) but stops just shy of actively constraining a hard tactical choice.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Marlow will remain sealed in inherited, unspoken grief or step into voiced, shared grief—is clear but plays out gently. Pressure increases somewhat through the ongoing mentorship and the approaching culmination of the migration, yet there’s little real contestation or reversal; once the voice appears, he mostly complies. The main tightening is the “final night” deadline where he chooses to read his own poem, which does crystallize his stance. Overall, conflict is more contemplative than fraught, limiting escalation.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Theme—grief as bridge rather than burden, as something shared and transmuted through words and rituals—is well integrated into setting (greenhouse as repository), objects (ribbon, gravestones), and action (reading the poem). However, the story leans into explicit statement (“Because words outlive us,” “Grief is not a hole, but a door”) that edges toward didacticism rather than relying on implication. The closing shift from loss to love resonates, but its articulation is somewhat on-the-nose.</comment>

Rhythm and diction are controlled, with varied sentence structures and a sustained meditative tone. Now and then the narration slips into a generalized grandness, but not enough to break point-of-view integrity.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The synthesis of elements—a gravestone carver, an ancestral greenhouse that remembers poems, spectral mentorship as “echo,” and a butterfly migration as temporal and emotional frame—feels fresh and memorable. Some conceptual moves (grief as door/bridge, the power of words to outlive us) are well-trodden and expressed in familiar language. Still, the particular configuration of craft, setting, and quiet haunt gives the piece a distinct identity just shy of exceptional.</comment>
