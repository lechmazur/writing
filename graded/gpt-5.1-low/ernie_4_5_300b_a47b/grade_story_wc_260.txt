While the environment doesn’t strongly constrain her options, it functionally operates as a cocoon where her reconstruction can occur, and the material details (tools, glass, velvet, adhesive) are concrete and well-used.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Lira can maintain compassion without being exploited—is clear, and pressure accumulates through her reflections and the meticulous work on the tail. The metaphorical linkage between reconstructing the tail and reconstructing her moral stance provides a meaningful deepening rather than multiple reversals. However, tension is primarily internal and contemplative; there’s little sense that she might genuinely choose hardness or nihilism, so the conflict feels profound but not sharply contested on the page.</comment>

The main weakness is explicit moralizing in lines like “compassion wasn’t a liability.
