While the environment doesn’t strongly constrain physical options, it does successfully constrain psychological experience and tone.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elara can “unbind” the board and, by extension, unbind her own relationship to crisis and history—is clear and progresses. The internal and external conflicts align, but the cost is relatively gentle; there’s little sacrifice beyond effort and a conceptual shift, and no option feels meaningfully foreclosed on the page. The piece is tense in a meditative, symbolic way, not a high-friction one.</comment>

However, several lines state the thesis quite directly (“history wasn’t written by kings or queens, but by the pawns who dared to move”), edging into mild sermonizing and reducing subtext. The resonance is real but a bit over-articulated for top-tier literary subtlety.</comment>
