The world detail around the empathy virus is sketched economically and plausibly enough to support the premise. Setting could do more to actively constrain the climax rather than sit as backdrop.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how to manage dangerous truth in a traumatized, hyper-empathic world—is clear, and the arrival of Jorn and the ledger is a real escalation from personal foraging to systemic conspiracy. Mara’s final decision (“We occlude”) is thematically meaningful but doesn’t visibly foreclose an option with an immediate, felt price in-scene. Tension is present but never quite tight.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Themes of truth as weapon/tool, empathy’s double edge, and “psychological safety” as an active choice are well integrated into objects (eye, ledger, truffles) and Mara’s vocation. The notion of occluding with truth is intriguingly paradoxical and could carry more of the thematic load if dramatized more and explained less. Overall, the piece invites reflection but doesn’t fully trust subtext.</comment>

Dialogue is functional and clear but leans expository, with limited subtext or idiolect distinction beyond tone.
