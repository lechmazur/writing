Overall, it’s richly specific and thematically tuned, if more symbolic than tactical.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The primary stake is Lira’s need to understand transformation and her own role in shaping change, which is signaled early and addressed by the stranger’s challenge. However, resistance is minimal and the external situation is never truly fraught; the piece largely avoids real loss or a sharp trade-off beyond the internal shift. As a result, escalation feels gentle and contemplative rather than gripping, effective but not maximally taut.</comment>

The ending still reweights prior material nicely—her choice to listen and to work “with purpose, not just habit” offers a resonant non-literal answer to the opening question. The piece could gain sophistication by leaving slightly more of that wisdom in subtext instead of overt exposition.</comment>

Overall, the voice feels unified and confident without drawing undue attention to itself.</comment>
