<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Lorn’s drive to uncover what clocks “really” measure—is clear and developed, and the counting ritual provides a simple but effective pressure structure. However, there’s little sense of danger, irreversibility, or conflict beyond wonder; he’s never forced into a difficult choice or trade-off, so the tension plateaus. <question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Themes of time, silence, perception, and longing are woven coherently through the key’s razor edges, the hydraulic clocks, and the fjord’s quiet, with good use of recurring images and inherited wisdom. The piece still lands with emotional and conceptual resonance, but not with the subtlety that would push it higher.</comment>
