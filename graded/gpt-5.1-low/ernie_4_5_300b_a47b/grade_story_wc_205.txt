Sentences vary in length and cadence in ways that fit emotional beats, especially around the storm and the final quiet garden scene. There is a faint whiff of omniscient summary in places (“The asteroid’s people, once passive, began to question”), but it doesn’t fracture focalization enough to damage coherence.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The prose is often striking—“sharp as regret,” “fungus of revolution,” “time’s cobalt haze”—with a high information density where images carry worldbuilding, theme, and mood simultaneously. Some metaphor stacking and adornment edges toward purple, particularly early on, but it doesn’t seriously bog momentum or muddle peaks. Overall the piece reads as polished and intentional, if not quite at the “nothing-to-cut” level of elite flash.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The synthesis of elements—a baroque asteroid where time is visible, temporal storms, mirror-covering as a tactic against mythic propaganda, fungal DNA archives, and a memory-hoarding cult—feels fresh and particular rather than template.
