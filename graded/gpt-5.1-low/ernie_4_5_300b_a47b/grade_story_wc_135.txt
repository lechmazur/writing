<question>2</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The structure—a three-site mosaic (cottage, quay, plaza) tied together by the valve and the exile—reads cleanly under an accumulative/textural lens. While the environment doesn’t deeply constrain choices, it does charge the images and supports tone and theme.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—recovering a fractured history and symbolically healing the harbor—is clear but remains largely abstract. Each shard adds information but doesn’t significantly narrow Lirael’s options or force a painful trade-off; she never risks losing status, safety, or a relationship. As a result, escalation feels gentle and contemplative rather than pressured, limiting impact.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of history as mosaic, the value of patience over force, and “navigating absences” emerge coherently from the archivist’s method and the exile’s lingering voids. However, the final paragraphs slide into explicit thesis statements (“history wasn’t a line, but a mosaic…”) that flatten complexity and feel like moralizing rather than letting images do the work. There’s limited tension or counter-pressure to complicate the comforting closure.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>8.1</grade><comment>The conceit of an “ocean-spray archivist” using staggered intervals and a single trumpet valve to draw out communal memories after an exile feels fresh and thoughtfully integrated. However, the emotional arc and tidy, heartening resolution track familiar “healing the community through restored symbol” patterns. It’s imaginative and specific, but not quite radical or risky enough in its consequences to reach the very top band.</comment>
