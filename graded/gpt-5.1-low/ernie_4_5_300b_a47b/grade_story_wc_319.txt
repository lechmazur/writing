<question>1</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>Mara’s motivation (homesickness, oath, suspicion of “light”/finality) is clear and her final choice feels earned by prior interiority, with embodied emotion in details like claws in ice and the pendant burning her thigh. <question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: waiting → cultist’s arrival → confrontation over the pendant → decision to discard it → threshold crossing to the library. Closure is a decisive change with conceptual repricing (boundaries as doors), and the scene break to the atrium reads as the direct consequence of her choice. The cost to the garden/cultist is gestured at rather than fully felt, so the aftermath lands more as elegant pivot than wrenching sacrifice.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The frozen garden and mist are vividly drawn and consistently used as more than backdrop: they cloak Mara, distort sound, and literally part to reveal the moss path. The library atrium is efficiently but evocatively rendered, and the recurring tools (lantern, fountain) help stage the key turns. A bit more concrete interaction with the harshness of the cold or physical constraints of the space could push the environment from strong mood to inescapable pressure.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Mara clings to the key/control or relinquishes it to step into an uncertain homecoming—is clear and does tighten toward the toss into the fountain. Her agency drives the main turn and resolution. However, external pressure from the cultist feels limited and the choice reads relatively low-friction; the risk of what the pendant or path might unleash is more implied than viscerally tested on the page, so escalation tops out at “firmly competent” rather than bracing.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Themes of liminality, uncertainty as habitat, and light-as-boundary emerge organically from the mist, the veil-of-sleep frame, and Mara’s worldview. The final reconfiguration of “boundaries of light” into “doors” coherently reweights earlier images. That said, lines like “uncertainty? That was just another word for home” come close to thesis statements, nudging the piece toward mild didacticism instead of letting the images shoulder the whole load.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The close third-person voice has a distinct texture—feral, lyrical, slightly occult—that feels consistent with Mara’s mind and modulates well between contemplation and confrontation. A few abstract locutions (“ritual of clarity,” “boundaries of light”) lean into conceptual language, but they stay within the established voice’s ambit.</comment>

At times the diction veers near purple, and a couple of conceptual phrases feel more clever than embodied, but clarity and momentum remain strong throughout.</comment>

The ending’s conceptual turn about light-as-door rather than wall is elegant, though not radically surprising.</comment>
