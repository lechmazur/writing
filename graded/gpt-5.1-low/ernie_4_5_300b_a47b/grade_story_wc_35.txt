The pavilion’s cold, the wet stone, and the rare rain all affect what he can sense and how he acts. The final image of the fortress “tempered by the rain” elegantly ties atmosphere to thematic resolution.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Nessar will risk subsuming stored remorse to lighten the fortress’s burden—is clear and escalates logically as he moves from vials to the pool itself. The key turn is his decision to submerge deeper and accept the founder’s guilt, which is well-motivated and carries a credible internal cost. Overall tension remains more meditative than acute, which slightly flattens potential intensity.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of remorse, burden-sharing, and transforming pain into nourishment emerge organically from images (rain, coral prison, broth, onions) rather than explicit moralizing. The final line edges toward statement (“sometimes, he thought, that was enough”) but is sufficiently grounded in concrete action to avoid sentimentality. The piece embraces complexity about whether the remorse is “gone” or merely carried differently.</comment>

Psychic distance is steady, and interior access feels plausibly limited to Nessar’s perceptions and inferences. The absence of dialogue avoids potential pitfalls and keeps the voice focused and unified.</comment>
