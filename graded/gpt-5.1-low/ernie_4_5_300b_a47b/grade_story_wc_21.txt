Description generally serves mood and pressure rather than feeling like a catalog.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preserving dangerous truth versus personal annihilation—is signaled early and escalates through Mara’s visions, the lagoon’s warning, and the lethal tunnel network to the final decision to destroy access to the codex. Her agency drives the key turns: choosing to parley, to descend despite foreknowledge of her death, and finally to trigger the trapdoor. However, the climax is rushed: the confrontation with the antagonist and sacrifice of the codex happen almost back-to-back with limited on-page reckoning with the human and political costs. The sense of having “just bought time” is thematically apt but could carry a sharper felt price on the page.</comment>

The ending’s notion of buying time and leaving the truth dispersed “out there” offers a resonant if familiar answer. With a touch less direct exposition about regimes and more reliance on implication, the thematic resonance could deepen.</comment>
