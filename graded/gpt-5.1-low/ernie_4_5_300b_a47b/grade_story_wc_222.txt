The conflict remains largely procedural and environmental rather than moral or relational, so tension stays moderate even as the prose signals danger. The result is engaging but not gripping.</comment>

Kael’s internal shift from thief to “guardian of wonder” feels thematically apt but arrives as a tidy conclusion rather than a hard-won, ambiguously priced realization. The subtext is present but could be deepened by more dramatized conflict between safety and curiosity.</comment>
