<question>2</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The causal chain is straightforward and legible: prophecy → preparation → ritual at the brook → prophecy hushed. Under a causal lens, the story reads more like a successful procedure than a pressured decision with consequences.</comment>

That said, the setting rarely constrains choice or introduces friction; it primarily supports atmosphere rather than exerting pressure. Still, for a short, quiet piece, the specificity and consistency are strong.</comment>

The result is tension that feels conceptual rather than felt.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>Themes of silence as language and peace preserved through quiet, deliberate action recur coherently through the crane, the brook, and the “smoothed hush.” However, the piece edges toward tidy moralizing (“The smoothed hush had done its work… the world remained quiet”) without much ambiguity or counterpressure. There is little interrogation of the price of silence or of hushing prophecy; success reaffirms her worldview rather than complicating it. The resonance is pleasant but not deeply surprising or unsettling.</comment>
