<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The causal chain is clear and coherent: a failing ship and dimming relic force Lira into the meditation chamber, where engaging in shadow work allows her to open the locked dimension and stabilize the *Eirenicon*. The main weakness is that no beat seriously threatens failure once she commits; the process reads as smooth rather than precarious.</comment>

Details like walls patched with fused asteroid metal and the “ghostly resolve” of systems feel specific and thematically apt rather than touristic. Environment and objects (the ship, the altar, the garden) all charge the final images and help define before/after.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Internal and external stakes are clearly articulated—the ship’s possible failure and the ethical risk of “becoming the monster” by engaging. <question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of pacifism, engagement, shadow work, and the twinship of grief and love are well integrated with setting and action, and the final image of the garden as “not an escape but an invitation” offers a resonant reframing.
