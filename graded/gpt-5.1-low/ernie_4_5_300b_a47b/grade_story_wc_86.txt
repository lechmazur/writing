However, the setting rarely constrains or redirects concrete choices; it’s more atmospheric than tactical. The recurring object-as-setting (the drawer pull) does charge the final image, but the physical environment itself could press harder on action to reach the highest tier.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The dominant stakes—lives at risk in a prisoner exchange and Clara’s fear of repeating past mistakes—are articulated early and clearly. The result is thematically charged but dramatically soft, with limited sense of options narrowing or real-time prices being paid.</comment>
