The recurring parapet and terrace atmosphere charge the final image even if they don’t tightly constrain choices. This is strong, publishable-level atmosphere and specificity.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she can persuade the mirrors to show and seed new possibilities, emotionally bound to her sister’s memory—is clear but remains largely internal and abstract. Escalation exists (successive mirrors, her growing fatigue, then the bells threatening to break the spell), yet each tightening feels relatively low-consequence on the page. There is no sharp trade-off moment where she must clearly sacrifice one good for another; she simply pushes harder until the bells end. The cost of her exertion and the risk of failure could be made more viscerally legible to lift the conflict.</comment>

The piece avoids sermonizing, but it also avoids deeper complicating angles (ethical cost of borrowed heartbeat, darker doubts), keeping the thematic field somewhat safe. Still, the emotional aftereffect is coherent and earned.</comment>

Overall the voice feels deliberate and publication-ready, if not radically distinctive.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Line-level craft is strong: sentences vary in length and structure, sensory images are clear, and most lines do multiple jobs (worldbuilding, mood, and character at once). <question>8</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The combination of elements—mirage painting that only appears in reflections, persuading mirrors with coded lullabies, a preserved heartbeat as artistic instrument—is imaginative and feels like a coherent, singular vision rather than a prompt checklist.
