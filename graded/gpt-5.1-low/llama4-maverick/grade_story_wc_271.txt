<question>2</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The structure is a straightforward walk-to-cliff-and-reflect sequence with a clear before/after: she arrives anxious and leaves calmer, which gives the piece basic resonant closure (RR under an accumulative lens). The main weakness is that cost and difficulty are underplayed; the turn toward healing feels relatively frictionless, with no concrete obstacle or reversal on the page.</comment>

Details are sensory and purposeful, with the setting shifting from “twisted, tortured” to “sanctuary” in step with her emotional arc. However, the environment mostly provides mood rather than materially constraining her choices, which keeps this out of the very highest band.</comment>

As a result, tension plateaus rather than escalates, and the resolution feels more like a gentle fade-in than a choice under strain.</comment>

The final reassurance lands emotionally but feels somewhat generic rather than uniquely earned from these exact particulars.</comment>

There is no head-hopping or stance confusion, but the voice could feel more idiosyncratically Amira rather than a generalized lyrical narrator.</comment>

A leaner line-level pass cutting redundant sentiments and tempering the metaphors would significantly sharpen momentum and force.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The combination of a drone-strike survivor and a windswept arboretum is promising, and the photograph motif adds a small visual twist with the “smoothing” metaphor.
