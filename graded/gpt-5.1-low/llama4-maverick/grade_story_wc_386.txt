Causality is intact (touching the globe triggers the vision, deeper understanding triggers her decision to leave), but the turns are gentle and largely expository. Still, the shrine and snow globe recur in the final image and help charge the ending as a quiet beacon. This is strong mood work, though not yet using environment as a full dramatic lever.</comment>

Pressure does not meaningfully tighten; the shipwreck vision is evocative but not a test of her resolve or values. The result is a contemplative vignette with minimal conflict rather than a story with felt escalation.</comment>

There’s limited sense of Aria’s idiosyncratic perceptual signature beyond standard poetic tropes. No POV slips or head-hopping occur, but the voice lacks the distinctiveness expected at a top-tier venue.</comment>
