<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Lena’s need to break out of stifling certainty into a more open, enchanted relationship with her work—is present from early on. Her agency drives the central turn (rearranging mirrors to “bewitch” reality), yet this decision is almost frictionless, with no hard trade-off or visible loss beyond a vague letting go of certainty. Tension remains mostly atmospheric rather than genuinely pressurized.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.0</grade><comment>The thematic through-line—exchanging certainty for curiosity and discovering a more enchanted, plural reality—is coherent and integrated with the imagery of reflections, sound waves, and the mutable mural. As a result, the ending flirts with didacticism and smooths away complexity; Lena pays little discernible price for this new stance. The hobo nickel and garden motifs support the theme but could bear more ambiguous or double-edged meaning.</comment>

However, the emotional arc (stifled mastery → transformative experiment → serene embrace of curiosity) is quite conventional and resolved without much ambiguity.
