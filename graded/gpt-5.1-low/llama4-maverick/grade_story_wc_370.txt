That limits its impact at a “prestige” level despite its solid vividness.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Elys’s need to re-evaluate a painful mistake and their chosen isolation—is clear but remains almost entirely internal and reflective. The masquerade invitation could have embodied an external test, yet it never becomes a decision with consequences, only a symbol. As a result, tension is mild and the sense of price paid is more asserted than felt.</comment>

Yet the voice lacks a strongly individual texture; many sentences feel interchangeable with other introspective prose, and the philosophical register occasionally veers toward vague abstraction. It’s competent and smooth but not yet distinctive or sharp enough to stand out at an elite level.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>6.0</grade><comment>The prose is generally clear and readable, with no major grammar or usage errors, and a steady, unhurried rhythm. The piece feels sincere but conservative in both stakes and execution, with limited transformative surprise.</comment>
