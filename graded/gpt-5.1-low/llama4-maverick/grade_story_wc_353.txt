<question>3</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The siskin alder grove, strings between trees, and the porcelain insulator create a distinct, mildly uncanny atmosphere, and the forest’s subtle responses (“whispered secrets,” trees swaying) support the tone. Setting is consistently present and filtered through the sculptor’s craft. However, the sensory palette leans heavily on visual and conceptual language, with limited tactile or auditory workaround given the silent-world premise. The environment charges the final image but never quite constrains or complicates choices.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>4.2</grade><comment>Conflict is the story’s weakest axis: the silence is described as a world-scale wound, yet it never resists the protagonist’s efforts. Stakes (“bring empathy into motion”) remain lofty but untested in concrete terms, and no genuine option is foreclosed. The result is a pleasing mood piece with minimal tension or escalation.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.0</grade><comment>Themes of empathy, art as repair, and music-in-silence are coherent and recur through objects (the grove, the sculpture, the insulator). The ending offers a gentle conceptual repricing of silence—no longer oppressive—but does so in near-moralistic phrasing. There is some resonance, but little complexity or counter-pressure.</comment>

Lexicon fits an almost-mythic, fable-like register without obvious slips. While competent and occasionally graceful, the voice lacks the highly distinctive perceptual signature or modulation under pressure that would lift it higher.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The central inventions—the “tree-song sculptor,” a world made silent by “the hedgehog uncurled,” and a porcelain insulator woven into arboreal music—are genuinely fresh and memorable. It feels like a strong sketch of an original world rather than a fully realized, risk-taking piece.</comment>
