The closure is present but cost-light—memories return in a single touch with no visible tradeoff or repercussion. Structurally sound but too smooth for elite-level immediacy.</comment>

However, the environment rarely constrains choices or drives tactics; it’s more backdrop than active pressure. The recurring crystals do subtly charge the final image but stop short of becoming a decisive agent in the narrative.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>4.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—uncovering where “deleted” memories go—is mostly abstract and never faces friction; nothing threatens the lighter or meaningfully resists their inquiry. Once the whale beaches, the protagonist follows the path presented with no real risk, sacrifice, or wrong turn, so tension remains low. Touching the crystal produces an instant, benign reward rather than a tradeoff or tested commitment. The piece functions as a mood-piece quest rather than a story with escalating conflict and visible cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>5.5</grade><comment>Themes of memory, loss, and rediscovery are clear and recurrent, with the wildflowers, lanterns, and recovered memories all pointing toward reclamation of the past. Yet the piece leans into generalized uplift (“even in the darkest of times, there was always the possibility of rediscovery”) that borders on platitude rather than hard-won insight. Subtext about what it might mean to live with or without those memories, or why some should perhaps stay buried, never materializes. The final image reframes the lantern-lighting, but without enough ambiguity or conceptual cost to linger deeply.</comment>

Diction is slightly elevated but consistent, matching the meditative, speculative tone. However, the perceptual signature of the lighter is not strongly individuated; nearly any contemplative protagonist could inhabit this narration. Rhythm is pleasant but somewhat uniform, contributing to the sense of gentle drift rather than calibrated intensity.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The prose is generally clean and fluent, with a few striking phrases and a coherent sonic texture; sentences are grammatical and easy to follow. The descriptive density occasionally verges on static, as similar kinds of luminous imagery recur without adding new pressure or specificity. Overall, line-level craft is competent and sometimes lovely but not yet ruthlessly precise.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The fusion of a dock-side lantern lighter, gravity pockets, a salt-crystal chamber, coffee-ground divination, and a beached whale as inciting anomaly is notably inventive and non-template. The idea of “where deleted memories go” being spatially locatable via warped gravity and crystal is conceptually fresh. However, the originality is more in ingredients than in how conflict or consequence unfold; the resolution is a straightforward mystical recovery rather than a surprising or risky transformation. With deeper exploitation of the premise’s darker or stranger implications, this could rise to a more memorable level.</comment>
