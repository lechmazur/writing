<question>4</question><grade>4.5</grade><comment>Stakes are theoretically high (a coup that could upend the observatory) but remain distant and undifferentiated, with no specific consequences or antagonistic force on the page. The final sense of purpose and universe “awakening” offers some resonance but risks pseudo-profundity because it’s not grounded in specific, earned moments. The moral and conceptual stakes remain soft rather than sharply reweighted at the end.</comment>

Still, POV control is clean and there is no head-hopping or confusing stance change.</comment>

Momentum suffers slightly under the weight of similar sentences and reiterated concepts, though clarity remains intact. <question>8</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The central image of a galaxy-chart embroiderer in a cobalt observatory, sewing conspiratorial truths from the stars, is fresh and appealing.
