The environment rarely constrains choices or action; it functions more as mood-board than engine.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>5.0</grade><comment>Themes of remembrance, the dignity of overlooked lives, and “small kindnesses” are coherent but heavily asserted. We get little sense of Emilia’s idiosyncratic way of noticing beyond a generalized wistful reverence. The voice is competent but not yet distinctive enough for a top-tier literary publication.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>4.8</grade><comment>The prose is generally grammatical but weighed down by repetition and abstraction—“testament,” “sanctuary,” “resilience,” “power of the human heart” recur without adding precision. Figurative language tends toward the generic (“unfolded like a flower,” “pushing back against the darkness”) rather than fresh, sensory images. Line-level craft feels more like a competent draft than refined literary work.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>5.4</grade><comment>The combination of a foster-care archive, a found infantryman’s button, and a focus on “unknown histories” offers a potentially fresh frame.
