Closure qualifies as a Resonant Reconfiguration, though the cost is only lightly implied. Structural repetition (trellis/leaf/storm) provides cohesion but also flattens dynamic progression.</comment>

Sensory detail skews visual and becomes somewhat generic and repetitive (“serene beauty,” “warm light of a new day”). Stronger use of environment as pressure or resource would lift this above competent lyric background.</comment>

The “turn” is essentially a decision to reframe her attitude, with minimal felt sacrifice. Stakes remain conceptual and gentle, limiting tension.</comment>

The linden leaf and trellis reprise do create a faint resonant echo, but the message feels familiar and platitudinous. Complexity or ambiguity around the cost of change is largely omitted.</comment>

The final paragraphs, in particular, read like variations on the same sentiment without added pressure or insight.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>5.4</grade><comment>Paralleling an internal metamorphosis with plant life and storms is an intuitively sound but familiar conceit.
