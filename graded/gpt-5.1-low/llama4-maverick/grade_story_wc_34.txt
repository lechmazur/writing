Nonetheless, it does charge the final image of a changed courtyard and lingering song.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>4.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—achieving communication/understanding between artist and ghost—is signaled, but there is almost no genuine resistance or risk. The “storm brewing” and heightened energy suggest danger without ever concretely threatening her or the connection. Her path from intent to success is linear, with no meaningful reversal, narrowed option, or visible price. As a result, tension remains low, and the ending feels like a fulfillment rather than an earned, costly resolution.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>Themes of creative expression as a bridge between realms and the encoding of ephemeral experience into lasting marks are clear and consistent. However, the piece drifts toward overt statement (“a testament to the power of creative expression”) instead of letting implication carry the weight, and it shies away from complicating or undercutting its own wonder. The thematic aftereffect is pleasant but not deeply challenging or surprising.</comment>

Yet the diction often defaults to generalized mystical language (“underlying energy,” “mystical realm,” “otherworldly hum”) that could fit many similar pieces, limiting the sense of a sharply personal perceptual signature. POV control is clean, but the voice rarely modulates or sharpens under the story’s supposed peak pressures.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>6.0</grade><comment>The prose is fluent and mostly clear, with pleasing rhythm and no major grammatical issues. Figurative language and abstractions are dense enough to slightly blur impact rather than sharpen it at climactic moments. <question>8</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The fusion of a mystical, cloud-high courtyard, a “ghost in code,” and graffiti as a communication medium is a moderately fresh conceptual blend. Nonetheless, execution falls back on familiar mystical-art tropes (charged storms, hidden truths, ineffable energy) and does not take many surprising risks in character, stakes, or structure. The story feels gently inventive rather than daring or indispensably original.</comment>
