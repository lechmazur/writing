<question>4</question><grade>6.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—waking Tomás and, through him, the courtyard from the phoenix-induced fugue—is intelligible, but pressure remains gentle. The concept of bartering awakenings has moral bite, yet no one on the page pushes back or forces a hard choice, so there’s no substantial narrowing of options or escalation. The resolution feels clean and somewhat frictionless, limiting tension and cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of awakening, perception beyond color, and beauty in imperfection are woven consistently through Maya’s gift, the bent reed, and the phoenix’s disruptive effect. However, several lines state the thesis outright (“There’s beauty in what’s been worn down by living,” “proof that being broken by the world was just another way of being beautiful”), tipping slightly into didactic clarity rather than trusting subtext. The ending still resonates, but it feels a touch explained rather than fully left to image and implication.</comment>
