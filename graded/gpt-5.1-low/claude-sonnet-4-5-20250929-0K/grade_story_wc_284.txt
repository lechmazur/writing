Details feel filtered through Marcus’ eye as an ex-operative, with attention to terrain and patterns, not generic landscape. The only limitation is that, after the strong opening, later village and interior spaces are sketched more minimally, slightly reducing environmental pressure there.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake (saving and transmitting the snow-coded evidence before thaw) is clear and tightened effectively by the three-day window. However, opposition feels light: no institutional resistance, physical barrier, or moral dilemma truly tests his resolve once he decides, and Emma’s cooperation is frictionless. As a result escalation feels more like steady execution than a pressured narrowing of options.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of damage, courage, and what it means to “fix what’s broken” are coherently developed through the winter/spring imagery, Marcus’ scars, and Sarah’s elaborate snow archive. Yet the story undercuts some of its own subtlety with explicit statements like “Fixing what's broken, he understood now, meant…” and the engraved line “You were never broken, only afraid,” which read as direct thesis delivery. This moralizing edge flattens what might otherwise linger more ambiguously and powerfully.</comment>

Prose modulates reasonably with pressure (more concrete during the climb and observation, more abstract at the emotional turn), and the few dialogue exchanges feel natural and economical. Marcus’ perceptions—attention to patterns, tactical thinking—anchor the POV convincingly. There is mild drift into authorial/philosophical diction near the end, but it doesn’t break the established voice.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Line-level craft is strong: sentences are varied, generally lean, and carry multiple functions (image, mood, and information) without obvious scaffolding or loops. The main weakness is a slight tilt toward abstraction and statement in the final paragraph, where insight is told rather than entirely earned through image, softening the closure’s sharpness. Still, there are no major artifacts, and momentum remains intact.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The fusion of espionage with an almost pastoral, seasonal dead drop—codes in snowdrifts, preserved by winter and erased by spring—is a fresh and memorable angle. The ending’s conceptual shift about brokenness is sincere but somewhat conventional, limiting the sense of radical newness. Overall, the piece feels polished and specific, if not formally or thematically daring enough for the very top band.</comment>
