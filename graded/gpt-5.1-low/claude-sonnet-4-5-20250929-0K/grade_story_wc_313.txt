<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The stakes are primarily internal and situational: whether Millbrook’s resurfacing will actually be witnessed and integrated into Marcus’s understanding before it is “expunged” by returning water. However, conflict remains gentle—there’s no hard on-page test of his duty versus his longing, and no moment where he risks breaking rules or losing status. The result is a satisfying but low-intensity escalation that fits the piece but keeps it short of maximal sharpness.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Theme—about submerged histories, the need for witnesses, and the rootedness of meaning—is strong and well-supported by recurring images (reservoir, photographs, the distillery label, the wind chime). However, some thematic statements are quite explicit (“stories without witnesses become lies,” “meaning doesn’t transplant”), edging toward didactic rather than fully subtextual resonance. The story still leaves an emotional aftertaste, but a touch more restraint in articulating the thesis would increase its literary subtlety.</comment>
