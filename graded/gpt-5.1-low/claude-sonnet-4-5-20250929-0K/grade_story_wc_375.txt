The cave enables crucial plot mechanics (hidden carving; natural gallery) but rarely constrains options or creates physical stakes. Still, specificity and atmosphere are strong and consistently support tone and theme.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elena will remain imprisoned by perfect recall or transform it into art—appears early and tightens meaningfully with the discovery of the wild self-portrait. Escalation is present via the hours of obsessive work, bleeding fingers, and emotional flood, but there’s little sense of resistance or a real alternative path once she begins. As a result, the cost is felt but not sharply contested on the page.</comment>

Complexity is more implied in the mother’s tragic choices than fully explored in Elena’s neatly resolved acceptance.</comment>
