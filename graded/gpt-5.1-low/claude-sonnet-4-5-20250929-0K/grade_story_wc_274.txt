<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—risking societal stability by awakening a frozen witness to systemic crime—is clearly articulated early and tightens toward the choice to wake one Testimonial. The cost is acknowledged (“ending her own life as she’d known it”) but we don’t see any concrete loss yet, which keeps the stakes slightly conceptual.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Thematic concerns—what humanity owes truth when survival depends on curated forgetting—are well integrated with the world-building of frozen testimonials and contraband archives. The ending offers a resonant but fairly straightforward answer (some truths are worth destabilization), with less lingering ambiguity than top-tier literary pieces often sustain.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is consistent and controlled, with professional, slightly elevated diction that matches an archivist in a high-tech dystopia.
