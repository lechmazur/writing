<question>4</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The dominant stake—can Margaux secure an encore/a stabilizing performance before she unravels—is clear and paid off by her garden dance and the cross-time audience. However, the escalation is more conceptual and textural than felt as narrowing options; we never see a realistic alternative path or a moment where she could meaningfully fail, which softens tension. The cost (her dissolution into “everything”) is conceptually rich but lands as serene rather than sharply conflicted, which limits the sense of risk.</comment>

The final image of Margaux woven into the garden and visitors tasting ghostly music as starlight offers a clear, non-literal answer to the opening problem of abandonment and erasure. There’s a slight neatness to the resolution—very little thematic abrasion or moral cost—which keeps it from feeling radically complex.</comment>

Rhythmic variation and repetition are purposeful, supporting the accumulative, incantatory mood of the performance and its echoes.
