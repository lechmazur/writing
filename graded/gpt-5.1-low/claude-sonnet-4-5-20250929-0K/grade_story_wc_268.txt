<question>2</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: corrupted marginalia → arrival of the ink pellet → use of the rune → worldwide exposure of the pretender. Closure is decisive and legible, with a before/after in the realm’s political reality and in the texts themselves. The drive lens reads as Causal and holds through the end, though the decisive struggle remains fully off-stage (the pretender never appears). The cost is conceptual rather than personal, which slightly flattens the climactic beat.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The “fold between moments” temple library is vivid, specific, and well-integrated, from constellation-leather tomes to starlight-water and impossible windows. The setting isn’t mere backdrop: its out-of-time nature explains Ossian’s role and how the books can rewrite reality. The final image of constellations drifting to the ceiling and stars wheeling outside meaningfully charges the ending and underlines the story’s cosmic-temporal theme. A touch more tactile interaction with the environment under pressure could push this higher.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The stakes—royal succession and the exposure of a murderous pretender—are large and clearly signaled, and the use of the rune creates a satisfying conceptual “unraveling.” However, the conflict is nearly frictionless: the pretender’s cleverness is described but never forces Ossian to adapt or risk anything tangible. Escalation is mostly procedural (find each corrupted passage, inscribe rune fragments) rather than pressured, so tension stays intellectual rather than visceral.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of historical truth versus revisionism, and of records as living forces that shape power, emerge organically from the premise and rune mechanics. The ending’s image of marginal rune fragments ensuring truth’s eventual resurfacing offers a resonant, if tidy, conceptual answer. Some moral or emotional ambiguity around the cost of such absolute correction could deepen the resonance.</comment>

While the diction is occasionally lush, it rarely tips into purple or confusion. The voice is strong and pleasant, though not yet so idiosyncratic as to feel unmistakably singular.</comment>
