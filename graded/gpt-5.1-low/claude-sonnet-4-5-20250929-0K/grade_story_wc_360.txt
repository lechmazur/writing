The stakes (understanding emotion to anticipate/survive cosmic collapse) remain slightly abstract and are never brought to a concrete on-page decision point. While the setting doesn’t strictly constrain tactical choices, it permeates every action and heavily charges the final image. World rules (halo fragments, Seraph Collapse, empathy work) are integrated smoothly without expository drag.</comment>

Her agency is primarily sustained attention and analysis rather than a pressured choice between competing costs. The final image leaves a lingering, ambivalent sense of persistence amid cosmic and personal erosion.</comment>

Psychic distance is managed well, staying close enough to her perceptions while allowing occasional broader cosmological framing that still feels filtered through her. <question>8</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The synthesis of letterpress craft, divine debris, collapsing cosmology, and quasi-physical “equations of emotion” feels distinctly original. Its only limitation is that the emotional/cosmic stakes, though conceptually bold, don’t quite crystallize into a sharply reconfigured choice at the end, keeping it just shy of exceptional.</comment>
