The environment meaningfully charges the premise—this is where worlds thin, where impossibility already exists—and the final act of planting on that parapet reuses the space with new weight. It doesn’t quite *force* difficult tactical choices, but it decisively shapes the story’s texture and closure image.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The stakes are conceptually huge (the survival of human wonder) and clearly signaled, and there is a modest escalation as Mara moves from preparation to crossing to confronting the resisting essence. Her agency is present but never seriously tested—once she commits, nothing on the page threatens her goal in a way that would force a costly trade. The piece functions more as a successful ritual vignette than a pressured conflict.</comment>

Some lines lean toward explicit explication (“not just a color, but everything it represented”) and gently flatten ambiguity, edging close to sermonizing. The piece invites reflection but ultimately offers a fairly clean, untroubled moral about stewardship of joy.</comment>
