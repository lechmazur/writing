<question>2</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The story follows a clean, single-scene structure in the attic with well-motivated flashbacks that are always anchored in present stimuli (dust, meteorite, cylinders). <question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The attic setting is vivid and tonally integrated: the smell of leather bindings, shafts of light, dust motes, and phonograph cylinders all feel essential rather than decorative. Clara’s professional past filters what she notices (air currents, dust patterns) in a way that makes the environment inseparable from her inner life. The attic directly enables the core actions—cataloging cylinders, placing the meteorite in the skylight—and the dust and light charge the final images. It’s hard to imagine this story working in any other setting without major conceptual loss.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Clara will keep erasing and reinventing or commit to a more honest, enduring self—is clear and quietly tense throughout. Pressure escalates texturally as the motifs (meteorite, dust, paper trails, Marcus below) layer until she chooses to anchor the meteorite and stay with the cylinders. That choice forecloses, at least for now, the path of flight and self-erasure, with a legible emotional cost in the risk of trust and continuity. The limitation is that external pressure remains minimal and there’s no single sharply turning moment; the stakes deepen rather than tighten, which slightly blunts the sense of escalation.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Themes of permanence vs. erasure, authenticity, and trust are woven through concrete particulars: destroyed résumés, the meteorite’s age, dust making things visible, and the act of cataloging. The ending reweights earlier material by reframing withering as possibly “natural, seasonal, rather than self-imposed,” offering a nuanced, non-moralistic answer to the central question. Thematically it’s resonant and cohesive, if a touch on-the-nose in a couple of sentences.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The prose is clean, economical, and rhythmically varied, with most sentences doing double duty (advancing both atmosphere and character). There’s little to no scaffolding or paraphrase looping; each paragraph adds new nuance or pressure. The line-level work is nearly publication-ready as-is, with only very minor risk of slight over-lyricism in spots.</comment>

The dust-mapping and opposite/visibility motif give the piece a conceptual cohesion that rises above template introspection.
