<question>4</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how to execute a goodbye once the results confirm impending death—is clear and signaled early. Mira’s agency is primarily in decisions of attention and framing (pocketing the truth, offering the shard, guiding the hymn of the mundane), which foreclose more clinical or evasive goodbyes. The cost is emotional and professional rather than plot-dramatic, but it’s legible and satisfying for this scale.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Themes of mortality, brokenness that still shines, and the sacredness of the mundane emerge organically from particulars: the shard, the hymn about trash and coffee, the clouds erasing the city. “If God is anywhere, he is taking out the trash with me on Monday” and the final sense that the silence “was… enough” crystallize a worldview without sermonizing. The piece embraces ambiguity (no miracle, no explicit afterlife; just a waiting room between worlds) while still taking a stance on where meaning resides. Subtext in objects and atmosphere does the work; nothing needs to be spelled out.</comment>
