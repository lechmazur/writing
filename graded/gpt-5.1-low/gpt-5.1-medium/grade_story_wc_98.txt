<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The central stake—whether to let a new, disruptive dream rewrite collective memory—is clearly articulated and pressed by the anomalous events and the council’s demand. Because the story ends at the moment of reweighted knowledge rather than a concrete decision, we don’t see a path explicitly foreclosed yet, but the narrowing of options is clear. A small on-page move toward or away from action after waking could have intensified perceived cost.</comment>

The piece resists simple answers: to accept the broader history is both enlargement and loss of comforting self-sufficiency. A few conceptual phrases (“cultural memory,” “collective memory”) skirt abstraction but are anchored enough in scene to avoid didacticism.</comment>
