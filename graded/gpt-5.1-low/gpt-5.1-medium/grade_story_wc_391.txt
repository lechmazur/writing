The main turn is knowledge-based rather than decision-based; the protagonist accepts rather than chooses under duress, which slightly softens the sense of paid cost. A sharper on-page dilemma (stay in slow discipline vs. rush back with half-learning) would push this higher.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Themes of speed vs. patience, structural versus spectacular revolution, and unyoking change from frenzy are strongly integrated into image and structure (frozen lake, remembered storms, slow lightning). However, Orren’s explicit statement—“Not to end haste, but to unyoke change from frenzy”—and some summarizing sentences flirt with thesis-delivery. It’s effective, but a touch more implication and a touch less direct formulation would feel truer to elite venues.</comment>
