<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—understanding and possibly accessing the town’s lost time—is clearly signaled and tightened as anomalies escalate from failing lanterns to missing days to the narrator’s risky experiment. The escalation is primarily intellectual and atmospheric rather than conflictual; there is little overt resistance or counter-force beyond the eerie physics themselves. This keeps the tension quiet but coherent, with a definite deepening at the moment the coffee grounds pattern reality and reveal the missing memories.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Themes of memory, gravity, and what accumulates or escapes (light, time, residue, scent) are woven tightly through concrete motifs: coffee grounds, lantern wicks, salt crystals, the whale plugging an unseen channel. The ending’s vision of missing days circling “like stunned fish” offers a resonant, non-literal answer to where forgotten time goes, with a strong sense of conceptual cost. The story embraces ambiguity about what can be done with this knowledge, which keeps the thematic resonance from feeling didactic.</comment>
