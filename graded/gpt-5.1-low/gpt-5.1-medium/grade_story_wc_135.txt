<question>2</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The accumulative structure—Mara’s walk, the discovery of the valve, the gathering, and Caven’s tentative note—builds cleanly toward a charged final poise. Closure lands as a resonant reconfiguration: the community’s relationship to its own absences shifts in that hovering note. Stakes remain largely conceptual, which slightly limits the punch despite the elegance.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The harbor is fully realized as both physical place and emotional pressure-field: “ivory harbor after rain,” bruised sky, seawall, bollards, and unclaimed moorings all feel integral rather than decorative. Setting details are consistently filtered through Mara’s archivist sensibility, with tide and weather explicitly “editing” the fragments she’s racing. The environment charges the final image of the note hanging over the wet stones and tarnished brass. The harbor doesn’t quite force a concrete tactical decision in-scene, which keeps this just below the 9-band.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether loss and exile can be reassembled into communal meaning—emerges early and tightens as Mara shifts from solitary collecting to public curation, culminating in Caven’s risk of playing again. Pressure escalates texturally rather than through sharp reversals, with the crowd’s gradual straightening and the accumulating fragments as the core deepening. The cost is implied in Caven’s scarred, hesitant note and the community’s vulnerability in listening for it. Because the conflict never bites in a more concrete or oppositional way, tension remains beautifully poised but somewhat gentle.</comment>

A few lines edge toward explicit thesis (“Hope, she knew, was just another kind of fragment”), slightly reducing subtextive charge. Overall, thematic coherence and resonance are high and organically grown from action and image.</comment>

Occasional mildly omniscient moves (community history, Caven’s exile) feel smoothly braided into her perspective rather than jarring head-hops. The ending’s image of a question-note over the harbor elegantly fuses concept, character, and world.
