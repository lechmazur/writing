<question>2</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The structure is clean: a single ritual scene framed by brief, anchored memories, with each knot release progressively deepening the situation. The causal chain (accepting the net → working through knots → voices → final name) is legible under an accumulative/textural lens, and the ending offers a clear reconfiguration: the captain named, spirits resting, Ilan’s future work subtly altered. Closure carries cost in Ilan’s willingness to bear the name and then release it, tied to his own grief. The only minor weakness is that the external risk level remains low, so the climax feels more like a deepening than a decisive test.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The prism sanctuary, starlight, glass columns, and ancient net are vivid and integrated, with details consistently filtered through Ilan’s ritual-focused attention. Setting does more than decorate: the sanctuary’s silence, the way light refracts, and the stone that "breathes" all charge the mood and make the final "grounded stillness" feel like a real transformation of place. The environment doesn’t strongly constrain tactics—he could arguably perform this rite elsewhere—so its role is more atmospheric than logistical. Nonetheless, the recurring sanctuary and net imagery clearly charge the final image.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—can Ilan restore the captain’s name and settle the spirits—is clear early and sharpened by the rule that each knot holds a night at sea. Pressure accumulates through the successive knots and ghostly voices, and the last "tooth-hard" star knot creates a real moment of risk and hesitation. However, the escalation is almost entirely internal and textural; there is little sense of a consequence if he fails beyond continued unrest, which keeps the tension more contemplative than urgent. The resolution is earned but feels like a single smooth gradient rather than a sharp narrowing of options.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Themes of grief, memory, the ethics of keeping and releasing names, and the transformation of fear-filled silence into meaningful stillness are woven through image and action rather than stated outright. Subtext around Ilan’s brother and the villagers' cracked faces quietly underpins his commitment to the dead captain. The piece resists tidy lessons, though it edges close to a neat thematic summation in the closing sentence.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is lyrically confident and consistent, with a clear perceptual signature in its focus on light, silence, and texture. Dialogue is sparse but effective, carrying subtext without breaking the tone. One slightly awkward construction (“across sharing a secret”) momentarily jars, but overall control of rhythm and distance is strong.</comment>

Even so, momentum is maintained, and each line tends to advance character, atmosphere, or stakes simultaneously. There are no major loops or scaffolding artifacts dulling the closure image.</comment>

While not radically experimental, the piece’s particular synthesis of ritual, grief-work, and maritime haunting feels distinctive and publication-ready.</comment>
