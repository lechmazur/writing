<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Stakes are primarily moral and conceptual: whether small kindnesses can tilt timelines toward life in a dying world, localized in this oasis. His final decision to actively seed the hives with clay and wildflower seeds is a meaningful tightening of commitment, foreclosing passive observation. The main limitation is that opposition remains abstract—there is little immediate risk or counterforce beyond the ambient harshness of the world.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The thematic throughline—that accumulated, often invisible kindness can make places resistant to catastrophic change—is organically developed through bees, clay memory, and fractured timelines. However, the explicit thought “Perhaps…places resist change when enough kindness has soaked into their stories” verges on stating the thesis, slightly flattening what is otherwise beautifully implied. The ending still leaves room for resonant ambiguity (he may never see the swarm return), avoiding tidy moral closure.</comment>
