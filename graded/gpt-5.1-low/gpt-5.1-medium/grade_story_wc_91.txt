Lightning bugs as “tame constellations” and the observatory details are filtered through character and theme rather than reading as tourist catalog. The external conflict is strong, while the internal conflict around prophecy and agency is gestured at more than fully tested; Niko decides quickly and cleanly once the danger is known. <question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Themes of transformation (fear to courage, ash to soil, coincidence to intention) and reluctant myth-making emerge organically from events and images, not from didactic statements. The prophecy thread and the lightning bugs as bottled stars effectively question agency versus fate without forcing a pat moral. The final hush of “astonished gratitude” offers a resonant, non-literal answer about mercy and unintended heroism without closing off conceptual ambiguity.</comment>

Lila’s speech and presence are distinct—quieter, cosmos-facing—without derailing the established voice.
