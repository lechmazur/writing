Some middle material (“So we worked together...”) compresses what could be dramatized, slightly softening causal sharpness and felt cost. Still, the through-line from mapping fungi to storm-night revelation is coherent and satisfying.</comment>

The setting does exert some constraint (isolation, suspended pods, dependence on cables), though most pressure is emotional rather than logistical. It very effectively charges tone and theme, even if it seldom forces concrete tactical choices.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether unbearable empathy can be made livable without surrendering selfhood—is clear and well tied to the virus, the “oversensitive” exiles, and the truffle-mapping project. However, escalation is quiet and largely internal; conflict is more about reinterpreting objects and conditions than confronting a sharp dilemma with a visible loss. The ending feels like an earned soft turn rather than a high-cost inflection.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Themes of empathy as both contagion and connection, the dangers of surveillance, and the difference between mere safety and true “psychological safety” are tightly interwoven with truffles, mycelial networks, and the glass eye. Occasional abstract formulations (“praxis of fractured grace,” “residual moods”) skate close to essayistic, but they’re grounded enough in concrete context to avoid sermonizing. The piece leaves a strong conceptual and emotional afterglow.</comment>
