<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The mangrove labyrinth, phosphorescent algae, and condensation “windows” create an immersive, non-generic setting that is always tied to the emotional and thematic stakes (apologies, regret, unspoken words). <question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—can Lysa recover and articulate the exact instant before the brother’s fatal decision—is signaled early and honored through to the end. Lysa’s agency is genuine in what she chooses to ask for (“not your death, but the last moment…”) and in her refusal to decide for the living client, which marks an earned stance at closure. However, the external conflict is mostly frictionless ritual rather than a choice under overt threat, so the sense of real jeopardy to Lysa or her mission, while present, is somewhat muted.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Themes of unsent apologies, fear masquerading as pride, and the limits of mediation are woven through specific images (letters on a table, condensation words, a stone that “had tasted centuries of storms”). The final beats—spirits singing a cradle song, the shard’s pulse quieting, Lysa carrying “not absolution, but a fragile map”—offer a nuanced, non-literal answer about what healing can and cannot be outsourced. There are no POV slips or mechanical issues, though the uniform lyric elevation risks slight sameness of register across calm and high-pressure moments.</comment>
