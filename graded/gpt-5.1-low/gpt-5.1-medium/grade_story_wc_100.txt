<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—answering a massed distress call to restore “rest” to incomplete lives—is clear and grows from personal weariness into metaphysical responsibility. Elias faces no direct threat or hard trade-off beyond taking on more of what’s already breaking him, which is thematically apt but dramatically muted. The conflict functions well for a lyric piece, yet lacks a decisive on-page test of his limits that would sharpen cost.</comment>
