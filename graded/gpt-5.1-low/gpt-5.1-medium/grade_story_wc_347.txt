The arrival of the last traveler searching for the blueprint is an excellent late complication that reframes the conflict. Because the decisive response to him is deferred, the piece stops at a heightened question rather than a paid-off moral or practical cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Theme coheres around fate versus agency, memory versus erasure, and the ethics of guiding others through a lethal system you only partly oppose. The ending’s idea that “the blueprint wanted to be remembered” reprices the entire premise in a satisfying way. The only restraint is that the narrator’s stance toward this new knowledge is left gestural rather than deeply worked through in scene.</comment>
