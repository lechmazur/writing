There’s no orientation debt: who/where/when are legible early, and the one-scene flow is coherent. The cost is quieter and more conceptual than dramatic, but it’s present and on the page.</comment>

Details feel filtered through an artist’s eye, with consistent attention to color and light. The setting is not just backdrop but an active emotional and conceptual frame.</comment>

That’s workable for an accumulative lyric piece, but there’s little true escalation or on-page test of commitment. As a result, tension is gentle and the piece leans more toward mood than conflict.</comment>
