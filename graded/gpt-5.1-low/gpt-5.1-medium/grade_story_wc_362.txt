Cost is present but mostly conceptual—the narrator’s embrace of “truthful lies” over strict empiricism and the town’s impending confrontation with its own emotions. The environment doesn’t just decorate; it provides the very medium of communication (frost, quartz, hush) and charges the final thematic claim about objects absorbing emotion. The only reason this doesn’t nudge higher is that setting never directly constrains a concrete, high-cost decision on the page.</comment>

As a result, the pressure is largely atmospheric rather than narrowing options or exacting an explicit price in action.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Theme is beautifully integrated: objects as repositories of refused emotion, communal denial, and the ethics of translating those feelings into “truthful lies.” The story earns its closing line through concrete particulars: the frost patterns, the mirror heavy with unshed tears, the resentful wrench. There’s real lingering ambiguity about comfort versus violation in what the mystic does, which deepens the resonance.</comment>
