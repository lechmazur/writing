<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—understanding why her sister’s voice did not echo and whether the canyon judges worth—is evident early and carries through. The conflict is primarily internal and epistemic rather than choice-based; her key “decision” is to ask and to listen, so the escalation reads as deepening understanding rather than being driven by hard, alternative actions under duress. For this quiet mode, the price and narrowing of psychic options are clear but not razor-sharp.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Themes of memory, debt, and the fragility of human voices are woven through echoes, trade-route intrigue, and the half-shell, culminating in the reframing of echoes as debts the canyon carries rather than judgments it passes. A small weakness is the near-explicit statement of theme (“Her task was not to force the stone to answer…”) which edges toward thesis instead of fully trusting image and implication.</comment>
