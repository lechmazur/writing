The final image—starlings as constellations amid crystal flowers in a reversed sky/orchard—charges the environment with clear thematic weight. At times the lavish description approaches saturation but remains legible and purposeful.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elian will cling to control or risk aligning with something beyond his equations before the orchard’s deadline—is clear and tight. Pressure escalates from meticulous preparation through the failure of his charts to predict the flock, to the forced choice of trusting his heartbeat over his instruments. His agency at the pivot (closing his eyes, breathing with the birds, turning the lens) directly produces the transformative outcome. The cost is primarily internal and contemplative, not harrowing, which slightly softens the felt intensity for a “final transformation” setup.</comment>

The ending’s reframing—that the true transformation is of the gaze—nicely reweights all prior material. A couple of lines verge on explicit thesis-stating, but they’re still anchored enough in scene to avoid sermonizing.</comment>
