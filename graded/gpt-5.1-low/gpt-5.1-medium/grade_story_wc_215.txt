The world’s rules around ghosts and the beekeeping craft feel internally consistent and learned through attention, not exposition dumps.</comment>

The final decision to “keep it, not as prison, but as witness” forecloses the path of release or abandonment, with a felt cost of continued solitude and burden. However, the final sentence explicitly states this thesis in almost essayistic form, which blunts some of the earned subtlety and reads close to moralizing. Subtext elsewhere (e.g., the villagers binding themselves out of fear of speaking plainly) operates with more pleasing indirection.</comment>

The final conceptual turn—reframing the covenant as between silence and meaning, not living and dead—reweights the entire piece in a non-obvious but well-prepared way.
