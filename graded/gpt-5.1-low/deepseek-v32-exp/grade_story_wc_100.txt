<question>2</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: package → journey to the coast → descent → research → repair → transformed beacon and release of souls. The main weakness is convenience: the key method arrives via dream, and the research is summarized rather than creating concrete setbacks or choices on the page. Closure still lands with cost (time, risk, and relinquishment of his old role), but the middle lacks structural complexity.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The underwater city library and its surrounding seascape are vividly evoked—coral-encrusted spires, marble columns, warped books, the moonlit shaft and pocket of air—and feel specific rather than generic. Environment genuinely constrains choice: the submerged architecture, the air pocket, and the meeting of light and water are what make the repair possible. Sensory detail is purposeful and filtered through Elijah’s aim for rest and understanding. The setting strongly charges the final image of the reactivated lens and the quieted library.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—restoring the beacon so lost souls can find peace, aligning with Elijah’s life mission—is clear and coherent. Pressure escalates in a mild, linear way (journey, exploration, research, precise reconstruction), culminating in a single concentrated task in the impossible chamber. However, there is little active opposition or genuine risk beyond endurance, and he never seriously confronts the possibility of failure or sacrifice beyond effort and age. The dream-delivered solution undercuts potential tension, keeping the conflict functional but not gripping.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically from particulars: a man who has tried to give rest a permanent address finds a literal mechanism for doing so, using a broken artifact to complete a larger, almost spiritual duty. The piece flirts with over-explicitness in phrases like “rest had found its permanent address,” but the closing image of true stillness as home rather than escape carries genuine resonance. Moral or thematic complexity is more gentle than challenging, but it lingers.</comment>

Overall, it’s solid and coherent, if more competent than remarkable.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The synthesis of elements—a weary undertaker, a broken lighthouse lens that sends distress signals for souls, a forgotten underwater library, and an impossible light-water chamber—is fresh and conceptually strong. Structurally, the quest is straightforward and risk-averse, limiting how far the ingenuity travels into character and conflict. Still, the worldbuilding concept and closing reconfiguration are notably original in this length band.</comment>
