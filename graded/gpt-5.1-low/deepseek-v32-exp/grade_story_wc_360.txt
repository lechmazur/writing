The interiority is vivid and embodied, especially when multiple borrowed emotions collide. Closure lands as a conceptual repricing of her entire project: she reframes where the “mathematics” lie and alters her behavior by putting the fragment away. The cost—recognizing limits and giving up the dangerous experiment—is clear enough for this length, though it remains mostly internal.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The topaz workshop, open dome, and vanishing stars are vividly rendered and strongly tonal, and the halo’s light physically interacting with the stone adds specificity. The setting functions as a liminal laboratory whose changing light mirrors the progression from turbulence to clarity. While the environment doesn’t strongly constrain choices, the etched memory of the stars in the topaz at the end charges the final image. The world’s magical rules feel consistent and are shown through action rather than exposition.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—pursuing an abstract understanding at the risk of losing herself—is well signaled and coherently escalated through increasingly overwhelming empathy and personality layering. Each new imprint raises the internal cost, culminating in despair and the risky decision to let her essence flow into the fragment. The final renunciation (shelving the now inert halo) closes off a path and implies continuing cost: she accepts partial knowledge and the end of this method. Still, the threat level remains somewhat conceptual; a clearer sense of what she might irretrievably lose could sharpen the tension.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The piece is thematically focused on empathy, identity, and the limits of quantifying feeling, and these ideas arise organically from the mechanics of the press and halo. The closing assertion that “the mathematics were not in the feeling, but in the space between feelings” is a bit thesis-like but grows out of the prior imagery rather than arriving as a bolt-on moral. The result is resonant, if slightly more explicit than top-tier literary work often allows itself to be.</comment>

Psychic distance is well managed, moving smoothly between close interiority and broader atmospheric observation. There is no dialogue to test, but the narrative voice alone carries mood and meaning capably.</comment>

Overall, the prose supports rather than overwhelms the story’s pressure.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The fusion of letterpress mechanics, personality theft, mathematical metaphor, and a halo fragment in a topaz dome under vanishing stars feels strikingly specific. “Transposed empathy” as both method and danger, plus the idea of becoming a “living graph of fractured sentiments,” offers a non-obvious angle on familiar themes of identity and artistic obsession. The final conceptual turn—math in the spaces between feelings—reweights the entire experiment in a way that feels earned. This is high-level originality that could sit comfortably in a prestigious speculative-literary venue.</comment>
