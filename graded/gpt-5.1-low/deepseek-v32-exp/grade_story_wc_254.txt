Details are filtered lyrically through the poet’s sensibility, avoiding generic fantasy décor. The main limitation is that the environment mostly reflects theme rather than constraining choices, which keeps it just shy of top-tier.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether he can and will cross a once-sacred boundary—is clear but never feels genuinely risky on the page. Pressure rises mildly as he approaches the colonnade and as the humming intensifies, and the act of paraphrasing old laws is an intriguing form of agency. However, there’s no on-page opposition, consequence, or real doubt beyond a “tremor of certainty,” so escalation is more atmospheric than felt. The crossing works conceptually but lacks a visible cost or trade-off, so the conflict reads as largely pre-solved.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.6</grade><comment>Themes of change vs. permanence, fear-based prohibition, and beauty in dissolving limits are coherent and threaded through recurring images of boundaries, ice, and glass. The thematic arc resonates, but it feels more proclaimed than discovered through nuanced conflict.</comment>

There’s no POV slippage or explanatory intrusion, which keeps the voice clean and publishable with light tightening.</comment>
