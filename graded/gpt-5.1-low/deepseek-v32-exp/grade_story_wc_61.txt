However, the environment rarely constrains concrete tactics beyond “no fire,” so its role is more atmospheric and thematic than mechanical. Still, it strongly charges the final image of a remembered, vanished place.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preserving some form of the garden against inevitable decline—is clear and tightens with the discovery of systemic generational weakening. The choice to repurpose sonar is a meaningful narrowing of options with a visible conceptual cost (accepting loss), but the conflict remains largely contemplative and low-friction. There’s little sense of risk or competing harms beyond that single binary, which keeps the tension from feeling fully acute.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Themes of generational decline, imperfect infinity, and non-destructive legacy are tightly woven through objects (gear), methods (sonar), and repeated images (petals, light). Even so, the emotional and conceptual aftereffect feels earned and lingers.</comment>
