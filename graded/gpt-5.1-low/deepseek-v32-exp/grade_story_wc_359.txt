<question>3</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The ice cavern, refracted light, and aurora are vividly rendered and tightly woven into the premise of prism-songs and visible sound. The environment supports theme and mood more than it constrains choice, but it is far from postcard description; it feels like a necessary medium for the event. Sensory specificity is strong and consistently filtered through the story’s synesthetic logic.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—liberating wonder to prove its essential nature—is declared, but it remains largely abstract and untested on the page. There is anticipation and a mild sense of “will it work now,” yet no genuine resistance, competing demand, or risk of failure; the aurora arrives, the chisel strikes, and the plan succeeds seamlessly. Escalation is mostly tonal (the hush “deepens”) rather than driven by narrowing options or sharpened costs. As a result, the climax feels beautiful but frictionless.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.0</grade><comment>The theme that awe and wonder are essential is coherent but over-articulated, often stated outright (“to certify wonder as essential,” “proving that awe could heal and inspire”) rather than allowed to emerge solely from the images. However, the explicit moralizing intrudes, flattening complexity and reducing ambiguity. The ending resonates conceptually but edges toward sermon rather than earned discovery.</comment>

At times, the elevated register leans toward uniform solemnity, which slightly blunts modulation under peak pressure. Still, focalization remains clean and there is no head-hopping or confusing stance shifts.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The line-level writing is polished and grammatically clean, with a strong rhythmic sense and some striking images (“a rainbow imprisoned and yearning for freedom”). Figurative language appears densely and sometimes redundantly without always adding new facets, which slows momentum. Despite this, clarity is maintained and the closure image lands intact.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The conceptual constellation—prism-song bells, an amber music sheet, “sculpted hush,” synesthetic perception, and a synchronous distant sculptor—is fresh and memorable. What holds it below the very top band is that the emotional and conflict skeleton (pure idealism effortlessly triumphant) is familiar, so the human stakes don’t match the inventiveness of the imagery. Nonetheless, the synthesis of elements is distinctive and cohesive.</comment>
