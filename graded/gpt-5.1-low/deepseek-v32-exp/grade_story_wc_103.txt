Still, the setting clearly shapes the final image and is tightly interwoven with the story’s concept.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The main stake—escaping the retrocognitive prison—is well signaled, and the disintegration of the bridge is a visible turning point. Her internal conflict (desire to understand time vs. the pain of being trapped in it) is gestured at but not sharply tested in the climax. <question>5</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>Thematic throughline—time as texture/tapestry, transformation rather than escape, memory and decay—is consistent and supported by recurring images (bridges, photographs, weaving). This veers toward gentle sermonizing and reduces ambiguity and aftertaste. The resonance is pleasant but a bit too neatly articulated for elite literary work.</comment>

At the same time, the diction often feels generically poetic rather than idiosyncratically Elara’s, and there’s some repetition of favored abstractions (“texture of time,” “woven”) without fresh modulation.
