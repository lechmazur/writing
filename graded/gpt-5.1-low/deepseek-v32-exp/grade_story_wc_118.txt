<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of memory, displacement, and the salvaging of a collective consciousness are coherent and carried by recurring images (walls as archives, the tapestry as map, dreams as severed axis). Yet key insights are told in thesis-like statements (“He understood that…” / “To collect the stories walls have heard was to reassemble a broken consciousness”), which blunts subtext and edges toward didacticism. More of this wisdom arising from dramatized struggle, less from explanation, would raise the score.</comment>
