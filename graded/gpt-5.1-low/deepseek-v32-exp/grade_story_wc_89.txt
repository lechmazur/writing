The ritual’s spatial and sensory staging—moss altar, spices from the world’s corners—anchors the magic in concrete environment. While the orchard doesn’t strongly constrain tactics, it functions as a coherent, atmospheric micro-world that supports theme and closure.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The central stake—whether Elara will sacrifice her old self to reclaim her bond with Kael—is legible and resolved, with metamorphosis as the key pressure point. Her internal reluctance appears but is quickly overridden without real on-page struggle or alternative options tested. As a result, the climax feels moving but low in tension for a piece aiming at elite literary stakes.</comment>

This flattens complexity in the closing paragraphs, where more ambiguity or unsaid cost could deepen resonance.</comment>
