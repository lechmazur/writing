<question>3</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The lunar observatory above the clouds is vividly and economically sketched—airlessness, crystalline dome, lunar clouds, tremors—and clearly shapes tactics (needing a recorded leaf sound because no air, instruments threatened by quakes). Environmental shifts (quivering foundations that later still) mirror and influence the narrative arc without devolving into touristic description. The one limitation is that sensory range is heavily visual and conceptual, with relatively little tactile or bodily specificity under stress.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—capturing the ephemeral solar flare before tremors or time’s normal flow make it impossible—is clearly signaled and sustained. However, the opposition never seriously adapts or complicates her path; her solution works on the first try, and the external danger (tremors) ceases immediately after success in a way that feels convenient rather than hard-won. The emotional cost is hinted but the practical cost or real risk never quite bites.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Themes of harnessing the ephemeral, the tension between chaos and constancy, and the ache of beauty’s transience are coherent and clearly emerge from the interplay of flare, clock, leaf, and Elara’s vocation. The resonance is genuine but a bit over-explicit and thematically tidy for top-tier literary venues.</comment>

Figurative language remains coherent and non-muddled, but a slight tendency toward aphoristic phrasing and redundancy keeps this out of the very top band.</comment>
