<question>2</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The circular structure (library → origami passage → refuge → return) is clean and legible, with a clear before/after stance that qualifies as a resonant reconfiguration. The closure cost, however, is soft and mostly implied—weariness and the burden of endless witnessing—so the final re-seeing of decay lacks a sharper hinge.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The overgrown library, subterranean river, and obsidian refuge are vividly evoked and tonally coherent, with moss, dust, and black reflective walls building a strong atmosphere. Yet these environments rarely impose concrete constraints or alter tactics; they function more as mood and metaphor than as active pressures.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the keeper can sustain their role without being crushed by accumulated decay—is present but largely conceptual. The only escalation is the initial restlessness and the decision to seek refuge; the journey itself is frictionless, without real obstacle, trade-off, or narrowed options. As a result, the ending reads as a gentle deepening rather than a pressured stance crystallized at visible cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>Theme (impermanence, regenerative silence, nonjudgmental witnessing) is tightly woven through recurrent images of decay and renewal and culminates in the keeper’s reframed perception of collapse. The resonance is genuine but a bit over-declared.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Line-level craft is clean and mostly concise, with clear syntax, few if any grammatical slips, and sentences that reliably advance mood or idea. Nonetheless, there is noticeable repetition of concepts and phrasing without added pressure, and abstract noun strings occasionally replace more specific or surprising turns of phrase, modestly dulling momentum.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The combination of a chronicle keeper in catacomb libraries, an origami swan as portal, and a refuge of “regenerative silence” along an underground river feels fresh and internally coherent.
