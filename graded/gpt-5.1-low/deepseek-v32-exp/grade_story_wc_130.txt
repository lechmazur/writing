<question>4</question><grade>6.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elian can transform his grief-driven quest for a “math of emotion” into something that yields meaning—is present, but conflict is largely internal and low-friction. The key tightening is his realization that integration, not solution, is the goal, yet this turn doesn’t force him to relinquish something concrete on the page. As a result, tension feels contemplative rather than truly pressured.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>Themes of ambiguous loss, integration of grief, and beauty in imperfection are coherent and well-linked to mosaics, orchard rhythms, and Elian’s methods. The Lyra-image and the chipped mosaic could have carried more of this weight by implication alone. The ending is emotionally honest but verges on tidy reassurance instead of leaving a more complex, lingering aftertaste.</comment>
