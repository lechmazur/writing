<question>2</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The structure is clean: the looming silence, the building of the resonant field, and the climactic wave all follow a clear causal chain under a hybrid causal/accumulative lens. The main weakness is that the success feels nearly effortless; no prior tactic fails or even strains, and the cost of victory is minimal. Still, for this length, the single tightening and decisive act at the end are coherent and satisfying.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The floating citrus orchard on lashed driftwood is vivid, specific, and consistently used: the trees, roots, shells, and sea-glass resonator all participate in the music and in the wave-deflection. Environment shapes action—there is literally nowhere else to go, so he must work with sky, sea, and orchard as instruments. The weather (clouds, wave) and time of day (“blue hour”) tonally infuse the piece and evolve rather than sit as backdrop. This is rich, well-integrated setting rather than postcard description.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.3</grade><comment>The stakes—protecting the orchard and answering the predicted silence—are clear and the conflict escalates from abstract (silence) to concrete (approaching wave). The decisive turn is his choice to strike the resonator with full strength to meet the wave, which pays off in a transformed threat. However, he is never seriously in danger of failing; he reads as fully in control of both method and outcome, so the cost and tension feel muted. The conflict is elegant but relatively safe.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The theme of transforming threat and absence into art is strongly embodied in the silence turned into latticework sound and the wave converted into a “carillon of rain.” The piece resists overt moralizing; the final note that “his work was never done” suggests an ongoing, uneasy coexistence with danger rather than triumphalism. Still, the prophet’s near-flawless mastery keeps the thematic field from exploring deeper ambiguity or failure—there’s little sense of what it would mean to be broken by an obstacle. The imagery nonetheless leaves a resonant aftertaste about creation through adversity.</comment>

There is tight control of focalization—no head-hopping, no explanatory intrusions that break the spell. At times the elevated register risks uniformity, but it largely feels intentional and character-appropriate.</comment>
