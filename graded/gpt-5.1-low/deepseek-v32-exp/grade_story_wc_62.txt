<question>2</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The structure is linear and easy to follow, with a clear drive from entering the greenhouse to achieving an understanding of grief, and the accumulative lens (ritual over days) is coherent. Escalation across the days of migration is more stated than dramatized; few beats hinge on concrete turning points. The piece sometimes reads like a paraphrased outline of a longer story rather than a fully pressured scene.</comment>

<question>3</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The ancient greenhouse, broken glass, ivy, and monarch migration create a vivid, consistent atmosphere that supports the lyric, meditative mode. Details like the “cathedral” feel of the crumbling structure and the butterflies’ “final dance” tie setting to theme in a satisfying way. However, the environment rarely constrains choices or tactics; it is beautifully scenic but mostly passive. The greenhouse and migration charge the mood and imagery more than they generate situational pressure.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>5.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—understanding the shape of collective grief—is clear, but it encounters almost no meaningful resistance. Elara never faces a sharp dilemma, reversal, or genuine risk of failure; each step merely deepens the same trajectory toward insight. “Over days, as the migration peaked” suggests scope for tightening, yet nothing actually forces a hard trade-off or renunciation. As a result, the final peace feels unopposed and low-cost, limiting dramatic tension.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>6.0</grade><comment>The themes of grief as connective, cyclical, and transformative are coherent and well-matched to butterflies, ribbons, and greenhouse imagery. The story leans into a tidy, comforting moral rather than letting images and actions carry the weight. The emotional aftereffect is gentle but somewhat sentimental and didactic at this standard.</comment>

<question>6</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The third-person limited voice is stable and competent, with a consistent lyrical register that suits the contemplative material. There is little modulation in distance or rhythm as stakes rise, which keeps the voice from feeling truly alive under pressure. Still, there is no head-hopping or major stance confusion, and the tone remains controlled.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>6.7</grade><comment>The prose is clean and mostly clear, with a smooth rhythm and a few strong images (the ghost “shimmering like heat haze,” monarchs as “airborne dance”). Figurative language clusters (“network,” “woven,” “fabric”) are conceptually aligned but occasionally feel overexplained. Line-level craft is solid but not sharp enough for immediate top-tier publication.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The combination of a gravestone carver, spectral mentor-grandfather, woven ribbon map of the dead, and butterfly migration is genuinely appealing and gives the piece a distinct aesthetic. The story shows imagination, but it doesn’t take the thematic or structural risks that would make it feel truly singular at a prestigious level.</comment>
