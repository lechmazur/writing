<question>2</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>Orientation is clean and the accumulative structure is coherent: ghosts appear, purpose is inferred, the box is used, the covenant is renewed, and the ghosts depart. The setting is more backdrop than constraint—nothing in the physical environment forces particular tactics beyond supplying the relic. Still, tone and world rules remain consistent and contribute meaningfully to the final image.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The central stake—the preservation or loss of the covenant and its unspoken words—is clear but remains abstract and largely unopposed. Escalation is modest: the realization that the covenant is failing and the act of opening the box are the main pressure points, but they unfold without friction or real risk. The protagonist’s agency resolves things cleanly, with no visible price or foreclosed path; his success seems assured as soon as he decides to engage. As a result, tension never tightens beyond a gentle contemplative hum.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.0</grade><comment>There is a coherent thematic throughline about silence, memory, and the sustaining power of unspoken promises, reinforced by the bees/ghosts/covenant triad. Subtext exists in the ghosts’ “mute eloquence” and the beekeeping rituals, but it is often explained instead of trusted. The ending offers a gentle, contemplative answer, yet it edges toward tidy rather than complex resonance.</comment>

Repeated phrases like “through contextual clues” and “mute eloquence” give a slight templated feel but also reinforce the contemplative mode. The narrative tends to explain interior states in an essayistic register rather than letting the recluse’s perceptions fully individualize the voice. Even so, point of view is consistent, distance control is clean, and the overall texture is controlled and legible.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The prose is generally clear, grammatical, and rhythmically steady, with some strong sensory moments (“scent of honey and dust,” “drifting through the dry air like pale smoke”). Several sentences flatten momentum by telling what could be shown, especially in thematic statements and labels. Still, there are no major figurative muddles, and most lines do at least one piece of work for story or atmosphere.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The combination of an abandoned beekeeping oasis, visible ghosts, a stained glass box, and a covenant of unspoken words is a fresh and appealing synthesis. However, the arc—a solitary caretaker gently restoring a broken spiritual order and finding peace—is familiar in its emotional contour and resolves with minimal surprise. The piece feels thoughtfully conceived rather than generic, but it stops short of a truly startling or transformative reimagining.</comment>
