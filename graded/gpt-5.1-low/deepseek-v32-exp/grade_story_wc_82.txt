<question>2</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The causal spine—shadow loss → vow to fold cranes and polish dreams → intensifying work → cranes stir → shadow returns—reads cleanly and closure lands on the page with a visible change. The cost of the process (painful emotional excavation) is present, though escalation between beats can feel a bit uniform rather than sharply turning. Overall, the piece satisfies a hybrid causal/accumulative design with a solid, if not breathtaking, ending.</comment>

The final image of the glowing basilica and stirred cranes meaningfully recharges earlier environmental imagery. The worldbuilding is lush yet legible, though the environment rarely constrains concrete choices beyond mood and metaphor.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—transmuting grief and reattaching the shadow—is clear early and persists. Pressure accumulates via repeated polishing and folding, with “descending while ascending” and intensifying luminous pain marking a deepening rather than a flat loop. However, the opposition is almost entirely internal and ritualized, with little sense that failure is a live option or that she adapts tactics under duress. The cranes’ awakening and the shadow’s hesitation add some late-stage tension, but the resolution feels expected once the ritual premise is established.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Theme coheres around grief transformed into art, the cost of clarity, and reintegration of the disowned self (shadow). The piece avoids outright moralizing, though the neatly successful ritual (final crane → immediate shadow return) edges toward a tidy allegory of healing. Some additional ambiguity or unresolved cost could lift the thematic complexity further.</comment>

Some lines—“This luminous torment defined her existence,” for instance—lean on abstracted summary rather than sharp, character-specific language, softening peak intensity. Overall, the voice is controlled and coherent but not yet distinctive enough for top-tier literary venues.</comment>

Syntax and rhythm vary enough to keep momentum, and there are no major grammatical or structural slips. Tightening redundancy and trusting concrete moments over conceptual phrases would markedly elevate the prose.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The synthesis of a floating tea-garden island, a glass basilica that archives dreams, gem-polishing as narrative alchemy, and grief folded into origami cranes feels strikingly fresh. While the emotional arc of grieving-through-art is familiar, the specific fantastical apparatus and imagery render it memorable. With slightly more risk in how the ritual resolves—or how success complicates Elara—the piece could edge toward truly distinctive territory.</comment>
