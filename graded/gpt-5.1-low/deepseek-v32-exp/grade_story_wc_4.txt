There’s little escalation beyond “more people come” and feel better, so options are never sharply narrowed nor is a painful trade-off dramatized. As a result, the ending is emotionally warm but low-tension.</comment>

Overall, the voice is pleasant and consistent but not yet singular or surprising enough for top-tier literary publication.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The prose is clean, grammatically sound, and uses specific, sensory images effectively (bees humming, metallic tang of decay, children playing in the dust). <question>8</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The juxtaposition of a lavender field and derelict factory, combined with an archivist arranging photographs in arcs to curate communal healing, offers a moderately fresh frame. Elara’s role as “subtle puzzle archivist” is intriguing but not exploited in any structurally inventive or psychologically surprising manner. The piece is thoughtful and coherent, but it does not take the kind of formal or emotional risks that would feel indispensable or truly original.</comment>
