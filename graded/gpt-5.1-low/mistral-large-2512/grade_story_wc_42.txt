<question>2</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: anomalous image → radical modification of the camera with the clock hand → revelation on the photograph → collapse and escape, with the photograph as the earned prize. The drive lens reads as primarily causal with a lyric overlay, and that lens holds consistently. Environmental shifts—the shaking foundations, the moon revealed through the clouds, the final fall of the observatory—track cleanly and mirror the story’s thematic arc. There’s room to deepen tactile engagement with the space under stress, but what’s here is purposeful and effective.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—capturing and preserving the impossible phenomenon before the station fails—is clearly signaled and sustains a gentle but real pressure. However, the pressure rarely forces a painful trade-off; he completes the experiment, gets the photograph, and then leaves once that goal is safely achieved, so no option feels truly foreclosed at a cost he actively weighs. The conflict thus feels elegant but somewhat under-tested for this level of ambition.</comment>

Overall, the piece leaves a distinct conceptual and emotional aftertaste without over-explaining.</comment>
