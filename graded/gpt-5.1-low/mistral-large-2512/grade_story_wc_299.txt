<question>8</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The premise—an ex-roadie haunted by past failure, working in a quarry and hearing ghost-music—is a fresh enough angle on “after the fall” narratives, with some nice specificity in profession and setting. The emotional arc (shame → muted openness to the future) is honest but follows a familiar template, so the story feels well-executed rather than formally or conceptually daring.</comment>
