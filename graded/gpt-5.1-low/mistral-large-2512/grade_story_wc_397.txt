Overall specificity is high and well-filtered through Elias’s preoccupation with endings.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Elias’s relationship to death and whether he remains a detached tourist—emerges early and tightens as he moves from signing up to trawl, to seeking the tents, to witnessing a death up close. Stakes stay mostly internal and existential, with little external obstruction, so the pressure is more atmospheric than confrontational. That quietness is coherent with the piece’s mode but keeps escalation from feeling truly acute.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Themes of commodified death, witnessing vs. participating, and finding solace in others’ endings are woven through action rather than lecture, culminating in Elias’s shift from seeking his own “sound of ending” to serving those “still waiting.” The parasol tip and the recurring language of wind and breath accrue meaning and reweight the final lines. The ending offers a resonant, non-literal answer: solace comes not from choreographing one’s own death as tourism but from entering the communal work of grief. Ambiguity around whether Elias will still seek his own euthanasia adds complexity without confusion.</comment>
