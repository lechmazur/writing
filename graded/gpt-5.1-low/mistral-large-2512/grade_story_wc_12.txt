The only limitation is that the anchor’s world-logic and constraints remain evocative rather than fully exploited to force specific tactical choices.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>The central pressure—fear of being possessed versus the drive to understand—is clearly signaled and does tighten as the ritual progresses and the anchor physically reacts. <question>5</question><grade>7.1</grade><comment>Themes of silence as canvas, sound as revelation, and fear versus understanding are well-woven into concrete acts (looping questions, scraping the palette, violet as truth-mark).
