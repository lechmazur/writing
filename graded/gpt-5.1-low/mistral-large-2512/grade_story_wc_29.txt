The campus outside, described as a powder keg, broadens the sense of atmosphere and stakes without muddling spatial clarity. More sensory texture or evolution of the environment during the climax could push this higher, but what’s here is purposeful and coherent.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—freeing the community’s perception from cosmic tricksters—is established early and tied to Mara’s need to act subtly. Pressure increases somewhat as she readies the generator and Voss challenges her, and the moment before she presses the button carries a modest charged pause. Yet there’s essentially one straight line of action with no reversal or meaningful setback: once she has the idea, nothing truly threatens to derail it, and Voss’s skepticism folds quickly. The price paid is mostly conceptual (“if you’re wrong, we’re lost”), with little dramatized cost or narrowing of options at the climax.</comment>

The ending image of smoothed charts and a community no longer on the brink supports a nuanced hope without full resolution. Overall, the piece hints at contemporary resonances (misinformation, factionalization) without over-explaining them, but the thematic texture could be more surprising or morally tangled.</comment>
