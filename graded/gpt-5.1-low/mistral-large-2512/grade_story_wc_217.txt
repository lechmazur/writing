The recurring feather-water-light triad also charges the final image of her writing by the lagoon.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The central stake—whether Mira will remain locked in grief or find a way to metabolize it through her art—is clear and gently escalates via the snapped string and the encounter with the woven dawns. Pressure is internal rather than dramatic, but the story does mark a tightening when she chooses to enter the water and then to treat the feather as a quill. The cost (continuing to feel grief while refusing numbness) is implied rather than sharply dramatized, which keeps tension solid but not searing.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>Thematic throughline—beauty in incompleteness, healing as harmonizing discord rather than mending—is consistent and materially arises from the feather, the snapped string, and the lagoon’s fractured reflections. However, several lines (“Beauty lived in the incomplete…”, “even broken things could sing”) slide toward explicit thesis, slightly dulling the lingering ambiguity an elite venue would favor.</comment>

Rhythm and syntax modulate well between long, lush sentences and shorter, cutting beats like “Mira flinched, then exhaled.” There is no POV leakage or register drift; the elevated diction feels organic to this contemplative, poetic focalizer.</comment>
