Internal and external pressures (priests, world after the shadow’s detachment, her brother’s memory) are evocative yet underexploited as active conflict. The brother’s death, the priests’ theories, and the detached shadow all echo questions about what remains after something essential is torn away. Some added ambiguity around her complicity or responsibility at the end could deepen the thematic bite further.</comment>
