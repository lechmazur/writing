<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—saving the child while uncovering what’s happening to time—is clear and escalates as the boy worsens and the ink entity manifests. <question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of stolen time, crisis-induced temporal distortion, and exploitation of the vulnerable are woven through concrete images (disappearing ink, the message “They’re taking the time,” the post-eclipse unease). However, the story stops short of a deeper moral or emotional complication—Elias doesn’t, for example, confront his complicity, limits, or a genuine dilemma. The result is thoughtful and suggestive but not yet as complex or cutting as top-tier literary work.</comment>
