<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether to expose dangerous truths to protect fragile, culturally vital memory before the monsoon steals more—is well signaled. However, pressure stays mostly internal and abstract; no one else or no concrete circumstance forces a painful trade-off or visible sacrifice on the page. The decision to plant and later to “tell them, not all of it” is thematically apt but feels comparatively low-friction for the scale of the implied stakes.</comment>

Even so, the final choice to reveal “not all of it” gives a resonant, non-didactic answer about curated truth.</comment>
