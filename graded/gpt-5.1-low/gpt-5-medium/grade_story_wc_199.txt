Closure lands in the recognition that the real transformation is in the listener and that her role is to “keep ears growing,” which reweights everything before it. The final images (descending with mossy knees under the ashen sky, valley windows unaware) are strongly charged by the environment. It’s superbly handled; the only minor limit is that the setting constrains mood and ritual more than it forces hard tactical trade-offs.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—properly decoding and grounding the arbor’s message so the world is “steadied”—is clear, and the hush provides a temporal window of pressure. However, resistance, risk, or competing options are minimal; she accepts and executes the instruction without significant friction. The result is a beautiful but relatively low-conflict arc with limited sense of price paid.</comment>

The notion that her work “steadies the world” by giving memory places to “sit and breathe” is powerfully implied through concrete action. The only small drawback is that a couple of lines in the final third edge close to explicit thesis statements, though they remain grounded in image.</comment>

There’s a strong perceptual signature in how the narrator notices craft, texture, and folklore over generic emotion words.
