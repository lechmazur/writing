The pilgrim’s arc from “perpetually surprised” to steadier reader of the final message is subtle but present. Causality is more conceptual than event-driven but holds consistently under that lens, with no orientation debt. This is elite-level integration of environment with character and theme.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—finding or tuning a sense of belonging—appears early and is consistently pressured by the reappearance of “missing pieces” in maps and printouts. There is a clear deepening when the blanks are reinterpreted from errors to rests and the algorithm is adjusted, which narrows options conceptually and unlocks the final inscription. However, the conflict remains mostly contemplative; there’s little sense that failure would cost them anything beyond continuing alienation, and no moment where a genuine alternative path is foreclosed on the page. The escalation is effective for lyric fiction but stops short of a truly bracing test.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Themes of belonging, absence, and attention are woven through concrete motifs—missing tiles, blank sorts, exiled mosaic pieces, breath as letter—so the conceptual insights feel grounded rather than abstract. Some lines verge on direct thesis-stating and compress the moral into aphorism, which slightly blunts the subtextual power. Still, the piece leaves a strong afterimage of unfinished people finding shelter in attuned silence.</comment>

The voice could run in a top-tier venue with minimal or no line edits.</comment>

Nonetheless, clarity is maintained and there are no obvious loops, scaffolds, or syntactic artifacts dulling momentum.</comment>
