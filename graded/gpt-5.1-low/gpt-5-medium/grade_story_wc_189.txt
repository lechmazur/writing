Setting and object together are indispensable to the story’s logic and emotional resonance.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether to leave the coast for an inland life with the beloved—is clear early (“You were asking whether I would go inland, away from the tide”) and tightens through the quiet but escalating inner projections of possible futures. The decisive “Yes” forecloses real options with visible cost, and the relationship’s tenor shifts from uncertainty to shared passage. The tension stays gently modulated; an added on-page snag or sharper fear might have yielded an even more acute sense of risk.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>9.0</grade><comment>Themes of transition, thresholds, and the “texture of time” are woven through concrete motifs (tides, cicada shell seams, star-fed hinges) rather than delivered as abstract thesis. Subtext about how love and vocation can be reconciled by reimagining boundaries lingers past the last line. A few explicit formulations (“My journals call such crossings transition periods”) flirt with didacticism but remain grounded in character and craft.</comment>

Despite the high lyric density, clarity of action and stakes never blurs, which is unusual at this register.</comment>
