<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The thematic architecture—performance vs authenticity, happiness as ballast vs grief as lightness, state appropriation of emotion vs unmeasured hush—is exceptionally cohesive and arises from concrete images rather than thesis statements. The story resists simple moralizing; it acknowledges complicity and material need (tokens, permits) alongside quiet rebellion. <question>6</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The first-person voice is precise, idiosyncratic, and consistent, blending bureaucratic jargon (“happiness mandate,” “Gratitude Transition”) with lush, metaphor-forward perception in a way that feels character-true to a professional performer. Psychic distance is well controlled; we stay inside this mind without intrusive explanation, and the brief bits of dialogue (inspector, child) carry distinct registers and subtext. The only small limitation is that the high lyric register is nearly unbroken, leaving little modulation into rougher or more fractured rhythms under peak stress.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The line-level work is densely figurative yet remarkably clean: metaphors are fresh, coherent, and rarely feel strained (“brittle as paper breath,” “narrated like apocalypse for a spoon”). Repetition patterns (“Behold,” turbines, hush) are deliberately patterned rather than looping, and there’s minimal scaffolding or synonym-churn. The only slight drag is that the unrelenting metaphorical richness risks blurring a few beats emotionally; judicious pruning at one or two moments could sharpen impact even further.</comment>
