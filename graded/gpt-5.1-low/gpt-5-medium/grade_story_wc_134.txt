The cave and pearl together constrain choice and charge the final image of the cloak-like dusk. A slight density of metaphor edges toward luxuriant, but clarity and utility of setting remain intact.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—who will be chosen by the pearl, and what that means for the village and the guide’s role—is clear and threads the scene. However, tension stays gentle and contemplative rather than truly pressured; the loss of authority and the ethical question of taking vs. sharing are not pushed into sharper conflict. The resolution feels earned but a bit low-intensity for elite-level stakes.</comment>

The cave pearl’s power to gather small wonders and return them magnified, and the redefinition of darkness as “the color of listening,” deepen the conceptual frame without feeling like a grafted moral. There is a slight risk of over-articulation, but resonance outweighs didacticism.</comment>
