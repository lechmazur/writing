Causality is coherent and motivation-driven even in a lyric mode, with no orientation debt. Setting and tools (broom, plastic sheet, hand lens) constrain choices and directly charge the final images. The environment’s physical state mirrors and informs her intellectual stance without feeling like mere allegory. A minor further tightening could be to let one environmental constraint force a specific hard choice rather than only framing her contemplative method.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how Mara will respond to loss and recalibrate her relationship to work and disaster—is clear and coherently held. Pressure “escalates” texturally rather than causally, through accumulating observations and the arrival of neighbors shifting her from solitary contemplation to communal, methodical action. However, conflict remains very low-key: no option is truly foreclosed, and her new stance faces little resistance or real risk. The result is thematically rich but somewhat low-tension for an elite market.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The thematic weave between spectacle vs. patience, drama vs. humility, and gradual change is strong and organically mirrored between stars and greenhouse. However, several explicit thesis statements (“recovery is not a rescue but a rehearsal”; “to preprocess reality without the bias for explosions”) verge on essayistic moralizing, slightly undercutting subtext. Letting more of this wisdom stay in image and action would lift the work into a higher tier.</comment>

The narrative distance stays stable and character-bound, avoiding omniscient drift or head-hopping.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The prose is dense yet largely precise, with many lines doing double duty for atmosphere and theme (“the compost barrels hummed an earthy arithmetic of survival” is representative). A few sentences verge on over-packed abstraction, but they do not materially slow momentum or blur sense.</comment>

The choice to keep the story quiet and accumulative is a meaningful risk that mostly pays off.
