<question>2</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The structure is a clean accumulative arc: arrival on the bluff → encounter with Mirelle → walk through the election → weather’s “restraint over spectacle” → conceptual revaluation at the end. Closure is strong as a Conceptual Repricing: the compass’s “north” becomes “us,” and the skeptic commits to being carried by the story when “the taste of alone returns.” Stakes stay mostly conceptual, with little sense of what would concretely be lost by refusal, which keeps this just below top-tier.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The setting is lush, specific, and integrated: “lavender bluff under aurora,” forests holding elections with glowing spore-ballots, and weatherbearers gathered like seated constellations all feel lived-in and strange in a coherent way. Environment and micro-objects (ring, sensors, spores, aurora) don’t just decorate; they help define the rules of the world and charge the final images of quorum and return. The bluff and valley frame the action and reappear at closure, giving spatial and emotional symmetry. A small limitation is that physical constraints of place (terrain, distance, danger) don’t much pressure choices beyond mood and spectacle.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake is the narrator’s stance toward the weatherbearers’ logic—will they remain a remote skeptic or step into the quorum. Pressure does deepen when Mirelle invites them to walk during the “final count,” and the internal barometer of tastes (citrus, ash, honey) registers risk to memory and companionship. However, conflict remains very low-friction; the narrator never seriously considers refusing or challenging Mirelle, so escalation feels more like continuous revelation than a tightening vise. The final choice carries conceptual weight but only a softly implied cost, keeping tension strong-but-not-bracing.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>Themes of more-than-human democracy, stewardship, and the reconciliation of skepticism with belonging emerge organically from concrete images and dialogue (“Weather lives where agreements fail,” “so the sky can remember it belongs to the ground”). The metaphor of elections—forests voting, storms counted for restraint—beautifully reweights both weather and political process by the end. The closing stance (“I will carry your story… when the taste of alone returns”) gives a resonant, non-didactic answer to the opening question about why people carry weather. A few aphoristic lines skate close to thesis-statement territory, but they’re voiced in-character enough to avoid sermonizing.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The first-person voice is distinctive, with a strong perceptual signature built around synesthetic taste and speculative-tech language (“coat full of sensors,” “history had turned into circuitry”). Psychic distance is controlled; the narration stays inside this one consciousness without leakage or cheat-omniscience. The only mild drawback is that the density of lyricism stays high throughout, with little modulation toward plainer phrasing at moments of peak pressure.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The line-level craft is elegant and information-dense: most sentences do multiple jobs of worldbuilding, characterizing, and thematizing at once (“She climbed the path as if gravity were reciting her biography”). The extended metaphoric texture is mostly coherent and earned, with few if any mixed images that break sense. At times the unrelenting figuration edges toward overrichness, slightly blurring impact and keeping it just shy of the very top band.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The synthesis of elements—forest elections, a taste-based emergent compass, weather as carried quorum, “futuristic nostalgia”—feels fresh and particular rather than like a collage of familiar tropes. Almost every speculative choice deepens character and theme rather than serving as quirky ornament. Its main limitation is that the underlying emotional arc (skeptic to believer/participant) is familiar, even if expressed through a strikingly original lens.</comment>
