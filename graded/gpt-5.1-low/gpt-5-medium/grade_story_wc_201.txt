No orientation debt or lens slippage undermines the ending.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>9.2</grade><comment>The ultraviolet forest is vivid, specific, and fully integrated: its behavior (equations on leaves, flaring with remorse, forming a path) both mirrors and shapes the protagonist’s process. Environmental rules stay consistent and directly constrain choices, especially in how the forest “accepts” the proposed limit and opens a way forward. The pewter strap and the body itself function as micro-settings that charge the final image. Description is sensorially dense without losing spatial or conceptual clarity.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she can define and commit to an ethical limit that prevents becoming an instrument of torture again—is established early and tightened through the forest’s unforgiving honesty. Pressure escalates as remorse intensifies (“my chest tightens and the forest flares”) and the equations begin extracting ratios of intention to harm, culminating in the decisive act of knotting the strap as a sworn boundary. The cost is moral and psychic rather than situationally dire, but it’s legible and scaled appropriately. The only slight limit is that external stakes (the valley torturers) remain mostly projected, so tension leans heavily inward.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>9.1</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically from image and action: the body as truth-teller, the danger of turning people into instruments, and mercy as an earlier, stricter boundary than cruelty admits. The paragraph declaring “ultraviolet penance is not punishment; it is calibration” flirts with explicit thesis but is so well-earned by prior scene work that it deepens rather than flattens. The final turn toward the valley with “a forest inside me that refuses to be silent” offers a resonant, non-literal answer without moralizing.</comment>

Risk-taking in style and concept heightens rather than obscures stakes.</comment>
