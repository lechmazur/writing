<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The frozen courtyard, plinth, and ice violin are vividly rendered and purpose-driven—weather, temperature, and surfaces all interact with the robot’s work and the violin’s behavior. The courtyard constrains and shapes choices (routes, what can be cleaned, how the violin behaves, what happens when the generator fails) and decisively charges the final image. <question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how the janitor will relate to second chances and the origin of thoughts—is clear but remains largely internal and philosophical. The clearest tightening is the recognition that the program amalgamates errors into meaning and the generator’s failure, yet these do not force a painful or narrow trade-off so much as an adjusted understanding. The cost of accepting repetitive practice is present but understated, softening the sense of hard conflict.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Theme is rich and well-integrated: second chances as practice, work done with care as a source of meaning, and thought as something that accumulates in the “sweep between sound and step.” Recurring images (broom bristles, loops, ice violin, storms and calendars) consistently deepen the question of where thoughts come from and what a “redo” really means. The ending’s reframing—that the program gives practice rather than forgiveness and that the janitor’s faith lies in mundane, looping work—offers a resonant, non-didactic answer. A few explicit abstractions (“That is my job, and my faith, and the mundane mysticism…”) brush close to naming the thesis outright, slightly reducing subtlety.</comment>

Psychic distance and temporal handling of the loops are confidently managed, with no head-hopping or stance drift. <question>7</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The line-level writing is elegant and information-dense; many sentences do double or triple duty in image, concept, and voice (“Meaning is the way the petals remember they were gears.”). There are minimal redundancies or scaffolding phrases, and rhythm varies pleasantly across the piece. At times the figurative layering approaches saturation, which risks numbing individual images, but it does not materially blur causality or closure.</comment>
