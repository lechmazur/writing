World rules around “reversible exile” and the mists stay consistent and are learned through action rather than exposition. Setting and atmosphere are not decorative; they’re inextricable from plot, mood, and theme.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The dominant stakes—liberating the exiles and reclaiming the ancestor’s intended refuge from a punitive system—are clear early and tighten well as the librarian moves from decoding to direct action and confrontation. The cost is more conceptual and systemic (defying authority, destabilizing a regime) than personally painful on the page, and the director’s power feels blunted quickly. Nevertheless, tension builds coherently and resolves through the protagonist’s agency, not coincidence.</comment>

The “librarianship… stewardship with better indexes” line flirts with overt thesis but is leavened by wit and embedded in action. The piece avoids tidy moralizing about “storms require sacrifices,” clearly rejecting the director’s stance while retaining complexity about cost and responsibility.</comment>
