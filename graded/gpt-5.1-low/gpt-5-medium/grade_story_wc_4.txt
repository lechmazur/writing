<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>Conflict is gentle but coherent: can the narrator orchestrate a ritual that helps a wounded town reconfigure its relationship to past harm? The stakes—collective emotional risk and the possibility of remaining stuck—are felt, especially in the mayor’s nervousness and the trembling “places we have not named.” However, escalation remains low-intensity and largely unopposed; there’s little sense that the process might fail or backfire, which trims the upper range.</comment>
