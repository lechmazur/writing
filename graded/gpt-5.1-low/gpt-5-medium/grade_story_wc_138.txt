World rules, though surreal, stay internally consistent and are revealed through action rather than exposition. The final image of aurora and hushed thunder clearly charged by prior environmental detail gives the piece strong atmospheric closure.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—forcing the universe to reveal what happens after goodbye, with Lio’s past farewell as crucible—is clear and present from early on. However, tension is primarily intellectual and lyric rather than viscerally fraught; the protagonists never feel in serious danger or forced into a wrenching trade-off. The final turn to shared purpose and softened resentment is earned but arrives with a relatively gentle sense of cost.</comment>

However, several lines state the thematic thesis almost didactically (“After goodbye, we learned…”, “goodbye is not a door closing; it is the blueprint of the next room”), softening the subtextual power. Despite this explicitness, the emotional and conceptual resonance of the final stance remains strong.</comment>

There is mild risk of over-saturation—nearly every sentence is metaphor-laden—but clarity survives and momentum rarely stalls. Emotional stakes—immortality’s relationship to endings and forgiveness—are not sacrificed to concept; they interlock well. Some tropes (immortal narrator, cursed amulet) are recognizable, but they’re sufficiently transformed by the language and conceptual frame to read as original rather than recycled.</comment>
