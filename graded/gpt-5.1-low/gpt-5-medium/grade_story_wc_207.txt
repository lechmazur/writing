<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she can face grief and uncertainty without forcing explanation—is signaled early and coherently deepened through the psychometry visions and familial reveal. <question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Themes of failure versus explanation, love as the hinge of awakening, and industry’s interference with deeper, quieter truths are woven through concrete motifs (hinges, hush, jars of scent, the factory’s drained aquifer). The explicit statement “That is the lesson the lagoon gives” edges toward aphorism, but it is anchored in the tactile scarf memory and prior emotional groundwork. Overall, the piece invites reflection on mystery, grief, and courage without collapsing into a simple moral.</comment>

Psychic distance is managed well: the prose can zoom out to reflective aphorism without losing the tactile present of jars, boards, and stone. A few slightly lofty turns of phrase mildly risk over-uniform elevation, but they remain plausible for a “sensibly eccentric” collector and do not blur the POV.</comment>

Trimming one or two metaphor stacks in the middle and late sections would sharpen impact to elite level.</comment>
