<question>7</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The line-level craft is consistently strong, with high information density—most sentences do double duty for atmosphere and character—and very few redundancies. Momentum holds despite reflection, as summary compresses history into charged images (“smudged star logs, tidal scratches, griefs written sideways”). The main limitation is sheer density: the sustained lyricism flirts with overload and could benefit from a few more moments of plainer texture to sharpen contrast.</comment>

The conceptual risk—running a highly lyrical, idea-forward piece that still lands concrete emotional stakes—mostly pays off without collapsing into abstraction. The story’s specific constellation of tile, tower, whales, and municipal doubts would be hard to transplant elsewhere without losing its identity.</comment>
