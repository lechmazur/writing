Recurring objects—the hunger stone, the lantern, the staircase spiral—charge the final image when the yoyo’s path mirrors the descent and is then reversed. <question>4</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether to step beyond fate’s design or remain bound to human and temporal constraints—is signaled early and steadily tightened. The final choice is a genuine trade-off between transcendence and relational/ethical responsibility, not a foregone conclusion. The only mild limitation is that external opposition is almost entirely metaphysical, but the internal conflict remains strong enough to carry the pressure.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.9</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically from action and image: control vs. surrender, hunger vs. care, and the costs of stepping outside prescribed time. The final refusal of the door in response to the keeper’s unembellished call reweights the whole piece toward chosen entanglement over abstract power, without stating a moral. Ambiguity remains—he has not erased his hunger, only chosen against it in this moment—giving the ending a resonant aftertaste.</comment>
