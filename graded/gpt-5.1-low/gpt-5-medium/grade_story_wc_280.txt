<question>3</question><grade>8.9</grade><comment>The setting and atmosphere are outstanding: the converted playhouse, hedge maze, mystic orchard, and mossy amphitheater all feel tactile, strange, and lived-in, filtered through the narrator’s synesthetic senses (“green cymbals,” “apples bright as cues”). <question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—tending a fractured timeline and helping the ghost seek encore without collapsing into despair—is clear and coherently maintained. Escalation arrives when the fracture becomes visible (“a crack across the air where another night falls through like confetti”) and the chorus bleeds through, prompting the ghost’s panic and request to “anchor the Star.” The narrator’s response with the coin and marquee letters is a real tightening choice that re-stabilizes the situation and slightly advances their shared project of letting go. However, the conflict stays emotionally low-intensity and nearly always under control; we rarely feel that failure is a live, dangerous option in the moment.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of nostalgia, performance, and the labor of letting go are woven organically through ritual, not exposition—e.g., “We are both caretakers…of a fractured timeline that keeps breaking on the rim of applause.” The orchard, coin, and ghost function as thematic machinery for how communities rehearse and re-stage their own past until it can be released. The ending’s “house light rising to guide everyone gently out” is a resonant, non-literal answer to the central question of what they’re doing each night. The piece stops just short of deeper moral complication (e.g., what it might cost to stop coming, or to finally end the encores), so the ambiguity is poignant but relatively safe.</comment>

Rhythm and diction are well controlled, with varied sentence lengths and a confident handling of repeated motifs (“echoes,” “applause,” “lantern fruit”). A few phrases flirt with over-lushness, but they remain legible and on-tone rather than showy for its own sake.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>Line-level craft is highly polished: nearly every sentence carries multiple jobs (world-building, mood, and character) without obvious scaffolding or redundancy. The only minor limitation is that such sustained lyricism flattens dynamic contrast slightly—there are few plain, sharp lines to cut against the richness when pressure peaks.</comment>
