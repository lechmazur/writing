<question>3</question><grade>9.0</grade><comment>The volcanic hot springs, bathhouse reconstruction, vault, and porous rock form an integrated, constraint-rich setting: the hottest pool is required for the ritual and the reconstruction context charges the final image of a new living scaffold. Environment actively shapes action (the vault under the hottest pool, the edge of the spring as ritual locus). The final image of tree-walkways under steam is powerfully charged by everything that came before.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether tradition will be fixed, imposed, or consciously evolved—is clear early and coherently pressed by the council’s insistence on waiting versus Lida’s urgency. Escalation is primarily ethical and situational rather than peril-based; the council’s refusal forces Lida into open transgression, which is a meaningful tightening of options. The main limitation is that opposition poses little active resistance once she decides to act; there’s no serious on-page attempt to stop her, so tension peaks more as moral risk than concrete jeopardy. Still, her act notably forecloses the path of passive preservation and commits the community to a new, costed route.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>9.0</grade><comment>Themes of tradition, agency, and “implanted destiny” are woven through concrete images (nails in seeds, trellises, amber, living wood) rather than abstract sermonizing, culminating in the seedling that learns with the community instead of nailing them down. The final stance—destiny as a tool we wield together—emerges organically from Lida’s choices and the world’s response. One line (“implanted destiny was only tradition without our hands on the wheel”) edges toward thesis-statement explicitness, but it’s earned and supported by preceding imagery. The ending leaves productive ambiguity: this new path is hopeful yet clearly carries long-term, unpredictable consequences.</comment>

The retrospective “we” never drifts into omniscience that breaks the frame; it feels like a community narrating with earned reverence rather than bland consensus. Rhythm modulates well, tightening around the vault and spring scenes then broadening again at the communal ending.</comment>
