<question>4</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—can a fragile network of anonymous care maintain safety’s illusion when the literal light fails?—is clear and steadily tightened. Internal and external conflicts braid effectively, though the piece remains gently pressurized rather than sharply cornering the protagonist into a painful, binary choice. The cost is nonetheless felt in her endurance and the myth of the drowned previous keeper, which narrows her available emotional paths.</comment>

Still, the closing gestures (thank-you line, almost-reached stones) reassert image-based resonance over didacticism.</comment>
