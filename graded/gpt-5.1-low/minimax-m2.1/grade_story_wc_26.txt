The final vigil and warmed stone offer a quiet, non-literal answer: preservation has a cost, but metamorphosis can coexist with loss. The piece invites reflection on memory, embodiment, and archival witnessing without collapsing into a tidy moral.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The central combination—a post-physical, dream-sharing humanity watched over by a dusk-noticing archivist in a blooming sky-library—is fresh and feels like a single, coherent vision rather than bolted-on tropes. While the idea-space (post-human archives, cloud libraries) has genre precedents, the particular inflection here feels personal and specific.
