<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—sustaining communal hope and knowledge under sanctions—is clear, and the pressure of scarcity and “slow erosion of hope” is well established. <question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of continuity in exile, quiet resistance, and intergenerational transmission are coherently developed through motifs of the bell, water’s persistence, and the arbor as an altar. Yet the piece leans into overt thematic statement (“not against any enemy they could name, but against the slow erosion of hope,” “The sanctions would end eventually, as all cruelties eventually do”), which edges toward essayistic moralizing.
