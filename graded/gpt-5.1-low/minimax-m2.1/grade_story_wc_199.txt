However, the actual search in the vault is frictionless (no real obstacle, doubt, or time pressure dramatized), which softens the sense of cost at the climax. The drive lens reads as Hybrid (causal spine with accumulative texture), held consistently but without a truly sharp tightening.</comment>

The moonstone speck and arbor function as micro-settings that charge the final image, but they seldom constrain choice in a way that forces different tactics. Still, for this length, the atmosphere and specificity are strong enough to feel immersive and purposeful rather than decorative.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—uncovering and acting on a prophecy that will secure her people’s survival—is clear but plays out with very low resistance. Internal and external conflicts barely rub against each other: she is prepared, competent, and essentially unchallenged in executing what the prophecy demands. The ending reads more like a deepening of meaning than a true escalation of pressure or a forced trade-off.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of memory, caretaking, and the value of overlooked things are well integrated: her lifelong “tiny monuments” to forgotten seeds become the key to collective survival. Importantly, the story resists flat moralizing; it lets the image of the humming monuments and the charged seed carry the resonance instead of stating a lesson. That said, the transformation is largely affirmational—the piece doesn’t bring her values into serious doubt, which keeps the thematic arc from feeling fully tested.</comment>
