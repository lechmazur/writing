<question>4</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—plant the Untaxable seed without alerting the thought-tax apparatus—is clear, but the pressure escalates only modestly. Internal and external conflicts are more declared than tested; her desperation and the seed’s importance are told, but no choice forces her to sacrifice or improvise under fire. <question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Themes of commodified cognition versus untaxable defiance are woven through concrete images: coded gardens, thought-monitors, insects that consume bureaucracy, and a seed that scrambles surveillance. The final stance—guarding a “spark” that can’t be quantified—offers a resonant, non-literal answer to the central question of how resistance survives in a monetized-mind regime.
