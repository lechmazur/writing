<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Marcus remains frozen in grief or reengages with creation as letting go—is established early via his three-year withdrawal from painting. The choice to relinquish the shell into the pool is a credible, costed resolution that forecloses his prior stance. However, the conflict is almost entirely internal and contemplative, remaining low-temperature; stakes stay conceptually clear but never feel viscerally urgent.</comment>

However, the final paragraphs verge on essayistic explanation (“capturing beauty was not the same as possessing it,” “the keeping was in the releasing”), tipping into overt thesis rather than pure image-driven resonance. The piece is thematically rich but could trust subtext more for a top-tier literary venue.</comment>
