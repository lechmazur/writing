<question>2</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The structure is clean: one continuous scene framed around a single high-stakes pause, with backstory tightly tied to present cues. The main limitation is that the sharp words and prior rupture are kept off-page, so the inciting spark feels slightly abstract.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The grotto, tidal pools, and star-slit opening are vividly rendered and deeply fused to Mira’s way of seeing, avoiding tourist-list description. Environment operates as more than backdrop: the still pool, carved stone, and starlight all mirror and charge the idea of liminal transition. The grotto’s ancient, tidal-carved quality directly informs how she frames the argument and the work of forgiveness, and the closing image depends on this setting. A touch more tactile interaction with the physical space (sound, temperature, bodily position) under peak pressure could push it even higher.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The central stake—whether the sisters can re-enter relationship after a three-year silence—is legible and emotionally weighty. However, most of the story inhabits a single static pause, with the main turn occurring only when Mira places the cicada down and her sister answers. This produces a gentle deepening rather than a sharp escalation; options never feel significantly narrowed or riskier as the scene proceeds. The off-page argument and lack of on-page friction beyond silence keep tension from cresting as strongly as it could.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Theme is coherent and resonant: liminality, transition, and the texture of time are embodied in tides, cicadas, the grotto, and the sisters’ estrangement/return. That said, several lines articulate the thesis almost directly (“I collect transitions,” “The texture of time…”) which blunts subtext and flirts with essayistic explanation. A bit more reliance on implication over statement would heighten its literary subtlety.</comment>

Dialogue, though sparse, feels natural and carries subtext (“You still collect things” as both observation and emotional reach). Rhythmic variety and sentence music support the mood without tipping into mannerism.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The prose is polished and largely economical, with many sentences doing double duty for mood, character, and theme. Line-level clarity is high, and the closing cadence lands cleanly.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The combination of a tidal-pool chronicler, a jade cicada heirloom, and a grotto setting as a lens on reconciliation feels fresh and thoughtfully unified. Slightly bolder or stranger choices in the sisters’ dynamic or in how her chronicling practice concretely affects the interaction could push originality further.</comment>
