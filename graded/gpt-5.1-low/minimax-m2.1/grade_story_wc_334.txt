<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—regaining stolen memories of his grandmother and the old ways—is clear and emotionally resonant, and his endurance in the ring’s light until the Heron scatters provides a quiet, credible cost. Some thematic lines are a bit overt (“The chronically curious become converts…”, “acceptance that some things exist beyond the boundary of proof”), which slightly undercuts subtextual power, but the final image of him planning more expeditions after a hard-won recovery lands with honest, unsentimental resonance.</comment>

A few repeated phrases (“chronically curious”) nudge toward thematic hammering, but they don’t materially slow momentum or blur the closure image.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The fusion of hands-on folklore, a sunken orchard greenhouse, memory theft by “shipwrecking barons,” and a celestial-mycological detection method (broken constellations mapping onto a glowing fairy ring) feels fresh and idiosyncratic. While the broad theme of reconciling rational inquiry with the uncanny is familiar, the chosen imagery and method give it a distinctive inflection.</comment>
