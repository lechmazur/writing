Closure lands as a Resonant Reconfiguration: she forecloses the path of safe stasis by letting the leash fall and committing to the middle way, with emotional cost legible on the page. Details are filtered through the protagonist’s sensibility, avoiding tourist cataloguing. Environment functions as both mood and metaphorical constraint without over-explication.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she will finally cross into a more courageous, alive self—is signaled early and sharpened by the guides’ silent question. Escalation is primarily internal but tracks: from static vigil to committing to the journey, to confronting the nature of courage, to choosing a path and dropping the leash. The price is believable but quiet: relinquishing long-guarded safety for uncertain aliveness. What keeps this from a higher band is the relative softness of external pressure; nothing in the world resists her beyond her own inertia.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The piece coherently explores where courage “waits”—in avoided rooms, unspoken conversations, forsaken roads—and ties that insight to the protagonist’s concrete act of stepping forward. There is a slight tilt toward explicit statement (“courage was not something one gathered like shells”) that edges into essayistic explanation. Nonetheless, the ending’s image of walking into the dark with “reclaimed” courage offers a resonant, non-didactic answer to the central question.</comment>

There is no head-hopping or stance confusion, and shifts in intensity are handled through modulation of sentence length and image density. A few abstractions skim the edge of omniscient essay-voice but do not seriously disrupt focalization.</comment>

Rhythm varies well across paragraphs, especially around the climax at the ridge crest. <question>8</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The psychospiritual crossing with guides at a liminal shore is a familiar archetypal pattern, but it’s refreshed by specific inventions (ticket punch with star stamp, leash as self-imposed bond, the “indirect route” concept). However, the piece remains within well-trodden mythic territory and doesn’t quite achieve a startlingly new synthesis. It’s strong and thoughtful but not uniquely transformative.</comment>
