<question>3</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Echo gorge and the hidden sunrise are richly rendered and meaningfully integrated: the acoustics create the premise (unfinished words amplified), and the gorge’s narrowing “to a wound” mirrors the emotional work. The environment shapes tactics (she comes here because this is where spectral confessions can be reached), and the final image of luminous morning over the gorge charges the closure. It stops short of absolute formal inventiveness, but the setting is both beautiful and functional.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>The central stake—can Mira help this soldier move from paralyzing shame to laughter and release—is clear, and the piece offers one main tightening as she begins to play and tell his specific story. Her method appears reliable from the outset, and it works on the first attempt without complication or reversal. Some lines edge into thesis territory (“healing required joy even, or especially, in parting” and “laughter could integrate itself into the process of grief”), which slightly flattens the subtext. Still, the final image of her carrying “tired hope into the luminous morning” lands with genuine emotional resonance.</comment>

While the arc itself is straightforward, the imaginative apparatus around it feels singular rather than templated.
