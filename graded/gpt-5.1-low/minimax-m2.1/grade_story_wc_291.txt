<question>2</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The structure is clean and coherent: a single scene at the quarry framed by concise backstory about Marguerite and Elena’s prior cases, all driving toward the completion of the portrait. Causality is modest but intact (the ribbon → quarry visit → painting → decision to send it to the sister), and the closure is legible with a conceptual cost. <question>3</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The quarry in mist is vivid and integral: fog, stone faces, and the hollowed crater all mirror the erasure and erosion of evidence and memory. Details like workers sifting gravel and “time had swallowed the fog” embed the place in the central question of what remains after disappearance. While the environment doesn’t strongly constrain Elena’s choices tactically, it clearly charges the emotional and thematic field, especially in the final return to “shadowed silence.” The specificity and consistency of the setting’s mood justify a strong score even without a heavier mechanical role in the plot.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether anything meaningful can be recovered when evidence and justice are gone—is compelling but expressed almost entirely as premise rather than evolving conflict. Elena encounters no resistance, dilemma, or reversal: she arrives with a clear method, executes it smoothly, and leaves with her stance essentially unchanged. The internal conflict between solving crimes and offering peace is more told than tested, so there’s little escalation in pressure or narrowing of options. This leaves the story emotionally resonant but dramatically flat in terms of stakes and intensification.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Thematic strands around memory, visibility, empathy versus justice, and the cost of unresolved grief are woven thoughtfully through quarry imagery, Elena’s face blindness, and the cold case frame. That said, theme occasionally edges toward explicit thesis (“That was her gift to the living: not answers, but completion. Not justice, but peace.”), which slightly undercuts the otherwise organic emergence from scene and image. Still, the piece leaves a clear, lingering conceptual aftereffect that feels earned by the particulars.</comment>

The main constraint on an even higher score is that the story doesn’t fully exploit the inherent dramatic potential of this unusual premise—it stays in reflective, low-tension territory.
