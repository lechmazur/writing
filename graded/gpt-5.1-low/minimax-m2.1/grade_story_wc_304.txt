The caveat is that the setting mostly charges tone and symbolism rather than constraining physical options or tactics. Still, for a lyric piece, atmosphere and object-as-setting work at a high level.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—saving the last spark before oblivion—is clearly signaled and ties naturally to Mira’s role and the chip. Conflict remains mostly conceptual and spiritual, with no antagonist beyond gentle entropy, so the climax feels more like a foregone culmination of competence than a contested win. The piece would benefit from one real moment where failure feels plausibly imminent.</comment>

Some lines shade toward abstract, thesis-like phrasing (“evidence that even in the face of oblivion, something beautiful could endure”), which slightly flattens complexity. More tension between preservation and letting go could enrich the subtext.</comment>

However, the voice feels more “literary-generic lyrical” than singularly Mira-specific; little in the syntax or idiom could not belong to many similar stories. Still, figurative language remains internally coherent and doesn’t obscure causality or the closing image. Tighter pruning of redundant descriptors would elevate the prose.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>7.7</grade><comment>The fusion of “beautician mortician,” inherited soul-capturing emeralds, and an aurora-lit sky grotto is an appealing, distinctive configuration, and the static-pattern reading of a life is a fresh touch.
