The outside “wounded season” and snow give temporal/atmospheric counterpoint that charges the final image. This is high-level setting work, though the lushness sometimes leans toward aesthetic pleasure over additional constraint.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preserving the world’s lost sounds before the wounded season silences them—is clear and present early. However, conflict remains mostly procedural and unopposed; no genuine alternative path or serious chance of failure is dramatized. As a result, the cost is conceptually strong but the on-page tension never quite sharpens into real suspense.</comment>

The ending image—a custodian of the past walking into the snow with the captured echoes—offers a resonant, non-literal answer without overt moralizing. The one explicit thematic statement in the motivation paragraph verges on on-the-nose but doesn’t fully tip into sermonizing.</comment>

No evident loops, scaffolding, or syntactic monotony significantly drag momentum.</comment>

The final decision to imprison rather than release the echoes, framed as rational custodianship, reweights the premise in a non-obvious way. It stops just short of paradigm-shifting brilliance, but this is distinctive, publishable-level ingenuity.</comment>
