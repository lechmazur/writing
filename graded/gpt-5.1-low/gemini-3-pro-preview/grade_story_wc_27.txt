<question>2</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: Elara enters with a clear goal, uses the stone circle and glasses in a specific way, and the frigate reignites, ending the rebellion’s lull—solid Decisive Change. Sensory detail (“ozone, rust, and the copper tang of dried blood”) and spatial relationships remain clear and evolving as power builds. The final image of blue glow bathing ruin and monoliths fusing past and future shows the environment actively charging the closure moment.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>The dominant stake—reviving the frigate as a symbolic and tactical heartbeat for the rebellion—is clearly stated and does culminate in a concrete success. Internally, her desire for meaning doesn’t clash much with fear, doubt, or competing needs, so emotional stakes stay a bit abstract. The piece functions but stops short of real tension that would force a sharper, costed choice.</comment>

Subtext about connection to ground versus severing boots, and about hope in a stalemated war, is present but lightly handled rather than deeply conflicted. The resonance is real and coherent, though not yet at the layered, ambiguity-embracing level of top-tier literary work.</comment>
