Closure functions as Earned Stasis/Charged Poise: he forecloses fleeing and accepts a costly role as internal dissident, and that cost is explicit (likely death outside vs living-captivity inside). <question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The cobalt fortress, blizzard, and freezing stairwell are vividly rendered and consistently filtered through Elian’s body and purpose. The world-rule of enforced silence and the absent piano are made legible through physical details rather than exposition alone. Environment and recurring object (the bolt in his palm in the cold blue dark) meaningfully charge the final image, justifying a high score.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—physical safety versus spiritual/psychic freedom—is established and sharpened toward the end when Elian explicitly weighs fleeing into the blizzard against remaining as a hollowed but subversive servant. Escalation is primarily internal but tracks: physical pain + punitive task → discovery of erasure of music → conceptual reframing of the Keep → decision to stay with a new, oppositional intent. The cost of his choice is legible (continued captivity and pain against the thin hope of contamination), though the external situation doesn’t tighten beyond the initial punishment. It’s strong situational pressure rather than high-tempo conflict, but coherent and earned.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Themes of safety versus freedom, the violence of enforced silence, and the erasure of beauty are well-integrated into action and image—the purged piano, traded voices, and rusted bolt as relic. However, thematic articulation sometimes becomes explicit (“This was the core concept of their existence: unpardonable silence”), edging toward essayistic statement rather than pure emergence from scene. That slight didacticism keeps it just below the very top tier.</comment>

Internal access is steady, and the narrative doesn’t smuggle in knowledge Elian couldn’t plausibly infer from the fortress and its history. The only small limitation is that the voice is sharply literary in a way that risks slight distance from the character’s immediate sensory panic under physical strain.</comment>
