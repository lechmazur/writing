<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the noble lie continues to underpin the regime or Elara reclaims the truth—is announced early and pursued cleanly through confrontation. That said, the story occasionally edges into thesis-statement territory (“Your noble lie acts exactly like this ivy”), reducing some of the ambiguity and letting the rhetoric explain what the images already demonstrate. It’s strong, thoughtful thematic work that could reach a higher tier with slightly more trust in implication over explicit argument.</comment>
