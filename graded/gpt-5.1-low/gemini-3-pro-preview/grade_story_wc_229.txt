Sensory specificity—especially the impossible garden scent over the ocean—deepens both atmosphere and theme. The setting also charges the final image of Elias on the gallery in the wind, recorking the vial.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The central stake (preventing the ship from hitting the Teeth) is clear, and the pressure escalates from passive watching to an intense, embodied act of projection. However, the outcome feels almost too assured once the vial is opened; there’s little sense of potential failure or resistance to his method in the peak beat. A single complication or moment of near-miss on the ship’s side could heighten stakes.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Theme—gentle/quiet care as a more powerful safeguard than aggressive noise—is woven through the lighthouse doctrine, Elias’s motive, and the method of rescue without overt moralizing. The guild/explanation edges toward abstract thesis but is mostly anchored in concrete action. The ending could deepen ambiguity or cost slightly more to avoid feeling like a clean victory for the “whisper” philosophy.</comment>

A few lines drift toward authorial exposition about universe “physics,” but they still broadly fit Elias’s worldview.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The prose is lush but mostly disciplined; images like “screaming tenderness” and the scent of a night garden over open water are striking and precise. Some motifs (guild, intent-physics) are familiar in speculative fiction, but here they’re combined in a notably idiosyncratic manner.</comment>
