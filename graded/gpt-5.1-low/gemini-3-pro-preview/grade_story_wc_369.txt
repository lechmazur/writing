<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: blockage threatens reality → Elias maps the sorrow with the sextant → timed adjustment shatters it → he repairs the tear → listens to the trees. Cost is somewhat abstract (avoiding a potential rupture) and never bites him personally, which keeps the arc from feeling fully consequential. Still, for this scale, the structure is solid and satisfying.</comment>

Details are filtered through his maintenance-worker lens (e.g., “mundane miracle,” “plumber fixing a leak”), giving the place lived logic rather than postcard fantasy. World rules are consistent and conveyed through action rather than exposition dumps.</comment>

The repair of the hedge is almost frictionless, further flattening pressure. The piece works as a calm, procedural miracle more than a genuinely contested struggle.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Themes of emotional residue, unseen maintenance work, and the quiet hunger to witness deep time emerge organically from the imagery and Elias’s vocation. The “mundane miracle” line flirts with explicit statement but is well-seated in character and doesn’t tip into sermonizing. Ambiguity about the moral valence of vented sorrow (released to the clouds) adds a mild, productive unease.</comment>

Rhythm modulates nicely between long, baroque sentences for atmosphere and tighter clauses at structural turns (the pin, the twist of the screw).
