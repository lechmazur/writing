The price is largely internal and conceptual (abandoning the idea of a fixed, human-scaled sky and accepting an immense, lonely task) rather than external danger or a ticking clock. While the pressure could escalate more sharply, the conflict remains coherent and culminates in an earned stance crystallization.</comment>

The final image—turning a fresh page to “map the singularity”—offers a non-literal answer to the story’s question without overt moralizing. The piece leans toward affirmation, but retains enough existential weight (isolation, cost, probable futility) to avoid sentimentality.</comment>
