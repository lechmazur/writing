<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Elian’s willingness to trade his unending life to finally learn what lies beyond goodbye—is clear, and external pressure (storm, dimensional instability, spatial mutiny) coherently tightens around his experiment. However, the internal conflict side is underdeveloped: he seldom seems genuinely torn, so the choice reads more as inevitable than contested, slightly flattening tension. Still, the piece earns a meaningful narrowing of options and visible price at the end.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Themes of mortality, boredom with immortality, and the nature of “goodbye” as reintegration rather than departure emerge organically from the ritual’s mechanics and his long history of farewells. That said, some of the thematic articulation edges toward declarative exposition (“The goodbye was not an exit, but a reintegration...”), slightly reducing the power of purely imagistic resonance. There is no dialogue to test, but internal perception and description carry a stable, character-colored stance. At a few points the prose veers close to self-aware grandiosity, yet it remains coherent and in-character rather than slipping into neutral essay-voice.</comment>

Overall, the prose is vivid and propulsive but just shy of the razor precision expected at top-tier publication.</comment>
