<question>2</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The structure is clean: one continuous scene that moves from discovery of the wooden body and tooth to the decisive arrival of the parents and Aris’s chosen interpretation. Closure lands as a resonant reconfiguration: he refuses a simple death declaration and instead redefines the body as “map rather than corpse,” with the cost of embracing terrifying, uncertain hope. All anchors (who/where/when) are clear, and there are no causality breaks.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The hedge maze, fog, and secret garden are vivid and tonally apt, giving the scene a liminal, fairy-tale atmosphere that suits the changeling premise. Details like the moss bed, ancient roses, and boxwood layers feel specific and consistently filtered through Aris’s perspective. However, the environment mostly serves mood and symbolism rather than concretely constraining choices—this could largely play in any secluded room. Still, the sense of hiddenness and ritual approach through the maze subtly charges the final sanctuary-like confrontation.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The central stake—how Aris will define the girl’s fate for her parents, and thus whether despair or dangerous hope frames their grief—is clear and carries through. Pressure escalates via his discovery that the body is wood, the tooth as proof of substitution, and the impending arrival of the Lord and Lady that forces a decision. The internal ethical conflict (truth vs. compassionate reinterpretation, anger vs. forgiveness toward the fae) is compelling but remains mostly contemplative rather than sharply contested in action. The cost of his choice is implied rather than dramatized—there’s limited sense of what could go wrong if he misleads them.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Themes of science vs. superstition, grief reframed as navigation, and the moral imagination required to treat magic with “pragmatic tenderness” emerge organically from Aris’s choices. A few lines approach overt thesis-stating (“We do not discard the broken… We reset it so it heals stronger”), edging toward explicit moralization but still anchored in his character. The final image of hope as both terrifying and beautiful offers a nuanced, resonant answer rather than a tidy moral.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is consistent, intellectual, and gently lyrical, well matched to a reflective doctor encountering the supernatural. The narrative distance remains stable, with no head-hopping or unjustified omniscience. At times the register skews slightly abstract or essayistic, but it doesn’t break plausibility for this educated, contemplative protagonist.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The prose is polished, rhythmic, and dense with meaning; many sentences do double duty for atmosphere and characterization (“turn mistakes into constellations” is a strong, memorable line). There is some near-redundancy (splint metaphors appear twice, a few philosophical phrases echo) and moments of purple lean, which slightly dampen momentum. Nonetheless, clarity remains high and the final cadence lands cleanly.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The fusion of medical practice (bone-setting, diagnosis, battlefield triage) with changeling folklore and grief counseling feels fresh and thoughtfully executed. The doctor’s attitude—curiosity and forgiveness toward the fae rather than simple horror—further distinguishes the piece from standard dark-fantasy treatments. While it works within familiar mythic territory, the synthesis of tone, profession, and theme gives it a distinct identity.</comment>
