<question>2</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: arrival in the temple → retrieval and handling of the vial (with psychic bleed) → mechanical obstacle at the altar → unsnarling under a ticking oxygen clock → successful broadcast and death. Closure is strong Earned Stasis: she deliberately trades her life for the signal, and the story lands this cost on the page. Structure is straightforward and well-contained in one scene, though the “distress signal” idea could be more tightly contrasted between expected personal rescue and actual ancestral broadcast to heighten the structural irony.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The underwater temple and quartz dome are vividly rendered with specific, sensorial detail (bioluminescent algae, brass and obsidian altar, coral-encrusted cables) and always tied to action. Environment constrains her choices—depth, pressure, invasive coral, and the need to physically disentangle cables all directly shape tactics and stakes, and the temple’s lonesome splendor charges the final image of her “glorious tomb.” A minor reservation is that some descriptive density verges on luxuriant, but it rarely stalls momentum and remains purposeful.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake (save the temple’s “ghosts” vs save herself) is clear and escalates as oxygen dwindles and the mechanical snarl proves intricate. The crucial tightening comes when she must choose to keep working the cables despite hypoxia symptoms, culminating in her conscious decision to complete the signal at the cost of her life. The tension is strong but relatively linear; an additional on-page complication or reversal (beyond mere difficulty and time) could have deepened the sense of mounting jeopardy.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of memory versus oblivion, the worth of history relative to individual life, and communion with the dead are organically expressed through the vial, the broadcast altar, and Elara’s sacrifice. The ending’s idea of becoming “part of the signal” reweights earlier images and offers a resonant, non-literal answer about legacy and dissolution. The piece skirts, but mostly avoids, overt moralizing; still, it leans toward a relatively tidy valorization of self-sacrifice rather than exploring more morally knotted angles of what’s being preserved and for whom.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is consistently lyrical, with controlled elevated diction that fits the quasi-mystical SF setting. POV is stable and limited to Elara’s perceptions and bodily experience, including her cognitive degradation under hypoxia, which is well-handled. While the prose texture is distinctive and rhythmically varied, the high-register tone is fairly uniform across beats, offering limited modulation between introspection and acute crisis.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Line-level craft is strong: sentences are clear, syntactically varied, and information-dense, often doing double duty for atmosphere and action. There’s little scaffolding or redundancy, and the final lines land cleanly without figurative confusion.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The combination of elements—a psychic perfume vial, an ancient broadcast altar in a submerged temple, and a “distress signal” redefined as a memorial transmission—is fresh and memorable. While the sacrificial-hero pattern is familiar, the specific worldbuilding choices and conceptual twist give it a distinctive flavor rather than a template feel.</comment>
