Minor restraint on description in places could sharpen impact further, but the setting already performs structural work at a high level.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The central stake—whether she will escape/stand outside time or accept living within its losses—intensifies as the maze deepens and solidifies at the door. The main escalation comes from the revelation of the hawthorn’s film-rings and the disintegration/straightening of the maze, which concretizes the cost of tearing a ring free. Her final choice to seal the hourglass rather than cross the threshold is clearly a renunciation with visible cost and consequence. Because the conflict is primarily internal and philosophical, pressure is more contemplative than harrowing, but it does tighten appropriately for this length.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Theme—time as serrated, memory as both archive and abrasion, and the necessity of living rather than merely observing—is woven through specific images (fading/reconstituting photographs, serrated instants, periscope-geometry). The final act reweights the premise: she rejects transcendence in favor of embodied repetition, which resonates with the opening motive. There is a slight tilt toward explicit statement (“the texture of time was not to be observed but to be lived”), nudging into on-the-nose territory. Even so, the thematic aftereffect is strong and supported by concrete, recurring motifs rather than abstract sermonizing.</comment>
