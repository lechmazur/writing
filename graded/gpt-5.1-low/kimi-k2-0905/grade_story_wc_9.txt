<question>4</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether and how she can reconfigure her relationship to the lost child and stolen time—is clear from early on and tightens as the ritual deepens. <question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of grief, maternal love beyond death, and the persistence of time where “love refuses to end” are deeply integrated into concrete acts: tracing hands, reading cards, repurposing a broken saucer. Nonetheless, the final constellation image and the idea of teaching clouds to forgive themselves for raining preserve complexity and avoid tidy moralizing.</comment>
