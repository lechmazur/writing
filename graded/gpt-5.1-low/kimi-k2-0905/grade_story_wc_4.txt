The only reason this doesn’t push into 9+ is that the setting shapes mood and meaning more than concrete tactical choices.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether pain can be transmuted into a communal masterpiece—is clear, but on-page pressure remains gentle and largely uncontested. The “tightening” is more a crescendo of imagery than a narrowing of options or heightening of cost. As a result, the ending feels lovely and apt but not hard-won.</comment>

However, some lines verge on thesis statements—“collective healing begins with admitting you are crowd enough to ache”—which slightly undercut the power of the images to carry theme alone. The resonance is genuine, but just shy of the restraint expected at the very top tier.</comment>

Emotionally, the notion of “bottling sorrow so it tasted like penny candy” feels like an original inflection on intergenerational trauma.
