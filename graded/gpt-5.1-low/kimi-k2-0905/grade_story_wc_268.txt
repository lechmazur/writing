<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The ancient temple library is richly realized and consistently used: balustrades, gutters of folios, stone lions, and dust all matter to how the titan moves and executes his plan. World rules (constellation pauses, glamour, rune in ink) are internally consistent and conveyed through action rather than exposition. Occasionally the atmosphere edges toward decorative lushness, but it remains legible and purposeful.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—restoring cosmic/historical balance by unmasking a false king—is clear and tracks through to the sundering. Pressure escalates from long-term search to a focused confrontation night, then to the ink touching skin and the glamour failing. Yet the titan never seems in danger; the guards’ arrival is neutralized almost effortlessly, and no alternative loss scenario is felt. As a result, the conflict is conceptually rich but dramatically cushioned, limiting the sense of cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The piece weaves a strong thematic fabric around truth versus illusion, drift versus anchoring, and the moral cost of false authority (“a throne weighed by illusion throws every future hour out of balance”). Images of rust, ink, and fading inscriptions resonate with the idea that power is a temporary glamour subject to erosion, culminating in “every throne is only ink yet to fade.” The titan’s choice to forget the man’s face and the dust’s abortive coronet complicate the triumph with mercy and anti-coronation subtext. A slight tendency toward aphoristic closure (“Truth drifts until someone chooses to anchor it”) keeps it just shy of truly exceptional subtlety.</comment>

The narrative stance balances cosmic scale and small physical details of the titan’s movement effectively. At times the compression of high-concept metaphors in close proximity risks slight overrichness, but clarity holds.</comment>

A demanding editor would likely trim or space some images for maximum impact.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.9</grade><comment>The core synthesis—a tiny titan in a timeless library using marginalia and an ink-embedded rune to unmask a glamoured pretender during a cosmic pause—is strikingly fresh. The unshowy, principled motive (precision, balance) and the gentle-but-absolute sundering add an unusual ethical texture.
