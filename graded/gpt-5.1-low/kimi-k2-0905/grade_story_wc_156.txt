<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The conflict—can Liora continue to work from certainty, or will she accept the nickel’s bargain and a never-finished picture—is clearly posed and escalates through the refusal of the stones, the nickel’s offer, and the nested awakenings. However, tension remains contemplative; there’s little sense of what she stands to lose beyond an abstract identity, and no strong counterforce tries to pull her back to safety. The resulting stakes are resonant but gently felt rather than gripping.</comment>

The story invites reflection on process and perception without collapsing into a simple moral.</comment>
