<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she can and will risk using the crystal to merge logic and wonder before the “final transformation”—is clearly marked and does deepen as she finds, then activates, then dissolves the crystal. However, tension remains mostly contemplative; we never feel a sharp narrowing of options or a palpable nearness of catastrophic consequences beyond atmosphere. As a result, the conflict feels resonant but never fully sharp-edged.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Thematic integration is strong: concrete motifs (starlings as living equations, fire that reveals rather than consumes, the crystal as condensed “wonder”) accumulate to dramatize the reconciliation of rationality and awe. Her internal arc mirrors the theme tightly, culminating in the reweighing of every future calculation with “the touch of wonder.” However, the explicit thesis sentence—“one lesson: that to harmonize logic and intuition is not to solve the mystery, but to sing it rightly”—tips into overt moralizing and slightly undercuts the otherwise implicit resonance. Without that declarative line, the ending’s images would carry the meaning more powerfully on their own.</comment>
