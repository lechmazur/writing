<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake is internal—whether she will cling to skepticism or accept her identity as a weather-bearer—and it is signaled early via her investigative motive. The conflict is effective but relatively gentle, lacking a sharper trade-off or harder choice that might elevate the stakes.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Themes of shared weather as shared emotional/experiential burden and the “election” of how we inhabit the world are inventive and coherently woven through images of aurora, forests voting, and personal microclimates. The insight feels earned but somewhat over-articulated, dulling subtext that was already present in the imagery.</comment>
