<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The setting is richly particular—from “cinder-block building that smelled of burnt solder and decades of vinyl dust” to sodium lights “the color of dried blood”—and always filtered through Lyra’s professional and emotional lens. Density is high but purposeful; there’s almost no white-room abstraction.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preventing external manipulation from tipping a divided town into violence—is clear and present from the outset, with pressure escalating through the probes, the first counter-wave, the near-overload of the equipment, and the adversary’s frequency-hopping adaptation. Tension holds throughout, but a slightly more visceral sense of what failure would look like on the ground could push the stakes even higher.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically from particulars: media as conduit for both manipulation and care, “interference” reframed as a kind of ethical counter-speech, and the idea that power plays are endemic but can be resisted in moments. The closing movement—“interference, when practiced with humility, might yet be another word for love”—skirts the edge of explicit thesis but is grounded enough in the preceding struggle to feel earned rather than sermonizing. There’s no head-hopping or stance slippage; all knowledge and inference plausibly belong to Lyra. The prose feels like a singular mind noticing the world in a specific way rather than a generic narrator draping style over events.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Line by line, the writing is dense, rhythmic, and multi-purpose; many sentences carry atmosphere, world-building, and emotional state at once (“a gritty lullaby that tasted of copper and thunderstorms” both describes sound and her embodied response). There’s minimal scaffolding or paraphrase, and syntax varies enough to keep momentum through a primarily interior/action blend. The chief constraint on a higher score is that in a few places the lyrical texture slightly risks over-luxuriance in what is ostensibly a time-critical beat, though it never quite slips into muddle.</comment>
