<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake is her relation to continuity vs. decay, focused through the approaching storm and the directive; escalation tracks from preparation, to dream-vision of devouring paths, to literal merger with the storm. <question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The thematic spine—continuity, decay, choice, and the mutual shaping of path and walker—is coherent and revisited through concrete motifs (directive, bunker, storm, footsteps, bird-map). However, the story repeatedly states its insights outright (“Decay is only a directive that forgot how to stop giving orders,” “Decay is freedom remembering its first shape”), edging toward aphoristic sermon rather than fully trusting subtext. That explicitness trims some of the complexity and mystery that the imagery could carry on its own.</comment>
