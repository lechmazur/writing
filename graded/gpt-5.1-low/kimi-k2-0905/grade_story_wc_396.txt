<question>4</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The dominant stakes—preventing the border from thinning into void and breaking the cycle of apathy—are signaled early and tightened as Mara chooses to sacrifice her training and confront the Keeper. <question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Themes of empathy vs professional detachment, commodification of memory, and responsibility to intervene are organically embedded in images (crated emotions, sold laughter, fragmenting conscience) rather than pure thesis. However, a few lines (“map empathy onto action,” “until mapping feeling onto action became the only protocol that mattered”) edge into explicit thesis-statement territory, slightly dulling subtext. The piece still invites reflection beyond the plot, but a shade more restraint in the final third would deepen its literary subtlety.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Sentence-level craft is strong: varied rhythm, high information density, and images that generally do more than one job (worldbuilding plus theme, or mood plus stakes). Tightening 2–3 of the most ornate or expository sentences would elevate the piece to a cleaner, more austere high-literary finish.</comment>

Its only limitation at this band is that a couple of thematic articulations hew close to familiar “empathy vs apathy” framings, where a more idiosyncratic moral calculus could push it into rarer territory.</comment>
