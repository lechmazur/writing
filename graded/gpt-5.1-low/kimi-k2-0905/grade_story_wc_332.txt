Closure is strong: the world’s rules visibly reconfigure (films vanish, shelves pulse, voices return) and Mireya’s status shifts from private seeker to custodian of communal longing, with the cost written onto her skin. The only slight limitation is that the risk of losing an unnamed memory remains conceptual rather than fully dramatized at the decision point.</comment>

The closing image of the altered library and paper boat mobile is charged by all the prior environmental rules, so setting directly powers the final resonance.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—recapturing the dream/cipher to restore erased voices at personal cost—is clear and escalates through the journey to the islet, the painting of light, and the chase of the paper boat. The payoff (implosion of light, palette cracking, cipher inscribed in her veins) delivers a real price, but the dream’s cooperation reads a touch smooth; there’s little resistance or serious possibility of failure on the page. <question>5</question><grade>9.0</grade><comment>Themes of longing, memory, erasure, and “luminous absence” are woven through concrete images rather than stated as thesis. The final paragraphs avoid moralizing; instead they offer a resonant answer—Mireya turns personal hunger into communal restoration—without closing off ambiguity about the cost to her. Subtext about archives, who is remembered, and who is allowed to speak accrues organically.</comment>

Under peak beats (the decisive frame, light implosion), the voice tightens but does not break tone or slide into expository explanation.</comment>

No obvious scaffolding, loops, or mixed metaphors undercut the climax.</comment>
