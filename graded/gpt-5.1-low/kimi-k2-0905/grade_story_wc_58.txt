<question>2</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The structure is cleanly accumulative: arrival → ritual with the thread → apparitions → self-revelation → departure. Closure reads as a resonant reconfiguration and conceptual repricing: she finishes the work, plants the thread, and chooses re-entry into life with a new understanding of healing. <question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The shadowed autumn garden is vivid and used purposefully: light through bare branches, leaves forming words, and deepening shadows becoming portals all participate in the story’s mechanics, not just its mood. The recurring objects (garden, ships, fountain, carpet-thread) function as a micro-ecosystem that constrains options to this liminal space. A slightly broader sensory range beyond the visual might have pushed it even higher, but what’s here is tightly integrated.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she can transform her compulsion to explain absences into a genuine “geography of healing”—is clear from early on. Pressure escalates as she unspools the thread, moving from individual clients to the realization that she herself is implicated, culminating in the choice to finish the thread and leave the garden. The conflict stays mostly internal and contemplative; there is no sharp external obstacle, which keeps tension at a low simmer rather than a boil. Still, the narrowing of options (continue as obituary-writer vs. relinquish the role) is legible and costs her an identity she’s clung to for seven years.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Themes of grief, self-authorship, and the ethics of “explaining absence” emerge organically from the premise and the ritual with the thread, and the ending reweights prior material in a satisfying way. However, several late sentences edge into thesis-statement territory—e.g., “the geography of healing revealed itself as the courage to be witnessed”—which slightly blunts the subtext by stating what the imagery already implies. With a touch less explicit moralizing, this would feel even more resonant.</comment>
