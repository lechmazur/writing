<question>2</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The story follows a clean causal spine: arrival → locating the bloodiest acre via the gull → choosing a specific pod → speaking dead tongues → seeds transforming the battlefield → his vow and departure. Closure is strong, with a resonant reconfiguration as the battlefield becomes a garden and his role redefines from weapon-bearer to seed-bearer, with on-page cost in his bleeding tongue and relinquished past function. The accumulative, ritualistic structure is consistent and legible, with no orientation debt. The only limitation is that the ritual encounters almost no friction, so the structural turn lands more as inevitable than hard-won.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>9.2</grade><comment>The setting is richly imagined and omnipresent: dead reef, blooming jetty, flower-drunk sand, and iron-bleeding battlefield all shape what is possible and necessary for the coachman. Environment directly constrains choices (he must work at the bloodiest acre beneath the moon’s reflection, on a jetty already turning to soil) and charges the final image as land and sea dissolve into a continuous garden. Details are selective yet dense, filtered through the coachman’s task and lore rather than tourist listing. The recurring jetty/garden imagery powerfully anchors the closing reconfiguration.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the battlefield’s wound can be transformed and the treaty restored—is clear and steadily pursued. Pressure escalates texturally as we move from seed choice to voice-breaking incantation to visible transformation, with his bodily cost registering. However, conflict is largely one-sided; nothing pushes back against his attempt, and no real alternative path or failure mode is dramatized. This makes the resolution feel beautiful and moving but somewhat foregone, with less sharpness in the ethical or practical trade-offs.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>Themes of ecological restoration, memory of violence, and the possibility of non-cruel remembrance emerge organically from the reef, battlefield, and treaty motifs. There is a slight tendency toward spelled-out meaning in lines like “promise that nothing buried would stay forgotten, and nothing remembered would stay cruel,” which edges close to thesis-statement territory. Even so, the piece largely trusts image and action to carry its thematic weight.</comment>

A hairline deduction comes from the uniform high-register lyricism, which occasionally flattens modulation across emotional beats.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The line-level craft is unusually strong: nearly every sentence does multiple jobs—worldbuilding, mood, and thematic echo—while maintaining clarity. There is mild risk of over-saturation, with metaphor following metaphor in a way that could fatigue some readers and slightly blur emphasis at peak moments. Risk-taking in style and concept is high but coherent; the story commits fully to its own mythos without retreating into vagueness or gimmickry. The final reweighting of “begin” and the reef’s potential regrowth crystallize this originality with conceptual and emotional bite.</comment>
