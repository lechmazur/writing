The “centuries of layered dust” and hum of the bone towers subtly embody his people’s history and the waiting of the canyon for someone like him. The only modest limit is that the environment constrains choices more symbolically than through hard logistics.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Cael will successfully locate and integrate the wandering pulse / learn to “navigate without moving”—is clearly flagged, but the pressure around it is gentle. His agency (choosing to sing, to press the arrowhead into his chest, to stand still) does drive the turn, yet those choices face no real alternative or risk. The lack of a palpable threat or sacrifice keeps the conflict band solid but not high.</comment>

Combined with relatively low on-page pressure, the thematic resonance is strong but not at the most rarefied level.</comment>
