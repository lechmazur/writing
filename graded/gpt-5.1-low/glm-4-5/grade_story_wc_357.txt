World rules around inversion stay consistent and are learned through observation and navigation. The environment does shape tactics, though it could impose more constraint or danger to reach the highest tier.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—securing Verdantis’s agricultural resilience—is signaled, but remains abstract and largely untested on the page. Escalation thus feels more like a guided tour than a narrowing of options with visible cost.</comment>

The result is conceptually pleasant but somewhat didactic and low on lingering complexity.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The core conceits—inverting gravity, navigating by rivers that flow upward, an orchard planet, and an arrow-tip that’s actually a primordial seed—are fresh and appealing.
