<question>2</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The structure is cleanly accumulative: daily routine, family storytelling, experiments with the bowl and wrecks, culminating in the performance that redefines the hush. Details are filtered through sound and memory, matching his perceptual priorities. The environment meaningfully underwrites the premise and closure, though a touch more constraint or resistance from the setting could have pushed this even higher.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the post-catastrophe hush is deadness or latent music—remains largely internal and philosophical, with little concrete pressure testing Kael’s stance. Escalation is gentle: we move from practice, to family stories, to a single performance that reframes the hush, but there’s no strong opposition, risk, or narrowing of options. His choice to play with the bowl at his feet functions as the tightening beat, yet it doesn’t foreclose a meaningful alternative or entail visible loss. The result is thematically coherent but dramatically mild.</comment>

The final realization harmonizes with earlier motifs but offers a tidy, unambiguous moral. Some complexity or tension—e.g., costs of this worldview—would deepen the thematic impact.</comment>

The line-level craft is competent but not yet sharp or surprising enough for top-tier literary venues.</comment>
