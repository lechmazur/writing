However, the journey itself is almost frictionless: no real risk from the forbidden ritual, no institutional pushback, no personal cost beyond implied rule-breaking, so pressure never truly bites. The resolution thus feels more like a graceful unlocking than a conflict tested under strain.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of automation versus authentic presence, standardized creativity versus flawed wonder, and light without source are woven coherently through the keystone, the Regulation Pack, and the recurring “brilliant dimness” image. Yet the ending leans into explicit moralizing (“wonder couldn’t be programmed or standardized,” “the most luminous light often comes from within”), which flattens complexity into a clear thesis. The piece would feel more literary if those conclusions remained subtextual in image and action rather than being directly stated.</comment>

There’s some expository abstraction, especially around the Great Standardization and “automated creativity,” but it generally integrates smoothly with the character’s perspective. Overall, the prose is above-average and unobtrusive, but not yet sharp or surprising enough—especially in peak beats—to reach elite literary polish.</comment>
