<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The nominal stakes—reaching the village before the solstice and ensuring the community garden’s germination—are clear but never feel under real threat on the page. Internally, she relinquishes the pendant, but that decision is not presented as wavering or contested; she arrives essentially ready to do what she does. As a result, tension stays mild and the story reads more as a graceful fable than a pressured arc.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Theme—wonder emerging from routine encounters and being germinated rather than delivered—is coherent and strongly woven through the pendant’s function, the garden, and Ayla’s evolution. However, the closing lines lapse into explicit moralizing (“wonder wasn't delivered to people—it was awakened within them”), which undercuts subtext and feels like an explained thesis. The piece leans more on stated insight than on letting images alone carry the full resonance.</comment>

Overall, line-level craft is solid and publishable but would benefit from tightening and trusting the images more.</comment>
