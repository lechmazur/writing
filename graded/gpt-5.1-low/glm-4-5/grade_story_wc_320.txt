Nonetheless, the setting is visually memorable and thematically aligned with broadcast, reception, and suspended endings. The sense of place is a major strength of the piece.</comment>

There’s a mild tightening around the moment lunar perigee peaks, but no serious risk of loss or reversal. The conflict functions but never truly bites, which undercuts the climax’s impact.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>Themes of death/rebirth and beginnings inside endings are coherent and echoed in images (fallen star fragment, abandoned city awakening, lunar cycle). The shard of hush and the silent towers offer good symbolic potential that could carry more of the theme without explanation. As is, the thematic spine is solid but a bit on-the-nose.</comment>

Psychic distance stays reasonably tight without head-hopping or stance confusion. The narration is competent and occasionally pretty, though it doesn’t yet have a sharply distinctive perceptual signature.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The prose is mostly clean and clear, with smooth sentences and reasonably efficient paragraphs; there are few outright clunkers. Overall craft is solid mid-tier but short of elite literary sharpness.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>The combination of a “Hollywood star fragment,” a “shard of hush,” and an abandoned radio-tower city under lunar perigee is genuinely fresh and intriguing. The piece feels more like a well-realized vignette of a concept than a fully surprising story. The worldbuilding ideas are inventive; the emotional and structural moves are safer.</comment>
