The meteorite-as-tool functions as an object-as-setting, altering how she uses the space. World rules are consistent and the environment mirrors her internal renewal/decay without feeling like blunt symbolism.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The central conflict—whether Elara will remain in self-erasure as a bookseller or risk reclaiming her identity as a trainer—is clear and does meaningfully resolve. However, opposition is gentle and mostly internalized; we rarely feel a moment where she might genuinely choose to stay “safe,” which softens the sense of cost. A single vivid beat of resistance or fear before committing would sharpen stakes considerably.</comment>

Occasionally the language edges toward abstract thematic labeling (“reinvention of personal history,” “cosmic timeline”) rather than letting scene carry the weight. Nonetheless, the emotional aftereffect is cohesive and avoids explicit moralizing.</comment>
