However, the environment rarely constrains choice or creates friction; it primarily soothes and aestheticizes. Strong mood work lifts this, but the setting doesn’t quite become a decisive agent in the drama.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preventing a dangerous prophecy from spreading—is announced, but remains conceptual and untested on the page. The biggest “turn” is the wing beginning to glow and then successfully absorbing the prophecy, which unfolds without genuine uncertainty or visible sacrifice. As a result, tension feels more ceremonial than fraught, limiting conflict value.</comment>

Distance control is mostly clean; we stay anchored in his perspective without head-hopping. <question>7</question><grade>6.0</grade><comment>The prose is grammatical and clear, with some attractive images (the brook’s sound “like breathing,” the wing becoming translucent where prophecy touches). The overall effect is competent but slightly overwrought for elite-venue standards.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The fusion of origami, a “mapping of silence,” and a code-spoken prophecy in a forest ritual is genuinely fresh and visually memorable.
