<question>5</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>Themes of connection, memory, and the porous boundary between “music” and “noise” are clear and coherent, and the miners’ voices emerging from the lens is a strong concrete embodiment of them. This meta-commentary blunts subtext and leaves little space for the reader’s own inference. The final paragraphs border on sermonizing about curiosity and discovery rather than letting the images alone carry resonance.</comment>

At times, the diction drifts toward uniform, elevated abstraction, with repeated constructions that flatten the character’s distinct perceptual signature. Still, POV control is clean and there are no stance or distance slips that confuse the reader.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The core premise—a laboratory that resurrects lost sounds from objects, framed as an “artificial pilgrimage”—is fresh and evocative. Execution remains somewhat safe, though: the narrative stays in a reflective comfort zone instead of pushing toward stranger, riskier implications or messier human entanglements. The piece feels distinct and memorable in concept, but it stops short of fully exploiting its own imaginative possibilities.</comment>
