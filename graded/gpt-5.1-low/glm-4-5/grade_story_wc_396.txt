<question>3</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The lighthouse orchard above the ice floes is vivid and specific, with strong visual and textural details (gnarled trees, diamond-like branches, groaning ice) that create a memorable atmosphere. The vintage mirrors and their differing eras add layered worldbuilding filtered through Elena’s perspective. While the environment doesn’t strongly constrain her tactical options, it does charge the magic and final image of her holding the crystal against the stark seascape. Overall, setting feels purposeful and texturally rich without devolving into a tourist catalog.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—saving the settlement from a coming storm and proving her method to the elders—is stated clearly but felt only mildly because opposition stays abstract. The “weighted breath before reply” notion attempts to frame time pressure but doesn’t meaningfully narrow her options. As a result, conflict is present but not sharply escalating or costly.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.0</grade><comment>Themes of empathy-as-action and seeing beyond conventional reflections are coherent and supported by the mirror conceit and her role as an unconventional peacekeeper. The final assertion that this act will let her people navigate change with understanding instead of fear borders on a stated moral. Resonance exists, but the piece leans on explanation where suggestive image could do more work.</comment>

Repeated phrasing (“weighted breath before reply”) and occasional abstract formulations slightly dilute distinctiveness, yet there is a recognizable perceptual signature in how she notices shadow, light, and time. The lack of dialogue removes one avenue for voice differentiation but doesn’t harm clarity.</comment>
