A bit more sensory texturing beyond light and distance could elevate it further, but what’s here is strong and purposeful.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The dominant stakes—Orion’s self-concept and the safety of the arches/crops—are legible, and the decision to accept the silk is a real turning point. The elders’ request reads as a test but feels frictionless, with no on-page possibility of catastrophic collapse or personal loss if he fails. However, the story undercuts its own subtlety by stating the takeaway outright (“true resilience wasn't about avoiding brokenness...”) and then reiterating the moral in the closing sentences. The result is coherent but more didactic than resonant, with limited subtext or lingering ambiguity.</comment>
