<question>4</question><grade>5.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—regaining forbidden artistic/spiritual access and crossing “sacred” boundaries—is articulated early and paid off via the gateway crossing. There is no adversarial force or meaningful chance of failure once the ritual begins, and no tangible sacrifice accompanies success. As a result, conflict feels more ceremonial than pressured, limiting dramatic impact.</comment>

The ending offers a conceptual answer—boundaries as thresholds—but does so with a near-moralizing explicitness.
