<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The central stake—preventing a prophecy from shaking the community—is declared early, and the musical confrontation with it does escalate from tentative notes to urgent, oppositional “whisper battling whisper.” Yet the danger remains conceptual: we never see or concretely imagine what “shaking the foundations” would mean, so risk and cost feel distant. Ethan’s agency is strong (he chooses to act, persists, and finishes), but the conflict is almost frictionless aside from his exertion, with no serious doubt, setback, or price beyond effort and a soft melancholy.</comment>
