<question>4</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Lillian’s need to decode her destiny through this final heist—is established, and there is a nominal time pressure from the fleeting golden hour. Internally, the “criminal vs historian” tension only crystallizes after success is assured, so we never see her desire challenged by meaningful risk. The ending reconfigures meaning but without a strong sense of price paid or options foreclosed in the moment.</comment>

However, the language becomes somewhat declarative and thesis-like (“wasn’t a criminal but a historian”), edging into explicit moral labeling rather than letting the images fully carry the insight. The piece still leaves a resonant sense of light as memory and destiny, but with slightly on-the-nose framing.</comment>
