<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—completing the ritual before sunrise to activate the throne for the true heir—is clearly declared, and the ticking clock is established. Elara never faces a meaningful trade-off, moral or practical, and no option is visibly foreclosed by her actions. <question>5</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>Themes of custodial power, honoring history while embracing the future, and the ethics of not claiming power one could take are present and coherent. Subtext is relatively thin; most meaning is carried by explicit explanation rather than implication or irony. The piece invites some reflection but resolves into a tidy lesson more than a lingering, complex resonance.</comment>
