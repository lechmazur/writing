The recurring objects (glass, feather, light) charge the final images of sunrise and the map. Occasional coined phrases (“shadowseam wonder”) edge toward ornament but do not break clarity.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake is internal—Maya’s search for belonging and understanding—signaled early and closed at the end. Her key “choices” (following the feather, removing gloves, staying until sunrise) are largely unopposed and low-cost. The piece functions more as a lyric revelation than as a pressured conflict.</comment>

The emotional aftereffect is warm and contemplative, though slightly sentimental and tidy. Subtext exists but is often overshadowed by explicit articulation.</comment>

Distance is generally well controlled, blending external action with interior perception without head-hopping. At times the register drifts toward abstract, quasi-philosophical language that sounds more authorial than character-bound, but not enough to break voice coherence.</comment>

Several sentences restate similar emotional beats (wonder → belonging) without adding new pressure or specificity. Rhythm and clarity hold, but line-level tightening would notably improve momentum and impact.</comment>

Still, the emotional arc (seeker finds affirmation and a path forward) tracks a familiar, comfortingly neat template. Strong originality in premise and imagery is slightly undercut by a conventional, low-friction epiphany structure.</comment>
