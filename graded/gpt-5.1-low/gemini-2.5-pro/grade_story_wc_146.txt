The archway and tuning fork charge the final image as tools embedded in place rather than mere props. At this length, the integration of environment with method and mood is notably strong.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>Stakes are declared (reviving the town, restoring balance) but largely uncontested; nothing on the page opposes or complicates Elias’s work beyond time and effort. His patience and competence mean his path never narrows or feels at risk. As a result, the story reads more as an elegant vignette than a conflict-driven narrative.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of rhythm as identity, communal life as vibration, and balance as an ongoing practice emerge organically from the method and final cadence. Still, the recurring images of sound, footsteps, and ghosts of daily life give the piece a resonant conceptual core. The emotional aftereffect is contemplative rather than piercing, but coherent.</comment>

Diction and rhythm show deliberate craft without slipping into incoherence or mixed metaphor. Psychic distance remains stable; we stay with Elias’s perceptions even when the narration grows more abstract. While the voice is not radically idiosyncratic, it is polished and confident.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The core premise—a bell tuner restoring a town’s metaphysical pitch via ritualized footsteps—is genuinely fresh and well realized.
