<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Elias’s job and philosophy vs data-driven automation—is announced early and remains clear. However, the climax hinges more on an emergent ecological payoff than an on-page decision under duress from Elias, so his agency peaks in preparation rather than in a visible, high-cost choice at the end.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The piece weaves a strong thematic thread about what cannot be captured by metrics—luck scores, aesthetic algorithms—versus slow, relational care, embodied in the garden “knowing” its gardener. The cracked marbles, especially the deliberate cracking of the previously pristine one, reweight earlier images into a statement about resilience, imperfect victories, and the value of recorded struggle. Thematically, the automation’s sterile perfection and the butterflies’ choice create resonant, non-didactic irony without lapsing into overt moralizing.</comment>

There is no head-hopping or stance confusion, and the occasional elevated phrasing feels earned rather than imposed.</comment>
