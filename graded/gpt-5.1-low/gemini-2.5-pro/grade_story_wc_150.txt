Closure lands with a solid Conceptual Repricing when the ship carving reconfigures the supposed “tragic end” into a perpetual beginning, with emotional cost visible in the filling of her hollow faith. The environment recurs and subtly evolves from eerie to sustaining as Elara’s understanding shifts. This integration of place with both action and theme is strong enough to charge the final image but stops just shy of absolute indispensability.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elara can reclaim and reframe her lineage’s severed connection—is clear and present from the outset. Pressure escalates modestly rather than dramatically: the discovery and slow freeing of the sundial deepen emotional investment but are not sharply opposed by time, antagonists, or major risk beyond labor and emotional exposure. The meaningful turn occurs at the ship carving, which reweights the entire enterprise, but the sense of cost is primarily internal and contemplative, not sharply tested. As a result, conflict feels convincing yet low-intensity, well suited to a quiet piece but short of maximal tension.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Theme—recasting “interrupted journeys” as enduring beginnings and finding faith in continuity rather than triumph—is woven through particulars (diaspora, bones, roots, sundial, ship) rather than moralizing statement. Recurring images (roots, bones, light on stone) genuinely shift valence by the end, from morbid to sustaining. The piece edges close to explicit statement (“Her faith… was slowly being restored”) but keeps enough concrete grounding to avoid sermonizing.</comment>

A slight density of modifiers in places keeps it just shy of total sharpness, but it never obscures sense or closure.</comment>
