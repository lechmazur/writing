<question>3</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The shipping-container labyrinth and geomagnetic storm are vividly evoked and tied to function—acoustics, humming metal, the converging energy in the central chamber. Still, the environment rarely forces hard choices; it enables his success more than it constrains him under pressure. The final charged image (labyrinth’s heart, glowing coin, subsiding storm) is strong but could lean harder on concrete, sensory specifics.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—winning the bet and proving that whispers travel farther than shouts—is clearly stated and directly tested. Because no option is truly foreclosed and no visible price is paid, the tension plateaus rather than tightens toward the end.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The central synthesis—a maintenance worker in a shipping-container labyrinth using sound, a zodiac coin, and folded maps to access a storm-charged “heart”—is fresh and conceptually appealing. However, the rivalry/bet structure and easy resolution feel familiar, somewhat dampening the originality of the premise.
