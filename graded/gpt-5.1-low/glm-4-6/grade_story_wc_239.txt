The softcover dictionary and word-cloud function as key micro-settings/objects that constrain what he can do. A touch more sensory variety beyond the visual might elevate it further, but as-is the setting is a major strength.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.3</grade><comment>Conflict is primarily internal and conceptual: can he integrate contradictory truths and relinquish certainty to perform spiral arbitration. Pressure does grow—library responding to his purpose, words waiting “to be arranged into healing patterns,” the demand for sacrifice—but escalation is smooth rather than sharply turning, with no singular reversal or hard trade-off explicitly dramatized. The dominant stake (repairing fractured reality vs. cost to his own identity/certainty) is present but could be made more concrete in a specific dilemma or failure beat. As a result, tension is intriguing yet muted rather than gripping.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of language as scaffolding of reality, integration of contradictions, and relinquishing certainty in favor of a higher, non-binary harmony come through strongly. However, several lines state theme directly (“Spiral arbitration was never about resolution, but integration...”), tipping into essayistic explanation rather than letting scene and image carry all the weight. The ending image reweights prior material effectively, even if some wisdom feels a bit on-the-nose.</comment>

There are no serious clarity errors or scaffolding loops, but a demanding editor would ask for some tightening.</comment>

Some motifs (cosmic archive, mystical library) are familiar, but they’re recombined in a distinctive way that feels like a coherent vision rather than a prompt collage.
