<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The causal spine is clean: greenhouse → alignment → planting with synaptic focus → growth and pruning → fruit → her redistribution choice, with no orientation issues. The alignment as a ticking frame and the staged plant development give the piece a clear beginning, middle, and end. Cost is present conceptually (she forgoes immediate knowledge and power) but the threat of failure or alternative paths could be more sharply dramatized.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The rusted greenhouse and the post–Great Silence world are evoked with selective, purposeful detail that supports mood and premise. The setting is filtered through Elara’s professional and sensory priorities, especially in how tools, soil, and glass interact with her work and the cosmic alignment. However, the environment rarely constrains her options or tactics beyond generalized isolation and decay; the rust and ruin are more tonal than operative. The final image of a reclaimed, glowing greenhouse is strong but could be even more charged if the physical space had exerted more concrete pressure earlier.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elara can grow and direct a new locus of knowledge and memory as an act of resistance—is clear but stays mostly internal and conceptual. Pressure does increase somewhat as the plant mirrors her negative emotions, and the pruning moment is a modest tightening where she must discipline her inner state. Still, there’s little sense of genuine risk (personal, moral, or practical) that might derail her project or corrupt the fruit. The climax rests on a single quiet decision (sharing vs. consuming), which works thematically but lacks visceral escalation.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Themes of memory, resilience, and “gentle rebellion” against accepting ruin emerge organically from the gardening rituals and the knowledge-fruit’s properties. However, the text sometimes states its thesis a bit directly (“This was her gentle rebellion,” “The world may have forgotten flavor...”), slightly flattening subtext. The ending lingers with hope and cost, but a touch more ambiguity or tension in that hope would deepen its aftereffect.</comment>

Rhythm and syntax vary well, especially around key moments like planting, pruning, and harvesting.</comment>
