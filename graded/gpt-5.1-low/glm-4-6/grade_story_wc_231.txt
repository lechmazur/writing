<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Hemlock can meaningfully decode and honor the stranger’s final gesture—is present but extremely low-pressure, with no real risk of failure or competing obligation. The resonance is genuine but somewhat over-explained rather than fully left to image and implication.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The prose is polished, rhythmically varied, and mostly efficient, with many sentences doing double duty for mood and information. <question>8</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The combination of an eccentric lighthouse keeper, mnemonic objects that carry others’ memories, and a quiet story about a sister’s late, unfulfilled reunion feels fresh and thoughtfully fused. However, the emotional arc itself—witnessing a stranger’s unrequited faithfulness and honoring it—is familiar and not pushed toward a surprising moral calculus or structural risk. Distinctive and well-realized, but not boundary-pushing enough for the highest band.</comment>
