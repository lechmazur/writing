<question>4</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preventing dimensional bleed and answering “what comes next” to preserve balance—is stated early and returns at the end, and the “weighted breath” offers a nominal time pressure. The internal conflict around what answer to give (“Equality”) is sketched rather than dramatized, so escalation is intellectual rather than felt.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of empathy, interconnected futures, and non-binary “equality” between different beings are woven through the crystal, the mirrors, and the final image of two children sharing a path. While the voice sometimes flirts with abstraction, it largely maintains a distinct, confident texture without head-hopping or register breaks.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The prose is lush and often beautiful—“trees bearing crystalline fruit,” “shadow-stitched silk,” and “weighted breath” create a dense, evocative texture. However, the figurative density is high in nearly every line, edging toward purple and occasionally blurring urgency; there’s little sparseness where pressure should tighten. Some phrases repeat conceptually (empathy, balance, accumulation) without new angles, creating a mild redundancy drag.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The combination of a lighthouse orchard over ice floes, empathy-accumulating crystal, and mirror-based reconstitution feels genuinely fresh, not a template. The main limitation is that the emotional arc is simpler and safer than the worldbuilding—Shael never makes a morally fraught or surprising choice to match the inventive setting. Still, the piece reads as a distinctive vision with memorable images and a coherent speculative logic.</comment>
