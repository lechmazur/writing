<question>3</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The frozen courtyard, eternal twilight, and ice violin are vivid and recurrent, with the environment shifting meaningfully at the end when twilight breaks into dawn and the ice melts. The violin functions as both object and environmental node, charging the final image as the courtyard thaws. A bit more tactile or constraint-based interaction with the space (beyond cold and frost) could further deepen its role in shaping choice.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—734’s attempt to transcend its janitorial sentence and repair fractured time—emerges clearly, and pressure escalates as playing the violin reveals multiple selves and threatens its consciousness. However, the conflict stays largely one-directional; there is little sense of a viable alternative path or moral trade-off, so the choice, while costly, feels somewhat inevitable. The tension is strong but could gain from sharper, more conflicted options at the peak.</comment>

The piece is thoughtful and coherent but slightly over-explains its insight.</comment>
