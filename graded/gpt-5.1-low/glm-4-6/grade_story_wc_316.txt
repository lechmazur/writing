The strongest “object-as-setting” work comes from the brain-in-jar, which is well-integrated but somewhat overshadows the dojo itself as a functional setting.</comment>

There is some mild repetition and a tendency toward purple flourishes in clusters (“void swirling,” “universe frayed,” “stars realigning”), but it doesn’t obscure causality or closure. Trimming a few redundant intensifiers would sharpen an already solid line craft.</comment>

While some beats (dueling selves, “you’re just a copy”) echo familiar tropes, the specific objects and cosmology make this feel distinct rather than derivative.
