<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>Stakes are conceptually high (souls trapped in unresolved grief), but on the page the conflict is almost entirely unopposed. The main “tightening” is merely moving from woman to child and then to the final ritual, without a true escalation of difficulty or cost. This leaves the piece more as a contemplative vignette than a pressured scene.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Themes of grief, release, and the integration of laughter into healing are well embodied in specific images: the gray tapestry threaded with gold, the giggle pursued through warped notes, the tooth as a symbol of grief’s primal edge. Strong, but not yet at an elite level of subtlety.</comment>
