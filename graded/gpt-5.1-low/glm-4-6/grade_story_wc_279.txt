<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—grasping and possibly altering the emergent hush/unlearning—is clear but remains largely conceptual and low-friction. Yet there is little on-page sense of risk, loss, or alternative paths foreclosed; nothing credible pushes back against her goal. The conflict reads more as puzzle-solving in a safe sanctuary than as a contested transformation with palpable stakes.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of unlearning, emptiness as precondition for meaning, and reconnection through pattern are coherently woven via the hush, mandala, and tile. However, the final line spells out the thesis rather than letting it sit in charged implication, edging into mild moralizing. Subtext exists but is somewhat over-articulated where a more oblique ending could have deepened resonance.</comment>

Overall, the voice is polished and coherent, if not radically surprising.</comment>
