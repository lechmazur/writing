<question>2</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The piece follows a clean accumulative/ritual structure: restive sky and garden → deliberate voicing of silence → arrival of the echo → altered cosmos and self, all causally linked under its own rules. Orientation is strong from the first paragraph and the drive lens (accumulative textural ritual) stays stable. Closure lands as a resonant reconfiguration: he succeeds, the constellations reorder, and he walks away changed, with the goddess’s echo now inside him. The main limitation is that the cost of success is implied rather than sharply tested or complicated at the climax.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The “sound wave garden” and “sleepwalking constellations” are vivid, specific, and consistently re-invoked, with sensory and conceptual precision that feels organic to the fisher’s worldview. Setting is not mere backdrop: the humming flowers, their drooping under imposed silence, and the sky’s unstable sorrow all interact directly with his method. The rail spike as profane object against a lyric environment charges the final image. A slight bias toward visual/abstract over fully embodied sensory detail is the only thing keeping this from the very top of the band.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—catching the goddess’s echo tonight—is clear and the pressure coherently intensifies as the garden grows restive and the constellations distort, then fall into step with his ritual. Escalation is primarily textural and internal; there’s minimal resistance or reversal once he begins, so success feels somewhat assured rather than hard-won. The cost (his longing poured into the act, the echo becoming part of him) is present but doesn’t materially complicate his options at peak pressure. The result is strong mood-level tension rather than sharp conflict.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of longing, translation of emotion into form, communion with the divine, and the price of attaining one’s quest emerge organically from the ritual and its aftermath. The ending’s sense of satisfied but altered solitude offers a nuanced, unsummarized answer to the initial desire. A touch more dramatized ambivalence or ethical complexity around taking/holding the goddess’s echo could deepen the resonance further.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The third-person voice is confidently lyrical with a distinct texture—phrases like “a ghost in the machine of the cosmos” and “a feral lullaby” feel of a piece and controlled. There’s no head-hopping or register slippage, and sentence rhythm modulates well with the ritual’s intensification. At times the diction flirts with uniform high lyricism, which slightly flattens peaks that could benefit from sharper rhythmic contrast.</comment>

Grammar and sequencing are clean, and the final lines land with a strong, earned cadence. The final reconfiguration of the stars and the echo bound into iron gives the piece a memorable conceptual stamp.
