Still, the final stabilization of the library nicely charges the ending image.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—eroding a stolen reality and unmasking the pretender—is signaled early and paid off clearly when the false magic is sundered. Kael’s agency is strong in the long game and in the final act of speaking the master rune; the pretender’s collapse is causally tied to that choice. The cost, however, feels more conceptual than personally painful on the page, and the confrontation resolves quickly with little visible risk of failure.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Theme coheres elegantly around truth as a slow, erosive force versus grand, brittle lies, embodied in the “rune of drift,” the tilting library, and the “crown of glass” that shatters from within. The final transformation of the king into a “hollow, terrified man” offers a resonant, non-literal answer to who truly holds authority. Some of the thematic beats (“true royalty requires no proclamation”) tread close to aphorism, slightly blunting complexity.</comment>
