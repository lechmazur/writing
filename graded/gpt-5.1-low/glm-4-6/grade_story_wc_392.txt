<question>4</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The dominant stakes—solving a suspicious death linked to her father and freeing herself from inherited control—are clear, but conflict remains largely internal and contemplative. Her final decision to keep going and reinterpret her father’s role is meaningful but not sharply contested by any live opposing force. Subtext exists—particularly in the parallel between her father’s “accident” and the present case—but is often overshadowed by direct statement. Overall, the voice is competent and occasionally striking but not yet distinctive enough for top-tier literary venues.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>6.7</grade><comment>Prose is grammatically clean and mostly clear, with good rhythm and some efficient integration of description and thought. <question>8</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>The combination of a preventable-death investigator, a historic Underground Railroad lantern, and generational trauma in a subway tunnel is a fresh and evocative mix.
