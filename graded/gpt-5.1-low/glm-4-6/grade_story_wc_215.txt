Environment meaningfully charges the final image—the oasis as rare refuge where this covenant can exist—though it rarely constrains specific tactical choices in the moment. Overall, the world feels fresh, tactile, and thematically aligned with fragility and preservation.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preserving the oasis’s fragile harmony against encroaching, angrier ghosts—is clear, and the narrative correctly pivots on the covenant’s strain and renewal. Elisha’s main act (ritual mediation) resolves the issue without serious resistance or sacrifice, so the pressure curve remains relatively gentle. The cost of failure is conceptually high but not viscerally felt on the page, limiting the intensity of the conflict.</comment>

The covenant as a metaphor for community and interdependence mostly arises organically from events.
