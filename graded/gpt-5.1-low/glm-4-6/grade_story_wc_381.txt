<question>4</question><grade>5.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the prophecy’s sound-weapon will be neutralized—is signaled, but it never feels precarious on the page. Conflict is almost wholly internal and contemplative, but even internally his resolve is unwavering, so escalation reads as steady amplification of imagery rather than tightening of options. As a result, the resolution feels more like a foregone consummation than a hard-won turn.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The theme of silence as potential weapon versus silence as chosen peace is coherent and organically bound to the central ritual and imagery. However, the piece edges toward statement by summarizing his resolution (“to let silence be peace, not power”) rather than leaving more of that work to image and implication. The emotional aftereffect is gentle and meditative rather than piercing, which suits the piece but keeps it just short of high resonance.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The prose contains many striking images (“his mind a tranquil blaze—burning quietly, consuming doubt”) and mostly clean syntax, but it is also heavily saturated with modifiers and repeated figurative patterns. Several adjacent sentences paraphrase similar ideas about silence, mapping, and hush, which slightly drags momentum and dilutes individual lines. Overall clarity remains high, but selective cutting and diversification of imagery would markedly tighten and elevate the line-level craft.</comment>
