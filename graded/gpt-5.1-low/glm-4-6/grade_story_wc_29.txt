Much of the world—the divided community, religious leaders, startups, military—is sketched at a distance instead of localized in concrete scenes. The final image of “celestial silence” is thematically apt but could be charged more through a stronger sense of the room and her body in it.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—humanity’s susceptibility to manipulative cosmic signals—is strong in concept but plays out with limited on-page pressure. The departmental power plays and social chaos are described but never actively resist or endanger her plan. As a result, escalation is conceptual rather than felt, and the climax reads more like a procedural step than a truly tested choice.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.7</grade><comment>Themes of manipulation, media amplification of division, and the fragility of human interpretation emerge coherently from the scenario of responsive “cosmic trickster” signals. However, the thematic work leans toward explicit explanation (“carefully constructed deceptions,” “manufactured divisions”) rather than letting scenes and images carry the weight. The ending’s notion that people will cling to their interpretations even after the signals fade is effective, but it’s delivered as a statement more than an evocative, reweighting image.</comment>
