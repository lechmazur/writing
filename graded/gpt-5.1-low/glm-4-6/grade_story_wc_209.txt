<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The primary stake—Lena’s need to understand night-blooming flowers and what her obsession means—is clear and escalates texturally through time and deepening fixation. Yet conflict remains relatively low-intensity and one-directional; there’s little real resistance or risky trade-off besides lost time and emotional unease, and no competing goal to sharpen the calculus. The result is a graceful but gentle arc rather than a truly tense or costly one.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The thematic through-line—value of darkness, unseen ecosystems, and revelations that require patience and obscurity—is coherent and reinforced by recurring images of night blooms, postcards, and glass. The themes are strong but somewhat over-articulated rather than fully entrusted to image and implication.</comment>

Still, the overall arc—obsession, research, mysterious site, revelatory realization, and serene acceptance—is familiar in shape and handled elegantly but not radically. Strong originality in texture and premise, if less so in macro-structure.</comment>
