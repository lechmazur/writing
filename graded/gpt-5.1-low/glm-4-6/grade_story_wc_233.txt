While the environment does not heavily constrain tactical choices, it does meaningfully charge the final revelation and reframe her entire life. <question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether she will remain as human queen or reclaim her celestial role—is signaled early and escalates as she moves from castle to ridge to grotto. The seneschal’s question at the end sharpens the fork, but her answer comes too easily.</comment>

The final line about the kingdom paying the price gives a dark, non-moralizing answer that lingers. Still, the moral/emotional calculus is relatively one-sided—her lack of visible struggle flattens potential complexity around betrayal, obligation, and power.</comment>
