However, escalation is very smooth—problems present and then resolve without much friction, and the storm never truly constrains options. However, the storm and landscape rarely impose concrete constraints on Lyra’s choices—they frame rather than force decisions. Strong textural work and coherence, but limited functional pressure keeps it shy of the highest band.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.1</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Lyra finding purpose/direction when her technological guidance fails—is legible, and there is a soft deepening when she realizes the sculptures are markers and the slide is a purpose-map. Yet there is minimal genuine conflict: no antagonist force, no hard trade-off, and the storm functions more as background mood than an active threat. Each step (discovering, interpreting, following, completing) succeeds without reversals or loss, so tension remains low. The resolution feels more like fulfilling a puzzle than paying a visible price.</comment>
