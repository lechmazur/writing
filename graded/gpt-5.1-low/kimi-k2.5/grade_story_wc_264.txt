<question>4</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the remnants of green possibility can be preserved against entropy—is clear and threads the piece, and there is one meaningful tightening when Kael realizes he must “become the spark himself” and places the clock. Still, his renunciation of individuality (remaining “occasionally everywhere” as sentinel) constitutes a credible cost that reweights the stakes. The story could reach a higher band with one more concrete beat of resistance or risk in approaching or opening the archive.</comment>

The resonance at the end is strong without sliding into sermonizing, though a few phrases (“cultivate the sparks in the unlit corners”) come close to explicit thesis. Overall, the piece leaves a clear, lingering conceptual and emotional aftertaste grounded in its particulars.</comment>
