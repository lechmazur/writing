<question>4</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The central stake—whether truth about the bees and the tower’s real function will surface despite corporate suppression—is clear but under-pressured. The engineer asks a practical safety question, which could create a sharp conflict between immediate bodily risk and metaphysical truth, but the story quickly pivots to revelation and wonder without dramatizing competing stakes. As a result, escalation is more tonal/intellectual than confrontational, and the cost of the reveal never quite bites on the page.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of labor vs. wonder, evolution vs. control, and the value of “failures” as sites of emergence are strong and coherently woven through bees, blackout, and the toll keeper’s vocation. The emotional aftereffect is pleasant and thoughtful, but some of the insight feels told rather than earned purely in scene.</comment>
