The drive lens is primarily accumulative/textural, and it holds: each fold deepens the central pressure of grief-transformation until the final release. The only limitation is a relative smoothness of path—no serious on-page complication once the premise is established—keeping it below the very top band.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The setting is rich, specific, and consistently filtered through Mira’s craft and state of mind: the glass basilica, floating isle, upward-falling cherry petals, and translucent garden floor all echo the themes of inversion and elevation. The fantastical rules (shadow detachment, floating islands, enchanted soil) are introduced smoothly through action rather than exposition. It falls just shy of 9-band because, while highly charged, the environment rarely constrains concrete choices; it primarily mirrors and amplifies interiority.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether and how Mira can transform her lifetime of grief into something that allows her soul’s migration—is clearly signaled and steadily intensified as she folds more personal losses into the cranes. However, the pressure line is relatively unopposed once the ritual begins: there’s no real risk of failure, hesitation, or alternative path dramatized on the page. This keeps escalation more contemplative than tense, effective but not maximally sharp.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Themes of grief-as-material, transformation through craft, and the paradox of rising by surrendering are deeply woven into specific images (gem memory, cranes, shadow/self inversion) rather than abstract statement. That said, a few lines verge on explicit moralizing (“the only way to rise is to let go”) and slightly flatten the otherwise strong subtext. The piece still elicits a concrete emotional aftereffect, but with a touch more telling than an elite venue would prefer.</comment>

Diction is elevated but mostly feels organic to a master artisan in a numinous setting, and there’s no head-hopping. At times the register leans a bit grand or abstract in clusters, which slightly blunts immediacy, but overall control remains high.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The prose is generally beautiful, with high information density—many sentences do triple duty in worldbuilding, emotion, and theme—and the rhythm varies in a way that suits the meditative arc. There is some tendency toward saturation of figurative language and overt thematic phrasing in the final third, which edges into mild overwriting and could be tightened for greater impact. These issues don’t harm closure clarity but keep the line-level craft from feeling utterly effortless.</comment>
