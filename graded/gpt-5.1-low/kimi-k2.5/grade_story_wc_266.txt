<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Setting is vivid and integrated: fog shore, lantern-lit waterline, “veiled ridgeway,” and the bioluminescent grotto all mirror Mara’s internal state and the liminal courage/fear theme. The recurring waterline/shoreline imagery and the twilight-to-dawn shift explicitly charge the final image of “waiting light.” The only thing keeping this from the absolute top band is that the environment shapes mood and metaphor more than concrete tactical constraints, though it still feels essential.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—will Mara move through panic to act without her usual tether—is clear and tested by the ridgeway traversal and the moment at the grotto entrance. Internal and external conflicts interweave well (finding Atlas vs. confronting dependence), and the decisive turn is her deliberate choice not to reattach the leash. However, escalation is gentle; there’s minimal sense of rising external peril or narrowing options beyond the accumulative strangeness and depth of the fog. The result is thematically potent but somewhat soft in felt pressure, more meditative than tense.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of unused courage, dependence versus autonomy, and the redefinition of safety are coherent and emerge organically from the leash, twilight, and shoreline motifs. That said, the thematic articulation leans into on-the-nose aphorism (“Unused courage waits not in the absence of fear…”) which drifts toward moralizing rather than pure image-based resonance. This explicitness slightly blunts the otherwise nuanced symbolic work.</comment>
