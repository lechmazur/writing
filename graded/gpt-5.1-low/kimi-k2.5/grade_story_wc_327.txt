<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean and legible: cursed diamond → need to act before the last elder dies → use birds to decode the canyon → redirect curse via the tunnel of echoes. Closure lands as a decisive change with conceptual cost: the curse is re-housed and the language survives only as resonance in bone. The pressure progression is more told than dramatized (e.g., “she was running out of time”) but still structurally sound.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The setting is vivid, strange, and specific: fossilized ribs, towers of bone, and a canyon eroded by sound feel tactile and original. Environment is not ornamental; acoustics and material (porous bone, echoing tunnels) directly shape her tactics and enable the resolution. The final image—language living on as resonance within the bone towers—reweights the whole landscape as an archive and a prison at once. A slight risk of density overload is present, but clarity and atmospheric cohesion remain intact.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the last remnants of a language will be devoured or preserved—is clear and scaled appropriately for the length. Pressure does rise, particularly through the time limit of the sleeping elder and the rule that loud sound feeds the curse. However, the path to success is comparatively smooth: once the method is understood, her execution faces no significant setback, trade-off, or on-page resistance beyond urgency. The cost is more conceptual than felt; she wins without a visible sacrifice beyond fatigue and the implied dimming at the end.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Themes of language death, memory, and the translation between textures, sounds, and stories are richly embodied in concrete images (bone, echoes, birds, the fractured diamond). The idea that a language can survive as resonance—shorn of living speakers—offers a nuanced, unsentimental take on preservation versus loss. Subtext about colonial erasure and archival violence hums beneath the surface without being declared, especially in the “library of voices” and the curse’s hunger for final speakers. The ending’s quiet, completed pattern feels thematically apt, though a sharper sense of her personal emotional loss could deepen the aftertaste.</comment>
