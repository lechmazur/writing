However, the outcome (democratic approval) arrives without any serious sense that refusal was live, and Elara is never forced into a costly decision; she’s told her skepticism is needed and she accepts that without resistance. The conflict lands more as an elegant unveiling of roles than a true test of competing stakes.</comment>
