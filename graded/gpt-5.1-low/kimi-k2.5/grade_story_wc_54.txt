<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: survey → discovery of the sextant → decision to reroute systems → overnight work → proof-of-concept at dawn → impending colonist arrival. The piece runs under a causal lens with a light accumulative texture, and there are no orientation issues. However, the middle lacks a concrete complication or reversal in her process, so escalation feels smooth rather than sharply tested.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.9</grade><comment>The abandoned spaceport, layered with carnival detritus, is strikingly specific and tightly filtered through Maya’s technical and imaginative eye. Environmental details (ruptured mains, auxiliary pumps, solar collectors, hydroponic rails) directly constrain and enable her tactics. The sextant and stained-glass skylight image charge the final moment, and the architecture-as-“maze of intentions” is both spatially and thematically active. A slight excess of conceptual phrasing in a few lines is the only thing keeping this just shy of the 9+ band.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether a condemned, dead system can be repurposed into a living sanctuary—is clear and thematically rich. There is one meaningful tightening as she decides to commit the night to rerouting flows, with colonists due by noon providing a soft time pressure. Still, conflict remains largely unopposed; the environment yields to competence without dramatized setbacks or trade-offs, so cost and risk feel muted. The result is satisfying but not maximally tense or fraught.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The thematic throughline—hope as redirected flow rather than finite resource, resurrection via repurposing ruins—is coherent and well integrated with image and action (circulatory systems, mazes, the sextant). However, several sentences, particularly “Hope, she concluded, was not a finite resource…” verge into essayistic statement, undercutting subtext. The ideas are strong enough to carry more implication and less explicit articulation.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is stable, with a distinct perceptual signature: mathematically precise silences, seeing infrastructure as circulatory systems, language of flows and intentions. Diction is elevated but credibly belongs to a reflective, technically literate protagonist. The modulation from survey to improvisational engineering to quiet awe feels controlled and intentional. There is occasional slide into abstract, essay-like phrasing, but it does not break POV or clarity.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.1</grade><comment>Line-level craft is consistently strong: sentences carry multiple functions (worldbuilding, character, theme) with generally clean rhythm and minimal scaffold. <question>8</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The combination of an abandoned spaceport, post-carnival infrastructure, and an intern turning derelict systems into a greenhouse via redirected flows feels fresh and organic rather than gimmicky. While not radically experimental, the piece distinguishes itself within SF-tinged literary fiction through its conceptual and imagistic coherence.</comment>
