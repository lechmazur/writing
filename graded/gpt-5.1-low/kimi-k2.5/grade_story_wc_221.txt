Recurring objects (viewport, compass, journal) function as micro-settings that charge the final image. The density of description occasionally edges toward ornamental, but the environment consistently frames and pressures her choices.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—enduring isolating temporal torture versus finding a way out—is clear and present from early on. Her agency drives the turn: she chooses to systematize her perception into cartography and then to act on the impossible glimpse. The cost (centuries of subjective isolation and the gamble of stepping through a one-chance threshold) is implicit rather than sharply re-tested in the final moment, which keeps this just shy of a higher tier.</comment>

At times the prose approaches purple, but it still feels like the textured thought of someone trapped in stretched time rather than authorial ornamentation. POV remains stable with no head-hopping or stance confusion.</comment>
