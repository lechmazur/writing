A touch more tactile interaction with the space under pressure could elevate it further.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Mira can move from suffocation in Elias’s shadow to autonomous breathing—is legible and paid off. Pressure escalates modestly as she arrives, confronts him with blame, and submits to a painful, high-voltage transmission. However, the internal and relational conflict resolves quickly and harmoniously once the scars connect, with little resistance or real risk that the lesson could fail or harm her. The emotional price is implied but not deeply dramatized, keeping escalation solid but not searing.</comment>

Some lines flirt with on-the-nose thesis (“I burned the bridges so you would learn to swim”; “teach suffocating to breathe”), though they remain just inside the bounds of character-motivated speech. The piece invites reflection on autonomy vs. codependence without collapsing into a tidy moral.</comment>

Some images echo common lyric tendencies, but the overall synthesis reads as a distinctive artistic stance.</comment>
