<question>3</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The underground anti-gravity garden, bioluminescent fungi, and the phone-on-altar form a distinctive, lived-in environment that shapes the action: the cavern becomes the cocoon and later the sealed habitat for Elena’s new existence. Setting is tightly integrated into event (the garden blooming in response to the ritual, stone sealing the entrance) and charges the final image of her no longer needing the sun. Details are selective, vivid, and filtered through her scientific/mystic sensibility, though there’s a slight tilt toward ornamental lushness that just barely keeps this from feeling utterly inevitable in every line.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elena will remain a wounded human observer or cross over into being the cryptid herself—is clear and resolved at the end. The cost (abandoning her old life, being sealed underground) is present conceptually but not sharply dramatized as a contested choice, so the conflict feels more like a solemn initiation than a pressured crucible.</comment>
