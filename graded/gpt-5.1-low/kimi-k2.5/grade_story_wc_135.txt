<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The “ivory harbor after rain” is vivid and particular—wet planks, bone-like stones, driftwood, violet dusk—firmly anchoring place and mood without feeling like a generic seascape. The main limitation is that the physical environment shapes atmosphere and symbolism more than it constrains concrete choices or tactics in the moment.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The central stake—whether and how meaning can be reconstructed after exile—remains coherent and deepens as Kael moves from solitary cataloging to communal reassembly.
