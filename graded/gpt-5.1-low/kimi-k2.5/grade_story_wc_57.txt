<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The structure is clean: a single continuous scene with a clear problem (recovering a nearly lost storm-calming technique), a focused process (decoding the rope), and a closure beat (realizing the secret lives in the negative space and carrying it away as a “seed”). Setting isn’t just backdrop; it frames the encounter as ritual and charges the ending’s tone.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The central stake—preserving a nearly lost storm-calming technique—is clear but mostly abstract, with little sense of what is concretely endangered in this moment if Elias fails. The main tightening is reaching the final, most complex knot and coaxing out its meaning, which works but feels procedurally smooth rather than pressured. As a result, the piece reads as a beautiful ritual more than a test with felt cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Themes of preservation vs. forgetting, embodied knowledge vs. textual record, and custodianship across generations emerge organically from particulars: the weavers’ consciousness in fibers, the insistence on retieing the knot, and Elias carrying the “seed” to a future apprentice. The ending offers a resonant, non-didactic answer: old ways live only in practiced bodies and patient lineages. The relative softness of on-page conflict keeps the thematic aftershock from feeling as sharp as the concept allows.</comment>
