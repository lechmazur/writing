The main limitation is cost: the “unlearning” clock stays abstract, and the solution feels achieved without any significant sacrifice or irreversible loss beyond the tile itself. Still, the before/after of the grotto’s state is legible and satisfying on the page.</comment>

The setting directly charges the ending image, with the mist spiraling in time with her breathing and the grotto’s loneliness resolved into “patterned silence.” A slight touch of decorative proliferation in color adjectives (lilac, violet, lavender, amethyst) keeps this just shy of the absolute top band.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The central stake—restoring coherent whispering before the unlearning completes its erasure—is clear, and tension escalates through the discovery of the wrong-feeling tile, the deepening lilac unease, and the mist’s oppressive silence. The piece still invites reflection, but the last paragraphs lean closer to articulated lesson than an entirely image-driven repricing.</comment>
