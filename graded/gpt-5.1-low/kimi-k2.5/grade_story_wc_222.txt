Internal and external pressures (cold, altitude, fear of the unknown) are invoked but rarely force a hard trade-off or genuine risk of failure. Escalation is more accumulative and ceremonial than tense, which blunts the sense of cost despite the grand stakes.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Themes of truth versus comforting falsehood, approaching magic as “sacredly playful,” and history as something that can be amended rather than erased are strong and coherent. However, the text occasionally states its thesis a bit directly (“magic demanded to be approached sacredly playful,” “some treasures could only be handled with the heart”), nudging toward didactic. The ending image of time ticking forward “toward hope instead of circling tragedy” is resonant, though a touch on-the-nose.</comment>
