Causality is internally consistent under a lyric, non-literal rule-set; no orientation issues. <question>3</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The twilight garden, fountain, moonlight residue, and bioluminescent pollen are vividly specific and consistently filtered through Kaelin’s tide-obsessed perception. The garden and its objects (especially the fountain and moonstone dust) charge both the process and the final image, where the environment responds to her transformation and acknowledges her as one of its own. World rules—nostalgia ban, calcified moonlight, memory in dust—are strange but internally consistent. Setting is not just a backdrop but a participating presence in the closure, though it rarely constrains tactical choices.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preserving outlawed memory by turning her body into a cosmic archive—is clear, and there is a discernible tightening when the dust speck “feels heavier” and she recognizes the final phase of exile. However, antagonistic pressure is largely abstract or offstage; the authorities and their alarms appear only as a late conceptual shadow. Her main conflict is internal, but it unfolds with almost no resistance—she decides to inscribe herself and succeeds without serious doubt, reversal, or loss beyond what the narrator asserts. This keeps tension from escalating to a level commensurate with the grand stakes the prose claims.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of memory vs. erasure, body as archive, and choosing exile to protect the past are strongly woven through recurring images of tides, stars, and skin as canvas. Some lines veer into explicit thesis (“The nostalgia ban had made memory illegal, but cosmic memory operated on different statutes”), which slightly flattens subtext. Overall, the ending’s reweighting—her body as constellation accepted by the universe—delivers solid resonance without a neat moral.</comment>

At times the register drifts toward abstract or thesis-like (“cosmic memory operated on different statutes”), which briefly breaks the spell of embodied voice. Still, control is generally strong and consistent, with purposeful rhythm and image-driven narration.</comment>

Figurative language is very frequent in high-pressure beats, sometimes blurring rather than sharpening the emotional stakes.
