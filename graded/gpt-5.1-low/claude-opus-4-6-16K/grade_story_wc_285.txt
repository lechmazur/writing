As a result, stakes read as elegant but somewhat muted for top-tier conflict scores.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The palimpsest of grief and promises, the erased road, and the woman seeking “where a road had been” converge into a resonant meditation on loss, irreversibility, and the ethics of retracing rather than restoring. The ending avoids moralizing, letting the page-turning river and accepted promise carry the thematic answer. A slight softness is that Fen’s thematic insight is already fully articulated midway, so the ending refines rather than transforms it.</comment>
