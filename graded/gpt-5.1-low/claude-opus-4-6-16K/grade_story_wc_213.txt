Closure is an Earned Stasis/Charged Poise hybrid: she forecloses the obedient path and settles into the cost-laden choice to listen, but the consequences remain offstage and somewhat abstract. The sanctuary’s hush and the granite itself become active at the end, literally hosting the “pulse” she’s been straining to hear. The place constrains the premise—solitude, altitude, siren network—and charges the final image without becoming over-described.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The central stake—duty to the Authority and the villages versus the chance to hear what lies beneath the noise—is clear early and tightens as zero hour approaches. That decisive move is strong, but the sense of cost and danger could be intensified; the potential harm to villages or the depth of institutional consequences never quite bite on the page. Tension remains more contemplative than fraught, which slightly softens the impact given the scale of the transgression.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Theme—listening versus noise; institutional protocol versus deeper, older forms of knowledge—is tightly integrated with character and setting. The ending reweights everything we’ve read through the discovery of the subterranean breathing, offering a quiet but resonant conceptual shift.</comment>
