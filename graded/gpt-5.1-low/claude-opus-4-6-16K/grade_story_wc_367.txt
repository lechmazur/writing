<question>2</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The piece functions as an accumulative/lyric single scene: climb → light → commune → prophecy → charged final image, all cleanly oriented in time and place. Closure lands as Charged Poise/Conceptual Repricing: dawn is not merely arriving but “being steered into existence,” reframing her routine as active world-shaping. Still, structurally tonight’s events don’t decisively alter options or status quo; it feels like a deepening vignette rather than a beat that clearly crosses a before/after threshold.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The emerald lighthouse, mineral-veined stone, and interior of angled glass/total internal reflection are exceptionally vivid and specific, with the setting doing conceptual work rather than just providing backdrop. Weather and sea are present as living pressures (storm in her teeth, shadows pooling) that shape what she can sense and do. The final sense of dawn being “steered…one reflected beam at a time” is inseparable from this constructed environment.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Maren’s attempt to shape a new kind of dawn/world through her fraught prophecy-ritual—is clear but remains largely abstract on this night. Pressure rises slightly with the incoming storm and involuntary prophecy about “ships…with sails made of questions,” yet nothing on the page forces a sharp trade-off or forecloses a specific option for her. Her agency here is endurance and continued participation in the ritual rather than a new decision under duress. The result is textured tension and mood, but limited escalation in terms of concrete cost or narrowed choices.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of light/shadow integration, memory versus amnesia, and the burden of accidental prophecy emerge organically through the lighthouse’s design and Maren’s role at the boundary of old and new worlds. A few lines verge on essayistic (“The new world was formless, terrified, lurching…”) but they still feel grounded in her worldview rather than detached authorial lecture. The ending reweights earlier motifs of reflection and gathering light in a thematically coherent way without stating a moral.</comment>

The narrative stance sits close enough to Maren to feel personal while occasionally widening into a lightly oracular register that suits the subject. There is no head-hopping or register whiplash; the voice modulates with intensity rather than drifting.</comment>

The final paragraph in particular marries cadence and concept effectively without muddle.</comment>
