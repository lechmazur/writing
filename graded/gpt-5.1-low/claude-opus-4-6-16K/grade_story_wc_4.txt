The environment directly charges the final images (vines climbing, lavender growing, archive resting) and functions as more than backdrop—it is the story’s argument. It stops just shy of 9+ because the environment, while beautifully evoked, doesn’t materially constrain her choices beyond tone and thematic charge.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake is largely conceptual/emotional: whether fragmented grief can be assembled into something like a masterpiece or healing gesture. There’s little real risk, loss, or narrowness of options; no serious alternative path is tested or foreclosed. As a result, the piece is satisfying on a lyric level but relatively soft on genuine escalation and cost.</comment>
