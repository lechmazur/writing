The final image of precedents breaking “like tide pools draining” effectively turns the setting into thematic architecture.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the labyrinth is curse or sacred journey, ruin or reverence—is clear and tied to a rigid social precedent. However, the town’s opposition remains largely off-stage, with no concrete pushback or consequence beyond unease; everything unfolds with minimal friction. That keeps the pressure more contemplative than truly testing, limiting the sense of paid cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Themes of memory versus repression, curse versus devotion, and how communities mislabel what they fear are woven through concrete images (orchids in shade, overlapping paths as a loom, lavender thread, ruin → reverence). The “carve a path from ruin to reverence” line edges toward articulating the thesis a bit too baldly, slightly undercutting subtext. Still, the closing image of “precedents broke like tide pools draining” leaves a resonant, non-didactic aftertaste.</comment>
