Closure lands as a decisive change with cost: Maren abandons her hard-won stasis in Grieve to walk into the wasteland. No orientation issues or causality breaks undercut the ending.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>9.0</grade><comment>The scrapyard settlement, copper-tasting dust, and fissured plains are vivid and efficiently sketched, always filtered through Maren’s priorities rather than as static backdrop. The wax leaf works as a micro-setting object that charges the final image when she holds it to the firelight and then packs it to go. World rules (resource war, territories of rust and silence) stay consistent and inform tone and choice.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Maren will remain in the safety of numb retirement or risk herself to seek her living daughter—is signaled early and tightens as she invests more time, hope, and identity into decoding the cylinder. The pressure deepens not through external attack but through the accumulating work and the emotionally loaded fragments (“Mama,” “safe,” “come”) culminating in the full, possibly misread sentence. Her final choice to leave Grieve clearly forecloses a path with real implied danger and emotional cost. A slightly more acute on-page challenge to that decision (e.g., a concrete competing obligation in Grieve) could have intensified the sense of sacrifice.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>9.0</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically from particulars: the tension between dead and living, distance and closeness, preservation and decay (bodies vs. leaf vs. cylinder). The final decision reframes her body-finding past into a life-finding present, quietly answering the central question of whether she will choose stasis among the dead or risk for the living. The lingering possibility that she has misinterpreted the grooves adds complexity and conceptual cost.</comment>

There is no head-hopping or register drift; the elevated diction reads like an omniscient-leaning but character-attuned storyteller rather than implausible inner monologue. Modulation at the end into the “wild hush” of the wind neatly matches the story’s emotional cadence.</comment>
