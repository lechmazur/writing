<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—witnessing and recording the last migration while restoring Dawa to the record—is clear and threads the whole scene. Lien’s agency is mostly in endurance and careful attention, plus the renunciatory act of dismantling; there is no serious on-page temptation to keep the structures or otherwise deviate. The conflict is effective for a quiet literary piece but doesn’t quite reach a charged, high-cost crux.</comment>
