<question>1</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>Maren’s motive (to fulfill the nonlinear inheritance and pursue forbidden knowledge) is clear and consistent, and her curator’s mindset shapes what she notices and how she thinks. The embodied moment of “rearrangement…vertebra by vertebra” is an excellent, particular way to show cognitive upheaval. Her final acceptance—choosing to carve and become a link—feels earned by the mirror realization. The journals, cameo, orchard, and bathroom all pay off without loose threads, and the nonlinearity is legible rather than gimmicky. The piece remains relatively low-pressure, but within its quiet frame the structure is tight.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The astral orchard is vivid and particular (“colors that existed nowhere else,” “honey and static electricity”), and the bathroom-as-temple of revelation is a sharp, characterful micro-setting. The environment is not mere backdrop: the impossible breathable air, the pulsing fruit, and the bathroom’s mirror all participate in the story’s logic of impossible-yet-coherent rules. The setting directly charges the final image, as her decision to carve and plant a new tree is inseparable from this orchard on a nameless asteroid. A bit more tactile interaction with the orchard under pressure could push this even higher.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake is Maren’s stance toward her inheritance: will she accept being bound into a nonlinear chain of obligation and love, or resist it. Pressure coheres and tightens in the bathroom, where her inquiry and the light-shift force a revaluation of her grandmother and of time itself. Her agency at the end—choosing to carve and implicitly to plant a new tree—resolves the internal conflict in favor of acceptance with a conceptual cost (loss of the illusion of linear autonomy). The conflict is thoughtful but mild; we never feel a serious possibility that she will refuse.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Thematic throughline—inheritance, temporality, and love moving backward—is tightly woven through journals, orchard, cameo, and mirror. However, that thesis is stated almost as a motto rather than left fully to image and implication, tipping slightly toward over-articulation. Even so, the piece invites reflection about time, agency, and familial obligation beyond its literal events.</comment>

POV boundaries are clean, with no leakage into knowledge Maren doesn’t plausibly have. The modulation from bewilderment to charged acceptance is handled smoothly in the narrative stance.</comment>

Rhythm varies well across description, realization, and the quieter closing beats. There are no notable scaffolding phrases or loops dragging momentum.</comment>
