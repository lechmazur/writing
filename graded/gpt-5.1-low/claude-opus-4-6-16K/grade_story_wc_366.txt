<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The northern chain of lighthouses, stone cairns, and the lantern room above an Atlantic storm feel vivid and lived-in, with details always filtered through Margit’s working familiarity. Recurring objects—the lamp, the sea glass, the stone stacks—charge the atmosphere and are reweighted at the end. The piece is more emotionally than tactically constrained by environment, which keeps it just short of the very top tier.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The dominant stakes are emotional and professional: maintaining safety across the chain and Margit’s stance toward connection versus anonymity. The cost—risking a sliver of vulnerability by entering the letter chain—registers clearly as a renunciation of her absolute silence. Pressure remains coherent but mostly interior and quietly scaled, which fits the piece yet keeps it from feeling fully relentless.</comment>

The final realization that she has been “trying to bottle the feeling of safety” and that their light in her hold “was never heavy” reweights every prior action with moving clarity. The ambiguity around authorship and anonymity keeps the piece from collapsing into a tidy moral.</comment>

Rhythm modulates well between summary and in-the-moment beats.</comment>

The central reconfiguration—that her silence and routine have been a form of carrying their light all along—lands as inevitable in hindsight.
