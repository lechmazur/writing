<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how Maren will respond to her mother’s demand for destruction and the impossibility of true preservation—is clear early and steadily tightened through the ritual. <question>5</question><grade>9.0</grade><comment>Theme is deeply woven: the mirage of safety, incomplete information as a way of making truth felt, the tension between preservation and erasure, and the honesty of the vanishing point all converge in specific images rather than abstract claims. The piece resists moralizing; it acknowledges the beauty and lie of safety without punishing Maren for needing both the ritual and the renunciation. The ending offers a resonant non-literal answer: true safety is a vanishing construct, and accepting that loss is the cost of any honest memorialization.</comment>
