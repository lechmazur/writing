Details like “tide pools glowed with bioluminescence” and salt deposits blurring lower paintings evolve with the narrative. The environment constrains options (hidden, ephemeral, tidal) and directly shapes what her act of painting can mean.</comment>

Her decision to paint and then leave the shell behind is an earned renunciation with a felt cost, though the tension stays more meditative than urgent. A touch more friction against her choice in the moment could intensify the sense of risk.</comment>
