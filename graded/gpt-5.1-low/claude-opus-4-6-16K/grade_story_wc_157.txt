Environmental objects (moths, plants, seed) quietly mirror themes of pattern, metamorphosis, and control. The setting stops just short of directly constraining a tactical choice at the climax, which keeps it below the very top band.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preserving untaxed, unsurveilled thought and communication—is clearly established and tightened when the Bureau’s botanical sensor threatens to expose their main tactic. However, the solution comes somewhat swiftly and cleanly; we don’t feel a serious trade-off or the narrowing of options beyond the conceptual elegance of the move. The stakes remain mostly abstract and ideological rather than viscerally pressured in the moment.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Themes of cognitive control, taxation of imagination, and language as both weapon and refuge emerge organically from premise, setting, and Maren’s choices. Recurring images—moths, grids of plants, the hum of Verek—accrue to a resonant sense of collective, embodied defiance. Complexity is present but could be deepened by sharper acknowledgment of the potential ethical ambiguities or costs of recruiting thirty-one people into this risk.</comment>

Maren’s counter-move (turning surveillance infrastructure into a language-distribution vehicle) is a satisfying, non-obvious yet inevitable-feeling twist.
