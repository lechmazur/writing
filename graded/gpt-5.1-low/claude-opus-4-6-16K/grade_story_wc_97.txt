Recurring objects (clock, reel, projector) are tightly woven into environment and stakes. This is very close to exemplary; a slight expansion of how the city below contrasts tactically with the arbor could push it into rarefied territory.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elena will accept the verdict of her tremor or attempt an “encore” against fate—is signaled clearly and tightens through the discovery of the maps and the ticking clock. Internal and external pressures interweave effectively, but the conflict remains largely interior; no on-page reversal or resistance beyond her own fear tests the commitment. As a result, cost is present but not maximally sharp.</comment>

A few lines (“She wanted to surprise fate with an unplanned encore”) tread close to thesis-statement territory but are still anchored in character desire. The piece invites reflection on what constitutes an ending without collapsing into platitude.</comment>
