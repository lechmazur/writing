The external conflict (potentially lethal systems failures) and internal conflict (purpose vs. stellar doubt) are sensibly interwoven. However, escalation remains largely conceptual; no on-page confrontation with immediate consequence forces a sharp trade-off, so the cost at the peak is more inferred than viscerally felt.</comment>

The final lines (“someone had to choose what came next… Especially then.”) offer a resonant, non-literal answer about ethical persistence in futility. Some thematic phrases are explicitly abstract (“intent… beneath the collapsing architecture of spacetime”), which is effective but just short of the subtlest possible integration.</comment>

The story commits to its strange premise and follows it into character-level questions about agency and purpose under temporal inversion.
