The whale carving as a micro-setting/object is effectively used in the final image as a charged tool of time and truth. Overall, atmosphere is lush without becoming disorienting.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Themes of truth vs. forgery, erasure vs. record, and the material weight of guilt emerge organically from specific images (false documents, driftwood alphabet, the clock hand pointing to a “time that demands to be acknowledged”). Symbolism around the sea’s remnants and time is strong but not over-explicit, and the ending image reweights prior material effectively. There’s minimal moralizing; the complexity of her complicity is allowed to stand.</comment>
