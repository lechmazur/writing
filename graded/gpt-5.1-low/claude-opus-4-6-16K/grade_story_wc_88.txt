<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Maren will truly confront the harm she’s done or use the procedure as escape—is clear and internal, and pressure increases as she descends, sees “They trusted you,” meets Oren, and finally faces the lake’s visions. The stakes are powerful but largely moral/psychic, with few concrete options visibly foreclosed beyond the implied loss of easy absolution.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of memory, accountability, and the insufficiency of erasure are well-embedded in objects (the baseball, the encoded newspaper, the lake as mnemonic reservoir) and in the arc from guilt-as-wound to guilt-as-compass.
