<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—decoding the arbor’s message and responding appropriately—is clear and present from early on. Still, there’s almost no resistance, uncertainty, or risk of failure; the arbor’s demands align perfectly with her desires and skills. As a result, escalation feels more like a graceful unfolding than a narrowing of options with real cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Themes of memory, reverence for the forgotten, and transformation of grief into celebration are organically embedded in seeds, monuments, and the folklore of the Moth Queen. Rhythmic control is good: long, lulling sentences in atmospheric passages, tightened phrasing at turns (e.g., “The message assembled itself”). There’s a mild tendency toward abstraction in a few phrases, but overall the voice is confident and distinctive.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The line-level craft is polished: sentences are clear, varied, and information-dense, often doing double duty for atmosphere and character. A few metaphors edge toward the ornate, but they don’t materially cloud causality or the closure image.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The fusion of a skyborne arbor, prophetic leaves, seed-sorting as vocation, and a folklore-based Moth Queen cosmology feels fresh and coherent. While the broad pattern (quiet caretaker becomes chosen translator of an ancient power) is familiar, the specific textures and mechanics distinguish it from template fantasy.
