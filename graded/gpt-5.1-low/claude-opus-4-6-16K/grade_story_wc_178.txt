Closure is solid as a conceptual repricing/Earned Stasis—her work is reweighted as part of an ancient covenant, and she chooses to persist—with a quiet but legible cost (accepting devotion without reciprocation). The recurring contrast of reconstructed interior and open desert sky charges the final courtyard moment. This is near top-tier micro-worldbuilding, slightly shy of perfection only because the environment constrains mood and meaning more than concrete tactical options.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake—what stance Miriam will take toward a possibly numinous pattern in an indifferent universe—is clear and coherent. Her final whispered covenant is an active choice to embrace devotion-without-answer as sufficient, which retroactively sharpens the earlier beats. The conflict remains largely internal and contemplative, so the sense of risk and cost is real but muted rather than knife-edge.</comment>

The final exchange—her vow versus the void’s silence—lands with clear emotional and conceptual resonance.
