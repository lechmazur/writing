<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Stakes are legible early (truth of the archive vs. loyalist erasure under an unstable interregnum) and escalate meaningfully once with the vellum message, then again when guards approach and the narrator opts to tear and smuggle records under direct threat. <question>5</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>Themes of absence, erased lives, and who controls the record emerge organically through small, concrete gestures (ration lines, nurses sharing water, a soldier lifting a child) rather than abstract exposition.
