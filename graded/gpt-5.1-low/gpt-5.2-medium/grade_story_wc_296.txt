<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—confront and undo the curse tied to the failed mirror trick and frozen confession—is clear, and pressure escalates from public humiliation to solitary descent, to confronting the coffin, to choosing how to dismantle the curse. External and internal stakes braid well, but an extra beat of resistance or potential loss would deepen the felt cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Themes of truth vs. vanity, mirrors as tools vs. moral choices, and the desire “to be seen without being known” are organically embedded in the magic system and the confession. However, the explicit line “the real enemy was the wish to be seen without being known” tips into direct moralizing, slightly flattening what the images and events have already made clear. The piece still invites reflection beyond its plot, but would be stronger letting the images carry the thesis without stating it.</comment>
