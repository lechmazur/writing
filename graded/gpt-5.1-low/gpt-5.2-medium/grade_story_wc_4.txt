<question>2</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The structure is cleanly accumulative: Mara begins with a private ritual, one encounter with Mr. Gant teaches the social pattern, and this scales outward into a community practice that culminates in the candlelit walk-through. Closure lands as a resonant reconfiguration—the town’s stance shifts from isolated grief to shared ownership of hurt, and Mara’s “doorway” exists on the page. Causality is soft but coherent; each expansion logically follows the previous beat without contrivance. The main limitation is that there’s little sense of a risk that the project might fail or fracture, so the final success feels more inevitable than pressured.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The lavender field and rusted factory are vividly rendered and recur as charged anchors—the scent vs. metallic taste, the vines climbing ruins, and the placement of acetate on the fence all knit environment to emotion. Setting is not postcard-static; it evolves (vines rising, storm, power outage, candles) and actively shapes the form and feel of the memorial-walk. Specific details like the chain-link fence, broken windows, and pallet scanning table keep the space tactile and legible. The environment clearly charges the final image of communal walking and candlelight-infused photos.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Pressure here is primarily situational and emotional rather than conflictual: the town’s unresolved grief and the anniversary create a low, steady tension. The escalation is a density increase—one person’s photo becomes many, individual stories become a crowd, then the storm tests their commitment and they collectively hold the sheets in the rain. The stakes (whether grief remains isolated vs. shared) are clear but rarely feel in jeopardy, as there’s no substantive resistance to Mara’s project beyond initial quietness. As a result, the ending is moving but not sharply hard-won.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically from particulars: photographs as arcs linking lives, lavender scent as a counterpoint to metallic trauma, and the shift in language from “your loss” to “our hurt” beautifully embody collective healing without explicit moralizing. The final emphasis on “living hands holding the story together” and a masterpiece “everyone had made” reprice the whole premise in resonant terms. There’s a hint of neatness, but it stops short of sentimentality.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The fusion of archival work, puzzle logic, and a communal grief ritual built from matching arcs of photographs feels fresh and particular rather than templated. The piece takes a quiet structural risk by relying on accumulative texture instead of conventional plot, and largely earns it. Its only constraint is that the emotional arc resolves a bit too cleanly to reach the most radical band of originality.</comment>
