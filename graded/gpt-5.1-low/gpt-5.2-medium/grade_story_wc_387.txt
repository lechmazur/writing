<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether quiet, “invisible” vigilance and labor matter—is signaled early and tightened when sliced market nets introduce an external threat. Escalation is modest but effective: from teaching and philosophical musing to an applied test where trade is actively endangered and a smuggler must be caught. Ren’s agency in placing guards based on the pattern of cuts constitutes a genuine narrowing of options and an earned resolution. The primary cost is conceptual and social (remaining largely uncredited, renouncing the desire for loud proof of worth), which lands but keeps the pressure in a quiet register.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The theme of hidden patterns and unseen effort is tightly integrated with events and images: whisk ribs, knotwork, currents, fireflies, and stone whispers all echo and deepen it. However, some lines of dialogue state the philosophy quite directly (“every repair was a philosophy,” “you honor what cannot be seen”), nudging toward didactic rather than purely emergent resonance. The final quiet act of whisking tea on the parapet still offers a strong, non-literal thematic answer that lingers.</comment>

POV distance is steady third-person with close access to perception but without head-hopping or unjustified knowledge claims. Rhythm and diction modulate well between lush description and more clipped, purposeful dialogue or action beats.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The prose is dense with imagery (“clouds left the moon in shreds,” “air was scintillatingly somber”) yet generally avoids muddle or mixed metaphors; most lines do multiple jobs (setting, character, and theme) without feeling padded. Sentence rhythm varies and momentum carries smoothly from atmosphere into instruction and then into the small investigative climax. There are no noticeable paraphrase loops or scaffold sentences; almost every line advances either the emotional arc or the pattern motif. The only minor limitation is that figurative language is frequent enough that a few images feel ornamental rather than strictly pressured by scene needs.</comment>

Recurring motifs (whisk, nets, moonlight, currents) are organically tied to character and stakes rather than grafted on.
