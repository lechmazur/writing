<question>4</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Mara will continue to expunge Bellford’s truth or re-open it for the living—is clear and does tighten as more of the town resurfaces and her father’s complicity emerges. However, external resistance and concrete consequences remain mostly implied rather than dramatized on the page, so pressure feels more contemplative than acute. The internal/external conflicts do interweave effectively, but without a sharp on-page test beyond her own resolve.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Themes of institutional erasure, inherited guilt, narrative responsibility, and how communities live with buried harms are woven through concrete particulars—photos, reports, flood marks, artifacts—rather than abstract statements. The chime’s silence and later sanctioned song embody the shift from suppression to acknowledgment without a didactic moral. Ambiguity remains—Mara’s choice doesn’t undo past deaths—yet the piece takes a clear ethical stance against expunging the drowned.</comment>

The twist that the haunting is bureaucratic—voices ignored to meet a deadline, then buried in paperwork—is conceptually rich.
