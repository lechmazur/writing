<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The mangrove labyrinth, stilt house, salt-filmed window, and condensation-writing spirits form a richly textured, coherent environment that shapes what is possible—she must pass through roots “like questions,” use the house and window as her interface, and rely on moonlight to heal the shard. Setting details are not decorative; the window is the literal and symbolic plane of communication, and the condensation fading in the final image charges closure. Weather and water are integrated into action via floating letter strips and the spirits’ singing in the leaves. The world’s rules (shard memory, moonlight healing, condensation messages) are consistent and taught through use, not exposition.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Mara can transform a paralyzing, unsaid apology into a spoken one without erasing the intervening years—is clear early and tightens as she sacrifices her written apologies and confronts the spirits’ refusals (“DO NOT CHOOSE YET,” then “NOT BACK, BEFORE”). The key deepening comes when she uses the shard to re-experience the courthouse moment, gaining a more precise sense of the hinge she’s been chasing. Elias’s appearance forces her into a live, relational test rather than a purely internal exercise, and choosing to speak to him forecloses the safety of perpetual rehearsal. The main limitation is that external stakes feel mostly emotional and spiritual, with little sense of what she risks losing now beyond her pride, though the story partially compensates through tonal intensity.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically: apologies, unsent communication, and the desire to inhabit choice-points instead of living with decisions made in isolation. Objects and rules (unsent letters, moon-healed shard, condensation messages) all embody aspects of revision, rehearsal, and the cost of finally speaking. The line that unsent is “a state of courage” reweights the premise without lapsing into moralizing, and the spirits’ refusal to offer a rewind insists on living with consequence rather than fantasy. The ending offers a resonant, non-literal answer—connection happens when she risks saying the apology “here,” not in a perfected elsewhere—without spelling out a tidy lesson.</comment>

Rhythmic control is generally strong, though there is some tendency toward long, similarly cadenced sentences that risk a slight blur in high-pressure beats. When brief dialogue appears (“SAY IT HERE”), it lands with weight and subtext rather than exposition.</comment>

A few sentences verge on overload or breathlessness, particularly in chained clauses, which slightly dulls impact in places and could be tightened for elite-level polish. Still, there are no serious clarity failures, and the final image of condensation fading because the choice is made is clean and memorable.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The synthesis of elements—mediumship focused on unsent apologies, a gargoyle shard that remembers, mangrove spirits writing in condensation, and the idea of seeking “the moment before deciding”—feels genuinely fresh rather than stitched from familiar tropes. Emotional truth underlies the magic system, so the supernatural devices never read as gimmicks. The choice to make the ultimate power not resurrection but the reconfiguration of a living relationship is a subtle, non-obvious turn that deepens the premise.</comment>
