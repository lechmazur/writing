<question>4</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake is the survival and spread of a fragile, subversive literacy/hope network under conditions of surveillance and civic fracture, which is signaled early and steadily deepened. <question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Themes of “beauty from anarchy,” mutual aid, and literacy as insurgent cultivation emerge organically from seeds, books, and the twin libraries rather than from overt moralizing. The ending line about fracture shining if allowed to bloom reweights the entire narrative as a manifesto in miniature without collapsing into a tidy slogan. A couple of lines (“blooming resistance starts in dirt and in language”) come close to stating the thesis outright, slightly reducing the subtextual subtlety.</comment>

Dialogue is sparse but effective, carrying subtext (Mara’s question about what else could fit in the tin) and advancing both relationship and theme.</comment>
