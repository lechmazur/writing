Object-as-setting (mirror, shutters, cord) is especially strong and deeply integrated. Agency is shared: he engineers conditions, but she must look, recognize, and then speak to the authorities. The conflict is more moral/emotional than physically perilous, which slightly limits the visceral sense of risk but fits the story’s scale.</comment>

The final image reweights the entire story: perception itself becomes an ethical demand, not a neutral act. Ambiguity remains—he doesn’t forgive her; the men who burned the boat still exist—but the moral stance is clear without sermonizing.</comment>
