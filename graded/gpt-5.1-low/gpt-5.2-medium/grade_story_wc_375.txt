Closure lands as a Resonant Reconfiguration/Decisive Change when she accepts the larger vocation and activates the hidden stage with her mural. The main structural limitation is that external resistance is minimal, so the lock problem resolves relatively smoothly once she chooses to stay and solve it.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The subterranean crystal cave is vivid and specific (“walls glitter like frozen music,” “auroral sorrow”) and clearly filtered through an artist’s perception. Environmental features meaningfully constrain choices: the broken dressing-room lock, the quartz door, the crystals (as light source, emotional register, and literal wedge), and the pigments that enable the climactic mural. World rules stay coherent, and the fairground frame ties surface and underground spaces into a single ecology. A hair less description in the fairground opener and a touch more tactile interaction with the cave under pressure would move this toward the very top tier.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Mara answers or refuses the inherited calling—is clearly signaled and does tighten as she moves from tunnel to fissure to ruined lock to notebook. Her decision to “rectify what can be rectified” by making an opening art-piece, and later to use the pigments to awaken the stage, provides genuine agency-driven turns. However, opposition largely remains atmospheric (sorrow, ruin) and technical (a broken lock) rather than truly threatening, so the sense of risk or sacrifice is muted. The escalation is thus more contemplative than pressured, effective but not bracing.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of suppressed creativity, inherited vocation, and art as healing/memory emerge organically from objects and actions: the ruined lock meant to protect laughter, the notebook of jokes and cues, portraits that can “sing.” The final image—her mother’s smile returning as “living glow” and the stage waking—reweights the earlier duty-versus-self tension without stating a moral. Some thematic material is delivered a bit on-the-nose in interpretive sentences about why her mother hid the book and what the “true calling” is, trimming subtlety. Even so, the afterimage is emotionally resonant and conceptually coherent.</comment>

The narrative confidently withholds exposition in favor of sensate detail and motivated reflection, e.g., the joke-rhythm memory cue. At times the register edges toward the ornate, but it remains internally consistent and controlled.</comment>

A few metaphors border on density/purple (“as if the crystals remember brighter colors they can no longer throw across the sky”), risking slight over-saturation. Minor tightening of explanatory clauses around the mother’s intent would sharpen momentum without cost.</comment>
