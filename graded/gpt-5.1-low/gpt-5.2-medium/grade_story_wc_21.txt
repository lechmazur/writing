<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The central conflict—retrieve and reveal versus protect the vulnerable—is clearly articulated and escalates from eerie curiosity to an explicit negotiation with Lila’s residue. Internal and external pressures interweave well, though the external danger (from deputies, state, or others) remains largely retrospective, making the stakes feel slightly more conceptual than immediate. The final commitment to redact names until consent ties theme cleanly to action, avoiding neat moral closure while acknowledging danger. The one explicit thematic statement in dialogue flirts with didacticism but is anchored enough in character and stakes to avoid feeling like a thesis drop.</comment>
