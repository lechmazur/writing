<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The greenhouse/orchard in an endless-night, winter-locked valley is vivid and constantly active: panes, heaters, frozen rivers, and moonlight sap all constrain what Eli can do. The final pear that “glowed with bottled dawn” is an excellent charged-object culmination of the setting. On a ruthless line, a tiny trim of descriptive redundancy would sharpen impact without dulling atmosphere.</comment>

Eli’s choice to return the bead and match his heartbeat to it is a true foreclosure with cost, not a cosmetic gesture. The main place left to intensify would be a more explicit moment where not complying remains a live, tempting option before he commits.</comment>

The recurring bead and pear motifs serve as quiet but potent thematic carriers without blunt allegory. A slightly sharper articulation—through action, not statement—of Eli’s complicity in the old riot could deepen the moral knot even further.</comment>
