World rules around the circles and the occupied/abandoned status of the port feel coherent with minimal exposition. The environment doesn’t physically constrain her options much, but it meaningfully shapes both method and mood, especially in the final image.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether to risk breaking the lull and reawakening the rebellion—is clear and threads the piece. Opposition remains off-stage (the Directorate, jammers, the danger of being discovered), which keeps tension somewhat abstract; the sudden footsteps arrive very late and function more as a final charge than a true complicating turn. The conflict works and pays off, but at this level a bit more on-page resistance or sharper sense of what she stands to lose would deepen it.</comment>
