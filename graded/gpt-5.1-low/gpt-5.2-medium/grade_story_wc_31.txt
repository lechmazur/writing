<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether and how he can authentically “discover the color of sound” and change his relationship to honesty—is clear early and meaningfully tightened by the descent into the wreck and the encounter with the luminous vows. Tension operates more as spiritual pressure than imminent threat, which is effective but a bit gentle for top-band stakes.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Themes of perception, truth-as-shield vs truth-as-lens, and the ethics of witnessing (“to let sound paint me and then pass”) emerge organically from concrete images and actions rather than explicit moralizing. The final covenant to keep walking the pier so others can “find their own colors in the dark” offers a resonant, non-literal answer to the opening question about the color of sound. A hair more on-page friction around the cost of this new stance could deepen the thematic charge even further.</comment>
