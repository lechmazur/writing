Closure lands solidly as a Decisive Change plus Resonant Reconfiguration: the hush around the orchard breaks and Mara’s stance toward “unfinished” beauty crystallizes. The main limitation is that the unbinding proceeds smoothly on first attempt, with little on-page sense that failure or misstep would significantly alter options.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The setting is exceptionally textured and particular—Echo Harbor, shipwreck-ringed sunken greenhouse, ash-stained palms, glass ribs “like a whale’s skeleton,” all filtered through Mara’s acoustic focus. Environmental rules are consistent: the hush actually alters behavior (no creaks, gulls’ silent cries, speech feeling rude) and the cistern’s unrippling water physically embodies the knot. Place constrains tactics: she must go inland to this specific greenhouse, attend to the cistern, and use the bowl/violin there for the attempt. The recurring bowl and the harbor’s auditory landscape strongly charge the final image of her walking back, though the greenhouse itself, while integral, could in theory be swapped with another singular ruin without breaking logic.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Mara can relieve the hush enough to keep meaningfully listening/playing in this altered world—is established early (council shuttering the hall, her gift turned burden). However, stakes remain largely conceptual and aesthetic; there’s minimal sense of personal risk, irreversibility, or cost if she fails or misjudges the unbinding. The conflict is beautifully rendered but gentle—more puzzle and ritual than true crucible.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Themes of unfinishedness, beauty in incompletion, and listening as an ethical act are woven through the bonfire stories, the stubborn half-formed fruit, and the hush-as-knot metaphor. The mendicant’s bowl and the notion of vows knotted into silence offer a strong metaphorical apparatus that the ending reweights: she doesn’t restore the old world but permits incompleteness to sound “in public.” The final line lightly underlines the thesis (“magnificence… simple and still unfinished”) in a way that risks a touch of explicitness but mostly feels earned by prior images. Subtext around grief, post-catastrophe adaptation, and the politics of what gets silenced is present but kept decorously in the background.</comment>

There’s no dialogue to test, but the nonverbal interaction with Jory and the restrained exposition of family lore both feel organic to this voice. The prose sustains a high descriptive density yet maintains clarity and forward motion through the scene.
