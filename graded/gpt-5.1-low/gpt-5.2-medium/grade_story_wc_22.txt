<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether a new garden-oath can meaningfully replace a drowned sea-concord—is clear and threads the piece. However, opposition is mostly atmospheric; success comes without a clear on-page risk of failure beyond implication, keeping the conflict more contemplative than fraught. The story earns a satisfying deepening rather than a sharp narrowing of options.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Theme emerges powerfully from particulars: treaties with nature and the dead, labor as liturgy, language as a means of bargaining with history. Complexity remains: the sea is still empty, the reef is still dead, and the garden’s promise is real but not redemptive in a cheap way.</comment>
