<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The cavern orchard is vivid, strange, and consistently filtered through Lysa’s priorities—roots, stone branches, and stalactite drops all matter to the spell, not just as décor. The orchard’s atmosphere mirrors the emotional stakes (ambiguous, half-living space) without sliding into blunt allegory. This is publish-ready setting work; only the sheer density of images risks, very slightly, tipping toward decorative in places.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how to live with Orren’s ambiguous loss—is signaled early and steadily intensified as the ritual begins to work and the seam opens. The price is clear: she renounces the fantasy of clean retrieval in favor of a harder, unresolved connection. Conflict remains primarily internal and contemplative, so moment-to-moment tension sometimes feels more meditative than sharply pressured, which keeps this just short of the very top band.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Theme and image are tightly braided: mathematics, chipped mosaics, missing eyes, and ambiguous thresholds all accrue toward a nuanced meditation on unresolved grief. However, the explicit statement of that “axiom beneath grief” drifts into overt thematic articulation instead of letting the images and choices carry all the weight. The piece still achieves a strong lingering aftereffect, but the on-the-nose line slightly undercuts the otherwise exemplary subtlety.</comment>
