<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The central stake—whether kindness can be proven to persist—is clearly signaled and tightened by Ro’s pained half-recognition and the experiment to paint an “alphabet of remembered kindnesses.” Pressure escalates in a quiet, accumulative way as the writing spreads and the first strangers respond with offerings, giving the abstract goal visible risk and reward. The cost is primarily emotional vulnerability and effort rather than a concrete loss or risk of harm, which suits the mode but keeps tension gentle. A sharper moment of potential failure (e.g., a night when no one responds, or Ro almost leaves unconvinced) would deepen the sense of jeopardy.</comment>

Sayeed’s line about kindness changing handwriting is slightly aphoristic but mostly earned by the surrounding specificity. <question>6</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The third-person voice is lyrically confident with a clear perceptual bias toward light, language, and small acts of care, matching Mira’s sensibility. Dialogue is sparse but natural and subtextual, especially Ro’s admission that the marks make his chest hurt, which carries both memory and fear. Emotional truths about mistrust, exhaustion, and the quiet radicalism of mutual care ground the more fantastical conceit.
