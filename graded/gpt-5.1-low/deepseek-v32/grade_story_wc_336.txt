Closure lands with a clear before/after: the seed diminished but active, the other world saved, the orchard permanently transformed, and the dull wind dispelled. Tools and environment—the silver knife, the gnarled apple over the falls—constrain how gateways can be opened and where the crucial transfusion occurs. The final rainbowed mist and radiant hush recharge the initial atmosphere in a way that supports the thematic turn.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preserving the color of wonder in the face of encroaching dullness—comes through early and tightens when the crystal world’s “desperate thirst” makes inaction untenable. The main escalation is Elara’s choice to spend some of the seed’s finite radiance to save another realm, creating a genuine trade-off. However, opposition is mostly abstract and never bites back on the page; once she chooses, success feels assured and relatively easy. A bit more resistance or risk—internal or external—around that transfer would sharpen the pressure.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The thematic core—wonder is preserved by sharing and interweaving, not hoarding—is organically present in the mechanics of the plot and the orchard’s transformation. A touch more trust in implication over statement would elevate the thematic subtlety.</comment>
