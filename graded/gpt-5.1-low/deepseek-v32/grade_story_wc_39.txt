<question>1</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Arlo’s core motivation—to preserve and translate the place’s vanishing voices—is clear and consistently enacted, and his micro-choices around the jars and the music box align with that drive. However, we get little in-scene contradiction or genuine struggle; his final decision to stay in the loft is emotionally right but comes with only lightly sketched cost. <question>3</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The “feral loft beneath the dunes,” jars, gulls, marram grass, and tide-sighs create a vivid, cohesive micro-world that feels lived-in rather than touristic. While the environment doesn’t harshly constrain tactics, it clearly shapes what’s possible and what matters.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Arlo can find and preserve the “vital word” of his sound-dictionary and thus avoid being the last, futile listener—is clear but gently pressured. There’s little external opposition or real risk of failure beyond implication, so tension remains low-key and textural rather than gripping. Raising the felt cost of failure or including a moment of genuine doubt or near-loss around the music box would strengthen escalation.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Themes of memory, intergenerational transmission, and redemption through attentive art emerge organically from concrete actions: jars of sound, the dead net-mender’s shanty, and Arlo’s shift from coins to “preserved time” as currency. The aftertaste is quietly resonant rather than didactic, though a hair less abstraction in the closing paragraph would deepen that effect.</comment>
