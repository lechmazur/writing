<question>2</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The accumulative structure, framed by the kettle’s boil, is clean and satisfying: whispers → murmur → whistle → aftermath map neatly onto her emotional progression. Closure lands as an Earned Stasis/Resonant Reconfiguration when she recognizes the carving as a “tool for living” and resumes work. The drive lens (textural accumulation around grief-as-containment) is clear and consistent.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The skylight loft, rain, kettle, and ash wood are vividly rendered and always filtered through Marta’s state, creating a strong, unified atmosphere. The environment isn’t mere backdrop: the rain’s tempo, the boiling kettle, and diffused light all modulate the emotional beat and charge the final image. The “capsule” of the loft and the rain-beaded glass concretize her suspended state between past and present. Setting choices are selective and purposeful rather than catalogued.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The central stake—whether Marta is overwhelmed by grief or can build a container sturdy enough to hold it—is clear and mostly well-pressured. Tension escalates through the movement from handling the blank, to memory of the storm, to consciously hollowing “not with sadness, but to make room.” The kettle’s rise to a whistle gives a subtle but effective tempo shift. However, the conflict remains entirely internal and gently modulated, so escalation reads as deepening rather than a sharp narrowing of options.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Thematically, the story does rich work with grief-as-containment, “here and gone,” and crystallized time; the carving becomes a non-literal answer to how to live with loss. Recurring motifs (rain, rust-hued peace, vessels, negative space) are reweighted by the ending without feeling contrived. A few lines verge on explicit thesis (“not about ending grief, but about building a structure sturdy enough to hold it”), slightly reducing subtlety. Overall, the theme is integrated through concrete actions rather than pure abstraction.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is lyrically controlled and coherent with Marta’s sensibility, with distinctive phrasing like “rust-hued peace” and “a quiet capsule suspended between grief and the living city below.” Diction and rhythm are consistent, and the narrative distance remains steady. The prose modulates gently with emotional intensity (e.g., at the kettle whistle) without losing its texture. There are no POV slips or register breaks.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Line-level craft is strong: sentences are varied, dense with meaning, and largely free of redundancy or scaffolding. One could argue the metaphor density edges toward lush, but it does not blur clarity or stall momentum.</comment>
