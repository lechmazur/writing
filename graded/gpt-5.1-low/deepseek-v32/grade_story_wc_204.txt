<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—capturing eternity in a finite medium—is clear, and the rupture plus destabilization of the library offers real potential cost. The main tightening is the press’s destabilization and the appearance of the rupture, but these complications don’t meaningfully narrow options. As a result, tension feels more lyrical than pressured, limiting the sense of hard-won achievement.</comment>

However, the final explicit statement—“some systems needed destabilizing to awaken greater truths”—tips toward moralizing and undercuts some of the subtextual power. The piece invites reflection but lands a bit too neatly on its thesis.</comment>
