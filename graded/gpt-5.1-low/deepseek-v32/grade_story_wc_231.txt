The “borrowed memories” ability is introduced and enacted smoothly enough to feel like a world rule rather than a deus ex machina. Cost is present but understated—Arvid assumes the burden of knowing and carrying the story of the dead man, though that price could be made a shade more explicit on the page.</comment>

The recurring image of the opal dock and the mist at dawn subtly charges the ending, even if it doesn’t heavily constrain choice. This is publish-ready atmospherically, with very minor room to deepen how the physical space shapes tactics.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Arvid’s need to understand and honor the anonymous dead man before officialdom arrives—is clear but remains low-intensity. Pressure escalates modestly when the first contact with the flower shocks him and then when he chooses to re-engage, yet there’s little resistance or real risk that his efforts might fail or harm him. The constable’s impending arrival and the fog delay offer structural pressure but aren’t fully weaponized as ticking-clock tension. As a result, the story feels more like a beautifully executed vignette than a fully pressurized narrative.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Themes of isolation, witness, fidelity to memory, and the dignity of the unknown dead emerge organically from Arvid’s role and the Edelweiss motif. The mountain/sea contrast and the idea of a flower as a vessel of promise build a coherent meditation on promises that outlive their makers. The piece stops short of more troubling ambiguities (e.g., ethics of inventing or channeling a life), which keeps it strong but not quite radical in resonance.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is consistent and well-controlled, with a measured, slightly formal diction that suits an introspective, solitary keeper. POV boundaries are respected; we never leave Arvid’s consciousness except through the explicitly magical borrowed memories. A slight tendency toward uniformly elevated phrasing in all beats keeps it just short of truly exceptional modulation under pressure.</comment>

Momentum is maintained despite the reflective tone, and transitions between physical action and interior experience are clean. There is minor risk of over-ornamentation in cumulative effect, but it doesn’t materially dull the closure image.</comment>

The narrative takes a relatively safe structural path (simple, linear, quiet), so the ingenuity lies primarily in texture and motif rather than bold form. Still, the final reweighting of Arvid’s role gives the piece a distinctive, memorable angle.</comment>
