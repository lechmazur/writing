<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether and how to resolve an unsent apology and free the spirits/labyrinth from stasis—is signaled early and followed through. As a result, cost and tension feel subdued, and the decision, while thematically weighty, doesn’t feel hard-won on the page.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of apology, forgiveness, and the idea that some apologies must be lived rather than spoken are tightly woven through letters, shard, condensation, and spirits. However, the piece tends to state its insights directly (“some apologies are lived, not spoken,” “The timeframe of unspoken apology was over”) instead of trusting image and implication. That explicitness flattens subtext and keeps the thematic texture from feeling fully organic or quietly haunting.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The prose is clean and grammatical, with mostly clear sequencing and a coherent rhythm; there are no glaring line-level confusions. Still, the arc of mystical journey → inner vision → quiet resolution is familiar, and the execution sometimes leans on standard spiritual language rather than fully idiosyncratic insight.
