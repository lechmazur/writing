The environment doesn’t sharply constrain choices, but it credibly shapes the contemplative, ritualistic mode of the scene.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.3</grade><comment>The dominant stake is Eli’s relationship to his own trauma—whether it remains isolating or becomes communicable—and this is clearly signaled. The key “turn” is his decision to share with others, but that choice feels prefigured from early lines and not significantly tested. As a result, conflict reads more like steady meditation than a tightening crucible.</comment>

The final paragraphs in particular lean on explicit moralizing rather than letting image and action carry the weight. The resonance is present but could be stronger with more implication and less declared insight.</comment>

Still, control of distance and clarity remains solid throughout.</comment>

Execution tends toward safe, earnest lyricism rather than formally or emotionally risky choices. The story feels solidly crafted but not yet surprising or idiosyncratic enough for the very top tier.</comment>
