The atmosphere of post-blackout recovery and archival technology supports the story’s preoccupation with memory and connection. The final image of the lab as a beacon rooted in this environment effectively charges the closure.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>4.8</grade><comment>Stakes remain almost entirely abstract—understanding “the texture of connection” and helping communal healing—without ever facing a concrete obstacle or meaningful risk. Every procedure Elara attempts essentially works; there’s no serious resistance, setback, or trade-off. As a result, escalation is minimal: each new discovery is a variation on success rather than a tested gain. The ending is emotionally warm but underpressured, lacking a moment where something real might be lost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>Theme is coherent—connection as something textured, woven from fragments, facilitated by questioning assumptions and shared memory—and the miner’s lamp/lab motifs serve it well. The artificial pilgrimage and post-blackout setting hint at richer complexities (e.g., who gets remembered, what’s irretrievably lost) that are not fully explored. The final paragraphs deliver resonance, but in a fairly tidy, platitudinal register.</comment>

POV distance is steady, with no head-hopping or stance confusion. The main limitation is a certain generic-poetic smoothness that avoids sharper, more idiosyncratic textures of thought or perception.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>6.4</grade><comment>The prose is clean and largely free of syntactic or grammatical errors, and many images (prism-song splitting silence into color, worn glass as texture) are effective. The line-level rhythm is fairly uniform, with many similarly structured declarative sentences and minimal dynamic variation. The effect is pleasant but slightly numbing, softening the impact of peak beats.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The core imaginative blend—post-blackout sound laboratory, prism-song bells, a miner’s lamp lens as temporal key, and “artificial pilgrimage” through archives—is genuinely fresh. However, the narrative arc itself (gentle genius heals community with art/technology) is familiar and proceeds without surprising turns or moral complications. The ingenuity lies more in the worldbuilding motifs than in the emotional or dramatic patterning.</comment>
