His final act feels earned but emotionally undercomplicated—more triumph of method than of a conflicted self. The cost is sketched (exhaustion, the caves’ shattering) but not deeply felt through his body and psyche.</comment>

Closure lands as a decisive change with on-page consequences: the storm is ended, the beings transformed, Kael depleted, and the caves reconfigured. The only limiting factor is that the beings’ intrusion and the world-scale reversal resolve very smoothly, with minimal complication or surprise in the final turn.</comment>

The recurring contrast between shrieking sky and blue-white interior radiance effectively charges the final image of a “silent, luminous heart.” Detail is weighted well to stakes rather than devolving into static description.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—countering the chaotic energy beings and saving the world’s precarious calm—is clear from early on and escalates with their direct invasion of the cave. The resolution is driven by his informed choice rather than luck, and the price (his exhaustion, transformation of the beings and caves) is explicit. What’s missing is a sharper sense of risk or trade-off in the moment—there’s little doubt or serious possibility of failure once he “realizes the truth.”</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Thematic strands—seeking quiet without annihilation, harmonizing chaos rather than opposing it with more force—are embodied well in the bloom of hush transforming the beings into lichen. The ripple-marks, fracture patterns, and “hammered repose” collectively suggest a world where peace is an ongoing, costly forging, which the ending reconfigures as “settled, earned peace.” However, the piece edges toward explicit thesis statements (“not to fight noise with noise, but to find the quiet in the storm and set it free”), slightly flattening subtext. The resonance is strong but would benefit from a touch more ambiguity and less overt moral articulation.</comment>

Psychic distance is steady, and there is no head-hopping or stance confusion.
