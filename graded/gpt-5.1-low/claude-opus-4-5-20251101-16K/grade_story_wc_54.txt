<question>2</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The causal chain is tight: surveying damage leads to discovering the box, which leads to Chen’s designs, which in turn generate the alternative reclamation plan and her final decision. <question>3</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The abandoned spaceport is vivid and specific—“cathedral built for departures,” frozen countdowns, broken holograms—filtering through an engineer’s eye in a way that feels lived-in rather than touristic. The recurring juxtaposition of ghost-carnival and rust desert supports the emotional tone without overstatement. Setting already charges the final image of the desert and emergency lights; a slightly stronger sense of physical constraint during her design process would be needed to reach the very top band.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—the fate of the terminal and what it represents for Maya’s sense of possibility—is clear, with a meaningful tightening when she realizes she can’t save everything and must argue for a partial resurrection. Internal and external conflicts are braided: committee expectations vs. her emerging philosophy of “realistic dreams.” However, antagonistic pressure is mostly abstract (no on-page pushback, no immediate risk beyond implied professional disapproval), so tension never quite sharpens into a hard trade-off under fire. The escalation is more conceptual than lived, which limits felt cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>Theme coheres strongly around reclamation, constrained hope, and navigation through ruin; Chen’s pump-less systems and the sextant nicely embody an ethic of working with constraints rather than overpowering them. The ending’s navigation metaphor is apt but veers into near-thesis (“Navigation wasn’t about…”), edging toward didactic rather than fully implied. More reliance on image and less on explicit formulation in the final lines would deepen subtextual power.</comment>

Structural and thematic moves remain relatively safe; the story doesn’t take major formal risks or upend expectations beyond offering a quiet, modestly scaled victory.
