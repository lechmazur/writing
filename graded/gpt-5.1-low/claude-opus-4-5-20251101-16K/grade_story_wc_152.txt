<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: years of observation → recognition of a coup → lack of authority → strategy to encode warnings for Adara’s visit. Structure is simple but sound—a single continuous scene in the observatory with embedded backstory anchored to present stimuli. <question>3</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The cobalt observatory, cracked dome slats, eternal blue flame, and autumn wind are vividly rendered and filtered through Mira’s work-focused perception. Setting isn’t just backdrop: the observatory’s status, the eternal candle, and the planned eclipse visit create the physical and social context that makes her plan possible. Weather and architecture (wind through cracks, leaves on tiles, blue shadows on silk) reinforce the mood of quiet, precarious watchfulness without feeling like generic atmosphere. The environment doesn’t physically constrain choices in a dramatic way, but it meaningfully charges the final image and logic of the story.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—averting or exposing a coup with almost no direct power—is clear, and there’s a real problem-solving beat when Mira shifts from diagnosis to tactic (using Adara and the altered chart). However, the conflict is largely one-sided; no antagonist pressure or obstacle pushes back in-scene, so escalation remains intellectual rather than visceral. Time pressure (three nights; coup within a fortnight) is stated but never tightened further on the page, which keeps the tension from peaking. The ending earns a credible crystallization of stance, but the visible cost and narrowing of options could be more sharply dramatized.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of overlooked labor, encoded truth, and wisdom masked as foolishness emerge organically from Mira’s status, her craft, and the conspirators’ disdain. The final lines (“Fools, after all, were never suspected of saving kingdoms”) edge close to explicit moralizing but are still grounded enough in character to avoid feeling like a tagline. Overall the piece invites reflection on power, perception, and who gets listened to, without heavy-handed abstraction.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is controlled, slightly elevated, and consistent with Mira’s reflective, craft-driven mind. Psychic distance remains stable; we stay in her perceptions and inferences without head-hopping or unearned omniscience about others’ inner states. The lyricism modulates gently with pressure (her planning feels more taut than earlier observation), though a sharper rhythmic shift at the tactical turn could deepen impact.</comment>

There’s minimal scaffolding or looped phrasing; most lines carry multiple functions (world, mood, and character) efficiently. A bit more restraint in the final paragraph’s figurative stacking would sharpen the closure image further.</comment>

Still, the piece doesn’t quite push into surprising moral or structural territory; outcomes and roles align with expectations for this setup.
