<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether and how the dwindling light will be witnessed and remembered—is clear, but pressure remains largely static. Her agency is endurance and attention rather than overt decision, which is valid but underplayed in terms of visible trade-off on the page. As a result, tension is present but never quite tightens into a palpable narrowing of options.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Theme—witnessing the end, the value of testament in the face of inevitable loss, and the articulation of silence—is beautifully woven through particulars (pebble-shore training, gold flake absences, soldiers’ shifting questions). There’s no moralizing; the piece trusts images and situation to carry its meditation on memory vs. oblivion. Complexity is maintained, though the meditation is more elegiac than interrogative, which keeps it slightly below the very top tier.</comment>

Emotional truth (choosing testament over solution) grounds the conceptual originality, avoiding mere cleverness.
