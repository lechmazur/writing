<question>3</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The “glass orchard library” above a cloud-sea is vividly specific and intimately tied to function: trees hold proto-stories, the clouds and moonlight are tools, not just décor. The environment charges the final image (tending the orchard, bottled moonlight, a tree about to flower) and is hard to swap out without breaking the premise. It does not quite constrain tactical choices moment-to-moment, which is the only thing holding this just below the very top.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The central stakes—risk of re-imprisonment if she violates parole, and the ache to open stories prematurely—are strongly stated and thematically apt. The customer interaction resolves smoothly with no challenge to her constraints, and the flowering tree in the deep grove is felt more as hinted potential than immediate test. As a result, tension is atmospheric rather than sharply escalating, limiting the impact of the final renunciation.</comment>

The ending’s emphasis on tending origins without entering them offers a resonant, non-literal answer to the initial question of where stories begin and what it costs to seek them. Slightly stronger on-page pressure would deepen the emotional aftershock, but the conceptual resonance is already high.</comment>
