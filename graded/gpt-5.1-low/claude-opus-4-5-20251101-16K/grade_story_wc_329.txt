The main structural weakness is the lack of a serious on-page test of her plan—once she plays the right way, opposition largely dissolves rather than forcing adaptation. Cost is implied (uprooting a sacred place, unsettling the city) more than made viscerally legible in the ending beat.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The setting is vivid and specific—the sky-bridges, clockwork citizens, silver trees, and intention-sensing statues create a coherent, atmospheric world rather than a generic steampunk gloss. The locust shell, violin, and trees all act as charged micro-settings by the end. A slight limitation is that sensory range leans heavily visual and conceptual; more tactile/aural integration in the city segments could deepen embodiment.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether wonder can be restored without violating the orchard’s rules—is clear and escalates from private nostalgia to public transformation. Pressure rises in a single, effective tightening when statues begin to move and trees start to uproot, shifting from potential punishment to radical change. However, the conflict remains relatively gentle: the statues’ resistance never truly bites, Mira faces no real risk of harm or failure, and she never has to choose between competing goods. As a result, the climax feels beautiful but low-cost, reducing tension and limiting impact.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of transformation, non-extractive liberation, and the utility of “useless” beauty emerge organically through the locust shell, the orchard’s agency, and the shift in the statues’ role. That said, the piece edges into overt thesis statements (“She had uprooted the assumption that wonder was inefficient...”), which flatten some of the ambiguity and subtext. More trust in image and consequence, less direct framing, would raise the work to a more rarefied literary tier.</comment>

The prose modulates well from city description to orchard encounter to city-return, keeping the same tonal signature while adjusting intensity. Emotional and thematic territory (wonder vs. efficiency) is well-trodden, and some framings are familiar enough to blunt distinctiveness. Still, the heart-rhythm signaling and use of a locust shell as transformative inheritance give the piece a memorable, if not fully groundbreaking, stamp.</comment>
