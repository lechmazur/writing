<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The structure is clean: one continuous ritual scene with embedded backstory, leading to the bead being sent and a renewed commitment to wait. Closure lands as Earned Stasis/Charged Poise—she forecloses ordinary life to become a vessel for Lien’s answer. The main limitation is that the middle feels more like elaboration of a known pattern than a distinct structural tightening or turn.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The forgotten orchard in a bamboo forest, wild apples, and the time-pocket create a vivid, specific atmosphere that feels integral to the premise of thin boundaries. Details are well-filtered through Mira’s purpose—this is a place “no one would ever harvest,” echoing her unclaimed grief. The micro-settings (puzzle pieces on moss, the amber bead, the preserved fly) are tactile and return in the final image, charging the ending. The environment influences possibility conceptually (it’s the only place she can reach Lien), though it constrains mood more than concrete tactics.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—reaching Lien at the cost of Mira’s memories and self—is clear and emotionally legible. There is a meaningful deepening when we learn exactly what she has already traded and that “tonight, she would give more,” culminating in sending the bead. Still, the conflict remains mostly internal and static; her stance never truly wavers, so pressure intensifies in description rather than through new dilemmas or reversals. The cost is visible but the escalation from start to finish is relatively gentle for a piece aiming at elite-level impact.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of love as a form of weaving, the cost of communication, and self-erasure in the name of maternal devotion emerge naturally from the ritual and the occupational metaphor. However, the line “The wistful loom spinner had learned that love was a kind of weaving too” edges into explicit thematic statement, slightly flattening what the images already convey. The piece leans more toward elegiac affirmation than challenging or complicating its own emotional premise.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is lyrically controlled and consistent, with a strong perceptual signature (textile metaphors, attention to edges, threads, and patterns). Diction and rhythm match Mira’s reflective, ritualistic state and modulate subtly as the ritual peaks. There’s no head-hopping or stance confusion, and the cadence of longer sentences suits the accumulative design. At times the narration approaches a generalized lyrical register that could belong to any thoughtful adult rather than one uniquely rooted in Mira’s specific history.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The blend of loomcraft, jigsaw fractures as inter-world fissures, an orchard outside normal time, and memory-as-currency for messages feels distinctive even though the core “sacrifice to reach a lost child” premise is familiar. The story commits to its conceit and follows its implications with decent rigor, especially around the cost of each message. It stops short of a truly surprising conceptual reweighting at the end, landing instead on a beautifully-rendered but thematically expected endurance.</comment>
