<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The central stake—Marek’s need to know what happened to his sister—is established early and remains focused. The cost at closure is that he must accept a long apprenticeship of perception instead of immediate reunion, which is credible but emotionally quiet. The final image of ongoing stones and rings carries resonance beyond the scene in a satisfying way.</comment>
