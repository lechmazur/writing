The drive lens reads as Hybrid (causal internal turn rendered in accumulative lyric beats), and closure lands as both decisive change and resonant reconfiguration. <question>3</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The soundless, between-moments sanctuary with shifting reality is vivid and tonally apt, and the silence rule specifically shapes how communication and the music box’s “song” are rendered (telepathy, vibration rather than sound). Details like walls becoming “the geometry of dreams” feel purposeful rather than decorative, mirroring Mira’s unmooring. The micro-setting of the music box on the sanctuary floor charges the final image. The environment doesn’t heavily constrain tactical choices beyond enforcing silence, but it consistently supports theme and mood.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Mira will cling to or release her resentment—is clear from the outset and narrows into a stark internal trade-off. Escalation comes through the enforced confrontation with bodily, contradictory memories, which strips away the protective simplicity of “she was a monster.” The visible price is the surrender of a sustaining hatred and the symbolic abandonment of the music box, which costs Mira her old identity anchor. The conflict remains largely internal and single-turn, but for this length the deepening of pressure and the cost are sufficiently legible.</comment>

The piece invites reflection on grace without collapsing entirely into platitude, but trims would heighten subtlety.</comment>

The thematic territory itself is familiar and at times voiced in recognizably self-help language, which limits the sense of radical novelty.
