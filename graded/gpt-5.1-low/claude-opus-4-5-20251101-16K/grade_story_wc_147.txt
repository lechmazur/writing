Closure lands as a resonant reconfiguration: Mira’s understanding of “harnessing the ephemeral” shifts and she commits to carrying presence rather than seeking permanence. <question>3</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The underwater temple is vividly rendered—bioluminescent kelp, barnacle-jeweled altars, a “broken dome”—and its physical conditions (depth, water, oxygen) materially constrain choices. Setting details are filtered through Mira’s purpose and grief rather than reading like a neutral tour. A slightly greater sense of physical resistance and danger in moving/manipulating objects underwater could have made the environment even more tactically present.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—retrieve the correct vial before her oxygen runs out—is clearly signaled and coherently escalated as the minutes tick down (amber to red, 12 minutes, 9, 6). Internal and external pressures interweave well: the oxygen clock sharpens her philosophical realization about honoring the ephemeral. However, the conflict path is fairly frictionless once the bones and bracelet appear; the correct vial is obvious and unguarded, so there’s minimal complication or reversal beyond initial despair. As a result, the cost is more conceptual/emotional than felt as a hard-earned, contested success.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The theme—presence vs permanence, and the honoring of fleeting experience in the face of dementia—is organically tied to the temple’s function and Mira’s arc. The final image of the glowing vial as “holding everything that mattered and nothing that could last” is resonant. Nonetheless, the explicit thesis statements (“Not permanence. Presence.”) edge toward didactic clarity rather than trusting implication.</comment>

Psychic distance is well managed: we move naturally between Mira’s perceptions and her internal reflections without head-hopping. The voice, while polished, feels more like a high-quality genre-literary default than something unmistakably singular.</comment>
