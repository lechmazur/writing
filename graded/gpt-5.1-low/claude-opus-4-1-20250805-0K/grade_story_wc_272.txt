<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The lighthouse library is vivid, specific, and integrated: waves, dust-breathing books, salt, and the dead beacon all filter through Marina’s artistic perception and collapsing reality. The tarot box and cards function as micro-setting objects that shape tactics (sketching on them, using beacon charcoal) rather than remaining mere props. This is rich, purposeful atmosphere rather than postcard description, though the physical precariousness of the cliffside could be tied even more tightly to immediate choice.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Marina’s capacity to make art and relate to reality without breaking—is clear early and developed consistently. The internal conflict pays off when she chooses to re-engage with portraiture on new terms, implicitly sacrificing the safety of conventional realism and social approval. Still, the “walls closing in” and shattering glass remain more metaphorically charged than tactically constraining, so the escalation feels intense but slightly low on concrete trade-offs.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The piece engages deeply with perception, brokenness, and the relationship between darkness and insight; images like faces with “three faces,” cards that change with angle, and the rekindled lighthouse beam all support this thematic fabric. However, several lines tilt toward explicit thesis-stating (“What if our eyes were always the limitation?”, “every broken thing was whole in its own dimension”) that slightly undercut the power of the images by over-explaining them. The voice does occasionally veer toward explicit philosophizing, but it remains embedded enough in her crisis that it doesn’t break stance.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>The prose is lush, rhythmic, and mostly efficient: images like “glass fell upward, tinkling like chimes” and “books breathing dust” are both vivid and functional, advancing mood and world simultaneously. The density of figurative language approaches the edge of overload but generally maintains clarity, with only a few moments of near-redundant abstraction around “threads” and “wholeness.” A touch more restraint or occasional plainer sentence in high-intensity moments would sharpen impact further.</comment>
