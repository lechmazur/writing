Embodied emotion is mostly filtered through image and metaphor rather than felt bodily detail, which slightly softens impact.</comment>
<question>2</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The accumulative, single-scene structure is clean: Marina’s ritual is established, then the external call to testify interrupts, and her decision to remain in the gallery functions as an Earned Stasis with conceptual cost. Setting is always seen through Marina’s priorities: the gallery as sanctuary, the board as a battlefield missing its dead. Environment and objects charge the final image—the “parliament of whispers” and remembered pawn-spaces—without becoming static backdrops. The only reason this doesn’t edge into 9+ is that the environment constrains mood and stance more than specific tactical options.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Marina will join the official “witnesses” or maintain her private, subversive witnessing—is clear and thematically pointed. The cost (further erasure from public narrative, self-exile into the margins) is conceptually strong but stays mostly inferred rather than dramatized with concrete consequence. As a result, tension feels more contemplative than pressing, effective but not maximally charged.</comment>

A few lines (“Tell them the real witnesses are the gestures no one notices”) skate close to overt thesis, slightly reducing the need for reader inference. Still, the final cadence reweights the whole piece without collapsing into a simple moral.</comment>

Sparse dialogue is natural enough and serves as action and pressure rather than exposition.</comment>

A hair of abstraction in a couple of phrases keeps this just shy of the absolute top tier.</comment>

The walking-as-writing ritual and focus on micro-gestures give the piece a distinctive conceptual and emotional signature that would be hard to transplant elsewhere. Risk-taking in premise and style heightens stakes without obscuring clarity, justifying a high originality score.</comment>
