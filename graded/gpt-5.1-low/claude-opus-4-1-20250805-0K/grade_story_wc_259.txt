The final image of the emptied pagoda retroactively charges the earlier descriptions with a sense of completion and loss. Description flirts with the ornate but generally stays purposeful and grounded in action.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The dominant stake—prevent the memory plague from enslaving populations versus Mira’s mind and life—is clear and paid off via her decision to inhale it. However, the external conflict with the guardians never quite bites; they arrive only after the decisive act and do not force a tactical adaptation. However, the final paragraphs tilt toward explicit thesis statements (“She understood now why the archive existed—not as a treasure but as a warning…”) that verge on moralizing rather than trusting image and implication. The piece still leaves a resonant afterimage, but with a slightly didactic edge.</comment>
