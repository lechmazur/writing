<question>2</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The accumulative structure—rain’s aftermath → shared work → lit installation → realization—coheres cleanly under a textural drive lens, and closure lands as a resonant reconfiguration when their shared “thread” is named. <question>3</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The lotus temple, lily pond, and post-storm light are vividly rendered and recur with evolving charge: from glistening petals to reflective doubling to the final image of the installation glowing between water and sky. The closing image of temple above and pond below explicitly reweights the whole story’s universality/particularity concern.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stakes—Mira’s need to understand the connecting thread and to mend her relationship with Chen—are legible, and the act of building the installation together serves as a modest deepening of pressure. However, opposition is low: Chen arrives willing to help, and their estrangement never tangibly threatens collapse of either the installation or the relationship. The result is a lovely but relatively low-tension piece where escalation is more intellectual and atmospheric than truly fraught.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>Thematic through-lines—patterns in love letters, universal longing, secrets, and translation—are well integrated with concrete images like the spirals and reflections. This slightly blunts what could otherwise be a subtler, more lingering aftereffect.</comment>

Psychic distance remains stable; any minor omniscient-seeming touches feel more like lyrical compression than leakage. Overall the voice feels controlled and particular rather than generic.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Line-level craft is strong: sentences are varied in rhythm, rich in image yet mostly precise, with few redundancies and clean mechanics. There is a mild risk of lushness tipping into sentimentality in the final paragraphs, but it doesn’t significantly obscure clarity or momentum. The prose consistently carries multiple jobs—setting, emotion, and theme—without obvious scaffolding.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The combination of a professional love-letter ghostwriter, pattern-recognition across anonymous intimacies, and a light-based installation in a lotus temple is an appealingly fresh synthesis. The story takes a stylistic risk in leaning fully into lyricism and quiet rather than overt drama, and that choice coheres. It doesn’t quite break new ground thematically, but its particular configuration and imagery are memorable.</comment>
