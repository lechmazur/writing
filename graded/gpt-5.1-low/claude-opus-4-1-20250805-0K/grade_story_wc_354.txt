<question>3</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The magnetic nexus, iron cliffs, aurora, and magnetite/quartz board are vivid, specific, and consistently filtered through Viktor’s chess-trained mind. Setting is not decorative: the magnetic field literally alters piece behavior and symbolically mirrors the “confused” yet meaningful exile, then charges the final image with the aurora’s “ancient dance.” The micro-setting (board, worry stone, aurora) constrains thought and choice and is tightly interwoven with theme. Line-level handling of atmosphere is strong without slipping into incoherence.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Viktor returns to the Academy or remains at the nexus to teach a different kind of mastery—is clear and thematically apt. However, on-page pressure is modest: Viktor’s early reflection already frames the covenant as gift, so Mira’s question doesn’t materially tighten his options or force a wrenching trade-off. The game itself does not generate tangible risk beyond intellectual beauty; the decision to stay reads as confirmation rather than a hard-won turn. As a result, escalation feels contemplative rather than truly testing his commitment.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>Themes of “beauty in difficulty,” exile as transformation, and chess as a language for deeper patterns are coherent and arise from repeated, concrete motifs (worry stone, impossible positions, magnetism, silence). That said, several lines articulate theme directly (“the Academy teaches chess while this place teaches truth”), edging toward sermonizing and reducing subtextual power. A touch more reliance on image and implication over thesis-like statements would elevate it.</comment>

At times the narration slides into essayistic proclamation, which flattens the otherwise strong perceptual signature.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>7.7</grade><comment>The synthesis of high-level chess pedagogy with a literal magnetic anomaly and auroral phenomena feels fresh, especially the magnetite/quartz board and pieces leaning toward magnetic north. Emotional territory (finding home in exile, beauty in difficulty) is well-trodden but handled with enough specificity to avoid genericity. Strong, but not transformative enough in premise or moral calculus to reach the very top band.</comment>
