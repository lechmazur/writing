The drive lens (largely accumulative with causal throughline) holds consistently. Sensory detail is selective and filtered through her long familiarity with the place. The world’s rules around moonlight, origami, and natural noise remain internally consistent and legible.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preventing a prophecy from becoming self-fulfilling—is clear and present from early on, and the “struggling” luminous script offers a tangible opposing force. However, tension remains mostly intellectual and lyrical; there’s little sense she might fail or pay a sharp personal price, so escalation feels measured rather than urgent. Rhythm and diction adjust subtly with pressure (more active, tactile when she’s catching fragments, more expansive at dawn). <question>8</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The central conceit—prophecy as a living, contagious narrative that must be silenced by mapping hush and feeding it into a brook—is fresh and imaginatively worked through. The final repricing of “heroic prevention” as maintaining ignorance for peace feels specific to this setup rather than a generic anti-prophecy moral. The piece takes stylistic and conceptual risks that largely pay off without sacrificing clarity.</comment>
