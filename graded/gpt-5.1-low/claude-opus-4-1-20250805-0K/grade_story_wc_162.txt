<question>4</question><grade>7.9</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Marina can re-encounter or meaningfully “reach” her father by understanding his maze—is clear and present throughout. Her agency (choosing to follow the codes, kneel at the center, and then write her own) drives this escalation, but external jeopardy is low and there’s little sense of alternative options foreclosed. The cost is primarily emotional and conceptual, and while it reads, it could be sharpened by one more moment of on-page dilemma or risk.</comment>

However, the line borders on explicit thesis-statement and edges into mild moralizing, slightly dulling the subtextual power built by the imagery. Overall, the piece invites genuine reflection on grief and liminality without collapsing into simple binaries.</comment>
