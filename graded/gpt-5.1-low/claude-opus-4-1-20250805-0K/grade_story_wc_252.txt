<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Mira will detach from her constructed self and risk abandoning her dock identity—is clear and tight. The internal and external conflicts interweave neatly via Torven’s parallel quest and the world’s nonviolence constraint. The conflict is, however, quite gentle; there’s little sense she might truly refuse the call, which mutes some potential tension.</comment>

This explicitness blunts nuance and keeps the piece from a higher literary tier.</comment>
