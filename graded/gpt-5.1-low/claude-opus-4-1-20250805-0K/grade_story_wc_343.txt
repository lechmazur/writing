<question>4</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake is largely intellectual and conceptual—understanding stranded luminance and, by extension, a lost language of navigation. Her big turn is an interpretive realization rather than a hard choice among costly options, and nothing significant is sacrificed to pursue it. The result is a beautifully textured piece with relatively soft conflict and low felt stakes.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of rediscovery vs discovery, hidden knowledge in children’s play, and nonhuman custodians of memory are coherent and emerge naturally through concrete images (hopscotch as cipher, birds as “living cartographers,” stranded luminance as waypoints). However, the thematic articulation veers toward the explicit (“The Guild would call this her great discovery, but Elara knew better”) and does some work the images could carry alone. It’s resonant and well-integrated, but not quite as subtle or ambivalent as top-tier literary work.</comment>
