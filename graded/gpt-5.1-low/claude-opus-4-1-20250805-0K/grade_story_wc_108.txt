<question>4</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The dominant stakes—Marcus’s autonomy and the control over his own mind—are clear and escalated by the direct confrontation with Jake and the threat of a “reset protocol.” The shell-to-shell clash is a solid functional peak with an intelligible reversal (the fears transferring). A brief on-page moment where Marcus’s plan nearly fails or his remaining fears complicate his execution would sharpen the sense of risk and cost.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>Theme emerges coherently from particulars: fear as safeguard, stunt doubling as metaphor for constructed identity, a mother encoding salvation into her child’s body. If the story trusted its images (the seashell, the rust, the stunt work) a bit more and spelled out the lesson a bit less, the thematic aftertaste would be subtler and stronger.</comment>

There’s mild register stretch in the exposition about “synthetic memory compounds, identity reconstruction protocols,” but it’s justified by his origin as more than human.
