<question>3</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The extinct language archive is vividly evoked (“dust motes,” “labyrinthine shelves”) and clearly anchored as the locus of both magic and memory. The inkwell as a charged object is effectively embedded in the setting and directly involved in the climax. However, the archive rarely constrains options or tactics; it’s more atmospheric than actively pressuring her choices.</comment>

The symphony ritual operates more as an inevitable catharsis than a pressured test of her resolve. As a result, escalation feels gentle and the cost of success is relatively light.</comment>
