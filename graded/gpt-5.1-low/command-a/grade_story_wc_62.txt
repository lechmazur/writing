While the setting doesn’t strongly constrain her physical options, it does meaningfully charge her realizations and the ending mood. <question>4</question><grade>6.6</grade><comment>The primary conflict is internal—Elara’s struggle to translate collective grief into stone—and that stake is signaled early. However, escalation is gentle and largely reflective; there’s no moment where she risks a meaningful loss beyond remaining stuck. The absence of sharper trade-offs or foreclosed options keeps the stakes from fully tightening.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Thematic threads—grief as space vs. shape, migration, collective memory, artistic practice as transformation—are coherent and integrated with the objects and images. That said, the theme is sometimes over-articulated (“Collective grief, she realized, was not something to be solved but something to be carried”), edging toward didactic instead of fully trusting the images. A bit more reliance on implication rather than explicit statement would elevate this.</comment>
