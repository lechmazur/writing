The drone as tool-object and the eclipse as backdrop frame the final image but stop short of fully charging the choice.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—Elara’s need to understand her brother’s death and time’s distortion—is clear from early on. Yet opposition is soft: the ink’s keeper is cooperative, and there’s no real risk of failure or misstep beyond emotional discomfort. The result is an inward, one-step escalation that feels sincere but relatively low-tension for the scale of the questions raised.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>Themes of control vs understanding, facing the past vs trying to rewrite it, and grief’s transformation are coherent and appropriate to the imagery of disappearing ink and eclipse. The final image of her walking into the desert with a lighter heart has emotional clarity but borders on tidy moral. More implication through scene and fewer direct statements would deepen the lingering effect.</comment>
