<question>2</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The structure is clean: a night of work, a spike in the hum, a singular intense recording, and a quiet descent. <question>3</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The bonsai labyrinth, mountaintop, and town below are vivid and specific, with sensory detail that fits the contemplative mode. However, the setting rarely constrains or complicates her choices; it is more atmosphere than active pressure or resource. Strong, lyrical worldbuilding that stops short of fully instrumental integration.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—preserving and deepening her archive of invented histories—is clear, and the hum’s intensification provides a notional escalation. The hum “calling to her” hints at pressure, but this never sharpens into conflict or cost beyond fatigue. The piece functions as a mood piece more than a story under pressure.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>Themes of memory, preservation, and the intimacy between solitude and creation emerge organically from the particulars (forest as guardian, town’s silence, her private archive). Effective but not yet piercing.</comment>
