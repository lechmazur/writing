Still, the setting rarely constrains choices or tactics—heat, distance, or ruin don’t meaningfully shape what Elara can or can’t do—so it functions more as evocative backdrop than active pressure. World rules around the oasis’s temporal oddity are sketched lightly but remain internally consistent.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.5</grade><comment>The main stake—understanding why the oasis resists change and what that means—is present from the start, but conflict remains almost entirely internal and low-friction. Once Elara understands the kindness-beacon nature, the decision to teach villagers and heal the oasis is essentially unopposed and costless. There’s no serious trade-off, loss, or test of commitment on the page, so pressure never tightens in a way that would suit a “prestige” slot.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The thematic core—that kindness and care can resist decay and mend fractures in time and community—is coherent and supported by recurring images of hives, gardens, and the glowing glass. Elara’s realization feels largely declarative rather than wrestled-from-experience, and counter-pressures or ambiguities about kindness’s limits are absent. The ending is emotionally sincere but thematically tidy.</comment>

Still, it’s clean, coherent, and publishable in basic craft terms.</comment>
