<question>5</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>Themes of honoring the discarded, listening to quiet stories, and finding vocation in attention emerge clearly from the objects and Elias’s ritual. However, the theme is often articulated overtly—“I sanctify you… to honor what you once were,” “reasons why some houses felt alive”—nudging toward didactic rather than fully subtextual resonance. <question>8</question><grade>7.7</grade><comment>The combination of a “shard of hush,” sanctifying displaced objects, and using that to perceive why houses feel alive is a fresh and appealingly odd premise. The story feels distinctive and thoughtfully conceived, but not yet indispensable or startling in its ingenuity.</comment>
