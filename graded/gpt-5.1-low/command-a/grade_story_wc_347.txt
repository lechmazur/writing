However, her internal conflict remains relatively straightforward; we don’t see truly contradictory impulses under pressure so much as a noble temperament fulfilling its obvious course. <question>2</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>Cause and effect are clean: discovery of the nut’s power → assembling impressions into a map → using the map to save a traveler → using the nut to weaken the curse. Closure lands via a Decisive Change/RR hybrid—Elara reorients her purpose and the orchard bud appears—but the cost is mostly implied rather than sharply dramatized; nothing is visibly lost or risked beyond generic danger. <question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant external stake—rescuing the traveler from the labyrinth and birds—is clear and escalates from distant cry to active chase and near-attack. Escalation is mostly one clean tightening (discover function → use in crisis), with modest interweaving of internal and external conflict. The absence of a sharper price (injury, irrevocable loss, or moral compromise) keeps the tension solid but not standout.</comment>

However, the closing articulation (“She had found her purpose, not as a caretaker bound by duty…”) leans toward explicit thematic statement, slightly flattening what had been effectively carried in image and action. The resonance lingers, but with a shade of over-clarity rather than oblique power.</comment>
