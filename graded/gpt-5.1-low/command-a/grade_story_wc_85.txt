The recurring terrace image at the end reweights earlier description to mirror his fractured soul. This is rich, economical setting work that serves mood and meaning.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The main stake—whether Elias will continue to bury truths for money or confront his complicity—is signaled early and tightened through the ritual of sifting and the watch-hand/clock carving moment. However, external pressure is minimal and the final choice to “perhaps” stop lying is somewhat softened, so the felt cost and narrowing of options are more implied than acute. <question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of fabricated lineage, erasure, and the persistence of truth despite human revision emerge organically from Elias’s profession and the shore ritual. The piece resists overt sermonizing, though some lines skim close to stating theme (“Now, it felt like a piece of evidence, a tangible link to the truth he had buried”). Overall the closing image offers a resonant, non-literal answer about living with unerasable guilt.</comment>

The piece takes tasteful risks in imagery and conceit without sacrificing clarity.</comment>
