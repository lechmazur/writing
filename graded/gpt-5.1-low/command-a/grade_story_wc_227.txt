It stops short of 9-band because the acid sea’s constraints feel more gestural than fully exploited tactically.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>The dominant stakes (salvaging memory/meaning under the literal and metaphorical threat of dissolution) are clear, and pressure does escalate when the statue activates and the workshop lurches toward possible destruction. Yet the resolution feels low-friction: her declaration alone calms both workshop and sea, with no evident sacrifice or narrowed future options. The conflict functions but doesn’t achieve a sharp sense of irreversible cost or truly tested commitment.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>6.5</grade><comment>Themes of kintsugi, resilience in brokenness, and salvaging memory are coherent and threaded through recurring images (pockmarks, scars, charm, aging workshop). Subtext is present but often overwritten by summary of what the story already implies. The result is emotionally clear but edges into sentimentality instead of layered ambiguity.</comment>

<question>6</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is stable, readable, and mostly well-matched to the slightly elevated, lyrical mode. At key thematic beats, though, the diction drifts into generic inspirational language that blunts the distinctiveness of the voice. POV control is clean, but the narrative texture doesn’t quite rise to a singular or surprising register.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The prose is generally clean and fluent, with good sensory density and a few strong images (statue surface “pitted like the moon,” “breaths stolen from the abyss”). The final paragraph in particular leans on abstract nouns (“meaning,” “hope,” “stories she carried within her”) rather than fresh concrete turns. Line-level craft is solid but not yet refined to elite compression and sharpness.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>7.3</grade><comment>The fusion of a floating clockwork workshop on an acid sea, memory-conjuring statuary, and a kintsugi-inflected philosophy of repair feels fresh and visually striking. However, the underlying emotional arc—lost/pained protagonist discovers that brokenness can be beautiful and emerges with hope—is familiar and largely expressed through familiar formulations. The piece shows promising imaginative synthesis but doesn’t yet transform its themes into something unmistakably singular.</comment>
