<question>2</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Orientation is clean from the outset: we know who Elara is, where she is, and what she wants. Setting meaningfully shapes the narrative: the altar contains the scroll, the soil receives the petals, and the glowing tree with the ribbon charges the final image. Details feel filtered through Elara’s priorities, reinforcing themes of reclamation and renewal. The environment’s symbolic resonance is strong without becoming confusing.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether Elara can meaningfully mend the broken vow and her sense of failure—is clear, and the “Persist” moment provides a modest tightening. However, conflict remains largely internal and under-opposed; once she commits, the tools all work on first use without setbacks or hard choices. The result is gentle escalation rather than a felt testing of her resolve, keeping tension in a reflective band.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>Themes of renewal, generational connection, and making something new from broken pieces are coherent and well-linked to objects like the ribbon and the deciduous vow. Yet the thematic articulation is often explicit (“Perhaps the breach wasn’t a failure but a necessary shedding”) and risks didacticism. The ending offers resonance, but the insights feel somewhat pre-packaged rather than discovered at personal cost.</comment>

Still, distance and access remain consistent and functional throughout.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The prose is generally clean and fluent, with good rhythm and no significant grammatical or clarity issues. Overall momentum holds, but the line-level could be tightened and sharpened for a top-tier venue.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>7.4</grade><comment>The central conceits—a quartz-eye mender, deciduous vows, and a lush orchard inside an abandoned cathedral—are fresh and evocative.
