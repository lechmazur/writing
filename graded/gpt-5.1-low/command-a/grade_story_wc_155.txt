Some details remain safely atmospheric rather than constraining choices—the orchard itself and “centuries of thick fog” are more mood than active pressure. Still, the environment plays a meaningful role in the climax, especially the fog as living canvas.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>The dominant stake—will Lira rediscover her passion or remain numbed—is clear, and the story does move from numbness to engagement. Yet conflict and escalation are largely internal and relatively untested: the film and network appear as gifts rather than things she must earn or fight for, and nothing concrete pushes back against her desire. Her “choice” to engage the network and then paint reads as a gentle slide into inevitability, not a pressured decision with trade-offs. As a result, tension never tightens beyond curiosity and wonder.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>Themes of art as memory, preservation of communal joy, and the distinction between medium and meaning are coherent and supported by the film projection and her realization that “her art had never truly been about the pigments or the canvas.” However, that realization borders on direct thesis statement and feels somewhat summarized rather than fully dramatized through a concrete, hard choice. The emotional aftereffect is warm and wistful but risks sentimentality because no significant cost or ambiguity shades the epiphany. More friction—e.g., confronting the ruins’ loss as well as their joy—would deepen resonance.</comment>
<question>6</question><grade>7.5</grade><comment>The close third-person voice is consistent, clear, and well-matched to the reflective, slightly lyrical tone. While the prose is competent and sometimes lovely (“within the fold of a daydream”), it doesn’t yet have a sharply individualized perceptual signature that could only belong to Lira. A few more idiosyncratic observations tied to her muralist sensibility would elevate distinctiveness.</comment>

The closing image is clear and strong, even if the diction leans a bit generic in summarizing her inner shift.</comment>
