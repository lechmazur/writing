<question>2</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The accumulative, ritual structure is clean: song → leaf blower → rising effort → altered echoes → faint laughter yields a legible before/after reconfiguration. The environment, though, mainly amplifies mood rather than constraining his choices or tactics in concrete ways. The final “exhale” of the tunnel nicely charges the ending image but stops short of fully inextricable world-pressure.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—restoring lost laughter—is clearly declared, and there is a sense of effort intensifying as Eli sings louder and grows more exhausted. Escalation is primarily volumetric (more sound, more insistence) rather than a tightening of options with visible cost. Some phrases drift toward abstraction or slogan (“a place that had forgotten how to laugh without reservation”), softening the complexity.
