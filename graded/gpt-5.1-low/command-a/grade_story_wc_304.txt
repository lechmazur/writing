Closure qualifies as a conceptual repricing, but the lack of a concrete price or foreclosed path blunts impact.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>7.0</grade><comment>The skyborne grotto, crystalline walls, and aurora create a vivid, coherent atmosphere that suits the meditative tone, and the aurora’s response to events ties environment to emotion. The emerald chip as object-functions-as-setting does some additional work, but the grotto itself remains largely static.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The main stake—whether Elara clings to sparks or embraces stillness—is stated early but escalates gently rather than through sharp turns. The proclaimed apocalypse outside never concretely impinges to raise pressure in the scene.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.9</grade><comment>Theme is coherent—movement vs stillness, life-sparks vs enduring quiet—and the emerald chip and aurora recurrently frame that inquiry. The ending image reweights earlier material but the epiphany feels somewhat pre-packaged rather than hard-won.</comment>

The traveler’s brief speech has a slightly generalized, aphoristic quality, but it doesn’t break the POV or tone.</comment>

Most lines do at least two jobs (world-building plus mood, or action plus interior tilt), supporting momentum for a lyric piece.</comment>
