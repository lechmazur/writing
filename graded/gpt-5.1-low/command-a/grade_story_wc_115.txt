<question>2</question><grade>6.6</grade><comment>The causal chain is clean: father’s stories → interpreting his calls → entering the tunnel → discovering the valve → freeing the ghost shift, with clear spatial and temporal orientation. The ghost chamber at the heart of the tunnel nicely mirrors the “frozen in time” theme of the trapped workers. While atmospheric, the details lean familiar for the genre and don’t quite achieve singular distinctiveness.</comment>

<question>4</question><grade>6.2</grade><comment>The dominant stakes—truth about her father and the fate of the trapped workers—are clearly signaled and structurally present. When the shadows “lunged,” they fail to inflict consequence and do not alter her tactics; she simply continues forward and succeeds on the first attempt. The conflict functions but lacks a sharp, pressured decision point with a felt price.</comment>

<question>5</question><grade>6.3</grade><comment>Themes of belief vs skepticism, inherited stories, and the “weight of a swallowed truth” emerge coherently from Mara’s relationship to her father and the literal ghost shift. There is little ambiguity or lingering complication—the story implies her father was right and peace follows without cost or residue. Subtext is present but relatively surface-level, without a strong countercurrent to complicate the takeaway.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>5.9</grade><comment>The idea of a “factory ghost shift” bound to a tunnel and released by a beaded valve wheel has some freshness, but the overall arc (skeptic enters haunted site, proves parent’s supernatural claims true, cleanses and finds peace) is familiar.
