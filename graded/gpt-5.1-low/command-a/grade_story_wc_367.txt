This was no accident.”) comes through explanatory narration rather than dramatized discovery, softening impact. Cost at the ending is largely conceptual, with no concrete loss or foreclosed option to sharpen the before/after divide.</comment>

The lighthouse as a focused source of light meaningfully charges the final gateway image. Still, sensory texture leans heavily visual; other senses or more tactile interaction with the space could further deepen atmosphere.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>5.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—guiding or confronting the unknown force in the fog—is present but remains abstract and low-risk. There is no point where the Herald must trade something away, endure failure, or seriously consider retreat. As a result, the climax reads more as a smooth unveiling than an earned crisis with palpable stakes.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>6.8</grade><comment>Themes of illumination, guidance, and using “ancient shadows” to shape a new future are coherent and recur through the wick, the fog, and the green dawn. However, much of the thematic work is stated outright (“It had been a bridge between worlds”) rather than left to implication, which flattens subtext. The ending suggests ongoing responsibility but sidesteps harder questions about cost, duty, or ambiguity that would elevate the aftertaste.</comment>

This was no accident.”) that feels prosy rather than embodied. Overall, though, the voice is coherent and controlled, if not yet distinctive enough for top-tier literary venues.</comment>

<question>8</question><grade>7.3</grade><comment>The combination of a post-print era, an emerald lighthouse, a laughing prophetic keeper, and a green interworld dawn feels fresh and thoughtfully assembled. Emotional and thematic moves, however, stay relatively safe, keeping this in the realm of strong but not standout originality.</comment>
