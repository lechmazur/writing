The desert/oasis covenant meaningfully constrains what action is possible (listening and ritual rather than speech), satisfying the high-band gate.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the broken covenant and its unspeakable weight can be reconfigured without repeating the past’s destructive “speaking in fear”—is clear and resolved. However, there is little on-page resistance or risk of failure beyond conceptual dread; everything proceeds with a calm inevitability that softens tension. The cost (taking on the burden of unspoken weight) is real but felt more in idea than in a sharply pressured moment.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>Themes of silence vs. speech, the ethics of listening, and covenants between humans and environment emerge organically from bees, ghosts, and ritual rather than from any overt moralizing. The emotional aftereffect is quiet but resonant, with strong conceptual repricing of what “communication” and “keeping” mean.</comment>
