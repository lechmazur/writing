Stakes remain mostly internal and conceptual rather than concretely risky on the page (the inspector never truly menaces), which keeps tension from fully peaking. Still, conflict is coherent and deepens meaningfully through the scene.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.9</grade><comment>Themes of enforced happiness vs. authentic grief, moral complicity, and “lightness that does not deny weight” are woven through events and images, not explained. The story resists simplistic moralizing: his past betrayal and sheltering coexist without neat absolution, and the ending’s quiet preference for no applause lingers. It invites reflection on state mandates over emotion and the ethics of performance without ever stating a thesis.</comment>

The moral ambiguity—he both reported and protected—gives the premise emotional teeth rather than allegorical flatness. The risks in style and concept pay off, yielding a piece that would be memorable in a strong submission pile.</comment>
