<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stakes—whether she can find a way to see and paint that doesn’t shatter her subjects or herself—are clear and present early (“every canvas inhaled a luminescent ache”). Her agency in “untangling threads” functions as the central tightening move, linking external maintenance to internal reorientation and foreclosing a purely avoidant path. <question>5</question><grade>8.3</grade><comment>Themes of perception, fragmentation, and the ethics of representation are woven effectively through objects and action: tarot cards as “weather not prophecy,” the lighthouse as a model for a humane gaze, the choice to honor “the fragmented reality of a soul.” The final understanding—that her work is to shape and hold luminescent ache, not smooth it away—feels earned by the physical labor of restoring the beam. However, lines such as “the world is a lighthouse, that art is its book room” edge toward explicit thesis, slightly reducing the subtlety of what is otherwise deeply subtextual. The piece still leaves room for reflection about vocation and witnessing without collapsing into a simple moral.</comment>
