<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether and how she can continue to preserve forgotten names in a world of deserted marvels—remains coherent, and pressure does deepen when the shelves’ new “hunger” manifests and the names seek a home. Additional escalation comes from the influx of remembered marvels needing rooms and light, forcing her to reconfigure the library. Still, tension stays mostly atmospheric rather than sharp; we never quite feel a concrete risk of failure or an alternative path she nearly takes.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Theme is rich and organically conveyed: memory, the ethics of preservation, and the value of damage (cracks, regret) as a conduit for fragile, otherwise-lost truths. The story resists moralizing, instead offering an image-based answer: preservation means accepting impurity and cost, not restoring some ideal “pure” tone. It invites reflection on how we remember the forgotten without spelling out a thesis.</comment>
