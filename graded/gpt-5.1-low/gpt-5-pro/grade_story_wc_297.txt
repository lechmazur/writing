<question>4</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>The dominant stake—how to complete a prematurely triggered covenant without being destroyed or lost—is clear and escalates as the maze moves from object to skin to shared wound. However, antagonistic pressure is largely atmospheric; there’s little concrete risk of harm beyond inference, and no serious alternative path is tested on the page. As a result, tension feels finely textured but never quite sharp-edged.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>Themes of prematurely arrived futures, covenants deferred rather than broken, and wounds as sites of consent emerge organically from specific actions (linking through scars, futures ferried back by dream-versions). There is rich subtext around trauma, mutual recognition, and communal responsibility carried by image rather than thesis. A slight limitation is that “covenant” and “promise” recur often enough that they skate near abstraction, though the concrete scar-work mostly anchors them.</comment>
