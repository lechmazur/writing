<question>3</question><grade>9.2</grade><comment>The setting is exceptionally vivid and integrated: the sunken orchard, greenhouse glass, and ring of shipwrecks are not postcard backdrops but active constraints on how she moves, breathes, and bargains. The fairy ring, silt geometry, and glazed roof/porthole “broken constellations” all become functional tools for her methods. The final images—spores like first snow in the greenhouse freed from its old debt—are charged directly by the environment.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The central stake—recovering stolen memories and settling an old, misnamed bargain—is planted early and tightened through the active encounter with the ring and the film of the past. Internal and external conflict align well, but the danger of failure remains mostly atmospheric rather than concretely pressured. A more legible sense of what she risks losing now if she fails would raise the stakes further.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Themes of memory as currency, the ethics of bargains, the power of names, and the way habits “steal” time emerge organically from action and recurring images rather than overt statement. The ending—bowing to the mushroom as spores fall and knowing “which promises to keep this time”—offers a resonant, non-literal answer without moralizing. It resists tidy closure by acknowledging cost and selectivity in what gets restored.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The prose is lush but purposeful: most sentences carry multiple loads (worldbuilding, interiority, theme) without redundancy, and the figurative language is coherent and well-chosen (“crisp nostalgia cuts the water like peppermint” is exemplary). Metaphors stay within the piece’s established logic of sea, orchard, and folklore, avoiding mixed or strained comparisons. The only minor reservation is that figurative density is nearly continuous, and a touch more spareness around the climactic correction might increase impact.</comment>
