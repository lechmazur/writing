<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The “ancient ridge in bloom,” stone hollow, cliffside sink, and reef are all sensuously drawn and consistently exploited as ritual sites, not just scenery. The final images—the sapling with ink-veined leaves, rain as “a library walking”—are charged directly by the setting’s properties. Description is dense but purposeful, consistently filtered through the narrator’s perceptual biases.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the island’s cultural memory will thin into forgetfulness—is clear from the outset and steadily pressured by the monsoon clock and the risk of planting the seed in the wrong place. Escalation is gentle but present: the seed’s census-test that makes some leave, the discovery of the missing reef treaty through fishermen’s dreams, and the looming storm all narrow options. Agency is strong in the sequence of ritual choices (where to plant, what to recite, how to waken sites), and the cost of curation vs. forgetting is legible. The conflict remains largely conceptual and ceremonial rather than viscerally tense, which slightly mutes the sense of danger.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Theme—reality as carefully curated fiction, the ethics of memory-keeping, and doubt as a necessary light—is deeply integrated with the ritual acts and collective dreaming. However, several lines articulate the theme quite directly instead of leaving it entirely to image and implication, which blunts some subtlety. Overall, the synthesis of method (interpreted dreams) and theme (collective memory as chosen) is strong and resonant.</comment>

<question>7</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The line-level craft is strong: nearly every sentence carries multiple jobs (worldbuilding, mood, theme) with vivid metaphors that generally cohere and remain concrete.
