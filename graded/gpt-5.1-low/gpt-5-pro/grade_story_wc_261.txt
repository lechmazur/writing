<question>4</question><grade>8.5</grade><comment>The dominant stake—finding a non-destructive way to achieve closure—is clear early and sharpened when the ivy sets a price: the miracle “is not only yours” and requires a consentful wound. The tension is mostly quiet but persistent, with each consultation (fern, coffee, ivy, elm) tightening the moral calculus. The only modest constraint is that physical jeopardy is muted, so pressure remains more contemplative than viscerally tense.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>9.2</grade><comment>Theme emerges organically: scars as narrative rather than damage; healing as remembering rather than erasing; consent and shared cost in miracle-working. The final stance—seeking closure by listening to scars instead of opening new wounds—offers a resonant, non-literal answer to the core question.</comment>

POV boundaries are clean; all knowledge is plausibly anchored in the narrator’s perceptions and the plants’ communications.
