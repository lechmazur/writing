The ivy’s final metaphor—truth “like ivy” nailing itself to what stands—recharges the physical environment into theme. A touch more non-visual sensory grounding (sound, smell, cold) during the descent could make the atmosphere even more immersive.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.2</grade><comment>The central conflict—whether to exhume buried truth at the risk of social rupture—is well-defined, and the stakes are clear through Tomas’s fear of “blister[ing] the town” and frightening children from the west stair. However, opposition softens quickly: Tomas’s resistance collapses after a brief exchange, and there are no real setbacks or competing tactics under pressure. The final decision to publish feels ethically weighted but insufficiently hard-fought on the page, keeping escalation in the strong-but-not-searing range.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.6</grade><comment>Themes of noble lies, archival erasure, and the ethics of memory emerge organically from the investigation and the station’s history, with the “pit of lies” and ivy/letter parallels providing strong symbolic coherence.
