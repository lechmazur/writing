<question>4</question><grade>8.2</grade><comment>Stakes—preventing the erasure of people and histories—are established early and escalated concretely when the contact is found bound near revision squads actively “cutting us from the records.” Jessa’s interventions with the key and wiring under increasing pressure drive the major turns, culminating in a risky, irreversible act (breaking the ground plate) that transforms the situation. The cost is more communal and conceptual than personal; she and the contact escape without visible individual loss, which slightly softens the felt price. Nonetheless, pressure stays coherent and tightens appropriately for the scale.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>Themes of memory versus erasure, witness versus indifference, and stubborn custodianship are woven effectively through concrete actions (seeding truth into the relay, the lagoon projecting the erased, neighbors recognizing faces). This explicitness blunts some of the otherwise strong subtext and prevents a higher score.</comment>
