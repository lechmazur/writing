<question>2</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The structure is accumulative/textural, with each beat (receiving instructions, first dust-figure, neighbors gathering, patrol threat, violinist arrival) layering pressure and meaning. Closure lands as a resonant reconfiguration: the world has shifted from famine to a precarious but real return of narrative, with dust learning “the taste of words again.” A slightly clearer articulation of what could still go wrong (beyond generic patrol danger) would sharpen the felt cost at the end.</comment>
<question>3</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The setting is rich, specific, and constantly functional: ember alcove, mist, patrol winds, moss-walled ridge, and the “wintered valley” all shape tactics and mood. A single concrete anchor for era/tech level beyond “shortwave” might further orient some readers, but the lyric mode mostly earns its slight vagueness.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—reviving forbidden stories under surveillance—emerges early and tightens meaningfully when the broadcast warns of searchlights and when boots pass outside. Mara’s choice not to cloak her answer, and later to trust the gathered group to breathe and hide as a unit, provides clear agency under pressure. The storm-of-strings sequence and dust-wall of rain offer a strong escalation, but the direct, on-page price for defiance remains relatively abstract (they evade danger rather than suffer a visible loss). The tension works well for a quiet, lyric piece, but a sharper sense of what might be irretrievably lost would deepen the stakes.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>Themes of censorship vs. memory, communal authorship, and the ethics of keeping/teaching stories are woven through concrete particulars (scores in the alcove, outlawed staves, dust figures wearing names “like coats”). The ending’s notion that “in time, the dust would not need her” reframes the project from heroic rescue to eventual decentralization, which is a sophisticated thematic move. The piece resists moralizing while clearly staking a stance in favor of fragile, risky storytelling as a communal act.</comment>

POV remains cleanly with Mara’s frame of knowledge, even as it adopts an almost-mythic narrative distance. One could argue the diction is slightly elevated beyond what Mara herself might articulate, but the quasi-fable stance feels intentional rather than a leak.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.6</grade><comment>The line-level craft is exceptionally strong: images are inventive yet coherent, and most sentences do multiple jobs (world-building, emotion, and forward motion at once). There’s minimal redundancy and no major scaffold or template cadence; the prose sustains momentum despite its lyricism. A few phrases (“thistled calm,” “gullible powder”) verge on precious, but they remain anchored enough in context to avoid purple-prose drag.</comment>
<question>8</question><grade>8.7</grade><comment>The central conceit—a pirate broadcast teaching a copyist to animate dust into communal, resistant storytelling—is fresh and specific, not a re-skin of a common template. The risk-taking in form (high lyric density, accumulative structure, quasi-mythic register) largely pays off, giving the piece an artisanal, singular feel. A slightly clearer articulation of the regime or mechanism enforcing the “story famine” might push the originality from resonant allegory into even more startling particularity.</comment>
