<question>4</question><grade>8.0</grade><comment>The dominant stake—whether the operator can reshape a volatile, grief-rattled soundscape into a comforting silence—is clear and escalates when the child’s bell “buckles” the hush and spikes both instruments. The cost is subtle but present: he must work delicately within an inherently fragile medium, and the mother’s release feels like something hard-won rather than given. The conflict stays mostly internal and atmospheric, so while tension is finely sustained, it never quite reaches the sharper edge that would justify a higher score.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>Themes of silence as care rather than absence, travel as attention, and grief being held rather than erased emerge organically from the operator’s techniques and the city’s metaphors.
