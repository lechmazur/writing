<question>3</question><grade>9.2</grade><comment>Setting is exceptionally vivid and integral: the parapet’s acoustics enable the whistle, rain and sluices shape movement, and the estuary ecology justifies Yazhen’s turtle rescues and the need for nets. The parapet, moonlight, and fireflies all return in the closing image, charging the final covenant-like walk home. Environment consistently constrains and enables choices rather than sitting as backdrop.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake—livelihood versus compassion for non-human life and “hidden effort”—is clearly posed and tightened as Li’s hunger and the elders’ impatience bear down. Escalation is quiet but real: the threat of punishment for a “thief,” the discovery that Yazhen is defying communal norms, and the public test of her methods through the philosopher’s lesson. The ethical calculus at the climax (“justice is a net that also learns to open”) arises naturally from this pressure. The tension remains more contemplative than visceral, which may limit peak intensity but suits the piece’s mode.</comment>
<question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Theme coheres strongly around hidden patterns, mercy embedded in systems, and the revaluation of what counts as “theft” or “justice.” Nets, moon paths, foam maps, and fireflies all accrue thematic weight and are reweighted in the ending without needing external explanation. The philosopher occasionally voices the theme explicitly (“justice is a net that also learns to open”), but the line is earned by prior scene work and doesn’t read as sermonizing. The story leaves room for complexity—the cost to the community is felt even as the more compassionate choice prevails.</comment>

POV is stable and never confusing despite its poetic compression. Rhythmic control is excellent, with sentence length and cadence modulating appropriately at moments of discovery and teaching.</comment>
<question>7</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Line-level craft is highly polished: images are often doing double-duty (world-building and theme), and there is minimal redundancy or scaffolding. Prose maintains momentum despite its density, in part because each sentence tends to add a new facet of pressure, image, or character. A few phrases edge toward ornamental, but they do not materially slow or obscure the closure.</comment>

The conceptual shift from punishment to designed mercy within a fishing village feels both surprising and inevitable in hindsight.
