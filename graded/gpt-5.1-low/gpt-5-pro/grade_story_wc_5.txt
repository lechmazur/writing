<question>3</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>The setting is exquisitely particular—subarctic crater, hoarfrosted orchard, packing shed debris—and always filtered through her purpose rather than feeling touristic. The final sense that the orchard “seemed less dead” and the tripod points east shows setting participating in the ending, not just framing it. This is elite-level atmospheric work, though the external constraints of the crater could press her choices even harder.</comment>
<question>4</question><grade>7.8</grade><comment>The dominant stake—find Fen before “white noise” erases him—comes through clearly, and the mapping ritual functions as the on-page pressure test of that desire. Conflict stays largely internal and situational; nothing seriously obstructs her ritual, and we never see an option foreclosed beyond general time and cold. The result is resonant but a shade underpowered for top-tier stakes/escalation bands.</comment>

The ending reweights prior material: the orchard’s apparent deadness, the safekept starlight, and the plate’s dual role (map and seal) all accrue meaning around remembrance and repair. Complexity is maintained without sermonizing, leaving room for multiple readings.</comment>

There is no head-hopping or stance wobble; distance remains steady and apt. A hair more modulation—brief sharper, plainer beats under peak pressure—could sharpen contrast without sacrificing lyricism.</comment>

The prose does skate close to purple in places, but it remains coherent and supports mood and theme rather than obscuring them.
