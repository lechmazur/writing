<question>4</question><grade>8.4</grade><comment>The dominant stake is whether beauty and meaning can be reclaimed from failure (Mara’s phoenix, the traveler’s lost music, the child’s cracked prism), and this is signaled early and kept tight. The phoenix’s song is a clear tightening and turn, forcing the courtyard—and then city—to reinterpret what counts as success. <question>5</question><grade>8.8</grade><comment>Themes of imperfection, attention, and practice over miracle are woven organically through repeated, concrete particulars: cracks, herbs, breath, and light. Subtext around communication (plants instead of speech, silence instead of compliments, listening in hands) deepens the idea of “awakening” as mutual and partial. The ending invites reflection on how communities ritualize safety and failure without closing off alternative readings.</comment>
